Using Common-Size Financial Statements for Analysis


When it comes to financial analysis, common-size financial statements are a powerful tool that can provide valuable insights into a company’s financial health and performance. By expressing each line item in a financial statement as a percentage of a base figure, common-size financial statements allow for easy comparison between different companies, different time periods, or different divisions within the same company. In this blog post, we will explore the benefits and applications of using common-size financial statements for analysis.

Understanding Common-Size Financial Statements

Common-size financial statements are created by expressing each line item as a percentage of a base figure within the same financial statement. The base figure is typically total sales for the income statement and total assets for the balance sheet. By doing so, common-size financial statements highlight the relative proportions of different line items, making it easier to identify trends, patterns, and anomalies.

Benefits of Common-Size Financial Statements

  1. Facilitates Comparison: Common-size financial statements allow for easy comparison between companies of different sizes and industries. By standardizing the presentation of financial data, analysts can compare the financial structures of companies without being biased by their individual scales.

  2. Identifies Trends: Through common-size financial statements, trends and changes in a company’s financial structure become more apparent. Analysts can quickly identify if a particular expense item is increasing as a percentage of sales or if there are significant shifts in the asset composition.

  3. Assists in Ratio Analysis: Common-size financial statements provide a foundation for ratio analysis. Ratios such as gross profit margin, operating profit margin, and return on assets can be calculated directly from the common-size income statement and balance sheet, allowing for a deeper understanding of a company’s financial performance.

  4. Useful for Internal Analysis: Within a company, common-size financial statements can be used to compare the performance of different divisions or business units. This can help management identify areas of strength and weakness and allocate resources more effectively.

Applications of Common-Size Financial Statements

  1. Industry Benchmarking: Common-size financial statements are valuable for benchmarking a company’s financial structure against industry averages. This can provide insight into how a company’s financial structure compares to its peers and where it may need to make adjustments.

  2. Investment Analysis: Investors and analysts can use common-size financial statements to assess the financial health and performance of potential investment opportunities. By comparing the financial structures of different companies, investors can make more informed decisions.

  3. Forecasting: Common-size financial statements can be used to forecast future financial performance. By identifying trends and patterns in the relative proportions of different line items, analysts can make educated predictions about a company’s future financial health.

Conclusion

In conclusion, common-size financial statements are a valuable tool for financial analysis. By standardizing the presentation of financial data, common-size financial statements facilitate comparison, identify trends, assist in ratio analysis, and have various applications in industry benchmarking, investment analysis, and forecasting. Whether you are an investor, analyst, or a company’s management team, leveraging common-size financial statements can provide deep insights into the financial health and performance of a company.
