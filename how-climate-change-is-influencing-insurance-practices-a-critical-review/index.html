How Climate Change is Influencing Insurance Practices: A Critical Review


Climate change is increasingly becoming a significant factor in shaping insurance practices worldwide. As the effects of climate-related disasters intensify, insurance companies are confronted with unprecedented challenges in assessing and insuring risks. The escalating costs of natural catastrophes coupled with the growing severity of weather-related events have injected a sense of urgency in the insurance sector. Risks of Insuring Climate-Related Losses The unprecedented frequency and ferocity of climate-related disasters have uncovered long-standing weaknesses in insurance systems. Insurers, previously relying heavily on historical data, are now grappling with rising uncertainty on actuarial assumptions. In such a fluid environment, insurers face significant challenges in estimating probabilities and quantifying potential losses. Policyholders, too, often overlook or underestimate the climate hazard risks, which exacerbates insurers' difficulties in accurately assessing premiums. Devastation from 2017 Hurricanes in the United States Events such as the devastating 2017 hurricanes in the United States serve as stark reminders of the irreparable damage wrought by severe weather events. The massive categories 4 and 5 storms, Hurricanes Harvey, Irma, and Maria, perpetrated unprecedented destruction across the Gulf Coast and the southeastern United States. Financial estimates highlighted staggering losses felt by both private and public sectors, emphasizing the immense strain experienced by insurance companies. Growing Recognition and Response from Insurers and Policy Makers Recognizing the reality of undeniable losses linked to climate-related events has led the insurance and financial community to systematically reevaluate how catastrophes are addressed. This recognition fosters a collaborative environment encouraging solutions and change from both the regulated and unregulated sectors.
