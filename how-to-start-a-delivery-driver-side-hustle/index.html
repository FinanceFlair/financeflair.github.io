How to Start a Delivery Driver Side Hustle


In today’s fast-paced world, the need for convenient delivery services has skyrocketed. People are looking for efficient, reliable, and cost-effective ways to get food, groceries, and packages delivered right to their doorsteps. As a result, the delivery driver side hustle has become one of the most popular and accessible opportunities for earning extra income. Whether you’re looking to pay off debt, save for a special purchase, or simply supplement your main income, starting a delivery driver side hustle can offer flexible hours and a steady flow of work.

But how do you get started? In this post, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know about starting your own delivery driver side hustle—from the requirements and equipment to tips for success and maximizing your earnings.

Why Choose a Delivery Driver Side Hustle?

Before diving into the logistics, it’s important to understand why becoming a delivery driver is a great side hustle option. Here are some of the main benefits:

  1. Flexible Hours: One of the biggest advantages of working as a delivery driver is the flexibility. You can work whenever it fits into your schedule, whether that’s a few hours on the weekend, after work, or even late at night.

  2. Low Entry Barriers: Most delivery companies don’t require a formal interview or significant prior experience. As long as you meet the basic requirements (such as owning a reliable vehicle), you can start almost immediately.

  3. Control Over Earnings: Unlike traditional part-time jobs with fixed hourly rates, many delivery platforms offer drivers the ability to earn based on the amount of work they complete. This means the more hours you put in, the more you can potentially earn.

  4. Opportunity for Growth: As you gain experience, you can refine your driving skills and become more efficient, leading to better tips and higher ratings from customers.

Step 1: Understand the Delivery Platforms

The first step in starting your delivery driver side hustle is selecting the right delivery platform. There are several options, each with its own unique set of requirements, pay structures, and benefits. Here are a few of the most popular:

1. Food Delivery Services

  • Uber Eats: One of the most well-known platforms, Uber Eats allows you to deliver food from local restaurants to customers. This service offers flexible hours, competitive pay, and the option to work in your spare time.
  • DoorDash: DoorDash operates similarly to Uber Eats but may have different payment structures. They often have a high demand for drivers, especially during peak meal times.
  • Grubhub: Grubhub is another popular food delivery service that can help you earn money delivering meals to people in your area. Grubhub offers the option to schedule deliveries in advance or work on-demand.
  • Postmates: Acquired by Uber, Postmates delivers food, groceries, and other essentials. Postmates can be a great option for drivers who want variety in the types of deliveries they take.

2. Grocery Delivery Services

  • Instacart: Instacart allows drivers to deliver groceries. As a shopper, you will be responsible for picking out the groceries for the customer before delivering them to their doorstep.
  • Shipt: Similar to Instacart, Shipt allows you to pick and deliver groceries for customers. It’s another flexible side hustle, with the added benefit of working directly with stores like Target and Costco.

3. Package Delivery Services

  • Amazon Flex: If you’re interested in delivering packages instead of food, Amazon Flex is a great option. You’ll be delivering Amazon orders directly to customers. Amazon Flex allows you to work in blocks of time, which gives you flexibility in scheduling.
  • Roadie: Roadie focuses on delivering items like packages, luggage, and other non-food items. They often allow you to deliver on longer routes, making it ideal for those who don’t mind driving longer distances.

4. Specialty Delivery Services

  • GoPuff: GoPuff is a delivery service that delivers snacks, drinks, and other convenience store items. If you enjoy working late hours, GoPuff operates 24/7 and often needs drivers during late-night shifts.
  • Deliv: Specializing in same-day deliveries, Deliv focuses on delivering large items such as furniture or retail orders. It can be a great option for those who are comfortable delivering large items.

Choosing the right platform for your side hustle will depend on the type of deliveries you’re interested in and the hours you want to work. Some drivers choose to work across multiple platforms, while others specialize in one. Either way, researching your options and reading reviews from other drivers is a good first step.

Step 2: Meet the Requirements

Each delivery platform has specific requirements that you’ll need to meet in order to become a driver. While the exact requirements may vary by company, here are some of the most common criteria:

1. Age: Most platforms require drivers to be at least 18 years old, but some, like Amazon Flex, may have a higher age requirement (21 years old).

2. Vehicle: You’ll need a reliable vehicle, whether it’s a car, scooter, bike, or even a truck, depending on the type of deliveries you plan to make. Be sure to check each platform’s requirements to see if your vehicle meets their standards.

3. Driver’s License and Insurance: A valid driver’s license is essential, and many platforms will also require proof of insurance. Make sure your insurance covers commercial driving if necessary.

4. Smartphone: Almost all delivery services rely on apps for managing deliveries, tracking payments, and communicating with customers. A smartphone with reliable internet access is a must.

5. Background Check: Delivery companies often perform background checks to ensure drivers have a clean driving record and no criminal history. This process may take a few days to complete.

Once you’ve met the requirements and signed up with a delivery platform, you’ll usually need to attend a brief onboarding session or watch an orientation video to get familiar with the app and policies.

Step 3: Get the Right Gear

In addition to meeting the basic requirements, having the right gear can make your delivery driver side hustle more efficient and enjoyable. Here are a few things to consider:

1. Phone Mount: Using a phone mount is crucial to ensure you can follow navigation directions safely without needing to hold your phone while driving.

2. Insulated Bag: If you’re delivering food, an insulated bag can help keep hot items hot and cold items cold. Many platforms provide bags, but it’s worth checking to see if you need to purchase your own.

3. Charger: Long hours on the road can drain your phone’s battery, so having a portable charger or car charger is essential for keeping your device powered throughout the day.

4. Car Supplies: If you’re using your personal vehicle for deliveries, make sure it’s well-maintained. Regularly check your tire pressure, oil levels, and brakes. It’s also a good idea to keep a few essential tools (like jumper cables or a tire repair kit) in your car.

5. Comfortable Clothing: You’ll be spending a lot of time driving or walking, so comfortable clothing is a must. Depending on the weather, you might need to invest in a rain jacket, winter coat, or sunscreen.

Step 4: Learn How to Maximize Your Earnings

Once you’re on the road, there are several ways you can maximize your earnings as a delivery driver. Here are some key strategies to help you make the most out of your side hustle:

1. Work During Peak Hours: Most delivery platforms have peak hours when demand is highest. For food delivery, this is typically lunch and dinner time. For grocery delivery, it’s often weekends or evenings. Working during these busy times can help you earn more in tips and incentives.

2. Be Efficient: Plan your routes ahead of time to avoid traffic and minimize delivery time. The more efficient you are, the more deliveries you can complete in a shorter amount of time.

3. Maintain a High Rating: Customer ratings can impact your earnings. If you’re friendly, professional, and deliver orders quickly, you’re more likely to receive positive reviews and higher tips.

4. Take Advantage of Bonuses: Many platforms offer bonuses for completing a certain number of deliveries in a specific timeframe. Keep an eye on these promotions to increase your pay.

5. Manage Your Expenses: As a delivery driver, you’ll need to cover your own fuel, vehicle maintenance, and other expenses. Keep track of your expenses and consider using apps like Stride or Everlance to track mileage and tax deductions.

Step 5: Stay Safe on the Job

Safety is a top priority when working as a delivery driver. Follow these tips to stay safe on the road:

  1. Obey Traffic Laws: Always follow traffic rules to avoid accidents or fines. Speeding or running red lights is not worth the risk.

  2. Be Aware of Your Surroundings: Be cautious when making deliveries, especially to unfamiliar or remote areas. If you feel uncomfortable, trust your instincts and avoid going into potentially dangerous locations.

  3. Protect Personal Information: Keep your personal information private, and be cautious when interacting with customers. Avoid sharing details such as your home address or phone number unless necessary.

  4. Have Emergency Contacts: Share your route or delivery schedule with a friend or family member, so they know where you’ll be. It’s always a good idea to have someone you trust aware of your movements, especially if you’re working late hours.

Conclusion

Starting a delivery driver side hustle is a fantastic way to earn extra income while enjoying the flexibility of choosing your own hours. By understanding the requirements, selecting the right platforms, investing in the right gear, and maximizing your earnings, you can turn this side hustle into a profitable venture.

Whether you’re driving food, groceries, or packages, the world of delivery offers endless opportunities for those willing to put in the time and effort. So, hit the road, stay safe, and start earning today!
