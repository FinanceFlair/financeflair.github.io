Best Side Hustles for People Who Love to Teach


Teaching is a passion for many, and while some prefer a full-time teaching role, others might be looking for opportunities to bring in extra income while still sharing their knowledge and skills. Whether you’re an experienced educator or someone who simply loves explaining concepts, there are a variety of side hustles you can consider that allow you to leverage your teaching abilities.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best side hustles for people who love to teach. These side gigs can fit into your schedule, cater to your teaching strengths, and provide you with the opportunity to work from home or in various environments. Let’s dive into these amazing options.

1. Online Tutoring

Online tutoring has exploded in popularity in recent years, and for good reason. It’s flexible, rewarding, and can be done from the comfort of your home. The demand for online tutors spans a variety of subjects, from elementary school math to advanced college courses, as well as test prep for exams like the SAT, GRE, or TOEFL. As an online tutor, you can create your own schedule, choose the subjects that best match your expertise, and work with students from around the world.

How to Get Started:

  • Platforms to Consider: Websites like VIPKid, Tutor.com, Chegg Tutors, or Wyzant offer platforms where you can sign up to become an online tutor. Each of these platforms has a different process for vetting and onboarding tutors, so it’s important to research the requirements of each.
  • Teaching Style: Some platforms cater to specific age groups or education levels, so make sure to choose one that aligns with your teaching style and expertise.
  • Rates and Payment: Most platforms pay on a per-hour basis, and rates can vary based on the subject, your experience, and the platform you choose. Additionally, you may be able to set your own hourly rate on some platforms, giving you the freedom to determine how much you want to earn.

Online tutoring allows you to share your expertise in any subject area you’re passionate about, whether it’s math, language arts, science, or even niche areas like coding or music theory.

2. Create and Sell Online Courses

If you have a wealth of knowledge in a particular field and enjoy structuring lessons, creating an online course might be the perfect side hustle for you. Websites like Udemy, Teachable, and Skillshare provide a platform for you to develop and sell courses to a global audience. Whether it’s a course on history, technology, cooking, or photography, the possibilities are endless.

How to Get Started:

  • Choose a Niche: Think about what you are passionate about and what people are willing to pay for. It’s important to target a specific audience so that your course can stand out in a crowded marketplace.
  • Create the Course: This could involve writing lessons, recording videos, creating downloadable materials, and providing resources that will help students learn. Make sure the course is well-structured and easy to follow. High-quality content will attract more students.
  • Marketing Your Course: Promote your course through social media, your personal website, and email lists. The more you market your course, the more likely it will gain traction.

Creating an online course allows you to earn passive income. Once the course is created, students can enroll at any time, and you’ll continue to earn money as they go through the content.

3. Educational YouTube Channel

If you love to teach and have a flair for video creation, starting a YouTube channel might be a perfect side hustle. YouTube offers an immense opportunity to reach a wide audience with your educational content. Whether you’re explaining complex scientific concepts, teaching a new language, or offering study tips, YouTube can be a platform for you to share your passion for teaching.

How to Get Started:

  • Find Your Focus: Decide on the niche you want to teach. For example, you could focus on high school biology, beginner coding tutorials, or tips for acing standardized tests. Focus on what excites you, as your enthusiasm will come across to your audience.
  • Create Engaging Content: Invest in good equipment (such as a quality camera and microphone) to ensure your videos are clear and professional. Keep your content engaging by using graphics, animations, or real-life examples to explain concepts.
  • Monetize Your Channel: Once your channel grows and gains traction, you can monetize it through YouTube ads, affiliate marketing, or even selling your own educational materials.

Educational YouTube channels are a great way to combine your teaching skills with creativity, while also providing an avenue for passive income.

4. Writing Educational Content and E-books

For those who love to write and educate, writing educational content or e-books is a natural fit. Whether you’re writing blog posts, curriculum guides, or educational e-books, there’s a significant demand for well-crafted content that helps people learn new skills or concepts.

How to Get Started:

  • Choose a Topic: Focus on a specific subject where you have expertise. It could be related to your professional field, or you can target more general topics that are in demand, such as personal finance, study strategies, or mindfulness techniques.
  • Create High-Quality Content: Write in a way that simplifies complex ideas and makes learning enjoyable. Break down the content into digestible sections and incorporate examples, illustrations, and practical tips to keep readers engaged.
  • Sell Your Work: You can sell your e-books on platforms like Amazon Kindle, Gumroad, or through your personal website. Additionally, you could write guest posts or articles for educational websites and get paid for your contributions.

Writing educational content allows you to showcase your knowledge and teach others without the need for live interactions, which can be a great option for introverts or those who prefer to work independently.

5. Curriculum Development for Schools or Homeschooling

If you’re skilled at creating structured lessons and educational materials, curriculum development could be an excellent side hustle. Schools, tutoring centers, and homeschooling parents are always on the lookout for high-quality curriculum that aligns with academic standards and fosters effective learning.

How to Get Started:

  • Understand the Market: Research the specific needs of schools, homeschooling communities, or educational organizations. For instance, you might design lessons that adhere to state standards or cater to specific age groups.
  • Create Resources: Develop comprehensive lesson plans, worksheets, activities, and assessments. Be sure that the materials you create are interactive, engaging, and educational.
  • Sell Your Curriculum: You can sell your curriculum on sites like Teachers Pay Teachers, or directly to parents or schools through networking and outreach. Additionally, you might partner with educational organizations to distribute your materials.

Curriculum development allows you to influence education on a broader scale, and you can earn income based on your ability to craft useful, high-quality materials.

6. Teach a Language

If you are fluent in multiple languages, teaching a language online can be a rewarding side hustle. Platforms like iTalki, Preply, and Verbling allow you to teach students from around the world who want to learn a new language. You can teach English to non-native speakers, or teach other languages like Spanish, French, Mandarin, or German.

How to Get Started:

  • Sign Up for a Platform: After creating a profile on a platform, you can start teaching students. Set up your teaching hours and list the languages you can teach.
  • Prepare Lessons: Tailor lessons to your students’ needs, whether it’s for beginners, business language, or conversational skills. You can provide lessons via video chat, offering flexibility and real-time interaction with your students.
  • Promote Yourself: The more students you work with, the more reviews you’ll collect. Word of mouth and positive reviews will help you grow your presence on these platforms.

Language teaching is in high demand, and it’s a great way to connect with people from different cultures while earning money.

7. Educational Consulting

If you have extensive experience in a particular field of education, educational consulting could be a lucrative side hustle. As an educational consultant, you could help schools, teachers, or parents with improving educational practices, curriculum development, or setting up homeschooling plans.

How to Get Started:

  • Build Your Expertise: Draw on your years of teaching experience, any certifications, or specialized knowledge you have in education. Establish yourself as an authority in your chosen area.
  • Market Your Services: Network with schools, education-focused non-profits, and parents. Create a website or social media profiles to advertise your services.
  • Offer Tailored Advice: As a consultant, you may help with things like teacher training, educational program evaluation, or advising parents on how to set up a successful homeschooling routine.

Consulting offers a great way to work flexibly while providing valuable insight to those in the education sector.

8. Create Educational Games or Apps

If you have a passion for technology, creating educational games or apps is a unique side hustle that combines teaching with innovation. Parents and educators are always looking for fun, interactive ways to teach children new concepts. By creating a game or app, you can provide an engaging educational tool while also generating revenue.

How to Get Started:

  • Learn the Basics of Game/App Development: You don’t need to be a professional developer, but learning the basics of game design or app development will help you bring your ideas to life.
  • Focus on a Niche: Identify what educational area your game or app will target—whether it’s math, reading, or science—and design it around that.
  • Monetization: Apps can be monetized through in-app purchases, ads, or premium versions. Educational games also have the potential to be purchased by schools or parents who want to supplement their child’s learning experience.

Developing educational apps or games allows you to tap into the growing edtech market while using your teaching skills to create something enjoyable and educational for children and adults alike.

In conclusion, the side hustles listed above are fantastic options for people who love to teach. They offer flexibility, creativity, and the opportunity to impact others’ lives positively. Whether you’re tutoring online, creating a YouTube channel, or designing educational content, there are numerous ways to share your passion for teaching while earning extra income. Choose one that aligns with your skills and interests, and start sharing your knowledge with the world!
