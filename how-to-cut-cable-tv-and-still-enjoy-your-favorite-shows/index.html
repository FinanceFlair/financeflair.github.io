How to Cut Cable TV and Still Enjoy Your Favorite Shows


Cutting the cord on cable TV has become increasingly popular in recent years. With the rise of streaming services, cheaper internet plans, and affordable digital antennas, more people are discovering that they can enjoy their favorite shows without the hefty monthly fees cable companies charge. If you’re tired of paying for channels you rarely watch or simply want to take control of your entertainment options, this comprehensive guide will help you make the switch while still keeping your favorite TV shows within reach.

Why Cut Cable TV?

Cable TV may have been the go-to entertainment hub for decades, but it comes with a few unavoidable drawbacks:

  • High monthly costs: Cable packages often come with a premium price tag that can bust your budget.
  • Bundled channels you don’t watch: Many packages force you to pay for dozens, if not hundreds, of channels you’ll never use.
  • Contract commitments and fees: Cancellations, equipment rentals, and installation fees can add up.
  • Limited flexibility: You can only watch shows on TV at specific times unless you have a DVR.

Cutting cable doesn’t mean sacrificing your favorite shows or entertainment variety. It’s about gaining control over what you watch, how you watch it, and how much you spend.

Step 1: Understand Your Viewing Habits

Before deciding how to replace your cable service, take stock of what shows and channels you watch the most. Ask yourself:

  • What are my go-to TV shows and networks?
  • Do I watch live sports, news, or special events regularly?
  • How much time do I spend on TV daily or weekly?
  • Do I prefer watching on a big screen or on-the-go via mobile devices?

Knowing your habits helps you choose the right replacement options. For example, if live sports are a priority, you might want a streaming package that includes live event channels. If you prefer binge-watching scripted series, on-demand streaming platforms might be enough.

Step 2: Choose Streaming Services Wisely

Streaming services have revolutionized how we consume TV content. Many offer a vast library of shows and movies, custom recommendations, and the flexibility to watch on multiple devices.

Popular Streaming Platforms

  • Netflix: Extensive catalog of original series, movies, and documentaries.
  • Hulu: Strong selection of current-season TV episodes, next-day airings, and live TV options.
  • Amazon Prime Video: Large content library, including movies, series, and extras with Amazon membership.
  • Disney+: Home to Disney classics, Pixar, Marvel, Star Wars, and National Geographic content.
  • HBO Max: Access to HBO original programming, recent film releases, and exclusive series.
  • Apple TV+: Focus on original programming with unique shows and movies.
  • Peacock: NBCUniversal’s platform offers free tiers with ads and premium options for exclusive content.

Most platforms offer monthly subscriptions with no contract, giving you control over what you pay.

Step 3: Consider Live TV Streaming Services

If you miss watching live broadcasts or sports events as they happen, several streaming services specialize in live TV without the cable bill. These provide access to major networks along with local channels, news, and sports.

Leading Live TV Streaming Options

  • YouTube TV: Wide channel selection, unlimited DVR storage, accessible on many devices.
  • Sling TV: Flexible packages that let you customize channel lineups based on your preferences.
  • FuboTV: Sports-centric streaming service with a strong mix of entertainment and news.
  • Hulu + Live TV: Combines Hulu’s on-demand library with live broadcast channels.

These services often charge less than traditional cable and let you cancel or pause service anytime.

Step 4: Use a Digital Antenna for Local Channels

Local channels broadcasting news, weather, sports, and primetime shows can be picked up for free with a digital antenna. Depending on your location, a simple indoor antenna can receive HD broadcasts from multiple nearby stations.

Tips for Choosing an Antenna

  • Placement: Try different spots near windows or high locations for the best signal.
  • Range: Antennas are rated for range; longer ranges work better in rural areas.
  • Cost: Indoor antennas are inexpensive, sometimes under $30, while outdoor versions can cost more.

A digital antenna supplements your streaming services, ensuring you never miss your local news or network shows.

Step 5: Leverage On-Demand Options and Catch-Up Apps

Many networks and streaming services offer catch-up TV apps or websites where you can watch episodes after they air without needing a live subscription. Some popular apps include:

  • CBS All Access (Paramount+): For CBS programs on demand.
  • The CW App: Free episodes of shows currently airing on The CW network.
  • ABC and NBC Apps: Access to their original programming and popular series.
  • Network Websites: Often have the latest episodes available for limited time.

These platforms are a handy way to keep up on current episodes without subscribing to full cable or live TV plans.

Step 6: Optimize Your Internet Service

Cable cutting means your internet connection becomes the backbone of your entertainment experience. Whether you stream shows or watch live sports, a reliable high-speed internet plan is key.

  • Aim for at least 25 Mbps download speeds for HD streaming or 50 Mbps or higher for multiple users.
  • Check for unlimited data plans to avoid overage charges with heavy streaming.
  • Consider bundling internet with your mobile or other services for cost savings.
  • Regularly update your modem and router to support faster streaming and multiple devices.

Investing in good internet infrastructure will keep your shows streaming smoothly.

Step 7: Explore Screen Casting and Multi-Device Viewing

Modern technology allows you to enjoy your content on any screen you want:

  • Smart TVs: Most come with built-in apps for Netflix, Hulu, and others.
  • Streaming devices: Roku, Amazon Fire Stick, Apple TV, Google Chromecast give access to numerous apps.
  • Mobile devices: Stream or download episodes to smartphones and tablets.
  • Gaming consoles: Xbox and PlayStation support popular streaming apps.
  • Chromecast or AirPlay: Cast shows from mobile devices to your TV seamlessly.

This flexibility means you don’t have to be chained to your living room set to enjoy your favorite programs.

Step 8: Manage and Save Money Smartly

Cutting cable saves money, but multiple streaming subscriptions can add up quickly. Here are some tips to keep your entertainment budget under control:

  • Rotate subscriptions: Subscribe to one or two services at a time, switching every few months to catch what you want.
  • Share family plans: Many services offer plans that allow multiple simultaneous streams.
  • Take advantage of free trials: Test services before committing.
  • Watch ads on free tiers: Some platforms let you watch content free with ads.
  • Bundle services: Look for combos or packages that offer discounts.

Tracking your monthly subscriptions helps you keep spending in check while enjoying diverse programs.

Step 9: Explore Additional Entertainment Alternatives

Cable was once the centerpiece of home entertainment, but it’s no longer the only option. To complement your favorite shows consider:

  • Podcasts: Dive into engaging audio series on a wide array of topics.
  • YouTube and independent creators: Discover creative original content.
  • Classic TV streaming sites: Platforms offering old favorites for nostalgic viewing.
  • Library digital resources: Check out free movies and shows via apps like Hoopla or Kanopy.

These options expand your entertainment palette beyond traditional TV.

Wrapping It Up: Embrace the Cord-Cutting Lifestyle

Cutting cable TV isn’t just about ditching a bill—it’s a chance to customize your entertainment experience tailored perfectly to your tastes and schedule. With the combined power of streaming services, digital antennas, reliable internet, and savvy subscription management, you can watch your favorite shows without compromise.

The key steps—evaluating your habits, picking the right mix of streaming and live channels, using free options, and optimizing your tech setup—will ensure that the transition is easy and rewarding. You might even find that freeing yourself from cable’s limitations opens the door to a broader, richer world of content than ever before.

Enjoy the future of TV your way!
