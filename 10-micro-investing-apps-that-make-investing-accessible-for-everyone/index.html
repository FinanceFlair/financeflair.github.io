10 Micro Investing Apps That Make Investing Accessible for Everyone


Investing in the stock market used to be a privilege reserved for the wealthy and financially savvy. However, with the rise of micro-investing apps, individuals can now start investing with small amounts of money, making it accessible to everyone. These apps have revolutionized the investment landscape by eliminating high barriers to entry and empowering people to grow their wealth, no matter their income level. In this article, we’ll explore 10 micro-investing apps that are making investing more inclusive and convenient for all.

1. Acorns

Acorns is a popular micro-investing app that enables users to invest their spare change. By linking their debit and credit cards to the app, users can automatically invest the spare change from their everyday purchases. Acorns offers various investment portfolios based on the user’s risk tolerance, making it a seamless and low-barrier entry point for new investors.

2. Stash

Stash is another micro-investing app that aims to simplify the investment process. With Stash, users can start investing with as little as $5. The app provides educational content and personalized investment recommendations, making it ideal for beginners who want to learn as they invest.

3. Robinhood

Robinhood has gained significant popularity for its commission-free trading model. The app allows users to buy and sell stocks, ETFs, options, and cryptocurrencies without paying any commission fees. With its user-friendly interface and no minimum balance requirement, Robinhood has attracted a diverse user base, including first-time investors.

4. Betterment

Betterment is a robo-advisor that offers automated investing services. Users can set their investment goals and risk preferences, and Betterment will create and manage a diversified portfolio on their behalf. The app’s hands-off approach to investing appeals to individuals who prefer a more passive investment strategy.

5. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is another robo-advisor that provides automated investment management. The app offers features such as tax-loss harvesting and portfolio rebalancing to optimize users’ investment returns. With a minimum investment requirement of $500, Wealthfront caters to investors who are ready to commit slightly larger amounts to their portfolios.

6. M1 Finance

M1 Finance combines the features of a robo-advisor with the flexibility of a self-directed brokerage. Users can create custom investment portfolios or choose from pre-built portfolios called “Pies.” M1 Finance also allows fractional share investing, making it easier for users to diversify their investments with limited funds.

7. Public

Public differentiates itself by offering a social investing experience. Users can follow friends and other investors, share insights, and invest in stocks and ETFs commission-free. The app aims to create a community-driven investing platform, making investing more engaging and collaborative.

8. Raiz

Originally launched in Australia as Acorns, Raiz is a micro-investing app that rounds up users’ everyday purchases to the nearest dollar and invests the spare change. Raiz also offers additional investment options, including recurring investments and portfolio customization, catering to a wide range of investment preferences.

9. Stockpile

Stockpile allows users to invest in fractional shares of stocks and ETFs with as little as $5. The app is designed to make investing more accessible to younger generations, offering features such as custodial accounts for minors and educational resources to teach them about investing from an early age.

10. Round

Round is a micro-investing app that focuses on sustainable and socially responsible investing. The app offers curated investment options that align with users’ values, allowing them to invest in companies that prioritize environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors. Round’s emphasis on impact investing sets it apart in the micro-investing landscape.

In conclusion, micro-investing apps have democratized the investment process, allowing individuals from all walks of life to participate in the financial markets. With low minimum investment requirements, user-friendly interfaces, and a range of investment options, these apps are breaking down traditional barriers to entry and empowering a new generation of investors. Whether you’re a novice looking to dip your toes into investing or a seasoned investor seeking a more accessible platform, these 10 micro-investing apps offer something for everyone. Happy investing!
