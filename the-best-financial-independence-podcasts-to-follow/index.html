The Best Financial Independence Podcasts to Follow


In recent years, the concept of financial independence has gained significant traction. More people are exploring ways to take control of their finances, reduce reliance on traditional careers, and build a lifestyle aligned with their values. One of the best ways to stay informed and inspired on this journey is by tuning into financial independence podcasts. These podcasts offer a mix of expert advice, personal experiences, actionable strategies, and motivation for building a secure financial future.

Whether you’re just starting your path toward financial independence or well on your way, there’s a podcast for you. Let’s dive into some of the best financial independence podcasts to follow, covering a variety of perspectives, topics, and styles that will keep you learning and motivated.

1. ChooseFI

Arguably one of the most popular podcasts in the financial independence space, ChooseFI focuses on practical wisdom and actionable advice. Hosted by Brad Barrett and Jonathan Mendonsa, the show features interviews with financial experts, educators, and everyday individuals who have significantly advanced on their FI journey.

The hosts do an excellent job breaking down complex concepts into digestible tips for listeners. They cover wide-ranging topics such as tax optimization, investment strategies, reducing expenses, and travel hacking. What makes the show even more appealing is its relatable tone and community-based approach that helps listeners feel connected to a larger financial independence movement.

With hundreds of episodes, ChooseFI has become a cornerstone for people who want to accelerate their financial independence goals while building meaningful connections along the way.

2. The Mad Fientist Podcast

Known for his popular blog, The Mad Fientist (aka Brandon), extends his expertise into the podcast world. This podcast focuses on advanced financial independence strategies, particularly for those interested in early retirement. As an FI enthusiast himself, Brandon shares first-hand insights about his own journey toward achieving financial independence and interviews a range of experts in personal finance and investing.

If you’re interested in topics like tax optimization for early retirees, the psychology of financial independence, or the mechanics of low-cost index investing, this show delivers a wealth of value. The Mad Fientist’s analytical approach and deep dives into topics make it a must-listen for those refining their FI strategies.

3. Financial Independence Europe Podcast

The Financial Independence Europe Podcast offers a refreshing, non-American perspective on financial independence. Hosted by Alvar, Mathias, and Araminta, this podcast focuses on FI strategies, stories, and tips specific to the European audience.

While the core principles of financial independence are universal, the hosts acknowledge that certain strategies might differ depending on geographical location. They explore topics like international investing, tax systems in Europe, cost-effective cross-border living, and lifestyle design.

With interviews from European FI enthusiasts and experts, this podcast is perfect for listeners based in Europe or those curious about global approaches to achieving financial independence.

4. Afford Anything

Paula Pant’s Afford Anything podcast dives deep into the intersection of money, mindset, and personal growth. Her mantra, “You can afford anything, but not everything,” underscores the importance of intentional spending and decision-making.

The podcast features interviews with a diverse range of guests, including best-selling authors, behavioral economists, entrepreneurs, and financial independence experts. Paula’s thought-provoking questions and empathetic hosting style make the conversations engaging and relatable.

This podcast goes beyond budget tips and dives into the deeper “why” behind financial choices, making it an excellent resource for listeners who seek to align their values and priorities with their financial goals.

5. BiggerPockets Money Podcast

Brought to you by the team behind BiggerPockets, a well-known resource for real estate investors, the BiggerPockets Money Podcast is hosted by Scott Trench and Mindy Jensen. While the podcast is rooted in real estate investing, it covers a broader range of topics that align with financial independence.

Episodes explore everything from saving strategies and debt repayment plans to building side hustles and understanding the psychology of money. With a mix of expert guests and anecdotal success stories from regular people, the podcast offers actionable tips for beginners and seasoned FI enthusiasts alike.

For anyone looking to grow their wealth through property investment or other means to achieve FI, this podcast is a goldmine.

6. The Stacking Benjamins Show

Known for its lighthearted and entertaining tone, The Stacking Benjamins Show combines personal finance with humor. Hosted by Joe Saul-Sehy and OG, the podcast delivers useful insights and actionable advice without overwhelming listeners with jargon or complex concepts.

The show stands out for its variety format, featuring diverse segments like interviews with financial experts, listener-based Q&A, and tips on everything from mastering your budget to planning for FI. The balance of education and entertainment makes it a great choice for those who want to tackle finance with a bit of fun.

7. FIRE Drill Podcast

While the FIRE Drill Podcast concluded its run in 2020, its library of episodes remains a treasure trove for anyone interested in financial independence and early retirement. Hosted by J, also known as FIRECracker from the blog Millennial Revolution, and Gwen, the podcast provides smart, witty, and insightful discussions on a range of FIRE topics.

The show delves into strategies for growing wealth, alternative paths to FI, and the behind-the-scenes stories of people who took unconventional steps to achieve their financial goals. Its engaging and conversational tone keeps listeners coming back for more.

8. The Frugal Friends Podcast

If you’re looking for fun and effective ways to cut costs without feeling deprived, The Frugal Friends Podcast is perfect for you. Hosted by Jen Smith and Jill Sirianni, the show focuses on frugality, budgeting, and financial independence.

The hosts emphasize the importance of keeping spending in check while still enjoying life. By weaving humor, honesty, and practical advice into every episode, the podcast provides a refreshing perspective on achieving financial freedom through mindful saving and intentional living.

9. Journey to Launch

Hosted by Jamila Souffrant, Journey to Launch is a motivational podcast that explores financial freedom through the lens of wealth-building and entrepreneurship. Jamila shares her personal journey of transitioning from a traditional career to becoming financially independent, all while balancing her role as a mother and wife.

The podcast features expert interviews and success stories from individuals who’ve implemented unique paths to FI. Jamila’s candid approach and willingness to tackle complex topics like generational wealth, systemic financial barriers, and cultural money dynamics make this podcast both inspiring and relatable for a wide audience.

10. Radical Personal Finance

For those who enjoy no-nonsense finance advice with an academic touch, the Radical Personal Finance podcast by Joshua Sheats is a must-listen. It’s designed for listeners who want to achieve financial independence by maximizing efficiency in every area of life.

Joshua’s episodes cover extensive topics, including insurance planning, entrepreneurship, tax strategies, investing, and even survivalism. The depth of the content makes it perfect for thinkers and strategists aiming to create a comprehensive financial independence plan.

Final Thoughts

Podcasts are the perfect medium for anyone looking to improve their financial literacy while on the go. From entertaining shows with laughs to deep-dive discussions on advanced financial topics, there are options to suit every level of experience and interest.

Listening to these podcasts not only keeps you updated on the latest trends but also connects you to a vibrant community of people striving for freedom and security. The blend of practical strategies, personal stories, and expert advice provides a well-rounded education in financial independence.

So grab your headphones, find a quiet moment, and let these podcasters guide and inspire you on your journey toward financial independence.
