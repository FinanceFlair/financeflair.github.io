How to Make Money with NFT Royalties


The digital frontier has unleashed countless ways to generate income, and one of the most groundbreaking developments is the rise of non-fungible tokens (NFTs). Beyond the initial sale of an NFT, creators now have the opportunity to earn ongoing revenue through built-in royalties — a paradigm shift in how art, music, collectibles, and other digital assets generate value over time.

If you’re intrigued by the prospect of leveraging NFT royalties to create a sustainable income stream, this deep-dive will walk you through everything you need to know: what NFT royalties are, how they function, platforms that support them, strategies to maximize earnings, and potential pitfalls to avoid.

Understanding NFT Royalties

At its core, NFT royalties enable creators to receive a percentage of the sale price every time their NFT is resold on a compatible marketplace. Unlike traditional art or collectibles, where creators typically profit only once from their initial sale, royalties embedded in NFTs allow artists, musicians, and content creators to benefit continuously as their work gains popularity and changes hands.

This is achieved via smart contracts — self-executing pieces of code stored on a blockchain — that automatically distribute the royalty fee to the creator whenever a sale is finalized. The royalty percentage is usually set by the creator at the time the NFT is minted and cannot easily be altered later.

How NFT Royalties Work in Practice

  1. Minting the NFT: When an NFT is minted, the creator specifies a royalty percentage, e.g., 5-10% of the resale price.
  2. Primary Sale: The first buyer typically pays the creator the full price without any royalty involved.
  3. Secondary Sales: Every resale of the NFT on participating marketplaces triggers the smart contract to automatically send the predetermined royalty percentage to the creator’s wallet.
  4. Multiple Resales: This process repeats indefinitely, ensuring ongoing compensation aligned with the asset’s market activity.

This mechanism is revolutionary in digital asset monetization, as it shifts revenue from a linear, one-off transaction model to a more decentralized, recurring income stream.

Platforms Supporting NFT Royalties

Not all NFT marketplaces provide full support for automated royalty payments, and interoperability between blockchains can vary. Picking the right platform is essential for ensuring your royalties are honored without manual intervention. Here are some of the leading marketplaces that facilitate NFT royalty payments effectively:

1. OpenSea

OpenSea is the largest NFT marketplace, built on Ethereum and supporting many other blockchains. It allows creators to set royalties ranging from 0 to 10% on secondary sales. OpenSea executes these royalties automatically for all sales that occur on their platform, supporting a wide range of NFTs, including art, music, domain names, and virtual worlds.

2. Rarible

Rarible is a decentralized NFT marketplace where creators can mint, buy, and sell NFTs freely. It allows royalty rates customizable up to 50%, providing flexibility to creators who want to maximize earnings on secondary sales. Rarible operates on Ethereum and offers community governance over platform policies.

3. Foundation

Foundation is a platform focused on digital art and creative work. Creators set a 10% royalty fee on secondary sales by default. The site prides itself on curating high-quality digital artworks and offers exposure to serious collectors.

4. Zora

Zora is a decentralized marketplace that supports dynamic pricing models and royalties. Its open protocol enables anyone to build their marketplaces with royalty logic embedded, creating opportunities for creators to tap into various audiences.

Setting the Right Royalty Percentage

Deciding on the royalty percentage requires a balance between maximizing long-term income and making your NFT attractive to buyers. Too high a royalty might deter potential investors, while too low might undervalue your ongoing effort.

  • The industry standard tends to hover around 5-10%.
  • High-profile artists have commanded royalties as high as 15-20%, but this is less common.
  • Consider your reputation, demand projections, and the typical royalties commanded in your NFT niche.

Understanding market sentiment and pricing strategies helps you set a royalty that incentivizes both you and your buyers, fostering a healthy trading ecosystem.

Strategies to Maximize Earnings from NFT Royalties

1. Focus on Quality and Scarcity

The probability of earning royalties increases when your NFTs maintain or gain value over time. Creating unique, high-quality art or content with limited editions helps build scarcity, which can drive secondary market activity and higher resale prices.

2. Build a Strong Community

A passionate fan base and community engagement amplify the demand for your NFTs. Active promotion on social media, collaborating with influencers, and participating in NFT forums can boost visibility and desirability, translating into more frequent sales and resales.

3. Collaborate and Cross-Promote

Partnering with other creators or brands can open doors to new audiences. Cross-project collaborations can lead to exclusive releases that sell for higher values, triggering more lucrative royalty payouts.

4. Leverage Multiple Marketplaces

Because NFT royalty enforcement depends on platform compatibility, listing your NFTs on multiple marketplaces that honor royalties helps maximize exposure and income. Check the specifics of each marketplace to ensure royalty settings are applied correctly.

5. Incorporate Utility

Adding real-world or digital utilities to your NFTs—such as exclusive access, physical goods, or experiences—can increase their desirability and secondary market activity. This extra value encourages trades and boosts royalty earnings.

6. Monitor and Participate in the Secondary Market

Keep an eye on your NFTs’ trading activities, and engage with collectors. Offering buy-back options, creating limited-time offers, or launching companion NFTs can revitalize interest and trading, increasing the volume of royalty-generating transactions.

Potential Challenges and How to Overcome Them

1. Royalties Not Honored Off-Platform

Some marketplaces do not enforce NFTs’ royalty clauses, especially decentralized platforms or custom-built venues. This means secondary sales could slip through without compensating creators.

Workaround: Advocate for universal royalty standards, educate buyers to purchase only on royalty-supporting platforms, and monitor NFT activity across blockchains.

2. Gas Fees Eroding Profits

Ethereum and other blockchains with high transaction fees can reduce the effective amount received from royalties, especially on lower-priced trades.

Workaround: Explore NFTs minted on blockchains with lower fees like Polygon, Flow, or Solana, or employ Layer 2 solutions that minimize costs for both creators and buyers.

3. Market Saturation and Volatility

The NFT space is subject to hype cycles, and oversupply can deflate prices, reducing resale activity and royalty income.

Workaround: Focus on niche markets or specialized content, build lasting relationships with collectors, and diversify your NFT offerings.

The Future of NFT Royalties

The technology and ecosystems supporting NFT royalties are evolving rapidly. There is increasing momentum toward more standardized, cross-platform royalty mechanisms that ensure creators get paid no matter where their NFTs are traded. Projects developing interoperable protocols and more sophisticated royalty tracking systems promise to create more reliable income streams for creators in the near term.

Additionally, smart contracts are becoming more sophisticated, enabling dynamic royalty structures, tiered payments, and shared royalties among multiple contributors. This opens exciting avenues for collaborative projects, music rights, and mixed media pieces to earn ongoing revenue in groundbreaking ways.

Conclusion

Making money with NFT royalties is one of the most exciting financial opportunities in the digital era for artists, musicians, and creators. By embedding royalty payments into your NFTs, you unlock the potential for perpetual earnings, aligning your incentives with the long-term success and popularity of your work.

To succeed:

  • Choose the right platform to mint and sell your NFTs,
  • Set a balanced royalty rate,
  • Create valuable, scarce content,
  • Cultivate an engaged community,
  • Explore multiple sales channels,
  • And keep up with technical developments supporting royalties.

While there are challenges, savvy creators who understand the mechanics and actively manage their NFT ecosystem are well-positioned to capitalize on the royalty economy, turning one-time artistic creations into enduring revenue machines.

The digital art and collectibles revolution is just beginning. With the right approach, NFT royalties can become a cornerstone in your creative business strategy, generating income that grows with your reputation and influence in the blockchain space.
