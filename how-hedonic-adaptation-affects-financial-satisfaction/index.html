How Hedonic Adaptation Affects Financial Satisfaction


Imagine achieving your long-term financial goals, acquiring wealth, and securing a financially stable future for yourself and your loved ones. Does this thought bring you unparalleled happiness and fulfillment? Probably not. This phenomenon is due to the concept of hedonic adaptation, coined by philosopher Jeremy Bentham in the late 18th century. It suggests that our brains have an innate mechanism to return to a basal level of happiness or satisfaction despite significant life changes, including financial gains. The Hedonic Treadmill Concept The notion of the hedonic treadmill implies that our brains strive to return to a state of equilibrium where we experience a neutral level of pleasure or satisfaction, known as the "happiness set point." This mental equilibrium defends us against extreme variations in emotional experiences, ensuring long-term well-being. However, it often leads us to pursue more and more, thinking that the next milestone or financial achievement will finally guarantee lasting happiness. Psychology of the Hedonic Adaptation Psychologists Richard Easterlin and Angus Deaton conducted extensive research on the concept of hedonic adaptation, producing groundbreaking insights into human psyche. They demonstrated that significant financial gains lead to temporary increases in happiness but eventually stagnate on the baseline set point due to hedonic adaptation. Moreover, significant financial losses render individuals less happy. According to Easterlin's paradox, however, within a society where average incomes increase significantly over time, rising overall incomes do not correlate with enduring happiness. Adaptation and Expectations When individuals anticipate acquiring a desired financial goal, their brain naturally prepares itself for increased happiness upon obtaining it. This shrinks the magnitude of uplift expected once the goal is reached, rendering adaptation inevitable. Expectations create a sort of ceiling, defying the potential happiness that should realistically result from achieving a desired goal. Research studies support this idea: in longevity experiments involving significant financial gains, initial spikes in well-being were observed, but with time, baseline happiness returned to initial levels. Cognitive Dissonance and Growth Mindset To prevent continued dissatisfaction and avid pursuit in going after overly ambitions financial goals, awareness about our brain's limitations becomes essential. Cognitive dissonance, a psychological phenomenon where we revise our behaviors to mitigate discomfort from opposing goals and beliefs, educates individuals about maintaining moderate and genuine aspirations. Growth mindset deficiency fuels the relentless expectations, provoking people to constantly strive for an even greater improvement, particularly concerning financial milestones, causing discontent with what has been accomplished. Why Does the Brain Favor Hedonic Adaptation? Evolutionary roots remain behind this tendency: maintaining optimism allows us to chase improvements in both financial and emotional lives undergoing extraordinary experiences. Strategies for Mitigating the Hedonic Treadmill Given the limitations of the hedonic adaptation and its detrimental effects on lasting financial satisfaction, it is essential to adopt conscious strategies to minimize its influence:

Set realistic expectations: Align your goals with what is realistically achievable, considering your circumstances and capabilities. Cultivate gratitude: Reflection on the good things you have in your life can help maintain a balanced perspective. Focus on personal growth: Emphasize self-improvement and development over accumulation, which often yields lasting satisfaction. Practice mindfulness: Regularly assess and reassess your goals to ensure they align with your values and priorities. Find intrinsic motivation: Pursue activities that bring joy and fulfillment without relying solely on external markers of success.

Understanding the concept of hedonic adaptation and its effects on financial satisfaction highlights the need for a more mindful approach to goal setting and pursuit. By adopting strategies to mitigate the hedonic treadmill, you can cultivate lasting fulfillment and overcome the limitations that threaten to undermine your financial satisfaction.
