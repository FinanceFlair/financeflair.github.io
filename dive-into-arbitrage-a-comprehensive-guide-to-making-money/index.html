Simply put, arbitrage refers to the strategy of exploiting price disparities in the same or related products across different platforms or markets. Intrigued? Let’s dive into this fascinating field.

To start, we must categorize two essential types of arbitrage: statistical and transaction.

Statistical Arbitrage

Statistical Arbitrage (SA) is a model built on statistically significant price discrepancies that tend to equalize with strong mathematical precision. An excellent analogy is to consider SA as a trading strategy, primarily used by hedge funds, that is fully maintained by hidden biases. Although these discrepancies can sometimes persist for prolonged durations, dedicated traders armed with quantitative capabilities decisively exploit these windows of opportunity. Notable examples of SA are mean reversion, pairs trading, and convergence trading.

Transaction Arbitrage

On the other hand, Transaction or Traditional Arbitrage seeks to benefit from the fundamental inefficiencies in market liquidity by delivering a product at a price lower than the acquiring cost. Importantly, this type of arbitrage isn’t as mathematically perfect as SA. Discrepancies are rare, and traders primarily deploy intuitive strategies. Examples of transaction arbitrage are border trade, price differentials between exchanges, and grey market trading.

Investing in Stocks through Arbitrage

Investors can generate significant profits during turbulent periods caused by sudden market shocks. E.g., assuming a publicly-traded company is involved in a merger. If there are two separate markets with varying liquidity levels, the stock price might differ. Trading the stock simultaneously on each will result in profits.

How to be Industrious in Arbitrage

In Times of Pure Volatility: Think about options trading, trading in arenas with volatile stock prices, and making use of stronger price discovery platforms.

During Comparative Stabilities: Adopt a market-maker mentality, exploiting discrepancies in cryptos and forex at a small scale.

Finally, Lean on Sound Experience: Rely on your intuition when leveraging traditional trading methods in transaction arbitrage.
