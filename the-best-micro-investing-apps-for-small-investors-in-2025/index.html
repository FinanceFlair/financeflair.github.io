The Best Micro-Investing Apps for Small Investors in 2025


Micro-investing has come a long way over the past decade. In the early 2020s, the concept of turning pocket change into stock market investments still had something of a novelty factor. Fast forward to 2025 and micro-investing has transformed from a fintech experiment into a staple financial strategy for millions of people. More platforms are making it easier for anyone, regardless of income, to step into the world of investing with confidence.

If you are just getting started with investing or looking to put small amounts of money to work, finding the right micro-investing app can make all the difference. The landscape is filled with options—some tailored for beginners, others focused on passive automation, social investing, or even crypto micro-investing. Here’s a deep dive into the top micro-investing platforms of 2025, a comparison of their pros and cons, and a guide to determining which of these apps is best suited to your goals.

What Makes Micro-Investing Apps Essential in 2025?

Micro-investing apps address several hurdles that small and first-time investors have traditionally faced. High minimum deposit requirements, trading fees, intimidating interfaces, and the risk of putting in more money than they could afford to lose were all once barriers to entry. In recent years, the best platforms have responded aggressively to these problems by:

  • Reducing or eliminating minimum investments: Start with $1, or less.
  • Offering commission-free trading: No extra charges just to buy fractional shares.
  • Utilizing automation: Round-up features, autopilot portfolios, and automatic rebalancing.
  • Building education and community: Easy-to-understand investing content and peer forums.

Instead of saving up thousands just to get a seat at the table, micro-investors can now jump in with spare change and still get access to diversified portfolios, index funds, ETFs, and even alternative assets.

Top Micro-Investing Apps for Small Investors in 2025

Here’s a breakdown of the top micro-investing apps, each bringing a unique twist to making small investment amounts work smarter and harder.

1. Acorns: The Pioneer That Still Delivers

Acorns has held its spot as a leader in micro-investing for several years, consistently evolving its offerings. In 2025, Acorns is still the go-to app for automated investing. The signature round-up feature—link your debit or credit cards, and every purchase is rounded up, with the change invested—is a simple but powerful tool to grow investments without effort.

User experience: Acorns’ clean dashboard and mobile-first design make investing approachable. Educational resources are more dynamic than ever, with in-app guides, simulations, and community forums.

Investment choices: Acorns offers a set of well-designed ETF portfolios based on your risk tolerance. There’s also now enhanced access to SRI (socially responsible investing) and ESG funds—making it easy for those who want to align their dollars with their values.

Bonus features: Acorns has expanded its financial wellness suite, with retirement IRAs, checking accounts, and family investing. The fee structure has remained transparent and low, and all-in pricing includes unlimited investing and account flexibility.

Drawbacks: Investors looking for individual stock or crypto investing will find Acorns limiting. The automated approach won’t be ideal for those who want more control.

2. Stash: Flexibility for Self-Directed Investors

While Acorns is about set-it-and-forget-it, Stash gives users a bit more control. Stash continues to offer fractional shares in thousands of stocks and ETFs, meaning micro-investors can invest in companies and sectors that inspire them, no matter their balance.

User experience: Stash has further improved its onboarding process in 2025, allowing users to zero in on interests, values, and personal goals to curate investment recommendations. Customization is neatly balanced with accessibility, so beginners won’t feel lost.

Investment options: Beyond basic ETFs, Stash users can invest in individual stocks and curated themes, from renewable energy to global tech developments and even cutting-edge AI companies. There are also beginner-friendly crypto portfolios—though with clear disclosures and robust risk management tools.

Bonus features: Banking, budgeting, and stock-back debit cards are part of the ecosystem, making money management seamless. Stash has no commissions on trades, and provides coaching, market news, and educational mini-courses delivered right inside the app.

Drawbacks: Monthly subscription fees may not be worthwhile for the smallest balances. Power users might outgrow the analytical depth sooner than on larger brokerages.

3. Public: Investing Meets Social Collaboration

The rise of social investing has been swift, and Public’s hybrid approach of traditional and social finance makes it stand out. Public allows users to invest in fractional shares of stocks, ETFs, and select cryptocurrencies, but overlays a unique social feed where investors can discuss strategies, market moves, and portfolio choices.

User experience: The social feed feature gives first-timers a way to learn by example and interact with experienced investors. Public is designed to strip away jargon, emphasizing transparency and a learning-by-doing approach.

Investment options: The platform allows fractional stock and ETF investing, as well as curated cryptocurrency options for micro-investors. The app is free to use—relying on optional “tips” instead of commissions or membership fees.

Bonus features: Public’s “Town Hall” sessions connect users with business leaders, market experts, and other investors through live Q&A and panel discussions, bringing new voices and perspectives to the investing journey.

Drawbacks: Public’s social features may be more noise than value for those uninterested in communal investing or discussion. Crypto support remains limited, and some advanced tools are still lacking.

4. Robinhood: The OG Low-Cost Platform Gets Micro

Robinhood was a catalyst for the zero-commission broker era. Once best known for free equity trading, Robinhood has broadened its appeal with micro-investing tools in 2025. The platform’s “fractional investing” opens up blue-chip stocks and major ETFs to anyone with as little as $1.

User experience: Robinhood streamlines account creation, transfers, and order placements. For micro-investors, the interface is familiar yet increasingly focused on guidance and risk checks, showing a clear evolution from earlier years.

Investment options: Apart from stocks and ETFs, Robinhood’s crypto platform is robust—offering a broad range of digital assets for small-dollar investing. Retirement planning, IRAs, and high-yield savings accounts are integrated, making Robinhood a one-stop shop.

Bonus features: Real-time market data, price alerts, and a growing library of educational videos are available free to all. The controversial “payment for order flow” business model is now fully disclosed, and users can opt-out for a nominal premium.

Drawbacks: Robinhood’s sometimes gamified interface and history of technical issues have led some to seek alternatives. Customer support can be hit-or-miss, especially at peak times.

5. SoFi Invest: Beyond Micro-Investing

SoFi has grown from a fintech disruptor to a full-service financial hub. Its investment arm, SoFi Invest, excels at making investing accessible, blending micro-investing options with robust goal-oriented planning.

User experience: The onboarding wizard leads users through goal setting, risk assessment, and investment selection. Micro-investing is made easy with the ability to buy fractional shares of major companies, and “Auto-Invest” portfolios offer a hands-free approach.

Investment options: Beyond U.S. equities and ETFs, SoFi Invest is branching into international stocks and alternative assets, all accessible with minimal upfront cash. Their themed portfolios, such as “Future Tech” or “Sustainable Impact,” allow micro-investors to target specific interests.

Bonus features: SoFi integrates loans, banking, and credit monitoring into its platform for a comprehensive financial dashboard. Members enjoy access to expert financial planners at no cost.

Drawbacks: Some advanced investors may find SoFi’s tools and depth limiting. Newer features may lag behind competitors in user experience.

6. M1 Finance: A Hybrid Approach for Goal-Setters

M1 Finance earns a spot for those who want a layer of automation with a bit more room for customization. The “pie” investing model lets users build portfolios from slices (stocks or ETFs), allocating exact percentages for auto-investing—even with a few dollars.

User experience: M1’s interface is crisp and modern, making the process of building, reviewing, and updating investment “pies” fun and intuitive. Deposits are automatically invested according to your pie’s allocations.

Investment options: Choose from individual stocks, ETFs, or expert-built portfolios. More recent upgrades include access to crypto slices, real estate investment trusts (REITs), and specialty sector pies—all available with low or zero minimums thanks to fractional shares.

Bonus features: Automated rebalancing keeps portfolios aligned with original targets over time. There’s also integrated borrowing, margin investing, and robust reporting tools.

Drawbacks: No real-time trading; trades go in on scheduled windows. Some beginners may find the pie-building process a step too involved compared to more “fire-and-forget” apps.

7. Greenlight: Micro-Investing for Families and Kids

Greenlight started as a fintech app aimed at parents who wanted to teach their kids about money. In 2025, Greenlight has doubled down on family financial education—adding micro-investing for teens and pre-teens looking to get started early.

User experience: Parents control accounts and approve investments. Young investors get access to curated educational materials, quizzes, and safe, SEC-regulated investing choices.

Investment options: Fractional shares of U.S. companies and index funds, with strong risk disclosures and a cap on high-risk assets.

Bonus features: Family budgeting, allowance management, chore tracking, and parental controls make Greenlight stand out for households looking to build generational wealth habits.

Drawbacks: Investing is limited compared to full-service platforms. Subscription fees may be high for families who only use a small part of the app’s suite.

How to Choose the Right Micro-Investing App for You

The best micro-investing app is the one that fits your lifestyle, personality, and long-term financial goals. Each platform excels in a different way:

  • Want everything automated, simple, and hands-off? Acorns or SoFi Invest are your best bets.
  • Interested in learning through social interaction and peer engagement? Public will feel like home.
  • Prefer to pick your own stocks or ETFs, or want to dip a toe into crypto? Stash or Robinhood offer a flexible gateway.
  • Have kids itching to invest? Greenlight brings micro-investing securely to the next generation.
  • Seeking hybrid control with advanced portfolio tools? M1 Finance’s “pie” model strikes a great balance.

It’s smart to consider not just the cost structure, but also how each app fits into your overall money habits. Do you want banking and investing all in one app? Are you tempted by high-yield saving features or family accounts? Does automated rebalancing appeal to you, or do you want to be in the driver’s seat?

Start small, see which interface and community vibe feels best, and then consider growing with the platform as your knowledge and investment amounts increase.

What’s Next for Micro-Investing?

Micro-investing is no longer just spare change and side bets. In 2025, these platforms give users of any age or balance a real shot at wealth building. The barriers are lower than ever, and the technology is only getting smarter—machine learning-driven risk profiles, real-time advice, and even AI-powered savings triggers are quickly becoming standard.

The future will likely bring even more collaborative features, new asset classes, and ways to link micro-investing to everyday financial life. One thing’s certain: for anyone just starting out, or looking to make the most of every dollar, there’s never been a better time—or a broader range of apps—to get invested.

Final Thoughts

Micro-investing apps in 2025 aren’t just for beginners or those with limited funds. They provide a versatile, user-friendly gateway to long-term investing habits—a crucial first step for financial security and independence. Whether you want complete automation, the chance to learn and experiment, or a place to teach your kids how to invest, there’s a platform ready to support your goals.

Check out a few by signing up, testing their interfaces, poking around the educational tools, and making that first micro-investment. No pressure, no need to wait. The world of investing is open—one dollar, or one cent, at a time.
