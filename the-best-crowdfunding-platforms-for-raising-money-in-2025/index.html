The Best Crowdfunding Platforms for Raising Money in 2025


Crowdfunding has firmly established itself as one of the most versatile and effective ways for individuals, startups, nonprofits, and creative minds to raise capital in 2025. With so many platforms vying for attention, the choices can feel overwhelming for both new and veteran fundraisers. That’s why making an informed decision about which platform to use is a critical first step toward a campaign’s success.

This in-depth guide breaks down the leading crowdfunding sites for 2025, focusing on their features, specialties, fees, user experiences, and the types of projects they support best. Whether you’re launching a new product, seeking support for a medical emergency, funding community initiatives, or unleashing your next artistic masterpiece, there’s a platform tailored to your goals.

Types of Crowdfunding Platforms

Before diving into individual platforms, it’s helpful to distinguish between the primary models:

  • Reward-Based Crowdfunding: Backers receive a reward (often the finished product or a related token of appreciation) in exchange for their support. This model dominates creative and product-based campaigns.
  • Equity Crowdfunding: Supporters actually invest in your company in exchange for equity or shares, ideal for startups and growing businesses.
  • Donation-Based Crowdfunding: Most popular for medical funds, funerals, charities, and community causes; donors receive no material reward.
  • Peer-to-Peer Lending: Borrowers receive money as a loan, to be repaid with interest.

The lines between these categories are blurring, but understanding your project’s needs and goals ensures you choose the optimal platform.

1. Kickstarter: Unmatched for Creative Projects

Kickstarter continues to lead for creative, innovative, and design-driven campaigns. With unmatched reach and credibility, it’s the first destination for filmmakers, game designers, inventors, musicians, and entrepreneurs looking to pre-sell products or build communities.

Key Features:

  • All-or-nothing model: Projects must reach their funding goal to receive funds, fostering urgency and trust.
  • Extensive built-in audience passionate about backing creativity.
  • Tools for communicating with backers and managing reward tiers.
  • Detailed analytics to monitor campaign progress.

Fees: 5% platform fee + payment processing (around 3% + $0.20 per pledge). If the goal isn’t met, no fees are charged and no money changes hands.

Best For: Creative projects, physical products, art, games, and tech innovation. Not suitable for charities or equity campaigns.

2025 Update: New tools for live project previews, enhanced social sharing, and AI-assisted campaign optimization further cement Kickstarter’s leadership.

2. Indiegogo: Flexibility for Every Project

Indiegogo remains the go-to for creators who need flexibility. With both all-or-nothing and flexible funding options, a broad mix of campaign categories, and a thriving community of early tech adopters, Indiegogo meets a wide spectrum of fundraising needs.

Key Features:

  • Choose between fixed goal (all-or-nothing) and flexible models (keep funds even if goal isn’t met).
  • Indiegogo InDemand allows continued fundraising after campaign success.
  • Supports charitable, gadget, and creative campaigns.
  • Integrated shipping and fulfillment partnerships.

Fees: 5% platform fee; payment processing around 3% + standard transaction fee. Nonprofits eligible for reduced fees.

Best For: Tech startups, consumer gadgets, design, film, and social change projects. Versatile enough for creative and cause-based campaigns.

2025 Update: Integrated fulfillment logistics and a robust post-campaign marketplace allow project creators to transition smoothly from raising funds to delivering and selling products.

3. GoFundMe: Compassion and Community Funding

GoFundMe is rooted in helping individuals and communities with personal causes. From medical emergencies to education costs or unexpected disasters, GoFundMe continues to be the first stop for direct donation fundraising.

Key Features:

  • No platform fee for personal campaigns; donors can tip to support platform costs.
  • Social media sharing and quick, secure setup.
  • Responsive customer support.
  • Withdraw money as funds come in—no waiting for campaign completion.

Fees: No platform fee for the majority of countries; payment processing averages 2.9% + $0.30 per donation.

Best For: Personal emergencies, medical treatments, community projects, memorials, and charities.

2025 Update: Video-first storytelling tools and real-time updates increase campaign transparency, while robust fraud prevention keeps the platform secure.

4. SeedInvest: Equity Crowdfunding for Startups

For entrepreneurs seeking investment rather than donations or pre-orders, SeedInvest is a leader in the equity crowdfunding space. It connects vetted startups with investors of all sizes, simplifying compliance and management.

Key Features:

  • Rigorous vetting process enhances investor confidence.
  • Accepts investments from accredited and non-accredited investors.
  • Direct support for legal compliance and campaign creation.
  • Investor management dashboard and reporting.

Fees: 7.5% placement fee on committed capital + 5% equity or additional fee; due diligence fee applies in some cases.

Best For: Startups ready to exchange equity for growth capital, with a solid business plan and traction.

2025 Update: Newly integrated digital data rooms and seamless e-signature capabilities streamline document handling for both founders and investors.

5. Patreon: Membership Funding for Creative Independence

Patreon empowers creators to monetize their audience via subscriptions and recurring support. Unlike project-based platforms, Patreon is built for ongoing work—making it perfect for YouTubers, podcasters, writers, musicians, and independent educators.

Key Features:

  • Recurring revenue model.
  • Tiered rewards for supporters.
  • Direct connection to superfans, fostering loyal community.
  • In-depth analytics and integration with major creator tools.

Fees: 5%–12% of pledged income, depending on chosen plan, plus payment processing fees.

Best For: Artists, creators, and educators with ongoing output who want sustainable monthly income and a deeper connection to their audience.

2025 Update: Advanced community management tools, storefront features, and video hosting have made Patreon a one-stop shop for independent creators.

6. Fundly: Simplicity for Social Good

Fundly has carved out a niche for personal and nonprofit causes, thanks to its straightforward interface and mobile-friendly design. Quick setup and robust sharing features make Fundly ideal for first-time fundraisers.

Key Features:

  • No minimum goal or deadline.
  • Mobile-optimized for easy updates on the go.
  • Visual storytelling with image and video integration.

Fees: 4.9% platform fee + 2.9% payment processing and $0.30 per transaction.

Best For: Local charities, education funds, animal rescues, and community events.

2025 Update: New charitable project templates, donation matching, and automated thank-you emails simplify donor engagement.

7. Fundrise: Real Estate and Alternative Investments

Fundrise specializes in connecting investors with real estate projects, offering both equity and debt positions. It appeals to those looking to diversify portfolios or finance property developments.

Key Features:

  • Wide range of investment opportunities, including eREITs and eFunds.
  • Intuitive dashboards for tracking performance.
  • Lower investment minimums compared to traditional real estate.

Fees: 0.85% annual investment advisory + 0.15% asset management fee on project-specific investments.

Best For: Real estate developers, investors seeking alternative assets, and those looking to fund commercial or residential projects.

2025 Update: Integration of environmental and social impact scoring helps investors align with values-based development.

8. Mightycause: Powering Nonprofit Fundraising

Formerly known as Razoo, Mightycause offers robust support for nonprofit organizations and grassroots campaigns. Its suite of donor management tools and recurring giving features give nonprofits a leg up in ongoing fundraising.

Key Features:

  • Peer-to-peer fundraising, event support, and team campaigns.
  • CRM tools for donor retention.
  • Embeddable donation widgets and customized campaign pages.

Fees: 1.2% platform fee + 2.2% payment processing, with special pricing available for registered nonprofits.

Best For: Nonprofit fundraising, giving days, team events, and recurring charitable donations.

2025 Update: Real-time progress tracking, mobile donation optimization, and text-to-give capabilities enhance donor engagement.

9. StartEngine: Equity Funding for Startups and SMEs

StartEngine is another major player in equity crowdfunding, with a robust platform for startups ready to exchange shares for working capital. Its marketing support and investor network are particularly strong.

Key Features:

  • Accepts both accredited and non-accredited investors.
  • Secondary market for share trading post-funding.
  • Built-in compliance and legal support.
  • Marketing and PR support for campaigns.

Fees: 7%–12% of total capital raised (varies by offering type); $10K–$20K in out-of-pocket legal and marketing expenses depending on scope.

Best For: Startups and small businesses seeking substantial investment, those open to public involvement in early growth stages.

2025 Update: Rollout of fractional investment options and enhanced post-funding resale tools increase liquidity and appeal to diverse backers.

10. Crowdfunder: Social Good Meets Lean Fundraising

Crowdfunder focuses on cause-oriented campaigns, social businesses, and organizations prioritizing community impact. Its social sharing emphasis and transparent fee structure attract mission-driven entrepreneurs.

Key Features:

  • Support for charities, social enterprises, and community projects.
  • Built-in viral marketing tools.
  • Emphasis on transparent progress tracking and storytelling.

Fees: Varies based on project type; typically under 5% with direct-to-cause payments.

Best For: Social enterprises, community groups, arts and culture initiatives, small-scale nonprofits.

2025 Update: AI-assisted story personalization and automated social media promotion drive higher engagement with less manual effort.

What to Consider

  • Type of campaign: Is your project creative, charitable, or investment-driven?
  • Target audience: Where does your ideal backer already spend time?
  • Funding goal: Larger goals merit platforms with more visibility.
  • Timeframe and urgency: Some platforms let you access funds as you go; others require the goal to be met.
  • Fees and payout structure: Weigh platform and payment fees alongside potential reach.

Maximizing Campaign Success on Any Platform

Raising money online is about more than just picking the right site. Here are proven tips to elevate your crowdfunding campaign in 2025:

  • Craft a compelling story: Emotion and authenticity win support. Use video, photos, and clear, consistent messaging.
  • Set realistic goals: Overambitious targets can discourage support. Start smaller, build momentum, and scale if necessary.
  • Mobilize your own network: Early momentum from friends and supporters attracts strangers. Don’t wait for “viral” magic.
  • Offer creative rewards: For reward-based platforms, unique or limited rewards drive engagement.
  • Update backers regularly: Sharing progress, hurdles, and victories keeps donors engaged and instills trust.
  • Promote everywhere: Social platforms, email newsletters, blogs—cast a wide net and don’t shy away from direct asks.
  • Follow through: Deliver on your promises to build long-term credibility and pave the way for future campaigns.

The Future of Crowdfunding: Trends to Watch

Looking ahead, several trends are shaping the crowdfunding landscape in 2025:

  • Decentralized ownership: Blockchain-based platforms are allowing backers to own unique digital assets or participate in DAOs.
  • Integrated fulfillment: Platforms now offer cradle-to-door solutions for campaigners with physical products.
  • Personalized storytelling: AI-powered content tools help tailor messaging for maximum impact.
  • Micro-funding and real-time engagement: Live streaming and one-click donations make raising money more interactive than ever.
  • Cross-border giving: Improved international payment processing makes global campaigns smoother and more inclusive.

Conclusion

The best crowdfunding platform for your project in 2025 hinges on your campaign’s nature, your supporters, and your strategic goals. Kickstarter and Indiegogo are unrivaled for creativity and innovation, while GoFundMe and Fundly excel in compassion-driven causes. Equity crowdfunding giants like SeedInvest and StartEngine empower ambitious startups with investor capital, and platforms like Patreon and Mightycause fuel creative and nonprofit sustainability.

Regardless of your chosen platform, success is built on your ability to connect with supporters—sharing a vision, telling a story, and following through on your promises. With dedication, the right tools, and a touch of inspiration, your campaign can become the next crowdfunding success story in 2025.

Choose wisely—and don’t forget to harness the power of your community every step of the way.
