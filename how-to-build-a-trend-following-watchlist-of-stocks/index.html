How to Build a Trend Following Watchlist of Stocks


In the world of stock trading, trend following is one of the most popular and effective strategies. It involves identifying stocks that are moving decisively in one direction—upward or downward—and riding that wave to maximize gains. But before you can jump on these trends, you need a carefully curated watchlist. This blog post will guide you step-by-step on how to build a trend following watchlist of stocks that can help you capitalize on market momentum.

Understanding Trend Following in Stock Trading

Trend following is based on a simple principle: stocks that have shown a strong trend in price movement are likely to continue in that direction, at least in the short to medium term. This strategy doesn’t predict reversals or try to catch the exact bottom or top; instead, it rides the trend as long as it’s intact.

Successful trend followers develop a watchlist of stocks that exhibit strong price momentum because monitoring a select group enables faster, more focused decision-making. A good watchlist filters out noise and highlights genuine trend candidates.

Why Building a Watchlist Is Crucial for Trend Following

Trying to track the entire stock market for potential trades can be overwhelming and inefficient. A well-constructed watchlist does the hard work of narrowing down thousands of stocks to a manageable number that meet specific trend criteria. This approach increases your chances of identifying genuine market momentum and reduces the chance of false signals.

Step 1: Define the Criteria for Trend Stocks

Before you start compiling a list, you need to establish clear criteria for what counts as a “trend stock.” These criteria will act as a filter and alignment tool for your watchlist.

a. Price Movement

Look for stocks exhibiting consistent upward or downward price movement over a certain period. Common time frames include:

  • Short-term: 5 to 20 days (intraday or daily trends)
  • Medium-term: 20 to 50 days
  • Long-term: 50 to 200 days

b. Volume Confirmation

Volume plays an essential role in confirming trends. A trend without strong volume is more likely to fail. Look for stocks where volume supports the price movement, indicating genuine investor interest.

c. Volatility and Liquidity

Trend following works best with stocks that have sufficient price volatility and liquidity. Volatility provides movement to ride, and liquidity ensures that entering or exiting positions doesn’t cause price distortion.

d. Technical Indicators

Many traders incorporate technical indicators like Moving Averages (especially the 50-day and 200-day), Relative Strength Index (RSI), and Average True Range (ATR) to validate trends and momentum.

Step 2: Use Stock Screeners to Filter Candidates

Stock screeners are indispensable tools when building a trend following watchlist. They allow you to automate the filtering process based on your criteria. Here’s how to use them effectively:

a. Filtering for Price Trends

Use moving average criteria such as:

  • Price above the 50-day moving average: Indicates recent upward momentum.
  • Price crossing above the 200-day moving average: Often seen as a sign of a long-term upward trend.

Alternatively, you can filter stocks whose recent price has increased by a certain percentage over a chosen period (e.g., last 10 or 20 days).

b. Filtering for Volume

Set minimum average daily volume requirements (e.g., at least 500,000 shares traded daily) to ensure liquidity. Look for an increase in volume alongside price movement as a confirmation.

c. Relative Strength Index (RSI)

Screen for stocks with RSI values typically between 50 and 70 during an uptrend (indicating healthy momentum without being overbought). Avoid RSI above 70, which may suggest overheating.

Step 3: Categorize Stocks by Sectors and Industries

A diversified watchlist benefits from sector variety. Trends often start or accelerate in specific sectors due to economic cycles, news, or innovations.

Create subcategories within your watchlist, for example:

  • Technology
  • Healthcare
  • Energy
  • Consumer Discretionary

Monitoring multiple sectors allows you to spot rotation of leadership in the market, increasing your opportunities.

Step 4: Monitor Sector Rotation and Market Conditions

Trend following doesn’t happen in isolation. Broader market sentiment and sector shifts can affect individual stock trends. For instance, in a bull market, tech stocks may lead, but during rotation phases, utilities or industrials might show stronger trends.

Keep tabs on:

  • Economic indicators
  • Sector ETFs to see which industries are trending
  • News that may trigger sector-specific rallies or sell-offs

Adjusting your watchlist by emphasizing sectors showing strength can improve your success rates.

Step 5: Incorporate Chart Analysis for Final Selection

Once the screener outputs potential stocks, bring your watchlist into chart analysis tools like TradingView, ThinkorSwim, or your brokerage’s platform.

Look for:

  • Clear trend lines (higher highs and higher lows in uptrends, or lower highs and lower lows in downtrends)
  • Breakouts from significant resistance levels
  • Moving average crossovers (e.g., 50-day crossing above 200-day)
  • Volume spikes confirming moves

This visual confirmation helps weed out false positives and fine-tunes your watchlist.

Step 6: Set Alerts and Regularly Review the Watchlist

Trends can develop quickly and fade just as fast. Setting alerts on price or indicator thresholds helps you catch emerging opportunities without needing to watch the market constantly.

Example alerts:

  • Price crossing above 20-day high
  • Volume spikes above average
  • Moving average crossovers

Schedule routine reviews of your watchlist—daily or weekly—to add new candidates and remove stocks whose trends have ended.

Step 7: Risk Management and Position Sizing

While this is more about execution than building the watchlist, always align your list selection with your risk management rules.

  • Define stop loss levels based on support/resistance or ATR.
  • Limit position sizes to avoid overexposure to any single stock.
  • Track overall portfolio exposure across sectors and stock types.

Sample Framework to Build Your Trend Following Watchlist

Here’s a practical checklist summary:

  1. Start with a stock screener: Apply filters for price above 50-day MA, volume > 500k shares, 10-20 day price changes > 5%.
  2. Filter by sectors: Select a mix of sectors showing strength.
  3. Technical indicator screening: RSI between 50-70; MACD trending upward.
  4. Chart confirmation: Confirm trend via higher highs/lows and volume.
  5. Set alerts: For breakout or pullback opportunities.
  6. Monitor & refresh: Weekly review to add/remove stocks.
  7. Integrate risk rules: Position size and stop loss for each stock.

Tools and Resources to Assist Watchlist Building

  • Finviz: Excellent free screener with customizable filters.
  • TradingView: Powerful charting and alert system.
  • Yahoo Finance: Basic screening plus news and analysis.
  • Broker Platforms: Many offer built-in screeners and alert systems.
  • Sector ETFs: Track sector momentum for rotation insights.

Final Thoughts

Building a trend following watchlist isn’t just about collecting popular stocks—it requires a disciplined approach, clear criteria, timely reviews, and a blend of technical analysis and market awareness. With patience and practice, this watchlist becomes your first line of defense against market noise and the pathway to catching genuine momentum trades.

Remember, no watchlist is foolproof, and trends can reverse unexpectedly. Continuously educating yourself about market behavior, staying updated on economic developments, and refining your criteria will make your trend following watchlist a powerful tool in your trading arsenal.

Trend following is both an art and a science. The watchlist you create today could be the foundation for tomorrow’s winning trades. Start building yours with intent, insight, and adaptability. Happy trading!
