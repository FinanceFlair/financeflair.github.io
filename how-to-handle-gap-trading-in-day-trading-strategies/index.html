How to Handle Gap Trading in Day Trading Strategies


Gap trading is a popular strategy employed by day traders to take advantage of price gaps that occur between the closing and opening prices of a security. These gaps can occur due to various factors such as news releases, earnings reports, or market sentiment. In this blog post, we will explore how to effectively handle gap trading in day trading strategies. Before delving into the strategies, it is essential to have a clear understanding of what gap trading entails. A gap is defined as a significant price difference between the last closing price and the opening price of a security. These gaps can be categorized into three types:

  1. Common Gaps: These gaps occur regularly and do not signify any significant change in the market sentiment. They are typically filled within a short period.

  2. Breakaway Gaps: Breakaway gaps occur when a security breaks out of a well-established trading range. These gaps usually suggest a shift in market sentiment and can lead to powerful trends.

  3. Exhaustion Gaps: Exhaustion gaps occur near the end of a trend and indicate that the prevailing trend may be coming to an end. These gaps are often followed by a reversal in price direction.

Gap Trading Strategies

Now that we have a basic understanding of gap trading, let’s explore some strategies to effectively handle gap trading in day trading:

1. Fade the Gap Strategy

The fade the gap strategy involves taking a contrarian approach by trading against the direction of the gap. This strategy assumes that the gap will eventually be filled, and the price will revert to its previous levels. Traders employing this strategy will short a security that has gapped up or buy a security that has gapped down.

2. Breakout Strategy

The breakout strategy involves trading in the direction of the gap. Traders using this strategy anticipate that the gap will lead to a significant price movement and aim to capture the momentum. When a security gaps up, traders will buy the security, and when a security gaps down, they will short the security.

3. Gap and Go Strategy

The gap and go strategy is suitable for traders who prefer quick and aggressive moves. Traders using this strategy look for stocks that have gapped up significantly on the open and continue to show strength. They will enter a trade as soon as the market opens and ride the momentum.

4. Gap Reversal Strategy

The gap reversal strategy aims to capture the reversal in price direction after a significant gap. Traders using this strategy wait for the price to fill the gap partially or completely before entering a trade in the opposite direction.

Risk Management in Gap Trading

While gap trading can be profitable, it is essential to implement proper risk management techniques to protect your capital. Some key risk management practices include:

  1. Setting Stop Loss Orders: Always set stop-loss orders to limit potential losses if the trade goes against you.

  2. Position Sizing: Determine the appropriate position size based on your risk tolerance. Avoid risking too much capital on a single trade.

  3. Monitoring News and Market Sentiment: Stay updated with the latest news and market sentiment that could impact the security you are trading. This will help you make informed decisions and avoid unexpected price movements.

  4. Using Technical Analysis: Incorporate technical analysis tools and indicators to identify potential entry and exit points. This can help you make more accurate trading decisions.

In conclusion, gap trading can be a powerful strategy for day traders if employed effectively. By understanding the different types of gaps and implementing suitable strategies while considering risk management techniques, traders can increase their chances of success. Remember to practice these strategies in a simulated trading environment before risking real capital. Happy trading!
