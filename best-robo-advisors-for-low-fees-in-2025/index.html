Best Robo Advisors for Low Fees in 2025


The world of investing has undergone a quiet revolution. More investors are skipping expensive financial advisors and going straight to robo advisors, seeking smarter technology, easy-to-understand portfolios, and, above all, low fees. With inflation still gnawing at savings and the markets ever unpredictable, minimizing costs has never been more vital. Below, you’ll find a comprehensive guide to the best robo advisors for low fees in 2025, taking into account current pricing, account flexibility, and user experience.

Why Fee Structure Matters for Robo Advisors

Robo advisors have democratized investing, but not all fee models are created equal. Even a small difference in annual management fees—say, between 0.15% and 0.25%—can add up to thousands of dollars on a six-figure portfolio over the years, thanks to compounding. That’s why investors hunting for robo advisors in 2025 should dig into every layer of fees: advisory fees, fund expenses, trading costs, and any additional charges.

How We Evaluated the Best Robo Advisors for Low Fees

This breakdown is the result of deep research into the actual costs investors pay—including hidden ones—and factors like minimum investment, features, and whether the robo advisor automatically rebalances portfolios or offers tax-loss harvesting. Only robo advisors with proven track records, sound customer service, and robust digital platforms made the cut.

1. SoFi Automated Investing

Best for Zero-Fees and Extra Perks

SoFi Automated Investing continues to lead when it comes to keeping investor costs as close to zero as possible. In 2025, the service remains completely free—no advisory fees, no commissions. The only cost you pay is the underlying fund expenses (typically between 0.05%–0.15%, depending on your portfolio), which are par for the course with ETFs.

What Sets SoFi Apart

  • Zero Management Fees: SoFi’s platform is a rarity. Not only does it charge no management fee, but it also has no account minimum.
  • Free Access to Financial Planners: Investors can chat with SoFi’s human advisors free of charge—a standout feature.
  • Automatic Rebalancing and Diversification: Portfolios are rebalanced as the market fluctuates, and new investors set their risk tolerance through a slick onboarding process.

Limitations

  • Fund Fees Still Apply: You can’t completely escape ETF expense ratios, but they’re minimal.
  • Less Sophisticated Tax Strategies: No tax-loss harvesting, so higher-net-worth investors might prefer a more advanced service.

2. Fidelity Go

Best for Transparent, Tiered Pricing

Fidelity Go continues to offer one of the most transparent and budget-friendly robo platforms. As of 2025, their pricing remains tiered:

  • No Advisory Fee for Balances below $25,000: Truly costless for beginners.
  • 0.35% / year for Balances above $25,000: All-in, with no additional ETF expense for their in-house mutual funds.

What’s remarkable is that Fidelity Go’s portfolios use proprietary funds with no expense ratios, making the “real” cost incredibly straightforward.

Standout Features

  • No ETF Expense: For anyone with a portfolio above the $25K threshold, you get a flat-fee model.
  • Intuitive Interface: Built for beginners, but flexible enough for more advanced users.
  • Integration with Fidelity Ecosystem: Easy to manage across IRAs, taxable, and 401(k) rollovers.

Limitations

  • No Tax-Loss Harvesting: A bit of a bummer for investors with sizable taxable accounts.
  • No Customization: You’re limited to their set model portfolios.

3. Vanguard Digital Advisor

Best for Die-Hard Index Fund Fans

Vanguard continues to live up to its reputation for ultra-low costs. In 2025, Digital Advisor charges a 0.20% management fee, with no minimum for IRAs and a manageable $3,000 minimum for taxable accounts. The service invests exclusively in Vanguard ETFs—famous for their low expense ratios, averaging 0.05%–0.09%.

Strengths

  • Clear Pricing: Total cost hovers around 0.25% when combining the advisory fee and ETF costs—making it one of the lowest offers from a legacy institution.
  • Automatic Rebalancing: Keeps your investments aligned without manual intervention.
  • Retirement Planning Tools: A dream for long-term, hands-off investors.

Weaknesses

  • No Direct Tax-Loss Harvesting for Digital Clients: You’ll need Vanguard Personal Advisor for that, which comes at a higher fee.
  • Less Robust for Non-Vanguard Accounts: Not ideal if you want to use outside ETFs or add non-traditional assets.

4. Wealthfront

Best for Tech-Lovers and Tax Optimization

Despite fierce competition, Wealthfront persists as a leading digital-first robo advisor for sophisticated investors who want low fees and smart tax tactics. Their price remains at 0.25% (advisory fee), with ETF expense ratios averaging about 0.08%.

Why Wealthfront Stands Out

  • Tax-Loss Harvesting: Available for all taxable accounts, which can offset that 0.25% fee for many investors.
  • Highly Customizable Portfolios: Add or remove ETFs, include crypto, and access alternative strategies like risk parity.
  • Cash Management: Integrated high-yield cash accounts and debt payoff tools.

Cons

  • $500 Minimum: Still accessible, but not the lowest on the market.
  • No Free Human Advisors: All-digital, which might not suit every investor.

5. Schwab Intelligent Portfolios

Best for $0 Advisory Fee with Robust Automation

Charles Schwab’s Intelligent Portfolios continues its streak: $0 in advisory fees, with the user paying only ETF expense ratios of about 0.06%–0.15%. While Schwab is known for inserting more cash into portfolios, it’s a fair tradeoff considering the absence of management fees.

Pros

  • No Annual Fee: Just the ETF costs and whatever interest Schwab earns on required cash holdings.
  • Automatic Rebalancing and Tax-Loss Harvesting: For portfolios over $50,000, Schwab offers tax-loss harvesting, a feature often limited to high-fee competitors.
  • Strong Selection of ETFs: Covers all the core asset classes.

Cons

  • High Cash Allocation: Portfolios typically keep 6%–10% in cash, potentially lowering returns vs. fully-invested portfolios.
  • Less Customization: Portfolio options are solid but fixed, with no individual ETF selection.

6. M1 Finance

Best for DIY Investors Who Want Automation

M1 Finance blurs the line between a robo advisor and a self-directed platform. It charges no management fees, and, using “Pies,” allows you to fully customize your portfolio. You only pay the underlying ETF expense ratios, averaging around 0.06%–0.12%.

What’s Great

  • Zero Advisory Fee: All automation, no ongoing fees.
  • Custom Portfolios: Mix and match ETFs, stocks, and even add thematic “expert pies.”
  • Free Automatic Rebalancing: M1 “Smart Transfers” distribute deposits based on your targets.

What’s Not as Great

  • No Tax-Loss Harvesting: No direct tax minimization tools.
  • Less Access to Human Help: Perfect for independent investors, less so for those needing hand-holding.

7. Acorns

Best for Micro-Investing and Round-Ups

Acorns attracts first-time investors by rounding up spare change from purchases and investing the difference. All accounts use diversified ETF portfolios. The fee structure is technically a fixed cost: $3/month for individual accounts, $5/month for families, but on lower balances, this works out to a much higher percentage.

Pros

  • Low Entry Barrier: Start with pennies; no minimum.
  • Automatic Saving: Passive, hands-off micro investing.

Cons

  • Not Fee-Efficient for Large Balances: At $3/month, the implied percentage becomes steep if your account grows into five figures.
  • Limited Portfolio Options: No tax-loss harvesting or deep customization.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

Even the best robo advisors for low fees can sneak in catch-all charges or quirks:

  • Fund Expenses: All robo platforms will invest in ETFs, which each carry their own small expense ratios.
  • Cash Drag: Some platforms force you to hold more cash, bringing down long-term returns.
  • Transfer or Closure Fees: Moving your money to another institution? Be sure to check for outgoing transfer fees, which can be as high as $75.
  • Customer Service: While not a “fee,” the time lost to poor service is a hidden cost for anyone relying on automated platforms.

Which Low-Fee Robo Advisor Is Best for You?

For Complete Beginners

SoFi Automated Investing and Fidelity Go are hard to beat. Both have zero or very low minimums, are built for investors new to markets, and charge nothing (assuming you’re below Fidelity’s $25,000 threshold).

For Investors Focused on Customization

M1 Finance lets you design exactly the portfolio you want, offering all the algorithmic rebalancing and ease of a robo, but none of the one-size-fits-all limitations.

For Tax Efficiency

Wealthfront and Schwab Intelligent Portfolios (above $50,000) have automated, year-round tax-loss harvesting, which could shave serious dollars off your tax bill—outweighing small differences in advisory fees.

For Index Fund Purists

Vanguard Digital Advisor keeps costs stunningly low and sticks to its tried-and-true core of low-cost index funds. It’s the closest thing to Boglehead autopilot available in digital investing.

What to Prioritize Beyond Fees

A rock-bottom fee is great, but a smart robo advisor should also offer:

  • Solid, Automatic Rebalancing
  • Responsible Risk Assessment
  • Broad Asset Allocation
  • Dependable Customer Service and Tech Support
  • Alignment with Your Financial Goals

Chasing the lowest price can sometimes mean missing out on key features—especially for high-net-worth or tax-sensitive investors.

The Future of Robo Advisor Pricing

Already in 2025, advisory fees are tumbling, and it’s clear that the real value will increasingly come from services layered on top of generic ETF portfolios: automated tax planning, personalized service, and ever smarter portfolio algorithms. Expect more platforms to roll out tiered pricing with free baseline options and “premium” features (tax optimization, retirement planning tools, or hands-on human help) for those willing to pay up.

The Bottom Line

Saving on fees—and watching your nest egg grow instead of leaking away in tiny increments—makes a huge difference. The best robo advisors for low fees in 2025 deliver effortless investing, automated strategy, and peace of mind at almost no cost to you. Whether you’re just starting out or finally ready to ditch high-priced advisors, there’s never been a better, cheaper, or more intelligent time to use a robo advisor.

Ultimately, the smartest move is to look at your needs (automation, access to humans, tax features), match them with a platform’s pricing, and let your money start working for you—with less of it wasted on fees. If you’re switching in 2025, the only bad option is sticking with outdated, high-cost solutions that don’t put your interests first. Take a closer look at the platforms above and give yourself a well-deserved raise—by investing sensibly, with low fees, and with confidence.
