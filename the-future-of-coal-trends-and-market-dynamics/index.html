Conclusion

The future of coal is uncertain, but it is clear that the industry faces significant challenges as the world moves toward cleaner, more sustainable energy sources. While coal will continue to play a role in energy production for the foreseeable future, its dominance is likely to wane as renewable energy becomes more affordable and widespread. Technological innovations such as carbon capture and storage may help extend coal’s viability, but the long-term future of the industry will depend on its ability to adapt to a rapidly changing global energy landscape.

As the world shifts toward a low-carbon future, the coal industry must navigate a complex set of challenges and opportunities. By embracing innovation, investing in cleaner technologies, and responding to regulatory pressures, the coal industry may still have a role to play in the energy transition. However, the era of coal dominance is coming to an end, and the future of energy will likely be defined by cleaner, more sustainable alternatives.
