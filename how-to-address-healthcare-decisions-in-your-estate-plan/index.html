How to Address Healthcare Decisions in Your Estate Plan


Creating an estate plan can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to making difficult healthcare decisions. Individuals and families often struggle with balancing personal autonomy with the potential need for others to make life-or-death decisions on their behalf. In this post, we will explore the importance of addressing healthcare decisions in your estate plan and provide guidance on incorporating these critical elements into your plan. Understanding Medical Power of Attorney A Medical Power of Attorney (MPOA) is a crucial document that empowers another person to make healthcare decisions on your behalf when you are unable to do so. This can be a valuable tool for individuals who want to ensure their wishes are respected, even if they become incapacitated. However, choosing the right person to serve as your agent is a critical decision. Consider the following factors when selecting your MPOA:

Trust: You must trust your chosen agent to make decisions that align with your values and preferences. Availability: Ensure your agent is available to make decisions on your behalf, potentially at short notice. Willingness to act: Verify that your agent is willing to take on the responsibilities and potential burdens associated with making life-or-death decisions.

Designating a Healthcare Proxy When selecting a healthcare proxy, consider the following factors:

First-tier choices: Identify one or two individuals you trust and who are likely to be available to serve as your healthcare proxy. Alternates: Name one or two individuals who can serve as backups if your first-tier choices are unavailable. Communicate with your proxy: Make it clear to your designated proxy how you wish to be treated in various healthcare scenarios.

Advanced Care Planning Advanced Care Planning (ACP) involves creating a comprehensive document outlining your healthcare preferences, values, and goals. An ACP typically includes:

Values and preferences: Share your personal values and preferences for medical treatment, including end-of-life care. Treatment objectives: Iterate upon the medical treatments you do or do not want to receive, including specific medications, surgeries, or hospitalizations. Resource allocation: Determine how to allocate medical resources on your behalf, based on your personal circumstances.

Incorporating HIPAA Rights The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) protects your medical information from unauthorized disclosure. However, if you want friends or family members to be involved in your medical care, you can sign a HIPAA Release form to grant them access to your medical records. Establishing a Healthcare Fiduciary Account A Healthcare Fiduciary Account allows you to specifically designate funds for healthcare expenses, maximizing the allocation of your estate for medical care. Updating and Amending Your Estate Plan Estate plans are not set-in-stone documents; they can be updated or amended to reflect changes in your situation. It is essential to:

Periodically assess your plan: Review your estate plan to ensure it remains relevant to your changing circumstances. Revise and update: Make necessary changes to your estate plan as your healthcare priorities, preferences, or circumstances evolve.
