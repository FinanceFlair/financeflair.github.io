Investing Against the Grain: The Benefits of a Contrarian Mindset


As the great investor and thinker, Warren Buffett, once said, "Price is what you pay. Value is what you get." This intuitive phrase underscores the essence of contrarian investing - a mindset that anticipates the unexpected by beating the crowd and investing in assets that are undervalued. Going against the prevailing sentiment in the market can be both exhilarating and infuriating, but done correctly, it can be the key to long-term financial success. The Hyperventilation of the Crowd There are times when the market sentiment is at its most extreme - manic highs or despairing lows. Under such conditions, it's crucial to separate the opinions of the sea of people from the value of the asset. This may require one to go contrary to the general understanding and push through cognitive distance when the general public has been misled by rumors, misinformation, or herd mentality. A contrarian investor will avoid gimcrack fads or trends characterized by inflated prices due to 'mob enthusiasm.' Selective skepticism: Never accept at face value what appears contradictory to common sense. Evaluate and reason over supposedly sound information.
