How to Score Free Stuff: 12 Frugal Living Hacks


Are you tired of breaking the bank every time you need something new? Do you wish you had more money in your wallet to spend on experiences, travel, and savings? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle to make ends meet, and it can be overwhelming to try and stay afloat on a limited budget. But here's the good news: you don't have to spend a fortune to get what you need. With a little creativity and some smart thinking, you can score free stuff and live a more frugal lifestyle. Here are 12 frugal living hacks to help you get started:

  1. Take Advantage of Free Trials Many companies offer free trials for their products or services, and they're a great way to get what you need without spending a dime. For example, streaming services like Netflix, Hulu, and Spotify all offer free trials, which can be cancelled at any time. Online magazines and software programs often offer free trials as well. Before signing up for a free trial, make sure you plan to use the service or product regularly, otherwise you may end up wasting your money on unwanted subscriptions. Take advantage of free trials and see what you like before committing to a purchase.
  2. Use Store Loyalty Programs Many stores offer loyalty programs that reward you with points or discounts for every purchase you make. Some programs are more generous than others, so it's worth shopping around to see who's offering the best deal. Some examples of store loyalty programs include:

Amazon Prime (get free shipping, streaming, and other perks) Walmart Rewards (earn points for every dollar you spend) CVS ExtraCare (get discounts, free products, and more)

  1. Take Advantage of Cashback Offers Cashback offers give you a percentage of your purchase back as a reward. It may not seem like a lot, but it can add up over time. Some popular cashback programs include:

Ibotta (get cash back on groceries and other purchases) Rakuten (formerly known as Ebates, get cash back on online purchases) TopCashback (get cash back on a wide range of purchases)

  1. Use Free Wi-Fi Free Wi-Fi is a game-changer for anyone on a budget. Instead of buying a data pack or using up your limited allowance, take advantage of free Wi-Fi at hotels, restaurants, and other public spaces. Some tips for using free Wi-Fi safely include:

Make sure the network is secure and password-protected Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities, such as online banking or shopping Consider using a VPN to stay safe

  1. Get Free Stuff in the Mail There are many websites that offer free stuff in the mail, from samples to full-sized products. Some popular options include:

Try The Freebie (get free stuff in the mail) Swagbucks (earn points for taking surveys and redeem for rewards) Toluna (earn points for taking surveys and redeem for rewards)

  1. Attend Free Events Free events are a great way to have fun without breaking the bank. Some examples include:

Outdoor concerts and festivals Community gatherings and potlucks Library events and workshops

  1. Use Government Assistance Programs If you're struggling to make ends meet, you may be eligible for government assistance programs. Some examples include:

SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) Medicaid TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)

  1. Shop During Sales Shopping during sales is a great way to save money on everyday items. Some tips for shopping during sales include:

Plan your shopping trip around sale days and times Use store loyalty programs to stock up on essentials Consider shopping at discount stores or second-hand shops

  1. Get Free Gardening Seeds Gardening is a great way to grow your own food and save money on groceries. Some companies offer free gardening seeds as a promotion, or you can get seeds for free by participating in seed swaps and exchanges.
  2. Take Advantage of Discounted Tickets Discounted tickets are a great way to see a movie, attend a concert, or visit a local attraction without breaking the bank. Some tips for getting discounted tickets include:

Look for promo codes and special offers online Consider buying tickets in advance or for off-peak hours Take advantage of student or senior discounts

  1. Use Free Online Resources Free online resources are a great way to learn new skills and get information without spending a fortune. Some examples include:

Online tutorials and courses on YouTube and Skillshare Free e-books and audiobooks from public libraries Podcasts and online radio stations

  1. Get Free Books Free books are a great way to add some new titles to your collection without breaking the bank. Some tips for getting free books include:

Visit your local library and borrow books for free Participate in free book exchanges and swaps Take advantage of Kindle freebies and other digital promotions

Conclusion: Scoring free stuff is easier than you think, and with these 12 frugal living hacks, you can start living a more frugal lifestyle today. Whether you're looking to save money on groceries, or just want to treat yourself to some free stuff, there are many ways to get what you need without breaking the bank. So next time you're thinking of splurging, consider these hacks and see if you can score some free stuff instead.
