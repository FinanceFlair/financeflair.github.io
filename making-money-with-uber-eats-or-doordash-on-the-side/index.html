Making Money with Uber Eats or DoorDash on the Side


The gig economy has revolutionized the way people think about earning extra money, providing unparalleled flexibility and opportunities to supplement income. Among the top players in this space, Uber Eats and DoorDash stand out for their ease of entry, flexible scheduling, and significant earning potential. Whether you’re saving for a specific goal, paying off debt, or just looking for a side hustle to make ends meet, delivering food can be a great way to boost your income. Let’s dive into how you can make the most of these platforms while maximizing your time and effort.

Understanding the Basics: How It Works

At its core, both Uber Eats and DoorDash operate as delivery services connecting customers to local restaurants or stores. Here’s a simplified breakdown:

  1. Onboarding: The first step to making money is signing up. Both platforms usually have simple application processes requiring a valid driver’s license, proof of insurance (for those driving), and passing a background check.

  2. Receiving Orders: Once approved, all you need is a smartphone and the relevant app. You’ll be notified of nearby orders and can evaluate the delivery details before accepting them.

  3. Fulfillment: After accepting an order, you’ll either pick up prepared food from a restaurant or shop for groceries (depending on the type of order), then deliver the items to the customer.

The ease of this process, combined with the flexibility to set your hours, makes driving for Uber Eats or DoorDash a popular choice for side hustlers of all backgrounds.

Why Delivering Food Works as a Side Hustle

There are several factors that make food delivery an attractive option for extra income:

  1. Low Barrier to Entry: Unlike many part-time jobs that require specific skills or certifications, delivery driving is accessible to just about anyone with a reliable vehicle or even a bicycle in some markets.

  2. Highly Flexible Schedule: You can work whenever it fits your lifestyle. This is perfect for individuals with irregular availability, such as students or parents.

  3. Quick Earnings: Most platforms offer weekly payouts, and some even have daily cash-out options, allowing you to access your earnings almost immediately.

  4. Independence: You work without a boss looking over your shoulder, focusing instead on your pace and goals.

Tips to Maximize Earnings with Uber Eats and DoorDash

Making the most money as a delivery driver isn’t just about being on the road; it’s about working smarter, not harder. Here’s how to maximize your income while conserving time and resources:

1. Strategically Choose When to Work

Not all hours are created equal when it comes to delivery driving. High-demand times, such as lunch (11 AM - 2 PM) and dinner (5 PM - 9 PM), are the most profitable due to greater order volumes and potential peak-time bonuses. Additionally, Friday nights, weekends, and major events can bring in more substantial earnings.

2. Prioritize High-Tip Orders

Some delivery platforms allow you to see customer tips before accepting an order, enabling you to prioritize high-paying deliveries. Large orders, for instance, often generate greater tips. Be mindful of your acceptance rate, however, as declining too many orders may impact your overall standing or eligibility for promotions.

3. Work in High-Demand Areas

Your location can significantly influence your income. Urban areas, college campuses, and neighborhoods with a dense restaurant presence tend to have more frequent orders. Monitor your app to identify “hot spots” where demand is highest, and position yourself strategically to minimize downtime between deliveries.

4. Combine and Stack Orders

Both platforms sometimes allow you to accept multiple orders simultaneously. If feasible, stacking orders from the same restaurant or nearby locations can be a great way to save time and mileage, leading to increased efficiency per hour.

Managing Expenses: The Hidden Side of Delivery Driving

While earning money with Uber Eats or DoorDash is straightforward, it’s essential to manage and track your expenses to determine your net income accurately. Here’s what to consider:

1. Gas and Vehicle Maintenance

For those who drive, these are among the largest ongoing expenses. To minimize costs:

  • Use apps such as GasBuddy or Upside to find the cheapest fuel in your area.
  • Stay on top of regular vehicle maintenance to prevent unexpected repair costs.
  • Consider using a fuel-efficient vehicle.

2. Self-Employment Taxes

As an independent contractor, you’ll need to set aside a portion of your earnings for taxes. Familiarize yourself with tax rules for freelancers and gig workers in your area, and save receipts for vehicle-related expenses, as they may be deductible.

3. Driving Insurance

Standard car insurance often doesn’t cover commercial activities. Research and invest in a rideshare or delivery-specific policy for added protection.

4. Wear and Tear

In addition to immediate costs, frequent driving accelerates wear and tear on your vehicle. Consider the long-term expenses of maintaining your car or eventually replacing it and factor this into your earnings.

Advanced Strategies for Long-Term Success

For those serious about turning delivery driving into a reliable income stream, the following strategies can help:

1. Leverage Multiple Platforms

Driving solely for Uber Eats or DoorDash limits your opportunities. Many successful drivers maximize their availability by signing up for several delivery services simultaneously. This ensures you always have orders to choose from, even when demand dips on one platform.

2. Track and Analyze Your Data

Keeping a detailed log of your hours worked, deliveries completed, and expenses allows you to calculate your true hourly rate and identify inefficiencies. Apps like Gridwise or Stride can make tracking seamless.

3. Provide Excellent Customer Service

Positive customer experiences not only boost the likelihood of generous tips but also improve your ratings on the platform. Respond to customers courteously, follow any specific delivery instructions, and ensure food items arrive promptly and in excellent condition.

4. Split Earnings Wisely

Consider how you allocate your earnings. Using a percentage-based system can help build financial stability. For example:

  • 50% for immediate expenses.
  • 30% for future needs or emergency savings.
  • 20% for leisure or discretionary purchases.

Beyond the Money: Additional Perks of the Job

While the financial benefits are the main draw, there are additional perks to delivering for Uber Eats or DoorDash:

  1. Freedom to Explore Your Community: Delivery drivers often discover new restaurants and neighborhoods, helping them feel more connected to their area.

  2. Physical Movement: Although you’re driving most of the time, certain deliveries involving bicycles or walking can provide light exercise.

  3. Career Bridge: For those in transition or looking to change careers, this side hustle can provide financial stability while pursuing other goals or upskilling.

Challenges to Consider

Like any job, food delivery comes with challenges. Being aware of these ahead of time can help you prepare:

  • Unpredictable Demand: Some days or shifts may yield fewer orders than others.
  • Weather Conditions: Rain, snow, or extreme temperatures can make driving less enjoyable but may also increase demand.
  • Inconsistent Earnings: Since income is order-based, it may fluctuate significantly depending on factors like location, time, and customer tips.

Is It Worth It?

Delivery driving can be an incredibly rewarding side gig when approached thoughtfully. The flexibility, independence, and income potential make it a top choice for many. However, success depends on careful planning, efficient execution, and a willingness to adapt based on your experiences.

Whether you’re looking to generate a modest supplementary income or take your earnings to the next level, food delivery platforms like Uber Eats and DoorDash provide an empowering framework for side hustle success. Take control of your financial future, and you might find that this simple yet effective opportunity aligns perfectly with your goals. Now it’s time to hit the road and start earning!
