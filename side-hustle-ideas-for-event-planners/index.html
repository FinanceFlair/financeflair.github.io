Side Hustle Ideas for Event Planners


Event planning is a dynamic and rewarding profession, but it often comes with variable income and periods between major gigs. For many event planners, developing side hustles isn’t just about making extra money—it’s a way to leverage their unique skills, expand their network, and build a more stable business foundation. If you’re an event planner looking to diversify your income, explore new passions, or simply fill the gaps between big projects, this blog post dives deep into creative and profitable side hustle ideas tailored just for you.

Why Event Planners are Perfect Candidates for Side Hustles

Your expertise in logistics, creativity, communication, and organization gives you an edge when adopting additional income streams. Moreover, your existing client base, vendor relationships, and knowledge of customer needs position you well to cross-sell services or branch into related disciplines.

Unlike many other industries, event planning requires a blend of project management and interpersonal skills—attributes that are highly transferable. This makes side hustles both natural extensions and fresh opportunities for growth.

Side Hustle Idea #1: Wedding and Party Decor Rentals

If you have a knack for aesthetics and already own a stash of event decor, turning those assets into a rental business can be lucrative. From vintage vases, fairy lights, and linens to custom signage, rentals bring recurring income without needing constant new investment.

Getting Started

  • Inventory Assessment: Catalog items you have or can easily source.
  • Storage and Transportation: Figure out how you’ll store, clean, and deliver the items.
  • Marketing: Use social media and your existing network to promote rental options.
  • Pricing: Research local market prices and offer package deals for different event sizes.

Side Hustle Idea #2: Virtual Event Coordination

With the rise of remote work and virtual gatherings, offering virtual event coordination services is a booming niche. This could include webinars, online conferences, or hybrid events combining in-person and digital participation.

Why It Works

  • Flexibility: You can handle multiple virtual events simultaneously.
  • Lower Overhead: No physical venue or equipment to manage.
  • Expanding Market: Companies and organizations are increasing their digital presence and require expert planning.

You’ll want to familiarize yourself with platforms like Zoom, Hopin, or Microsoft Teams, and build a toolkit for engagement strategies and troubleshooting.

Side Hustle Idea #3: Event Planning Workshops and Coaching

If you have years of experience, packaging your knowledge into workshops or one-on-one coaching can attract beginners eager to learn the ropes. This works well for both aspiring event planners and clients looking to DIY their own events.

Potential Focus Areas

  • Basics of event budgeting and vendor management
  • How to create memorable event themes
  • Crisis management and contingency planning
  • Marketing and promotion for events

You could offer these sessions in person, online, or as pre-recorded courses.

Side Hustle Idea #4: Personalized Event Stationery Design

Event planners often understand the power of detail, including event stationery like invitations, programs, and menus. If you have graphic design skills, or are willing to outsource design, creating personalized event stationery is a profitable and creative option.

You can leverage platforms like Etsy or your personal website, and collaborate with clients to reflect their event’s personality and aesthetics perfectly.

Side Hustle Idea #5: Vendor Management and Consulting

Many small businesses looking to host events lack the time or expertise for vendor sourcing and negotiation. Your role as a vendor manager or consultant can alleviate that burden.

You can:

  • Source reliable vendors and negotiate better pricing.
  • Coordinate timelines between multiple suppliers.
  • Ensure quality control on behalf of the client.

This model can be tailored for corporate clients, non-profits, or individual hosts.

Side Hustle Idea #6: Event Equipment Setup and Breakdown Services

Not every event planner wants the labor-intensive parts of their job, but many clients do. Offering dedicated setup and teardown services is a practical side hustle, especially if you have a strong, dependable crew or work solo and enjoy physical tasks.

Clients appreciate receiving extra help handling heavy equipment, arranging furniture, or decorating on short notice.

Side Hustle Idea #7: Social Media Event Promotion

Marketing is essential for event success but is time-consuming. You can offer social media services focused on event promotion—everything from crafting posts and stories to running targeted ads and engaging with attendees online.

Benefits of this side hustle:

  • Syncing promotional efforts with event timelines.
  • Measuring engagement and adjusting campaigns in real time.
  • Building your reputation as an event marketing expert.

Side Hustle Idea #8: Themed Pop-Up Experiences

If creativity drives you, launching themed pop-up events or experiences can be exciting and profitable. Think along the lines of seasonal markets, immersive art installations, or intimate dining experiences.

These can be ticketed events that you organize and promote yourself, offering complete creative control and potential for significant earnings.

Tips for Balancing Side Hustles with Your Primary Event Planning Work

Balancing multiple ventures requires clear boundaries and smart scheduling:

  • Prioritize Client Relationships: Never let side projects interfere with main client commitments.
  • Leverage Automation: Use scheduling and communication tools to streamline operations.
  • Delegate When Possible: Build trusted teams to help you manage workload.
  • Maintain Clear Branding: Separate identity of side hustles if they target different markets.
  • Evaluate Profitability Regularly: Track time and income to focus on the most rewarding gigs.

Conclusion: Turning Passion into Profit

Side hustles for event planners aren’t just about making money; they’re about expanding your skills, growing your professional network, and ensuring financial stability in a career known for unpredictability. Whether it’s turning your decor into a rental empire, coaching future planners, or diving into virtual events, the possibilities are vast and exciting.

By building on what you already do well, supplementing your schedule strategically, and staying open to new trends, you can transform your event planning side hustles into thriving ventures.

Ready to start your side hustle journey? Focus on what excites you most, do your research, and take the leap. The events industry is broad, and your experience is a valuable asset waiting to unlock new success pathways.
