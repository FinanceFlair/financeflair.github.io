Angel Investing in Gaming: Capitalizing on Entertainment Trends


Angel investors play a significant role in funding new and innovative businesses in various industries. The gaming industry is no exception. With the rapid growth of technology, games have evolved beyond simple board games and arcade machines. Today, gaming experiences are immersive, interactive, and accessible to billions worldwide.

For angel investors, the gaming industry offers lucrative opportunities to capitalize on emerging entertainment trends. This text explores these opportunities and the factors contributing to the sector’s increasing appeal to investors and players alike.

Market Growth and Opportunities

The gaming industry is continuously expanding, driven by advancements in technology and growing player demographics. In 2021, it was valued at over $159 billion and is projected to grow by nearly 8% annually through 2030. As more people incorporate gaming into their daily lives, both casual and professional gamers stand to benefit from investments in this sector.

Moreover, mobile gaming, in particular, has created an enormous market for developers worldwide. With smartphones now integral to our lives, games have proliferated and reached untapped audiences in regions where traditional gaming systems were unavailable. With such growth opportunities, angel investors can find promising returns through early investments in development studios or game distribution platforms.

Revolutionary Technologies and Platforms

Gaming technologies like augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) have redefined how players interact with games. Virtual and augmented realities make their way into homes through games like Pokémon Go and Beat Saber. Innovations in these areas create new investments opportunities for angel investors, from developing VR goggles to supporting software development for these platforms.

Furthermore, the advent of blockchain technology has given investors another avenue to capitalize on the game’s potential. Cryptocurrencies like NFTs (non-fungible tokens) and in-game assets have led to the emergence of games like Axie Infinity and CryptoVoxels. These games offer players unique, tradable assets with real economic value, creating new avenues for investors to exploit this emerging trend.

Strategies for Angel Investors

To make successful investments in the gaming industry, angel investors must assess opportunities based on three crucial factors: game development, target audience, and business model. Effective due diligence involves evaluating the executive team’s expertise and experience in the gaming industry and the potential for scalability. Cosmic Riot, a blockchain-based card trading game, exemplifies these criteria. Led by former Actoz Soft Co. President Dalmin Jo, the team’s experience in game development increases the likelihood of success.

Target audiences should also be carefully considered, as certain games resonate better with specific player personas. Games catering to niches like hardcore role-playing game (RPG) players or casual mobile gamers can coexist, but investors must choose wisely. Additionally, virtual and augmented reality games are particularly suited for younger, tech-savvy demographics.

Lastly, investors must assess the business model: is it based on subscriptions, in-game purchases, advertising, or the sale of tradable in-game assets like NFTs? The ideal investment opportunity includes a combination of multiple revenue streams that will provide long-term sustainability and profitability, such as Puzzle Crypt.

Innovation and Retail Appeal

The gaming industry remains a responsive, ever-evolving landscape where success relies on innovative, marketable ideas. As angel investors, we must weigh in on the potential appeal of a game or platform. Games like Among Us have seen meteoric rises based on their viral trends, reflecting popular culture and garnering tremendous popularity.

On the other hand, companies like Magic Leap have struggled to break into the market even with innovative AR technology. The company’s ability to deliver products appealing to mainstream audiences failed to materialize, making it a less attractive investment opportunity for angel investors.

Conclusion

The gaming industry offers substantial opportunities for angel investors who keep up with emerging trends and technology developments. As the industry’s appeal broadens, angel investors should keep an eye on developments in VR, AR, blockchain, subscription models, and NFTs to capitalize on entertainment trends. With careful consideration of game development, target audience, and business models, savvy investors can capitalize on the gaming industry’s continued growth and success.
