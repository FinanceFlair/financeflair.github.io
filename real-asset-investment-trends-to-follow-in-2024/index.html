Real Asset Investment Trends to Follow in 2024


As the global economy continues to evolve and navigate uncharted territories, investors are looking for alternative asset classes to hedge their portfolios and achieve potential long-term growth. Real assets, encompassing tangible assets such as real estate, infrastructure, and natural resources, are increasingly gaining attention as a means to diversify and stabilize investments. Rise of Private Real Estate Investing Private real estate investing has witnessed significant growth in recent years, with the US alone accounting for over $700 billion in transaction volume in 2022. The rise of crowdfunding platforms and online marketplaces has democratized access to private real estate investment opportunities, formerly reserved for high-net-worth individuals and institutional investors. This trend is expected to continue, particularly in emerging markets, as demand for quality housing, office space, and logistics facilities increases. A key driver of this growth is the increasing need for institutional-grade real estate investment vehicles that cater to smaller investors and family offices. Private real estate investment groups, offering a managed platform of direct property investments, are proliferating, addressing this demand. With the emergence of private real estate investing 2.0, investors can now diversify their portfolios with a wider range of property types and structures. Infrastructure Investment Opportunities Infrastructure investment has traditionally been limited to institutional investors and pension funds. However, with the rise of new infrastructure financing instruments and the growth of demand-side financing, opportunities for individual investors and family offices are expanding. From sustainable wind farms and solar parks to high-demand hydroelectric power stations, investors can now access a diversified range of infrastructure investments directly. Real estate investment trusts (REITs) specialized in infrastructure are also emerging, providing further liquidity and ease of access into these assets. Natural Resource Investing Natural resource investing, including timberland, farmland, and precious metals, is another significant trend in real assets. Rising global demand for natural resources, driven by the increasing urbanization and middle-class consumption, makes these investments an attractive option for performance and diversification. Farmland investing, for example, offers income-generating opportunities, economic benefits, and principled ownership.
