How to Set Realistic Goals on Crowdfunding Platforms


Crowdfunding has become a popular and effective way to raise funds for a variety of projects, from launching new products to supporting charitable causes. However, one of the most critical aspects of a successful crowdfunding campaign is setting a realistic fundraising goal. Too high, and you risk not meeting your target, leaving your project underfunded. Too low, and you might not raise enough to cover the full costs of bringing your idea to life. Setting the right goal can make or break your crowdfunding campaign.

In this guide, we’ll walk through how to set realistic goals on crowdfunding platforms, helping you plan strategically for success. Whether you’re new to crowdfunding or an experienced campaigner, the following steps will provide you with actionable insights to create a goal that resonates with your backers and sets your project up for success.

Understanding Your Project’s Needs

Before you can even begin to set a crowdfunding goal, you need to have a thorough understanding of your project’s needs. What are you trying to fund? What are the specific expenses involved? Understanding the financial requirements of your project is the first step in determining your fundraising goal.

Break Down Your Expenses

Start by creating a detailed list of all the costs associated with your project. Depending on the nature of your campaign, this could include:

  • Production Costs: Materials, manufacturing, packaging, and shipping costs for physical products.
  • Marketing and Promotion: Budget for advertising, social media campaigns, influencer collaborations, and other forms of promotion.
  • Platform Fees: Crowdfunding platforms often take a percentage of the funds raised (typically around 5% to 8%).
  • Payment Processing Fees: These fees are charged by payment processors like Stripe or PayPal and can be up to 3-5%.
  • Contingency Funds: Unexpected costs can arise, so it’s important to set aside a buffer for unforeseen expenses.
  • Rewards Fulfillment: If you’re offering rewards to backers, factor in the cost of producing and shipping these rewards.

Having a clear breakdown of your costs will help you set a goal that reflects the true financial needs of your project.

Determine Your Target Audience

Your fundraising goal should also take into consideration the type of backers you expect to attract. Understanding your target audience’s willingness to contribute will help you set a realistic goal. If you’re targeting niche backers, such as a small group of specialized enthusiasts, your goal may need to be more modest. If you’re targeting a broader audience with mass appeal, you may need to set a higher goal to account for a larger number of small contributions.

Evaluate Market Trends and Similar Campaigns

One of the best ways to determine a realistic crowdfunding goal is to analyze similar campaigns that have been successful in your niche. By researching similar campaigns, you can gauge the typical funding amounts for projects like yours and adjust your goal accordingly.

Look for campaigns that:

  • Are similar in terms of project scope and goals.
  • Have achieved successful funding within your project’s category or industry.
  • Offer similar reward tiers to the ones you’re planning.

Pay attention to the length of these campaigns as well. A 30-day campaign may require a different goal than a 60-day campaign. The funding momentum tends to slow down after the first week or two, so having an accurate sense of the average funding rate for similar projects can help guide your goal-setting process.

Consider the Platform’s Success Rate

Different crowdfunding platforms have different success rates. For example, platforms like Kickstarter tend to have a success rate of around 36%, while Indiegogo may have a slightly lower success rate. The platform you choose can affect the type of backers it attracts and how easily you can reach your goal.

Research the platform’s general funding success rate and history of successful campaigns in your category. This will help you set a goal that is aligned with the platform’s typical performance.

Set Stretch Goals but Avoid Unrealistic Targets

It can be tempting to set a very high goal, especially if you want to make a big splash or attract attention. However, setting a goal that’s too high can often backfire. If your goal is far beyond what your backers are willing or able to contribute, you may fail to reach it, which can result in your project being cancelled and your backers losing trust in your ability to deliver.

Instead, set a primary goal that is achievable and consider adding stretch goals. Stretch goals are additional funding targets you can introduce once your primary goal is met. These goals should be ambitious but realistic, offering your backers exciting additional rewards or features if certain funding milestones are reached. Stretch goals can provide the incentive for backers to contribute more once they see that your campaign is gaining momentum.

Example of a Stretch Goal Strategy:

  • Primary Goal: $10,000 – To fund production and shipping of the first 1,000 units of your product.
  • Stretch Goal 1: $15,000 – Additional color variations for the product.
  • Stretch Goal 2: $20,000 – Enhanced packaging or limited edition rewards.

This approach allows you to stay grounded with your initial goal while providing your backers with added value if the campaign exceeds expectations.

Include Buffer Room for Unforeseen Expenses

It’s essential to factor in a buffer for unexpected costs. Even with the most meticulous planning, unforeseen expenses can crop up during the production, marketing, and fulfillment stages. These can include delays in manufacturing, additional shipping costs, or changes in platform fees.

While it’s difficult to predict every cost that may arise, a general rule of thumb is to add a buffer of 10-15% to your initial funding goal. This gives you a safety net in case something goes wrong, and ensures that you’re not left scrambling for additional funds if unexpected expenses arise.

Consider Timing and Backer Expectations

When setting your crowdfunding goal, also consider the time of year and the economic environment. Crowdfunding campaigns launched around major holidays, such as Christmas or Black Friday, may attract more donations due to the increased spirit of giving. Similarly, crowdfunding campaigns launched during economic downturns or recessions may face more challenges, as people may be less inclined to spend money.

Backer expectations can also play a role in determining your goal. For example, if you’re crowdfunding a technology product, backers may expect more in terms of rewards and development than if you’re raising funds for a local community project. Be sure to align your goal with the expectations of your backers and the type of rewards you plan to offer.

Set Milestones to Track Progress

Once you’ve set your goal, break it down into smaller milestones. These milestones will help you track your progress throughout the campaign and ensure that you’re on target to meet your overall goal.

For example, if your goal is $20,000, you can break it down into weekly or bi-weekly milestones. Tracking your progress against these milestones allows you to identify potential gaps in your strategy and adjust your marketing efforts accordingly.

Milestones also provide transparency for your backers, allowing them to see how far along the campaign is and giving them a sense of excitement as the campaign progresses.

Engage Your Community Early On

Finally, one of the most important factors in setting a realistic goal is engaging your community early on. Building a solid base of supporters before you launch your campaign can help you reach your goal faster and with less effort. The more passionate and committed your community is, the more likely they will help you spread the word and encourage others to contribute.

Building a community can take time, so it’s important to start early. Use social media, email newsletters, and other channels to engage with potential backers before launching your campaign. The earlier you start building momentum, the more likely you’ll be to reach your fundraising goal.

Conclusion

Setting a realistic goal on crowdfunding platforms is an art and a science. By understanding your project’s financial needs, analyzing market trends, considering backer expectations, and setting stretch goals, you can craft a fundraising goal that is both achievable and motivating. A well-thought-out goal will not only help you secure the funds you need but will also establish credibility and trust with your backers.

Remember to stay flexible during the campaign, monitor your progress regularly, and adjust your strategy as necessary. With the right planning and a strong community behind you, your crowdfunding campaign will be on the path to success.
