Frugal Living: How to Save Money on Back-to-School Shopping


Back-to-school season is just around the corner, and many parents are already feeling the pressure of preparing their children for the new academic year. One of the biggest expenses during this time is back-to-school shopping. However, with a little creativity and planning, it’s possible to save money on school supplies without sacrificing quality.

Plan Ahead

The key to saving money on back-to-school shopping is to plan ahead. Make a list of the supplies your child needs for the upcoming year, and check with their teacher or school to see if there are any specific requirements. This will help you avoid buying unnecessary items and reduce waste.

Shop Second-Hand

One of the easiest ways to save money on back-to-school shopping is to shop second-hand. You can find gently used school supplies at thrift stores, garage sales, or online marketplaces. Additionally, many schools and community organizations hold back-to-school supply drives, where you can pick up free or low-cost supplies.

Buy in Bulk

Buying in bulk is a great way to save money on back-to-school shopping. Consider purchasing items like pencils, pens, and paper in bulk, and storing them in a designated area for easy access. This will not only save you money, but also reduce waste and clutter.

Shop During Sales

Keep an eye out for sales and discounts on school supplies. Many retailers offer special promotions and discounts during back-to-school season, and you can often find deals on items like backpacks, lunchboxes, and school supplies.

DIY Projects

Get creative and make your own school supplies! There are many DIY projects you can make at home, such as making your own pencil cases, book covers, and lunchboxes. Not only will this save you money, but it’s also a fun and creative way to get your child involved in the back-to-school process.

Use Cashback Apps

Use cashback apps like Ibotta or Fetch Rewards to earn money back on your back-to-school purchases. These apps offer rebates on specific items, and you can earn cash or gift cards to use on future purchases.

Avoid Brand Names

Brand-name school supplies can be expensive, but they’re not always necessary. Consider purchasing generic or store-brand supplies instead, which are often cheaper and just as effective.

Shop Online

Shopping online can be a great way to save money on back-to-school shopping. Many retailers offer free shipping, discounts, and promo codes that can help you save even more. Additionally, you can often find better deals online than in-store.

Consider Used Textbooks

If your child is in middle school or high school, consider purchasing used textbooks instead of new ones. This can save you a significant amount of money, and many online retailers offer used textbooks at a fraction of the cost of new ones.

Get Your Child Involved

Get your child involved in the back-to-school shopping process! Encourage them to help you make a list of the supplies they need, and let them help you shop for them. This can be a fun and educational experience for your child, and they’ll be more likely to take care of their new supplies.

Use a Budgeting App

Use a budgeting app like Mint or Personal Capital to track your back-to-school expenses. These apps can help you stay organized and on budget, and you can set reminders and alerts to ensure you don’t go over budget.

Shop at Discount Stores

Discount stores like Target or Walmart often have a wide selection of back-to-school supplies at affordable prices. You can find everything from pencils and paper to backpacks and lunchboxes at a fraction of the cost of other retailers.

Consider a School Supply Box

Consider purchasing a school supply box or kit that includes everything your child needs for the upcoming year. These kits are often cheaper than buying individual supplies, and they can save you time and hassle.

Use a Price Comparison Tool

Use a price comparison tool like PriceGrabber or Nextag to compare prices on back-to-school supplies. These tools can help you find the best deals and discounts, and you can often find better prices online than in-store.

Get a Rebate

Check with your child’s school or teacher to see if they offer any rebates or discounts on back-to-school supplies. Many schools and teachers offer special promotions or discounts for students, and you can often find rebates on specific items.

Shop at a Local Store

Shop at a local store instead of a big-box retailer. Local stores often have a more personal touch and can offer better customer service. Additionally, you can often find unique and specialty items at local stores that you won’t find at bigger retailers.

By following these tips, you can save money on back-to-school shopping without sacrificing quality. Remember to plan ahead, shop second-hand, buy in bulk, and get creative with DIY projects. Happy shopping!
