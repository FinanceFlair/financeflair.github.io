Top 10 Micro Investing Apps to Grow Your Money


Investing has become more accessible than ever, thanks to the rise of micro investing apps. These platforms allow users to invest small amounts of money, often as little as a few dollars, in a wide range of investment options. Whether you’re a seasoned investor or just starting to dip your toes into the world of finance, micro investing apps offer a convenient and low-cost way to grow your money. In this article, we’ll explore the top 10 micro investing apps that can help you achieve your financial goals.

1. Acorns

Acorns is a popular micro investing app that simplifies the investment process by rounding up your everyday purchases to the nearest dollar and investing the spare change. This “round-up” feature makes investing effortless and allows you to gradually build a diversified portfolio over time.

2. Robinhood

Robinhood has gained widespread popularity for its commission-free trading and user-friendly interface. This app allows users to invest in stocks, ETFs, options, and cryptocurrencies without paying any fees, making it an attractive option for both new and experienced investors.

3. Stash

Stash aims to make investing approachable for beginners by offering personalized investment recommendations based on your financial goals and risk tolerance. With a low minimum investment requirement, Stash is an excellent choice for those looking to start small.

4. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is a robo-advisor that utilizes advanced algorithms to create and manage diversified investment portfolios for its users. This app offers automated investing, tax-loss harvesting, and financial planning tools to help users grow their wealth over time.

5. Betterment

Similar to Wealthfront, Betterment is a robo-advisor that provides automated investment management services. It offers a range of investment options, including retirement accounts and socially responsible portfolios, making it suitable for various investment preferences.

6. M1 Finance

M1 Finance combines the flexibility of a brokerage account with the automation of a robo-advisor. Users can create custom portfolios or choose from pre-built “pies” that align with their investment objectives, all without paying any commissions or management fees.

7. Public

Public is a social investing app that allows users to buy fractional shares of stocks and ETFs, follow other investors, and share insights with a community of like-minded individuals. This app encourages transparency and knowledge sharing among its users, making investing a collaborative experience.

8. Clink

Clink takes a unique approach to micro investing by focusing on saving and investing through automated transfers from your checking account. Users can set their own savings goals and watch their money grow through a diversified portfolio of ETFs.

9. Raiz (Acorns in Australia)

For our Australian readers, Raiz, formerly known as Acorns, offers a similar round-up feature to invest spare change. Additionally, it provides the option to make recurring investments and offers a range of diversified investment portfolios to suit different risk profiles.

10. Stockpile

Stockpile allows users to invest in fractional shares of stocks and ETFs with as little as $5. This app is particularly popular for gifting stocks, making it an excellent choice for introducing younger generations to the world of investing.

In conclusion, micro investing apps have revolutionized the way people invest, making it possible for anyone to grow their money, regardless of their income or experience level. Whether you’re saving for a specific goal, building a nest egg for the future, or simply exploring the world of investing, these top 10 micro investing apps offer a range of features and investment options to help you achieve financial success. With the power of technology at your fingertips, investing has never been more accessible, convenient, and rewarding.
