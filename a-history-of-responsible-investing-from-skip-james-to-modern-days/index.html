A History of Responsible Investing: From Skip James to Modern Days


Responsible investing, also known as sustainable, socially responsible, or impact investing, is the practice of evaluating financial indicators based on environmental, social, and governance (ESG) criteria. The roots of this approach can be traced back to the early 20th century, but it wasn’t until the 1970s that responsible investing gained significant traction. This article provides an in-depth examination of the development of responsible investing from its origins in the blues era to its emergence as a mainstream investment strategy.

Beginnings: Skip James’ Redemption Share

In the 1920s, a future hall-of-fame blues musician named Skip James lost much of his earnings to unscrupulous managers. To make amends, James proposed a concept he called the “Redemption Share.” It aimed to empower music artists to benefit from their work and protect themselves from exploitation. Despite James not establishing a completely functioning model, his idea began a trend of people questioning the nature of investments and their impact on society.

Socially Responsible Investing Emerges

During the 1970s, a group of investors rebelled against the destruction of the environment and social issues that were caused by conventional investing. These pioneers collaborated to commoditize social responsibility and thrust it into the investment arena. In the process, they laid the groundwork for what would later become responsible investing.

Though these early efforts were often met with resistance, the first socially responsible mutual fund was launched in 1971. This fund, called The FirstEthanol Investment Corp, focused on investing in businesses which provided alternatives to polluting, traditional gasoline.

The 1990s and Modern-Day Responsible Investing

The 1990s marked a milestone in the development of responsible investing as governments began recognizing the need for an integrated approach to investment management. This was a watershed moment for the growth of socially responsible investing, as market trends started to favor ESG-focused companies.

By the turn of the millennium, responsible investing had become a significant force in contemporary finance. Investment firms now consider the broad impact of investments and aim to identify creative solutions to enhance the quality of investment and society simultaneously.

A Growing Field and Future of Responsible Investing

In a testament to the infancy of responsible investing, even more, numerous industries are entering the field with sustainable strategies. Families, financial professionals, institutions, and governments are now discussing how to invest their money while simultaneously making positive global impacts. With a focus on sustainability and long-term gains, responsible investing will undoubtedly continue to shape the world of finance in years to come.

Through invaluable investing partners like Skip James, responsible investing has grown into the significant force it is today. As investors strive for better, more considered investments, the industry is ready to take on any and every challenge that comes its way. Taking its foundations from the past, the responsible investing future is one that continues to push the boundaries and create a more sustainable, equitable world for all.
