Crowdfunding for Books and Authors: Best Platforms to Use


Creating a book is a journey filled with passion, creativity, and countless hours of dedication. For many authors, funding this journey can be a challenge—whether it’s covering research expenses, editing, cover design, printing, or marketing. Crowdfunding has emerged as a powerful tool for writers to bring their projects to life, providing access to a broader audience and the finance needed to publish their work successfully.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best crowdfunding platforms tailored for books and authors. We’ll delve into what makes each platform unique, share tips on running successful campaigns, and offer insights into how writers can maximize their reach and funding potential.

Why Crowdfunding Works for Authors

Traditional publishing routes often come with gatekeepers, lengthy processes, and limited control over the final product. Crowdfunding breaks down these barriers by enabling authors to connect directly with readers and supporters who are eager to see their books come to life.

Benefits of Crowdfunding for Writers

  • Creative Control: Authors maintain control over content, design, and distribution.
  • Community Building: Engage directly with readers and foster a dedicated fan base.
  • Pre-Orders and Validation: Gauge demand before committing to print runs or large expenses.
  • Marketing Momentum: Campaigns double as marketing efforts, generating buzz and early reviews.
  • Financial Support: Access funds needed for high-quality production without upfront costs.

This democratization of the publishing process empowers independent authors and those exploring unique or niche topics that might not attract traditional publishers.

How to Choose the Right Crowdfunding Platform

Selecting the ideal platform depends largely on your goals, audience, timeline, and the scale of funding you seek. Key considerations should include:

  • Platform Fees: Understand the percentage taken from pledged funds.
  • Funding Model: All-or-nothing (funds only collected if goal is reached) vs. flexible funding.
  • Audience Match: Does the platform’s community align with your genre or style?
  • Campaign Features: Tools for updates, communication, reward tiers, and analytics.
  • Payment Options: Accessibility for your audience based on region and currency.

With these factors in mind, authors can find a platform that not only suits their needs but amplifies their ability to reach motivated backers.

Top Crowdfunding Platforms for Books and Authors

Here’s a deep dive into some of the most effective crowdfunding platforms that authors rely on, along with their strengths and potential limitations.

1. Kickstarter

Overview: Kickstarter is arguably the most popular crowdfunding platform and is well-suited to creative projects, including book publishing.

  • Funding Model: All-or-nothing.
  • Fee Structure: Approximately 5% platform fee plus payment processing fees (around 3-5%).
  • Audience: Vast and diverse; many users interested in creative arts.
  • Key Features: Rich project pages, reward tiers, updates, community feedback, and strong exposure due to Kickstarter’s high traffic.
  • Pros: Large audience, excellent for building broader awareness, integration with social media, and proven track record.
  • Cons: If funding goals are not met, no funds are released; competition is fierce.

Tips for Authors Using Kickstarter:

  • Craft a compelling video explaining your book project.
  • Design enticing reward tiers such as signed copies, exclusive artwork, or early access chapters.
  • Promote heavily across social media and literary communities.
  • Be transparent about project timelines to build trust.

2. Indiegogo

Overview: Indiegogo offers flexibility and supports a wide range of projects, including books. It has two funding options: fixed and flexible.

  • Funding Model: Fixed (all-or-nothing) or flexible (keep funds regardless of goal).
  • Fee Structure: 5% platform fee plus payment processing fees.
  • Audience: Global audience that supports innovative and creative projects.
  • Key Features: Flexible funding, option for “InDemand” campaigns to continue fundraising, integration with external sales.
  • Pros: Flexibility in funding options, continued campaign capabilities, supportive of international campaigns.
  • Cons: Less curated than Kickstarter, so visibility might be lower without active promotion.

Tips for Authors Using Indiegogo:

  • Decide whether flexible or fixed funding suits your risk tolerance.
  • Use the “InDemand” option for post-campaign sales and ongoing support.
  • Engage your backers with frequent updates and behind-the-scenes content.

3. Patreon

Overview: While it’s not crowdfunding in the traditional project-based sense, Patreon allows authors to receive ongoing monthly support from fans in exchange for exclusive content.

  • Funding Model: Subscription/membership-based pledges.
  • Fee Structure: 5-12% platform fee depending on plan, plus payment processing fees.
  • Audience: Loyal followers, fans of serialized content, and creatives offering regular updates.
  • Key Features: Membership tiers, exclusive posts, direct messaging, early access.
  • Pros: Provides sustainable funding, strengthens community relationships, encourages ongoing engagement.
  • Cons: Requires continuous content creation to maintain patron interest; not ideal for one-time book launches.

Tips for Authors Using Patreon:

  • Offer serialized chapters, writing insights, or exclusive Q&A sessions.
  • Maintain a consistent schedule to reward patron loyalty.
  • Use Patreon as a supplement rather than a sole funding source for book projects.

4. GoFundMe

Overview: GoFundMe is more commonly used for personal fundraising but can be harnessed by authors for projects with a heartfelt or community focus.

  • Funding Model: Keep what you raise, no goal requirement.
  • Fee Structure: Platform doesn’t charge a fee on personal campaigns (in certain countries), but payment processing fees apply.
  • Audience: Wide-ranging, often charitable or personal support-driven.
  • Key Features: Easy setup, no funding thresholds, donor shout-outs.
  • Pros: Simple and straightforward, no all-or-nothing pressure.
  • Cons: Less optimized for creative rewards and project-based campaigns.

Tips for Authors Using GoFundMe:

  • Pitch your story genuinely, highlighting the impact of your work.
  • Share widely within networks, asking for support and sharing updates.
  • Consider pairing GoFundMe with other platforms if you want reward-based incentives.

5. Unbound

Overview: Unbound is a hybrid crowdfunding and traditional publishing platform focusing exclusively on books. It allows authors to pitch projects and gain funding directly from readers.

  • Funding Model: All-or-nothing.
  • Fee Structure: Specific to projects; generally handled through contracts.
  • Audience: Readers who want to support innovative literary projects.
  • Key Features: Publishing support, editorial guidance, marketing help.
  • Pros: Publishing partnership, professional editorial support.
  • Cons: More selective; not every project qualifies.

Tips for Authors Using Unbound:

  • Research submission guidelines thoroughly.
  • Prepare a polished pitch and sample chapters.
  • Use the platform’s community to gain feedback and refine your work.

Crafting a Winning Crowdfunding Campaign

Simply choosing a platform isn’t enough; you need an effective campaign strategy to engage and motivate your audience.

Creating a Compelling Narrative

People finance stories they believe in. Your campaign should clearly present:

  • Your vision: What’s your book about? Why is it important or unique?
  • Your background: Share your journey as a writer and connection to the book’s topic.
  • Impact: How will the funds be used? What difference does seeing this book published make to you and to readers?
  • Rewards: Attractive, creative rewards that reflect the project’s value.

Design Eye-Catching Campaign Materials

  • Video: A heartfelt or creative video can dramatically increase backer trust and enthusiasm.
  • Images: Prototype cover designs, sample pages, or behind-the-scenes shots add authenticity.
  • Layout: Easy-to-read formatting and clear calls to action help maintain engagement.

Set Realistic Funding Goals

Base your goals on research and actual production costs. Underfunding can derail the final stages, while overambitious goals may intimidate backers.

Promote Strategically

  • Leverage Social Media: Regular posts, stories, and engagement on platforms your target audience frequents.
  • Email Lists: Reach out to existing contacts, book clubs, and literary groups.
  • Networking: Collaborate with other authors, influencers, or bloggers to expand reach.

Engage Backers Throughout

Provide consistent updates, respond promptly to questions, and show appreciation. Transparency builds trust and encourages repeat support.

Post-Campaign Steps for Authors

Reaching your funding goal is only the beginning. The way you manage the post-campaign phase can make or break your reputation.

  • Communicate: Keep backers updated on progress, delays, and milestones.
  • Deliver Rewards: Timely fulfillment of rewards strengthens goodwill.
  • Leverage Momentum: Use campaign success to attract publishers, bookstores, or media attention.
  • Collect Feedback: Early readers’ input can help refine future editions or marketing efforts.

Common Challenges and How to Navigate Them

Crowdfunding is not without pitfalls. Awareness helps you prepare:

  • Campaign fatigue: Avoid overwhelming your audience with too many asks.
  • Logistical complexities: Plan shipping, printing, and production details thoroughly.
  • Unrealized funds: Have backup plans if goals aren’t met on platforms with all-or-nothing funding.
  • Marketing demands: Campaigns require significant time and energy; collaborations or hiring help can ease the burden.

Conclusion

Crowdfunding offers a transformative path for authors to finance their dreams, build communities, and maintain creative freedom. Whether you choose Kickstarter’s vast audience, Indiegogo’s flexibility, Patreon’s ongoing support, or niche-focused platforms like Unbound, success hinges on authentic storytelling, strategic planning, and enthusiastic engagement.

For writers ready to navigate the evolving landscape of publishing, harnessing the power of crowdfunding might just be the key to transforming your manuscript from passion project to published reality.

Ready to start your crowdfunding adventure? Take time to research the platforms, plan your campaign, and embrace the opportunity to connect with readers eager to fuel your literary journey.
