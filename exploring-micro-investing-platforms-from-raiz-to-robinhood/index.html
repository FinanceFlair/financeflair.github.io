Exploring Micro-Investing Platforms: From Raiz to Robinhood


Welcome to the exciting world of micro-investing, where anyone can invest in the stock market with small amounts of money, opening doors for unprecedented financial liberties. In this blog post, we will explore two popular platforms: Raiz (previously known as Acorns) and Robinhood, comparing their functionality, ease of use, and benefits for newcomers.

Raiz

Raiz, founded in 2014, is an Australian micro-investing platform that simplifies the process of investing. At the core of their offering are “rounding up” transactions. When you make a purchase, Raiz will round it up to the nearest $1, depositing the difference into your Raiz investment account.

Raiz offers various investment options, including Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs). As a beginner, you can opt for a pre-built portfolio based on your risk appetite or choose a diversified Stocks & Shares Portfolio. One notable feature is the TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account) integration, offering a tax-efficient way to grow your investment.

Advantages of Raiz:

  • Easy to use for newcomers
  • Minimal transaction fees
  • Comprehensive educational resources
  • Integration with mobile banking applications

Disadvantages of Raiz:

  • Geographic limitations—Only available in Australia
  • Limited investment options compared to more established platforms
  • Lower market access, specializing in Australian markets
  • No fixed deposit investment options

Robinhood

Robinhood has made quite a name for itself as a disruptor in the investing space, focusing primarily on the United States market. Known for its commission-free trading, Robinhood has been a useful tool for many beginners seeking to explore the stock market.

Robinhood offers both individual stocks and ETFs, including fractional shares—a valuable feature for investors with limited capital wishing to diversify their portfolio. The platform offers a clean and simple interface, making it easy to navigate.

Advantages of Robinhood:

  • Available in the United States only
  • Commission-free trading
  • Broad market access, includes popular US stock exchanges
  • Offers fractional share investments
  • Easy-to-use, intuitive platform

Disadvantages of Robinhood:

  • Limited to US investors—Feel free to use a similar platform for your specific region
  • Account minimums can be comparable to other micro-investing platforms, despite the absence of trade commissions
  • Relies heavily on advertising and promotions for additional income

Choosing the Right Platform

When it comes to deciding between Raiz and Robinhood, it’s crucial to consider your needs and lifestyle.

If you’re based in Australia, live within their service area, prefer an intuitive platform, and are after a passive investing approach, Raiz could be your go-to. If you’re a beginner interested in investing in the US stock market with a DIY mindset and excited about fractional shares, Robinhood might be the more appropriate choice.

Takeaway

Both Raiz and Robinhood offer a useful alternative for those who want to dip their toes into the world of investing with smaller amounts. If you are an Australian reader, enjoy a rounded-up debit card spending experience, and wish to explore a more accessible entry point into the stock market, start your journey with Raiz. If you are based in the United States and seeking to devote yourself to commission-free trading, Robinhood’s approach could be the perfect fit for you.

Remember, investing always includes inherent risks. Do your research, educate yourself, and understand that investing is a long-term game. Regardless of which path you choose, Happy Investing!
