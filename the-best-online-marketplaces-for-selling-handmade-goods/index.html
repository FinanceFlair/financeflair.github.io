The Best Online Marketplaces for Selling Handmade Goods


Crafting handmade goods is not just about creating something beautiful or unique—it’s about sharing a piece of your creativity with the world. For artisans, artists, and makers, selling handmade goods is more than a business; it’s a passion. However, finding the most suitable platform to showcase these creations can make all the difference in reaching the right audience and achieving success. Today, we’ll explore the best online marketplaces for selling handmade goods, diving into their features, benefits, and what makes them an ideal choice for creative entrepreneurs.

Why Sell Handmade Goods Online?

Online marketplaces have revolutionized the way creatives approach their craft as a business. Selling handmade goods online provides unparalleled opportunities for visibility, flexibility, and scalability. It allows artisans to reach customers around the globe, surpassing the geographical limitations of local craft fairs or pop-up events. Additionally, online platforms often offer robust tools to help sellers manage inventory, track sales, and improve marketing efforts. Whether you’re just starting out or looking to expand your reach, the digital marketplace is a game-changer.

However, with an abundance of platforms available today, choosing the right one can be overwhelming. Each marketplace caters to a specific type of audience or niche and offers unique features that may align with your business needs. Let’s delve into some of the best online marketplaces tailored for handmade goods.

1. Etsy: The Global Hub for Handmade Goods

When it comes to selling handmade goods, Etsy remains one of the most renowned and widely recognized platforms. Since its launch in 2005, Etsy has built a reputation as the go-to marketplace for artisans, crafters, and vintage product sellers. Boasting a massive global audience, it provides an excellent opportunity to showcase handmade creations to millions of potential buyers.

Benefits of Selling on Etsy:

  • User-Friendly Setup: Opening a shop on Etsy is straightforward, even for first-time sellers. The platform offers helpful guides to set up, list, and promote your products.
  • Targeted Audience: Etsy’s audience is largely comprised of buyers specifically seeking handmade, unique, and custom-made items, making it easier to connect with customers who value craftsmanship.
  • Built-In SEO and Marketing Tools: Etsy provides in-platform tools to help your shop appear in search results, including keyword optimization tips and analytics.
  • Community Support: The Etsy community is full of resources for sellers, such as forums, groups, and tutorials to help navigate the e-commerce world.

While Etsy charges a listing fee and takes a small percentage of sales, its reach and established reputation make it an ideal starting point for handmade sellers.

2. Shopify: Your Personalized Handmade E-Commerce Website

Unlike Etsy, Shopify is not a marketplace but rather a platform that allows artisans to create their own standalone online store. For those looking for complete flexibility and ownership of their shop, Shopify is an excellent choice. Its customizable design and tools empower brands to fully reflect their unique aesthetic.

Benefits of Selling on Shopify:

  • Customization: You have complete control over your store’s design and branding, helping to create an unforgettable shopping experience.
  • Integrated Features: Shopify provides tools for inventory tracking, shipping calculations, abandoned cart recovery, and much more to streamline your operations.
  • Scalability: Shopify accommodates businesses of all sizes, which means your shop can grow without limitations as your sales increase.
  • Marketing Freedom: Unlike marketplaces with their preset models, Shopify allows you to take full control of your advertising and promotional campaigns, from SEO to email marketing.

While Shopify may require more effort to drive traffic to your store, it offers long-term advantages for building a recognizable handmade brand.

3. Amazon Handmade: Tapping Into the Giant E-Commerce Network

Amazon, known for being one of the largest e-commerce giants globally, also has a dedicated space for handmade goods. Amazon Handmade is a section of the platform tailored specifically for artisans, allowing them to showcase their creations to Amazon’s vast customer base.

Benefits of Selling on Amazon Handmade:

  • Unprecedented Reach: With millions of shoppers visiting Amazon daily, your products benefit from an expanded audience.
  • Trust and Credibility: Amazon’s name carries significant trust among consumers, which can boost sales for your handmade items.
  • Hands-Off Fulfillment Options: Amazon Handmade allows you to leverage Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA), where Amazon handles storing, packaging, and shipping your products.
  • Exclusive Access: Amazon Handmade is vetting sellers to ensure authenticity, so you won’t have to compete against mass-produced products in this space.

Despite its immense advantages, Amazon Handmade has higher fees compared to other platforms. However, sellers often find the exposure and sales potential worth the cost.

4. Folksy: A Haven for UK-Based Artisans

For those in the UK, Folksy is a fantastic option to consider. This online marketplace focuses exclusively on supporting British artisans and creators, making it an ideal platform for reaching a local market.

Benefits of Selling on Folksy:

  • Local Focus: By targeting UK customers, Folksy helps sellers build meaningful connections within their local community.
  • Affordability: Folksy offers affordable subscription models and lower fees compared to some broader platforms.
  • Niche Audience: Folksy’s buyers are enthusiastic supporters of handmade and local craftsmanship.

While Folksy doesn’t have the global reach of platforms like Etsy, its tailored approach ensures a loyal customer base for artisans within the region.

5. ArtFire: A Niche Marketplace for Artists and Makers

ArtFire is a lesser-known platform that caters specifically to artisans and small-batch creators. With a focus on handmade goods, art, and vintage items, ArtFire attracts a community of passionate buyers who appreciate craftsmanship.

Benefits of Selling on ArtFire:

  • Ease of Use: Setting up a shop on ArtFire is simple, and sellers can manage listings with ease.
  • Supportive Seller Tools: ArtFire offers tools for SEO, order tracking, and promotions to help artisans succeed.
  • Niche Audience: Like Etsy, ArtFire attracts a community of buyers seeking unique, handmade pieces.

While not as prominent as Etsy or Amazon Handmade, ArtFire’s niche audience means less competition and more opportunities to stand out.

6. Madeit: Connecting Australian Makers and Buyers

For Australian artisans, Madeit is an excellent platform to sell handmade goods online. Similar to Folksy in the UK, Madeit focuses entirely on showcasing local craftsmanship and supporting small businesses.

Benefits of Selling on Madeit:

  • Exclusive Marketplace: Only Australian-based sellers can join, ensuring a dedicated space for local makers.
  • Customizable Shops: Madeit allows sellers to create branded storefronts featuring their handmade goods.
  • Community-Oriented Approach: Sellers benefit from a strong sense of community as well as support from the Madeit team.

Madeit is a fantastic option for sellers looking to establish a local presence and connect with Australian buyers who value handmade goods.

Final Thoughts on Choosing the Right Marketplace

Selecting the best online marketplace to sell handmade goods requires careful consideration of your target audience, business goals, and personal preferences. Platforms like Etsy and ArtFire are well-suited for makers seeking global reach and community support, while Shopify offers unparalleled customization for building a distinctive brand. For artisans in specific regions, Folksy (UK) and Madeit (Australia) provide invaluable opportunities to connect with local buyers.

Additionally, as you grow, there’s no rule that says you have to stick to just one platform. Many successful makers diversify their presence by utilizing several marketplaces or pairing a marketplace like Etsy with their Shopify store. This approach allows you to reach a wider audience and ensure a steady flow of sales.

As you embark on your journey to selling handmade goods online, remember that success doesn’t happen overnight. Building a thriving handmade business involves creativity, persistence, and dedication—but the right platform can undoubtedly set you on the path to achieving your goals.
