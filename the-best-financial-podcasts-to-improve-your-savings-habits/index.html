The Best Financial Podcasts to Improve Your Savings Habits


When it comes to managing your finances and building stronger saving habits, knowledge truly is power. Keeping up with financial trends, understanding budgeting strategies, and learning how to save for long-term goals can be a lot to tackle, especially in today’s fast-paced world. That’s where financial podcasts come in. Accessible, informative, and often entertaining, podcasts provide a flexible way to deepen your understanding of personal finance while commuting, working out, or even washing dishes.

If you’re looking to enhance your financial literacy and improve your savings habits, these carefully curated financial podcasts are excellent resources to get started.

1. The Dave Ramsey Show

Dave Ramsey is a household name in personal finance, and his podcast is an excellent resource for anyone looking to manage money more effectively. Ramsey’s no-nonsense approach to budgeting, debt elimination, and saving makes it easy for listeners to grasp sound financial principles. The podcast often features listener calls, inspiring stories of individuals who’ve conquered debt, and actionable advice on how to save for retirement, emergency funds, and other goals.

What makes this podcast particularly impactful is its balance of tough love and motivational insights. Ramsey’s practical steps, such as his “Baby Steps” framework, resonate with listeners seeking to create a solid financial foundation. If you’re serious about developing better savings habits, this show can give you the structure and guidance you need.

2. Afford Anything

Hosted by Paula Pant, “Afford Anything” is an ideal podcast for those who want to rethink their financial priorities and align their spending and saving with what truly matters to them. Pant challenges the traditional mindset around money, offering a refreshing take on the trade-offs we make every day. After all, every dollar you spend is a dollar you can’t save or invest.

This podcast focuses on helping listeners craft a life they can afford by understanding values and weighing opportunity costs. Pant interviews experts on finance, psychology, and entrepreneurship, delving into topics like financial independence, real estate investing, and side hustles. Through insightful episodes, listeners learn to develop better savings habits by focusing on intentional spending and making the most out of their income.

3. The Smart Passive Income Podcast

Pat Flynn’s “Smart Passive Income” podcast isn’t solely dedicated to traditional personal finance topics—it’s also a fantastic resource for those looking to increase their income streams through entrepreneurial ventures. However, savings and budgeting often go hand-in-hand with income growth, making this podcast a valuable tool for maximizing your financial potential.

Flynn emphasizes the importance of leveraging time and money, inspiring listeners to use their resources wisely. When paired with saving habits, his advice on building passive income streams allows listeners to enhance their financial security. Whether it’s through starting a side hustle, scaling an online business, or developing passive income strategies, this podcast empowers listeners to take charge of their financial future.

4. Radical Personal Finance

Joshua Sheats, the host of “Radical Personal Finance,” delivers episodes that range from practical money-saving tips to deep dives into broader financial strategies. This podcast seeks to bridge the gap between basic personal finance education and advanced financial planning, offering something for everyone—whether you’re trying to build an emergency fund or aiming for financial independence.

What sets this podcast apart is its emphasis on critical thinking and individualization. Sheats provides listeners with the tools and knowledge to create a personalized approach to their finances. By understanding the nuances of topics like tax savings, debt reduction, and investment strategies, listeners can improve their savings habits while also preparing for long-term goals.

5. So Money with Farnoosh Torabi

“So Money” is a highly acclaimed podcast whose host, Farnoosh Torabi, brings a relatable and conversational approach to personal finance. Featuring interviews with leading experts, entrepreneurs, and even celebrities, this podcast offers actionable advice on everything from retirement planning to negotiating salaries.

Torabi’s skill lies in breaking down complex financial concepts into digestible, real-world advice. With an inclusive and diverse perspective, this podcast addresses savings habits in ways that resonate with people from all walks of life. The question-answer sessions with listeners add a personalized touch, making it easier for individuals to see how financial advice can relate to their unique circumstances.

6. ChooseFI

If you’ve ever been curious about the Financial Independence, Retire Early (FIRE) movement, “ChooseFI” is the podcast to explore. Hosted by Jonathan Mendonsa and Brad Barrett, this podcast breaks down the achievable steps toward financial independence through smart saving and intentional living.

The podcast covers a range of topics—from frugal living and travel hacks to investment strategies and tax optimization. What makes “ChooseFI” a great option for those wanting to improve their savings habits is its focus on everyday changes that yield long-term results. The hosts’ genuine enthusiasm and real-life examples create a sense of community, making financial independence feel attainable for anyone.

7. HerMoney with Jean Chatzky

True to its name, “HerMoney” is a finance podcast with a focus on empowering women to take control of their financial lives. Jean Chatzky, the host, creates a comfortable space for listeners to learn about money management, saving, and investing. While the podcast addresses topics relevant to women, its advice is universally applicable.

From retirement savings tips to managing student loans, “HerMoney” covers practical information with a warm, supportive tone. Chatzky frequently invites experts to discuss trends and strategies, making it easier to stay informed about financial tools and habits that work.

8. The BiggerPockets Money Podcast

If you’re interested in real estate as a method of building wealth while enhancing your saving habits, “The BiggerPockets Money Podcast” is a phenomenal choice. Hosted by Scott Trench and Mindy Jensen, this podcast offers insights on budgeting, building financial security, and leveraging real estate for investment purposes.

The podcast excels at exploring a wide variety of financial topics—such as debt repayment, frugality, and tax optimization—while keeping its discussions relevant to everyday listeners. By emphasizing actionable steps, “The BiggerPockets Money Podcast” provides concrete advice on how to improve saving habits and grow wealth.

Why Financial Podcasts Are a Game-Changer for Your Savings Habits

Podcasts are an invaluable tool for understanding personal finance because they offer access to experts, diverse perspectives, and actionable advice—all delivered in an easily digestible format. Here’s why incorporating financial podcasts into your learning routine can deeply influence your savings habits:

  1. Accessibility: Podcasts allow you to learn on your own terms, whether during a morning commute or while cooking dinner. This flexibility ensures that improving your financial knowledge is convenient and sustainable.

  2. Actionable Insights: Many hosts provide step-by-step strategies rather than abstract theories, helping you apply what you learn to your real-life savings goals.

  3. Community: Financial podcasts often create a sense of belonging by featuring relatable stories from guests and listeners, making saving and budgeting less intimidating.

How to Make the Most of Financial Podcasts

While listening to podcasts is a fantastic way to learn, you can maximize the benefits by taking an active approach to the content. Here are a few tips:

  • Take Notes: Jot down key lessons, strategies, or resources mentioned in episodes. Revisiting these notes can reinforce your learning.

  • Set Goals: Use what you learn to create tangible financial goals. Whether it’s cutting down unnecessary expenses or saving for a vacation, applying podcast lessons to your situation makes the experience more impactful.

  • Stay Consistent: Incorporate podcasts into your routine, like listening to one or two episodes weekly. Consistency helps you build momentum in your financial journey.

Final Thoughts

Improving your savings habits requires a combination of knowledge, intention, and actionable steps. Financial podcasts offer all three, giving you both the motivation and the tools to transform your financial future. Whether you’re new to budgeting or striving for complete financial freedom, the podcasts listed above can serve as invaluable companions on your journey. By dedicating time to learning and intentionally applying these lessons, you’ll find yourself moving closer to your savings goals with each episode.
