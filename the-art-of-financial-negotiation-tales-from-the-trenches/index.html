The Art of Financial Negotiation: Tales from the Trenches


Negotiation is an art that demands a perfect balance of skills - an ability to read the other party’s intentions, a convincing argument, and a keen sense of timing. In the financial domain, these skills become even more crucial as the stakes are generally higher. While there’s no one-size-fits-all approach, here are some insights from real-life tales from the trenches of financial negotiations.

Know Your Partner and the Game

A successful negotiation begins with understanding the other party and the principles they are guided by. Take the example of two contrasting negotiating personas:

  1. Mr. Armstrong: A fearless, Lone Ranger type who will walk away from any deal that doesn’t suit him.
  2. Ms. Smith: A team player, believing that a little give and take will ultimately get you further.

The negotiator’s approach should harmonize with their partner’s style. Mr. Armstrong needs a hardball player to match, while Ms. Smith would prefer a diplomatic negotiator.

Reframe Your Goal

When we think we’ve won, we often fail to spot new opportunities for improvement. A great example of this is how baseball negotiator Billy Beane redefined success itself in his team’s favor. Instead of focusing on signing renowned players, he sought undervalued potential champions and created a winning team.

The Power of Timing

Timing plays a pivotal role in getting what you want. It was midnight for the powers in the room when the dealmaker Walter Annenberg sensed it was time to agree: “The night bash was winding down, and people were becoming disenchanted. Suddenly I got up and announced that we had a deal.”

Opponents Should Converge at bargaining Not Duel

Negotiations should be seen as a dance floor rather than a battleground. Remember, you’re trying to move towards each other, not push each other away. Great negotiators avoid creating an us versus them mentality.

Show Empathy

Empathy can be a powerful grease for a deal. Steve Jobs, in a negotiation with John Sculley, used it to his advantage. He expressed how difficult a day he had experienced and asked Sculley whether, after such a day, he’d accept a product that didn’t feel empathetic to him.

Being Open to Win-win

Truly effective negotiators embrace a “win-win” negotiation strategy. They work diligently to understand their counterpart’s interests and strive to discover mutually beneficial solutions.

Close the Deal

The closing phase, often the most ill-discussed part of negotiation, is crucial. Consultants recommend the use of a ‘closing strategy,’ where you create a sense of urgency and make sure your counterpart feels an obligation to close the deal.

In conclusion, sharpening your skills in financial negotiation relies on understanding your counterpart, personal growth, developing strong listening skills, and adopting a “win-win” approach. Remember above all that patience and persistence are key.

As you navigate through these tales from the trenches, remember that every negotiation is different, and you must learn to adapt accordingly.
