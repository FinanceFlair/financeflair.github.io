How to Make Money with Your Phone: The Best Side Hustle Apps


In today’s digital age, smartphones are more than just communication tools. They have become powerful devices that can help you earn money, whether you’re looking for a part-time side hustle or just a way to supplement your income. From flexible gigs to passive income opportunities, there are numerous ways you can leverage your phone to make money in your spare time. This post will walk you through the best side hustle apps that can help you turn your smartphone into a source of income.

1. Ride-Sharing Apps: Drive for Cash

One of the most popular ways to make money using your phone is by becoming a driver for ride-sharing services. With apps like Uber and Lyft, you can easily start earning money by driving people to their destinations. The best part? You can choose your hours, making it a flexible and often lucrative option for those looking to make extra money.

Uber

Uber is one of the most well-known ride-sharing apps, offering a simple platform where you can sign up, connect with passengers, and get paid. The app provides the tools to track your earnings, set your schedule, and even see ratings and feedback from passengers. You can drive during peak hours to increase your earnings, and Uber also offers incentives like Surge Pricing to help you make more money.

Lyft

Similar to Uber, Lyft allows you to drive passengers to their desired locations. Lyft tends to offer a friendly and supportive environment for drivers, with many drivers citing the ease of use of the app and the company’s customer support as key benefits. As with Uber, you can earn more money by driving during high-demand periods and setting your hours according to your schedule.

2. Delivery Apps: Make Money by Running Errands

If driving passengers isn’t your thing, you can still use your phone to make money by delivering food and goods. Delivery apps like DoorDash, Postmates, and Uber Eats allow you to earn money by delivering meals from restaurants to customers.

DoorDash

DoorDash has become one of the top food delivery apps. You can start earning money right away by delivering food to people in your area. The app is user-friendly, and you have the flexibility to choose your hours. Additionally, DoorDash often provides opportunities for bonuses and promotions that can increase your earnings.

Uber Eats

Uber Eats is a great option for earning extra cash if you have a car, scooter, or even a bike. It’s another platform where you can earn by delivering food. With Uber Eats, you have the flexibility to work whenever you want, and you get paid weekly. You can also track your deliveries in real time and monitor your earnings directly through the app.

Postmates

Postmates offers delivery for a variety of items, including food, groceries, and even alcohol in some areas. The app’s versatility makes it an appealing option for those who want a broader range of delivery opportunities. With Postmates, you can earn extra cash on your own schedule, and the app often provides a range of promotions and bonuses.

3. Freelancing Apps: Offer Your Skills

Freelancing apps are another excellent way to make money using your phone. These platforms allow you to offer your services to a global audience, from graphic design to writing, coding, and virtual assistance.

Fiverr

Fiverr is one of the most popular freelancing platforms where you can list your services for as little as $5. Whether you offer design work, writing, social media management, or consulting, Fiverr allows you to connect with clients and get paid for your expertise. The app lets you create a professional profile, set your rates, and track your orders.

Upwork

Upwork connects freelancers with clients in need of various services, from tech and creative roles to customer support and marketing. Through the Upwork app, you can apply for gigs, track your time, and communicate with clients. While it may take some time to build a reputation, Upwork offers high-paying freelance opportunities once you have a solid portfolio.

Freelancer

Freelancer is another excellent platform for offering freelance services. You can create a profile, bid on projects, and receive payments all through the app. Whether you’re skilled in graphic design, programming, or content writing, Freelancer has a wide range of opportunities for different types of work.

4. Survey and Task Apps: Get Paid for Your Time

Many apps allow you to make money by completing surveys or small tasks. These apps are ideal if you’re looking to make extra money without any specialized skills. While the pay might not be high, it’s an easy and quick way to start earning cash.

Swagbucks

Swagbucks is one of the most well-known rewards apps. You can earn Swagbucks points by completing surveys, watching videos, shopping online, and more. These points can be redeemed for gift cards or PayPal cash. Swagbucks makes it easy to earn money in your free time by doing activities you’re already doing, like shopping or browsing the web.

InboxDollars

InboxDollars allows you to earn money by doing simple tasks such as reading emails, watching videos, or taking surveys. The app pays in cash rather than points, which can be a significant advantage for those who prefer direct payments. With a variety of tasks available, InboxDollars offers a flexible way to make money at your convenience.

Toluna

Toluna is another survey app that allows users to complete surveys and share their opinions on products and services. As you accumulate points, you can redeem them for rewards such as cash or gift cards. While the payout per survey isn’t significant, Toluna’s ease of use and frequent opportunities make it a popular app for making money in your spare time.

5. Selling and Reselling Apps: Turn Unused Items into Cash

Another fantastic way to make money using your phone is by selling items you no longer need or by reselling products for a profit. Platforms like eBay, Mercari, and Poshmark make it simple to list and sell your belongings.

eBay

eBay is a long-standing platform that allows you to sell both new and used items to a global audience. Whether you’re selling clothes, electronics, or collectibles, eBay makes it easy to upload photos, set prices, and manage transactions directly from your phone. It’s a great way to declutter your home while making money.

Mercari

Mercari is a popular marketplace app where you can sell new and used items, including clothing, electronics, and toys. The app allows you to easily list items for sale and manage your transactions from the comfort of your phone. Mercari provides a shipping label for easy shipping, and you can get paid via direct deposit.

Poshmark

Poshmark is a selling app geared primarily toward fashion and accessories. You can list your clothes, shoes, and accessories, and make money by selling them to buyers. Poshmark also allows users to shop for trendy items at discounted prices, and the app provides a built-in social aspect, where you can follow sellers and share listings to gain more visibility.

6. Investing Apps: Make Money While You Sleep

If you’re interested in long-term wealth-building, investing apps might be the perfect fit. These apps allow you to invest in stocks, bonds, and other assets directly from your phone.

Robinhood

Robinhood is a commission-free stock trading app that allows users to buy and sell stocks, ETFs, options, and even cryptocurrencies. The app is user-friendly, making it ideal for beginners who want to get into investing. Robinhood’s easy-to-use interface and access to various investment options have made it popular among those looking to invest using their phones.

Acorns

Acorns is an investment app that rounds up your everyday purchases and invests the spare change into a diversified portfolio. It’s an excellent choice for those who want to invest passively without needing to constantly monitor their portfolios. Acorns makes investing easy, and its “round-up” feature helps you save and invest small amounts over time.

Stash

Stash is another investment app that helps you get started with investing. It offers personalized investment plans based on your financial goals and risk tolerance. Stash makes it easy to invest in fractional shares of stocks, ETFs, and more, and provides educational resources to help you improve your financial knowledge.

7. Passive Income Apps: Earn Money While You Sleep

For those who prefer earning money with minimal active effort, passive income apps are the way to go. These apps allow you to generate income passively by doing things like renting out your space or earning royalties from content.

Airbnb

Airbnb allows you to rent out your property or spare room to travelers. If you have an extra space in your home, you can turn it into a passive income stream by listing it on Airbnb. The app helps you manage bookings, communicate with guests, and receive payments. It’s a great option if you have a space you’re not using but still want to generate income.

Turo

Turo is an app that lets you rent out your car to others when you’re not using it. Similar to Airbnb, Turo allows you to list your vehicle on the platform, and renters can book it for short-term use. It’s a fantastic way to make passive income if you have a car that’s sitting idle.

Honeygain

Honeygain is a unique passive income app that lets you earn money by sharing your internet connection. By allowing the app to use your unused bandwidth, you can earn credits that can be exchanged for cash. While the amount you earn is small, it’s a completely hands-off way to make money without putting in much effort.

Conclusion

Making money with your phone has never been easier. Whether you want to take advantage of ride-sharing, delivery services, freelancing, or passive income opportunities, there are plenty of apps that can help you turn your smartphone into a money-making machine. The key is to find what suits your lifestyle and interests best. With the right app, you can easily start earning extra cash in your spare time, and in some cases, even build a full-time income.
