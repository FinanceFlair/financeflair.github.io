Emerging Market Startups to Watch in 2024


In the dynamic landscape of global entrepreneurship, emerging markets have surged as hotbeds of innovation and growth. These regions, often characterized by rapid economic development, youthful populations, and increasing digital penetration, have become fertile grounds for startups aspiring to solve unique local challenges and sometimes reinvent entire industries. As we advance into 2024, several startups from emerging markets are poised not just to influence their local economies but to make significant global impacts.

Why Focus on Emerging Markets?

Emerging markets typically include countries in Latin America, Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, and parts of Eastern Europe. These economies offer a distinctive blend of opportunities and risks. Due to less saturated markets and an abundance of unmet needs, startups in these regions can quickly capture market share—particularly by leveraging technology to leapfrog traditional infrastructure deficiencies.

A few broad trends fuel this momentum:

  • Mobile-first innovations: The widespread adoption of smartphones allows startups to reach vast consumer bases previously outside the digital economy.
  • Financial inclusion efforts: Digital banking and fintech are pivotal for populations traditionally underserved by legacy financial institutions.
  • Sustainable and green technologies: Emerging economies face acute environmental challenges, creating demand for clean energy, waste management, and sustainable agriculture solutions.
  • Healthtech expansion: Limited healthcare infrastructure opens opportunities for telemedicine and AI-driven diagnostics.
  • E-commerce and logistics: Growing middle classes spur demand for better access to goods and services.

These forces set the stage for numerous startups to become market leaders and global influencers in 2024 and beyond.

Noteworthy Emerging Market Startups to Watch

1. Kudimoney (Nigeria) – Revolutionizing Access to Credit

Kudimoney has been making waves in Nigeria’s fintech industry by streamlining consumer lending. Focused on providing accessible microloans via mobile platforms, Kudimoney uses AI to underwrite loans instantly without collateral—a critical service in a country where formal credit access is scarce. Their approach addresses urgent liquidity needs for small business owners and everyday consumers, helping to stimulate economic activity.

In 2024, the company plans to enhance its AI models to better predict creditworthiness and reduce default risks, while expanding into neighboring countries across West Africa, poised to tap into a population of nearly 400 million people.

2. Agritech Innovations - AgroSmart (Brazil) – Translating Data into Farm Productivity

Brazil’s agricultural sector is monumental to the global food supply chain. AgroSmart is pioneering precision agriculture solutions by combining IoT sensors, satellite imaging, and localized data analytics to optimize crop yields. Small and medium farmers gain access to critical insights on soil health, weather patterns, and pest threats in real-time, helping them reduce costs and environmental impacts.

With the increasing pressure on sustainable farming methods, AgroSmart plans to integrate advanced climate resilience features into their platform in 2024 to support adaptation strategies for farmers dealing with volatile weather conditions.

3. Medix Health (India) – Democratizing Access to Quality Healthcare

Medix Health capitalizes on telemedicine and AI diagnostics to improve healthcare access in rural India. Leveraging inexpensive smartphones and regional vernacular interfaces, the startup offers consultation, health monitoring, and diagnostic support in areas where healthcare professionals are scarce.

2024 promises to be pivotal as Medix expands its AI symptom checker and integrates with local clinics to form a hybrid model, combining remote consultation with physical care delivery.

4. RecyGro (South Africa) – Innovating Waste Management with Circular Economy Models

Addressing the mounting waste management issues in urban South Africa, RecyGro offers an integrated app platform that connects households and businesses with recycling services. Incentivizing recycling through digital rewards and facilitating the resale of recyclable materials, they aim to build a circular economy that benefits both the environment and local communities.

The startup is gearing up for scaling partnerships with municipalities and corporations throughout 2024 to boost recycling rates and introduce new waste-to-energy solutions.

5. ShopEase (Southeast Asia) – Streamlining E-commerce for Small Retailers

ShopEase began as a simple mobile app helping small retailers digitize their storefronts across Southeast Asia. By offering inventory management, smart logistics, and customer engagement tools within a single platform, ShopEase empowers retailers to compete effectively with larger players.

As e-commerce penetration deepens in countries like Indonesia, Vietnam, and the Philippines, ShopEase plans to launch AI-powered recommendation engines and regional fulfillment centers in 2024 to reduce delivery times and improve customer retention efforts.

Emerging Market Startup Trends to Watch Closely

Digital Financial Services Become More Inclusive

Digital payment solutions, micro-loans, and insurtech platforms are addressing the financial exclusion that persists in many developing nations. Startups that harness big data and AI are carving pathways for customers who were once invisible to financial institutions.

For instance, AI underwriting is transforming credit scoring models by using alternative data sources like mobile usage and social behavior patterns. This enables lenders to offer tailored financial products with better risk management.

Sustainability Takes Center Stage

Entrepreneurial solutions centered around renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, water conservation, and waste management are increasingly critical. Emerging markets often face acute environmental deterioration and climate vulnerabilities, which in turn create demand for innovative green tech.

Startups working on affordable solar energy kits, biogas systems, or precision farming technologies are not only addressing local needs but potentially exporting scalable solutions worldwide.

Leveraging AI and Machine Learning

While AI adoption is often associated with developed markets, emerging market startups are leapfrogging traditional technologies by deploying machine learning across sectors like healthcare diagnostics, retail personalization, and supply chain optimization. These technologies enhance efficiency, cut costs, and widen service access.

As cloud infrastructure and regulatory environments mature, expect more startups to embed AI deeply into their core products.

Localization and Vernacular Interfaces

One principle essential for success in emerging markets is localization beyond language. Startups are customizing their interfaces, customer engagement, and product features to suit cultural preferences, literacy levels, and local behaviors.

This strategy reduces barriers to adoption and makes technology more inclusive. For example, voice-enabled applications and regional language support are becoming widespread components of user experience design.

Challenges Facing Emerging Market Startups

Despite the rapid growth, startups in these regions confront specific challenges including:

  • Infrastructure gaps: Unstable internet, limited electricity, and logistical hurdles continue to pose operational risks.
  • Regulatory uncertainty: Nascent regulatory frameworks, frequently changing policies, or bureaucratic complexities can inhibit scaling.
  • Talent shortages: Access to skilled engineers, data scientists, and business professionals remains constrained relative to demand.
  • Funding limitations: While venture capital interest has grown, early-stage startups often struggle to secure investment and scale sustainably.

However, rising governmental support, expanding accelerator programs, and increasing international interest are helping to mitigate these obstacles.

The Future Outlook for 2024 and Beyond

Emerging market startups are shaping up as pivotal forces in the global economy’s next chapter. Their innovations aren’t simply copies of Western models but often distinct, context-tailored solutions that address problems with ingenuity and resourcefulness. As digital infrastructure deepens and consumer expectations rise, startups that combine cultural insight with technological prowess will find immense opportunities.

Key sectors to monitor include fintech, agritech, healthtech, clean energy, and next-generation logistics. Moreover, cross-border regional expansion strategies and collaborations with global partners will accelerate growth pathways.

For investors, entrepreneurs, and ecosystem builders eyeing emerging markets in 2024, these startups embody the promise of not just financial returns but societal impact. The coming years will reveal which ventures scale from local disruptors to global titans—fueling economic inclusion and innovation on a massive scale.

In summary, 2024 stands out as a critical year for emerging market startups to solidify their footholds and chart new territory in innovation. From virtual lending platforms addressing credit gaps to agricultural data companies revolutionizing farming productivity, these startups embody the entrepreneurial energy driving global progress today. Watching them closely offers insights not only into commercial success but also the future of inclusive growth across the world’s most vibrant economies.
