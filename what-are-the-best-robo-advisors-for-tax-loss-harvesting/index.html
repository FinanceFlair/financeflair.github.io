What Are the Best Robo Advisors for Tax-Loss Harvesting?


Tax-loss harvesting is a powerful strategy that investors use to reduce their tax bills by selling investments that have declined in value to offset taxable gains elsewhere. While this technique can be a great way to manage your tax liability, it can also be time-consuming and complex. Fortunately, robo-advisors have made it easier to implement this strategy automatically, offering a way to take advantage of tax-loss harvesting without having to actively manage your portfolio. But with so many options available, how do you know which robo-advisors are best for tax-loss harvesting?

Understanding Tax-Loss Harvesting

Before we dive into the best robo-advisors, it’s essential to understand tax-loss harvesting and why it’s so important for investors. When you sell an asset at a loss, you can use that loss to offset gains you’ve made in other investments, thus lowering your taxable income. If you don’t have enough capital gains to offset, you can even apply the loss against up to $3,000 of ordinary income per year, with any excess losses carried forward to future years.

This strategy is especially valuable during market downturns, when many assets in a portfolio may have lost value. By selling at a loss and reinvesting in similar, but not identical, assets, you can maintain your portfolio’s exposure while minimizing your tax liability.

Why Choose Robo Advisors for Tax-Loss Harvesting?

Traditional investing often requires investors to manually track and manage tax-loss harvesting opportunities. For many people, this can become overwhelming, especially as portfolios grow larger and more complex. That’s where robo-advisors come in.

Robo-advisors are automated platforms that use algorithms to manage investments on your behalf. Many of these services include tax-loss harvesting as part of their offerings, using advanced software to monitor your portfolio and automatically sell off losing investments when it makes sense for your taxes. By automating this process, robo-advisors make tax-loss harvesting more accessible and efficient, even for those with minimal investment experience.

Key Factors to Consider in Choosing a Robo-Advisor for Tax-Loss Harvesting

Not all robo-advisors offer tax-loss harvesting, and those that do might not offer it in the same way. When evaluating which robo-advisor is right for you, consider the following factors:

  • Frequency of Tax-Loss Harvesting: Some robo-advisors perform tax-loss harvesting only once a year or when significant market shifts occur. Others may do it more frequently. If you’re looking to maximize your tax-saving potential, a platform that offers ongoing or even daily harvesting might be ideal.
  • Cost Structure: Many robo-advisors charge a flat fee or a percentage of assets under management (AUM). Some services that offer tax-loss harvesting may charge an additional fee for this feature, so it’s important to assess whether the benefits outweigh the costs.
  • Reinvestment Strategy: While tax-loss harvesting helps reduce taxes, reinvesting the proceeds efficiently is just as crucial. Some robo-advisors offer tax-efficient reinvestment options, like using exchange-traded funds (ETFs) that minimize capital gains distributions.
  • User Experience: Even though tax-loss harvesting is automated, it’s still essential that the platform is easy to use and navigate. Look for a robo-advisor that offers an intuitive interface, comprehensive tax reporting, and strong customer service.
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting Algorithms: The effectiveness of tax-loss harvesting depends on the algorithms the robo-advisor uses. More advanced algorithms will automatically monitor your portfolio for tax-loss harvesting opportunities, optimizing your tax strategy without manual intervention.

Top Robo-Advisors for Tax-Loss Harvesting

Now that we’ve covered what to look for in a robo-advisor for tax-loss harvesting, let’s explore some of the best options available. These platforms offer tax-loss harvesting as part of their service, using sophisticated algorithms to help investors reduce their tax liabilities.

1. Betterment

Betterment is one of the most well-known robo-advisors, and for good reason. It offers tax-loss harvesting as part of its premium plan, with the potential to significantly reduce your tax bill. Betterment’s tax-loss harvesting service is automatic and works year-round, so you’re constantly on the lookout for tax-saving opportunities.

Betterment’s algorithms take into account both short-term and long-term capital gains, ensuring that you harvest losses in the most tax-efficient way possible. The platform also uses “tax-sensitive asset location,” which means that it places tax-inefficient investments, like bonds, into tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs to minimize taxable income.

Betterment’s fees are reasonable, with a 0.25% annual fee for the Digital plan and a 0.40% annual fee for the Premium plan. Although tax-loss harvesting is available only in the Premium plan, it’s worth the investment for those with larger portfolios who want to take advantage of this strategy year-round.

2. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is another top-tier robo-advisor that offers tax-loss harvesting. Like Betterment, Wealthfront provides this service automatically, ensuring that your portfolio is always optimized for tax savings. Wealthfront’s tax-loss harvesting is available on all plans, including the lower-cost Digital plan, which charges a 0.25% annual fee.

Wealthfront’s tax-loss harvesting is more frequent than some competitors, with the service working throughout the year to identify opportunities for reducing your tax burden. In addition, Wealthfront utilizes a unique feature called “direct indexing,” where individual stocks, instead of ETFs, are used to build portfolios. This allows for greater flexibility in harvesting losses on a per-stock basis.

Wealthfront also provides tax-efficient asset location, ensuring that tax-inefficient investments are placed in tax-advantaged accounts, such as IRAs, to help reduce your tax exposure. This approach is designed to maximize your portfolio’s overall tax efficiency, especially for those with larger and more diversified investments.

3. Taxable Wealth Management by Personal Capital

Personal Capital is an all-in-one financial planning and wealth management platform that integrates robo-advisor services with human financial advice. Although Personal Capital is primarily known for its financial planning tools, it also offers tax-loss harvesting through its investment advisory services.

Personal Capital offers personalized financial planning alongside its tax-loss harvesting services. The robo-advisor works year-round to monitor for tax-loss harvesting opportunities and automatically make adjustments to your portfolio. However, this service comes with a higher fee than many of its competitors. The fees start at 0.89% for portfolios under $1 million and decrease with larger portfolios.

While Personal Capital’s fees may be higher than others, it provides an added benefit of having access to financial advisors who can assist with more complex tax situations, making it an ideal option for those who want both automation and expert advice.

4. SoFi Invest

SoFi Invest is a robo-advisor platform that offers both automated investing and tax-loss harvesting services. The platform charges no management fees for automated investing, making it one of the most affordable options available. However, it should be noted that tax-loss harvesting is available only on SoFi’s Active Investing platform, which requires a minimum balance of $100.

The tax-loss harvesting strategy on SoFi Invest works similarly to the others, automatically scanning your portfolio for tax-saving opportunities. SoFi Invest also provides a simple and easy-to-navigate platform, making it ideal for newer investors who want a hands-off approach to tax-loss harvesting.

While the lack of fees for managing investments makes SoFi Invest an attractive choice, the minimum balance requirement for tax-loss harvesting and the limited customization options make it a better fit for smaller investors who may not need as many advanced features.

5. Ellevest

Ellevest is a robo-advisor designed specifically for women, though its services are available to everyone. It offers tax-loss harvesting as part of its premium plan, which includes personalized financial advice and goal-based planning. The tax-loss harvesting service works year-round, ensuring that your portfolio is always optimized for tax savings.

Ellevest stands out by offering personalized portfolios designed around individual goals, such as retirement, buying a home, or starting a business. This approach can help ensure that tax-loss harvesting is aligned with your overall financial plan. Ellevest’s fees are reasonable, with a 0.25% annual fee for the Digital plan and 0.50% for the Premium plan.

Conclusion

Tax-loss harvesting is a powerful way to minimize your tax bill, but it can be complex and time-consuming to manage on your own. Robo-advisors make this process simpler by automating tax-loss harvesting and optimizing your portfolio for tax savings. Whether you’re looking for frequent tax-loss harvesting, a user-friendly interface, or access to financial advisors, there’s a robo-advisor that suits your needs.

Betterment, Wealthfront, Personal Capital, SoFi Invest, and Ellevest are among the best options for tax-loss harvesting, each offering unique features that cater to different types of investors. Before you choose a robo-advisor, make sure to assess your investment goals, fees, and the level of automation you want. With the right platform, tax-loss harvesting can be a great tool for improving your long-term financial success.
