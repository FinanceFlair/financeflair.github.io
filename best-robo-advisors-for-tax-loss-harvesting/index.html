Best Robo-Advisors for Tax Loss Harvesting


In the evolving world of personal finance, robo-advisors have become a game-changer for investors looking to build wealth with minimal hassle. Among their many features, tax loss harvesting stands out as a sophisticated tool that can help investors reduce their tax burdens while optimizing portfolio performance. But with so many robo-advisors available, which ones provide the best tax loss harvesting capabilities? This guide dives deep into the top robo-advisors offering tax loss harvesting, exploring their features, benefits, limitations, and how they can fit into your investment strategy.

Understanding Tax Loss Harvesting

Before diving into specific robo-advisors, it’s essential to grasp the concept of tax loss harvesting. This strategy involves selling securities at a loss to offset gains realized elsewhere in the portfolio, reducing the investor’s taxable income for the year. The harvested losses can offset capital gains and, to a limited extent, ordinary income. Crucially, the loss can be used to offset up to $3,000 of ordinary income per year, with any excess losses carried forward indefinitely.

This process is advantageous because it helps investors improve after-tax returns without taking extra investment risk. While tax loss harvesting has traditionally been a service accessible only through financial advisors, many robo-advisors now offer automated tax loss harvesting, democratizing this benefit for everyday investors.

What Makes Robo-Advisors Ideal for Tax Loss Harvesting?

Robo-advisors use algorithms and automation to manage investments efficiently and at low cost. Their technology continuously monitors client portfolios throughout the day, identifying opportunities for tax loss harvesting as market conditions fluctuate. This automated oversight ensures investors don’t miss out on potential tax savings that could be overlooked in a manually managed portfolio.

Key advantages robo-advisors provide in the context of tax loss harvesting include:

  • Continuous Monitoring: Unlike traditional advisors who may review portfolios quarterly or annually, robo-advisors can execute tax loss harvesting multiple times a day.
  • Automation: The process is seamless and requires no effort from the investor.
  • Low Fees: Robo-advisors typically charge lower management fees compared to full-service financial advisors, making tax-efficient investing more cost-effective.
  • Diversification and Rebalancing: By swapping out securities sold for losses with similar investments, robo-advisors maintain appropriate diversification while adhering to IRS wash sale rules.

With these factors in mind, here is a breakdown of some of the best robo-advisors offering tax loss harvesting.

1. Betterment

Overview: Betterment is often hailed as a pioneer in robo-advisory services and was among the first to offer automated tax loss harvesting. Its platform is user-friendly, making it well-suited for both beginners and seasoned investors.

Tax Loss Harvesting Features

Betterment offers daily tax loss harvesting on accounts with a minimum balance of $50,000 in its Premium service, while the basic Digital plan includes tax loss harvesting for taxable accounts. The service is automatic and integrated directly into portfolio management, meaning tax loss opportunities are identified promptly and executed without investor intervention.

  • Daily Harvesting: Betterment evaluates portfolios daily, capturing losses more efficiently than quarterly reviews.
  • Smart Replacements: When realizing a loss, Betterment replaces the sold security with a correlated ETF to avoid wash sales while maintaining market exposure.
  • Tax Coordinated Portfolio: This option considers tax implications across multiple accounts, including IRAs and taxable accounts, for more comprehensive optimization.

Advantages

  • No extra fees for tax loss harvesting—it’s included with the management fee.
  • Seamlessly integrated into portfolio management.
  • Strong tax optimization protocols, including tax-efficient withdrawals.

Considerations

  • Tax loss harvesting is only available in taxable accounts.
  • The $50,000 requirement may be a barrier for some investors wanting Premium services.

2. Wealthfront

Overview: Wealthfront is another industry leader, recognized for its robust technology and comprehensive financial planning tools. Wealthfront’s tax loss harvesting is one of its standout features.

Tax Loss Harvesting Features

Wealthfront provides daily tax loss harvesting on all taxable accounts with a minimum balance of $500, which makes this feature accessible to a wide range of investors.

  • Daily Harvesting: Like Betterment, Wealthfront assesses portfolios daily for opportunities.
  • Direct Indexing: For accounts above $100,000, Wealthfront offers Stock-Level Tax Loss Harvesting, a more advanced version that involves direct indexing, owning individual securities to maximize harvesting opportunities.
  • Automated Implementation: Trades are executed automatically without client input.

Advantages

  • Low minimum balance requirement for basic tax loss harvesting.
  • Stock-level harvesting for sophisticated tax optimization on larger accounts.
  • Also provides active tax-advantaged advice and financial planning tools.

Considerations

  • Direct indexing feature requires $100,000 minimum, which may exclude smaller investors.
  • Some users report limited customization in portfolio options.

3. M1 Finance

Overview: M1 Finance combines elements of robo-advisors and traditional brokerage accounts, offering investors more control over portfolio customization while automating core investment management features.

Tax Loss Harvesting Features

Unlike Betterment or Wealthfront, M1 Finance’s tax loss harvesting is not fully automated but can be executed manually through its interface. More recently, M1 introduced M1 Plus, which includes dynamic rebalancing and some tax-savvy features aimed at optimization, but automated tax loss harvesting remains limited.

Advantages

  • Greater portfolio customization.
  • No management fee for the basic service.
  • Tax-efficient investing can be managed with user engagement.

Considerations

  • Absence of fully automated tax loss harvesting.
  • Requires users to monitor and trigger losses themselves, potentially limiting efficiency.

4. SoFi Automated Investing

Overview: SoFi offers automated investing with a low-cost structure and additional financial perks like free financial advising to clients.

Tax Loss Harvesting Features

SoFi provides tax loss harvesting on taxable accounts automatically, with no minimum investment requirement, making it accessible for new investors.

  • Automated Process: Tax loss harvesting is integrated seamlessly.
  • Daily Opportunity Identification: Similar to industry leaders, SoFi monitors opportunities frequently.

Advantages

  • No minimum balance requirements.
  • Free financial advice adds value beyond robo-advisory features.
  • Competitive fees (0.25% management fee).

Considerations

  • Tax loss harvesting is not available for retirement accounts.
  • Smaller investment options may limit the impact of tax loss harvesting.

5. Schwab Intelligent Portfolios

Overview: Schwab Intelligent Portfolios is Charles Schwab’s robo-advisor solution that integrates automated investing with Schwab’s broad range of brokerage services.

Tax Loss Harvesting Features

Schwab does not currently offer automated tax loss harvesting in its Intelligent Portfolios product. However, the platform provides tax-efficient portfolio construction and rebalancing and offers tax loss harvesting on a manual basis through its broader brokerage account services.

Advantages

  • No advisory fees.
  • Access to extensive Schwab investment products.
  • Tax-efficient ETFs selected to minimize capital gains.

Considerations

  • Automated tax loss harvesting absent.
  • Manual harvesting requires investor involvement or advisor assistance.

Maximizing the Benefits of Tax Loss Harvesting with Robo-Advisors

To truly harness the power of tax loss harvesting, investors should consider a holistic approach beyond just selecting a robo-advisor with this feature:

1. Ensure Proper Account Setup

Tax loss harvesting only applies to taxable accounts. Make sure you’re investing in the right accounts that will allow you to benefit from tax loss strategies.

2. Understand Wash Sale Rules

The IRS wash sale rule disallows claiming a loss if you buy the “same or substantially identical” security within 30 days before or after the sale. Robo-advisors use algorithms to substitute investments appropriately and avoid wash sales, but understanding this rule helps you grasp how the system works.

3. Monitor Portfolio Contributions and Withdrawals

Frequent deposits and withdrawals can affect tax loss harvesting efficiency. Consistency in contributions lets the robo-advisor manage the portfolio optimally over the tax year.

4. Review Tax Documents Carefully

Annual tax documents like Form 1099-B include harvested loss details. These can be complex; ensure your tax preparer understands how to apply these to your tax return.

5. Combine With Other Tax Strategies

Tax loss harvesting works best when incorporated with tax asset location strategies, charitable giving, and long-term tax planning for maximum benefit.

Who Should Use Tax Loss Harvesting Robo-Advisors?

While tax loss harvesting can be beneficial for many investors, it is particularly advantageous for:

  • Taxable Investors: Anyone investing in taxable brokerage accounts who realizes capital gains or has significant unrealized losses.
  • High-Income Earners: Those in higher tax brackets can benefit more from reducing taxable income through capital loss harvesting.
  • Active Investors: Investors who frequently add or withdraw funds and want automated help managing taxable events.
  • Long-Term Investors: Individuals holding investments over multiple years who can harvest losses across market cycles.

If you frequently invest in tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs or 401(k)s exclusively, tax loss harvesting won’t provide benefits, as these accounts do not tax capital gains or dividends.
