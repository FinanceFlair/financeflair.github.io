It’s a departure from traditional methods that focus solely on asset allocation or market capitalization. Factor-based investing revolves around the idea that certain factors drive stock performance over the long term. These factors can include: Value: Stocks that are undervalued relative to their intrinsic worth. Size: Companies with smaller market capitalizations that have historically outperformed larger ones. Momentum: Stocks that have exhibited strong recent performance tend to continue performing well. Quality: Companies with strong balance sheets, stable earnings, and low debt levels. Volatility: Stocks with lower volatility may provide better risk-adjusted returns. Each factor represents a different investment thesis and can perform differently under various market conditions.
