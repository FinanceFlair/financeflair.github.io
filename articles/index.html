In this comprehensive guide, we’ll delve into the dynamics of market bubbles, explore common characteristics, and provide strategies for investors to avoid falling prey to these dangerous traps. A market bubble occurs when asset prices become detached from their underlying intrinsic value, driven primarily by speculative trading and irrational exuberance. Bubbles can manifest in various asset classes, including stocks, real estate, commodities, and cryptocurrencies. During a bubble, prices skyrocket to unsustainable levels, driven by a feedback loop of positive sentiment, fear of missing out (FOMO), and speculative buying.
