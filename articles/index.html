The Power of Prospect Theory in Shaping Investment Decisions

When it comes to making investment decisions, the human mind often operates in fascinating and sometimes unpredictable ways. The field of behavioral economics has shed light on the various cognitive biases and psychological factors that influence how individuals perceive risk and make choices, particularly in the realm of investment. One of the most influential theories in this regard is Prospect Theory, proposed by Daniel Kahneman and Amos Tversky in 1979. This groundbreaking theory has significantly impacted the understanding of investment behavior and has practical implications for investors and financial professionals alike. At the core of Prospect Theory is the recognition that individuals do not always make decisions based on expected utility theory, which assumes that people are rational and always strive to maximize their gains while minimizing their losses. Instead, Prospect Theory posits that individuals evaluate potential outcomes relative to a reference point, often their current wealth or a perceived baseline.
