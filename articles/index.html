Cognitive Biases and Their Impact on Financial Choices

Cognitive biases are systematic patterns of deviation from rationality in judgment, whereby individuals create their own “subjective reality” based on their perception of the world around them. These biases can significantly influence decision-making, especially when it comes to financial choices. Understanding how cognitive biases affect our financial decisions is crucial for achieving financial stability and success. Anchoring Bias Anchoring bias is the tendency to rely too heavily on the first piece of information encountered (the “anchor”) when making decisions. In the context of finances, this bias can lead individuals to fixate on initial information, such as the original price of a stock, and make subsequent decisions based on this anchor rather than current market conditions.
