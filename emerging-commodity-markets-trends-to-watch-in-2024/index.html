Emerging Commodity Markets: Trends to Watch in 2024


The world of commodity markets is a vast and diverse arena, with numerous markets opening up and closing down across the globe. As the world's demand for raw materials and natural resources continues to grow, the commodity markets are adapting to meet this increased demand. In this blog post, we will explore the emerging commodity markets that are expected to trend in 2024 and the factors driving these changes. Renewable Energy Commodities: Solar and Wind Power The renewable energy sector is set to continue its upward trajectory in 2024, driven by the increasing demand for clean energy and decreasing costs of production. Solar and wind power are leading the charge, with solar panel installations expected to increase by 15% globally and wind energy capacity set to grow by 12%. As the world transitions to cleaner energy sources, the demand for solar panels, wind turbines, and other renewable energy equipment is expected to drive growth in the project finance and infrastructure sectors. Market Trends

Solar panel prices are expected to decrease by 5-7% in 2024, making renewable energy more competitive with fossil fuels. The growth of battery storage technology will continue to improve the efficiency and flexibility of renewable energy systems. New technologies, such as smart inverters and energy storage systems, are expected to emerge and improve the performance of renewable energy systems.

Food and Nutrition Commodities: Sustainable Practices With the growing global population and increasing awareness of food security, the demand for sustainable food production and processing is on the rise. This demand is driving growth in the commodity markets for organic and specialty crops, as well as sustainable animal products. The increasing focus on food safety and nutrition is also driving demand for healthy and nutritious products. Market Trends

The demand for organic and specialty crops is expected to grow by 10% in 2024, driven by consumer preference and increasing awareness of the environmental and health benefits of sustainable farming practices. The use of precision agriculture and technology is becoming more prevalent, allowing for more efficient and sustainable use of resources. Companies are investing in sustainable supply chain management to ensure transparency and accountability in their operations.

Basic Materials and Mining Commodities: Diversification and Efficiency The commodity markets for basic materials and mining commodities continue to evolve as companies seek to diversify their operations and improve efficiency. The development of new mining technologies and companies is driving growth in the sector. Industry Trends

The global mining industry is expected to grow by 6% in 2024, driven by growing demand for raw materials. Companies are investing in technology to improve efficiency and reduce costs in the mining sector. The development of new mining technologies, such as autonomous vehicles and remote control operations, is enabling safer and more efficient mining practices.

China's Influence on Global Commodities Markets China's economic footprint continues to have a significant impact on global commodity markets. The country's increasing consumption of commodities, such as copper, iron ore, and coal, drives growth in the commodity markets. China's production of commodities, such as electronics and vehicles, also influences the demand for raw materials. Global Impact

China's economic growth is expected to slow in 2024, potentially leading to decreased demand for commodities. The Chinese government's efforts to reduce pollution and emissions may lead to increased investment in cleaner energy and sustainable technologies. The impact of the China-US trade agreement on commodity markets will continue to be significant.

Recycling and Circular Economy The trend towards a circular economy is driving growth in the recycling industry and demand for recyclable materials. The global recycling industry is expected to grow by 10% in 2024, driven by consumer awareness of the importance of sustainability. Market Developments

Recycling companies are investing in new technologies to improve efficiency and reduce costs. Governments are implementing policies and regulations to support the growth of the recycling industry. New business models, such as collaboration and sharing of resources, are emerging in the recycling industry.

Key Players in Emerging Commodities Markets Several key players in the commodity markets are driving growth and trends. Companies such as Vestas, Siemens, and ABB are investing in the renewable energy sector, while companies such as General Mills, Unilever, and Nestle are driving growth in the food and nutrition sector.

Vestas is investing in wind energy technology and expanding its product portfolio. Siemens is investing in the electrification of transportation and providing innovative energy storage solutions. ABB is providing products and solutions for the renewable energy sector, including smart grid technologies.

Conclusion The emerging commodity markets are a rapidly changing arena, driven by trends such as the growth of renewable energy, sustainable practices in the food and nutrition sector, and diversification in the basic materials and mining sector. The impact of China's economy, the growth of recycling, and the rise of new players in the market will continue to shape the commodity markets in 2024.
