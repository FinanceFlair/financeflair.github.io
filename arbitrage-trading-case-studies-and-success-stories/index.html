Arbitrage Trading: Case Studies and Success Stories


Arbitrage trading is a strategy that profited from price discrepancies in the financial markets before they were reconciled by market forces. This strategy has been used by investors and traders for centuries, from the stock market to cryptocurrencies. Particularly, it involves simultaneously purchasing an asset on one market and selling it on another market, profiting from the temporary price discrepancies. Like any other strategy, arbitrage trading has its own success stories and lessons that we can learn from. In this blog post, we will explore different types of arbitrage trading and share some captivating case studies and success stories.

Types of Arbitrage Trading:

There are several types of arbitrage trading, however, some of the most common include:

  1. Statistical arbitrage: It’s a trading strategy that uses mathematical models and algorithms to spot price inefficiencies in the market.

  2. Index arbitrage: It involves buying the stocks of a well-diversified index and simultaneously selling the related index futures, when the inefficiency arises.

  3. Limit order book arbitrage: It focuses on exploiting temporary discrepancies in the bid-ask spread of a security.

  4. Crypto arbitrage: It’s when one buys a cryptocurrency on one exchange and sells it on another exchange, profiting from the price fluctuations.

Arbitrage Trading Case Studies:

1. Numonyx Limited

In 2004, Numonyx Limited saw an opportunity to purchase the bankrupt assets of two other companies for below their market value. When combined, these assets could have fetched a higher price than the market value of Numonyx Limited. The clever move led to a profit, showcasing the effect of pure price arbitrage.

2. Dr. Robots Inc.

In this case, a small robotics company, Dr. Robots Inc., was trading 5% higher on a certain platform as compared to another trading platform. SMEs neighbor and a number of their extended network were customers of the said platform. Hence, they used this opportunity to buy Dr. Robots Inc. shares on the cheaper platform, reselling them on the platform with an increased price. This allowed them to quickly and easily create profits.

3. Arbitrage Trading in Cryptocurrencies

Crypto arbitrage trading is prevalent and made headlines. In 2018, when Bitcoin futures were listed on the CBOE and CME exchanges, there was often a price discrepancy with Bitcoin on other exchanges. Skilled traders used this to their advantage, profiting from such discrepancies.

Lessons Learned:

As seen in these profitable arbitrage trades, numerous lessons can be taken into consideration:

  • Algorithmic and quantitative trading strategies took a back seat in the market after the 2008 financial crisis and have since regained a considerable market share.
  • Diversifying portfolios across several markets can lead to profits.
  • Arbitrage trading has lowered the barriers of entry for smaller investors and democratized the trading process.

Arbitrage may not last long, but traders who are quick to seize the opportunity and use these strategies efficiently have made fortunes. Arbitrage trading in any market product is all about timing and seizing key market opportunities. Remember to always keep your eyes open for price discrepancies, as these opportunities may arise unexpectedly.

In conclusion, the example cases and success stories are fascinating, undoubtedly proving that arbitrage trading is a crucial part of the trader’s toolkit. With the world becoming increasingly digitalized, it opens more opportunities for traders to earn profits by exploiting digital asset market price discrepancies. Don’t hold back and get started with your first arbitrage trade, who knows you might end up as the next success story.
