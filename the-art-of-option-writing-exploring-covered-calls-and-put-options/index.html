The Art of Option Writing: Exploring Covered Calls and PUT Options


Welcome to a captivating and comprehensive guide to one of the most mysterious yet profitable realms of trading: options. If you’re still wondering how it all works, don’t worry. We’ve prepared this blog post for curious minds like yours to peel back the layers of the enigmatic world of options, particularly focusing on two main strategies: covered calls and PUT options.

Covered Calls: A Beginner’s Trick

Covered calls are essentially a two-in-one trade where an investor simultaneously owns the underlying stock and sells a “call option” for that stock to another trader. In return, the investor is permitted the potential to execute a call to buy the same stock – except, at a predetermined price, known as the “strike price.”

The temptation to entice other investors to sell their stocks to you can be rather attractive for selling high and profiting from a bullish market. When the strike price of the call is at or below the current market price of your stock, you snatch the opportunity to buy the stock cheaply. Thus, you pocket a profit from the spread of the higher selling price and the lower buying price.

PUT Options: The Bear’s Territory

On the other side of the option block, we have PUT options. These acts are a bit like the opposite twins of covered calls. Instead of selling your right to buy a stock to another investor, you sell your right to sell the stock. Here, you essentially agree to sell your stock at a given strike price within a specific time frame, regardless of whether the market sentiment is bearish or bullish.

PUT options flourish as the investor aims to capitalize on their expectation that the stock price will drop below the strike price. When that happens, they complete the contract and sell the stock at the predetermined, more favorable, high price. Investors assume these contracts in anticipation of a bearish market or a decline in stock prices.

The Formula for Profits in Options

For covered call writers, the rise in stock price is their crutch for leeching profits. If the stock price zooms higher than the strike price before the option expires, the call is exercised, and the seller is obligated to buy the stock.

However, in the case of PUT option holders, the fortune smiles upon them when the stock price crumbles lower than their strike price. In this screeching bear market, the trader can rock their capital gains from the sale of the stock.

To improve your options game, keep these principles in mind:

  1. Timing is everything: Keep a hawkish eye on market trends because options are most lucrative when paired with your anticipation of natural market movements.

  2. Strike the right balance: Don’t be timid or overly gutsy with your strike prices. Experimenting with varying strike prices will help you understand their relationship with market fluctuations.

  3. Choose your battles: Not every stock is option-friendly. Familiarize yourself with the stock options of companies you’ve thoroughly researched to find the ones with the most enticing potentials.

  4. The clock’s ticking: Options have an expiration date. Checking the time-box on your trades ensures you don’t get caught stagnant in an otherwise profitable trade.

  5. Stirring the pot: Be cautious while mixing derivatives such as options into your general trading strategy. Understand your limits and overall risk tolerance.

Conclusion

Narrowing down the art of option writing to just covered calls and PUT options is like choosing between.magnitude and latitude when discussing measurements. The world of options is shockingly vast and mind-boggling. Nevertheless, from this modest start, we hope you have gained the courage to dive into the deep end of the financial pool, equipped with the skills to explore, experiment, and ultimately excel in these binary-based trades.

Trading options can become a cat-and-mouse game, with you as both hunter and prey. Remember to stay in the loop by reading and learning continuously. As you wade through the seas of strike prices and expiration dates, may you find the conviction to craft your signature trading approach. Happy trading!
