Valuation Strategies for Fashion and Apparel Brands


Fashion and apparel brands typically face fierce competition in the market, which makes accurate and effective valuation a crucial aspect to maintain their position and growth. Valuation is an essential element in making strategic decisions related to business acquisition, merger, pricing, among others. In this blog, we explore some valuable strategies used in assessing and valuing fashion and apparel brands.

  1. Revenue-based Valuation

Revenue-based valuation focuses on a company’s potential revenue generation ability. Fashion and apparel brands that have a robust revenue-generating model but may lack other financial metrics could benefit from this valuation strategy. Revenue-based valuation is popular among investors and lenders since it is relatively easy to calculate and understand. Simply multiply your company’s expected annual revenues by a specific revenue multiple factor that reflects the perceived risk and growth potential. For instance, if an apparel brand expects $10 million in revenues next year and the industry multiple factor is 2, the brand’s valuation is $20 million.

  1. Market-based Valuation

Market-based valuation is the most common strategy for assessing the value of fashion and apparel brands. It considers factors such as brand reputation, customer loyalty, target audience, market share, and unique selling proposition (USP). A brand with high market popularity, creative designs, or a strong following may have a higher valuation than another with similar revenue or financial metrics. Market-based valuation often employs comparable sales (or comps) and comparable company analysis (or CCA) methods.

  1. Profit-based Valuation

Profit-based valuation focuses on a company’s past and future profits. Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (EBITDA) and Earnings before all expenses (EBAE) are the most commonly used profit-based valuation measurements. Fashion and apparel brands with stable cash flow and profits typically benefit from this strategy. Profit-based valuation methods can be comprehensive and provide a more accurate valuation of a company, considering various financial metrics. However, it’s essential to stress-test the growth forecasts and consider potential risks that may impact profitability.

  1. Digital Footprint Valuation

Digital footprint valuation is an increasingly popular strategy that considers the value of assets and engagement metrics on social media, e-commerce presence, domain names, and intellectual property. Nowadays, a robust digital strategy can significantly contribute to a fashion or apparel brand’s value. The more followers a brand has on social media, the higher the engagement rate, and the broader its customer reach (both online and offline), the more valuable the brand tends to be.

  1. Brand Frontier Valuation

Fashion and apparel brands with high growth potential can implement brand frontier valuation. This strategy considers the value of the brand in the context of future opportunities, including product extensions, geographies, and channels. Companies that have a strong likelihood of tapping into growing markets, expanding their customer base, or innovating their offerings could benefit from this method. Threadbare’s brand frontier valuation assessment methodology seamlessly assesses such opportunities and delivers the most comprehensive valuation for brands.

In summary, thoroughly assessing and valuing a fashion and apparel brand requires a variety of valuation strategies. Each method provides different insights, and combining them delivers the most accurate and comprehensive valuation. Fashion and apparel brands must focus on strategies that align with their growth, financial, and market objectives. As a result, brands with high valuation potential will be more attractive to investors, lenders, and other potential partners.
