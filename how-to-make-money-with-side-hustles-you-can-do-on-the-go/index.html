How to Make Money with Side Hustles You Can Do on the Go


In today’s fast-paced world, more and more people are looking for ways to earn extra income without being tied down to a traditional 9-to-5 job. Whether you’re looking to make some extra cash in your spare time or you’re interested in creating a flexible income stream that allows you to work from anywhere, side hustles that can be done on the go are becoming increasingly popular.

Side hustles on the go offer flexibility, autonomy, and the opportunity to tap into a wide range of skills and services. In this post, we’ll explore various side hustles you can do from virtually anywhere. Whether you’re on the train, waiting in line, or relaxing at a café, these side hustles allow you to generate income without a fixed office space or rigid schedule.

1. Freelance Writing

One of the most popular and accessible side hustles is freelance writing. With a laptop or even a smartphone, you can write articles, blog posts, product descriptions, and more for clients around the world. Writing is something that can be done on the go, whether you’re traveling or at home, making it an ideal side hustle for those who enjoy putting their thoughts into words.

To get started as a freelance writer, you’ll need a few things:

  • Writing Samples: Start by creating some writing samples that showcase your skills. You can write on platforms like Medium or start a blog to build a portfolio.
  • Freelance Platforms: Websites like Upwork, Fiverr, and Freelancer can help connect you with clients looking for writers. As you gain experience, you can also reach out to clients directly.
  • Time Management: Freelance writing may involve deadlines, so it’s essential to stay organized and manage your time efficiently. Many writers work in short bursts during travel, while waiting, or in other downtime moments.

Freelance writing offers the flexibility to choose projects that interest you and fit your schedule, making it a great side hustle for anyone who loves to write.

2. Virtual Assistant Work

With the rise of digital businesses, many entrepreneurs and business owners are outsourcing administrative tasks to virtual assistants (VAs). Virtual assistant work includes tasks like managing emails, scheduling meetings, social media management, data entry, and customer service. It’s a side hustle you can do from anywhere, as long as you have an internet connection and a device to work from.

Here’s how to get started as a virtual assistant:

  • Skills and Tools: Basic skills like communication, organization, and time management are essential. You may also need to familiarize yourself with tools such as Google Suite, Slack, Zoom, or project management software like Trello.
  • Find Clients: Websites like Upwork, Fiverr, and Belay offer platforms where you can find virtual assistant gigs. Alternatively, you can network within communities of entrepreneurs who need administrative help.
  • Offer a Niche: Specializing in a specific industry or task (e.g., social media management or real estate support) can make you stand out in a competitive market.

Being a virtual assistant is a great side hustle if you’re highly organized, tech-savvy, and have a knack for multitasking. Plus, it’s one of the easiest hustles to scale as you gain clients and experience.

3. Selling Handmade Goods

If you’re crafty or have an artistic flair, selling handmade goods can be a rewarding side hustle that you can manage on the go. Whether it’s jewelry, knitted items, custom art, or home décor, there is a market for unique, handmade products.

To start selling handmade goods:

  • Create a Portfolio: Build a portfolio of your creations to showcase online. You can use platforms like Etsy, eBay, or Instagram to display your products.
  • Use Social Media: Promote your work on social media platforms to grow your following. Instagram, Pinterest, and TikTok are great for visually appealing products.
  • Shipping and Inventory: Consider shipping logistics when you’re on the go. Partner with fulfillment services that handle storage and delivery, or focus on smaller items that are easy to manage from anywhere.

Selling handmade goods allows you to turn your hobbies into a profitable business while offering the freedom to work from anywhere. It also gives you the opportunity to be creative and bring your ideas to life.

4. Online Tutoring

Online tutoring is an excellent side hustle for individuals with expertise in certain subjects or skills. Whether it’s helping students with their homework, teaching English as a second language, or coaching professionals on specific skills, tutoring can be done from virtually anywhere with an internet connection.

Getting started as an online tutor:

  • Select Your Niche: Are you great at math, language arts, or science? Perhaps you can teach a language or a specific skill such as coding. Narrowing down your niche helps attract the right audience.
  • Choose a Platform: Platforms like VIPKid, Chegg Tutors, and Tutor.com allow you to find students in need of help. Alternatively, you can offer tutoring services through your own website or social media.
  • Set Your Hours: Tutoring is a flexible side hustle that allows you to set your own hours. Whether you’re tutoring in the morning or in the evening, you can tailor your work schedule to fit your lifestyle.

Online tutoring is a fantastic way to earn money on the go while helping others. Plus, it can be an incredibly fulfilling job if you enjoy teaching and mentoring.

5. Photography

If you have an eye for photography, you can monetize your skills while on the go. Whether you’re capturing stunning landscapes, portraits, or everyday moments, photography can be a highly profitable side hustle. There are multiple ways to turn your passion for photography into an income stream.

Here’s how to start:

  • Sell Stock Photos: Upload your photos to stock photography websites like Shutterstock, Adobe Stock, or Getty Images. You earn royalties every time someone downloads one of your photos.
  • Offer Photography Services: You can also offer local photography services for events such as weddings, parties, or family portraits. This can be done during your free time, and the equipment can be easily transported.
  • Social Media: Build an online presence by showcasing your photography on platforms like Instagram. Over time, you can attract clients who want to book photoshoots.

Photography is a great side hustle because it offers creative freedom and the potential to make money from a hobby. With portable cameras or even smartphones, you can snap photos wherever you go and sell them online.

6. Social Media Management

As businesses grow, many need help managing their social media presence. If you’re skilled at content creation, strategy, and audience engagement, social media management can be a lucrative side hustle. The best part? You can manage multiple clients remotely, making it ideal for someone constantly on the move.

To succeed as a social media manager:

  • Understand Social Platforms: Be familiar with the ins and outs of popular platforms like Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn. Each platform has its own strategy, so understanding these nuances will help you manage accounts effectively.
  • Content Creation: You may need to create content (posts, images, and videos) for your clients. Tools like Canva and Adobe Spark make it easy to create professional-looking content on the go.
  • Use Scheduling Tools: Tools like Buffer, Hootsuite, and Later allow you to schedule posts in advance. This is a huge time-saver and helps you stay organized.

Social media management is a side hustle that gives you the flexibility to work from anywhere and helps businesses grow their online presence.

7. Dropshipping

Dropshipping is a business model where you sell products online without having to keep inventory. When a customer places an order, the supplier ships the product directly to the customer. This means you can run your store from anywhere, as long as you have access to the internet.

To start a dropshipping business:

  • Choose a Niche: Find a market niche that you’re passionate about and has demand. Research trending products, and make sure there’s a reliable supplier for the items you plan to sell.
  • Set Up an Online Store: Use platforms like Shopify or WooCommerce to create an online store. Customize it to showcase your products and start driving traffic.
  • Market Your Store: Use digital marketing strategies like social media advertising, SEO, and influencer partnerships to drive traffic to your store.

Dropshipping is a low-risk business model, but it requires an investment of time to set up and market your store. Once established, however, it can generate passive income that can be managed from anywhere.

8. Voiceover Work

If you have a good voice and enjoy speaking, voiceover work is an excellent side hustle that can be done on the go. Voiceover work includes narrating audiobooks, recording ads, voicing animations, or providing commentary for videos.

Here’s how to get started:

  • Create a Demo Reel: Record a few samples of your voice to create a demo reel. This will serve as your portfolio when applying for jobs.
  • Find Gigs: Platforms like Voices.com, Fiverr, and Upwork offer a marketplace for voiceover jobs. You can also reach out to clients directly.
  • Set Up Your Equipment: Invest in a good quality microphone, headphones, and recording software. With portable recording setups, you can record voiceovers from virtually anywhere.

Voiceover work is a flexible, creative side hustle that you can do while traveling or at home.

Conclusion

In today’s digital age, side hustles on the go have become an attractive option for many people. They offer the flexibility to work from anywhere, on your own schedule, and often allow you to leverage your existing skills and interests to make money.

Whether you’re a writer, photographer, tutor, or virtual assistant, there are plenty of opportunities to earn extra income without being tied to a traditional office job. The key to success is finding a side hustle that aligns with your strengths and interests, and that fits into your lifestyle.

By choosing a side hustle you can do on the go, you’ll have the freedom to manage your work and personal life while earning money. So why not start exploring these opportunities today?
