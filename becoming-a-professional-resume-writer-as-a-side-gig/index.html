Becoming a Professional Resume Writer as a Side Gig


In today’s competitive job market, having a well-crafted resume is crucial for standing out to potential employers. For many job seekers, this can be a daunting task, often leaving them feeling overwhelmed or unsure about how to present their skills and experience effectively. This is where professional resume writers come in—helping individuals shape their career stories in a way that resonates with hiring managers and recruiters.

If you have a knack for writing, a strong understanding of what makes a resume successful, and a desire to help others, becoming a professional resume writer can be an excellent side gig. This line of work offers flexibility, the opportunity to make a significant impact on people’s lives, and the potential for steady income. In this post, we will explore how you can become a professional resume writer, the skills you need to succeed, and how to market your services effectively.

Why Become a Professional Resume Writer?

Before diving into the steps to start your side gig, it’s important to understand why this profession is in demand and why it might be a great fit for you.

  1. Growing Demand for Career Support Services
    As more professionals enter the job market or look to make career transitions, the need for expert resume writers continues to grow. Many people struggle to create resumes that stand out, and this creates an opportunity for you to offer value by helping them showcase their talents in the best light.

  2. Flexibility and Autonomy
    Becoming a professional resume writer allows you to work on your own terms. As a side gig, you can choose when and how much work you take on, making it a great option for people looking to supplement their income while keeping their primary job.

  3. Impactful Work
    Your work as a resume writer can have a profound impact on people’s lives. A great resume has the power to open doors to new job opportunities, promotions, or career changes. Helping someone land their dream job or secure a crucial interview is incredibly rewarding.

  4. Scalable Business Model
    Starting out as a side gig is a low-risk way to test the waters. As you build your portfolio and gain experience, you can scale your business to take on more clients or even transition into full-time resume writing if it proves to be successful.

Skills You Need to Succeed

While anyone with strong writing abilities can technically create resumes, there are specific skills and knowledge that will help you excel as a professional resume writer. Here’s a breakdown of what you need:

1. Strong Writing Skills

This is the foundation of resume writing. You need to be able to convey a client’s professional history, achievements, and skills clearly and concisely. The ability to use the right words, structure sentences effectively, and highlight the most relevant information is key to creating a compelling resume.

2. Knowledge of Resume Trends

Resumes are constantly evolving, and what worked five years ago may not be effective today. Understanding current trends, such as the importance of keywords for Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS), modern design aesthetics, and the types of skills most in demand in various industries, will give you an edge in the competitive resume-writing space.

3. Attention to Detail

A small mistake in grammar or formatting can make a significant difference in how a resume is perceived. As a resume writer, you must be meticulous about every aspect of the document—whether it’s spelling, punctuation, or layout. This attention to detail will ensure your clients’ resumes are polished and professional.

4. Understanding of Different Industries

While many resume writers focus on general resumes, understanding the specifics of different industries can give you a competitive advantage. Whether your clients are in tech, healthcare, marketing, or finance, tailoring resumes to the needs of each field is essential. Being familiar with the types of language, skills, and qualifications employers in different sectors look for will help you create more effective resumes.

5. Client Communication and Interviewing Skills

As a resume writer, you will be working closely with clients to gather information about their careers. Knowing how to ask the right questions and effectively interview clients will help you understand their goals, skills, and experiences, which is crucial for writing a targeted and effective resume.

6. Marketing and Branding

Once you’ve developed your resume-writing skills, you’ll need to market your services. Having a strong personal brand and the ability to promote yourself is essential to attracting clients. Whether it’s through social media, your website, or word-of-mouth referrals, knowing how to sell your services will determine how successful your side gig becomes.

Getting Started as a Professional Resume Writer

Now that you understand the skills you need, it’s time to get started. Here’s a step-by-step guide to launching your professional resume writing side gig:

1. Define Your Niche

While you can offer general resume writing services, focusing on a specific niche can help you stand out. For example, you could specialize in executive-level resumes, entry-level resumes, resumes for specific industries, or even LinkedIn profile optimization. Defining your niche helps you target the right audience and tailor your services to meet their needs.

2. Build a Portfolio

Potential clients will want to see examples of your work before hiring you. As you begin, consider offering your services at a discounted rate or even for free in exchange for testimonials and permission to showcase the finished products in your portfolio. If you’re transitioning from a different profession, you can also offer to rewrite resumes for friends or colleagues to build up your portfolio.

3. Set Your Pricing Structure

Pricing can be tricky when starting as a side gig. You want to remain competitive but also ensure you’re compensated fairly for your time and expertise. Research what other resume writers charge to get an idea of the going rates. You might offer different pricing tiers based on complexity (e.g., entry-level resumes vs. executive resumes) or package deals that include services like LinkedIn profile writing, cover letter writing, and interview coaching.

4. Create an Online Presence

In today’s digital age, an online presence is essential. Create a website where potential clients can learn more about your services, view your portfolio, and contact you for consultations. You can also build a social media presence on platforms like LinkedIn, Facebook, or Instagram, where you can showcase your expertise and connect with job seekers who might need your services.

5. Leverage Freelance Platforms

Freelance platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, and Freelancer.com can help you find your first clients. These platforms allow you to build your reputation and gain valuable experience as a resume writer. Once you’ve established yourself, you can start transitioning to higher-paying projects or private clients.

6. Network and Build Relationships

Word-of-mouth referrals can be a great way to grow your business. Let friends, family, and colleagues know about your resume writing services, and ask satisfied clients to refer you to others. Joining professional groups or career-related forums can also connect you with potential clients.

Tips for Success

While starting a side gig as a resume writer can be exciting, it’s important to keep a few tips in mind to ensure success:

1. Stay Updated on Industry Trends

The job market and resume trends are constantly changing. Stay up to date with the latest hiring practices, ATS requirements, and design trends by reading blogs, attending webinars, and networking with other professionals in the industry. This will keep your services relevant and in demand.

2. Provide a Personalized Service

A resume should reflect the unique strengths and achievements of each individual. Avoid using generic templates and instead tailor each resume to fit the client’s personal brand and career goals. Personalized service will set you apart from other resume writers.

3. Offer Additional Services

To increase your income and provide more value to your clients, consider offering additional services such as cover letter writing, LinkedIn profile optimization, or career coaching. Bundling these services can also help clients who are looking for comprehensive career support.

4. Set Realistic Expectations

As a side gig, your time will be limited. Be clear with clients about turnaround times and manage their expectations from the start. Ensure you have a workflow in place that allows you to meet deadlines while balancing other commitments.

5. Request Testimonials

After successfully completing a project, ask clients for testimonials or reviews. Positive feedback will help you build credibility and attract future clients. You can showcase these testimonials on your website, social media, and freelance platforms to build trust with potential clients.

Conclusion

Becoming a professional resume writer as a side gig can be a fulfilling and lucrative venture. With the right skills, a bit of marketing know-how, and a commitment to helping others succeed, you can build a thriving business that provides flexible income and personal satisfaction. Whether you’re looking to earn extra money or explore a potential full-time career, resume writing offers an exciting opportunity to make a positive impact while doing something you enjoy. So why not take the first step today and start helping others land their dream jobs?
