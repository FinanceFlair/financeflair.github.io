The Best Options Trading Books Every Trader Should Read


As the world of finance continues to evolve, options trading has emerged as a lucrative and exciting way to capitalize on market volatility. Whether you're an experienced investor or just starting out, understanding options trading strategies and techniques is crucial to achieving success in this arena. With the vast amount of information available, it can be overwhelming to sift through the noise and identify the most valuable resources. That's why we've curated a list of the best options trading books that every trader should read to stay ahead of the game.

  1. "Options Volatility & Pricing" by Sheldon Natenberg In this comprehensive guide, Sheldon Natenberg, a renowned options expert, delves into the intricacies of options volatility and pricing. The book provides a deep understanding of the relationship between volatility and the pricing of options contracts, offering readers a solid foundation to make informed decisions. With numerous illustrations and examples, Natenberg breaks down complex concepts into manageable parts, making it accessible to traders of all levels. Understanding option Greeks, also known as delta, gamma, and theta, is a crucial step for any trader. Natenberg's book takes a detailed look at the different types of volatility, including historical, implied, and realized volatility. By grasping these concepts, readers will be able to anticipate and manage risk more effectively. "Options Volatility & Pricing" is an essential resource for traders seeking to improve their fundamental knowledge of options trading.
  2. "Trading in the Zone" by Mark Douglas Mark Douglas, a pioneer in the field of trading psychology, offers a unique perspective on the mental aspects of trading in "Trading in the Zone." This insightful book delves into the human psyche, highlighting the importance of attitude, discipline, and self-awareness in achieving success in the markets. Douglas emphasizes the need to shift one's perspective on risk, profit, and loss, encouraging traders to focus on the process rather than the outcome. Douglas's teachings are just as relevant today as they were when first published, as traders continually struggle with managing their emotions and maintaining a clear head in the midst of volatility. Trading in the Zone provides actionable advice on how to cultivate a winning mindset, stay focused, and navigate the emotional rollercoaster that often accompanies trading. This book is not to be missed for any serious trader looking to improve their mental game.
  3. "The Hour Between Dog and Wolf" by John Coates John Coates, a derivatives trader turned neuroscientist, explores the fascinating connection between finance and psychology in "The Hour Between Dog and Wolf." Coates shows how traders, like animals, respond to stress and risk in the markets, revealing the importance of social interaction and brain chemistry in decision-making. By understanding the psychological and biological factors that influence trading behavior, readers will gain a deeper appreciation for the complexities of the trading process. This book offers a unique perspective on the psychological aspects of trading, highlighting the importance of social connections, trust, and interdependence in navigating the markets. Coates's engaging narrative and extensive research provide readers with a fresh understanding of the intricacies of trading psychology. "The Hour Between Dog and Wolf" is a must-read for anyone interested in exploring the human side of trading.
  4. "The Little Book of Trading" by Michael Marcus Michael Marcus, a veteran trader and options expert, shares his extensive knowledge and experience in "The Little Book of Trading." This concise and accessible guide covers the essentials of trading, including options, spreads, and overall market strategies. Marcus emphasizes the importance of risk management, providing readers with a practical framework for navigating the markets. This book is ideal for beginners or intermediate traders seeking to solidify their understanding of basic trading concepts. Marcus's straightforward approach and anecdotal stories from his trading career make the book easy to read and relatable. "The Little Book of Trading" serves as an excellent introduction to the world of options trading and a valuable resource for traders looking to refine their skills.
  5. "Options Adjustment" by Michael T. White Michael T. White, a renowned options trader, reveals the intricacies of options adjustment in this book. "Options Adjustment" is a valuable resource for advanced traders seeking to refine their skills and expertise. White's comprehensive guide covers various options strategies, including iron condors, credit spreads, and naked calls, offering readers an in-depth understanding of how to adjust options positions effectively. This book provides a detailed look at the intricacies of options adjustment, empowering readers to refine their trading strategies and achieve more precise control over their positions. White's extensive market experience and expertise shine through in this thorough guide, making it an essential resource for traders aiming to improve their options skills. Bonus Recommendation: For a more in-depth exploration of options strategies and techniques, consider the following:

The CBOE's "OptionsClear" course: A comprehensive online resource offering hands-on training in options trading. The Wall Street Journal's "Trading in Focus" series: A series of articles and webinars covering topics such as options, futures, and derivatives. ODIN Investing's "Options Trading" course: A beginner-friendly online course providing a thorough introduction to options trading.

In conclusion, these books offer a wealth of knowledge and insights that will elevate your trading skills and knowledge. Whether you're a seasoned trader or just starting out, "Options Volatility & Pricing," "Trading in the Zone," "The Hour Between Dog and Wolf," "The Little Book of Trading," and "Options Adjustment" provide an essential foundation for navigating the complex world of options trading.
