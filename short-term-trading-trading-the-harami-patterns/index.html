Short Term Trading: Trading the Harami Patterns


Are you a short-term trader looking for a reliable and effective trading strategy? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore the Harami patterns and how they can be used to enhance your trading game. So, let’s dive right in! The Harami pattern is a two-candlestick pattern that indicates a potential trend reversal. The word “Harami” comes from the Japanese word for “pregnant,” as the pattern resembles a pregnant woman, with the smaller second candlestick nestled inside the larger first candlestick.

The Harami pattern consists of two candles: the first one represents the existing trend, while the second one suggests a possible reversal. The first candle is usually larger, indicating strong buying or selling pressure, depending on the direction of the trend. The second candle, on the other hand, is smaller and occupies a position within the body of the first candle.

Identifying the Harami Patterns

To effectively trade the Harami patterns, you need to be able to identify them on your charts. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you:

  1. Look for a strong trend: The Harami pattern is most effective when it occurs after a strong trend, whether it’s an uptrend or a downtrend. This indicates a potential reversal.

  2. Spot the first candle: The first candle should be larger than the average candle size, indicating strong buying or selling pressure. In an uptrend, the first candle is bullish (green or white), while in a downtrend, it is bearish (red or black).

  3. Identify the second candle: The second candle should be smaller than the first one and should be opposite in color. For example, if the first candle is bullish, the second one should be bearish, and vice versa.

  4. Confirm the pattern: To validate the Harami pattern, wait for the next candle after the second candle to close. If the price starts moving in the opposite direction, it confirms the reversal signal provided by the Harami pattern.

Trading the Harami Patterns

Now that you know how to identify the Harami patterns let’s discuss how to trade them effectively:

  1. Wait for confirmation: As mentioned earlier, it’s crucial to wait for the third candle after the Harami pattern to close. This confirms the reversal signal and reduces the risk of false signals.

  2. Enter the trade: Once the reversal is confirmed, enter a trade in the direction opposite to the initial trend. For example, if the Harami pattern appears after a downtrend, enter a long position when the price starts moving upwards.

  3. Set stop-loss and take-profit levels: To manage your risk effectively, place a stop-loss order below the low of the second candle in a bullish Harami pattern, or above the high of the second candle in a bearish Harami pattern. Additionally, set a take-profit level based on your risk-reward ratio.

  4. Monitor the trade: Keep a close eye on the price action and the overall market conditions. Adjust your stop-loss and take-profit levels as needed to protect your profits or limit your losses.

Conclusion

The Harami patterns can be a valuable addition to your short-term trading strategy. By identifying these patterns correctly and trading them with proper risk management, you can increase your chances of capturing profitable reversals. Remember to always practice and refine your trading skills before committing real capital. Happy trading!
