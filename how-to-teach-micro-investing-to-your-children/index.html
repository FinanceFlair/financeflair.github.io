How to Teach Micro Investing to Your Children


Teaching children about money and investing is a valuable life skill that can set them up for financial success in the future. One approach to introducing the concept of investing to kids is through micro investing. Micro investing involves investing small amounts of money regularly, which can be an accessible and educational way to introduce children to the world of investing. Here are some tips on how to teach micro investing to your children in a fun and engaging way.

Start with the Basics

Before delving into the specifics of micro investing, it’s important to establish a foundational understanding of money and saving. Teach your children about the value of money, the concept of earning, saving, and spending. Encourage them to set aside a portion of their allowance or any money they receive as gifts, emphasizing the idea of saving for the future.

Make It Relatable

To make the concept of investing more relatable to children, use examples that they can understand. You can explain the idea of investing in companies by relating it to their favorite brands or products. For instance, if they love a particular toy or game, you can explain that by investing in the company that makes it, they are becoming partial owners of that company.

Create a “Mock” Investment Portfolio

Consider creating a “mock” investment portfolio for your children. Help them select a few companies they are familiar with and explain the concept of buying shares in those companies. Track the performance of these companies together and discuss how their investments are doing. This hands-on approach can make investing more tangible and engaging for children.

Utilize Technology

There are numerous investment apps and platforms designed for beginners, including children. Explore these platforms and find one that offers a user-friendly interface suitable for kids. Using a mock investment account, children can learn about the process of buying and selling stocks, monitoring their portfolio, and understanding the impact of market fluctuations.

Emphasize Long-Term Growth

Teach your children about the concept of long-term growth and the benefits of patience when it comes to investing. Discuss how, over time, their investments have the potential to grow and generate returns. This can instill the value of patience and perseverance, essential qualities for successful investing.

Use Real-Life Examples

Share real-life examples of successful investors or companies that have grown over time. Highlight stories of individuals who started investing at a young age and have achieved financial success. Connecting investing to real-life success stories can inspire and motivate children to take an interest in investing for their own future.

Encourage Questions and Discussions

Create an open environment where children feel comfortable asking questions and sharing their thoughts about investing. Encourage discussions about the companies they’re interested in, the reasons behind their investment choices, and the potential impact of global events on the stock market. This fosters a sense of curiosity and critical thinking about investing.

Teach the Value of Risk

In addition to the potential for growth, it’s important to teach children about the concept of risk in investing. Explain that all investments come with some level of risk and that it’s essential to make informed decisions. Discuss the idea of diversification and how spreading investments across different companies can help manage risk.

Celebrate Milestones

When your children reach certain milestones in their investment journey, such as reaching a specific savings goal or experiencing their first investment return, celebrate these achievements. Recognizing their efforts can reinforce positive financial habits and motivate them to continue learning about investing.

Lead by Example

Finally, one of the most effective ways to teach children about micro investing is to lead by example. Let your children see you making sound financial decisions, saving, and investing for the future. When they witness responsible financial behavior firsthand, it reinforces the importance of these principles in their own lives.

In conclusion, teaching children about micro investing can be an exciting and rewarding endeavor. By starting with the basics, making it relatable, utilizing technology, and emphasizing long-term growth, you can instill valuable financial knowledge in your children from a young age. With patience, encouragement, and a hands-on approach, you can help your children develop a strong foundation for their financial future through the power of micro investing.
