Real Asset Funds: What to Expect in 2024


As investors increasingly look for avenues to diversify portfolios and hedge against inflation, real asset funds have captured more attention than ever before. With the economic landscape undergoing shifts, understanding what lies ahead for real asset funds in 2024 can help both seasoned investors and newcomers make informed decisions.

Understanding Real Asset Funds

Real asset funds primarily invest in tangible assets such as real estate, infrastructure, commodities, and natural resources. Unlike traditional equity or bond funds, these funds offer exposure to physical goods that often have intrinsic value regardless of market fluctuations. This characteristic can provide a buffer during periods of volatility and inflationary pressures.

Real assets tend to have income-generating potential through leasing, usage fees, or commodity sales. Additionally, many of these investments offer diversification benefits, often moving independently from stocks and bonds. Therefore, integrating real asset funds into a broader asset allocation strategy can enhance portfolio resilience.

Economic Context Shaping Real Assets in 2024

The macroeconomic environment heading into 2024 paints a complex picture. Inflation, though more contained than previous years, remains persistent in certain sectors. Central banks balance tightening monetary policy with supporting growth, influencing interest rates and borrowing costs. Geopolitical tensions and supply chain disruptions continue to impart volatility.

Within this backdrop, real assets show promise as a tactical allocation. Inflation-sensitive by nature, assets such as commodities and infrastructure projects often have pricing power to adjust to changing costs. Additionally, real estate demand remains robust in select markets, fueled by demographic trends and structural shifts like urbanization and e-commerce growth.

Nevertheless, challenges exist. Rising interest rates can dampen real estate valuations by increasing financing costs. Supply chain issues may delay infrastructure projects, impacting timelines and returns. Therefore, careful selection within the real asset spectrum is crucial.

Key Trends to Watch in Real Asset Funds for 2024

1. Increased Focus on Sustainable and ESG Real Assets

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) criteria have become central to investor decision-making. In 2024, expect real asset funds to amplify their focus on sustainable investments. This means more capital flowing into renewable energy infrastructure, green buildings, and responsible mining operations.

Funds are increasingly embedding ESG assessments into their asset selection and management practices, aiming to reduce carbon footprints and promote social impact. Such a shift not only appeals to values-driven investors but often correlates with long-term financial performance.

2. Digital Infrastructure: The New Frontier

With the explosion of data consumption and reliance on cloud services, digital infrastructure such as data centers, fiber networks, and cell towers is gaining momentum. Real asset funds dedicated to or including digital assets are likely to continue outperforming, driven by growth in 5G deployment, artificial intelligence, and streaming platforms.

Investors interested in this space should look for funds with expertise in managing complex digital assets and understanding the regulatory landscape.

3. Commodities: Navigating Supply and Demand Dynamics

Commodity markets remain a cornerstone of real asset funds, encompassing energy resources, metals, and agricultural products. The ongoing energy transition creates volatility, with traditional fossil fuels facing regulatory and market pressures, while renewables demand increased raw materials like lithium and cobalt.

Strategic commodity exposure within real asset funds will thus be about balancing opportunities in emerging green technologies with traditional sectors that still underpin global economies.

4. Real Estate: Selectivity and Innovation Drive Returns

Real estate remains a significant component of many real asset funds. However, growth and returns will differ substantially by sector and geography in 2024.

  • Industrial and logistics properties continue to benefit from e-commerce growth.
  • Multifamily residential is supported by urban migration and housing affordability trends.
  • Office spaces face ongoing challenges due to hybrid work models; funds focused on adaptive reuse or redevelopment may perform better.
  • Specialty real estate like life sciences hubs and data centers attract premium demand.

Innovation in property management, tenant amenities, and technology integration can differentiate funds and improve outcomes.

5. Infrastructure Investment Pivots

Governments worldwide are increasing infrastructure spending to stimulate economic growth and address aging assets. This opens opportunities for real asset funds specializing in public-private partnerships or green infrastructure.

However, increased competition for attractive assets may compress yields, requiring fund managers to seek niche projects or novel financing structures. Transparent governance and long-term operational efficiency become key differentiators.

Risks and Considerations

While real asset funds offer compelling benefits, associated risks must be carefully balanced:

  • Interest Rate Sensitivity: Rising rates typically increase borrowing costs and reduce asset valuations, particularly in real estate.
  • Liquidity Constraints: Many real assets are less liquid compared to stocks or bonds, posing challenges for investors needing quick access to capital.
  • Regulatory Vulnerabilities: Changing policies on environmental standards, land use, and resources can affect asset viability and profitability.
  • Market Cyclicality: Commodity prices and infrastructure demand fluctuate with economic cycles.
  • Management Expertise: Success in real assets hinges on active asset management, detailed due diligence, and sector knowledge.

Investors should assess their risk tolerance, investment horizon, and diversification goals before allocating to real asset funds.

How to Position Yourself for 2024

  1. Diversify Within Real Assets: Don’t put all your eggs in one basket. Spread investments across various sub-sectors to mitigate cyclical downturns or sector-specific risks.

  2. Prioritize ESG and Sustainability: Align investments with environmental and social priorities, as these trends increasingly impact regulation, consumer preferences, and capital flows.

  3. Evaluate Fund Track Records and Expertise: Choose fund managers with demonstrated experience in navigating real asset complexities and delivering consistent returns.

  4. Consider Fee Structures and Liquidity Terms: Real asset funds can have varied fee models and lockup periods that influence net returns and flexibility.

  5. Stay Informed on Macro Trends: Keep an eye on interest rate movements, inflation data, global economic developments, and technological shifts affecting asset values.

Final Thoughts

Real asset funds remain a robust option for investors seeking tangible, inflation-resistant exposure in 2024. However, the landscape is neither uniform nor static. The coming year will demand heightened selectivity, awareness of evolving trends, and an appreciation for the nuanced dynamics at play across commodities, infrastructure, real estate, and beyond.

Embracing sustainability, leveraging innovation, and maintaining disciplined risk management can position investors to capture value and navigate uncertainties. As ever, thoughtful portfolio integration complemented by ongoing research is the path to optimizing real asset allocations in this transformative period.

By understanding the multifaceted nature of real asset funds and the economic currents steering 2024, investors can better dial in strategies that align with their objectives and the changing market realities ahead.
