How to Make Your Own Beauty Products: 12 Frugal Recipes


As consumers become increasingly aware of the harsh chemicals and environmental concerns associated with commercial beauty products, many have turned to making their own beauty products as a healthier and more cost-effective alternative. Creating your own beauty products at home allows you to control the ingredients, tailor the products to your individual skin type and needs, and avoid any harsh chemicals. Here are 12 frugal recipes to help you make your own beauty products at home:

  1. Sugar Scrub for Smooth Skin

1 cup sugar 1/2 cup olive oil 1/4 cup coconut oil 2 tablespoons honey 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Mix the sugar, olive oil, and coconut oil in a bowl until well combined. Add the honey and vanilla extract, stirring until the mixture forms a thick paste. Use this scrub 1-2 times a week to exfoliate and moisturize your skin. 2. Aromatherapy Bath Salts

1 cup Epsom salt 1/2 cup coconut oil 10 drops lavender essential oil 10 drops chamomile essential oil

Combine the Epsom salt and coconut oil in a bowl. Add the lavender and chamomile essential oils, stirring until well combined. Add 1 cup of this mixture to warm bath water for a relaxing soak. 3. Whipped Body Butter

1/2 cup shea butter 1/4 cup coconut oil 2 tablespoons honey 2 tablespoons aloe vera gel

Beat the shea butter, coconut oil, and honey in a stand mixer until light and fluffy. Add the aloe vera gel, mixing until well combined. Use this whipped body butter as a moisturizing treat after bathing. 4. Toner for Balancing Skin pH

1 cup distilled water 1/2 cup witch hazel 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar 10 drops tea tree oil

Combine the distilled water and witch hazel in a bowl. Add the apple cider vinegar and tea tree oil, stirring until well combined. Apply this toner to the skin with a cotton pad to balance the skin's pH. 5. Face Mask for Glowing Skin

1 ripe avocado 1 tablespoon honey 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 teaspoon plain yogurt

Mash the avocado in a bowl until smooth. Add the honey, olive oil, and yogurt, mixing until well combined. Apply this face mask to your skin for 10-15 minutes, then rinse with warm water. 6. Lip Balm for Chapped Lips

1 tablespoon coconut oil 1 tablespoon beeswax 1 tablespoon vitamin E oil 10 drops peppermint essential oil

Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler. Add the vitamin E oil and peppermint essential oil, stirring until well combined. Pour this mixture into lip balm tubes and let it cool before use. 7. Deodorant for Natural Protection

1/2 cup baking soda 1/4 cup coconut oil 1/4 cup arrowroot powder 10 drops tea tree oil

Mix the baking soda, coconut oil, and arrowroot powder in a bowl. Add the tea tree oil, stirring until well combined. Apply this deodorant to your underarms after showering to keep you fresh all day. 8. Cuticle Oil for Healthy Nails

1 tablespoon argan oil 1 tablespoon coconut oil 1 tablespoon beeswax 10 drops rosemary essential oil

Melt the argan oil and coconut oil in a double boiler. Add the beeswax, stirring until melted. Add the rosemary essential oil, pouring the mixture into small ramekins to cool and solidify. Use as a cuticle oil for healthy nails. 9. Exfoliating Body Scrub

1 cup brown sugar 1/2 cup coconut oil 1/4 cup lemon juice 2 tablespoons honey

Mix the brown sugar, coconut oil, and lemon juice in a bowl until well combined. Add the honey, stirring until smooth. Use this scrub 1-2 times a week to exfoliate and smooth out skin texture. 10. Tinted Lip Gloss

1 tablespoon coconut oil 1 tablespoon beeswax 1 tablespoon vitamin E oil 10 drops cherry blossom essential oil A few drops of beetroot powder (for color)

Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler. Add the vitamin E oil and cherry blossom essential oil, stirring until well combined. Add a few drops of beetroot powder to tint the lip gloss. 11.Moisturizing Cream for Dry Skin

1/2 cup coconut oil 1/4 cup shea butter 1 tablespoon aloe vera gel 1 teaspoon honey

Mix the coconut oil, shea butter, and aloe vera gel in a bowl until smooth. Add the honey, stirring until well combined. Apply this moisturizing cream to dry skin after bathing. 12. Travel-Size Shampoo Bars

1 cup olive oil 1/2 cup coconut oil 2 tablespoons castile soap 10 drops lavender essential oil

Mix the olive oil, coconut oil, and castile soap in a bowl until smooth. Add the lavender essential oil, placing the mixture into ice cube trays to solidify. Use as travel-size shampoo bars. By following these simple recipes, you can create a variety of beauty products at home without breaking the bank. With natural ingredients and a little creativity, you can make your own beauty products that work for your skin and budget. From exfoliating scrubs to moisturizing creams, there's a DIY beauty recipe for every need.
