Top Micro-Investing Apps to Invest with Just Pennies


The days of needing thousands of dollars to start investing are long gone. Enter the world of micro-investing: platforms and apps designed specifically for people eager to dip their toes in the financial markets without staking large sums of money. Whether you’re a college student with spare change or a seasoned investor interested in diversifying your portfolio with low effort, micro-investing apps transform savings into powerful long-term wealth-building engines.

In this detailed guide, we’ll take a deep dive into the best micro-investing apps available. You’ll learn how each works, their standout features, costs, pros, cons, and ideal users. By the end, you’ll know exactly which app (or apps) align with your financial goals and lifestyle.

What is Micro-Investing?

If you’ve ever thought, “I would invest, but I just don’t have enough money to start,” micro-investing flips the narrative. The concept is simple: use apps that allow you to invest tiny amounts — often as little as a single dollar, or even spare change — into stocks, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), or other assets. Automated saving, low fees, and simple interfaces are hallmarks of micro-investing platforms.

Rather than waiting until you can make a “significant” investment, micro-investing apps let you begin with the coins hiding in your couch cushions. Over time, persistent small investments (combined with compounding returns) can accumulate into surprisingly substantial portfolios.

Benefits of Micro-Investing for Beginners

Micro-investing stands out thanks to a handful of compelling benefits:

  • No Barriers to Entry: Traditional brokers often require minimum deposits or large initial investments. Micro-investing apps break down this barrier, enabling anyone to start investing immediately.
  • Automated Savings: Many apps use features like “round-ups,” where your purchases are rounded up to the nearest dollar and the difference is invested. It’s a near-zero-effort way to build your investment habit.
  • Educational Features: Most micro-investing platforms target first-timers, packing their apps with educational content, goal-setting tools, and easily digestible insights.
  • Diversification: Even tiny investments get split among multiple stocks or funds, reducing risk and helping you experience the benefits of a diversified portfolio.
  • Low (or No) Fees: These platforms typically offer low monthly fees, and some waive fees altogether for small balances or college students.

The Best Micro-Investing Apps for Everyday Investors

Let’s look at the top micro-investing apps shaping the way people invest with just pennies.

1. Acorns

Acorns is arguably the name in micro-investing. Its primary appeal lies in its round-up feature: link your debit or credit card, and every purchase you make gets rounded up to the nearest dollar. The “spare change” is transferred into your investment account and invested in a diversified portfolio of ETFs.

Key Features:

  • Automated Round-Ups: Seamlessly invest leftovers from regular spending.
  • Pre-Built Portfolios: Choose from portfolios crafted by expert advisors based on your risk tolerance.
  • Found Money: Earn extra investments when you shop with Acorns’ partner brands.
  • Educational Content: Acorns Grow offers a robust financial literacy resource center.
  • Retirement and Kids’ Accounts: Options to open IRAs and custodial accounts.

Pricing: Flat fee structure, typically $3/month for most individual investors (with other plans for families or retirement-focused users).

Pros:

  • True “set-it-and-forget-it” investing.
  • Variety of account types.
  • Generous partner rewards.

Cons:

  • Monthly fee can be a bigger percentage if your balance is small.
  • Limited control over specific ETFs or assets.

Who It’s Best For: Those seeking maximum automation, especially if you want to invest passively and have no desire to pick individual stocks.

2. Stash

Stash positions itself as an all-in-one financial platform, combining micro-investing with banking features. Unlike Acorns, Stash gives you more say in your investments — choose from hundreds of stocks and ETFs, or opt for curated portfolios based on various themes.

Key Features:

  • Fractional Shares: Buy as little as $1 of any stock or ETF, even popular expensive ones.
  • Stock-Back® Card: Get stock rewards whenever you use your Stash debit card.
  • Education Tools: “Stash Learn” delivers approachable lessons and personalized advice.
  • Flexible Portfolios: Pick your own funds or invest in themed portfolios (e.g., “Clean & Green,” “American Innovators”).
  • Banking Integration: Options for direct deposits, mobile banking, and no overdraft fees.

Pricing: Monthly plans start at $3/month, with higher tiers for additional features like retirement accounts or custodial accounts.

Pros:

  • Greater control without being overwhelming.
  • Robust educational ecosystem for beginners.
  • Investment rewards on everyday spending.

Cons:

  • Monthly fee means high relative cost for tiny balances.
  • Fewer automation features compared to Acorns.

Who It’s Best For: New investors who want to learn as they go and customize their portfolios, but aren’t ready for a traditional brokerage.

3. Robinhood

Robinhood is a trailblazer in commission-free stock trading and fractional share investing, making it a standout for folks who want micro-investing options paired with broader trading freedom. You can purchase tiny slices of shares, and there’s no account minimum or monthly fee for standard accounts.

Key Features:

  • Fractional Shares: Invest as little as $1 in thousands of stocks or ETFs.
  • No Commissions: Buy and sell assets without paying trading fees.
  • Options and Crypto: Supports trades in options contracts and select cryptocurrencies.
  • Intuitive Interface: Clean and minimalist design makes for easy navigation.
  • Instant Deposits: Add funds instantly to take advantage of market opportunities.

Pricing: No account maintenance fee. Robinhood Gold (optional) for $5/month gives access to research, larger instant deposits, and margin trading.

Pros:

  • Zero-dollar entry.
  • Broad asset selection.
  • Ability to freely trade (not just automate).

Cons:

  • Lacks built-in portfolio guidance or risk assessment.
  • Limited educational resources compared to others.
  • Past outages and regulatory concerns may bother some users.

Who It’s Best For: Self-directed investors confident in managing their trades, or those interested in dabbling across stocks, crypto, and ETFs with low friction.

4. SoFi Invest

A relative newcomer, SoFi Invest pairs no-fee micro-investing with live financial advice, automated investing, and access to other SoFi products (like loans and banking). You can start investing with just $1, and manage your investments manually or let SoFi’s robo-advisor handle allocations for you.

Key Features:

  • Fractional Shares: Buy fractions of popular stocks and ETFs.
  • Automated and Active Trading: Choose self-directed investing or set up recurring, automated investments.
  • Holistic Financial Services: Access to loans, banking, and financial planning in one app.
  • Live Advisors: Members get access to real, credentialed financial advisors at no additional cost.
  • Crypto Trading: Ability to invest in a wide array of cryptocurrencies.

Pricing: No commission for trades and no account fees. Certain upgraded services or trading outside default hours may carry fees.

Pros:

  • Combines hands-off and hands-on investing.
  • No monthly maintenance fees.
  • Rich suite of money management tools.

Cons:

  • Less focus on automating small savings (no round-up feature).
  • Limited customization for automated portfolios.

Who It’s Best For: Investors looking for a full-service platform that grows with them, or those who plan to use other SoFi financial products.

5. Public

Public is a social micro-investing app focusing on transparent, community-driven investing. With Public, you can buy fractional shares of stocks and ETFs, follow “social feeds” to see what others are trading, and interact directly with other users — all while learning from their choices.

Key Features:

  • Fractional Investing: Invest as little as $1 in US stocks and ETFs.
  • Social Community: Share your trades, follow friends or influencers, and discuss strategy.
  • No Commissions: Trades are commission-free for standard assets.
  • Robinhood-Alternative: Appeals to those who want social aspects and learning opportunities.
  • Crypto and Alternative Assets: Ability to invest in a curated selection of cryptocurrencies and alternative investments.

Pricing: No account or subscription fees. Public makes money through optional tipping or premium features.

Pros:

  • Engaging social experience for those who want to learn from others.
  • Strong focus on accessible, inclusive education.
  • Good for beginners and more advanced investors alike.

Cons:

  • Community feed can be noisy.
  • Smaller asset selection than larger brokerages.
  • No tax-advantaged or retirement accounts at this time.

Who It’s Best For: Novices who want a sense of community, mentorship, and transparency as they invest, or those weary of cold, isolated trading platforms.

6. M1 Finance

M1 Finance merges automation with customization, making it easy for users to build fully personalized portfolios (called “Pies”). Even with low starting funds, you can create a diversified investment plan and automate new deposits.

Key Features:

  • “Pie” Based Investing: Select from pre-built “Pies” or create your own by choosing slices (stocks and ETFs) and setting allocations.
  • Fractional Shares: Enables precise, personalized diversification with as little as $1 per investment.
  • Rebalancing: Automated rebalancing keeps your investments on track.
  • No-Commission Trading: No fees to buy or sell investments.
  • Borrowing and Banking: Optional lines of credit and checking account features.

Pricing: Standard accounts are free; M1 Plus ($10/month) adds perks like cash back and lower borrowing rates.

Pros:

  • High level of customization.
  • Totally commission-free for basics.
  • Convenient automation for long-term investors.

Cons:

  • Trades only execute during a single daily window (can be restrictive).
  • Can feel complex at first.

Who It’s Best For: Analytical investors eager to design a tailored portfolio, automate everything, and avoid trading fees.

How to Decide Which Micro-Investing App is Right for You

The explosion of micro-investing platforms means there’s no shortage of choice, and selecting the right one hinges on your personal priorities and preferences.

Consider the following:

  • Degree of Automation: Want everything handled for you (Acorns, SoFi)? Or do you crave flexibility and control (Stash, Robinhood)?
  • Fees: Monthly fees can eat up gains for very small balances — keep the cost in context with how much you’re investing.
  • Education: Platforms with robust learning resources can boost your confidence if you’re a first-timer.
  • Social Features: Prefer investing solo or learning in a community environment (Public)?
  • Additional Services: Do you want other features, like banking, loans, or retirement planning?
  • Asset Options: Are you interested in crypto or alternatives beyond stocks/ETFs?

Tips for Getting the Most from Micro-Investing

1. Start Small, but Stay Consistent: The magic of micro-investing comes from frequency, not size. Stick with regular, automated deposits and watch your portfolio bloom over time.

2. Leverage Automation: Round-up tools, recurring transfers, and auto-rebalancing tools exist for a reason. Use them to keep invested, even when life gets hectic.

3. Upgrade Gradually: As your portfolio grows, consider unlocking retirement accounts, tax-advantaged options, or even transitioning to commission-free traditional brokers for full flexibility.

4. Don’t Chase Fads: The most successful micro-investors stick to diversified, long-term investments instead of chasing meme stocks or the latest “hot” cryptocurrency.

5. Set Goals and Track Progress: Use in-app goal tracking (most apps offer it) to keep your eyes on the prize and avoid raiding your investments for short-term wants.

The Micro-Investing Revolution: Closing Thoughts

Micro-investing has made the world of stock and ETF investing accessible to everyone, not just the affluent or the financially savvy. Whether you’re stashing spare change, making $5 transfers each week, or buying single dollars of your favorite companies, these apps give you the building blocks for real financial progress.

Pick the platform that matches your vibe — maybe Acorns for pure automation, Stash for a little DIY action, Robinhood or Public for more trading control, SoFi for a complete financial ecosystem, or M1 Finance for customizable portfolios. The most important step you can take? Get started. Every penny counts, and your future self will thank you for turning small investments into big opportunities.

Remember, the journey to financial growth doesn’t happen overnight. But with the right micro-investing app in your pocket, the compounding effect of your efforts starts right now. Happy investing!
