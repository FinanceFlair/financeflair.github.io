Short Term Trading: Scalping Strategies Explained


Scalping is an extremely popular short term trading strategy used by both professional traders and amateur enthusiasts alike - especially in the fast-paced and unpredictable world of cryptocurrencies. This approach involves selling or buying multiple contracts or coins, making small profits for each trade and closing it quickly. The aim is to collect a few points in a short period of time.

What is Scalping Strategy?

A scalping strategy usually revolves around the following key principles:

  1. Fast Execution: Success in scalping largely depends on speed. You need to analyze market movements swiftly and make informed decisions almost instantaneously.
  2. Minimal Price Movement: Since scalpers make multiple trades per day, the goal is to make smaller profits with each trade, rather than relying on huge price movements.
  3. Liquid Markets: Being a somewhat risky endeavor, scalpers prefer to trade in liquid and highly actively markets to ensure smooth entry and exit.
  4. Advantage of Trading Algorithms: To maximize profits, many scalpers use computer programs and trading algorithms that quickly identify buying and selling opportunities.

Basic Techniques

Just like any other form of trading, there are several techniques in scalping as well, some of which include:

1. Breakout Strategy

This strategy involves identifying support and resistance levels on the charts to determine momentum in a particular direction. When price breaks through a key level, it could indicate a strong movement in one direction, and scalpers seize this opportunity to open their positions.

2. News Trading

This technique is all about timing. It involves making trades around key announcements or news, as these events can drastically affect the market price. Scalpers are quick to react to such changes in the price.

3. Arbitrage

Through arbitrage, traders buy and sell similar assets on different platforms to capitalize on price discrepancies. This discrepancy usually occurs due to lack of knowledge or delayed price updates on certain platforms.

4. High-frequency Trading

This is all about quick trading, where machines and computers are used to place a high volume of trades in a very short amount of time, profiting from small price movements.

Pros & Cons

As with any form of financial trading, scalping also has its advantages and drawbacks.

Pros:

  1. High-Volume Returns: As you make numerous trades each day, your income also becomes very high over time.
  2. No Need for In-Depth Analysis: For short-term trading, the need for deeper analysis of the broader market trend is less. Scalpers can focus on specific and immediate price movements.
  3. Decentralized Cryptocurrency Markets: Scalping can thrive in the decentralized cryptocurrency market which has high trading volume, making it highly advantageous.

Cons:

  1. Huge Pressure: The fast-paced and high-pressure nature of scalping puts a lot of pressure on traders, potentially impacting their decision making negatively.
  2. Transaction Fees: When you place multiple trades per day, transaction fees can really add up.
  3. Huge Capital: To successfully implement scalping strategies, you will need a large amount of trading capital, which can be risky.

Conclusion

Regardless of what your practice, giving it your all makes a big difference. Scalping in short term trading has its undeniable advantages, but you must remember that, much like any other style of trading, it requires knowledge, discipline, and constant vigilance.

So if you’re eager to flex your trading muscles and seize every opportunity in the market, start incorporating these scalping strategies today! Remember, the world of trading is not a game. It’s serious business, make sure you risk what you can afford to lose.
