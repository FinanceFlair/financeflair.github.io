Central Bank Digital Currency: A Review of the Literature


Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) has emerged as a hot topic in the world of finance and technology. As countries explore the potential of digitizing their national currencies, there has been a surge in research and literature examining the implications, benefits, and challenges of CBDC implementation. In this blog post, we will delve into the key findings and insights from the literature surrounding CBDC.

Understanding CBDC

Before diving into the literature review, let’s first understand what CBDC is all about. CBDC refers to a digital form of a country’s fiat currency that is issued and regulated by the central bank. It is a digital representation of traditional money that can be used for transactions, just like physical cash or commercial bank deposits. The underlying technology behind CBDC is usually based on blockchain or distributed ledger technology (DLT).

Benefits of CBDC

The literature highlights several potential benefits of CBDC. Firstly, CBDC can enhance financial inclusion by providing access to banking services for the unbanked and underbanked population. With CBDC, individuals can have a digital wallet directly linked to the central bank, eliminating the need for intermediaries.

Secondly, CBDC can streamline payment systems and reduce transaction costs. By leveraging blockchain technology, CBDC enables faster and more secure transactions, eliminating the need for intermediaries and reducing settlement times.

Thirdly, CBDC can facilitate monetary policy implementation. Central banks can have more direct control over the money supply, enabling them to implement policies effectively and respond swiftly to economic fluctuations.

Challenges and Considerations

While CBDC holds promise, it also comes with its fair share of challenges and considerations. One major concern is privacy and security. As CBDC transactions are recorded on a public ledger, ensuring user privacy and data security becomes crucial. Striking the right balance between transparency and privacy is a key challenge that central banks need to address.

Another consideration is the impact of CBDC on the existing financial system. CBDC could potentially disrupt the role of commercial banks and their intermediation function. The literature suggests that finding the right balance between CBDC and the existing financial system is essential to prevent unintended consequences.

International Perspectives

The literature also explores the international perspective of CBDC. Several countries, including China, Sweden, and the Bahamas, have made significant progress in piloting and implementing CBDC. These initiatives provide valuable insights into the potential benefits and challenges of CBDC adoption on a larger scale.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the literature surrounding CBDC provides valuable insights into the potential benefits, challenges, and considerations associated with the implementation of central bank digital currencies. While CBDC holds promise in terms of financial inclusion, streamlined payments, and effective monetary policy implementation, there are also concerns regarding privacy, security, and disruption to the existing financial system. As countries continue to explore the possibilities of CBDC, it is crucial to carefully evaluate and address these considerations to ensure a smooth transition into the digital future of finance.
