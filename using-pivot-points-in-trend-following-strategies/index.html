Using Pivot Points in Trend Following Strategies


As a seasoned trader, you're likely familiar with the concept of trend following – the strategy of identifying and riding market trends to achieve long-term profitability. However, navigating the vast and often turbulent waters of the financial markets requires a reliable and effective toolset. One such tool is pivot points, a vital component of trend following strategies that can help you stay ahead of the game. In this comprehensive guide, we'll delve into the intricacies of pivot points, uncovering their purpose, calculation, and application in a trend-following context. What are Pivot Points? Pivot points are calculated values that represent potential support and resistance levels in a market. They are typically derived from the previous day's or period's high, low, and close prices. The primary objective of pivot points is to identify critical areas where markets tend to experience significant turbulence, thereby enabling traders to refine their trading plans and capitalize on more profitable opportunities. The Formula: Calculating Pivot Points with Ease To calculate pivot points, you'll need to gather the following data:

Previous High (H) Previous Low (L) Previous Close (PC)

Using the S3, S2, S1, and R1, R2, R3 notation, you can compute the pivot points as follows: Pivot Point = (H + L + PC) / 3 Support Level 1 (S1) = 2 * Pivot Point - H Support Level 2 (S2) = Pivot Point - (H - L) Resistance Level 1 (R1) = 2 * Pivot Point - L Resistance Level 2 (R2) = Pivot Point + (H - L) Resistance Level 3 (R3) = 2 * Pivot Point - PC While the formula might appear complex, it's relatively straightforward once you grasp the underlying principles. By applying these calculations, you'll be well on your way to generating pivot points – an essential part of your trend-following toolkit. Pivot Points in Trend Following Trend-following strategies prioritize the identification of directional biases in the market, aiming to harness strong price movements. When combined with pivot points, traders can refine their market analysis and create valuable buy or sell signals. By recognizing pivot points as levels of confluence, traders can pinpoint the exact moments to enter trades, allowing them to join the trend at the optimal time. Blending Pivot Points with other Indicators and Trading Strategies Pivot points are often paired with other indicators, such as RSI or Bollinger Bands, to amplify their predictive potential. By combining the strengths of multiple tools and techniques, traders can fashion hybrid strategies tailored to their market vision and risk tolerance. Keep in mind that the diverse world of indicator combinations means there's no right or wrong solution – only a fresh array of trading concepts to explore. Avoid the Pitfalls of Geeking Out on Pivot Points Pivot points can easily become the focal point of your trading decision-making, leading to an overemphasis on a solitary metric. Don't forget that pivot points simply reflect market tendencies; they're an essential but not an absolute factor in making trading decisions.
