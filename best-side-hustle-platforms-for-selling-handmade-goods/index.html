Best Side Hustle Platforms for Selling Handmade Goods


Turning your passion for handmade crafts into a profitable side hustle is an exciting journey. Whether you create jewelry, home decor, clothing, art, or any other handmade product, the opportunity to reach customers beyond your local market is vast. However, with so many online platforms available, choosing the right one for your handmade business can feel overwhelming. This guide explores the best side hustle platforms to sell handmade goods, highlighting their features, fees, audience, and unique benefits to help you decide where to launch or expand your creative venture.

The Rise of Handmade Marketplaces

The past decade has seen a resurgence of appreciation for craftmanship, authenticity, and unique products. Consumers increasingly seek products that reflect individuality and a personal touch, turning handmade goods into a thriving niche within e-commerce. Digital platforms tailored for artisans and makers have become essential tools for side hustlers aiming to balance creativity with financial growth.

Key Factors to Consider When Choosing a Platform

Before diving into specific platforms, let’s outline critical factors to evaluate when picking the perfect home for your handmade side hustle:

  • Audience Reach: Does the platform bring in buyers specifically interested in handmade, artisanal products?
  • Ease of Use: How simple is it to set up your shop, list items, and manage sales?
  • Fees and Pricing Structure: Understanding listing, transaction, and payment processing fees is critical to maximizing profits.
  • Customization: Can you personalize your store to reflect your brand’s identity?
  • Marketing Tools: Access to built-in advertising, SEO support, and customer engagement features can amplify your reach.
  • Support and Resources: Availability of seller support, tutorials, and community networks can ease challenges.
  • Shipping and Payment: Seamless integration of payment gateways and shipping options improves customer experience.

With these in mind, let’s explore the leading platforms where handmade goods sellers have thrived.

Etsy: The Pioneer of Handmade E-Commerce

Overview

Etsy is arguably the most recognized marketplace focused on handmade, vintage, and unique goods. Since its inception in 2005, Etsy has nurtured a vast community of makers and buyers, ensuring a strong customer base looking specifically for handcrafted quality.

Why Etsy Shines for Handmade Sellers

  • Targeted Audience: Around 95 million active buyers visit Etsy regularly, many specifically seeking original handmade items.
  • User-Friendly Seller Experience: Setting up an Etsy shop is quick and straightforward with intuitive tools.
  • SEO-Optimized Listings: Etsy’s search algorithms reward sellers who use relevant keywords, helping your products appear in front of engaged buyers.
  • Marketing Options: Access to Etsy Ads, social media integrations, and email marketing functionalities.
  • Community and Learning Resources: Extensive forums, webinars, and seller handbooks to empower your business growth.

Fees and Costs

  • Listing fee of $0.20 per item lasting four months or until the item sells.
  • Transaction fee of 6.5% on the price including shipping.
  • Payment processing fees vary based on country (typically around 3% + $0.25 per transaction in the U.S.).

Best For

New and growing handmade sellers who want access to a broad, dedicated audience and prefer an easy-to-manage storefront without needing an independent website.

Shopify: For Handmade Sellers Ready to Build a Brand

Overview

Shopify offers a powerful e-commerce solution allowing sellers to create fully customized online stores. While not handmade-specific, Shopify’s flexibility and suite of tools make it ideal for creators ready to scale their side hustle into a full-fledged brand.

Advantages for Handmade Businesses

  • Full Store Ownership and Customization: Use templates or build your own, control every aspect of your branding.
  • Integrated Payment and Inventory Management: Shopify Payments and automated inventory tracking streamline operations.
  • No Listing Limits: Sell unlimited handmade products without additional listing fees.
  • Advanced Marketing Tools: SEO features, blogging, email campaigns, and integration with social media channels.
  • App Store: Access additional plugins for dropshipping, shipping discounts, analytics, and customer service.

Fees and Pricing

  • Monthly subscription plans start from $39/month (Basic Shopify).
  • Payment processing fees typically 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction (lower with higher-tier plans).
  • No listing fees, but some apps and themes may cost extra.

Best For

Handmade sellers who want to establish an independent brand and wield full control over the customer experience, marketing, and store design.

Big Cartel: Straightforward Indie Seller Platform

Overview

Big Cartel caters to independent artists and makers looking for a simple, no-fuss platform. It’s especially popular with small sellers who prefer a clean and minimal approach.

Highlights

  • Simple Setup: The sign-up process is fast, making it easy to launch a store quickly.
  • Customization: Choose from free themes that are highly customizable with CSS.
  • No Listing Fees: Only a monthly subscription cost depending on your plan.
  • Supportive Community: Designed to support creatives and artists.
  • Limited Product Offerings: Good for small product lines with plans ranging by the number of items you list.

Pricing Plans

  • Free plan for up to 5 products.
  • Paid plans ranging from $9.99 to $19.99/month with increasing product limits and features.

Best For

Handmade sellers with a small but curated product collection who want a straightforward and affordable platform without complicated fees.

Folksy: UK’s Handmade Marketplace

Overview

If you are based in the UK or want to tap into the UK handmade market, Folksy is a popular choice specifically tailored to British crafters. It focuses solely on handmade products and is often described as the UK’s answer to Etsy.

Benefits

  • UK-Centric Community: Build local customer trust and ease shipping within the UK.
  • Artisan-Focused: Sellers must adhere to strict handmade policies, ensuring quality and authenticity.
  • Affordable Fees: Listing fees are 15p per item, with a 6% commission on sales.
  • Support and Workshops: Folksy regularly hosts craft-related workshops, fairs, and online events.

Best For

UK-based creatives eager to grow within a community that values handmade craftsmanship and authenticity.

Amazon Handmade: Access to a Massive Audience

Overview

Amazon Handmade takes advantage of Amazon’s enormous marketplace while carving a space exclusively for artisans. This platform offers potential reach unparalleled by most competitors but comes with some trade-offs around control and fees.

Key Features

  • Unmatched Reach: Millions of active buyers worldwide.
  • Prime Shipping: Eligible sellers can offer Prime shipping, highly valued by customers.
  • Professional Seller Account: Amazon vets artisans to maintain quality.
  • Marketing and Analytics: Built-in tools for ads and performance tracking.

Costs and Fee Structure

  • No individual selling fee; instead, a 15% referral fee on each sold item.
  • Requires a professional seller account costing $39.99/month.
  • Strict application process to join.

Best For

Established handmade sellers ready to scale quickly and who can navigate Amazon’s more complex selling requirements.

eBay: The Versatile Marketplace for Handmade Goods

Overview

While not handmade-exclusive, eBay has supported handmade categories and provides access to a diverse buyer base. It can be an alternative alongside other specialized platforms.

Advantages

  • Huge User Base: Millions of users worldwide.
  • Auction and Fixed Price Listings: Flexibility in how you sell.
  • Low Entry Barriers: Easy to list products.
  • Global Reach: International shipping tools streamline global sales.

Considerations

  • Competition is high, and handmade items may need strong branding to stand out.
  • Listing and final value fees vary but can be competitive compared to some handmade-specific platforms.

Best For

Sellers who want flexibility and want to test pricing strategies through auctions or fixed pricing on a general marketplace with a vast audience.

Handmade at Amazon vs. Etsy: Which Should You Choose?

Though both target artisans, there are significant differences. Etsy’s community is more handmade-focused, allowing better discoverability for custom and unique products. Amazon Handmade’s advantage lies in sheer volume of traffic and fulfillment options but carries higher fees and a tougher seller vetting process.

For side hustlers just starting, Etsy’s ease and community support often make it more accessible. However, for those prepared to handle larger operations, Amazon Handmade could provide lucrative scaling opportunities.

Niche Platforms Worth Mentioning

  • Zibbet: An emerging marketplace catering to handmade, vintage, and craft supplies, with multi-channel integration.
  • Artfire: A community-driven marketplace with a simplified fee structure for handmade artisans.
  • Redbubble and Society6: Focused mainly on print-on-demand art and design products, great for creatives specializing in prints and home goods.

Building Your Handmade Business Beyond Marketplaces

While these platforms give you essential access to buyers, a sustainable handmade business often incorporates:

  • Social Media Presence: Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok—ideal for visual storytelling and building customer relationships.
  • Email Marketing: Building a subscriber list helps drive repeat business and loyalty.
  • SEO-Optimized Website or Blog: Even a small website can boost credibility and provide direct sales options.
  • Craft Fairs and Local Events: Offline presence connects you with local customers and builds authenticity.

Tips for Success Selling Handmade Goods Online

  • Invest in Quality Photography: Clear, appealing photos showcase craftsmanship and attract buyers.
  • Write Descriptive Listings: Detail materials, process, and care instructions to build trust.
  • Engage with Customers: Prompt responses and follow-ups can turn one-time buyers into loyal fans.
  • Offer Customization: Many buyers seek unique, personalized items.
  • Price Your Work Fairly: Consider materials, labor, fees, and market trends.
  • Stay Consistent: Regularly update your inventory and keep active on the platform to boost visibility.

Conclusion: Choosing the Right Platform to Fuel Your Handmade Side Hustle

Your creative side hustle deserves a platform that not only reaches the right audience but also supports your journey as a maker and entrepreneur. Etsy remains an unbeatable choice for many handmade sellers due to its focused community and ease of use. Shopify empowers those ready to take full control and scale their brand, while smaller platforms like Big Cartel and Folksy serve niche and regional markets beautifully. Meanwhile, Amazon Handmade offers massive potential for expansion but requires readiness for a more demanding setup.

By carefully considering your goals, product type, budget, and how much time you want to invest in managing your digital storefront, you can select the best platform to turn your handmade passion into a thriving side hustle. The marketplace is waiting for your unique creations, and the right platform is your gateway to making your artistic dreams profitable.
