Side Hustle Ideas for Event Planning and Coordination


Event planning is an exciting field that combines creativity, organization, and people skills. Whether you’re just starting out or looking to diversify your income streams, side hustles in event planning and coordination offer fantastic opportunities. The growing demand for personalized, memorable experiences means there’s no shortage of potential clients — from small birthday parties to corporate retreats, weddings, and community events.

In this deep dive, we’ll explore a range of side hustle ideas tailored for event planners and coordinators. Each one taps into different aspects of the profession, allowing you to leverage your expertise and build a thriving venture on the side.

Why Event Planning Makes a Great Side Hustle

Before jumping into specific ideas, it’s worth reflecting on why event planning is such a suitable side hustle. First, many events occur during evenings, weekends, or holidays, which often fits well with a daytime job or other commitments. Second, event planning skills are highly transferable—details management, vendor coordination, and budgeting are useful in numerous contexts. Third, you can grow your side hustle organically by starting small and building your reputation through word-of-mouth and repeat business.

Plus, modern tools and technology have simplified many tasks once done manually, enabling you to scale your planning without overwhelming your schedule. Apps for project management, budget tracking, and guest communication keep your workflow smooth.

Side Hustle Ideas in Event Planning and Coordination

1. Micro Wedding Planning

Micro weddings, which cater to small groups ranging from a handful to around 50 people, have surged in popularity. The intimacy and flexibility appeal to couples looking for something less traditional and more affordable.

As a micro wedding planner, you focus on personalized services like venue scouting, vendor coordination, and day-of coordination. Since these events are smaller, it’s often easier to manage logistics, making it a manageable and rewarding side hustle.

How to get started:

  • Build connections with local venues, caterers, and photographers that accommodate small weddings.
  • Develop customizable micro wedding packages.
  • Market through social media channels, targeting engaged couples interested in intimate ceremonies.

2. Corporate Event Coordination

Companies organize meetings, team-building days, and product launches all the time. Coordinating such events can be an excellent side income source, especially if you have corporate contacts or experience in business environments.

Tasks might include booking venues, arranging catering, preparing materials, and managing schedules. Since companies often plan events quarterly or biannually, this side hustle provides consistent seasonal work.

How to get started:

  • Reach out to small and mid-size businesses in your area.
  • Offer specialized services for workshops, seminars, or holiday parties.
  • Highlight your ability to manage budgets and deliver stress-free coordination.

3. Virtual Event Coordination

The rise of digital communication platforms has created a massive market for virtual events—webinars, conferences, panels, and more. Many organizers lack the expertise to handle the technical and logistical aspects of online events, creating an opportunity for skilled coordinators.

Virtual event coordination includes managing registration, ensuring smooth streaming, interacting with speakers, and handling attendee questions live.

How to get started:

  • Familiarize yourself with popular platforms like Zoom, Hopin, or Microsoft Teams.
  • Promote your services in forums or groups that support online communities.
  • Offer packages including rehearsal sessions, tech support, and post-event follow-up.

4. Party Planning for Kids’ Birthdays

Parents are often overwhelmed by the prospect of organizing elaborate birthday parties for their children. Many appreciate hiring a party planner who can design a fun, hassle-free celebration with themes, games, and decorations tailored to the child’s interests.

This niche allows creativity to flourish and often leads to repeat business from families as children grow and have new milestones for celebration.

How to get started:

  • Create party bundles around popular themes (superheroes, princesses, animals, etc.).
  • Partner with local entertainers, face-painters, or balloon artists.
  • Use testimonials and photos (with parental permission) to showcase your fun and organized approach.

5. Event Styling and Decoration Services

If you have a flair for design, offering event styling and decoration is a lucrative side gig. Many hosts want their event to look cohesive and polished but lack the time or skills to execute the vision.

Styling includes selecting color schemes, choosing centerpieces and fabrics, and setting up the event space. This work pairs well with planning services, but you can also market it as a standalone option.

How to get started:

  • Build a portfolio demonstrating your design sensibility.
  • Source affordable and reusable decorations to keep your costs low.
  • Offer themed packages or “day-of setup” services for clients who plan their events but need help decorating.

6. Vendor Liaison Services

Negotiating with caterers, florists, DJs, and rental companies requires finesse and persistence. Some clients prefer to arrange their event themselves but feel overwhelmed by vendor negotiations. Offering a dedicated vendor liaison service allows you to fill this gap.

You can handle calls, price comparisons, contract reviews, and coordination on behalf of your clients. This role is crucial for many who want professional support without full event planning.

How to get started:

  • Develop strong relationships with local vendors to understand pricing and availability.
  • Create a straightforward pricing model, such as hourly rates or flat-fee for package deals.
  • Highlight your negotiation skills in your marketing materials.

7. Event Marketing and Promotion Consulting

Great events can’t succeed without good attendance. Helping clients promote their events—whether community fundraisers, concerts, or launch parties—can be a potent side hustle that pairs well with planning services.

You might create event pages, run social media ads, handle email campaigns, or secure local media coverage.

How to get started:

  • Gain some familiarity with digital marketing tools (Facebook Ads, Eventbrite, Mailchimp).
  • Show results from any promotion you’ve done, however informal.
  • Position yourself as the go-to resource for event organizers who want to maximize attendance.

8. Day-of Coordination Services

For some event hosts, the majority of planning is done by friends, family, or themselves, but having professional assistance at the event to keep everything running smoothly is highly desirable.

Day-of coordinators make sure the schedule is followed, vendors arrive on time, guests are directed appropriately, and any crises are managed discreetly. This role is less time-intensive during the planning process but critical for event success.

How to get started:

  • Market your reliability and flexibility for busy weekends or holidays.
  • Collaborate with full-service planners who might outsource day-of coordination.
  • Build testimonials focused on stress reduction and smooth execution.

9. Specialty Workshops and Classes

If you have expertise in certain event-related skills—floral arrangements, DIY decor, budgeting, or party game design—you could monetize that knowledge by teaching workshops or classes.

Online formats expand your reach nationally, while in-person workshops foster community and direct interaction.

How to get started:

  • Develop a curriculum with clear learning objectives.
  • Use social media and local community centers to promote your sessions.
  • Offer tiered pricing or bundled materials to enhance perceived value.

10. Event Photography Coordination

While event photography is a specialized skill, event planners can still incorporate a photography coordination service by partnering with local photographers.

You act as the liaison to schedule sessions, plan photo timelines, and ensure photographers capture the right moments. This service alleviates stress for clients who want professional memories but are unsure about photographer selection and scheduling.

How to get started:

  • Build a directory of trusted photographers in different specialties.
  • Create packages that pair photography with your event planning or coordination.
  • Use testimonials and client feedback to reinforce the value of your curated approach.

Growing Your Side Hustle Successfully

Build Strong Networks

In event planning, relationships are everything. Attend local business mixers, join professional organizations, and get involved in community groups. Vendors, venues, and past clients are your best sources of referrals.

Develop a Clear Brand and Online Presence

Your brand should communicate your unique style and professionalism. An updated website, active social media accounts, and client testimonials establish credibility. Content marketing through blogs or videos sharing planning tips can attract organic interest.

Manage Your Time Wisely

Balancing a side hustle with other responsibilities requires clear schedules, setting boundaries with clients, and smart delegation. Use project management tools to track deadlines and tasks, and avoid overcommitting.

Price Competitively but Fairly

Research your local market rates and consider your experience level. Offering tiered pricing can help you attract a broader range of clients while maximizing earnings on larger projects.

Keep Learning

Event trends and technology evolve rapidly. Attend industry webinars, read relevant publications, and seek feedback from clients to continuously refine your services.

Final Thoughts

Starting a side hustle in event planning and coordination isn’t just about earning extra money—it’s a pathway to turning your passion into a meaningful, creative enterprise. Whether you gravitate toward micro weddings, corporate events, virtual webinars, or children’s parties, there is room in the market for your unique talents.

Taking the plunge with a clear plan, smart marketing, and attentive client management will help you build momentum and turn your side gig into a rewarding part of your career journey. Find the niche that suits you best, and start designing unforgettable experiences one event at a time.

Ready to explore which event planning side hustle fits your lifestyle and skills? The world of events is vast, and your next great opportunity is waiting to be planned!
