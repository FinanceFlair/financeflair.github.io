The Best ETFs for Micro-Investing in 2023


In the era of digital transformation, micro-investing has become an accessible and practical entry point for novice investors and those looking to grow their wealth incrementally. With minimal upfront capital required, micro-investing empowers individuals to put small amounts of money into diversified, low-cost exchange-traded funds (ETFs). This approach allows for gradual portfolio building while minimizing risk.

For 2023, a dynamic range of ETFs offers compelling opportunities to enhance growth, achieve diversification, and build financial resilience. Whether you’re a beginner or someone looking to optimize your investments, here’s a deep dive into the best ETFs for micro-investing and why they stand out.

Why ETFs Are Ideal for Micro-Investing

Before outlining the top ETFs, it’s crucial to understand why ETFs are particularly suited for micro-investing strategies. Unlike investing in individual stocks, ETFs provide instant diversification by pooling funds into a basket of assets. This mitigates the risk associated with the volatility of a single security.

Additionally, ETFs often have low expense ratios, which is key for growth when starting with small amounts of capital. Moreover, fractional share investing—a feature offered by many modern micro-investing platforms—allows investors to buy portions of ETFs instead of whole units. This ensures accessibility regardless of the ETF’s market price.

Types of ETFs for Micro-Investing

Micro-investors can select ETFs based on their financial goals, risk tolerance, and areas of interest. Broadly, ETFs fall into categories such as equity-focused ETFs, bond ETFs, sector-specific funds, and those that emphasize global diversification. Let’s delve into the types of ETFs that should be on your radar in 2023.

1. Broad Market ETFs for Core Diversification

A broad-market ETF is an excellent starting point for micro-investors. These funds track large indexes, offering exposure to hundreds or even thousands of companies in a single investment. Here are some to consider:

  • SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust (SPY): This ETF tracks the S&P 500, providing exposure to 500 of the largest U.S. companies. It’s a cornerstone fund for any long-term portfolio due to its strong historical performance and diversified holdings.

  • Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF (VTI): If you’re looking for even broader exposure than the S&P 500, VTI covers the entire U.S. equity market, including small-, mid-, and large-cap stocks.

  • iShares Core MSCI Total International Stock ETF (IXUS): For those aiming to include an international component in their portfolio, IXUS adds geographic diversification by including stocks from developed and emerging markets outside of the United States.

2. Sector-Specific ETFs for Targeted Investments

Micro-investing in sector-specific ETFs can be a strategic approach to benefit from high-growth industries or trends shaping the future. Some popular sector-focused ETFs for 2023 include:

  • ARK Innovation ETF (ARKK): This actively managed ETF focuses on disruptive and innovative technologies. With holdings in genomics, blockchain, and artificial intelligence, ARKK aligns with investors seeking bold, growth-oriented opportunities.

  • Energy Select Sector SPDR Fund (XLE): With the global push for energy transition, XLE includes a mix of both traditional energy giants and renewable energy players, making it a great pick for those betting on energy’s evolving future.

  • Fidelity MSCI Information Technology Index ETF (FTEC): Technology remains a driving force in the market, and this ETF captures upside from major players like Apple, Microsoft, and Nvidia.

3. Low-Volatility ETFs for Risk-Averse Investors

Not everyone is comfortable with the roller-coaster ride of market volatility, especially when starting small. Low-volatility ETFs offer balanced growth with reduced downside risk.

  • iShares MSCI USA Min Vol Factor ETF (USMV): This ETF invests in companies with a history of lower price fluctuations, providing less turbulent growth compared to broader market funds.

  • Invesco S&P 500 Low Volatility ETF (SPLV): Similarly, SPLV focuses on 100 low-volatility stocks within the S&P 500, offering predictability and stability for cautious micro-investors.

4. Dividend ETFs for Income and Stability

Investing in dividend-paying ETFs can be an excellent way to grow your wealth while earning consistent cash flow. Dividends are especially attractive to micro-investors who prefer reinvesting these payments into fractional shares to compound their returns over time.

  • Vanguard Dividend Appreciation ETF (VIG): Composed of companies with a reliable history of increasing dividends, VIG offers growth potential alongside steady income.

  • Schwab U.S. Dividend Equity ETF (SCHD): Known for its high dividend yield and low fees, SCHD is an excellent choice for income-oriented investors aiming to maximize cash flow while keeping costs minimal.

5. Bond ETFs for Fixed-Income Diversification

Bond ETFs provide a ballast to portfolios that may be equity-heavy, reducing overall risk and volatility. Micro-investors seeking stability and dependable returns should consider these options:

  • iShares Core U.S. Aggregate Bond ETF (AGG): This ETF offers broad exposure to U.S. investment-grade bonds, making it a foundational fixed-income investment.

  • Vanguard Total Bond Market ETF (BND): Covering the entire U.S. bond market, BND provides a diversified mix of government and corporate bonds at different maturities.

6. ESG and Sustainable ETFs

As environmental, social, and governance (ESG) considerations gain traction among investors, micro-investors may want to include sustainable funds in their portfolios. ESG ETFs not only support socially responsible enterprises but also offer competitive returns.

  • iShares ESG Aware MSCI USA ETF (ESGU): This fund invests in U.S. companies scoring well on ESG metrics without sacrificing strong financial performance.

  • SPDR S&P 500 ESG ETF (EFIV): By focusing on sustainability-driven companies within the S&P 500, EFIV aligns with investors’ growing preferences for conscious investing.

7. Global and Emerging Markets ETFs

International exposure is key to a well-rounded investment portfolio. Micro-investors can explore international ETFs to benefit from high-growth opportunities in global markets.

  • Vanguard FTSE All-World ex-US ETF (VEU): This fund provides comprehensive exposure to developed and emerging markets outside the U.S., making it a versatile tool for diversifying geographically.

  • iShares MSCI Emerging Markets ETF (EEM): For those interested in regions with significant growth potential, EEM offers exposure to economies like China, India, and Brazil.

Features to Prioritize When Selecting ETFs for Micro-Investing

When evaluating ETFs for micro-investing purposes, consider the following factors:

  1. Expense Ratio: Lower expense ratios mean less of your return is consumed by fees. Opt for ETFs with minimal management costs.
  2. Liquidity: Ensure the ETF you choose is highly liquid, with sufficient trading volume to avoid wide bid-ask spreads and ensure seamless transactions.
  3. Dividend Reinvestment: Seek ETFs that offer the ability to reinvest dividends automatically. This will accelerate compounding over time.
  4. Tracking Precision: An ETF that closely tracks its underlying index or asset class will deliver more predictable returns.
  5. Risk Profile: Match the ETF’s risk level to your investment goals and time horizon. For instance, aggressive funds may suit long-term goals, while conservative ones are better for short-term stability.

How to Start Micro-Investing in ETFs

To begin micro-investing, you’ll need access to a brokerage or investing platform that supports fractional shares. Set up your account, determine your financial goals, and commit to consistent contributions, even if they’re small. Some platforms offer features such as automated investing or round-ups, turning everyday transactions into incremental investments.

Remember to maintain a diversified approach. While ETFs inherently provide diversification, combining funds across asset classes allows you to balance risks and opportunities effectively.

The Long-Term Potential of Micro-Investing With ETFs

Micro-investing may involve starting with modest sums, but over time, the power of compounding coupled with strategic ETF allocation can lead to substantial portfolio growth. By regularly contributing and embracing a disciplined investment strategy, even small investors can achieve significant financial milestones.

Whether you’re focusing on dividend income, sector-specific growth, or global diversification, ETFs offer a wide array of opportunities tailored to different objectives. Begin today, and let the small steps you take now pave the way for a prosperous financial future.
