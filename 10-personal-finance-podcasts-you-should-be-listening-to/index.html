10 Personal Finance Podcasts You Should Be Listening To


In a fast-paced world driven by constant financial shifts and economic challenges, understanding personal finance has never been more vital. Budgeting, investing, debt management, and wealth building are topics that everyone can benefit from understanding a little better. Fortunately, the podcast world offers a goldmine of content for anyone looking to improve their money management skills with expert advice, inspiring stories, and actionable tips.

Here’s a deep dive into 10 standout personal finance podcasts that will not only entertain but empower you to take charge of your financial future.

1. ChooseFI

ChooseFI stands for Choose Financial Independence, and it’s a favorite among listeners striving for early retirement or simply smarter money habits. Hosted by Brad Barrett and Jonathan Mendonsa, this podcast blends listener stories with interviews from top financial planners, entrepreneurs, and authors. It covers diverse topics, from optimizing 401(k) plans to side hustles that generate passive income.

What makes ChooseFI stand out is its community-driven approach. The hosts regularly incorporate feedback and questions from listeners, making each episode deeply relevant and practical. If you want to stretch your dollars further and achieve financial independence at a pace that suits your lifestyle, this podcast is a must-add to your playlist.

2. The Dave Ramsey Show

Dave Ramsey’s no-nonsense approach to money has sparked hundreds of thousands of listeners into action. His podcast extends the principles he’s known for via his radio show, including paying off debt using the famous “Baby Steps” method, budgeting, and mastering money’s emotional side.

Episodes often include live calls where Dave provides real-time advice on a variety of personal finance challenges, which makes it feel engaging and very relatable. If your goal is to dig out from debt and build a stable financial foundation, you’ll find tremendous value here. The confidence Dave imparts stems from decades of experience, making this podcast a staple for anyone interested in pragmatic money solutions.

3. Money for the Rest of Us

Hosted by J. David Stein, this podcast offers an educational yet accessible approach to investing and money management. What makes it unique is its focus on how the financial markets work and how individuals can navigate them without getting overwhelmed or misled by industry jargon.

From macroeconomics to personal investment strategies, the episodes provide in-depth explains broken down clearly. Stein’s calm and thoughtful delivery helps listeners understand complex concepts like bonds, stocks, inflation, and asset allocation with ease. It’s an excellent resource if you want to move beyond simple saving and explore investing with confidence.

4. Afford Anything

Paula Pant’s philosophy is simple: you can afford anything, but you can’t afford everything. This podcast digs into financial independence, real estate investing, and entrepreneurship. Each episode challenges listeners to rethink the relationship between money and lifestyle choices.

Paula’s interviews often highlight unusual paths to financial freedom, including unconventional investing strategies and bold career shifts. If you are inspired by stories of people who have escaped the 9-to-5 grind or taken creative approaches to income, this podcast provides both inspiration and actionable insight. You’ll learn how to prioritize spending on what matters most to you while optimizing financial decisions for long-term happiness.

5. BiggerPockets Money Podcast

BiggerPockets is well-known in the real estate investing space, but their money podcast covers much more. Hosts Mindy Jensen and Scott Trench bring in a diverse range of guests—from financial planners to real estate experts—offering practical advice on budgeting, debt payoff, investing, and entrepreneurship.

Particularly helpful are their episodes on real estate strategies and how to use property investments as tools for wealth. But beyond that, the podcast also delves into general money management tactics that appeal to everyday listeners. The conversational tone makes complex financial topics relatable, making this one a favorite for both beginners and seasoned investors alike.

6. The Stacking Benjamins Show

If you’re looking for money advice mixed with humor and a lighthearted approach, The Stacking Benjamins Show hits the mark. Hosted by Joe Saul-Sehy and OG, this podcast combines interviews, listener questions, and expert breakdowns of personal finance topics.

It covers everything from investing basics to retirement strategies, making it a comprehensive weekly dose of money knowledge. The dynamic between the hosts brings energy and relatability to topics that can sometimes feel intimidating. Whether you’re commuting or working out, this podcast is an enjoyable way to stay financially informed without the dryness.

7. So Money with Farnoosh Torabi

Farnoosh Torabi is an award-winning personal finance expert and storyteller whose podcast delves into the emotional and practical sides of money. From dealing with financial anxiety to building wealth through savvy investments and entrepreneurship, So Money offers candid conversations with top industry leaders and everyday people alike.

What’s particularly refreshing is the human lens Farnoosh applies to so many finance topics. Listeners appreciate the blend of tactical advice and personal stories that destigmatize money struggles, making this podcast a compassionate companion on your financial journey.

8. HerMoney with Jean Chatzky

Designed primarily for women but valuable to all, HerMoney focuses on empowering listeners to make confident financial decisions. Jean Chatzky brings decades of experience as a financial journalist and educator, covering a range of topics from retirement planning to navigating money conversations within relationships.

What sets this podcast apart is its ability to simplify financial concepts and make them accessible regardless of your background or income level. It also explores the unique financial challenges women face, such as wage gaps and investing post-divorce, blending expert insight with practical tips.

9. The Mad Fientist

Targeting listeners interested in the FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement, The Mad Fientist dives deep into strategies for drastic saving, tax optimization, and investing to accelerate the path to financial independence.

Interviews with successful FIRE adherents and thoughtful analysis of financial systems set this podcast apart for those who prefer technical breakdowns and data-driven insights. It’s an ideal pick for people looking to execute a detailed plan toward retiring early or achieving financial freedom without following the traditional career timeline.

10. Planet Money

Planet Money by NPR takes a broader economic lens in exploring money from a storytelling and news perspective. While it covers macroeconomic trends, policy, and world finance, its approachable format and clever narrative bring light on how these forces affect our personal finances.

The podcast is a great supplement to the other personal finance shows, offering context and helping listeners connect individual money decisions to larger economic systems. It’s engaging for anyone curious about the why behind financial news and economic shifts impacting income, job markets, and investments.

Taking Control of Your Financial Future, One Episode at a Time

The beauty of these podcasts is their accessibility—ding your headphones, and suddenly you’re being coached by some of the smartest minds in personal finance, from anywhere in the world. Whether you want to crush debt, start investing, or just build healthier money habits, these shows provide comprehensive strategies and diverse perspectives.

Listening regularly can transform how you think about money. They demystify jargon, replace financial fear with confidence, and teach you to make choices aligned with your goals. Consider subscribing to one or two that resonate most with your current financial situation and long-term ambitions. You’ll find that a little financial literacy goes a long way in freeing up your time, reducing stress, and empowering wealth creation.

Dive deep into budgeting tips, investment wisdom, and inspiring success stories—all while you’re driving, working out, or relaxing at home. Your wallet will thank you for the education, and your future self will appreciate the groundwork you lay today.

No matter what stage you’re at in your money journey, these personal finance podcasts offer valuable guidance, fresh perspectives, and ongoing motivation. In a sea of information, they help you anchor to principles that encourage sustainable habits and mindful financial freedom. So pick a podcast, plug in, and let the wealth of knowledge flow. Your financial future is waiting!
