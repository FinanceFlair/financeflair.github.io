Why Every Investor Should Consider Sustainable Investing


Think back to the start of the 21st century when sustainability was just beginning to pepper the edges of mainstream conversation. Climate change was certainly a known issue, but it was yet to sink its teeth into everyday life on the same level it does today.

Fast-forward to 2021, and sustainability is taking center stage. From schools to workplaces to personal lives, we are now properly waking up to the importance of sustainable practices. Why, you ask, should investing be any different?

Introduction to Sustainable Investing

Sustainable investing, also termed as socially responsible investing or impact investing, is the conscious attempt to make investments that not only generate a financial return, but also consciously pursue social good or environmental benefits.

Appealing Reasons to Adopt Sustainable Investing

  1. Mission-driven investing: Isn’t it time to lay your money where your values lie? Sustainable investing allows you to combine your financial and personal goals. If you value a cleaner, greener environment, for instance, you’d prefer investing in a company leading the charge rather than another perhaps less conscientious business.

  2. Cramer returns: Environmental, social and governance (ESG) criteria have been shown to increase long-term returns for investors— providing you with a better bang for your buck! Responsible investing considers a company’s sustainability practices during the investment decision. Companies that prioritize ESG criteria have exhibited better risk management, lower liabilities, reduced regulatory fines, and higher employee productivity.

  3. Aligning with a Better Future: Would you like your investment to be a part of the problem or the solution? Sustainable investing can and will impact the world positively. By aligning your investment with companies and funds that are green, socially just, and sustainably focused, it’s not just your investments that grow, but the world around you too.

  4. More avenues to diversify: You must be thinking, “Anything related to sustainability means it’s all about investing in clean energy or going green.” Wrong! There’s more freedom than you think in the realm of sustainable investing. Money can be channeled into social impact bonds or even sustainable infrastructure.

  5. Revolutionizing investing: Sustainable investing is no longer just an ’eco-niche’. It’s becoming furniture in the investment space as more pension funds, university endowments and individuals move towards it.

Critics of Sustainable Investing

  1. Higher risk: A common argument against sustainable investments is the potential for higher risk, given the relatively underdeveloped and complicated nature of the market. It is essential to consider such risks and opt for rigorous research, analysis, and investment strategies.

  2. No guarantee: Even a well-diversified, mission-aligned portfolio cannot guarantee positive social or environmental impacts, nor steady returns for that matter, given the numerous uncontrollable and unpredictable factors.

The Future of Sustainable Investing

Since the 2015 Paris Agreement and the 2019 release of the Taskforce for Nature-related Disclosures (TNFD) proposal, companies and governments are taking sustainability more seriously. This likely paves way for more opportunities in this sector, while also emphasizing competitive forces for the smallest companies unable to keep up.

In this rapidly evolving landscape, investors need to gear up, learn, and invest accordingly. Make money and a difference. It’s the future, and it’s a win-win game. So why avoid? Not to mention, it’s high time to put your financial power to good use. Because as it turns out, your wallet and your values aren’t mutually exclusive. You just need to know where to look.

Conclusion

Now, you know that butterfly in your stomach every time you read the news isn’t just worry. It’s the unanswered question— what can I do to make a difference? The good news is you’re not powerless. With your investment, sustainable or otherwise, you have the power to shape a better world. It’s time to make every penny count, for your pocket and the planet. Start considering sustainable investing today.
