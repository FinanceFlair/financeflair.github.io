The Benefits of Ethical Investing for Wealth Preservation


As you grow your wealth, you're likely to hear countless investment options thrown at you, each promising astronomical returns and high-risk, high-reward possibilities. However, have you ever stopped to consider the true cost of investing in companies that prioritize profits over people and the planet? In today's rapidly changing business landscape, it's time to rethink the way you approach investing and explore the benefits of ethical investing for wealth preservation. A New Era of Investment For decades, the traditional investment model has been centered around growth, at any cost. This has led to a culture where companies are incentivized to cut corners, exploit resources, and disregard the well-being of the environment and their workers. But as the world grapples with pressing issues such as climate change, social inequality, and corporate malfeasance, investors are beginning to demand a change. The Good Jobs First, a non-profit organization that tracks government incentives for job creation, revealed that between 2008 and 2015, companies paid an estimated $90 billion in tax incentives for contemplating moving their operations to low-cost locations or tax havens. The Potential of Impact Investing Impact investing, a style of investing that aims to generate both financial returns and positive social or environmental impact, is gaining traction. According to a 2020 report by the Global Impact Investing Network (GIIN), impact investing more than doubled in 2020, reaching $502 billion in assets under management globally. This new investment paradigm allows you to review the activities which your money supports, be it renewable energy, education, or social justice. By choosing to invest in companies that prioritize people and the planet, you're not only diversifying your portfolio, but also aligning your wealth with your values. Increased Returns through Responsible Business Practices While many believe that socially responsible investing will come at the cost of returns, this is a common misconception. Research conducted by the Harvard Business Review has shown that companies with strong environmental and social performance, such as responsible labor practices, tend to outperform their industry peers in terms of financial performance. According to the Keywords: Environmental Data Commons, which aggregates sustainability data, companies that surpass industry peers on environmental concerns often have a competitive edge. This is because consumers, including investors, are increasingly motivated by values-driven purchasing and sustainable practices. Increased ESG Risk Management Ethical investing can also mitigate risks to your investment. The case in point is the dawn of ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) disclosures. As interest in ESG considerations grows, companies are compelled to adapt and demonstrate ethical stewardship or risk facing a reclassification of investors, public scrutiny, and challenges to long-term credibility. This represents lower perils for investors, reducing the risk of mismanagement, long-term non-performance, identity crises, and potential damage to brands due to unethical business behavior. Addressing Lack of Awareness and Knowledge Picking up the thread of business and market concerns, investors need support and guidance. To make educated investment decisions, you must navigate an abundance of data despite the typical slowness of official reporting and inconstant interpretations of industry accountability metrics.
