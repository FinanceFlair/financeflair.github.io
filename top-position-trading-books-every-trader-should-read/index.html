Top Position Trading Books Every Trader Should Read


In the intricate landscape of financial markets, the ability to identify and capitalize on long-term trends distinguishes exceptional traders from the rest. Position trading sits at the intersection of technical analysis, macroeconomic research, and psychological fortitude, offering a powerful framework for those who wish to hold assets for weeks, months, or even years. Whether you are transitioning from day trading or seeking to deepen your expertise, certain books stand out for their depth, clarity, and actionable strategies.

This curated list dives into the most valuable resources that can help refine your approach, boost your returns, and anchor your confidence amidst market volatility. These are the must-read position trading books that every serious trader should have within arm’s reach.

  1. Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini

If the goal is to master the art of holding profitable trades over extended periods, Mark Minervini’s “Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard” offers a blend of inspiration, technical precision, and real-world experience. Minervini—well known for his incredible trading records—lays out the SEPA method (Specific Entry Point Analysis) which brilliantly combines technical analysis with solid risk management.

Highlights:

  • Detailed explanation of SEPA and its application to position trades.
  • Real case studies illustrating winning trades and common pitfalls.
  • Emphasis on risk, reward, and portfolio management.
  • Tactics for identifying leading stocks and lasting trends.

Minervini’s conversational style bridges the gap between advanced concepts and actionable tips, making this book an indispensable starting point for anyone ready to venture into position trading.

  1. Trend Following by Michael W. Covel

Michael Covel’s “Trend Following” is often regarded as a trading classic. While the book spans various trading styles, its principles are deeply aligned with position trading. Covel strips away the myth of prediction and instead advocates for trading with the trend, which aligns perfectly with the long-term outlook of position traders.

Highlights:

  • In-depth interviews with legendary traders like Ed Seykota and Richard Dennis.
  • Thorough exploration of systematic trading vs. discretionary approaches.
  • An analysis of psychological factors that hold traders back from maximizing profits.
  • Explanations of how to develop trading systems that stay profitable across markets.

For those seeking to adopt a process-driven approach and ride multi-month or multi-year trends, Covel’s insights are truly valuable.

  1. Reminiscences of a Stock Operator by Edwin Lefèvre

A recurring recommendation in trading circles, “Reminiscences of a Stock Operator” is a thinly-veiled fictitious memoir of Jesse Livermore, one of history’s most famous traders. Despite its age, the book is packed with trading wisdom relevant to modern position traders.

Highlights:

  • Candid anecdotes on market psychology, timing, and risk management.
  • Timeless lessons on patience, discipline, and the struggle of holding winning positions.
  • Insights into speculative booms, busts, and the cycles of human behavior.

Lefèvre’s writing makes this classic read as engaging as a novel, while the embedded lessons about buying right and sitting tight are as valid today as they ever were.

  1. The New Market Wizards by Jack D. Schwager

Jack Schwager’s follow-up to his iconic Market Wizards series, this book brings together interviews with dozens of professional traders. While covering a spectrum of strategies, it pays close attention to traders who specialize in position trading, offering an inside look at the decision-making process behind substantial multi-week or multi-month trades.

Highlights:

  • Fascinating interviews revealing different approaches to position trading.
  • Emphasis on adapting strategies to changing market conditions.
  • Techniques for evaluating risk and maintaining emotional balance.
  • Discussions on system development, testing, and execution.

This book is a goldmine for anyone wanting to absorb wisdom from the world’s top traders and understand the mindset necessary for sustained, long-term trading success.

  1. How to Make Money in Stocks by William J. O’Neil

While often recommended for growth-oriented investors, William O’Neil’s landmark work is equally transformative for position traders pursuing high-potential stocks. The CAN SLIM method—O’Neil’s signature system—focuses on uncovering stocks with the greatest likelihood to produce outsized returns over several months or years.

Highlights:

  • Step-by-step breakdown of the CAN SLIM investing system.
  • Guidance on buying with a catalyst and selling at the right time.
  • Detailed chart analysis to identify base formations and breakouts.
  • Explanations on market timing for optimal entry and exit.

O’Neil’s strategy is rooted in technical and fundamental analysis, making it an exceptional blueprint for position traders looking to systematically grow their portfolios.

  1. Market Wizards by Jack D. Schwager

The original “Market Wizards” still commands respect as the quintessential collection of trading interviews. Readers are treated to firsthand stories from legendary trend-followers and position traders who have thrived during both bull and bear markets.

Highlights:

  • Anecdotes from trend-following traders, including Richard Dennis and Paul Tudor Jones.
  • Focus on the psychological demands of holding winners and cutting losers.
  • Profound discussions of risk control and the emotional challenges inherent in position trading.

Gain invaluable insights by learning how top traders structure their trades to maximize gains over months instead of minutes. Schwager’s ability to extract actionable advice from each conversation makes this book unmissable.

  1. The Art and Science of Technical Analysis by Adam Grimes

Position trading rests heavily on technical analysis, and Adam Grimes’ work offers an advanced yet practical approach to understanding price action. Grimes sets himself apart by connecting theory with practical application, making it ideal for position traders who need to distinguish between random noise and real opportunity.

Highlights:

  • Comprehensive guide to chart patterns and their context within market structure.
  • Analysis on how to identify strong trends and actionable reversals.
  • In-depth strategies for position sizing and trade management over longer time frames.
  • Evidence-based approach to dispel common trading myths.

This book empowers traders to blend technical skills with statistical thinking, a crucial combination for those navigating weeks or months-long trades.

  1. Japanese Candlestick Charting Techniques by Steve Nison

Steve Nison’s pioneering book dragged candlestick charting out of obscurity and into the mainstream. For position traders, candlestick patterns offer a nuanced perspective on market sentiment and the supply-demand dynamic. Nison’s explanations are clear, detailed, and practical.

Highlights:

  • Thorough catalogue of major candlestick patterns, both bullish and bearish.
  • Practical examples of how to integrate candlesticks with other forms of technical analysis.
  • Historical context on the effectiveness of candlestick signals in trending markets.
  • Tips to avoid false signals and recognize sustainable moves.

Armed with this knowledge, position traders can increase the accuracy of their entries and add context to price action over larger time frames.

  1. Super Trader by Van K. Tharp

Van Tharp’s focus is unique: he tackles the psychology, risk management, and position sizing strategies that underpin long-term trading success. “Super Trader” goes beyond chart patterns to address the internal game of trading, which is especially relevant for position traders navigating emotionally charged markets.

Highlights:

  • Methods to develop a winning trading mindset and process.
  • Strategies for creating a personalized position trading system.
  • Frameworks for optimizing risk and maximizing upside on each trade.
  • Actionable exercises for developing discipline and emotional resilience.

Trading is as much about execution as analysis; Tharp’s work ensures traders go into each trade with the proper mindset and toolkit.

  1. Al Brooks’ Trading Price Action Series

Al Brooks’ trio—Trading Price Action Trends, Trading Price Action Trading Ranges, and Trading Price Action Reversals—represents an exhaustive manual on price action theory. Brooks’ focus is on understanding the market’s natural rhythm, which perfectly aligns with the needs of position traders looking to spot and ride long-term trends.

Highlights:

  • Nuanced explanation of price bars and their significance at different time frames.
  • Techniques for identifying exhaustion, momentum, and continuation patterns.
  • Clear guidelines for trade entry, exit, and stop-loss placement over extended holdings.
  • Applications for trend trading, breakout strategies, and reversal recognition.

Brooks’ work is demanding, but the payoff is mastery over chart-based trading.

How to Choose the Best Position Trading Books for Your Needs

Selecting the right resource can catalyze rapid growth in trading skill and confidence. Here’s how to make your pick from the rich offerings above:

1. Define Your Style

Are you more inclined toward technical analysis, or do you prefer to blend fundamentals with your trades? Books like “How to Make Money in Stocks” and “Japanese Candlestick Charting Techniques” provide rigorous technical setups, while “Super Trader” and “Trend Following” focus more on psychology and system development.

2. Assess Your Experience Level

Beginners might gravitate towards narrative-driven works like “Reminiscences of a Stock Operator,” which distills complex lessons into memorable stories. More experienced traders may get greater value from Al Brooks or Adam Grimes, whose advanced techniques require solid background knowledge.

3. Leverage Multiple Perspectives

No single book will prepare you for every eventuality in the markets. Mixing works that focus on practical strategies with those that address trader psychology (such as “Super Trader”) gives you a 360-degree view.

4. Put Ideas to Test

As you conquer each book, test the principles in a simulated or low-risk environment. Practical experience cements theory and helps you internalize what resonates with your temperament and strategy.

The Enduring Value of Books in a Real-Time Trading World

In an era dominated by social media hot takes and algorithm-driven recommendations, the deep dives offered by books remain unmatched for building a robust framework. While online courses and signal services can be useful, the slow and careful construction of expertise through reading gives traders the edge required for consistent success.

Position trading demands patience, discipline, and an arsenal of proven strategies. With any of the books above, you’ll be equipped not just with tactics, but also the context and confidence to make the approach work for you.

Elevate your trading. Fill your bookshelves. And as you turn each page, remember: the real profits often come not simply from trades, but from the wisdom gained in pursuit of mastery.
