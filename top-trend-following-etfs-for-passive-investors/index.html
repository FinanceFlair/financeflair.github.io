Top Trend Following ETFs for Passive Investors


Investing in the stock market has evolved significantly over the past decades, offering a plethora of strategies tailored to different risk tolerances and time horizons. Among these strategies, trend following has emerged as a captivating approach for investors seeking to capture sustained market momentum without frequent trading. For passive investors looking to leverage this methodology with minimal hands-on management, trend following Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) present an appealing solution.

This comprehensive guide explores the concept of trend following, why it appeals to passive investors, and highlights some of the top trend following ETFs available in the market today.

Understanding Trend Following in Investing

Trend following is a strategy that seeks to identify and capitalize on market momentum by investing in assets showing upward price trends while avoiding or shorting those in downward trends. The approach eschews predicting market direction or fundamental values; instead, it relies on historical price movements and technical indicators to guide buy and sell decisions.

The core idea is simple: markets often move in sustained trends rather than random walks. By systematically identifying these trends through quantitative models or algorithmic signals, investors aim to ride profits as momentum builds and exit when trends reverse.

Why Does This Strategy Work?

  1. Behavioral Biases: Investor psychology—such as herding behavior and overreaction—can lead to prolonged trends.
  2. Market Momentum: Empirical evidence suggests that assets gaining in price tend to continue performing well over medium-term horizons.
  3. Volatility Capture: Trend following can exploit market volatility by dynamically adjusting positions across assets.

Why Trend Following ETFs Appeal to Passive Investors

Passive investors generally prefer low-cost, low-maintenance investment vehicles aligned with long-term wealth accumulation. Traditional passive strategies involve buy-and-hold approaches tracking capitalization-weighted indices, but trend following ETFs offer a unique blend of systematic asset selection embedded within a passive wrapper.

Benefits include:

  • Systematic Discipline: Trades based on rules reduce emotional decision-making.
  • Diversification: Many trend following ETFs invest across multiple asset classes.
  • Lower Turnover Than Active Trading: Though more active than pure indexing, many trend-following ETFs balance turnover with strategic timing.
  • Accessibility: ETFs trade like stocks, allowing easy market access and liquidity.
  • Risk Management: These funds often seek to avoid downturns by exiting positions when negative trends are detected.

While trend following ETFs aren’t purely passive in the traditional sense, they represent an automated approach allowing investors to benefit from momentum without daily management.

Criteria for Selecting Top Trend Following ETFs

When evaluating trend following ETFs, investors should consider a variety of factors to find products matching their risk profile and goals:

  1. Underlying Assets: Whether the ETF focuses on equities, commodities, bonds, or a combination.
  2. Strategy Transparency: Clear explanation of how momentum signals are generated and implemented.
  3. Expense Ratio: Lower fees help preserve returns over the long term.
  4. Historical Performance: Track record through different market cycles.
  5. Liquidity and Trading Volume: Ensures smooth transactions and minimal bid-ask spreads.
  6. Fund Size and Age: Established funds are generally more reliable.
  7. Risk Management Techniques: Look for volatility controls or drawdown protection features.

With these parameters in mind, let’s explore some top trend following ETFs for passive investors.

Top Trend Following ETFs for Passive Investors

1. iMGP Winton Strategic Alpha UCITS ETF (WINT)

  • Overview: This ETF employs a trend following strategy developed by Winton Capital, a pioneer in quantitative investing. It dynamically allocates across global futures markets covering equities, commodities, currencies, and bonds.
  • Key Features:
    • Focuses on medium-term trends.
    • Utilizes robust risk controls to limit drawdowns.
    • Diversified exposure less dependent on traditional equity markets.
  • Why It Stands Out: Combines a sophisticated, research-driven approach with broad diversification, making it suitable for those seeking absolute return profiles.
  • Expense Ratio: Approximately 0.90% (varies by share class).
  • Considerations: Suitable for investors comfortable with alternative risk exposures.

2. Invesco S&P 500 Momentum ETF (SPMO)

  • Overview: Although not a pure trend following fund, SPMO applies a momentum filter within the S&P 500, investing in stocks with strong recent performance.
  • Key Features:
    • Focuses on equity momentum within a large-cap universe.
    • Lower expense ratio relative to many alternative ETFs.
    • Transparent rules-based approach.
  • Why It Stands Out: Ideal for investors wanting exposure to momentum with familiar equity holdings.
  • Expense Ratio: Approximately 0.35%.
  • Considerations: Concentrated in equities, may exhibit higher volatility during market reversals.

3. Global X Guru Momentum ETF (GURU)

  • Overview: This ETF tracks firms with strong price momentum and earnings revision trends, blending both fundamental and technical signals.
  • Key Features:
    • Incorporates analyst sentiment and price momentum.
    • Exposure to mid and large-cap U.S. equities.
  • Why It Stands Out: Combines momentum with quality factors, providing a slightly hybrid approach.
  • Expense Ratio: Around 0.50%.
  • Considerations: Not a traditional trend following ETF but relevant for investors targeting growth via momentum.

4. AlphaClone Alternative Alpha ETF (ALFA)

  • Overview: By tracking hedge fund replication strategies, ALFA incorporates trend following among other quantitative signals.
  • Key Features:
    • Multi-strategy quantitative exposure.
    • Focus on stocks favored by hedge funds.
  • Why It Stands Out: Offers access to hedge fund-like strategies including trend following, in an ETF wrapper.
  • Expense Ratio: About 0.99%.
  • Considerations: Higher fees reflect complex strategy; investors must be comfortable with potential volatility.

5. ProShares Ultra S&P500 Momentum ETF (UWM)

  • Overview: This leverage-based fund aims to offer twice the daily return of the S&P 500 Momentum Index.
  • Key Features:
    • Aggressive exposure for momentum investors.
    • High turnover and complex risk profile.
  • Why It Stands Out: Designed for traders and investors with appetite for high risk/reward.
  • Expense Ratio: Approximately 0.95%.
  • Considerations: Not suitable for all investors, especially long-term buy-and-hold due to daily leverage reset risk.

How to Incorporate Trend Following ETFs Into Your Portfolio

Integrating trend following ETFs can complement traditional buy-and-hold investments by adding a dynamic dimension that may reduce drawdowns and improve overall risk-adjusted returns.

Portfolio Construction Tips:

  • Diversify Across Asset Classes: Combining trend following funds focusing on equities, commodities, and bonds can help smooth volatility.
  • Balance with Core Index Funds: Use trend following ETFs as satellites within a broader portfolio dominated by low-cost index funds.
  • Adjust Allocation to Risk Tolerance: Given the varying volatility profiles of trend ETFs, scale exposure accordingly.
  • Monitor Correlation: Track how trend following ETFs correlate with existing holdings to enhance diversification benefits.
  • Stay Informed on Strategy: Understand the rebalancing frequency and signal triggers to avoid surprises during market stress.

Potential Drawbacks and Considerations for Passive Investors

Though appealing, trend following ETFs come with some caveats:

  • Higher Fees: Due to active management elements, fees tend to be higher than the simplest index funds.
  • Variable Performance: While trend following does well in trending markets, it may lag during sideways or choppy conditions.
  • Complexity: Understanding the underlying model is crucial for investor confidence.
  • Tax Implications: Higher turnover can lead to short-term capital gains.
  • Market Dependence: When markets are volatile but non-directional, trend following strategies might generate false signals or whipsaws.

Investors need to weigh these factors relative to their investment horizon and psychological readiness.

Final Thoughts

For passive investors wishing to tap into the power of momentum without the stress and timing demands of active trading, trend following ETFs represent an intriguing middle ground. By automating the identification and exploitation of market trends, these funds seek to add value and improve risk management across market cycles.

Selecting the optimal ETF depends on individual preferences around asset exposure, risk appetite, fees, and strategy complexity. Incorporating one or more of these funds thoughtfully into a diversified portfolio can potentially enhance returns and reduce vulnerability to market downturns.

As with any investment, diligent research and ongoing portfolio review remain essential to aligning exposures with personal financial goals.

Investing in trend following ETFs offers a dynamic and systematic way to engage with market momentum while maintaining a passive stance. Knowing your options and their intricacies empowers you to make informed decisions that best suit your path to financial growth.
