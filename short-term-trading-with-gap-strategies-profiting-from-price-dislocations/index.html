Short Term Trading with Gap Strategies: Profiting from Price Dislocations


In the fast-paced world of trading, short-term strategies can be a valuable tool for investors looking to capitalize on market opportunities. One such strategy is gap trading, which involves profiting from price dislocations that occur when a stock opens significantly higher or lower than its previous closing price. This blog post will delve into the intricacies of short-term trading with gap strategies and how investors can make the most of these price discrepancies. Before we dive into the strategies, let’s first understand what gaps are. In the context of trading, a gap occurs when there is a significant difference between the price at which a stock opens and its previous closing price. Gaps can be categorized into three types:

  1. Full Gap Up: This occurs when the opening price is higher than the previous closing price, and there is no overlap in price between the two periods.
  2. Full Gap Down: This occurs when the opening price is lower than the previous closing price, and there is no overlap in price between the two periods.
  3. Partial Gap: This occurs when there is some overlap in price between the previous closing price and the opening price.

Strategies for Profiting from Gaps

Now that we have a basic understanding of gaps, let’s explore some gap trading strategies that can help investors profit from these price dislocations.

1. Fade the Gap

The fade the gap strategy involves taking a contrarian approach to trading gaps. When a stock gaps up, meaning it opens significantly higher than its previous closing price, a trader employing the fade the gap strategy would look to short sell the stock, betting that the price will eventually reverse and fill the gap. Conversely, when a stock gaps down, a trader would look to buy the stock, anticipating a price reversal.

2. Gap and Go

The gap and go strategy is the opposite of the fade the gap strategy. With this approach, traders look to capitalize on the momentum created by the gap. When a stock gaps up, traders would look to buy the stock, expecting the upward momentum to continue. Similarly, when a stock gaps down, traders would short sell the stock, anticipating further downside momentum.

3. Gap Fill

The gap fill strategy involves waiting for the price to retrace and fill the gap before taking a position. When a stock gaps up, traders would wait for the price to drop and fill the gap partially or completely before buying the stock. Conversely, when a stock gaps down, traders would wait for the price to rise and fill the gap before short selling the stock.

4. Breakaway Gap

The breakaway gap strategy is used when a stock gaps up or down on the back of significant news or events. Traders would look to take a position in the direction of the gap, expecting the stock to continue moving in that direction.

Risk Management and Conclusion

While gap trading strategies can be profitable, it’s important for traders to exercise proper risk management. Gaps can be unpredictable, and there is always the risk of a gap failing to fill or reversing unexpectedly. Traders should set stop-loss orders to limit potential losses and have a clear exit strategy in place.

In conclusion, short-term trading with gap strategies can be a lucrative endeavor for investors who understand the dynamics of price dislocations. By employing the right strategy and managing risk effectively, traders can capitalize on these gaps and profit from the market’s inefficiencies. Remember to always conduct thorough research and practice disciplined trading to increase your chances of success.
