The Best Dividend ETFs for Passive Income in 2023


Investing in dividend exchange-traded funds (ETFs) has become increasingly popular for building a reliable stream of passive income. These ETFs are particularly attractive because they enable investors to benefit from the stability and growth of dividend-paying companies, while also enjoying the diversification that ETFs inherently provide.

As we take a closer look at 2023, uncertainty in the global economy has prompted many investors to seek out safer and more consistent returns. Dividend ETFs can offer just that—steady income, plus the potential for capital appreciation over time. If you’re searching for ways to grow wealth passively, while minimizing the volatility associated with individual stock holdings, dividend ETFs deserve your attention.

In this guide, we’ll explore some of the best dividend ETFs available in 2023, discuss what makes them stand out, and provide insights into how they can play a vital role in your investment strategy.

What Are Dividend ETFs?

Before diving into specific funds, let’s establish a clear understanding of what dividend ETFs are and why they are so compelling as an investment option. Dividend ETFs are funds that hold a portfolio of dividend-paying stocks. When companies share a portion of their earnings with shareholders in the form of dividends, ETF investors receive a share based on the number of units they own.

The ETFs are managed strategically to keep the dividend income flowing consistently. This makes them ideal for income-seeking investors, retirees aiming to cover living costs, or anyone looking to supplement their active income.

Not all dividend ETFs are created equal, though. They can vary based on the types of stocks they contain—for example, high-yield dividend equities, dividend-growth-focused companies, or even dividend-paying companies from specific sectors or geographies. Choosing the best ETF depends on your individual goals, risk tolerance, and long-term investment strategy.

Key Characteristics of Top Dividend ETFs

Understanding what makes a dividend ETF stand out is critical before jumping into a list of the best options. Consider the following factors:

  • Dividend Yield: High-yield dividend ETFs are particularly suited for income-focused investors who want immediate cash flow. However, funds with extremely high yields can sometimes signal risky underperforming stocks.
  • Dividend Growth Sustainability: Dividend growth ETFs focus on companies with a history of consistently increasing their payouts, even in challenging market conditions.
  • Expense Ratio: Since ETFs are passively managed, they tend to have lower expense ratios than mutual funds. However, the lower the expense ratio, the more of your returns stay in your pocket.
  • Diversification: Sectoral and geographical diversification is critical to mitigating risk. A good dividend ETF isn’t too concentrated in one industry or region.
  • Total Return Potential: Dividend ETFs that offer both reliable income and capital appreciation are often the most attractive for long-term investors.

With these characteristics in mind, let’s take a look at some of the best-performing and most reliable dividend ETFs for passive income in 2023.

Top Dividend ETFs for 2023

1. Vanguard High Dividend Yield ETF (VYM)

One of the most popular names in the world of dividend ETFs is the Vanguard High Dividend Yield ETF. Managed by Vanguard, a household name in low-cost investing, VYM provides investors exposure to high-quality, high-dividend-paying companies.

Key Features:

  • Holdings: The ETF holds a broad range of U.S.-based companies across various sectors, including financials, healthcare, and consumer goods.
  • Dividend Yield: Slightly higher than market averages, offering consistent passive income.
  • Low Expense Ratio: With an expense ratio of only 0.06%, investors retain most of their earnings.
  • Stability: VYM heavily weights toward well-established, blue-chip companies, making it a safer option for conservative investors.

As a diversified U.S.-focused fund, this ETF is a great starting option for anyone interested in passive income.

2. Schwab U.S. Dividend Equity ETF (SCHD)

The Schwab U.S. Dividend Equity ETF has earned its spot as a top-tier choice for passive-income enthusiasts. It tracks high-quality dividend-paying U.S. companies, ensuring only financially sound corporations are included in the fund.

Key Features:

  • Strong Dividend Growth: This ETF focuses on companies with sustainable dividend growth.
  • Performance: Historically, SCHD has been known for outperforming its peers in terms of total return.
  • Ultra-Low Expense Ratio: At 0.06%, it is one of the most affordable dividend ETFs on the market.
  • Sector Allocations: Balanced exposure to industries like information technology, consumer staples, and industrials.

SCHD is a highly efficient fund when it comes to combining dividend income with potential for long-term market performance.

3. SPDR S&P Dividend ETF (SDY)

The SPDR S&P Dividend ETF tracks the S&P High-Yield Dividend Aristocrats Index, meaning it contains companies with at least 20 consecutive years of dividend increases. Such consistency adds an extra layer of confidence for income-seeking investors.

Key Features:

  • Long Track Record: Focuses on dividend aristocrats, which are some of the most reliable dividend payers in the market.
  • Sector Diversification: Includes holdings across sectors such as utilities, financials, and real estate.
  • Steady Yield: Provides a solid stream of recurring income, even in volatile market conditions.

SDY is particularly suitable for those who value both income consistency and the long-term growth potential of market leaders.

4. iShares International Select Dividend ETF (IDV)

Looking to diversify beyond U.S. borders? The iShares International Select Dividend ETF focuses on high-dividend-paying stocks from markets outside the United States. This makes it an excellent choice for those seeking greater geographic diversification.

Key Features:

  • Global Diversification: Invests primarily in developed markets, with a focus on Europe and the Asia-Pacific region.
  • High Yield Potential: Due to the nature of international companies, the yield tends to be much higher than U.S.-focused ETFs.
  • Currency Exposure: Offers the added benefit of currency diversification, albeit at a slightly increased risk.

IDV can complement your portfolio by tapping into the potential of international dividend-paying stocks.

5. Invesco High Yield Equity Dividend Achievers ETF (PEY)

The Invesco High Yield Equity Dividend Achievers ETF offers exposure to companies with a strong track record of consistently increasing dividends, making it an appealing choice for income and growth investors alike.

Key Features:

  • Focused Index: Tracks the NASDAQ U.S. Dividend Achievers 50 Index, emphasizing high-growth dividend companies.
  • Robust Yield: Provides one of the higher yields in the dividend ETF space.
  • Expense Efficiency: Charges a reasonable expense ratio, making it cost-effective over the long term.

PEY is ideal for those looking for an ETF that balances high income with the potential for dividend growth.

Building a Portfolio With Dividend ETFs

While each of the ETFs discussed above offers unique advantages, the ultimate success of your investment strategy depends on how you build and manage your portfolio. A well-rounded portfolio typically combines both high-yield and dividend-growth-focused ETFs, ensuring steady income along with the potential for capital appreciation.

Here are a few tips for constructing your dividend ETF portfolio:

  1. Diversify: Spread your investments across multiple ETFs to reduce risk and ensure income from a variety of sectors and regions.
  2. Reinvest Dividends: If you don’t need the income right away, reinvest the dividends to benefit from compounding growth.
  3. Balance Risk and Reward: Include a mix of low-risk, high-quality funds with moderate-risk ETFs to strike the right balance.
  4. Monitor Performance: Even though ETFs are passively managed, it’s important to periodically review their performance and realign with your goals if necessary.

Final Thoughts

Dividend ETFs serve as a powerful tool for anyone looking to generate passive income with minimal effort and maximum value. Whether you’re a retiree, a conservative investor, or someone just starting out, these funds offer reliable income streams and diversification opportunities.

The beauty of dividend ETFs lies in their versatility and accessibility—allowing anyone with a long-term perspective to achieve financial security. By identifying funds that align with your goals and risk tolerance, 2023 could be the year you take your passive income game to the next level.

As always, it’s crucial to conduct thorough research and consult with a financial advisor to ensure your investments align with your broader financial strategy. Make the most of what these top dividend ETFs have to offer, and pave a steady path toward financial independence.
