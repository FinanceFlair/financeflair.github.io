Real Assets: Building a Robust Investment Portfolio


Owning real assets can be a sound investment strategy, providing both financial returns and psychological satisfaction. Real assets are tangible items that hold value and can be used for various purposes. Unlike financial assets, which are more easily traded and fluctuate with market forces, real assets have intrinsic value that is less subject to market volatility. They include property, precious metals, fine art, and even collectibles.

Why Invest in Real Assets?

  1. Intrinsic Value: Unlike financial assets, real assets are tangible and have intrinsic value. This stable value helps to diversify your investment portfolio and reduce overall risk exposure.

  2. Long-term Returns: Real assets can offer substantial returns in the long run. For instance, property investments benefit from rental income and potential capital appreciation, while gold holds value during inflationary periods.

  3. Diversification: Real assets are often negatively correlated with the stock market, which can provide a hedge against market downturns.

  4. Tax Advantages: Some real assets, like primary residences, offer tax advantages, like deductions on mortgage interest and property taxes.

Understanding and Selecting Real Assets

  1. Real Estate: Investment properties, both residential and commercial, are perhaps the most common and widely understood real assets. Returns come from rental income and property value appreciation. Location, condition, and market dynamics significantly impact the investment.

  2. Precious Metals: Gold, silver, and other precious metals are seen as a safe-haven investment during uncertain economic times. These metals hold value over time, making them a stable, long-term addition to your portfolio.

  3. Fine Art: High-value art pieces can appreciate in value, making them a viable investment. However, it is essential to research and understand the art market and art piece authenticity to invest wisely.

  4. Collectibles: Rare and valuable collectibles, such as coins, stamps, or wine, can appreciate in value. However, they often have niche markets, limited liquidity, and require specialized knowledge.

Creating a Robust Investment Portfolio

  1. Diversification: Diversify your real asset investments by selecting different types of real assets and spreading them across various markets.

  2. Location: Choose well-located properties or markets with long-term growth potential to increase likelihood of successful investments.

  3. Knowledge: Understand the market dynamics, legalities, and risks involved in each real asset type to make informed, strategic decisions.

  4. Risk Management: Be prepared for market fluctuations and potential losses. Plan for unexpected costs, factoring in costs like maintenance, insurance, and property taxes.

  5. Monitor & Rebalance: Regularly review and assess your portfolio’s performance against your investment goals. Rebalance as needed to maintain your desired asset allocation.

Real assets can be an excellent addition to a well-diversified investment portfolio. By investing in different types of real assets, across various locations and markets, you can hedge against risk, enjoy long-term returns, and gain a sense of control over your wealth. With knowledge, strategic planning, and a long-term perspective, you can successfully build a robust investment portfolio with real assets.
