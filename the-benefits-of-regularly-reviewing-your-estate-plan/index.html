The Benefits of Regularly Reviewing Your Estate Plan


As life unfolds, circumstances change, and so do our priorities. What was once a comprehensive estate plan may no longer accurately reflect our current wishes, goals, and financial situation. Regularly reviewing your estate plan is crucial to ensure that your intentions are protected and that your loved ones are taken care of in the event of your passing or incapacitation. Why Review Your Estate Plan? Estate planning is not a one-time task; it's an ongoing process that requires periodic review and updates. Many people assume that their estate plan is set in stone, but the truth is that life's twists and turns can render your plan obsolete. Here are some compelling reasons to review your estate plan:

Changes in Family Dynamics: Marriages, divorces, births, and deaths can significantly impact your estate plan. Reviewing your plan ensures that your new family structure is reflected and that your wishes are still being met. Financial Changes: Changes in income, assets, or debts can affect the distribution of your estate. Regular reviews help you adjust your plan to ensure that your assets are being distributed according to your wishes. Tax Law Changes: Tax laws and regulations are constantly evolving, and failure to review your plan can result in unintended tax consequences. Staying up-to-date with tax changes ensures that your plan remains effective. Health and Disability: Illness or disability can impact your ability to make decisions about your estate. A review of your plan can help you appoint new decision-makers or update your advance directives. Goals and Priorities: As your life circumstances change, your goals and priorities may shift. Reviewing your plan allows you to reassess your objectives and make necessary adjustments.

What to Review in Your Estate Plan When reviewing your estate plan, consider the following essential components:

Will: Ensure that your will accurately reflects your wishes regarding the distribution of your assets, guardianship of minor children, and funeral arrangements. Trusts: Review your trusts to ensure they are still aligned with your goals and that the beneficiaries are properly identified. Power of Attorney: Verify that your power of attorney documents are up-to-date and that the designated agents are aware of their roles and responsibilities. Advance Directives: Review your advance directives, such as living wills and healthcare proxies, to ensure they reflect your current wishes. Beneficiary Designations: Check that your beneficiary designations, including life insurance and retirement accounts, are accurate and up-to-date.

Tips for a Smooth Review Process To make the review process efficient and effective, consider the following tips:

Schedule Regular Reviews: Set a regular review schedule, such as every 3-5 years, to ensure your plan remains current. Consult with an Attorney: Work with an experienced estate planning attorney to review and update your plan. Gather Important Documents: Collect all relevant documents, including your will, trusts, and advance directives, to review and update as needed. Communicate with Your Family: Keep your family members informed about any changes to your plan to avoid confusion or disputes.

Conclusion Regularly reviewing your estate plan is an essential step in ensuring that your wishes are protected and that your loved ones are taken care of. By understanding the importance of regular reviews and knowing what to review, you can maintain a comprehensive and effective estate plan that reflects your current circumstances. Don't wait until it's too late – schedule a review today and take control of your estate plan.
