How to Read Candlestick Charts for Options Trading


Successful options traders know that acquiring a comprehensive understanding of chart analysis techniques is essential to securing long-term gains in the financial markets. While many traders focus solely on price movements, a deeper understanding of candlestick patterns and charts can help traders unlock many hidden strategies. In this article, we will delve into the world of candlestick charts, and you will discover how to harness their power in options trading by focusing on price patterns, trends, and market sentiment. Understanding the Basics of Candlestick Charts Candlestick charts have been the most prominent charting technique used for centuries to analyze financial markets. Unveiling its origins in Japan during the 17th century, the main function of this chart type remains the same – clearly conveying valuable information of where the stock is headed by illustrating the key aspects such as prices of opening, highest or highest trade (high), lowest trade (low), and the closing price (low) for a set duration (formulated as ticks).
