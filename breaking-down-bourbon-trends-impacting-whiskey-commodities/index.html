Breaking (Down) Bourbon: Trends Impacting Whiskey Commodities


As the global whiskey market continues to grow and diversify, the reputation of bourbon, the quintessential American spirit, is riding a wave of renewed interest. Bourbon is no longer reserved for Sunday dinners or festive cheers. The once traditional whiskey is now being redefined and reimagined by both young and old, resulting in Novelties and trends that have surprised the bourbon community.

One of the most significant shifts in bourbon’s recent history has been the increased emphasis on the aging process. Traditionally, most bourbon producers rely on their bourbon’s age as a selling point. A 10-year-old bourbon would typically be more expensive than a 5-year-old. However, with the rise of ‘finish,’ a process where bourbon is aged in additional casks after the original barrel, producers have been able to transform the flavor of bourbons. Experimentation with finishes has unearthed unusual flavors like cherry wood, sherry cask, and even the more niche PX finish.

Another trend impacting bourbon is the emergence of smaller craft distilleries. For years, bourbon production has been dominated by a few large-scale producers. Not anymore. The rise of small batch, craft distilleries has made waves, taking the market by a storm. Craft distilleries have introduced a more personal and unique approach to whiskey production, with each distillery emphasizing and experimenting with elements such as mash bills and barrel finishes.

The availability of rare bourbons in online marketplaces is yet another unfolding trend. As demand for rare bourbons skyrockets, so does the temptation for whiskey collectors to buy and sell their prized possessions on platforms like auction houses and online stores. However, this has inevitably led to price inflation for rare bourbons, a trend certain to continue due, in part, to bourbon shortage. The bourbon industry is thriving, and as the mature trees that produce the raw material for the barrels dwindle, the shortage creates a natural bottleneck for bourbon production.

Finally, the confluence of the millennial palate and the sophisticated spirit-drinking culture has transformed how bourbon is consumed. The once archaic ‘Neat’ way of consuming bourbon is now trending as a ‘proper’ way to drink whiskey. Cocktails are no more the go-to;bourbon now comes with ‘finish’ and can be classed up with mere mixers like a simple bitters or even begrudgingly, a touch of honey.

In conclusion, bourbon is no longer just bourbon. Breaking it down results in a complex dynamic of trends, each impacting the bourbon market differently. The industry has matured with the consumers, aging alongside them, finding new palates and ways of consumption along the way. Understanding these trends is essential, whether a consumer, a distributor or simply an interested observer. So uncork a bottle of your favorite aged bourbon, raise a toast to the changing evolution of our beloved spirit, and let the passing of time mellow your drink, to unite perfectly with the passage of this captivating trend.
