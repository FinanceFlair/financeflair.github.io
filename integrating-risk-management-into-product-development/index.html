Integrating Risk Management into Product Development


Effective product development involves orchestrating numerous variables to deliver a well-crafted and desired end-product within a specified time and cost framework. However, this involves managing and mitigating different types of risks. These risks can emanate from so many corners: market factors, design flaws, economic changes, technology advancements, regulatory hurdles, and many more. Mitigating them effectively needs the incorporation of a disciplined risk management approach right from the inception of the product development process. Identifying the Why of Integrated Risk Management Product development endeavors often rely on extensive resource allocations, novel technological explorations, and breathtaking market predictions. As the stakes grow higher with each newer technological innovation, there emerges a positive correlation between innovation drivers and the likelihood of risk encounters. Operating at this extremely high-paced realm of intense innovation does invite the vulnerability of myriad external and internal factors that bear a significant threat to product performance quality, customer satisfaction, and, more evidently, enterprise viability. It is for these several compelling reasons that planners would advocate for security measures in product development, which arise from disciplined and careful definition of the requirements of the project from the planning phase, and involved constantly throughout the cycle of execution.
