Understanding Arbitrage

Before we dive into the specifics of Arbitrage 360, it’s essential to understand the fundamental concept of arbitrage. In a nutshell, arbitrage is the process of buying an asset at a lower price in one market and simultaneously selling it at a higher price in another market. This strategy is based on the idea that prices of identical assets should be the same across different markets, but due to various factors such as inefficiencies in pricing, market fragmentation, and regulatory differences, this is not always the case.

Arbitrageurs, as they are known, seek to capitalize on these price discrepancies by buying low and selling high. This approach is particularly attractive in markets where prices are volatile and subject to sudden changes, as even small price movements can result in significant profits.

The Challenges of Traditional Arbitrage

While arbitrage is a proven trading strategy, it’s not without its challenges. Traditional arbitrage involves manually scouring the market for price discrepancies, which can be a time-consuming and labor-intensive process. Moreover, it requires a deep understanding of the markets, as well as the ability to analyze complex data and make quick decisions.

In addition, traditional arbitrage is often limited by the availability of liquidity, market access, and the ability to execute trades quickly and efficiently. These limitations can result in missed opportunities, as well as increased exposure to market risks.
