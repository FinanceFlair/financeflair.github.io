How to Create a Compliance Risk Register


Creating a compliance risk register is an essential step for organizations aiming to manage their compliance obligations effectively. A compliance risk register serves as a centralized repository for identifying, assessing, and mitigating compliance risks. This document not only helps organizations stay compliant with laws and regulations but also enhances their overall risk management framework. In this guide, we will explore the steps to create a comprehensive compliance risk register, the key components to include, and best practices for maintaining it.

Understanding Compliance Risks

Before diving into the creation of a compliance risk register, it is crucial to understand what compliance risks are. Compliance risks arise from the potential for legal penalties, financial forfeiture, and material loss an organization may face if it fails to comply with laws, regulations, and internal policies. These risks can stem from various sources, including:

  • Regulatory Changes: New laws or amendments to existing regulations can create compliance challenges.
  • Operational Failures: Ineffective processes or systems can lead to non-compliance.
  • Employee Behavior: Lack of training or awareness among employees can result in compliance breaches.
  • Third-Party Relationships: Vendors and partners may pose compliance risks if they do not adhere to relevant regulations.

Step 1: Identify Compliance Obligations

The first step in creating a compliance risk register is to identify all relevant compliance obligations. This includes laws, regulations, industry standards, and internal policies that your organization must adhere to. To do this effectively, consider the following approaches:

  1. Conduct a Compliance Audit: Review existing compliance documentation and assess current practices against regulatory requirements.
  2. Engage Stakeholders: Collaborate with various departments, such as legal, finance, and operations, to gather insights on compliance obligations.
  3. Utilize Compliance Frameworks: Refer to established compliance frameworks, such as ISO 19600 or COSO, to identify relevant obligations.

Step 2: Assess Compliance Risks

Once you have identified your compliance obligations, the next step is to assess the associated compliance risks. This involves evaluating the likelihood of non-compliance and the potential impact on the organization. To conduct a thorough risk assessment, follow these steps:

  1. Risk Identification: For each compliance obligation, identify potential risks that could lead to non-compliance. Consider factors such as regulatory changes, operational inefficiencies, and employee behavior.
  2. Risk Analysis: Analyze the identified risks by assessing their likelihood and impact. You can use a simple scale (e.g., low, medium, high) to categorize risks.
  3. Risk Evaluation: Prioritize the risks based on their likelihood and impact. This will help you focus on the most critical compliance risks that require immediate attention.

Step 3: Develop the Compliance Risk Register

With a clear understanding of your compliance obligations and associated risks, you can now develop the compliance risk register. A well-structured compliance risk register should include the following components:

1. Risk Description

Provide a clear and concise description of each compliance risk. This should include the specific compliance obligation it relates to and the nature of the risk.

2. Likelihood and Impact Ratings

Assign likelihood and impact ratings to each risk. This can be done using a numerical scale (e.g., 1 to 5) or qualitative descriptors (e.g., low, medium, high). This information will help prioritize risks for mitigation.

3. Risk Owner

Designate a risk owner for each compliance risk. This individual will be responsible for monitoring the risk and implementing mitigation strategies.

4. Mitigation Strategies

Outline the strategies and actions that will be taken to mitigate each compliance risk. This may include training programs, process improvements, or policy updates.

5. Monitoring and Review

Include a section for monitoring and review. Specify how often the risk register will be reviewed and updated, as well as the metrics that will be used to assess the effectiveness of mitigation strategies.

Step 4: Implement Mitigation Strategies

After developing the compliance risk register, the next step is to implement the identified mitigation strategies. This may involve:

  • Training and Awareness: Conduct training sessions to ensure employees understand compliance obligations and the importance of adhering to them.
  • Process Improvements: Review and enhance existing processes to minimize the likelihood of non-compliance.
