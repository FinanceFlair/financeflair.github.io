Top 10 Grants for Small Businesses in 2024


In 2024, small businesses continue to be the backbone of the economy, driving innovation and job creation. However, they often face financial barriers that can hinder growth. Fortunately, various grants are available to support entrepreneurs and small business owners. This guide will explore the top ten grants for small businesses in 2024, highlighting their eligibility criteria, application processes, and how they can help sustain and grow your business.

1. Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program

The SBIR program is a highly competitive federal grant designed to encourage small businesses to engage in research and development (R&D) that has the potential for commercialization. In 2024, the program has allocated approximately $3 billion in grants across multiple federal agencies.

Eligibility:

  • Must be a for-profit business with 500 or fewer employees.
  • Must meet specific research and development requirements.

Application Process:

  • Submit a proposal detailing the project, its innovative aspects, and potential market applications.
  • Phase I grants typically range from $150,000 to $250,000 for six months.

Impact:

Businesses that secure SBIR grants can significantly reduce their R&D costs while bringing innovative products to market.

2. Economic Development Administration (EDA) Grants

The EDA offers grants aimed at supporting economically distressed communities throughout the United States. In 2024, these grants focus on projects that encourage economic diversification and job growth.

Eligibility:

  • Small businesses located in economically distressed areas.
  • Projects that demonstrate potential for job creation.

Application Process:

  • Proposals must outline how the project aligns with EDA goals and includes a detailed budget and timeline.
  • Grants can vary significantly in amount based on the project scope.

Impact:

These grants help stimulate local economies by funding infrastructure improvements, workforce development, and more.

3. National Association for the Self-Employed (NASE) Growth Grants

The NASE provides growth grants for its members to use for business development, marketing, and operational improvements. In 2024, this grant program has a budget of $100,000, with individual grants up to $4,000.

Eligibility:

  • Must be a member of NASE.
  • Open to any type of business, from start-ups to established entities.

Application Process:

  • Submit an application detailing how the funds will be used to help the business grow.
  • Applications are accepted year-round.

Impact:

NASE Growth Grants empower entrepreneurs to pursue their business ambitions, whether through marketing campaigns or purchasing critical equipment.

4. Amber Grant for Women

The Amber Grant, specifically targeting women entrepreneurs, provides funding and resources to help women-owned businesses succeed. In 2024, the program offers monthly $10,000 grants and a year-end $25,000 grant.

Eligibility:

  • Must identify as a woman.
  • Open to businesses in any industry.

Application Process:

  • Applicants submit a simple online application, with the option to include a video pitch.

Impact:

This grant not only provides financial support but also mentorship and networking opportunities specifically geared towards women entrepreneurs.

5. Comcast RISE Investment Fund

The Comcast RISE Investment Fund aims to support small businesses owned by people of color, offering grants for marketing consultations, media placements, and more. With a focus on community impact, this grant is vital in 2024.

Eligibility:

  • Small businesses owned by people of color.
  • Must be based in the Comcast service area.

Application Process:

  • Applications are submitted online, and recipients are chosen based on the impact of the proposed project.

Impact:

Grant recipients gain access to valuable resources that can help elevate their brand and reach new customers.

6. USAID Small Business Grants

The U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID) offers grants aimed at small businesses that are looking to innovate and provide solutions to developmental challenges. In 2024, the agency continues to focus on sustainability, technology, and health.

Eligibility:

  • Must be a U.S.-based small business with a focus on international development.

Application Process:

Impact:

Winning a USAID grant can provide not only funding but also credibility and access to international markets.

7. Federal and State Grant Programs

Various federal and state grant programs are available to small businesses in 2024, often focusing on specific industries or initiatives. For example, the USDA offers grants to agricultural businesses under its Rural Development program.

Eligibility:

  • Varies by program; often requires businesses to be in specific sectors.

Application Process:

  • Typically involves submitting a proposal with project specifics and budget.

Impact:

These grants are tailored to meet the needs of specific industries, providing targeted support that can lead to significant growth.

8. Local Chamber of Commerce Grants

Many local chambers of commerce offer grants to support small businesses within their communities. In 2024, these grants are often tied to community development projects or economic revitalization efforts.

Eligibility:

  • Must be a member of the local chamber.
  • Projects should benefit the local economy.

Application Process:

  • Applications vary by chamber but typically require a project proposal and budget.

Impact:

Local grants can strengthen community ties and foster relationships between businesses and residents.

9. Business Consortium Fund (BCF) Grants

The BCF provides grants to small businesses that are minority-owned or located in underrepresented communities. In 2024, the fund continues to focus on promoting economic equity.

Eligibility:

  • Minority-owned small businesses.
  • Must operate in specific targeted areas.

Application Process:

  • Submit an application along with documentation proving minority ownership and business structure.

Impact:

Grants from BCF help businesses overcome systemic barriers and access capital and resources they might not otherwise have.

10. Innovative Business Solutions Grant

The Innovative Business Solutions Grant is a relatively new entrant to the grant landscape, introduced in 2024 to support small businesses creating innovative solutions to social and technical problems.

Eligibility:

  • Open to all small businesses with innovative project proposals.
  • Must have a clear social or technical impact.

Application Process:

  • Detailed proposals are reviewed by a committee, with a focus on innovation and feasibility.

Impact:

This grant not only provides funding but also places businesses in a network of innovators, fostering collaboration and growth.
