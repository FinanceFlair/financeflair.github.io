Monetary Policy and the US Economy: A Review of the Literature


Monetary policy plays a crucial role in shaping the economic landscape of the United States. It involves the management of money supply and interest rates by the central bank to achieve specific economic objectives. As such, it has been the subject of extensive research and analysis by economists and policymakers. In this blog post, we will delve into the literature surrounding monetary policy and its impact on the US economy, examining key findings and insights from various studies.

Understanding Monetary Policy

Before delving into the literature, it’s important to grasp the basics of monetary policy. The Federal Reserve, the central bank of the United States, is responsible for formulating and implementing monetary policy. The primary tools at its disposal include open market operations, discount rates, and reserve requirements, which collectively influence the availability of credit and the cost of borrowing.

Monetary policy aims to achieve macroeconomic objectives such as price stability, full employment, and sustainable economic growth. By adjusting interest rates and influencing the money supply, the Federal Reserve seeks to stabilize inflation, stimulate investment, and mitigate economic downturns.
