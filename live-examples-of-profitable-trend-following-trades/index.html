Live Examples of Profitable Trend Following Trades


Trend following represents one of the most enduring and straightforward trading strategies employed by professionals and retail investors alike. Rather than trying to predict market tops or bottoms, trend following focuses on riding established market movements, capitalizing on sustained price momentum. The logic is elegantly simple: capture profits as an asset’s price consistently moves in one direction, whether upward or downward.

In this comprehensive blog post, we’ll dissect several live examples of profitable trend following trades across different asset classes—stocks, commodities, forex, and cryptocurrencies—to highlight the power and versatility of this approach. We’ll analyze what made these trades successful, the risk management techniques involved, and actionable takeaways for traders aiming to refine their trend following strategies.

Understanding Trend Following: Core Principles Before Diving In

Before jumping into real-world examples, it’s useful to briefly revisit what trend following entails:

  • Identification of a Trend: Using technical indicators like moving averages, MACD, or price action to determine the direction of the prevailing trend.
  • Entry Points: Typically after confirming trend continuation signals rather than chasing early breakouts.
  • Position Sizing and Risk Management: Deploying stop losses, trailing stops, and proper capital allocation to protect against reversals.
  • Exit Strategy: Exiting when the trend reverses or momentum fades, often signaled by crossing moving averages or diminishing volume.

With these pillars guiding a disciplined approach, trend following can generate strong long-term returns by systematically exploiting market momentum.

Example 1: Riding the 2020 Gold Bull Market

Gold is historically known as a safe haven but also as a trending asset during volatility spikes. In early 2020, global uncertainty stemming from the COVID-19 pandemic set the stage for an impressive gold rally.

Setup and Entry

  • Initial Trigger: In March 2020, as panic gripped markets, gold prices hit a low near $1450 per ounce.
  • Trend Confirmation: A 50-day moving average (50-DMA) crossover above the 200-day moving average (200-DMA) occurred in April—commonly known as a “golden cross”—signaling a bullish trend.
  • Entry Point: After the golden cross, many trend followers entered long positions around $1600.

Trade Progression

From April through August 2020, gold surged to over $2000 per ounce, representing a gain exceeding 25% in just four months. The trend follower would have trailed stop losses beneath successive lows, locking in profits while allowing the position to run.

Key Learnings

  • Patience Pays: Entering only after the golden cross prevented premature entries during the March dip.
  • Adaptability: Adjusting stop losses upward with the rising trend maximized gains and limited downside.
  • Macro Awareness: Aligning trend signals with a macroeconomic backdrop (pandemic-related uncertainty) improved confidence in the trade.

Example 2: Forex—EUR/USD Trending From Mid-2021 to Early 2022

Currency pairs often display distinct long-term trends driven by interest rate differentials and geopolitical factors. The EUR/USD pair from mid-2021 offers a perfect trend following case study.

Identifying the Trend

  • Background: In mid-2021, the euro began depreciating steadily versus the US dollar, influenced by diverging monetary policies—slowing European growth and a hawkish Federal Reserve.
  • Technical Setup: The 21-day exponential moving average (EMA) started sloping downward, with price consistently closing below it for multiple weeks.

Entry and Trade Management

  • Entry: Trend followers entered short positions once the price broke below support around 1.1900 and confirmed momentum on MACD.
  • Trade Duration: The downtrend persisted until early 2022, with EUR/USD falling from approximately 1.1900 to below 1.1300.
  • Risk Control: Stops were placed just above recent swing highs, adjusted downward as the trend progressed.

Outcome and Insights

Traders could realize profits near 5%, a sizable move in forex markets, while managing risk tightly due to the clear trend. This example also underscores:

  • The benefit of combining moving averages with momentum indicators for trade confirmation.
  • How macro fundamentals create durable trends that technical tools help exploit.

Example 3: Tech Stock Momentum – Tesla (TSLA) 2019-2020

Individual stocks, especially in dynamic sectors like technology, often showcase pronounced trends that trend followers can capitalize on if vigilant.

Market Context

  • Tesla entered 2019 amid skepticism but gained investor momentum due to production milestones and profitability improvements.
  • From early 2019 until February 2020, TSLA stock surged from roughly $40 (adjusted for splits) to above $150.

Signals for Trend Following

  • Moving Average Strategy: The 10-day moving average (MA) consistently stayed above the 50-day MA.
  • Breakouts: Price regularly broke above previous resistance levels with strong volume, confirming upward momentum.

Trade Execution

  • Entry: Taking long positions post-breakouts and when the 10-day MA crossed above the 50-day MA.
  • Stops: Used trailing stops just under 50-day MA or recent support to lock profits.
  • Exit: The rapid decline in March 2020 due to pandemic fears triggered stop losses, closing positions near $100.

Trade Outcome

A disciplined trend follower netted impressive returns exceeding 200% in roughly a year, exemplifying how swift trends in growth stocks can be highly lucrative but also volatile.

Example 4: Commodities—Crude Oil Trend During Early 2021 Recovery

After the crash of 2020, crude oil experienced a strong recovery trend fueled by rebounding demand and production cuts.

Trend Indicators

  • The 20-day and 50-day moving averages aligned higher starting January 2021.
  • RSI (Relative Strength Index) hovered between 50 to 70, suggesting sustained buying momentum without overbought extremes.

Entering the Trade

  • Entry was triggered by price holding above $50 per barrel after consolidating in December 2020.
  • Continued confirmations came as oil prices steadily climbed above $65 by mid-2021.

Managing Risk

Using stop losses below the 20-day MA allowed participants to capture nearly 30% gains while limiting risk during intra-week volatility.

Lessons Learned

  • Range breakouts into new price territory can signal strong trend development.
  • Combining momentum indicators with moving averages refines entry timing.
  • Commodities trends may dovetail with macroeconomic policy shifts, like OPEC+ production decisions.

Example 5: Cryptocurrency—Bitcoin Breakout in Late 2020

The cryptocurrency space’s notorious volatility also presents fruitful ground for trend following when timed properly.

Context and Trend Setup

  • After bottoming near $3,800 in March 2020, Bitcoin embarked on a multi-month uptrend.
  • The 50-day MA crossed above the 200-day MA in October 2020 (a golden cross), heralding a strong bullish trend.

Trade Implementation

  • Entry points included buying on pullbacks to the 50-day MA or breakouts above key resistance at $12,000.
  • Trailing stops tracked below the 50-day MA support, updated regularly to shield gains.

Results

Bitcoin soared to above $29,000 by December 2020, yielding gains of over 140% in several months for trend followers.

Strategic Takeaways

  • Adhering to technical signals amid highly volatile assets can safeguard against sharp reversals.
  • Discipline around stop losses is paramount given crypto’s frequent rapid swings.
  • Trend following can be as effective in newer markets as in traditional ones, granted sufficient liquidity and volume.

Practical Tips for Applying Trend Following Strategies Today

  1. Choose the Right Timeframe: Depending on your style, trend following can be applied to intraday, swing, or long-term charts. Longer timeframes often yield more reliable trends but require greater patience.

  2. Use Complementary Indicators: Combining moving averages with momentum tools like MACD, RSI, or volume trends improves signal accuracy.

  3. Risk Management Is Non-Negotiable: Never ignore stop losses. Trailing stops are invaluable in protecting accrued profits while allowing trends to run.

  4. Be Patient and Avoid Overtrading: Wait for confirmation and resist chasing entries. Trends develop and extend over time.

  5. Stay Emotionally Detached: Following the trend means sometimes enduring pullbacks and choppy periods. Trust the rules and system.

Conclusion

The live examples of trend following trades across diverse markets provide compelling evidence of its effectiveness. By systematically identifying trends, entering with conviction, and managing risk smartly, traders can generate significant profits without guesswork about tops and bottoms.

From gold’s pandemic-fueled spike, EUR/USD’s steady decline, Tesla’s explosive breakout, crude oil’s recovery, to Bitcoin’s historic rally, the common thread is disciplined trend following prowess. Every asset class offers its unique nuances, but fundamental principles remain constant—observe the trend, ride it, and protect gains.

Whether you’re new to trading or seeking to refine strategies, integrating tested trend following techniques can be a valuable addition to your playbook. By anchoring on these live example insights, there’s potential to harness momentum and capitalize on profitable market moves consistently.
