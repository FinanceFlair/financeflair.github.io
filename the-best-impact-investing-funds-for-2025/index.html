The Best Impact Investing Funds for 2025


As the world continues to face challenges related to climate change, inequality, and social justice, investors are increasingly looking for ways to align their portfolios with their values. Impact investing, a strategy that focuses on generating both financial returns and positive social or environmental outcomes, has emerged as a compelling option for those seeking to make a difference. In 2025, the landscape of impact investing continues to evolve, offering investors a diverse range of funds to choose from. This post explores some of the best impact investing funds for 2025, highlighting their strategies, performance, and potential to drive change.

Understanding Impact Investing

Before diving into the specific funds, it’s important to understand what impact investing is and how it works. Impact investing refers to investments made with the intention to generate positive, measurable social and environmental impacts alongside financial returns. This can take many forms, such as funding renewable energy projects, supporting affordable housing initiatives, or investing in companies that prioritize social justice and human rights.

Impact investing is distinct from socially responsible investing (SRI) and environmental, social, and governance (ESG) investing. While SRI and ESG focus on avoiding harmful investments and supporting companies with strong ethical practices, impact investing goes a step further by actively seeking investments that contribute to positive change.

As investors continue to prioritize sustainable and ethical practices, impact investing has gained significant traction. The market for impact investing is projected to grow exponentially in the coming years, with more investors looking for opportunities that align with their values while still offering competitive returns.

Why Invest in Impact Funds?

Impact investing offers several key benefits, both for investors and for society as a whole:

  1. Financial Returns: Contrary to the misconception that impact investing sacrifices financial gains, many impact funds have demonstrated strong performance. In fact, the growing demand for sustainable and ethical products and services often leads to above-average returns for companies in these sectors.

  2. Positive Change: Impact funds focus on making a tangible difference in the world. Whether it’s supporting clean energy, reducing poverty, or improving access to education, these investments contribute to creating a better future.

  3. Long-Term Sustainability: Impact investing tends to focus on industries and companies that are future-proof, with a strong emphasis on sustainability. These investments are less likely to be disrupted by environmental or social changes, making them potentially more resilient in the long term.

  4. Diversification: Many impact funds invest in a wide range of sectors, from renewable energy to healthcare, offering investors the opportunity to diversify their portfolios while still achieving their social and financial goals.

Top Impact Investing Funds for 2025

Now that we have a solid understanding of impact investing, let’s take a closer look at some of the best impact investing funds for 2025. These funds represent a range of strategies and asset classes, ensuring there’s something for every investor looking to make a positive impact.

1. The Vanguard ESG U.S. Stock ETF (ESGV)

The Vanguard ESG U.S. Stock ETF is one of the top choices for investors seeking exposure to U.S. equities while prioritizing environmental, social, and governance factors. This fund tracks the performance of the FTSE US All Cap Choice Index, which screens for companies that meet specific ESG criteria. The Vanguard ESG U.S. Stock ETF invests in large, mid, and small-cap stocks, offering diversification across a variety of sectors.

Why it stands out:

  • Vanguard’s reputation for low fees and long-term performance.
  • A diversified portfolio that includes companies with strong ESG practices.
  • A focus on sustainable growth and long-term value creation.

2. BlackRock Impact Bond Fund (BIBAX)

For investors looking for fixed-income investments with a social and environmental focus, the BlackRock Impact Bond Fund is an excellent option. This fund invests in bonds issued by companies, governments, and organizations that are making a positive impact on society. The fund’s goal is to provide investors with income while supporting projects related to clean energy, affordable housing, and other sustainable initiatives.

Why it stands out:

  • Focus on high-quality bonds that support positive change.
  • A commitment to integrating ESG factors into the bond selection process.
  • Strong historical performance, with competitive yields for a socially conscious fund.

3. Pax Global Environmental Markets Fund (PAXWX)

The Pax Global Environmental Markets Fund is an equity fund that focuses on companies leading the way in the environmental sector. This includes industries such as renewable energy, water conservation, and sustainable agriculture. By investing in companies that provide solutions to environmental challenges, the fund aims to generate both strong returns and a positive impact on the planet.

Why it stands out:

  • A dedicated focus on companies driving environmental innovation.
  • A diverse global portfolio, offering exposure to markets around the world.
  • A solid track record of performance, especially in the renewable energy space.

4. TIAA-CREF Social Choice Equity Fund (TICRX)

The TIAA-CREF Social Choice Equity Fund is an excellent choice for investors who want exposure to large-cap U.S. companies with strong ESG practices. The fund’s strategy is centered around selecting companies that are leaders in corporate social responsibility and sustainability. By investing in companies that prioritize social and environmental performance, the fund seeks to deliver strong returns while supporting positive change.

Why it stands out:

  • A focus on large-cap companies with a proven track record of responsible corporate governance.
  • A comprehensive approach to ESG, including environmental, social, and governance factors.
  • A low expense ratio, making it a cost-effective choice for long-term investors.

5. Calvert Emerging Markets Equity Fund (CVMEX)

The Calvert Emerging Markets Equity Fund is ideal for investors seeking to invest in emerging markets while ensuring that their investments are aligned with their values. This fund focuses on companies in developing economies that are making a positive impact in areas such as renewable energy, healthcare, and education. By targeting high-growth markets with strong social and environmental performance, the fund aims to offer both growth potential and positive impact.

Why it stands out:

  • Exposure to high-growth emerging markets with strong ESG practices.
  • A focus on sectors that can drive long-term, sustainable change in developing countries.
  • A diverse portfolio that includes companies in Asia, Latin America, and Africa.

6. ImpactShares Affordable Care Act Fund (IDCA)

Healthcare is one of the most important areas for impact investing, and the ImpactShares Affordable Care Act Fund is an excellent option for investors looking to support healthcare accessibility and affordability. This fund invests in companies that are committed to improving healthcare outcomes, reducing costs, and increasing access to care for underserved populations. It is particularly focused on companies that are aligned with the goals of the Affordable Care Act.

Why it stands out:

  • A strong focus on healthcare, a critical sector for social impact.
  • A commitment to investing in companies that prioritize healthcare access and affordability.
  • An innovative approach to impact investing within the healthcare space.

7. Nuveen Global Impact Fund (NIGAX)

The Nuveen Global Impact Fund offers investors exposure to a diversified portfolio of companies that are making a positive social or environmental impact on a global scale. The fund invests in sectors such as clean energy, sustainable agriculture, and financial inclusion, with a focus on companies that are addressing global challenges while delivering competitive returns.

Why it stands out:

  • A truly global portfolio that invests in companies from both developed and emerging markets.
  • A commitment to supporting sustainable solutions for global challenges.
  • A focus on sectors that are likely to experience significant growth in the coming years.

8. Mirova Global Sustainable Equity Fund (MSEF)

Mirova’s Global Sustainable Equity Fund is an equity-focused fund that invests in companies with a demonstrated commitment to sustainability and positive impact. The fund focuses on companies that are leaders in areas such as renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, and environmental protection. With a global scope, this fund provides investors with the opportunity to support sustainable companies around the world while benefiting from strong growth potential.

Why it stands out:

  • A global approach to sustainable investing, providing exposure to both developed and emerging markets.
  • A focus on companies that are leaders in sustainability and environmental stewardship.
  • A robust performance history, with competitive returns over time.

Factors to Consider When Choosing an Impact Investing Fund

While these impact funds offer a range of options for investors in 2025, it’s essential to consider several factors when choosing the right fund for your portfolio:

  1. Investment Goals: Are you looking for growth, income, or a combination of both? Different impact funds have different strategies, so it’s important to choose one that aligns with your financial goals.

  2. Risk Tolerance: Some impact investing funds, particularly those in emerging markets or niche sectors, can be more volatile. Assess your risk tolerance before making a decision.

  3. Fees: While impact funds have become more competitive in terms of fees, it’s still important to consider the expense ratio, as higher fees can erode long-term returns.

  4. ESG Criteria: Different funds have different approaches to ESG criteria. Some focus more on environmental factors, while others prioritize social issues or corporate governance. Make sure the fund’s strategy aligns with your personal values.

  5. Performance History: While past performance is not a guarantee of future results, it can provide insight into a fund’s ability to deliver consistent returns. Look at the historical performance of a fund to gauge its potential.

Conclusion

Impact investing continues to grow in popularity, and 2025 offers a wealth of opportunities for investors who want to make a positive impact on the world while still achieving strong financial returns. Whether you’re interested in clean energy, affordable housing, or healthcare, there’s an impact fund that can help you align your investments with your values.

The funds mentioned above are some of the best options for 2025, offering a diverse range of strategies and sectors to choose from. By carefully considering your investment goals, risk tolerance, and personal values, you can find the right impact fund to make a difference in the world and achieve your financial objectives.
