Short Term Trading: Strategies for Trading Triangle Patterns


Have you ever noticed that price charts look a lot like geometric patterns? You’re not imagining it! Some of the most popular chart patterns are identified by their distinct shapes, such as triangles. In technical analysis, trading triangles serve as reliable indicators of market trends and reversals. In this article, we’ll dive into trading triangle patterns and provide strategies for short-term trading.

What is a Trading Triangle Pattern?

A trading triangle pattern occurs when a stock’s price oscillates between support and resistance levels, creating a triangular formation. These patterns can be thought of as ‘birectangular,’ ‘symmetrical,’ or ‘ascending/descending,’ each possessing unique characteristics and predicting potential price movements. Trading triangles can form in any time frame – from hourly charts to monthly charts – and are commonly observed in all types of securities.

Identifying Trading Triangle Patterns

To identify a trading triangle pattern, examine the price chart for a clear three-point structure. Ascending triangles display rising resistance and flat support, while descending triangles show flat resistance and plunging support. Symmetrical triangles have a converging support and resistance line, with the breakout often occurring when the two lines intersect.

When attempting to identify trading triangles, consider these two parameters:

  1. Measured move: The measured move is the distance between the two lines in a triangle pattern, half the height of a symmetrical triangle, or the distance from pivot to resistance in an ascending triangle.
  2. Breakout Direction: Breakouts in ascending triangles tend to be bullish, while those in descending triangles are bearish.

Short-Term Trading Strategies for Trading Triangle Patterns

The beauty of trading triangle patterns lies in the tailored approach you can take depending on the type of triangle you’ve identified. Here are three powerful strategies to employ in short-term trading:

  1. Support and Resistance Strategies

Support and resistance are key principles of technical analysis. In a trading triangle, support serves as the floor for prices, while resistance caps upside momentum. Assuming a bullish triangle pattern, buy near the breakout point when prices breach the resistance line. Conversely, if prices break through support, take a bearish stance by shorting the security near the breakout point. 2. Moving Averages Crossover

Crossovers between a short-term and long-term moving average, like the 20-period and 50-period Exponential Moving Averages (EMA), can signal an upcoming price turn. As prices bounce between support and resistance, monitor trading triangle patterns for a crossover. A bullish crossover in an ascending triangle indicates a favorable entry opportunity, while a bearish crossover aligns with a descending triangle.

  1. Fibonacci Retracements

After a price breakout, traders can track Fibonacci retracement levels to identify potential pullback zones. In a trading triangle, these zones coincide with historical support and resistance within the triangle, poised to act as stops or buying opportunities.

Final Thoughts on Short-Term Trading and Trading Triangle Patterns

Trading triangle patterns offer distinct advantages to short-term traders, acting as predictors of future market trends with high accuracy. By employing strategies such as support and resistance, moving averages crossovers, and utilizing Fibonacci retracements, traders can exploit price movements within triangular patterns effectively. Remember, however, that no strategy is foolproof. Stay vigilant and adapt your tactics as needed, ensuring that your approach remains rooted in rigorous analysis and careful observation.

In the dynamic world of trading, keep your eyes peeled for the classic shapes of chart patterns. Triangle patterns can illuminate opportunities that may be lurking beneath the surface, waiting for savvy traders to capitalize on their predicted market moves. Strategy the fishes!
