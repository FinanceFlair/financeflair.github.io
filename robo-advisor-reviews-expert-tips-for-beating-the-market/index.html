Robo Advisor Reviews: Expert Tips for Beating the Market


In the last few years, robo advisors have become increasingly popular among individual investors looking to invest their hard-earned money with ease and simplicity. These automated investment platforms essentially manage your portfolio, investing your money in a mix of low-cost, diversified mutual funds or exchange-traded funds (ETFs) based on your specific goals and risk-appetite. Nonetheless, as enticing as they might seem, robo advisors don’t guarantee that you will beat the market. But how can you increase your chances? Let’s take a closer look at some expert tips that might help.

Tip 1: Choose the Right Robo Advisor for You

There are several robo advisors in the market, and the best one for you will depend on your specific needs, preferences, and goals. Start by researching and comparing different robo advisors, looking into their fees, investment options, and target market. Robo advisors like Betterment are tailored for the hands-off investor who simply wants to maximize returns, while Wealthfront targets affluent individuals looking for more sophisticated services.

As you weigh your options, look at their user interface and ease of use. A simple and intuitive interface can make a significant difference in your experience, particularly when it comes to making adjustments to your portfolio or understanding how your investments are being managed.

Tip 2: Opt for a Robo Advisor with Low Fees

One of the primary reasons why most people invest in robo advisors is to reduce investment costs. Low fees can significantly impact your returns over time, making it crucial to select a robo advisor that offers a fee structure aligned with your investing goals. Compare their management fees and any additional costs associated with your investment choices.

Affordable and top robo-advisors such as Wealthfront and Betterment offer low annual management fees, ranging from 0.25 percent to 0.40 percent per year. For superior passive portfolio management and tax loss harvesting (TLH), you may need to weigh additional fees alongside the benefits you’ll derive from each platform.

Tip 3: Look for Robo Advisors with Human Financial Advisors

Some robo advisors also offer access to human financial advisors for more personal support and guidance. At a glance, this might seem unnecessary since robo advisors are supposed to take the human bias out of investing. Nonetheless, the availability of human advisors can significantly improve your experience by offering personalized advice, especially when dealing with complex financial situations.

Robo advisors like Schwab Intelligent Portfolios and Betterment have integrated human advisors into their services, which provides customers access to both digital and human support when necessary.

Tip 4: Harness the Power of Tax Loss Harvesting and Smart Portfolio Rebalancing

Portfolio management strategies like tax loss harvesting and rebalancing are critical in optimizing your investment returns. Robo advisors that employ these strategies can help you manage your investments more efficiently, leading to less stress and potentially higher returns.

Tax loss harvesting involves selling investment securities that have lost value to offset capital gains, which can result in saving a significant amount of money on taxes. As for rebalancing, it helps to keep your portfolio diversified and in line with your targeted asset allocation, which is essential for long-term investment success.

Tip 5: Focus on the Long-Term

Lastly, the best way to beat the market with a robo advisor is by having a long-term focus. Robo advisors are built to manage long-term investing for individuals with a higher risk tolerance. While they aren’t a guaranteed ticket to beating the market, they have the potential to maximize returns and minimize risks when used consistently over time.

Keep in mind that no investment strategy can consistently surpass the market, particularly when it comes to overall returns. However, robo advisors can provide you with automated investment management capabilities to optimize your wealth and achieve your financial goals.

In conclusion, when it comes to choosing a robo advisor, it’s essential to do your homework and select one that meets your personal needs and investment goals. With proper research, planning, and a focus on long-term growth, a robo advisor could be the smartest investment decision you make.
