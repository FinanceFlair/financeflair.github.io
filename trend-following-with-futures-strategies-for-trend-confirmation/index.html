Trend Following with Futures: Strategies for Trend Confirmation


Trend following is a popular trading strategy that has been used by many traders and investors for decades. The idea is simple: identify the direction of the market trend and ride it until it changes. However, implementing a trend following strategy can be challenging, especially when trading futures. In this post, we will explore some strategies for trend confirmation when trading futures.

Understanding Trend Following

Before we dive into the strategies, let’s briefly discuss what trend following is and how it works. Trend following involves identifying the direction of the market trend and then trading in that direction. This can be done using various technical indicators, such as moving averages, relative strength index (RSI), and Bollinger Bands.

When using trend following, traders typically look for signals to enter a trade, such as a breakout above a resistance level or a breakout below a support level. Once a trade is entered, the trader will then look for confirmation of the trend before closing the trade.

Strategies for Trend Confirmation

There are many strategies that can be used for trend confirmation when trading futures. Here are a few examples:

1. Moving Average Crossover

One popular strategy for trend confirmation is the moving average crossover. This involves plotting two moving averages of different lengths on a chart, such as a 50-day moving average and a 200-day moving average. When the shorter moving average crosses above the longer moving average, it is a buy signal, and when it crosses below, it is a sell signal.

2. Relative Strength Index (RSI)

Another strategy for trend confirmation is to use the RSI. This involves plotting the RSI on a chart and looking for oversold or overbought conditions. When the RSI is oversold, it may be a good time to buy, and when it is overbought, it may be a good time to sell.

3. Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands are a technical indicator that consists of a moving average and two standard deviations plotted above and below the moving average. When the price of the futures contract is trading above the upper band, it may be a good time to sell, and when it is trading below the lower band, it may be a good time to buy.

4. Momentum Indicators

Momentum indicators, such as the momentum indicator or the force index, can also be used for trend confirmation. These indicators measure the rate of change of the price of the futures contract and can be used to identify when the trend is strengthening or weakening.

5. Market Sentiment

Market sentiment can also be used for trend confirmation. This involves looking at the sentiment of market participants, such as the number of buy and sell orders, and using this information to determine the direction of the trend.

Conclusion

In conclusion, trend following is a popular trading strategy that can be used to identify the direction of the market trend and ride it until it changes. There are many strategies that can be used for trend confirmation, including moving average crossover, RSI, Bollinger Bands, momentum indicators, and market sentiment. By using these strategies, traders can increase their chances of success when trading futures.
