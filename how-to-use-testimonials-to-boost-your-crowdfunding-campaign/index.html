How to Use Testimonials to Boost Your Crowdfunding Campaign


A successful crowdfunding campaign requires a combination of creative ideas, effective marketing, and strategic planning. One often overlooked yet incredibly powerful tool is the art of using testimonials to boost your campaign and gain the trust of your backers. Building Trust Through Social Proof

When potential donors are considering making a contribution to your campaign, they are essentially investing in you and your project. They want to know that their money will be used wisely and that you are reliable. This is where testimonials come in – they serve as testaments to your character, skills, and reputation. What are Testimonials in the Context of Crowdfunding? Testimonials are statements of praise and appreciation from your satisfied supporters, which demonstrate the value of your project and provide an air of authenticity. In the context of crowdfunding, testimonials can be in the form of written reviews, videos, or even phone calls. Why are Testimonials Effective? There are several reasons why testimonials are an essential part of a successful crowdfunding campaign:

They help to establish credibility by showcasing the positive impact of your project on supporters. They provide a personal touch, as people are more likely to trust the opinions of complete strangers than a brand or a salesman. They humanize your campaign by highlighting the real people behind your project.

How to Collect Testimonials

Identify Your Testimonial Influencers

Approach your loyal supporters and ask for their permission to feature their testimonials on your campaign page. Consider how you can personalize the request and make it clear why their testimonial matters to your campaign.

Make It Easy

Provide a simple and convenient method for your supporters to share their testimonials, such as a private Gmail address or a digital form. Ensure that your interface is easy to use, so supporters don't get discouraged and choose not to make a testimonial.

Minimum Information

To keep testimonials simple, it is recommended to request that contributors write a short statement stating their name, the category in which they support, and how their experience with the project changed their life and expectations for your project.

Types of testimonials:

Written testimonials (testimonial letters, emails, IM direct messages) Recorded testimonials

Open Line of Communication

Keep your communication lines open and active before and after you get testimonials. Check-in with supporters during and after the project to understand its progress in their lives or other thing they are facing now. Acknowledge feedback and make sure your supporters know they matter.

Integrating Testimonials into Your Campaign

Where to Display Testimonials

Start by displaying your testimonials in the right places. A logical spot is your project's introduction page. Also testimonials can go on the FAQs page for added credibility. On mobile portables think about putting your testimonials on sale pages which are bought by the most visitors.

Types of Visual Testimonials

Written comments you can put on any page, in quote format (just highlighting or differently) Videos commonly put at the campaign intro page.
