Short Term Trading: Trading the Symmetrical Triangle Patterns


Symmetrical triangles are popular patterns among short-term traders because of their potential to signify a significant price movement. This formation appears when a security’s price moves between two converging trendlines – one horizontally and the other angled upward or downward. Typically, a breakout occurs after the price has reached a point close to this convergence. When trading these patterns, here are a few essential elements to consider:

  1. Features of Symmetrical Triangles

Symmetrical triangles feature two converging trendlines, which create an arrow-like appearance on a chart. The horizontal trendline connects the highest high of an uptrend and the lowest low of the downtrend. The angled trendline connects the lowest lows and the highest highs in the pattern.

There are two types of symmetrical triangles: bullishly and bearishly inclined. The bull triangle has its ascending support line and a horizontal resistance line, while the bear triangle is characterized by a descending support line with a horizontal resistance line.

  1. Measure the Height

One of the crucial aspects of trading symmetrical triangles is measuring the height of the pattern. This height is the distance between the two trendlines at the widest point. Once you’ve identified the height, add or subtract this distance from the breakout point to forecast a potential price range after the breakout.

Remember that symmetrical triangles are continuation patterns. When a price breaks through the resistance or support line, it often sets off a significant move in the direction of the underlying trend.

  1. Entry and Exit Strategies

Once the breakout occurs, you can begin trading in the direction of the primary trend. For bullish triangles, buy when the price breaks above the resistance line. Alternatively, in bearish triangles, sell when the price breaks below the support line.

It’s crucial to have well-defined stop-loss levels to minimize risk. Place your stop-loss above the resistance line in a bull triangle and below the support line in a bear triangle. You should also employ a profit target based on the symmetrical triangle’s height and breakouts.

  1. Avoid Late Entries

One common mistake among traders is waiting for a breakout before entering the trade. This approach could be risky since it limits the potential profit and exposes the trader to a larger price swing. Instead, try entering the trade when the pattern has 80-90% formed. This strategy gives you better entry prices and reduces the risk of a false breakout.

Trading Symmetrical Triangle Patterns is both challenging and rewarding. With patience and practice, you’ll see how these patterns can help predict significant price movements. Remember, just like any other trading venture, extensive research, and disciplined strategies are crucial to optimize success.

In conclusion, understanding and recognizing symmetrical triangles are vital skills for short-term traders. Balance technical analysis, fundamental data, and your risk tolerance to maximize your trading experience. Happy Trading!
