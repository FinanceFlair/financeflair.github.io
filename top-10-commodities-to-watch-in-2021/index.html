Top 10 Commodities to Watch in 2021


Welcome to the world of commodities, where the value of goods and resources dictate the economic landscape of nations. As we move into the new year, the global commodity market will witness some interesting shifts. The focus will be on the emerging trends and innovations that will impact various sectors. Here are the top 10 commodities to watch in 2021:

  1. Lithium:

Lithium holds immense importance for the battery industry, especially for electric vehicles. The rising demand for electric cars and storage devices will propel the lithium market. Countries like Australia, Chile, and Argentina hold a significant share in global lithium production.

  1. Copper:

Copper is an essential element for several industrial applications. Its usage in electronics, construction, and transportation has increased, leading to greater demand. This growth is expected to continue in 2021, making copper a commodity to watch.

  1. Coffee:

Coffee lovers, take note! The worldwide coffee consumption has risen, and the trend is expected to continue. As the demand for premium coffee grows, the market for specialty coffee beans will remain buoyant.

  1. Natural Gas:

Natural gas is increasingly being relied upon as a cleaner alternative to coal. Moreover, its use in heating and power generation has grown substantially in recent years. This trend will likely continue, making natural gas a commodity to monitor in 2021.

  1. Aluminum:

Driven by the growth in construction and manufacturing industries, the demand for aluminum is expected to rise in the coming year. Aluminium’s light-weight nature and recyclability make it an attractive option for various applications.

  1. Corn:

Corn has numerous uses, including food, feeding livestock, and producing biofuels. As the global population continues to grow, so does the demand for corn, making it an essential commodity to watch.

  1. Iron Ore:

Iron ore is the key ingredient for steel production. With the construction and infrastructure industries on the rise, the demand for iron ore will likely increase in 2021, making it a commodity to monitor.

  1. Sugar:

Consumed by billions worldwide, sugar remains an essential commodity. As the demand for sweeteners continues to grow, the sugar market will remain a significant player in the global economy.

  1. Cotton:

Cotton is an irreplaceable fabric for clothing and home textiles. As fashion trends continue to evolve, the demand for high-quality cotton will remain steady in 2021.

  1. Crude Oil:

Despite global initiatives to reduce carbon emissions, crude oil remains an essential commodity. With the emergence of new markets and advancements in technology, crude oil will continue to play a crucial role in the global economy.

Conclusion:

The commodities you’ve read about today are vital drivers of the global economy. As trends and demands shift, it’s essential to stay attuned to these changes. By keeping an eye on these top 10 commodities, you’ll get an idea of the directions in which the economy will be heading in 2021.
