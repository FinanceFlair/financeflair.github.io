Short Term Trading: Trading the Morning and Evening Star Patterns


When it comes to short-term trading, one strategy that has proven to be effective is trading the morning and evening star patterns. These patterns are popular among traders due to their reliability and potential for significant profits. In this blog post, we will dive deep into understanding these patterns, how to identify them, and most importantly, how to trade them successfully. Morning and evening star patterns are candlestick patterns that signify potential reversals in the market. They are formed by a series of three consecutive candlesticks and are often observed at the end of an uptrend or downtrend. These patterns provide traders with valuable insights into the market sentiment and can be used to predict future price movements.

Morning Star Pattern

The morning star pattern is found at the end of a downtrend and indicates a potential reversal to an uptrend. It consists of three candlesticks:

  1. The first candlestick is a large bearish candlestick, indicating that sellers are in control.
  2. The second candlestick is a smaller candlestick that opens lower than the previous day’s close and closes higher than the midpoint of the first candlestick. This candlestick represents indecision or a potential shift in control between buyers and sellers.
  3. The third candlestick is a large bullish candlestick that closes above the midpoint of the first candlestick, indicating that buyers have taken control.

When identifying a morning star pattern, it is crucial to ensure that the second candlestick does not overlap with the first and third candlesticks. This pattern suggests that the downtrend is losing momentum, and a bullish reversal may be imminent.

Evening Star Pattern

Conversely, the evening star pattern is found at the end of an uptrend and indicates a potential reversal to a downtrend. It also consists of three candlesticks:

  1. The first candlestick is a large bullish candlestick, indicating that buyers are in control.
  2. The second candlestick is a smaller candlestick that opens higher than the previous day’s close and closes lower than the midpoint of the first candlestick. This candlestick represents indecision or a potential shift in control between buyers and sellers.
  3. The third candlestick is a large bearish candlestick that closes below the midpoint of the first candlestick, indicating that sellers have taken control.

Similar to the morning star pattern, it is crucial to ensure that the second candlestick does not overlap with the first and third candlesticks when identifying an evening star pattern. This pattern suggests that the uptrend is losing momentum, and a bearish reversal may be imminent.

How to Trade the Morning and Evening Star Patterns

Once you have identified a morning or evening star pattern, it is essential to wait for confirmation before entering a trade. Confirmation can be achieved by observing the price action after the pattern forms. Here are a few key points to consider when trading these patterns:

  1. Confirmation Candlestick: Wait for a confirmation candlestick to close before entering a trade. The confirmation candlestick should validate the reversal by moving in the opposite direction of the previous trend.
  2. Volume: Pay attention to the volume during the confirmation candlestick. An increase in volume can further validate the reversal and increase the probability of a successful trade.
  3. Stop Loss and Take Profit: Always set a stop loss to limit potential losses in case the pattern fails. Additionally, set a take profit level based on your risk-reward ratio to secure profits when the trade goes in your favor.

Remember, like any trading strategy, the morning and evening star patterns are not foolproof. It is crucial to combine them with proper risk management and additional technical analysis tools for a higher probability of success.

Conclusion

In conclusion, short-term traders can take advantage of the morning and evening star patterns to identify potential reversals in the market. These candlestick patterns provide valuable insights into market sentiment and can help traders make informed trading decisions. By following the guidelines mentioned in this blog post and incorporating proper risk management, traders can increase their chances of success when trading these patterns.

So, the next time you spot a morning or evening star pattern on your charts, remember to analyze the confirmation candlestick, volume, and set appropriate stop loss and take profit levels. Happy trading!
