Overcoming Self-Attribution Bias in Investment Decision Making


Investing can be a daunting task, filled with uncertainty and risk. As investors, we often face the challenge of making sound decisions amidst a sea of information and opinions. However, one psychological bias that can cloud our judgment and hinder our ability to make rational investment decisions is self-attribution bias. Self-attribution bias refers to the tendency to attribute positive outcomes to our own abilities and skills, while attributing negative outcomes to external factors or bad luck. In the context of investment decision making, this bias can lead us to overestimate our abilities and underestimate the role of luck or external factors in our success or failure.

So, how can we overcome self-attribution bias and make better investment decisions? Here are a few strategies to consider:

1. Recognize the Influence of Luck

Investing involves a degree of uncertainty, and luck plays a significant role in the outcome of our investments. It’s important to acknowledge that even the most skilled investors experience both wins and losses. By recognizing the influence of luck, we can avoid falling into the trap of attributing all positive outcomes solely to our own abilities.

2. Keep a Journal

Keeping a journal of our investment decisions and their outcomes can be a valuable tool in overcoming self-attribution bias. By documenting our thought process, expectations, and the actual results, we can gain a clearer picture of our decision-making patterns and identify any biases that may be influencing our judgments. This self-reflection can help us make more objective decisions in the future.

3. Seek Feedback from Others

Seeking feedback from others, such as financial advisors or experienced investors, can provide valuable insights and help us overcome self-attribution bias. By inviting external perspectives, we can gain a more balanced view of our investment decisions and identify any blind spots or biases that we may have overlooked.

4. Diversify Your Portfolio

Diversifying your investment portfolio is a proven strategy to mitigate risk and overcome self-attribution bias. By spreading your investments across different asset classes, sectors, and geographical regions, you reduce the impact of individual investment outcomes on your overall portfolio performance. This approach helps to minimize the tendency to attribute success or failure to a single investment decision.

5. Focus on Process, Not Outcome

Instead of solely focusing on the final outcome of an investment, shift your attention to the process itself. Evaluate the decision-making process, the research conducted, and the rationale behind your choices. By focusing on the quality of your decision-making process rather than the outcome, you can reduce the influence of self-attribution bias and make more rational investment decisions.

Conclusion

Overcoming self-attribution bias in investment decision making is crucial for making sound and objective choices. By recognizing the influence of luck, keeping a journal, seeking feedback from others, diversifying your portfolio, and focusing on the decision-making process, you can enhance your ability to make rational investment decisions. Remember, investing is a continuous learning process, and by being aware of our biases, we can strive to improve our decision-making skills and achieve better long-term results.
