Trading the Inside Bar Pattern: Strategies for Breakout and Reversal Trades


In the world of trading, there are countless strategies and patterns that traders use to make informed decisions and increase their chances of success. One such pattern is the Inside Bar pattern, which can be a powerful tool for identifying potential breakout and reversal trades. In this article, we will explore the concept of the Inside Bar pattern and discuss strategies for effectively trading it. The Inside Bar pattern is a two-candlestick pattern that forms within the range of the previous candlestick. The first candlestick, known as the “mother bar,” represents the existing trend, while the second candlestick, known as the “inside bar,” represents a period of consolidation or indecision. This pattern signifies a potential breakout or reversal in the market.

To identify an Inside Bar pattern, look for a candlestick whose high and low are both lower than the previous candlestick’s high and low. This indicates that the price has consolidated within a narrower range, suggesting a potential shift in market sentiment.

Breakout Trading Strategy

One common strategy for trading the Inside Bar pattern is the breakout strategy. When an Inside Bar pattern forms, traders look for a breakout above or below the mother bar’s range, indicating a potential continuation of the existing trend.

To execute this strategy, traders can place a buy order above the high of the mother bar if the breakout is bullish, or a sell order below the low of the mother bar if the breakout is bearish. This allows traders to enter the market at the early stages of a potential trend continuation.

It’s important to note that false breakouts can occur, where the price briefly moves beyond the mother bar’s range before reversing. To mitigate this risk, traders can wait for a confirmed breakout, such as the price closing above or below the mother bar’s range, before entering a trade.

Reversal Trading Strategy

In addition to breakout trades, the Inside Bar pattern can also signal potential reversals in the market. A reversal occurs when the price changes direction, moving against the existing trend. Traders can use the Inside Bar pattern to identify these reversal opportunities.

To trade reversals using the Inside Bar pattern, traders look for an inside bar that forms at a key support or resistance level. When an inside bar forms near a support level, it suggests a potential bullish reversal, while an inside bar near a resistance level indicates a potential bearish reversal.

To execute this strategy, traders can place a buy order above the high of the inside bar for a bullish reversal or a sell order below the low of the inside bar for a bearish reversal. By entering the market at the early stages of a reversal, traders can capture potential profits as the price changes direction.

Risk Management and Conclusion

As with any trading strategy, risk management is crucial when trading the Inside Bar pattern. Traders should always set stop-loss orders to limit potential losses if the trade goes against them. Additionally, it’s important to use proper position sizing and risk-reward ratios to ensure a favorable risk-to-reward profile for each trade.

In conclusion, the Inside Bar pattern is a valuable tool for traders looking to identify potential breakout and reversal trades. By understanding the formation of the pattern and implementing the appropriate strategies, traders can enhance their trading decisions and improve their overall profitability. Remember to always practice risk management and adapt your strategies to suit your individual trading style. Happy trading!
