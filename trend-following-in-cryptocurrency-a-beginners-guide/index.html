Trend Following in Cryptocurrency: A Beginner's Guide


Trend following is a widely used trading strategy in the cryptocurrency market, and it involves identifying and following the direction of a trend. This approach is based on the idea that financial markets tend to move in predictable patterns, and by identifying these patterns, traders can make informed decisions about their investments.

What is Trend Following?

Trend following is a type of technical analysis that focuses on identifying the direction of a trend and trading in the direction of that trend. This approach is based on the idea that the price of an asset will continue to move in the same direction as long as the trend is strong. Trend followers use various indicators and chart patterns to identify the direction of the trend and to determine when to enter and exit a trade.

Advantages of Trend Following

Trend following has several advantages, including:

  • High returns: Trend following can be a lucrative strategy, as it involves identifying the direction of a trend and trading in that direction. This approach is more likely to result in significant gains, as it is based on the idea that the price of an asset will continue to move in the same direction as long as the trend is strong.
  • Easy to understand: Trend following is a relatively easy strategy to understand, as it involves identifying the direction of a trend and trading in that direction. This approach is less likely to result in confusion or misunderstandings, as it is based on a simple and straightforward principle.

Disadvantages of Trend Following

Trend following also has several disadvantages, including:

  • High emotional involvement: Trend following can be a high-emotional-involvement strategy, as it involves identifying the direction of a trend and trading in that direction. This approach can be emotionally challenging, as it requires traders to stay focused and disciplined in the face of market volatility.
  • High risk of whipsaws: Trend following can be a high-risk strategy, as it involves identifying the direction of a trend and trading in that direction. This approach can result in significant losses if the trend is not strong, or if the trend reverses direction suddenly.
  • Requires significant capital: Trend following requires significant capital, as it involves trading in the direction of a trend. This approach can be costly, as it requires traders to have a large amount of capital available to trade with.
