Home Maintenance on a Budget: 10 Frugal Hacks


Maintaining a home can be a costly affair, especially with the rising expenses of labor, materials, and equipment. However, it doesn't have to be that way. With a little bit of creativity and resourcefulness, homeowners can perform routine maintenance tasks without breaking the bank. Here are 10 frugal hacks to help you maintain your home without overspending:

  1. DIY Repairs Don't be afraid to get your hands dirty and take on small repair jobs yourself. Learning basic repair skills can save you a fortune in labor costs. Websites like YouTube and The Family Handyman offer excellent tutorials on repairing leaky faucets, creaky doors, and more. For instance, instead of hiring a plumber to fix a leaky faucet, you can buy a universal faucet cartridge for under $20 and replace it yourself.
  2. Shop for Second-Hand Materials Before buying new materials, scour local thrift stores, garage sales, and online marketplaces for gently used and refurbished items. You can score amazing deals on gently used tools, hardware, and materials. For example, you can find affordable and almost-new cabinets, sinks, and fixtures.
  3. Use Multifunctional Items Opt for multifunctional tools that can perform various tasks. Examples include cordless drills that can also serve as impact drivers, saws, and sanders. This reduces clutter and saves space in your toolshed or garage. Some popular options include:

Multitool pocket knives Cordless hand saws Compact routers

  1. Apply Protective Coatings Yourself You can apply protective coatings like epoxy, urethane, or waterproofing agents yourself with minimal equipment. Protecting your home's surfaces from damage can save you money on repairs later. Pre-made kits for epoxy, urethane, or waterproofing are easily available online and cost significantly less than hiring professionals to apply them.
  2. Take Care of Your HVAC System Regular HVAC maintenance is essential for efficient heating and cooling, as well as increasing the lifespan of the system. Replacing your compressed air tank with a new one every few years is crucial. Proper maintenance also reduces costs associated with premature replacement. Websites like Cheng Hookbill offer cleaner-burn products which increases efficiency.

  4. Create Your Own Cleaning Solutions Chemical-based cleaning agents are not only a waste of money but also pose health risks. This hack lets you create your own eco-friendly and cost-effective cleaning solutions using basic household ingredients like vinegar, baking soda, and lemon juice. Mix equal parts water and white vinegar in a spray bottle for an all-purpose cleaner. For tough stains, combine baking soda and water to create a paste.
  5. Reduce Heat Loss Winterize your home's windows, doors, and exterior walls to reduce heat loss. Consider using thermal curtains, adding weatherstripping, and sealing gaps with caulk or spray foam. These simple measures can save you up to 10% on heating costs.
