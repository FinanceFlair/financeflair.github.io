Position Trading and the Power of Trend Following


For anyone who dabbles in the world of financial markets, the array of trading strategies can feel overwhelming. Day trading, scalping, swing trading, and buy-and-hold each have their merits and rabid communities of supporters. However, sitting quietly amidst the noise lies a time-tested approach that often flies just under the radar: position trading with a strong focus on trend following. This strategy’s staying power is rooted in its elegant simplicity and its ability to capitalize on the most powerful force in markets—major trends.

Let’s dig into how position trading works, why trend following remains such an enduring principle, and how you can combine these elements into a practical routine for success in stocks, forex, commodities, or cryptocurrencies.

Understanding Position Trading

Picture the spectrum of trading timeframes—scalpers hold trades for minutes, day traders for hours, swing traders for days to weeks, and long-term investors for years. Position traders occupy the sweet spot between swing traders and investors. They aim to capture the “big moves,” holding positions for weeks, months, or even longer, depending on the persistency of the trend.

Unlike short-term traders who fret about each uptick and downtick, position traders commit to their idea for a significant period. This approach not only reduces the impact of short-term noise but also minimizes the emotional rollercoaster induced by daily price swings. Importantly, position traders rely less on the minute details of market timing and focus more on the broad direction—the trend.

The Rationale Behind Trend Following

Since the earliest days of speculation, the idea that “the trend is your friend” has proven itself repeatedly. Trend following is built on two key premises:

  1. Markets Move in Trends: Prices, due to momentum, sentiment, and macroeconomic flows, often move directionally for protracted periods.
  2. Trends Last Longer Than Most Anticipate: Once in motion, trends have a tendency to persist as traders and investors gradually jump aboard.

Ed Seykota, Richard Dennis, and countless other legendary traders have shown that betting with the prevailing direction, rather than against it, consistently outperforms attempts to “call tops and bottoms.” Trend followers believe that they don’t need to predict; instead, they react systematically when the market confirms a significant directional move.

Core Principles of Position Trading with Trend Following

1. Trade With the Big Picture

Position trading is less concerned with day-to-day volatility. Instead, the focus is on catching major uptrends (bull markets) or downtrends (bear markets), often signaled by weeks or months of price action. By aligning with the dominant trend, position traders act as shepherds, rather than sheep, steering clear of the pitfalls that befall those attempting to time micro-movements.

2. Let Profits Run, Cut Losses Quickly

Perhaps the most pivotal discipline for the position trader is avoiding the temptation to “take profits too soon.” Trend following demands patience; letting winning trades grow while ruthlessly cutting losing trades before they inflict significant damage. This asymmetric approach—a few big winners and many small losers—mirrors the famous Pareto Principle, where a minority of trades generate the majority of returns.

3. Use Effective Trend Detection Tools

Identifying and confirming trends is both art and science. Simple moving averages, such as the 50-day and 200-day, are classic tools for a reason. Crossovers, price breaking above or below key averages, and consistent higher highs or lower lows all serve as signals of a dominant trend. Volume analysis, as well as advanced indicators like the Average Directional Index (ADX), can also provide valuable context.

4. Embrace Systematic Rules Over Gut Feelings

A successful position trader using trend following often relies on a well-developed system, rather than hunches. Entry and exit rules, position sizing, and risk controls are set in advance and followed slavishly. This mitigates the threat of emotional decision-making and overcomes some of the behavioral biases that routinely cripple traders—fear, greed, and impatience.

5. Manage Risk Above All

No approach is infallible. Even the best trend followers endure a string of losses. By keeping risk per trade modest (often 1-2% of portfolio equity) and using stop-losses religiously, position traders live to fight another day. Diversifying across several markets or sectors adds a further layer of protection.

The Mechanics of Trend Following Position Trading

A peek under the hood reveals the beautiful simplicity of the trend following system when applied to position trading. Here’s an example structure:

1. Scan for Trending Markets

At the start of each week, scan markets for strong, persistent trends. That might involve:

  • Identifying stocks making new 52-week highs or lows
  • Seeking currency pairs in sustained uptrends or downtrends
  • Spotting commodities breaking out of multi-month ranges

2. Enter on Confirmation

Rather than jumping in at the first hint of movement, trend followers look for confirmation—a decisive breakout, a crossover on the moving averages, or a fresh multi-period high. This minimizes the chances of being tricked by false starts.

3. Place Stops and Trail Them

Every position should have an initial stop-loss. As the trade moves favorably, trailing stops can be used to lock in profits. For example, a 20-day low might trigger an exit in a long position, allowing room for normal pullbacks but preserving gains when a trend genuinely reverses.

4. Scale Positions or Hold Steady

Some practitioners advocate for scaling into trends as they prove themselves, pyramid-style. Others hold their original size throughout. Either way, the objective is to hold as long as the trend remains intact.

5. Exit Only When the Trend Dies

The discipline is in refusing to flinch at every correction. Only when an established trend materially reverses—say, the price closes below a key moving average, or the price structure changes from higher highs to lower lows—does the position trader close up shop and look for new opportunities.

The Rewards and Challenges of Trend Following for Position Traders

Rewards

  • Reduced Frequency of Trading: Fewer trades mean lower fees and less time monitoring screens.
  • Capitalizing on Big Moves: Trends often produce outsized gains compared to rapid in-and-out trading.
  • Clarity and Simplicity: Objective rules reduce emotional stress and “paralysis by analysis.”

Challenges

  • Whipsaws and Flat Markets: In sideways or choppy markets, trend-followers may endure multiple small losses before a new trend emerges.
  • Patience Is Hard: Watching winners and losers develop over weeks and months requires emotional resilience.
  • Drawdowns: Every system has rough periods. Trusting a proven approach is crucial, especially when it feels unrewarding in the short term.

Case Studies: Position Trading in Action

Stock Market Example

In 2020, shares of tech giants like Apple, Microsoft, and Tesla exhibited relentless uptrends. Position traders who bought these stocks as they broke to new highs and trailed their stops below rising trendlines banked significant profits, while those who attempted to time short-term reversals missed the broader move.

Forex Market Example

The EUR/USD pair in 2017 delivered a textbook upward trend after years of malaise. A position trader entering on a breakout above a multi-month resistance level, and trailing a stop below each swing low, would have captured a substantial portion of the trend with minimal stress.

Commodity Market Example

Gold, in its 2019–2020 rally, broke out of a long base. Traders who followed the trend—using weekly chart signals and trailing stops—enjoyed the ride as gold soared and then exited when the trend ultimately broke.

Building Your Own Position Trading System

Interested in harnessing the power of trend following through position trading? Here’s a step-by-step roadmap:

1. Select Your Market(s)

Start by choosing markets with clear liquidity and volatility. Major stock indices, leading stocks, currency pairs, and popular commodities are all ideal candidates.

2. Define “Trend” for Your System

Will you use moving averages, new highs/lows, price structure, or a combination? Test a few techniques using historical data to see which fits your temperament and goals.

3. Set Entry, Exit, and Stop Rules

Clarity is essential. For example:

  • Buy when price closes above a 20-week high.
  • Place an initial stop at the 10-week low.
  • Exit if price closes below the 40-week moving average.

4. Backtest Your Approach

Before risking capital, run your system through historical data. This helps set realistic expectations for win rate, profitability, and drawdowns.

5. Position Size Wisely

Avoid putting too much on the line. Even the best systems endure losing streaks. Calculating risk as a percentage of capital ensures longevity.

6. Stick to the Plan

The hardest part isn’t spotting trends or building the system. It’s having the conviction to follow it through inevitable ups and downs. Changing rules midstream often leads to disaster.

Blending Position Trading and Trend Following in Today’s Markets

With the proliferation of low-cost trading platforms, more data than ever before, and cryptocurrencies adding 24/7 dynamics, the opportunity for position trading with trend following tools is greater than ever. Algorithms and high-frequency traders dominate the micro-movements. However, the big, enduring trends are too large for even the monsters of Wall Street to fully exploit.

Individual traders retain a distinct edge—an ability to be patient, flexible, and unconstrained by quarterly performance pressures. By resisting the lure of overtrading and focusing on the major trends in your chosen markets, it’s entirely possible to stack the odds in your favor.

The magic of position trading with trend following isn’t in chasing every tick. It’s in capturing the meat of the move, riding the wave longer than most are willing, and stepping aside at the first true sign that the tide has turned.

Final Thoughts

In the noisy cauldron of financial markets, simplicity often beats complexity. Position trading, buoyed by the timeless principles of trend following, offers a path for traders who crave both profitability and sanity.

You won’t win every battle—no strategy can promise that. However, by aligning both your method and your mindset with the genuine currents that move markets, you tilt the ultimate war for consistent returns in your favor.

If you haven’t explored the enduring power of trend following as a position trader, now’s as good a time as any. Markets may change, but the psychology of crowds and the staying power of trends never lose their relevance. Stick with the big moves, keep your rules simple, and you might just find that trend truly is your friend.
