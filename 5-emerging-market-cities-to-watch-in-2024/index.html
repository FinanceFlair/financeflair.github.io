5 Emerging Market Cities to Watch in 2024


As the world navigates through a volatile economic landscape, emerging markets are stepping into the spotlight. Cities in developing regions are undergoing rapid urbanization, driven by increasing population growth, access to modern infrastructure, and a desire for quality of life. The focus for the next year should be on understanding the transformation shaping these markets. Here are five cities leading this transformation and destined for prominence on the global stage. #1 Dar Es Salaam, Tanzania Tanzania's largest city, Dar Es Salaam, is at the forefront of urban renewal. Its strategic location on the Indian Ocean is driving expansion and growth. As part of this expansion, the city is seeing significant investment in infrastructure projects. Dar Es Salaam's growth is driven by both domestic and foreign investors. Investors are attracted to the burgeoning construction sector and a youth demographic increasing the workforce population, set to cross 1 million by 2025. With it being Tanzania's main financial, commercial, and industrial hub, Dar Es Salaam stands as a prime example of urban renewal, leveraging technological advancements and human ingenuity to become a beacon of opportunity in East Africa. #2 Lucknow, India Lucknow, India, is making strides under the earmarks of Uttar Pradesh’s governmental figures, aiming to transform it into a state-of-the-art city. Lucknow, often termed as the 'City of Nawabs', is emerging as a rising smart city, although is aspiring strong through governance and transparency refurbishing public services, technology driven urban governance and domestic economic expansion. As a result, infrastructure projects such as smart city initiative are being entrusted for improvement. Major reforms could emerge in the sector of road construction, water supply, public bus transport and insurance services aiming for better habitability.
