The Best Online Platforms for Real Asset Investing


Investing in real assets has steadily gained momentum among both seasoned investors and newcomers seeking diversification beyond traditional stocks and bonds. Real assets refer to tangible assets such as real estate, commodities, infrastructure, and natural resources, which possess intrinsic value and often act as a hedge against inflation. Thanks to technological advancements and the rise of fintech, a growing number of online platforms now make it straightforward and accessible to invest in these asset classes.

Whether you are interested in direct ownership, fractional investing, or passive income opportunities, the digital landscape offers a broad spectrum of options. This guide dives deep into some of the best online platforms for real asset investing, highlighting their unique features, investment types, fees, minimums, and overall user experience. By the end, you’ll have a clearer picture of which platform aligns best with your investment goals.

Why Real Asset Investing?

Before exploring platforms, understanding why real assets are a valuable addition to portfolios is key. Real assets often provide:

  • Inflation Protection: Unlike cash, tangible assets usually maintain value or appreciate during inflationary periods.
  • Diversification: They tend to have low correlation with traditional financial instruments like stocks and bonds.
  • Income Generation: Assets like real estate or infrastructure projects often yield rental or dividend income.
  • Tangible Value: Real assets represent physical property or commodities, giving a sense of security to many investors.

Historically, incorporating real assets has improved portfolio stability and returns during volatile markets. With that context, let’s dissect the best online options for real asset exposure.

1. Fundrise: Real Estate and More with Low Minimums

Overview:
Fundrise is a pioneer in democratizing real estate investing for individuals by enabling investments in diversified real estate portfolios via eREITs and eFunds. It’s designed for investors seeking passive income and portfolio diversification without the hassle of property management.

What’s Offered:

  • Residential and commercial real estate projects across the US
  • Dividend-focused real estate investment trusts (eREITs)
  • Low investment minimums, typically starting at $500

Key Benefits:

  • Transparent performance dashboards
  • Direct quarterly dividend payments
  • User-friendly mobile and web apps
  • Multiple portfolio options tailored to different risk preferences

Considerations:

  • Illiquid investment class with typical holding periods of 5 to 7 years
  • Some projects involve higher risk due to development or market fluctuations

Fundrise’s blend of accessible minimums and professional management makes it an excellent entry point for beginners seeking real estate exposure without acquiring physical property.

2. Roofstock: Purchase Rental Properties Online

Overview:
Roofstock specializes in single-family rental homes that investors can purchase directly online. It targets those who want hands-on real estate ownership with a streamlined transaction process and optional property management services.

What’s Offered:

  • Verified rental properties with detailed financial and inspection reports
  • Options to acquire turnkey rental homes already leased to tenants
  • Access to financing through preferred lenders

Key Benefits:

  • Ability to own rental properties remotely
  • Potential monthly rental income from day one
  • In-depth neighborhood analytics and investment scorecards
  • Optional property management services for hands-free ownership

Considerations:

  • Higher capital requirements typically starting at tens of thousands
  • Requires due diligence and some real estate savvy
  • Operational responsibilities if you choose a self-managed route

Roofstock appeals to investors wanting tangible real estate holdings along with an extensive online marketplace that simplifies buying, selling, and managing rentals.

3. Masterworks: Art as an Alternative Asset

Overview:
Masterworks provides an opportunity to invest in blue-chip art pieces by purchasing shares in the artwork. This platform taps into the historically appreciated art market, making it accessible through fractional ownership.

What’s Offered:

  • Shares in iconic paintings and artworks by renowned artists
  • Professional valuation and storage of physical assets
  • Secondary market trading for liquidity options

Key Benefits:

  • Access to a unique asset class unavailable on traditional exchanges
  • Artworks are insured and stored securely
  • Potential for long-term capital appreciation independent of market cycles

Considerations:

  • Extended holding periods—typically several years
  • Illiquid compared to stocks or ETFs, despite the secondary market
  • Complex tax implications depending on sale and holding

Investors seeking portfolio diversification beyond real estate can consider Masterworks to tap into the alternative investment space that art represents.

4. AcreTrader: Farmland and Agricultural Land Investing

Overview:
AcreTrader enables investors to buy shares in farmland properties across the United States. With agribusiness demand rising due to population growth and changing consumption patterns, farmland presents a compelling long-term investment.

What’s Offered:

  • Fractional ownership in income-generating farmland
  • Access to annual rental income and land appreciation
  • Detailed property reports and market insights

Key Benefits:

  • Farmland has exhibited strong historical returns and inflation resistance
  • Passive income through lease payments to farmers
  • Environmentally sustainable investment option with transparent impact reporting

Considerations:

  • Minimum investments around $10,000 or more
  • Limited liquidity as holdings are expected to be medium to long term
  • Dependent on crop yields and commodity prices, which carry market risks

AcreTrader suits investors looking for exposure to real assets beyond real estate who appreciate sustainable agriculture’s economic and environmental advantages.

5. YieldStreet: Diversified Alternative Asset Marketplace

Overview:
YieldStreet offers a diverse array of alternative investments across various real asset categories, including real estate, legal finance, marine finance, and commercial loans. Their offerings generally provide yield-oriented opportunities.

What’s Offered:

  • Real estate-backed loans and vehicle fleets
  • Art financing and litigation finance
  • Income-generating asset-backed securities

Key Benefits:

  • Potential for attractive fixed income-style returns
  • Range of investment terms and structures
  • Institutional-level due diligence for every offering

Considerations:

  • Investments can be illiquid with lock-up periods often 1 to 3 years
  • Higher minimum investments compared to some platforms
  • Risk profile varies substantially by offering, requiring careful review

YieldStreet is ideal for sophisticated investors seeking diversified alternative assets in a curated marketplace outside traditional financial products.

6. DiversyFund: Direct-to-Consumer Real Estate Growth Portfolio

Overview:
DiversyFund offers an opportunity to invest directly in growing multifamily real estate properties through their Growth REIT. They prioritize capital appreciation through value-add property improvements.

What’s Offered:

  • Access to multifamily apartment complexes
  • Value-add strategies to enhance property performance and appreciation
  • No fees charged on invested capital, unlike many competitors

Key Benefits:

  • Lower fees increase potential net returns for investors
  • Educational resources for new investors
  • Automated portfolio management with no active role required

Considerations:

  • Investment holds typically last 5 to 7 years
  • Requires accreditation status for certain offerings
  • Real estate market risks still apply, including tenant and market variability

DiversyFund is appealing for investors aiming to grow equity over time with a focus on multifamily properties and transparent fee structures.

7. Cadre: Institutional-Grade Commercial Real Estate

Overview:
Cadre provides access to commercial real estate investments typically reserved for institutional investors and high-net-worth individuals. Their platform offers a selection of commercial properties with rigorous underwriting.

What’s Offered:

  • Commercial office, retail, industrial, and residential income properties
  • Detailed investment summaries and market research
  • Ability to buy and sell shares on a secondary market

Key Benefits:

  • Higher transparency and due diligence standards
  • Secondary market provides some liquidity opportunities
  • Potential for both capital appreciation and income generation

Considerations:

  • Minimum investments often start at $50,000 or higher
  • Requires accreditation for full access
  • Commercial real estate risk profile with sensitivity to economic cycles

Cadre is well-suited for investors seeking institutional-quality commercial real estate with a platform designed for transparency.

Key Factors to Consider When Choosing a Platform

Selecting the right real asset investment platform depends on your individual objectives, risk tolerance, and capital availability. Here are some criteria to evaluate:

  • Investment Minimums: Some platforms cater to beginners with low entry points, while others require significant commitments.
  • Asset Class Preference: Real estate, farmland, art, or diversified alternatives—all have distinct characteristics and growth drivers.
  • Liquidity: Understand lock-up periods and whether secondary trading is possible for your investments.
  • Fees and Expenses: Compare management fees, performance fees, and any hidden costs that affect net returns.
  • Due Diligence and Transparency: Platforms vary in how much information they provide upfront about investments and risks.
  • User Experience: Quality research tools, customer support, and interface usability influence ongoing engagement.

Taking time to align platform offerings with your personal investment goals will greatly enhance your confidence and satisfaction.

Final Thoughts

The rise of online platforms has revolutionized access to real asset investing, removing many traditional barriers such as high capital requirements, geographic constraints, and operational complexities. Whether your focus is passive real estate income, alternative collectibles like art, sustainable farmland, or high-yield alternative credit markets, the digital investment ecosystem can cater to diverse appetites.

Remember that while real assets can diversify and potentially strengthen your portfolio, they also carry unique risks and often require longer time horizons compared to liquid stocks and bonds. Conducting thorough research, understanding the platform specifics, and considering your own financial context will help you maximize the benefits these opportunities offer.

Real asset investing is no longer an exclusive club—it’s available at your fingertips. Embrace the possibilities and explore the platforms best suited to your ambitions.

This guide synthesizes critical insights into today’s top online platforms providing real asset investment opportunities. Empower yourself with knowledge and take steps toward a more diversified, resilient investment portfolio.
