The Best Micro-Investing Books to Read in 2023


Micro-investing has emerged as a revolutionary way to grow wealth, especially for those who may feel intimidated by traditional investing. By focusing on small, incremental contributions, this strategy makes it possible for anyone—regardless of income level or experience—to start building a financial foundation. Whether you’re exploring ways to dip your toes into investing or looking to deepen your understanding of how micro-investing works, the right books can serve as invaluable guides.

To help you navigate the ever-expanding world of personal finance literature, we’ve curated a list of the best micro-investing books to read in 2023. These titles provide actionable advice, accessible language, and relatable examples, making them ideal for both beginners and seasoned investors eager to refine their strategies.

1. The Little Book of Common Sense Investing by John C. Bogle

A timeless classic, this book by John C. Bogle, the founder of Vanguard, espouses the power of index fund investing, which aligns closely with the philosophy of micro-investing. The book’s central focus is on minimizing fees, avoiding speculative behavior, and relying on the steady growth of diversified investments.

Bogle’s advice is particularly relevant for micro-investors because the principles of low-cost, incremental investing are consistent with the way most micro-investing apps are structured. Through this book, readers learn why simplicity, discipline, and patience are vital ingredients for long-term financial success.

2. Your Money or Your Life by Vicki Robin and Joe Dominguez

Although not specifically about micro-investing, Your Money or Your Life offers a holistic approach to managing finances. This transformative book tackles topics ranging from financial independence to how your relationship with money impacts your life.

For micro-investors, the real value lies in its focus on mindfulness and intentionality. By understanding what money truly represents in your life, you’ll gain clarity on why and how to invest, even if you’re starting small. This book provides the psychological foundation necessary to view micro-investing not just as a financial strategy but as a stepping stone toward a more meaningful relationship with wealth.

3. The Simple Path to Wealth by JL Collins

JL Collins has a talent for breaking down complicated financial concepts into digestible advice for everyday readers. The Simple Path to Wealth stems from letters Collins wrote to his daughter, explaining how to achieve financial independence through consistent and measured steps.

The book introduces readers to the “F-You Money” concept, focusing on living below your means and investing in low-cost index funds. This aligns perfectly with the ethos of micro-investing, where individuals prioritize regular investments—no matter how small—over sporadic, risky financial behavior. It’s a foundational read for anyone wanting to understand the basics of wealth-building.

4. The Automatic Millionaire by David Bach

David Bach’s The Automatic Millionaire is a gem for people looking to build financial security with minimal effort. The key takeaway is automation—setting up systems that take the guesswork and emotional decisions out of investing.

This approach works seamlessly with micro-investing strategies. Most micro-investing apps focus on automated contributions, round-ups, and allocations, which makes this book a great companion. With simple, no-nonsense advice, Bach teaches readers why paying yourself first is essential and how small, consistent efforts can yield substantial results over time.

5. I Will Teach You to Be Rich by Ramit Sethi

Ramit Sethi’s conversational tone and actionable steps make his book I Will Teach You to Be Rich one of the most approachable financial guides available. The book outlines a six-week program to get your finances in order, including automation, saving, investing, and optimizing spending.

For micro-investors, the section on “Conscious Spending Plans” and automated investing is especially relevant. Sethi emphasizes starting small but starting now, echoing the sentiment that waiting for “more money” to invest is usually counterproductive. His relatable anecdotes and enthusiasm make it an enjoyable yet informative read.

6. The Bogleheads’ Guide to Investing by Taylor Larimore, Mel Lindauer, and Michael LeBoeuf

Written by a group of devoted followers of John Bogle’s investment philosophy, this guide is a fantastic resource for micro-investors aiming to take the next step in their financial education. The Boglehead philosophy advocates a practical, no-frills approach to investing, including diversification, low fees, and consistent contributions.

While the book is rich in detail, the authors succeed in explaining complex topics in a way that’s understandable to beginners. The principles discussed here, like dollar-cost averaging and the power of compounding, are cornerstones of successful micro-investing.

7. Broke Millennial by Erin Lowry

Erin Lowry’s Broke Millennial is tailor-made for younger investors navigating the financial challenges of early adulthood. Written in a humorous, no-judgment tone, this book provides a guide to handling debt, budgeting, and investing without feeling overwhelmed.

Micro-investors will particularly appreciate the sections on starting small—overcoming the misconception that investing is only for those with significant disposable income. If you’re a millennial or Gen Z struggling to get your financial life in order, this book offers a step-by-step approach to becoming an educated investor.

8. Think and Grow Rich by Napoleon Hill

Though not a traditional investing book, Napoleon Hill’s Think and Grow Rich is a motivational classic that highlights the mental aspects of wealth creation. Hill emphasizes the importance of mindset, goal setting, and persistence, which are just as vital to micro-investing as they are to larger financial strategies.

For those new to investing, cultivating the right mindset is often the biggest hurdle. This book encourages readers to believe in their ability to grow wealth, no matter how small their starting point is.

9. Micro-Investing for Beginners by Emily Collins

Written specifically for those intrigued by the concept of micro-investing, this recently published book breaks down everything from choosing investment apps to understanding market basics. Collins provides actionable tips on how to maximize returns while minimizing risks, and she includes comparisons of popular micro-investing platforms.

What sets this book apart is its dedication to micro-investing as its central theme. The clear, concise advice is perfect for readers who want a focused guide to this specific investing method without being overwhelmed by unrelated financial concepts.

10. The Millionaire Next Door by Thomas J. Stanley and William D. Danko

This perennial favorite uncovers the habits and traits of everyday millionaires, focusing on frugality, discipline, and consistent saving and investing. For micro-investors, it serves as a wake-up call that building wealth is often about lifestyle choices rather than extraordinary income.

The book’s emphasis on living within one’s means and the power of compound growth resonates deeply with anyone engaged in micro-investing. It’s a valuable reminder that even small, consistent actions can lead to substantial results.

Why These Books Matter for Micro-Investing

Micro-investing is about more than just finding a convenient app or setting up automatic contributions. It’s about understanding your financial goals, building the discipline to stay consistent, and developing the knowledge to make informed decisions. Each title in this list was chosen because it aligns with these principles, offering unique insights that cater to micro-investors’ needs.

How to Choose the Best Micro-Investing Book for You

With so many excellent titles to choose from, it can be challenging to decide where to begin. Here are a few tips:

  • Consider Your Goals: Are you a beginner looking to understand the basics, or do you want to refine an existing strategy? Books like The Little Book of Common Sense Investing are great for starting out, while something like The Bogleheads’ Guide to Investing might appeal to those looking to delve deeper.
  • Evaluate Your Learning Style: If you prefer actionable steps, books like The Automatic Millionaire or I Will Teach You to Be Rich are ideal. For those who enjoy philosophical reflections, Your Money or Your Life and Think and Grow Rich may be more appealing.
  • Identify Specific Interests: If you want a book specifically about micro-investing, Emily Collins’ Micro-Investing for Beginners is a clear winner.

Discovering the best micro-investing books is a critical step toward financial independence. Armed with the lessons from these insightful reads, you’ll be better equipped to make informed decisions, stick with your investment strategy, and watch your wealth grow—one small contribution at a time.
