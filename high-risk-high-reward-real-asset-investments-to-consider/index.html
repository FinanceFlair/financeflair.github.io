High-Risk, High-Reward Real Asset Investments to Consider


Investors who are willing to take calculated risks and think outside the box may consider venturing into high-risk, high-reward real asset investments. These types of investments can provide potentially substantial returns, but they also come with a significant amount of volatility, making them unsuitable for risk-averse investors. If you're one of the brave souls who is eager to grow your wealth by taking calculated risks, here are some high-risk, high-reward real asset investments to consider:

  1. Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) Real estate investment trusts are companies that own, operate, or finance income-generating properties and provide investors with a way to gain exposure to the real estate market without directly managing properties. REITs offer the opportunity to diversify a portfolio by including a mix of property types, locations, and asset classes. This diversification can be particularly beneficial for investors who are new to real estate investing or looking to take a more conservative approach. However, some REITs are sensitive to economic downturns and can be volatile, making them suitable for investors who can withstand short-term price fluctuations. REITs have the benefits of regular income and the potential for long-term growth. You can choose to invest in publicly traded REITs or those that allow you to invest directly in a specific property or property development project. Keep in mind that while REITs are popular investment vehicles, it's crucial to do your due diligence before making a decision.
  2. Real Asset Investment Funds (RAIFs) Real asset investment funds function similarly to private equity funds but are made up of a mix of assets, such as real property, heavy machinery, and other commercial assets. These funds typically allow investors to pool together money with professional investors to create a diverse portfolio that aims to provide a significant return on investment. Real asset investment funds can be invested in a variety of areas, from energy and logistics to social housing and transportation systems. However, investing in RAIFs can be challenging, especially for smaller investors. These funds usually require substantial capital to secure a stake or even participate. There's also a significant amount of due diligence required to select the most advantageous fund. Once again, we urge caution as this type of investment is not suitable for risk-averse investors or those who are new to investing.
  3. Real Estate Crowdfunding Investing in real estate through crowdfunding platforms allows individuals to pool money with others and fund projects such as property development or mortgage projects. Crowdfunding can be beneficial for investors with smaller budgets as it reduces the required capital outlay and can help avoid large initial investment costs. Crowdfunding platforms are becoming more comprehensive and user-friendly, allowing for more straightforward exposure to high-yield investment opportunities. Real estate crowdfunding can be seen as a medium-risk investment due to measures such as insurance, revolving doors, and ongoing equity injections that ensure even smaller investors get their investment back. These investments are not suitable for risk-averse investors. Investment Options

  1. Private Equity Investments Private equity investments involve investing in privately held businesses, providing attractive returns but involving significant risks.
  2. Commodities Trading Commodities trading involves investing in industries centered around raw materials, providing substantial returns due to its highly leveraged nature.
  3. Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) REITs offer the opportunity to invest in property without directly managing properties, providing regular income and potential long-term growth but sensitive to economic downturns.
  4. Crowdfunding Crowdfunding involves pooling money with others to fund projects like property development or mortgage projects, carrying various risks and unsuitable for risk-averse investors. Warning and Risks Investing in these types of assets carries various risks. Thorough research and caution are advised before making an investment decision.

Associated risks include market fluctuation, liquidity risk, and potential losses. High-risk investments often come with lower liquidity, making it difficult to exit the investment quickly. High-risk investments can be sensitive to market volatility and economic downturns.

Conclusion High-risk, high-reward investments may provide substantial returns but are not suitable for risk-averse investors.
