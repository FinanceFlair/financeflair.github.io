Trend Following Success Stories from Famous Traders


Trend following stands out as one of the most enduring and effective trading strategies in the financial markets. This approach relies on the principle that asset prices often move in sustained directional trends, which traders can identify and capitalize upon. Over the decades, many legendary traders have embraced trend following and achieved remarkable success, inspiring countless investors and traders around the globe. In this post, we’ll delve into some of the most inspiring success stories from famous trend followers, uncovering their strategies, philosophies, and the lessons they offer for aspiring traders.

Understanding Trend Following: A Brief Overview

Before exploring the success stories, it’s helpful to revisit what makes trend following unique. Simply put, trend followers seek to enter markets when a clear upward or downward momentum is established, riding the wave until evidence suggests a reversal or exhaustion.

Unlike many traders who attempt to predict market tops and bottoms, trend followers patiently wait for confirmation of a trend and then trade in the trend’s direction. This disciplined approach often involves strict risk management and the use of technical tools such as moving averages, breakout systems, or momentum indicators. Historical data has shown that markets tend to exhibit persistent trends, making trend following a strategy that aligns more closely with market realities than trying to outguess short-term fluctuations.

Richard Dennis: The Turtle Trader Phenomenon

One of the most famous endorsements of trend following comes from Richard Dennis, often called the “Prince of the Pit.” A commodities trader on the Chicago Mercantile Exchange during the 1970s and 1980s, Dennis built a fortune trading futures based on systematic trend following principles.

What made his story legendary was an informal experiment in 1983. Dennis believed that trend following could be taught to anyone, while his friend William Eckhardt thought successful trading was more a matter of innate talent. To prove his point, Dennis recruited and trained a group of novices—later known as the “Turtles”—teaching them a specific, rule-based trend following system.

The Turtle Trading System

The Turtle system relied mainly on breakout techniques—entering long when prices broke above certain highs and short when they fell below lows. They used a combination of 20-day and 55-day breakouts and employed strict money management rules, such as limiting position sizes based on market volatility.

Over several years, many of these turtles generated extraordinary returns, sometimes up to 100% annually. Remarkably, most of these profits came from capturing multi-month trends across commodities, currencies, and financial futures. This experiment not only vindicated Dennis’s belief in trend following but also highlighted that disciplined, systematized trading can transcend emotional decision-making.

Lessons and Legacy

Dennis’s story exemplifies how trend following, when combined with strict risk management and mental discipline, can yield outstanding results. It also underscores the value of having a well-defined entry and exit strategy, rather than making subjective calls.

Ed Seykota: The Technical Pioneer with an Intuitive Edge

Ed Seykota is another towering figure in trend following history. He was one of the earliest adopters of computerized trading systems and was featured in Jack Schwager’s book Market Wizards. Seykota’s approach blended rigorous system development with a unique psychological understanding of trading behavior.

Early Innovations and Results

Seykota’s systems used moving averages and price channels to identify trends, a methodological approach during a time when electronic trading was in its infancy. What distinguished him was his ability to refine systems continuously and his emphasis on strict adherence to stops and position sizing.

Over time, his trading accounts achieved extraordinary compounded returns, often exceeding 50% annually, showing consistency across different market cycles. Seykota remarked that emotional control and alignment of the trader’s mindset with the system were as important as the system’s rules themselves.

Psychological Insights

He famously said, “The market is always right,” highlighting his acceptance of losses and the importance of cutting losing trades rapidly. His awareness that psychological biases could disrupt trading decisions contributed to his broader success.

Paul Tudor Jones: Macro Trends and Contrarian Edges

While Paul Tudor Jones employs various strategies, trend following has been a cornerstone of his trading philosophy. Jones, a billionaire hedge fund manager, gained notoriety by correctly predicting the 1987 stock market crash.

Spotting Major Macro Trends

Jones combines fundamental macroeconomic analysis with technical trend following signals. His approach seeks to identify global trends—whether in equities, currencies, bonds, or commodities—and deploy capital accordingly.

Trend following enables Jones to stay on the right side of market moves while the macroeconomic context informs his timing and conviction levels.

Risk Control Keeps the Ship Steady

He emphasizes capital preservation, using tight stops and prudent position sizing. Jones’s success includes capturing trends over weeks or months, allowing profits to accumulate rather than chase quick gains.

John W. Henry: From Baseball to Billion-Dollar Trend Follower

John W. Henry, well-known as the owner of the Boston Red Sox, first made his fortune in commodities trading through trend following.

Early Trading Days

Henry developed systematic trend following models that harnessed patterns across futures markets. His models emphasized the power of compound growth over time and the inevitability of market trends.

By applying quantified rules on when and how to enter and exit positions, combined with dynamic risk controls, Henry’s funds outperformed many peers during the 1980s and 1990s.

Business Lessons Translated to Trading

Henry’s success demonstrates the universality of trend following principles: patience, discipline, and sticking to tested rules win over guesswork.

The Common Threads Behind Trend Following Success

While each trader mentioned above has their unique story, certain themes emerge consistently:

  • Systematic Approach: Utilizing well-defined, rules-based systems to identify trends avoids emotional trading.
  • Risk Management: Protecting capital with stops and appropriate position sizing preserves longevity.
  • Patience and Discipline: Allowing profits to run and accepting losses quickly are essential.
  • Adaptability: Continually refining strategies in line with changing market dynamics enhances sustainability.
  • Psychological Resilience: Trend followers often emphasize the mental aspect, maintaining calm through drawdowns and volatility.

How Modern Traders Can Learn from These Legends

Studying these famous trend followers offers more than just inspiration; it provides practical guidance for traders aiming to adopt this strategy:

  1. Develop Clear Entry and Exit Rules: Define how and when you will enter trends and when you will exit, preferably before you even start trading.
  2. Implement Strong Risk Controls: Never risk too much capital on a single trade; use stops, and calculate your position sizes based on market volatility.
  3. Be Prepared for Drawdowns: Recognize that no system wins all the time. Longevity is more important than occasional big wins.
  4. Maintain Trading Discipline: Stick to your system even during losing streaks; avoid impulsive decisions driven by fear or greed.
  5. Review and Improve: Backtest your strategy, analyze your trades regularly, and adapt to new market environments.

Closing Thoughts

The success stories of Richard Dennis, Ed Seykota, Paul Tudor Jones, John W. Henry, and other renowned trend followers prove that consistent, disciplined application of trend following principles can generate exceptional trading results. By focusing on the market’s natural momentum and respecting risk management rules, these traders have navigated volatile markets with remarkable resilience.

For anyone interested in trend following, these stories reinforce the importance of patience, well-structured systems, and mental toughness as the foundation of success in trading.

The journey may be challenging, with inevitable periods of loss, but as history shows, persistence and adherence to tested trend following methodologies often reward traders handsomely over time.
