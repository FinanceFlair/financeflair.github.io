Savings Apps That Help You Stay on Track


In today’s fast-paced world, managing your finances can often feel overwhelming. Between bills, loans, and unexpected expenses, it can be difficult to stay on top of your savings goals. However, with the right tools, saving money and building wealth doesn’t have to be a daunting task. Savings apps have become a game-changer for people looking to improve their financial habits and stay on track toward their goals. These apps provide convenience, simplicity, and automation, ensuring that you can save without even thinking about it.

In this blog post, we will explore some of the best savings apps available today and how they can help you maintain your financial discipline. Whether you’re saving for a rainy day, a vacation, or a down payment on a home, these tools can guide you toward achieving your goals with minimal effort.

1. Acorns: Micro-Investing Made Simple

Acorns is an app that focuses on micro-investing. It connects to your bank account and automatically rounds up your purchases to the nearest dollar. For example, if you buy a coffee for $4.50, Acorns will round it up to $5 and invest the extra 50 cents. This process is known as “round-ups,” and over time, these small amounts can add up to a significant sum.

How It Works

Once you link your credit or debit card to Acorns, the app begins automatically rounding up your transactions. You can choose from different investment portfolios based on your risk tolerance, and Acorns will manage your investment for you. If you prefer, you can also make one-time contributions or set up recurring deposits to increase your savings.

Key Features:

  • Automated savings through round-ups
  • Personalized investment portfolios
  • Simple and user-friendly interface
  • Option to invest in a retirement account (IRA)

Acorns is ideal for those who struggle to make large contributions to their savings. The app allows you to save passively without having to think about it, while still growing your wealth over time.

2. Qapital: Goal-Oriented Savings

Qapital is a popular app that helps you save by setting up individual savings goals. Whether you’re saving for a vacation, an emergency fund, or a big purchase, Qapital makes it easy to create separate savings accounts for each goal. The app also provides a fun and interactive way to track your progress.

How It Works

Qapital allows you to set up different savings goals and customize them according to your needs. For instance, if you’re saving for a new phone, you can create a goal specifically for that purchase and set a target amount. You can contribute manually or set up automatic transfers from your checking account. The app also offers a feature called “Save the Change,” which rounds up your purchases and saves the difference for your chosen goal.

Key Features:

  • Create multiple savings goals
  • Automatic savings options, such as daily or weekly transfers
  • “Save the Change” feature to round up your purchases
  • Budgeting tools to help manage spending

Qapital makes saving money feel like a rewarding challenge. With its intuitive interface and goal-based approach, you can stay motivated and track your progress easily. The app also offers a debit card that rewards you for reaching milestones in your savings goals.

3. Digit: The Automatic Savings Assistant

If you’re someone who struggles with remembering to transfer money into your savings account, Digit might be the perfect solution for you. Digit is an app that uses algorithms to analyze your spending habits and automatically transfers small amounts of money from your checking account to your Digit savings account.

How It Works

After linking your bank account to Digit, the app will monitor your spending patterns and transfer small amounts of money into your Digit account when it detects you have extra funds. These transfers typically happen in the background, so you may not even notice them happening. Digit can also be set to save for specific goals, such as paying off debt, saving for a trip, or building an emergency fund.

Key Features:

  • Automatic savings based on your spending patterns
  • Set up savings goals and track your progress
  • AI-driven transfers that are designed to be unobtrusive
  • Access your savings with no fees

Digit takes the guesswork out of saving by automating the process, ensuring that you’re consistently building your savings without any effort on your part. It’s ideal for those who tend to forget about setting aside money for the future.

4. Chime: A Bank That Helps You Save

Chime is an online bank that comes with built-in savings tools. While it offers all the traditional banking services such as checking and savings accounts, it goes a step further by automatically helping you save. One of its standout features is the automatic savings feature that helps you save a percentage of your income without thinking about it.

How It Works

Chime automatically transfers a percentage of your direct deposits into a separate savings account. You can set your savings rate to a percentage that works for you, and Chime will ensure that the money is moved over before you even see it. This eliminates the temptation to spend money that you should be saving.

Key Features:

  • Automatic savings with every paycheck
  • No hidden fees or minimum balances
  • Round-ups on purchases to help you save
  • High-yield savings account with competitive interest rates

Chime is an excellent option if you want a bank that actively helps you build savings, especially if you’re already receiving direct deposits. The app makes saving easier by automating the process, and its round-up feature allows you to save effortlessly with every purchase.

5. Simple: Budgeting and Saving in One

Simple is a mobile banking app that helps users manage their money by combining savings and budgeting features. It offers an intuitive platform where you can separate your savings into different categories or “Goals.” It’s perfect for those who want a seamless experience that combines budgeting and saving in one place.

How It Works

Simple allows you to set up different savings goals, such as a vacation fund, an emergency fund, or a down payment on a house. Once you set up your goals, you can transfer money into them manually or set up automatic transfers. Simple also has a feature called “Safe-to-Spend,” which shows you how much money you can spend after accounting for your goals and bills.

Key Features:

  • Set up multiple savings goals and track your progress
  • Automatic transfers to savings goals
  • “Safe-to-Spend” feature to avoid overspending
  • Free ATM withdrawals at over 24,000 locations

Simple’s blend of budgeting and savings tools makes it an excellent choice for people who want to maintain control over their finances while still saving for the future. The app’s visual approach to budgeting makes it easy to see where your money is going and how much you can afford to save.

6. Twine: Shared Savings for Couples

Twine is a unique savings app designed specifically for couples. It allows two people to save together for a shared goal, such as a vacation, home, or wedding. Twine allows both partners to contribute and track their progress in one place, making it easier to manage joint savings.

How It Works

To get started with Twine, you and your partner link your bank accounts to the app. From there, you can create a shared savings goal and set a target amount. Both partners can contribute manually or set up automatic transfers to fund the goal. The app also allows you to track your progress and communicate about your savings goals.

Key Features:

  • Save together for a shared goal
  • Manual or automatic contributions
  • Track your savings progress in real time
  • No fees for using the app

Twine is ideal for couples who want to combine their financial efforts for a specific purpose. It helps both partners stay on track and remain motivated as they work together toward their goal.

7. SaveUp: Reward Yourself for Saving

SaveUp is an app that turns saving money into a game. As you save, you earn chances to win rewards such as gift cards, cash prizes, or even a vacation. This unique twist on saving turns the process into a fun experience, keeping you motivated as you work toward your financial goals.

How It Works

SaveUp connects to your bank accounts and tracks your savings. For every $25 you save, you earn one “SaveUp Credit,” which gives you a chance to win prizes. You can earn multiple chances based on how much you save, making it easier to rack up credits and increase your chances of winning. The app also allows you to set up specific savings goals.

Key Features:

  • Earn chances to win rewards for saving money
  • Connects to multiple bank accounts for seamless tracking
  • Set specific savings goals and track progress
  • No fees to use the app

SaveUp is perfect for people who want to make saving fun and rewarding. The app adds an element of excitement to the process, encouraging you to save more in order to increase your chances of winning.

Conclusion

Saving money doesn’t have to be difficult or time-consuming. With the right savings app, you can make progress toward your financial goals with minimal effort. Whether you prefer to automate your savings, create specific goals, or make saving a fun challenge, there’s an app that can help you stay on track. Each of the apps we’ve covered offers unique features designed to cater to different needs, ensuring that everyone can find a tool that fits their financial habits and lifestyle.

Start using a savings app today and see how easy it is to take control of your finances. No matter your goal—whether it’s building an emergency fund, saving for a trip, or investing for the future—these apps will help you stay on track and make saving an effortless part of your routine.
