The Best Robo-Advisors for Customizable Portfolios


The rise of technology in personal finance has made investing more accessible and affordable than ever. Robo-advisors, powered by algorithms and artificial intelligence, have revolutionized the way individuals manage their investment portfolios. These platforms offer a variety of investment strategies tailored to different risk appetites, time horizons, and financial goals. However, one feature that stands out in the modern robo-advisor market is the ability to create customizable portfolios.

Customizability allows investors to have greater control over their investments, ensuring that the portfolio aligns closely with their specific preferences and objectives. In this post, we will explore some of the best robo-advisors for customizable portfolios, detailing their features, benefits, and how they compare to one another.

What Are Robo-Advisors?

Robo-advisors are digital platforms that provide automated investment advice, using algorithms to build and manage portfolios based on an individual’s financial goals, risk tolerance, and other factors. These platforms typically use a questionnaire to gather data from investors, after which they create a personalized portfolio. Some robo-advisors focus on passive management (index-based investing), while others offer more active strategies or even socially responsible investing options.

The appeal of robo-advisors lies in their ease of use, low fees, and efficient portfolio management. They democratize investing, making it possible for people with little to no experience in finance to invest smartly and effectively. But the ability to customize one’s portfolio adds a new layer of personalization, which is essential for many investors.

Why Customizable Portfolios Matter

When investing through traditional financial advisors, customization typically comes at a high cost. Robo-advisors, on the other hand, are making personalized investment strategies more affordable and accessible. Customizable portfolios allow investors to fine-tune their asset allocations, choose specific sectors, adjust risk levels, and, in some cases, select individual stocks or exchange-traded funds (ETFs). This gives investors more control over their investment strategy while still benefiting from the convenience and automation that robo-advisors offer.

Customizability is crucial for several reasons:

  • Personalized Risk Management: Each individual has different risk tolerance levels. Customizable portfolios give investors the freedom to adjust their portfolios according to their comfort with market volatility, ensuring that they are not overexposed to high-risk assets.
  • Goal-Oriented Investing: Some investors have specific financial goals, such as saving for retirement, buying a home, or funding a child’s education. Customization allows investors to shape their portfolios in a way that aligns with these goals.
  • Ethical or Socially Responsible Investing: Many investors are increasingly interested in aligning their investments with their values, such as focusing on sustainability or socially responsible companies. Customizable portfolios allow for the inclusion of such strategies.

Let’s take a deeper look into some of the best robo-advisors that offer customizable portfolio features.

1. Betterment

Betterment is one of the most well-known robo-advisors in the market. It stands out for its user-friendly platform and wide range of customization options. Betterment offers a standard portfolio for investors who want a simple, hands-off approach to investing, but it also provides a customizable portfolio option for those who want more control.

Key Features of Betterment’s Customizable Portfolios:

  • Personalized Asset Allocation: Betterment allows users to adjust their portfolio based on their risk tolerance and financial goals. Investors can choose from a variety of portfolios, ranging from conservative to aggressive, depending on how much risk they are willing to take on.
  • Tax-Coordinated Investing: For investors seeking tax optimization, Betterment offers tax-loss harvesting and tax-efficient asset location. This feature is ideal for those looking to minimize their tax liability while maximizing returns.
  • Socially Responsible Investing: Betterment’s customizable portfolios allow investors to invest in socially responsible funds, including options that focus on sustainable companies or those with high environmental, social, and governance (ESG) scores.
  • SmartRetirement Portfolio: For retirement-focused investors, Betterment’s SmartRetirement portfolio provides an auto-adjusting allocation based on the individual’s age, risk profile, and time horizon.

Pros:

  • Excellent for both beginner and advanced investors
  • Transparent fee structure
  • Offers socially responsible investment options
  • Low minimum balance required

Cons:

  • The management fee can be high for larger accounts
  • Limited direct control over specific assets

2. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is another major player in the robo-advisor industry and is particularly well-regarded for its flexible portfolio construction. Wealthfront offers investors the ability to personalize their portfolios while benefiting from automated tax optimization and financial planning tools.

Key Features of Wealthfront’s Customizable Portfolios:

  • Risk Parity Portfolio: Wealthfront uses a risk parity approach, where asset allocation is determined based on risk rather than traditional market cap weightings. This results in a more balanced portfolio with less reliance on any single asset class.
  • Personalized ETFs: Wealthfront’s customizable portfolios allow investors to select ETFs based on their investment preferences, including sector ETFs, international ETFs, and socially responsible funds.
  • Financial Planning Tools: Wealthfront includes a suite of planning tools that help investors plan for major life events, such as buying a home or saving for retirement. These tools work in conjunction with the customizable portfolio to ensure that your investments align with your broader financial goals.
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting: Wealthfront offers automated tax-loss harvesting, which can help reduce your tax burden over time. This is particularly beneficial for those with larger portfolios or higher income brackets.

Pros:

  • Offers advanced financial planning tools
  • Automated tax optimization features
  • Low fees for higher balances
  • Customizable ETF options

Cons:

  • Higher minimum investment than some competitors ($500)
  • Limited ability to pick individual stocks

3. M1 Finance

M1 Finance is unique in the robo-advisor space because it combines the ease of automated investing with the flexibility of self-directed investing. While it does have a pre-built, automated portfolio option, M1 Finance is especially popular for those who want full control over their investments within a robo-advisor framework.

Key Features of M1 Finance’s Customizable Portfolios:

  • Custom Pie-Based Portfolios: M1 Finance uses a “pie” structure, where each “slice” represents an asset class, stock, or ETF. Investors can build their own pies from scratch or choose from pre-designed pies created by M1 experts. This flexibility allows investors to customize their portfolios with ease.
  • Fractional Shares: M1 Finance allows for fractional share purchases, which means that investors can create highly diversified portfolios even with smaller amounts of money.
  • Automatic Rebalancing: M1 Finance automatically rebalances your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation. This feature is particularly useful for investors who want to maintain a diversified portfolio without having to manually adjust their holdings.
  • Direct Control Over Stocks: For investors who want a bit more control over their investments, M1 Finance allows the addition of individual stocks into their portfolio, as long as they fit within the pie structure.

Pros:

  • Total customization through pie-based portfolios
  • No management fees
  • Ability to buy fractional shares
  • Transparent fee structure

Cons:

  • Limited tax optimization features
  • Requires a bit of hands-on management compared to fully automated robo-advisors

4. SoFi Invest

SoFi Invest is another excellent option for those seeking a customizable robo-advisor experience. It offers both automated portfolios and self-directed investing, with plenty of options for personalizing investments.

Key Features of SoFi Invest’s Customizable Portfolios:

  • Socially Responsible Investing: SoFi Invest provides the option to invest in socially responsible ETFs that focus on environmental sustainability, renewable energy, and other socially responsible themes.
  • Personalized Risk Profiles: SoFi’s platform tailors its recommendations based on an investor’s risk profile, allowing for customized risk management.
  • Active & Automated Investing: SoFi Invest allows for a hybrid approach to investing, where users can choose to use automated portfolios or actively manage their holdings. This flexibility is a key differentiator in the robo-advisor market.
  • Low Fees: SoFi Invest offers zero fees for most investment accounts, making it an affordable option for investors of all levels.

Pros:

  • No fees on automated investing
  • Simple, easy-to-use interface
  • Customizable portfolios with socially responsible investing options

Cons:

  • Limited portfolio options compared to other robo-advisors
  • Limited advanced features for seasoned investors

5. Personal Capital

Personal Capital is known for its combination of financial planning tools and investment management services. While it is not purely a robo-advisor, it offers some great options for investors looking to build a personalized, tech-driven portfolio.

Key Features of Personal Capital’s Customizable Portfolios:

  • Financial Planning Integration: Personal Capital integrates investment management with comprehensive financial planning tools, making it ideal for those who want to monitor their entire financial picture.
  • Custom Asset Allocation: Personal Capital offers customizable asset allocation based on your goals and risk tolerance. The platform allows for highly personalized portfolio design.
  • Holistic Approach: In addition to offering personalized portfolios, Personal Capital’s wealth management service provides retirement planning, tax optimization, and other wealth-building strategies that can complement your customized portfolio.

Pros:

  • Free financial tracking tools
  • Robust investment advice and planning features
  • Customizable portfolios

Cons:

  • High management fees compared to other robo-advisors
  • Minimum investment is higher ($100,000)

Conclusion

The best robo-advisors for customizable portfolios cater to a wide range of needs, from those who want full control over their investments to those seeking automation combined with some flexibility. Whether you are interested in socially responsible investing, tax optimization, or just a simple, well-balanced portfolio, there is a platform out there for you. Betterment, Wealthfront, M1 Finance, SoFi Invest, and Personal Capital all offer great features for investors looking to personalize their portfolios.

Each platform has its own unique set of strengths, so the best choice will depend on your investment goals, risk tolerance, and whether you want a fully hands-off experience or a platform that allows you to engage more actively with your investments. By choosing the right robo-advisor, you can achieve your financial goals while benefiting from the efficiency and affordability of automated investing.
