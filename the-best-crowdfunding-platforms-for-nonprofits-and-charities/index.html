The Best Crowdfunding Platforms for Nonprofits and Charities


In today’s digital age, crowdfunding has emerged as a powerful tool for nonprofits and charities to raise funds, increase visibility, and build stronger communities. The ability to connect directly with donors worldwide, share compelling stories, and generate support without the constraints of traditional fundraising methods makes crowdfunding an attractive option for many organizations.

However, with a multitude of crowdfunding platforms available, choosing the right one can be challenging. Each platform offers unique features, fee structures, and audience reach, which can either amplify or hinder your nonprofit’s fundraising efforts. This comprehensive guide will dive deeply into the best crowdfunding platforms tailored for nonprofits and charitable causes, helping organizations pick the ideal match for their goals.

Why Crowdfunding is Vital for Nonprofits

Before diving into platform specifics, it’s valuable to understand why crowdfunding is particularly effective for the nonprofit sector:

  • Broader Reach: Traditional fundraising often relies on local or regional networks, but crowdfunding breaks geographical barriers. You can tap into global audiences interested in your cause.
  • Storytelling Power: Crowdfunding campaigns are built around emotional storytelling and transparency. Sharing stories of impact encourages donor engagement.
  • Quick Fundraising Cycles: Campaigns can run from days to a few months, making it possible to gather resources quickly for urgent needs or projects.
  • Community Engagement: Donors often become advocates for your cause, spreading awareness and possibly contributing recurring gifts.
  • Cost-Effective: Compared to hosting physical events or direct mail campaigns, crowdfunding platforms often involve lower upfront costs.

Given these advantages, selecting the best platform to maximize crowdfunding results is critical.

Key Features to Consider When Choosing a Crowdfunding Platform

Not all platforms are created equal, especially when catering to nonprofits. Here are essential features and criteria that nonprofits should factor into their decision-making:

  • Fee Structure: Understanding platform fees and payment processing fees can significantly impact the net funds raised. Some platforms specialize in nonprofit fundraising and minimize charges.
  • Ease of Use: Both the nonprofit team and the donors benefit from an intuitive interface, making campaign creation and contributions smoother.
  • Customization Options: Being able to personalize your campaign page to match branding, share videos, photos, and detailed stories enhances donor engagement.
  • Marketing Tools: Integrated email outreach, social sharing tools, and analytics empower nonprofits to promote campaigns effectively and monitor performance.
  • Donation Flexibility: Options for recurring donations, suggested amounts, and different currencies can expand donor appeal.
  • Integration Capabilities: Compatibility with your nonprofit’s CRM or email marketing tools can streamline follow-ups and donor management.
  • Trust & Security: Since donations involve financial transactions, trustworthy platforms with robust security measures are imperative.
  • Payout Timelines: The speed at which funds are disbursed to your nonprofit can affect cash flow, especially in urgent campaigns.

With these factors in mind, let’s explore the top crowdfunding platforms that excel for nonprofits and charities.

1. GoFundMe Charity (Now part of Classy)

GoFundMe Charity was a prominent platform tailored specifically for nonprofits but was integrated into Classy to provide enhanced functionality. Classy is now a leading destination for online fundraising with a nonprofit focus.

Why Classy is a Winner

  • Nonprofit-Focused Experience: Classy offers powerful tools for nonprofits including ticketed events, peer-to-peer fundraising, and recurring donation capabilities.
  • Branded Campaigns: High levels of customization allow nonprofits to tailor campaign pages with multimedia elements that resonate with donors.
  • Comprehensive Analytics: In-depth insights help monitor donor behavior, campaign performance, and help optimize strategies.
  • Integrations: Syncs seamlessly with popular nonprofit CRMs like Salesforce and Blackbaud, helping streamline donor management.
  • Mobile-Friendly: Responsive design and mobile apps ensure donors can give anytime from anywhere.
  • Support & Training: Classy offers excellent customer support and educational resources such as webinars and fundraising guides.

Pricing

Classy operates on a subscription model with different tiers depending on services required; transaction fees vary by payment method but tend to be competitive.

Ideal For

Established nonprofits seeking a versatile, professional platform with extensive fundraising tools beyond traditional crowdfunding.

2. GlobalGiving

GlobalGiving is a popular crowdfunding platform that connects grassroots nonprofits with donors worldwide. It’s known for transparency and the curation of vetted projects.

Why Choose GlobalGiving

  • Curated Projects Only: To join GlobalGiving, nonprofits undergo an application process ensuring trustworthy and high-impact projects.
  • Large International Network: Donors come from many countries, making it easier for nonprofits with international priorities to raise funds.
  • Training and Capacity Building: They provide workshops and resources to help nonprofits run successful campaigns and strengthen organizational skills.
  • Impact Reporting: Tools facilitate regular updates to donors about how funds are making a difference.
  • Corporate Partnerships: Opportunities to connect with corporate donors and foundations through the platform.

Fee Structure

GlobalGiving charges a 15% fee initially, which might seem high compared to others, but the large international reach and support services often justify this cost.

Ideal For

Organizations looking to raise funds for specific international projects and willing to engage in accountability and transparency processes.

3. Facebook Fundraisers

With billions of active users, Facebook Fundraisers has become a powerful tool for nonprofits to raise funds quickly, tapping into the social networks of volunteers, supporters, and staff.

Benefits of Facebook Fundraisers

  • Zero Platform Fees: Facebook covers the fundraising fees, so nonprofits receive 100% of donations.
  • Viral Potential: Campaigns easily spread through shares, comments, and reactions, amplifying donor reach.
  • Seamless Integration: Donors do not need to leave Facebook, simplifying the giving process.
  • Personal Fundraising: Supporters can create their own fundraisers on behalf of the nonprofit, expanding outreach.

Limitations

  • Limited customization and branding options compared to specialized platforms.
  • Fundraising caps and eligibility requirements may apply.

Ideal For

Nonprofits that want fast, fee-free fundraising leveraging social networks and casual small donations.

4. Mightycause

Mightycause is designed specifically for nonprofits and adaptable for various fundraising needs including crowdfunding, peer-to-peer, and event management.

Highlights of Mightycause

  • No Platform Fees for Nonprofits: Donations incur only standard payment processing fees.
  • Robust Fundraising Tools: Supports recurring gifts, tribute gifts, employer matching campaigns, and team challenges.
  • Campaign Customization: Flexible page design to enhance storytelling.
  • Real-Time Donation Tracking: Helps organizations stay engaged with supporters.
  • Grant Opportunities: Access to funding opportunities through the platform’s partnerships.

Who Should Use Mightycause

Small to mid-sized nonprofits that want fee-free fundraising and a comprehensive toolkit for various campaign types.

5. Donorbox

Donorbox is a simple yet powerful donation management platform that doubles as a crowdfunding tool, offering easy setup and integration.

Features to Know

  • Quick Setup: Create customizable donation forms and embed them on your nonprofit’s website or landing pages.
  • Multiple Payment Options: Accept credit cards, PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and bank transfers.
  • Recurring Donations: Encourage steady income through easy monthly giving.
  • Suggested Donation Tiers: Optimize giving levels for better donor participation.
  • Donor Management: Provides a CRM with donor profiles and email automation.

Pricing

Donorbox charges a 1.5% platform fee and payment processing fees, which is quite low compared to typical platforms.

Best For

Organizations that want to harness crowdfunding through their website with simple donation forms and retain control over campaign branding.

6. JustGiving

JustGiving is a veteran in online giving and particularly popular in the UK, but supports nonprofits worldwide.

What JustGiving Brings

  • Broad Donor Base: Large and active donating community.
  • Fundraiser Pages: Crowdfunding pages can be personalized and linked to social accounts.
  • Mobile Optimized: Allows donors to give quickly on phones or tablets.
  • Gift Aid Processing: UK charities benefit from auto Gift Aid claims.
  • Cause Pages: Showcase multiple projects and campaigns in a centralized profile.

Fees

Platform fees range around 5%, plus payment processing costs.

Ideal Use Cases

UK-based nonprofits focused on general fundraising can leverage this platform for everyday donations and major campaigns.

7. Indiegogo

Though primarily known for entrepreneurial and creative crowdfunding, Indiegogo also hosts numerous charitable campaigns with flexible funding options.

What Makes Indiegogo Stand Out

  • Flexible Funding: Choose to keep funds even if goal isn’t met.
  • Global Reach: Access worldwide donor networks.
  • Marketing Support: Tools to grow donor base during and after campaigns.
  • No Application Process: Open platform for all charity projects.

Fees

Indiegogo charges a 5% platform fee on funds raised, plus payment processing fees.

Who Should Consider Indiegogo

Smaller nonprofits or causes seeking a versatile, worldwide crowdfunding audience willing to drive their own marketing.

Proven Tips for Maximizing Success on Crowdfunding Platforms

Choosing the right site is just the start. Here’s how nonprofits can make the most of their campaigns:

  • Tell Authentic Stories: Share vivid, emotional stories demonstrating impact.
  • Set Realistic Goals: Ambitious yet attainable targets increase donor confidence.
  • Use Multimedia: Videos and photos engage many more donors than text alone.
  • Engage Early Supporters: Mobilize known supporters to jumpstart momentum.
  • Regular Updates: Keep everyone informed about progress and how funds are utilized.
  • Social Sharing Campaigns: Encourage donors to promote your page.
  • Express Gratitude: Thank donors promptly and personally.
  • Analyze and Adapt: Use platform analytics to refine messaging during the campaign.

Final Thoughts

Selecting the best crowdfunding platform for your nonprofit or charity is a strategic decision that hinges on your organization’s size, objectives, geographic focus, and fundraising style. Whether you need the global reach of GlobalGiving or the user-friendly social integration of Facebook Fundraisers, there’s a platform tailored to your needs.

Investing time to understand each platform’s strengths, fees, and features allows your nonprofit to harness the full power of crowdfunding and turn generosity into meaningful impact.

Nonprofits that couple the right platform choice with compelling storytelling and savvy promotion will unlock new avenues to fuel their missions and touch more lives than ever before. Crowdfunding is no longer just a fundraising trend—it’s a transformative way to connect changemakers with donors ready to make a difference.
