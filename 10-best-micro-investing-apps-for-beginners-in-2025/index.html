10 Best Micro-Investing Apps for Beginners in 2025


Investing is no longer reserved for the wealthy or the experts. With the rise of micro-investing apps, individuals with minimal experience or limited funds can now start building their financial futures. These apps make it easy for beginners to dip their toes into the world of investing with small amounts of money, often starting as low as a dollar. If you’re a beginner looking to explore the world of investing, you’ve come to the right place. In this post, we’ll walk you through the 10 best micro-investing apps in 2025 that are perfect for beginners.

1. Acorns

Acorns is one of the most popular micro-investing apps for beginners. It is designed to help users invest small amounts of money through a method called “round-ups.” This means that every time you make a purchase, Acorns rounds the transaction up to the nearest dollar and invests the difference. For example, if you buy a coffee for $2.50, Acorns will round it up to $3.00, investing the extra $0.50.

Acorns also offers a wide range of portfolio options tailored to different risk profiles, from conservative to aggressive. Additionally, the app provides educational resources to help you understand the basics of investing, which is ideal for beginners.

Key Features:

  • Automatic round-ups on everyday purchases
  • Diverse investment portfolios based on risk tolerance
  • Educational content for beginner investors
  • Easy-to-use interface

2. Stash

Stash is another great option for micro-investing, especially for beginners. With Stash, you can start investing with as little as $5. It offers a range of investment options, including stocks, bonds, and exchange-traded funds (ETFs). Stash also allows you to invest in themed portfolios based on your interests, such as sustainability or technology.

One of the standout features of Stash is its educational content. The app offers personalized guidance and explanations to help users understand their investments and grow their financial knowledge. Additionally, Stash offers automated investing features and goal-based investing, making it an excellent choice for those new to the investment world.

Key Features:

  • Start investing with just $5
  • Personalized portfolio options
  • Automated investing and goal-setting
  • Educational resources tailored for beginners

3. Robinhood

Robinhood has become a household name in the world of investing, particularly among younger generations. Known for its user-friendly interface and commission-free trades, Robinhood allows you to invest in stocks, options, ETFs, and even cryptocurrencies. Although Robinhood is more commonly associated with traditional stock trading, it also offers a micro-investing feature called “Fractional Shares.”

Fractional shares allow you to buy a portion of expensive stocks, meaning you don’t need a lot of money to get started. This is an ideal feature for beginners who want to diversify their portfolios without committing large sums of money. With Robinhood, you can start investing with as little as $1.

Key Features:

  • Commission-free trading
  • Fractional shares for easy portfolio diversification
  • Access to stocks, ETFs, and cryptocurrencies
  • Intuitive and beginner-friendly app

4. Betterment

Betterment is a robo-advisor that is designed for individuals who want a hands-off investing experience. While the platform is not strictly for micro-investing, it is still an excellent choice for beginners who want to start investing small amounts of money. Betterment creates a diversified portfolio of ETFs based on your risk tolerance and financial goals.

The app’s key selling point is its automated approach. Betterment manages your investments for you, making it an ideal option for beginners who don’t want to spend a lot of time managing their investments. It also offers personalized financial advice, which is perfect for those who are new to investing and want guidance on how to grow their money.

Key Features:

  • Automated portfolio management
  • Personalized financial advice
  • Tax-efficient investing strategies
  • Low minimum investment requirement

5. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is another robo-advisor that makes investing easy for beginners. Similar to Betterment, Wealthfront creates and manages a diversified portfolio of ETFs based on your financial goals and risk tolerance. One of the standout features of Wealthfront is its tax-loss harvesting, which can help you minimize your tax burden.

Wealthfront also offers financial planning tools, such as retirement planning and college savings calculators. These tools help you plan your financial future while investing small amounts of money. With a low minimum investment of just $500, Wealthfront is an affordable way for beginners to get started with micro-investing.

Key Features:

  • Automated portfolio management
  • Tax-loss harvesting for tax efficiency
  • Financial planning tools
  • Low minimum investment requirement

6. Public.com

Public.com is a social investing platform that combines investing with social networking. It allows users to invest in stocks and ETFs, while also giving them the ability to follow other investors and see what they’re investing in. Public.com also offers fractional shares, which means you can buy a portion of high-priced stocks for as little as $1.

What sets Public.com apart is its social aspect. You can engage with other investors, share insights, and learn from the community. This makes it an excellent choice for beginners who want to learn from others and get real-time feedback on their investment strategies.

Key Features:

  • Commission-free trades
  • Fractional shares for small investments
  • Social investing features to learn from others
  • User-friendly app with a social networking component

7. SoFi Invest

SoFi Invest is a comprehensive investment platform that offers both automated investing and active investing options. For beginners, the app provides a “SoFi Automated Investing” feature that builds a diversified portfolio of ETFs based on your goals and risk tolerance. The platform has no management fees, which is a big plus for those who are just starting out with small investments.

SoFi also offers active investing, where you can buy and sell stocks, ETFs, and cryptocurrencies. SoFi Invest is a great choice for beginners who want to explore both passive and active investing strategies.

Key Features:

  • Automated portfolio management with no fees
  • Active investing in stocks, ETFs, and cryptocurrencies
  • Goal-based investing with personalized guidance
  • Free financial planning tools

8. Qapital

Qapital is a micro-investing app that focuses on saving and investing in a way that feels natural. The app allows you to set savings goals, such as building an emergency fund or saving for a vacation. It then automatically transfers small amounts of money into your investment account, helping you grow your savings over time.

One of the unique features of Qapital is its ability to combine savings with investing. You can choose from a variety of investment portfolios, including socially responsible options, and watch your money grow with minimal effort. Qapital is a great choice for beginners who want to save for specific goals while also dipping their toes into investing.

Key Features:

  • Goal-based saving and investing
  • Automatic transfers to your investment account
  • Socially responsible investment options
  • Easy-to-use app with a focus on saving

9. M1 Finance

M1 Finance is a versatile platform that allows you to invest in stocks, ETFs, and custom portfolios. The app offers fractional shares, so you can invest with as little as $1. One of the standout features of M1 Finance is its “Pie” system, which lets you create a personalized portfolio by selecting individual stocks and ETFs.

M1 Finance also offers automated investing features, so your portfolio is automatically rebalanced to stay aligned with your financial goals. With no fees for automated investing, M1 Finance is a great option for beginners who want to have more control over their investments while still benefiting from automation.

Key Features:

  • Customizable portfolios with fractional shares
  • Automated portfolio rebalancing
  • Low-fee structure for micro-investing
  • Hands-on control over your investments

10. Invstr

Invstr is a unique micro-investing app that combines investing with education and gamification. The app allows users to start investing with as little as $1, and it offers a wide range of investment options, including stocks, ETFs, and cryptocurrencies. What sets Invstr apart is its focus on helping users learn as they invest.

Invstr provides a virtual portfolio where you can practice investing with real-time data, allowing you to hone your skills before investing actual money. Additionally, Invstr has a social component where you can follow other investors and share insights. This makes it an excellent choice for beginners who want to learn while investing.

Key Features:

  • Start investing with just $1
  • Virtual portfolio for practice investing
  • Access to stocks, ETFs, and cryptocurrencies
  • Social features to learn from other investors

Conclusion

The world of investing is now more accessible than ever before, thanks to the rise of micro-investing apps. Whether you’re looking to invest spare change, learn about the stock market, or grow your wealth over time, there is a micro-investing app that suits your needs. The apps listed above are some of the best options for beginners in 2025, offering ease of use, educational resources, and the ability to start investing with minimal capital. So, take the plunge, start small, and let these apps guide you on your investment journey!
