Hedging Against Inflation in Actively Managed Funds: A Guide for Actively Managed Fund Investors


Inflation is an ever-present concern for investors, as it erodes the purchasing power of their investment returns over time. This is especially true for investors in actively managed funds, who seek to outperform the market through the expertise and skill of fund managers. In this guide, we’ll explore the strategies that actively managed fund investors can employ to hedge against inflation and protect their investments.

Understanding Inflation

Before delving into hedging strategies, it’s important to have a clear understanding of inflation. Inflation refers to the general increase in prices of goods and services over time, resulting in a decrease in the purchasing power of money. When inflation rises, the value of a dollar decreases, and investors need to ensure that their investments grow at a rate that outpaces inflation.

Actively Managed Funds: A Brief Overview

Actively managed funds are investment vehicles where fund managers make decisions to buy and sell securities based on their market expertise. These funds aim to outperform the market by actively selecting investments that they believe will generate superior returns. However, actively managed funds also come with higher fees compared to passively managed funds, which track a specific market index.

Hedging Strategies for Actively Managed Fund Investors

  1. Inflation-Protected Securities: Actively managed funds can allocate a portion of their portfolio to inflation-protected securities, such as Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS). These securities are designed to provide investors with a return that outpaces inflation, as their principal value is adjusted based on changes in the Consumer Price Index (CPI). By including TIPS in their portfolio, investors can mitigate the impact of rising inflation on their overall returns.

  2. Commodities: Commodities, such as gold or oil, have historically served as a hedge against inflation. Actively managed funds can invest in commodities through futures contracts or exchange-traded funds (ETFs) that track commodity prices. When inflation rises, commodity prices tend to increase, thus providing investors with a valuable asset that retains its value in times of inflationary pressure.

  3. Real Estate: Real estate investments can act as a hedge against inflation, as property values tend to rise with increasing prices. Actively managed funds can allocate a portion of their portfolio to real estate investment trusts (REITs) or real estate-focused funds, which allow investors to indirectly invest in a diversified portfolio of properties. By including real estate in their investment mix, fund managers can potentially protect against the erosive effects of inflation.

  4. Diversification: Diversification is a fundamental strategy that can help actively managed fund investors hedge against inflation. By diversifying their portfolio across different asset classes, sectors, and geographies, investors can reduce the risk of their investments being adversely affected by inflation. Diversification allows investors to spread their risk and potentially benefit from the performance of various asset classes that may react differently to inflationary pressures.

Conclusion

Hedging against inflation is a crucial consideration for actively managed fund investors. By incorporating strategies such as investing in inflation-protected securities, commodities, real estate, and diversifying their portfolios, investors can mitigate the impact of inflation on their investment returns. It’s important for investors to consult with their financial advisors and carefully consider their investment goals, time horizon, and risk tolerance before implementing any hedging strategies. By taking a proactive approach to hedging against inflation, actively managed fund investors can enhance the long-term sustainability of their investment portfolios.
