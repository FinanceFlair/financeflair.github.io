Top Trend Following Books Every Trader Should Read


Trend following is a popular trading strategy that focuses on identifying and capitalizing on market momentum. Rather than predicting reversals or trying to time the market, trend followers seek to ride the waves of existing trends, buying assets that are rising and selling those that are falling. Although seemingly straightforward, successful trend following requires discipline, a sound understanding of market behavior, and a carefully formulated approach. One of the best ways to grasp the nuances behind this strategy is by diving into the literature written by successful traders and analysts who have mastered the art.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore some of the most influential and insightful trend following books that every trader, whether beginner or seasoned, should consider adding to their reading list. These selected works not only shed light on the mechanics of trend following but also delve into behavioral finance, risk management, and practical systems, providing a well-rounded knowledge base.

Understanding Trend Following: Why Books Matter

Why bother reading books on trend following when so many articles and videos are available online? Books offer depth and structured insights that quick tutorials typically lack. Many top trend followers have written detailed accounts of their methods, experiences, and philosophies, which allow readers to understand the underlying principles rather than just the surface concepts. Additionally, these books often include tested strategies, real-world examples, psychological advice, and risk controls that are critical to success.

1. Trend Following: How to Make a Fortune in Bull, Bear, and Black Swan Markets by Michael W. Covel

Often considered the definitive modern guide on the subject, Michael W. Covel’s Trend Following is a must-read for those seriously interested in the strategy. This book goes beyond the “how-to” and explores the mindset of trend followers, illustrating how they handle volatility, losses, and uncertainty without succumbing to fear or greed.

Covel features interviews and stories from many top trend followers, providing a first-hand glimpse into their methods. The book emphasizes that trend following is not about predicting the market or timing it perfectly but about establishing rules and sticking to them. It also introduces key concepts such as moving averages, breakouts, and the importance of risk management.

Key takeaways from this book include:

  • The power of letting profits run and cutting losses quickly.
  • How trend following can work in any market, whether bullish or bearish.
  • The psychological resilience required to succeed.

2. Following the Trend: Diversified Managed Futures Trading by Andreas F. Clenow

For traders who appreciate a data-driven, systematic approach, Andreas Clenow’s Following the Trend is a standout work. The book is packed with clear charts, numerical examples, and practical insights into building a trend following strategy based on futures markets. Clenow breaks down the mathematics behind moving average crossovers and trend filters, illustrating the significance of position sizing and risk controls.

What makes this book particularly valuable is its transparency: Clenow openly shares the performance of his trend following models and explains the logic behind decision-making processes. This level of disclosure is rare in trading books and offers budding traders realistic expectations.

Highlights:

  • Building and testing quant-based trend following models.
  • Diversification benefits through multiple markets.
  • Practical insights on drawdowns and risk-adjusted returns.

3. The Complete TurtleTrader: How 23 Novice Investors Became Overnight Millionaires by Michael W. Covel

Another work by Michael Covel, this book tells the fascinating story of “The Turtles,” a group of novice traders trained by Richard Dennis, a legendary commodities trader, in the 1980s. Dennis believed that anyone could be taught to trade profitably using trend-following rules, and the Turtle experiment proved him right.

This book blends biography and trading strategy, offering a step-by-step look at the simple but effective rules these traders followed. It’s highly motivational for traders who may question whether trend following suits them, emphasizing that discipline, risk management, and sticking to a system can trump complex prediction tactics.

What readers gain:

  • A detailed recap of Turtle trading rules.
  • Insights into psychological challenges and successes.
  • An inspiring blueprint for systematic trading discipline.

4. Trading Systems and Methods by Perry J. Kaufman

Although this is a more general trading systems book, Perry Kaufman provides a significant section on trend following strategies within a broader context. This exhaustive text covers many technical indicators, key signal generation methods, and evaluation metrics that every trend follower should understand.

The strength of Kaufman’s work lies in its comprehensive approach to trading system design, offering extensive discussions on risk, money management, algorithmic application, and system testing. His sober treatment of trend following enhances the trader’s toolkit and encourages systematic rigor.

Important lessons include:

  • Differentiating noise and genuine trend signals.
  • Comprehensive risk/reward evaluation.
  • Combining trend following with other strategies for robustness.

5. The Little Book of Trading: Trend Following Strategy for Big Winnings by Michael W. Covel

Another accessible and concise read from Covel, this book distills the core ideas of trend following into a digestible format perfect for traders short on time but hungry for knowledge. It emphasizes the notion that trend following doesn’t require predicting the future, just reacting to price trends logically and unemotionally.

Though shorter than his comprehensive works, this book succeeds in removing common misconceptions and explaining why trend followers benefit during tumultuous market periods when others panic.

Readers will find:

  • Basic principles of trend identification.
  • How to manage risk and avoid emotional pitfalls.
  • Examples demonstrating trend following’s potential in various market conditions.

6. Market Wizards Series by Jack D. Schwager

While not exclusively about trend following, many traders featured in Jack Schwager’s Market Wizards series have employed trend following strategies or variants thereof. These interviews provide invaluable insights into the routines, mindsets, and methodologies of some of the world’s most successful traders.

The books teach that trend following isn’t a rigid system but can be adapted to individual personality and market conditions. The psychological preparation highlighted across interviews is especially valuable, illustrating how handling losing streaks and market noise separates winners from losers.

Themes explored:

  • Trade management during persistent trends.
  • Approaches to dealing with inevitable drawdowns.
  • The versatility of trend following strategies.

7. Beat the Market: Invest by Evaluating Probability by Edward O. Thorp

Although more focused on probability and quantitative investing, Thorp’s work is influential in the world of systematic trading. He pioneered techniques in risk management and edge-seeking that are integral to effective trend following. His insights into betting on probability rather than speculation mirror the scientific mindset required to follow trends profitably.

Reading Thorp helps traders understand:

  • The mathematics behind consistent profits.
  • How to evaluate risk-adjusted returns.
  • The importance of testing and validating trading systems.

8. Algorithmic Trading and DMA: An introduction to direct access trading strategies by Barry Johnson

For those interested in how trend following strategies have evolved in the era of algorithms, Barry Johnson’s book offers an introduction to direct market access and automated systems. It covers the technological frameworks and execution tactics that modern trend followers implement, including order types, trading platforms, and latency considerations.

This book is essential for traders who want to take trend following beyond manual or simple rule-based systems into automated, high-frequency applications.

Key learning points:

  • Building and deploying algorithmic trading strategies.
  • Managing execution risk in trend following.
  • Applying DMA for improved entry and exit timing.

Putting It All Together: Building Your Trend Following Library

With countless resources available, knowing which ones provide depth and practical utility can transform your learning curve. The books above cumulatively offer a blueprint: understanding the philosophy and psychology behind trend following, learning specific systems and models, exploring risk and money management, and adapting to modern trading technologies.

Start with Michael Covel’s foundational works to grasp the core of trend following philosophy, complement with Andreas Clenow’s practical quant insights, then explore the broader contexts through Schwager’s interviews and Kaufman’s system design. Finally, enhance your understanding of risk and automation with Thorp and Johnson.

Reading these books is more than an academic exercise—it’s a way to internalize the principles that can shape your trading discipline, strategy selection, and ultimately, your success in capturing the powerful momentum that markets offer.

Final Thoughts

Trend following is attractive because it works across asset classes and market cycles, but its simplicity can be deceptive. Achieving lasting profitability demands learning from those who have navigated the challenges of markets over time. The literature cited here is a starting point to not only understand how to follow trends but also how to cultivate the patience and resilience necessary to thrive.

Whether your ambition is to trade futures, stocks, commodities, or currencies, immersing yourself in these books will provide actionable insights, time-tested strategies, and the mindset needed to succeed.

Dive in, stay curious, and embrace the learning journey—because in the art of trend following, knowledge is truly power.

Happy trading!
