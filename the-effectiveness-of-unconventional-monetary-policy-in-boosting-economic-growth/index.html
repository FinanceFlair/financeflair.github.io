The Effectiveness of Unconventional Monetary Policy in Boosting Economic Growth


Economic growth is the milestone for any nation looking to secure and improve living standards for its citizens. Over the years, policymakers have experimented with numerous tools, techniques, and policies to stimulate growth in their economies. Among these methods, conventional monetary policy, such as adjusting interest rates or setting reserve requirements, has been the standard approach in the management of a nation’s economy. Yet, there is a growing interest in the effectiveness of unconventional monetary policy alternatives.

An unconventional monetary policy deviates from traditional policy frameworks to either directly inject money into the economy or seek unconventional ways of lowering borrowing costs. Its appeal lies in its potential to breathe life back into an economy weighed down by severe downturns or persistent stagnation. Can these unorthodox tactics truly transform the trajectory of economic growth?

To answer this question, let us delve into the innovative implementations of unconventional monetary policy methods that have occurred throughout history. We’ll start our journey in Japan, where the Bank of Japan –in the aftermath of the Great Recession– launched quantitative and qualitative monetary easing (QQE) policies in the early 2000s. This initiative involved vast purchases of Japanese government bonds with the aim of expanding the money supply and thereby lowering borrowing costs for Japanese companies.

The QQE strategy resulted in inflation being driven up amid a favorable economic turn-around. By supplying liquidity, the Bank of Japan made it easier for companies to secure funding, which buoyed investment and spending. Consequently, the country’s economy experienced a period of steady growth and recovery, stabilizing the economy’s vulnerability to deflationary pressures. The same principle applies elsewhere; unconventional monetary policy helped steer several economies, such as the United Kingdom and the United States, through the global financial crisis.

Another form of unconventional monetary policy worth examining is forward guidance. This technique, in various forms, has been employed by central banks in countries like the United States and New Zealand. Essentially, forward guidance is the communication strategy of central banks, wherein they forecast future monetary policy movements. It aims to boost economic activity by providing banks and businesses with information about the future and inducing greater certainty. The idea of forward guidance is that by being transparent about expected future actions, the central bank can influence people’s behavior today. By easing market expectations around rates, forward guidance may instill confidence and stimulate borrowing and investment today.

There are certainly criticisms and question marks that hang over the effectiveness of unconventional monetary policy. A commonly voiced concern is that these policies are seen as “experimental.” The non-conventional nature of these methods implies that their long-term effects and impacts remain uncertain. Moreover, their effectiveness may be limited when countries face problems rooted in deep-seated structural defects or supply-side constraints rather than demand-side problems.

Another potential problem lies in the detrimental impact that low interest rates can have on savers, who may lose out to the benefits of increased investment. Low interest rates warp the incentives between spending and saving, potentially leading to a range of financial discipline problems, especially amongst the elderly.

Furthermore, inflation targets and the neutrality of conventional monetary policy underpin much of the conventional wisdom in macroeconomics. Departing from these benchmarks requires policymakers to revisit established norms and potentially reset their operational frameworks. There is a risk of deviating too far from the core goals of monetary policy, which is to keep inflation stable and contribute to economic wellbeing.

That being said, the effectiveness of unconventional monetary policy in driving economic growth is not something that should be dismissed out of hand. Categorically, it has helped unprecedented times, resuscitating economies in dire need of revitalization. The important takeaway for policymakers lies in the creative use of these tools, applying them with wisdom and acumen to maximize their favorable effects while coming to grips with their limitations as well.

So, has unconventional monetary policy fulfilled the expectations that many hold for it? In many cases, the jury is still out. As these unorthodox ideas make their way into mainstream policy discussions, continual reassessment and scrutiny will be crucial. Not by keeping our monetary strategies frozen in time, but by breathing life into the stalest of economic doctrines and keeping an open mind to their incredible potential. In the end, a judicious use of unconventional monetary policy, combined with well-designed fiscal policies, can bring about an era of robust economic growth, and a sense of security for generations to come.
