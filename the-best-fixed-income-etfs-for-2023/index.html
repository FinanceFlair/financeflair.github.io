The Best Fixed Income ETFs for 2023


Fixed income investing has always been a reliable method for generating stable returns, especially for conservative investors. With the volatility in the equity markets, many are turning to fixed income exchange-traded funds (ETFs) as a way to diversify their portfolios while still achieving solid returns. Fixed income ETFs provide a convenient and cost-effective method for gaining exposure to bonds and other debt instruments, offering investors an opportunity to invest in bonds without having to directly buy individual securities.

In this comprehensive guide, we will explore some of the best fixed income ETFs for 2023. We will dive into their features, investment strategies, performance, and the benefits they offer. Whether you are a seasoned investor or a newcomer to the world of ETFs, understanding these options can help you make informed decisions about where to allocate your capital.

What Are Fixed Income ETFs?

Before we explore the best options available, it’s essential to understand what fixed income ETFs are. A fixed income ETF is a type of exchange-traded fund that primarily invests in bonds or other fixed income securities. These ETFs are designed to provide investors with regular income through interest payments and potential capital appreciation.

The most common types of bonds included in these ETFs are U.S. Treasury bonds, municipal bonds, corporate bonds, and international bonds. Fixed income ETFs can be an attractive option for investors seeking lower risk, more stability, and income generation, especially in uncertain market conditions.

Fixed income ETFs offer numerous benefits compared to directly buying individual bonds:

  • Diversification: Fixed income ETFs invest in a basket of bonds, which helps to reduce individual security risk.
  • Liquidity: As exchange-traded funds, these securities can be bought and sold throughout the trading day, providing greater liquidity than individual bonds.
  • Low Expense Ratios: Most fixed income ETFs come with lower fees compared to actively managed bond funds.
  • Convenience: Buying a fixed income ETF is as simple as purchasing stocks, and it provides immediate exposure to a broad range of bonds.

Now, let’s take a closer look at some of the top fixed income ETFs to consider in 2023.

1. iShares iBoxx $ Investment Grade Corporate Bond ETF (LQD)

The iShares iBoxx $ Investment Grade Corporate Bond ETF (LQD) is one of the most popular fixed income ETFs for investors looking to gain exposure to investment-grade corporate bonds. This ETF tracks the performance of the Markit iBoxx USD Liquid Investment Grade Index, which includes bonds from large and medium-sized U.S. corporations with solid credit ratings.

Key Features:

  • Dividend Yield: Approximately 3.3%
  • Expense Ratio: 0.14%
  • Top Holdings: The top holdings in the LQD ETF include bonds from companies like Apple, Microsoft, and Johnson & Johnson, offering strong credit quality.
  • Average Duration: Around 8 years

Why LQD is a Top Pick:

LQD provides exposure to investment-grade corporate bonds, which are relatively safer than high-yield bonds. The low expense ratio, combined with the diverse pool of high-quality issuers, makes it an ideal choice for investors seeking stability and income.

This ETF is well-suited for investors who want to balance the pursuit of higher yields with safety. With an attractive dividend yield, LQD has become a cornerstone for fixed income portfolios.

2. Vanguard Total Bond Market ETF (BND)

The Vanguard Total Bond Market ETF (BND) is another excellent choice for fixed income investors, particularly those looking for broad diversification. This ETF tracks the performance of the Bloomberg U.S. Aggregate Float Adjusted Bond Index, which represents a wide range of U.S. investment-grade bonds.

Key Features:

  • Dividend Yield: Approximately 2.5%
  • Expense Ratio: 0.03%
  • Top Holdings: BND’s portfolio includes U.S. Treasury securities, corporate bonds, and mortgage-backed securities, all weighted based on their market value.
  • Average Duration: Around 6.5 years

Why BND is a Top Pick:

BND offers exposure to a diversified mix of bonds, including government, corporate, and mortgage-backed securities. The ultra-low expense ratio is a major selling point, making it an affordable option for cost-conscious investors. The fund provides a well-rounded exposure to the bond market, making it suitable for those who seek low-risk investments with stable returns.

3. iShares U.S. Treasury Bond ETF (GOVT)

The iShares U.S. Treasury Bond ETF (GOVT) is ideal for conservative investors who prefer the safety and stability of U.S. government bonds. This ETF invests in a broad array of U.S. Treasury securities with varying maturities, ranging from short-term to long-term bonds.

Key Features:

  • Dividend Yield: Approximately 1.6%
  • Expense Ratio: 0.05%
  • Top Holdings: GOVT’s top holdings consist entirely of U.S. Treasury bonds, including bills, notes, and bonds across a variety of maturities.
  • Average Duration: Around 6 years

Why GOVT is a Top Pick:

GOVT offers the safety and security of U.S. Treasuries, which are backed by the U.S. government’s full faith and credit. The ETF’s low expense ratio makes it a cost-effective way to gain exposure to U.S. government debt. Given that U.S. Treasuries are considered one of the safest investments, GOVT is ideal for conservative investors seeking protection in times of market uncertainty.

4. Schwab U.S. TIPS ETF (SCHP)

The Schwab U.S. TIPS ETF (SCHP) is an ETF that focuses on Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS), which are bonds specifically designed to protect against inflation. TIPS offer a fixed interest rate that is adjusted for inflation, making them an attractive option for investors concerned about rising prices.

Key Features:

  • Dividend Yield: Approximately 1.2%
  • Expense Ratio: 0.05%
  • Top Holdings: SCHP invests exclusively in U.S. TIPS, which are government-issued bonds with inflation protection.
  • Average Duration: Around 7 years

Why SCHP is a Top Pick:

Inflation can erode the purchasing power of fixed income investments, making TIPS a great hedge. The Schwab U.S. TIPS ETF provides investors with a simple and low-cost way to protect against inflation while generating income. The combination of inflation protection and low expense ratio makes SCHP a great choice for conservative investors looking to safeguard their portfolios.

5. SPDR Bloomberg Barclays High Yield Bond ETF (JNK)

For investors who are willing to take on more risk in exchange for potentially higher returns, the SPDR Bloomberg Barclays High Yield Bond ETF (JNK) is an appealing choice. This ETF invests in high-yield corporate bonds, also known as junk bonds, which offer higher yields but come with more risk compared to investment-grade bonds.

Key Features:

  • Dividend Yield: Approximately 5.5%
  • Expense Ratio: 0.40%
  • Top Holdings: JNK’s portfolio includes bonds from companies such as Ford Motor Company, Sprint, and other high-risk, high-return issuers.
  • Average Duration: Around 4 years

Why JNK is a Top Pick:

JNK offers a higher yield than most investment-grade bond ETFs, making it a compelling choice for those seeking more income. However, investors should be aware that high-yield bonds come with more credit risk. JNK’s higher yield is attractive to investors who are comfortable with a higher level of risk in exchange for the potential for more substantial returns.

6. iShares iBoxx $ High Yield Corporate Bond ETF (HYG)

The iShares iBoxx $ High Yield Corporate Bond ETF (HYG) is another high-yield bond ETF that focuses on providing investors with exposure to lower-rated corporate bonds. HYG tracks the performance of the Markit iBoxx USD Liquid High Yield Index, which includes bonds issued by companies with lower credit ratings.

Key Features:

  • Dividend Yield: Approximately 5.3%
  • Expense Ratio: 0.49%
  • Top Holdings: The ETF’s holdings include bonds from companies such as T-Mobile, Sprint, and other corporations with high yields but lower credit quality.
  • Average Duration: Around 4 years

Why HYG is a Top Pick:

HYG is designed for investors seeking higher income, but it comes with higher risk due to the inclusion of junk bonds. For those willing to take on additional risk, HYG can provide substantial returns. The ETF’s diversification across various industries helps mitigate some of the risks, but it remains more volatile compared to investment-grade bond ETFs.

Conclusion: Choosing the Right Fixed Income ETF for 2023

Fixed income ETFs are a great way to add stability, diversification, and income to your investment portfolio. The best ETF for you will depend on your risk tolerance, income needs, and market outlook. If you’re looking for safety, U.S. Treasury bonds or TIPS ETFs like GOVT or SCHP might be the right choice. For those seeking higher yields, high-yield bond ETFs such as JNK or HYG offer attractive options, albeit with more risk.

When selecting a fixed income ETF, it’s essential to consider factors such as expense ratios, dividend yields, and the type of bonds the ETF holds. Additionally, understanding the macroeconomic environment and interest rate trends can help you make a more informed decision about which fixed income ETF aligns with your financial goals.

As 2023 unfolds, these ETFs will continue to be popular choices for income-focused investors looking for stability amid market uncertainty. Whether you’re looking to balance your portfolio or enhance your income stream, these fixed income ETFs can help you navigate the evolving financial landscape.
