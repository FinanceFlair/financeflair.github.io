Beyond the Basics: Advanced Strategies for Ethical Investment


As an investor, you’re likely familiar with the basic investment strategies like diversifying your portfolio, balancing risks, and researching companies. However, to build a more sustainable and responsible investment portfolio, you need to go beyond these basics. Advanced strategies for ethical investment can not only provide you with better returns but also contribute to making a positive impact on society and the environment.

Understand Sustainable Investing

Sustainable investing or responsible investment is the practice of incorporating environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors into your investment decisions. ESG factors assess how a company performs in areas like climate change, labor practices, and corporate governance. Sustainable investing helps investors align their values with their investment goals while potentially securing higher returns.

Engage with Companies

Engaging with companies involves communicating with business leaders to address ESG concerns and fostering a collaborative partnership. This approach can be more effective than just screening out companies that don’t meet specific ESG criteria. Two common methods of engagement are:

  • Sharholder Engagement: This approach involves asking companies to disclose their ESG policies and practices and questioning their management on ESG issues. As a shareholder, you have the right to request information and discuss concerns directly with company management.

  • Collaborative Approaches: These approaches involve working with other like-minded investors or organizations to influence companies’ ESG practices. For instance, the United Nations-supported Principles for Responsible Investment (PRI) brings together investors to align their investments with societal goals.

Thematic Investing

Thematic investing focuses on investing in themes that tackle global challenges like climate change, healthcare, and technology. Thematic investing aligns your investments with your values while benefiting from the growth potential of those industries. Some examples of thematic investments are:

  • Climate and Renewable Energy: Investing in companies that develop sustainable energy sources, energy-efficient technology, and renewable energy infrastructure can provide long-term returns while contributing to reducing carbon emissions.

  • Healthcare and Aging Population: With the global population aging, healthcare remains a growing industry with significant investment opportunities. Investing in companies that provide healthcare facilities, medical equipment, or pharmaceuticals can yield both returns and a positive impact.

  • Technology and Innovation: Investing in companies at the forefront of technological advancements, such as artificial intelligence, robotics, and the Internet of Things, can capitalize on the rapid growth in these sectors.

Private Investments and Impact Investing

Private investments, such as private equity, angel investing, or venture capital, offer a more hands-on approach to investing. By investing in private companies or funds, you can directly influence the business’s ESG practices and contribute to creating positive social and environmental impact. Impact investing focuses on achieving both a financial return and a measurable social or environmental impact.

Private investments can be illiquid and carry higher risks than publicly traded stocks. Therefore, it’s essential to research and diversify your private investments and work with experienced advisors to navigate the risks.

To create an advanced and responsible investment portfolio, it’s necessary to recognize the limitations of traditional investment strategies. By employing advanced strategies like engaging with companies, thematic investing, and private investments, you can align your investment goals with your values and contribute to a better world. Beyond the basics of investing, these advanced strategies can lead to potentially higher returns and a positive impact on society and the environment. Remember to consult with financial advisors to determine the best approach for your individual needs and risk tolerance.
