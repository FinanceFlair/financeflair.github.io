Understanding the Fees and Costs of Crowdfunding Platforms


Crowdfunding has revolutionized the way entrepreneurs, creators, and innovators raise capital for their ideas. Whether you’re an aspiring business owner seeking seed funding or a creative individual launching a project, crowdfunding platforms provide a unique opportunity to connect with backers who believe in your vision. However, while crowdfunding platforms offer numerous benefits, one important factor to consider is the cost associated with using them.

In this guide, we’ll dive into the various fees and costs involved in using crowdfunding platforms, so you can make an informed decision when choosing the right platform for your campaign.

What Are Crowdfunding Platforms?

Crowdfunding platforms are online platforms that allow individuals, businesses, and organizations to raise funds from a large number of people, typically via small contributions. These platforms can cater to various types of crowdfunding models, such as donation-based crowdfunding, reward-based crowdfunding, equity crowdfunding, and debt crowdfunding. Each model has its unique characteristics and goals, but they all share a common feature: leveraging the power of a crowd to fund a cause or project.

Popular crowdfunding platforms include Kickstarter, Indiegogo, GoFundMe, and Patreon, among others. These platforms connect creators with potential backers, making it easier for people to fund ideas they believe in. However, before you launch a campaign, it’s crucial to understand the costs involved.

Types of Fees Associated with Crowdfunding Platforms

When you’re exploring crowdfunding platforms, you’ll encounter several types of fees. These fees vary by platform and can significantly affect the amount of money you ultimately receive. Here are the primary fees to keep in mind:

1. Platform Fees

Most crowdfunding platforms charge a percentage of the total funds raised. This is typically referred to as the “platform fee” or “service fee.” These fees can range from 3% to 5%, depending on the platform and the type of crowdfunding model you choose.

  • Kickstarter: Kickstarter charges a 5% fee on successfully funded projects. If your campaign is unsuccessful, no fees are applied.
  • Indiegogo: Indiegogo’s platform fee is also 5%, but they have an added distinction. For flexible funding campaigns (where you can keep the funds even if the campaign doesn’t meet its goal), Indiegogo charges an additional 2% fee on the funds raised.
  • GoFundMe: GoFundMe doesn’t charge a platform fee for personal campaigns, though they do charge a payment processing fee (more on that below). For charity campaigns, they may charge a small fee to cover operational costs.

2. Payment Processing Fees

In addition to the platform fees, crowdfunding platforms charge payment processing fees. These fees are levied by the third-party payment processors (such as Stripe or PayPal) that handle the transactions. The processing fee usually covers the cost of securely transferring funds from backers to the campaign organizers.

Payment processing fees typically range from 2.9% to 3.5% plus a fixed fee per transaction (usually around $0.30). This fee is applicable to each donation or backer contribution, and it’s deducted from the funds raised before they are transferred to your account.

3. Currency Conversion Fees

If your crowdfunding campaign attracts international backers, you may encounter currency conversion fees. These fees are charged when contributions are made in a currency different from the platform’s primary operating currency.

For example, if your campaign is based in the United States but backers from Europe contribute in euros, the platform or payment processor may charge a currency conversion fee. This fee can range from 1% to 3%, depending on the platform and the currency involved.

4. Additional Fees for Extra Services

Some crowdfunding platforms offer additional services or premium features that can help enhance your campaign’s visibility or provide you with extra tools. These services can come at an extra cost. Examples of additional fees include:

  • Advertising or marketing support: Some platforms offer paid services that promote your campaign to a wider audience. These services can include featured placements on the platform, social media campaigns, email marketing, or PR outreach. Prices for these services can vary widely based on the scope and reach.

  • Customizable campaign pages: Platforms like Kickstarter and Indiegogo allow campaigners to customize their campaign pages, but some advanced design features may come at an additional cost.

  • Analytics and reporting: Some platforms offer in-depth analytics and reports that help you track your campaign’s performance. These tools can be extremely valuable for understanding your backers and adjusting your campaign strategy, but they may come at an added expense.

5. Shipping Costs for Physical Rewards

For reward-based crowdfunding campaigns, it’s essential to consider the shipping costs of delivering physical rewards to your backers. While the platform doesn’t directly charge for shipping, it’s still a significant part of your campaign budget. Shipping fees can quickly add up, especially if you have international backers.

You may need to factor in packaging, postage, and any associated taxes or customs duties. Make sure you calculate these expenses carefully and consider whether you will include shipping costs in your reward tiers or charge backers separately for shipping.

6. Campaign Success Fees (For Equity Crowdfunding)

If you’re using an equity crowdfunding platform, be aware that some platforms charge success fees based on the funds raised. These fees can range from 5% to 10% of the total capital raised. The success fee is only applicable if your campaign meets or exceeds its fundraising goal.

Platforms like SeedInvest and Crowdcube are examples of equity crowdfunding platforms that charge success fees. They may also charge additional fees for legal, administrative, or due diligence services, as these are often more complex than traditional reward-based campaigns.

How to Calculate Your Total Crowdfunding Costs

Understanding the total cost of your crowdfunding campaign is crucial for setting a realistic fundraising goal. Here’s how you can calculate your total costs:

  1. Platform Fees: Add the platform fee percentage (typically 5%) to your fundraising goal.
  2. Payment Processing Fees: Calculate the payment processing fee by multiplying the total amount of funds you expect to raise by the processing fee percentage (usually around 2.9%).
  3. Currency Conversion Fees: If you expect to receive international donations, estimate the currency conversion fee.
  4. Shipping Costs: Estimate the cost of delivering physical rewards to your backers. Factor in packaging, postage, and taxes.
  5. Additional Fees: Include any costs for premium services, advertising, or analytics.

For example, if your crowdfunding goal is $50,000 and you anticipate a 5% platform fee, 2.9% payment processing fee, and $2,000 in shipping costs, your total cost may look something like this:

  • Platform Fee: $50,000 × 5% = $2,500
  • Payment Processing Fee: $50,000 × 2.9% = $1,450
  • Shipping Costs: $2,000

Total Costs: $2,500 + $1,450 + $2,000 = $5,950

Thus, your net funds raised will be reduced by approximately $5,950, so you’d need to raise more than your goal to cover these expenses.

Choosing the Right Crowdfunding Platform for Your Budget

When choosing a crowdfunding platform, it’s important to consider your budget and fundraising goals. Each platform has different fee structures, so it’s crucial to compare them based on the following factors:

  • Fee Transparency: Look for platforms that provide clear and upfront information about their fees. Avoid platforms with hidden charges or unclear terms.
  • Funding Model: Consider the type of crowdfunding model you’re using (donation, reward, equity, or debt) and choose a platform that specializes in your model.
  • International Reach: If you’re expecting international backers, choose a platform that supports multiple currencies and offers competitive currency conversion fees.
  • Additional Services: Consider whether you need extra marketing or promotional services. Some platforms offer free tools to help you succeed, while others charge extra for these services.

Final Thoughts

Crowdfunding is an excellent way to bring your ideas to life, but it’s essential to understand the costs involved to ensure you raise enough funds to meet your goals. By being aware of the platform fees, payment processing charges, shipping costs, and additional service fees, you can better plan for your campaign and avoid any financial surprises.

Remember to carefully research the platforms you’re considering and weigh their costs against the benefits they offer. With the right strategy and a clear understanding of fees, crowdfunding can be an incredibly powerful tool for turning your dreams into reality.
