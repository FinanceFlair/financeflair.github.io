How to Start Micro Investing in 2021: A Comprehensive Guide


We are now witnessing an era where investors realize that wealth creation can begin as early as today and can fit even in the smallest corners of people’s financial lives, paving the way for micro investing. As more and more people dabble in investing, it’s crucial to understands the potential benefits and risks it entails. This comprehensive guide lays it all out for you, straight and simple.

Before diving in, invest some time to understand that not all investors are the same, nor are all investment types. Your risk appetite, time horizon, and financial situation will determine the right path for you. Understanding these factors will help guide you further.

Step 1: Set Clear Financial Goals

While it may sound obvious, understanding and clearly defining your financial objectives is vital. Consider your short, medium, and long-term goals. Whether it’s building an emergency fund, saving for a down payment on a house, or funding a child’s college education, specific goals will help guide your investment strategy.

Step 2: Research Investment Platforms

Next, choose a financial platform that matches your goals. There’s a broad range of investment platforms available today. From Robo-Advisor to brokerage accounts, they vary in terms of fees, functionalities, and the caliber of support they offer. Examples of such platforms include Acorns, Wealthfront, Robinhood, and E-Trade.

Step 3: Don’t Just Jump In

Before investing, consider whether you have the emotional bandwidth to cope with the highs and lows of the market. As losing money is often part of the game, remaining calm in the face of market turbulence is essential. Moreover, you want to avoid making rash decisions.

Step 4: Start Small and Diversify

In investing, as in life, it’s crucial to start small and diversify. A good place to begin micro investing is with ‘round-up’ or ‘spare change’ investing, wherein your investment platform rounds up your daily purchases to the nearest dollar and invests the change directly in a diversified portfolio. By doing so, you gradually build an investment portfolio without feeling the shock of a large initial outlay.

Step 5: Monitor and Rebalance Your Portfolio

Irrespective of your investment strategy or investment platform, keeping track of your financial health is crucial. Regular monitoring lets you understand the performance of different investments, review your goals, and rebalance your portfolio when necessary. This is how you ensure your financial goals stay on track.

Step 6: Learn and Grow

Investing always entails a learning curve, and that’s part of the fun. Use your investment platform’s educational resources to your advantage, and stay up to date on market trends, investment tactics, and financial news.

Micro Investing has garnered attention due to its potential to Mammon our wallets through small consistent savings. Nevertheless, it equally poses the challenge of catering to the individual’s unique financial needs. So, as you venture into the world of micro investing, remember to prioritize your financial goals, choose an investment platform that aligns with your objectives, start small, diversify and continue to learn. May the market trends forever be in your favor.
