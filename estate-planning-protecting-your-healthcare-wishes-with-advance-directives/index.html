Estate Planning: Protecting Your Healthcare Wishes with Advance Directives


When it comes to estate planning, many people focus on the distribution of their assets after they pass away. However, it is equally important to consider your healthcare wishes and how they can be protected in advance. This is where advance directives come into play.

What are Advance Directives?

Advance directives are legal documents that allow individuals to express their healthcare preferences in advance, ensuring that their wishes are upheld even if they become unable to communicate or make decisions in the future. These documents are crucial for anyone who wants to have control over their medical treatment and ensure that their values and beliefs are respected.

Types of Advance Directives

There are several types of advance directives, each serving a unique purpose:

  1. Living Will: A living will outlines the medical treatments and interventions you wish to receive or refuse if you are unable to communicate your preferences. It typically covers end-of-life decisions such as life support, resuscitation, and artificial nutrition.

  2. Durable Power of Attorney for Healthcare: This document designates a trusted individual, known as a healthcare proxy or agent, to make medical decisions on your behalf when you are unable to do so. It is essential to choose someone who understands your values and will advocate for your best interests.

  3. Do Not Resuscitate (DNR) Order: A DNR order instructs medical professionals not to perform cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) in the event of cardiac arrest. This directive is especially important if you have a terminal illness or do not wish to undergo aggressive life-saving measures.

  4. Provider Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment (POLST): POLST is a medical order that is completed in consultation with your healthcare provider. It documents your specific preferences regarding life-sustaining treatment, including resuscitation, intubation, and hospitalization.

Why You Need Advance Directives

Having advance directives in place can provide peace of mind for you and your loved ones. Here’s why they are essential:

1. Ensuring Your Wishes are Honored

By creating advance directives, you have the power to outline your healthcare preferences and ensure they are respected. This reduces the burden on your family members and medical professionals, who may otherwise struggle to make decisions on your behalf without clear guidance.

2. Avoiding Unwanted Medical Interventions

Advance directives enable you to specify the medical treatments you would like to receive and those you wish to refuse. This ensures that you receive the care that aligns with your values and beliefs, while also avoiding unnecessary interventions that may be against your wishes.

3. Relieving Family Members of Difficult Decisions

In times of medical crisis, family members often find themselves in the position of having to make difficult decisions on behalf of their loved ones. By having advance directives in place, you can alleviate this burden and spare your family from potential conflicts or guilt associated with decision-making.

4. Providing Clarity and Consistency

Advance directives offer clear instructions to healthcare providers, ensuring that your wishes are followed consistently across different medical settings. This helps prevent any misunderstandings or misinterpretations that may arise during emergency situations.

Creating Advance Directives

To create advance directives, it is recommended to consult with an attorney who specializes in estate planning or healthcare law. They can guide you through the process and ensure that your documents meet all legal requirements in your jurisdiction.

Reviewing and Updating Advance Directives

It is crucial to review and update your advance directives periodically, especially when significant life events occur, such as marriage, divorce, or the birth of a child. By keeping your directives up to date, you can be confident that your healthcare wishes remain current and reflective of your values.

Conclusion

Estate planning goes beyond the distribution of assets; it also involves protecting your healthcare wishes through advance directives. By creating these legal documents, you can ensure that your preferences are honored, unwanted medical interventions are avoided, and your loved ones are relieved of difficult decisions. Take the time to consult with an attorney and create advance directives that accurately reflect your values and beliefs. By doing so, you can have peace of mind knowing that your healthcare wishes will be respected, even if you are unable to communicate them in the future.
