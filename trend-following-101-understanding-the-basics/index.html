Trend Following 101: Understanding the Basics


Trend following is a popular trading strategy that has been used by many successful traders and investors for decades. However, despite its widespread use, many people still find it difficult to understand the basics of trend following. In this article, we will provide a comprehensive overview of the trend following strategy, its advantages, and its limitations.

What is Trend Following?

Trend following is a trading strategy that involves identifying and following the direction of a market trend. The strategy is based on the idea that markets tend to trend, meaning that they move in a specific direction for a certain period of time. Trend followers use various technical indicators and chart patterns to identify the direction of the trend and then use it to make trading decisions.

How Does Trend Following Work?

Trend following works by using a combination of technical indicators and chart patterns to identify the direction of the trend. The strategy involves the following steps:

  1. Identifying the trend: The first step in trend following is to identify the direction of the trend. This can be done using various technical indicators such as moving averages, relative strength index (RSI), and Bollinger Bands.
  2. Defining the trend: Once the direction of the trend has been identified, the next step is to define the trend. This can be done by using chart patterns such as trend lines, channels, and wedges.
  3. Entering the trade: Once the trend has been defined, the next step is to enter the trade. This can be done by buying or selling the underlying asset depending on the direction of the trend.
  4. Managing the trade: The final step in trend following is to manage the trade. This involves setting stop-loss levels, taking profits, and adjusting the position size based on the market conditions.

Advantages of Trend Following

Trend following has several advantages that make it a popular trading strategy. Some of the advantages include:

  1. Simple to understand: Trend following is a simple trading strategy that is easy to understand and implement.
  2. High returns: Trend following can generate high returns especially during strong trending markets.
  3. Low risk: Trend following can be a low-risk trading strategy especially when used in combination with other trading strategies.
  4. Flexibility: Trend following can be used in various markets and timeframes, making it a flexible trading strategy.

Limitations of Trend Following

Despite its advantages, trend following has several limitations that traders should be aware of. Some of the limitations include:

  1. Market volatility: Trend following can be affected by market volatility, which can cause the strategy to generate false signals.
  2. False signals: Trend following can generate false signals, especially during range-bound markets.
  3. Over-trading: Trend following can lead to over-trading, especially when used in combination with other trading strategies.
  4. Slippage: Trend following can be affected by slippage, which can cause the strategy to generate losses.

Conclusion

Trend following is a popular trading strategy that can generate high returns especially during strong trending markets. However, it is important to understand the limitations of the strategy and to use it in combination with other trading strategies to minimize risk. By following the steps outlined in this article, traders can successfully implement the trend following strategy and achieve their trading goals.
