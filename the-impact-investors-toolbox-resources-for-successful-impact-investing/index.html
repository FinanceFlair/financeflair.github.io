The Impact Investor's Toolbox: Resources for Successful Impact Investing


As impact investing continues to gain momentum, more and more investors are looking for ways to align their financial goals with positive social and environmental outcomes. Impact investing goes beyond traditional financial returns, focusing on investments that generate measurable social and environmental impact alongside financial returns. If you’re interested in becoming an impact investor or want to enhance your impact investing strategy, having the right tools and resources at your disposal is crucial. In this blog post, we’ll explore the impact investor’s toolbox and highlight some essential resources for successful impact investing.

1. Impact Measurement Tools

Measuring the impact of your investments is essential to ensure that they are effectively contributing to the desired social and environmental outcomes. Impact measurement tools help you assess the effectiveness and progress of your investments in terms of impact. Some popular impact measurement tools include:

  • IRIS: The Impact Reporting and Investment Standards (IRIS) provides a comprehensive set of metrics to measure social, environmental, and financial performance.
  • GIIRS: The Global Impact Investing Rating System (GIIRS) evaluates the social and environmental impact of companies and funds.
  • SDGs: The United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) provide a framework for measuring and tracking progress towards global sustainability objectives.

2. Impact Investing Networks and Communities

Being part of impact investing networks and communities can provide valuable insights, connections, and learning opportunities. These networks bring together impact investors, social entrepreneurs, and other stakeholders to collaborate and share knowledge. Some prominent impact investing networks include:

  • Global Impact Investing Network (GIIN): GIIN is a leading global network of organizations dedicated to advancing impact investing.
  • Impact Hub: Impact Hub is a global network of co-working spaces that fosters collaboration between entrepreneurs, investors, and changemakers.
  • B Corps: B Corps are for-profit companies that meet rigorous standards of social and environmental performance, accountability, and transparency. Being part of the B Corp community can provide access to a network of like-minded businesses.

3. Impact Investing Education and Research

Staying informed about the latest trends, insights, and research in impact investing is crucial for making informed investment decisions. Several organizations offer educational programs, publications, and research resources to deepen your understanding of impact investing. Here are a few notable resources:

  • Acumen: Acumen offers online courses and resources on impact investing, social entrepreneurship, and leadership.
  • Stanford Social Innovation Review: SSIR is a leading publication that covers research, trends, and best practices in social innovation and impact investing.
  • Global Impact Investing Network (GIIN) Resources: GIIN provides a range of resources, including reports, case studies, and tools, to support impact investors.

4. Impact Investing Platforms and Funds

Investing directly in impact-focused companies or through impact investing funds can provide access to a diversified portfolio of impact investments. These platforms and funds curate investment opportunities and offer guidance on impact measurement and due diligence. Some popular impact investing platforms and funds include:

  • ImpactBase: ImpactBase is an online directory of impact investing funds and products, helping investors find suitable opportunities.
  • Root Capital: Root Capital provides loans, financial training, and market connections to small and growing agricultural businesses in developing countries.
  • Invest with Impact: Invest with Impact is an online platform that allows investors to build personalized impact investment portfolios.

5. Impact Investing Legal and Regulatory Guidance

Navigating the legal and regulatory landscape of impact investing can be complex. Having access to legal and regulatory guidance specific to impact investing can help ensure compliance and mitigate risks. Some resources that offer legal and regulatory guidance for impact investors include:

  • Impact Investing Legal Working Group: This global network of legal professionals provides legal resources, model agreements, and best practices for impact investors.
  • Impact Investing Policy Collaborative: The Impact Investing Policy Collaborative offers resources and guidance on policy and regulatory matters related to impact investing.

As impact investing continues to evolve, the impact investor’s toolbox will expand, offering new and innovative resources to support investors in their journey towards creating positive change. Remember, impact investing is not just about financial returns; it’s about making a difference in the world. By leveraging the resources mentioned above and staying informed, you can become a successful impact investor who generates both financial and social value.
