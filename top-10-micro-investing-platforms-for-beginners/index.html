Top 10 Micro Investing Platforms for Beginners


Are you looking to dip your toes into the world of investing but don’t have a large sum of money to start with? Micro investing platforms are the perfect solution for beginners who want to start investing with small amounts of money. These platforms offer a low barrier to entry, making it easier for anyone to begin their investment journey. In this article, we will explore the top 10 micro investing platforms for beginners, helping you make an informed decision about where to kickstart your investment portfolio.

1. Acorns

Acorns is a popular micro investing platform that allows users to invest their spare change. By linking your credit or debit card to the app, Acorns automatically rounds up your purchases to the nearest dollar and invests the spare change in a diversified portfolio. This “set it and forget it” approach makes it easy for beginners to start investing without having to think about it.

2. Stash

Stash is another user-friendly micro investing platform that caters to beginners. With Stash, users can invest in fractional shares of stocks and exchange-traded funds (ETFs) with as little as $5. The platform offers personalized investment guidance and educational content, making it an ideal choice for those who are new to investing.

3. Robinhood

Robinhood has gained popularity for its commission-free trading and user-friendly interface. This platform allows beginners to invest in stocks, ETFs, options, and cryptocurrencies without paying any commission fees. With its intuitive mobile app, Robinhood is a great choice for beginners who want to start trading with ease.

4. Betterment

Betterment is a robo-advisor that offers a hands-off approach to investing. This platform uses algorithms to build and manage a diversified portfolio for its users. Beginners can set their investment goals and risk tolerance, and Betterment takes care of the rest. With automatic rebalancing and tax-loss harvesting, Betterment is a hassle-free way for beginners to start investing.

5. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is another robo-advisor that offers automated investing services. The platform creates a diversified portfolio based on the user’s risk tolerance and investment goals. With features like tax-efficient investing and automatic rebalancing, Wealthfront is a solid choice for beginners who want to take a hands-off approach to their investments.

6. M1 Finance

M1 Finance combines the best of both worlds by offering automated investing and customizable portfolios. Beginners can choose from pre-built portfolios or create their own, and M1 Finance takes care of the investing and rebalancing. With no commissions or management fees, M1 Finance is a cost-effective option for beginners.

7. SoFi Invest

SoFi Invest is a micro investing platform that offers commission-free trading of stocks, ETFs, and cryptocurrencies. The platform also provides access to financial advisors for personalized guidance. With its user-friendly interface and educational resources, SoFi Invest is a great choice for beginners who want to invest in a variety of assets.

8. Ellevest

Ellevest is a micro investing platform designed specifically for women, although it is open to all genders. The platform takes into account the gender pay gap and women’s longer life expectancies when creating investment portfolios. With a focus on closing the gender investment gap, Ellevest is a unique choice for beginners who want to invest with a socially conscious platform.

9. TD Ameritrade

TD Ameritrade offers a wide range of investment options, making it a versatile choice for beginners. With its robust trading platform and educational resources, TD Ameritrade caters to beginners who want to learn more about investing while building their portfolios.

10. E*TRADE

ETRADE is a well-established online brokerage that provides a variety of investment options for beginners. With its user-friendly interface and extensive research tools, ETRADE is suitable for beginners who want to take a hands-on approach to their investments while having access to educational materials.

In conclusion, micro investing platforms offer a convenient and accessible way for beginners to start investing. Whether you prefer a hands-off robo-advisor or a more hands-on trading platform, there are plenty of options to choose from. Consider your investment goals, risk tolerance, and preferred investment style when selecting the right platform for your needs. With the right platform, you can take the first step towards building your investment portfolio, no matter how small your starting amount may be.
