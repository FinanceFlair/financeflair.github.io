Enhancing Liquidity through Cash Pooling Strategies


Cash pooling is an umbrella term for a collection of tactics that companies use to assemble their cash reserves into a singular pool, from which they can make large payments or proactively invest. These strategies can help businesses enhance their liquidity, secure better interest rates, and optimize their cash management.

One of the primary motivations behind cash pooling is to improve liquidity, which is the ease with which an asset, such as cash, can be converted into something else (like services or goods) without significantly affecting its market price. The greater the liquidity, the easier it is for a business to make payments or investments, thereby enhancing its financial stability.

There are several effective cash pooling strategies that companies can implement, some of which are outlined below.

Setting Up a Cash Concentration Account

A cash concentration account entails creating a bank account that serves as a central repository for all cash holdings. This account should ideally be controlled by a treasury department that actively manages the funds.

The key benefits of this strategy are:

  1. Improved cash visibility: Correlating inflows and outflows becomes much simpler, particularly when you’re managing a complex organization with dozens of subsidiaries.
  2. Frees up resources: By reducing the number of bank accounts a company has to manage, treasury managers can focus their efforts on value-adding activities.
  3. Faster Payments: With all available cash in a single location, companies can make payments promptly and avoid disruptions that lengthen the payment chain.

Using Dynamic Hedging

Dynamic hedging is not a cash pooling strategy itself, but it plays an essential role in ensuring that cash pooling works effectively. As companies transact in foreign currencies, they’re subject to currency exchange rate risks. Dynamic hedging enables companies to manage these risks by undertaking derivative transactions, such as currency options and forwards.

The benefits of dynamic hedging include:

  1. Reduced Exposure to Risk: Hedging contracts can help reduce exposure to adverse currency swings, based on market risk analysis.
  2. Enhanced Forecasting: Cash flow forecasting becomes more accurate when derisks related to exchange rates are addressed.
  3. Low barriers to execution: Companies can easily manage hedge accounting using established software and systems.

Implementing Payment Factory

A payment factory is a straight-through processing (STP) framework that speeds up payment execution by automating payment initiation, validation, and reconciliation. The primary incentive here lies in reducing days sales outstanding (DSO) and accounts receivable by increasing efficiency in the payment management process.

Advantages of a payment factory include:

  1. Reduced Operational Costs: By automating payment management, a company can lower costs associated with manual processing.
  2. Minimized Manual Errors: The adoption of electronic payment systems helps minimize the possibility of human error.
  3. Faster Reconciliation: Electronic payment records simplify reconciliation processes, further improving liquidity.

Despite being a complex strategy, cash pooling offers significant benefits when implemented effectively. By adopting these strategies, companies can create more liquidity, manage risks, boost investment returns, and ultimately, achieve better financial performance.
