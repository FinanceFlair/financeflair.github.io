The Best Contrarian Investing Podcasts and Resources


Investing is often portrayed as a straightforward endeavor—buy low, sell high, follow market trends, and diversify—but the truth is far more nuanced. The crowd tends to chase the latest hot stock or pump-and-dump scheme, while contrarian investors chart a different path altogether. They seek value where others see risk, buy into markets when sentiment is bleak, and often profit when the herd panics. To navigate these uncharted waters successfully, contrarian investors need sharp insights, diverse perspectives, and a keen understanding of market psychology. Fortunately, an increasing number of podcasts and resources cater specifically to this contrarian mindset, offering deeper dives beyond mainstream investing narratives.

In this post, we explore some of the best contrarian investing podcasts and resources, helping you stay sharp, challenged, and ahead of the curve.

Why Contrarian Investing Requires Its Own Ecosystem of Information

Contrarian investing is about believing in the potential of undervalued or overlooked assets, often going against market sentiment. This approach demands not only solid fundamental analysis but also mental toughness to withstand short-term volatility and skepticism.

Mainstream financial media tends to amplify consensus views and herd behaviors, which might not align with contrarian strategies. Consequently, contrarian investors benefit immensely from voices that embrace independent thinking, challenge prevailing wisdom, and promote in-depth fundamental research.

Top Contrarian Investing Podcasts to Tune Into

Podcasts have become essential tools for modern investors, offering flexibility and access to a wide range of experts. Below are some standout podcasts that champion contrarian investing principles.

1. The Meb Faber Show

Hosted by Meb Faber, a well-known quant investor and author, this podcast delves into evidence-based investing but frequently explores themes around contrarian approaches such as value investing, tactical asset allocation, and alternative data analysis. Faber interviews fund managers and economists who challenge the status quo, providing fresh perspectives.

Why it stands out: The show blends quantitative rigor with storytelling that highlights why going against the grain can pay off, making it ideal for contrarian-minded listeners craving substance and strategy.

2. Invest Like the Best

Patrick O’Shaughnessy explores various investment philosophies, often featuring guests with unconventional approaches. The conversations uncover hidden ideas and unorthodox methods—from value investing in neglected sectors to long-term macro bets that defy consensus.

Why it stands out: It’s a deep-dive podcast that unearths nuanced thought processes behind successful contrarian investing, making complex ideas accessible without dumbing them down.

3. Animal Spirits Podcast

Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson bring humor and candidness to discussing market trends, behavioral finance, and investment psychology. They frequently critique popular investing clichés and explore why going against simplified narratives helps in the long run.

Why it stands out: Both hosts excel at challenging common misconceptions and provide realistic, no-fluff guidance, which is valuable for contrarian investors wary of hype.

4. The Acquirers Podcast

Hosted by Tobias Carlisle, who wrote “The Acquirer’s Multiple,” this podcast is a treasure trove for value investors. It focuses on finding cheap stocks ignored by the market, dissecting contrarian strategies through in-depth interviews and analysis.

Why it stands out: The show’s singular dedication to deep-value, contrarian investing makes it a must-listen for anyone wanting to hunt bargains in overlooked corners of the market.

5. Contrarian Outlook

This podcast dives directly into contrarian themes with discussions centered on undervalued sectors, alternative assets, and macroeconomic shifts that defy Wall Street’s consensus narratives.

Why it stands out: It provides targeted content that helps listeners identify risk where others see opportunity and vice versa, honing the contrarian investor’s edge.

Essential Contrarian Investing Books and Written Resources

Listening to podcasts provides dynamic conversations, but some deeper knowledge comes from meticulously researched written resources. These books and reports form the backbone of contrarian investing wisdom.

1. “The Intelligent Investor” by Benjamin Graham

Often called the bible of value investing, Graham’s principles underpin much contrarian thought—buying securities at a discount to intrinsic value and maintaining a margin of safety. Many contrarian investors trace their approach back to Graham’s teachings around market psychology and “Mr. Market.”

2. “Deep Value” by Tobias Carlisle

This book provides a meticulous analysis of the behavioral biases that cause investors to sell stocks too cheaply. It offers a modern take on contrarian investing through the lens of quantitative strategies and case studies.

3. “You Can Be a Stock Market Genius” by Joel Greenblatt

Despite its catchy title, this book delivers serious strategies on special situations like spin-offs, restructurings, and mergers—areas rich with contrarian opportunity due to market inefficiencies.

4. Howard Marks’ Memos

Though technically not a book, the investment memos from Howard Marks, co-founder of Oaktree Capital Management, are widely considered must-reads for contrarian investors. His insights on risk, market cycles, and contrarian thinking have shaped many professionals’ minds.

Online Communities and Newsletters to Follow

Contrarian investing thrives on the exchange of ideas and access to alternative viewpoints. Beyond podcasts and books, online communities and newsletters offer continuous streams of contrarian insight.

  • Value Investor Insight Newsletter: This publication dissects value investing strategies and highlights undervalued stocks worth watching.

  • The Contrarian’s Journal: Offering independent market commentary and contrarian outlooks on market trends and economic cycles.

  • Bogleheads Forum: While often associated with passive investing, sections of the community delve into contrarian discussions especially regarding risk tolerance and behavioral mistakes.

  • Twitter and Substack: A number of finance experts and independent research analysts share contrarian picks and market analysis regularly. Following thoughtful voices here can supplement other learning channels.

How to Incorporate Contrarian Resources Effectively

Access to quality content is just the beginning. To truly benefit from these resources, contrarian investors should cultivate habits such as:

  • Critical Listening and Reading: Question assumptions and consider why mainstream views might be wrong.

  • Note-Taking and Reflection: Capture insightful points to revisit when making investment decisions.

  • Diversify Learning: Balance quantitative analysis podcasts with macroeconomic and behavioral psychology content to build a holistic understanding.

  • Patience and Discipline: Embrace long time horizons and resist impulsiveness, hallmarks of effective contrarian investing.

Final Thoughts

Contrarian investing requires courage, conviction, and intellectual curiosity. The best contrarian podcasts and resources serve as guiding lights through periods of uncertainty and market noise. By engaging with diverse voices—from data-driven quants to seasoned value investors—you can sharpen your judgment and uncover opportunities hidden from the herd.

Whether you prefer absorbing knowledge on the go through podcasts or diving deep into foundational investment texts, continuously immersing yourself in contrarian perspectives empowers you to think independently and invest confidently in an unpredictable world. Keep questioning, keep learning, and remember: the crowd doesn’t always know best.

Embark on your contrarian investing journey armed with these resources, and find your edge where others see risk.
