Best Micro-Investing Platforms to Grow Your Money in 2025


In the ever-evolving landscape of personal finance, micro-investing platforms have emerged as a revolutionary way to help individuals grow their wealth with minimal effort and low initial capital. These platforms allow everyday people to start investing with small amounts of money, often as little as a few dollars at a time. In 2025, micro-investing continues to gain traction, thanks to advancements in technology, user-friendly interfaces, and growing financial literacy among the general population.

Micro-investing offers a practical solution to anyone who feels intimidated by traditional investing methods or doesn’t have the large sums of money typically required to enter the market. With platforms offering features like automated investing, fractional shares, and low-fee structures, it’s now easier than ever to start building wealth—regardless of your financial background.

In this guide, we will explore some of the best micro-investing platforms in 2025 that are designed to help individuals grow their money efficiently and affordably. Whether you’re just getting started or looking to optimize your investment strategy, these platforms provide powerful tools and strategies to help you on your financial journey.

1. Acorns: The Round-Up King

Acorns has established itself as one of the top names in micro-investing, and it’s easy to see why. The platform is built around a simple yet effective concept: round-up investing. Every time you make a purchase with a linked credit or debit card, Acorns rounds up the transaction to the nearest dollar and invests the spare change into a diversified portfolio.

For example, if you buy a coffee for $3.50, Acorns will round up the transaction to $4.00 and invest the 50 cents difference. While this may seem small, over time, these little investments can add up to significant amounts. Acorns allows users to create custom portfolios based on their risk tolerance and investment goals. It also offers a variety of features such as automatic rebalancing, tax-loss harvesting, and retirement accounts.

One of the unique selling points of Acorns is its simplicity. With no need to manually input any transactions, you can effortlessly build an investment portfolio with your everyday purchases. In addition to round-up investing, Acorns also offers recurring contributions, which allows you to set up automatic transfers to your investment account on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis.

Acorns’ fee structure is straightforward. The platform charges a monthly subscription fee, with different tiers based on the services you need. For those looking for a low-cost and hassle-free investment option, Acorns remains one of the best platforms to start micro-investing.

Key Features:

  • Round-up investing
  • Automated recurring contributions
  • Tax-loss harvesting
  • Diversified portfolio options
  • Simple user interface

2. Stash: Build a Portfolio with Small Contributions

Stash is another top-tier micro-investing platform that has been making waves due to its ability to allow users to invest small amounts of money in diversified portfolios. Unlike Acorns, which focuses primarily on round-ups, Stash provides users with the flexibility to invest in individual stocks, ETFs, and bonds. You can start investing with as little as $5, which makes it highly accessible for those just beginning to explore the world of investing.

One of the key features of Stash is its educational resources. For first-time investors, Stash provides a wealth of content that explains various aspects of investing, from how to select stocks to understanding different types of investment accounts. This makes it a great option for people who want to learn while they invest.

Stash also offers “Smart Portfolios,” which are pre-built portfolios designed to align with different risk levels and investment goals. Whether you’re interested in a conservative, balanced, or aggressive investment strategy, Stash has options that cater to all types of investors.

Stash offers a subscription model, with fees starting at $1 per month for the basic plan. As you upgrade to higher tiers, additional features such as retirement accounts and premium investing tools become available. This makes Stash a flexible platform for users who want to grow their wealth without committing a large amount of capital upfront.

Key Features:

  • Invest as little as $5
  • Pre-built Smart Portfolios
  • Educational resources and investment tools
  • Retirement account options
  • Flexible subscription plans

3. Robinhood: Free and Simple Stock Investing

Robinhood made headlines when it introduced commission-free trading to the masses, and it remains one of the most popular platforms for micro-investors in 2025. While it started primarily as a stock trading app, Robinhood has since expanded its offerings to include ETFs, options, and even cryptocurrency. This makes it an ideal platform for investors who want to invest small amounts of money in a wide range of assets.

One of the main appeals of Robinhood is its completely free trading model. There are no commission fees for buying or selling stocks, ETFs, or options, which means you can invest small amounts without worrying about your investments being eaten up by fees. Robinhood also offers fractional shares, which means you can buy partial shares of expensive stocks like Tesla or Amazon for as little as $1.

Robinhood is a great option for individuals who want to have complete control over their investments and are comfortable selecting their own stocks. While the platform doesn’t offer as much hand-holding or guidance as some other micro-investing platforms, its simplicity and low-cost structure make it an attractive choice for many investors.

Key Features:

  • Commission-free trading
  • Fractional shares available
  • Wide range of investment options (stocks, ETFs, options, crypto)
  • Simple and intuitive interface
  • No account minimums

4. Betterment: Automated Investing for Beginners

Betterment is one of the largest and most well-known robo-advisors on the market, making it a popular choice for micro-investing in 2025. Unlike other platforms that let you pick individual stocks or assets, Betterment focuses on automated portfolio management. The platform uses algorithms to build and manage a diversified portfolio based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.

Betterment offers several types of portfolios, including socially responsible investing (SRI) options for those interested in aligning their investments with their personal values. The platform also offers tax-efficient investing strategies and automatic rebalancing, which helps ensure that your portfolio remains aligned with your investment goals.

For micro-investors, Betterment offers an easy entry point with no minimum deposit required. It also has a low fee structure, making it a great choice for individuals who want a hands-off approach to investing without paying high management fees. Betterment’s fee starts at 0.25% of assets under management for its basic plan, with no hidden fees or commissions.

Key Features:

  • Automated portfolio management
  • Tax-efficient investing
  • No minimum deposit required
  • Socially responsible investing options
  • Low management fees

5. Wealthfront: Diversified Portfolio with Tax Optimization

Wealthfront is another leading robo-advisor that provides a fully automated approach to micro-investing. Like Betterment, Wealthfront builds a diversified portfolio based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. However, Wealthfront stands out with its emphasis on tax optimization. The platform offers features like tax-loss harvesting, which can help reduce your tax burden and increase your returns over time.

Wealthfront also allows you to open a variety of account types, including taxable brokerage accounts, retirement accounts (like IRAs), and even 529 college savings plans. This flexibility makes it an attractive choice for users who want to invest for multiple financial goals at once.

Wealthfront requires a $500 minimum deposit to open an account, but the platform’s low fees and automated investing strategies make it a great option for those who want a more hands-off approach to building wealth. Wealthfront’s fee starts at 0.25% of assets under management, similar to Betterment.

Key Features:

  • Automated portfolio management
  • Tax-loss harvesting
  • Diversified portfolio options
  • Low management fees
  • Multiple account types available

Conclusion: Making the Most of Micro-Investing in 2025

Micro-investing continues to grow in popularity due to its accessibility, affordability, and ease of use. Whether you’re starting with just a few dollars or looking to grow your wealth over time, these micro-investing platforms provide the tools and resources to help you succeed. From round-up investing with Acorns to fully automated portfolio management with Betterment and Wealthfront, there’s a platform that fits every investor’s needs and preferences.

The key to success in micro-investing is consistency. By committing to regular contributions, even if they’re small, you can take advantage of compounding returns and steadily build your wealth over time. It’s important to choose a platform that aligns with your financial goals, risk tolerance, and level of involvement.

As we move into 2025, the options for micro-investing are more robust than ever. With the right platform, even small, consistent investments can grow into substantial wealth, helping you secure a brighter financial future.
