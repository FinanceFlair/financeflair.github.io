Taking the Plunge: A Guide to Insuring High-Risk Pools and Clubs


Unlike more traditional swimming pools, insured high-risk pools and clubs cater to specific groups, often with unique risks. These can include actual swimming and water-based activities, prevention of water loss or contamination, as well as minor injuries, illnesses, and property damage in these facilities. To navigate the sometimes complex world of insurance for these facilities, follow these steps:

Evaluate Pool and Club Uses

First, carefully evaluate the specific types of activities and facilities offered by the pool or club. Factors to consider include the number and types of members, the amenities available, and the insurance requirements by local law.

Research Insurers and Quotes

Once you have a good understanding of the risks involved, research reputable insurers that specialize in insuring for high-risk pools and clubs. Compare prices and terms from various companies to find the best fit for your specific needs. Additionally, many insurers offer a variety of packages, so studying these in detail can provide critical insights and potential savings.

Choose Comprehensive Coverage

When choosing your insurance package, ensure that it provides comprehensive coverage for all potential risks. These can include liability, property damage, and loss of income, among others. Carefully review the policy terms to understand exclusions, deductibles, maximum payouts, and other important details.

Insure Against Cyber Risks

With the rise of technology and the use of online platforms for membership, booking, and communication, it’s crucial to consider cyber risks in your insurance coverage. This can include data breaches, cyber extortion, and online defamation. Speak with your insurer about incorporating these potential cybersecurity risks into your policy.

Consider Insurance for Members

The pool or club itself is not the only part that needs coverage; its members deserve protection too. Some high-risk pools and clubs offer insurance packages for members. Consult your insurer about the option to provide membership insurance, which can help cover medical expenses or accidents occurring during club activities.

Ensure Policy Renewal

Renewal of insurance policies is essential, both for staying legally compliant and maintaining coverage. Plan ahead for policy renewals and budget accordingly to avoid unexpected disruptions in insurance coverage.

Maintain Good Relationship with Insurer

Establishing and maintaining open lines of communication with your insurer is crucial. Being proactive about alerting your insurer of changes in activities, facilities, or membership can help ensure that your policy remains relevant and up-to-date.

In conclusion, insuring high-risk pools and clubs requires close collaboration with a reputable insurer. Understanding the activities, facilities, and potential risks involved is key to choosing the right policy. By following these steps and staying informed of your insurance needs, you can provide a safe, enjoyable environment for your members. So, take the plunge and secure your high-risk pool or club with the appropriate insurance coverage today.
