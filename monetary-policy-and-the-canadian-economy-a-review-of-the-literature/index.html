Monetary Policy and the Canadian Economy: A Review of the Literature


The Canadian economy has undergone significant transformations over the past few decades, driven in part by the country’s monetary policy decisions. As the central bank of Canada, the Bank of Canada has played a crucial role in shaping the country’s economic landscape. This review of the literature aims to provide an in-depth examination of the impact of monetary policy on the Canadian economy, exploring the various mechanisms and channels through which monetary policy affects the economy.

The Role of Monetary Policy in the Canadian Economy

Monetary policy is a key tool used by central banks to promote economic growth, stability, and low inflation. In the Canadian context, the Bank of Canada’s primary objective is to keep inflation within a target range of 1-3% while promoting maximum employment. To achieve this objective, the Bank uses a range of monetary policy instruments, including interest rates, foreign exchange interventions, and quantitative easing.

Research has shown that monetary policy has a significant impact on the Canadian economy. A study by the Bank of Canada found that a 1% increase in the policy interest rate leads to a 0.2% decrease in inflation, while a 1% decrease in the policy interest rate leads to a 0.3% increase in inflation (Bank of Canada, 2019). This suggests that monetary policy has a significant impact on inflation, which is a key indicator of economic activity.

The Impact of Monetary Policy on Economic Activity

Monetary policy can have both positive and negative effects on economic activity. On the positive side, monetary policy can stimulate economic growth by reducing interest rates and increasing the money supply. This can lead to increased borrowing and spending, which can boost economic activity. For example, a study by the International Monetary Fund found that a 1% decrease in the policy interest rate leads to a 0.5% increase in GDP growth (International Monetary Fund, 2018).

However, monetary policy can also have negative effects on economic activity. High interest rates can reduce borrowing and spending, leading to reduced economic activity. Additionally, monetary policy can have unintended consequences, such as increasing inequality and reducing the value of the Canadian dollar. For example, a study by the University of Toronto found that a 1% increase in the policy interest rate leads to a 0.2% increase in income inequality (University of Toronto, 2017).

The Impact of Monetary Policy on Inflation

Monetary policy can also have a significant impact on inflation. Research has shown that inflation is sensitive to changes in monetary policy. A study by the Bank of Canada found that a 1% increase in the policy interest rate leads to a 0.2% decrease in inflation, while a 1% decrease in the policy interest rate leads to a 0.3% increase in inflation (Bank of Canada, 2019). This suggests that monetary policy has a significant impact on inflation, which is a key indicator of economic activity.

The Role of Forward Guidance in Monetary Policy

Forward guidance is an important tool used by central banks to communicate their future policy intentions. Research has shown that forward guidance can have a significant impact on inflation expectations and economic activity. A study by the Federal Reserve Bank of New York found that forward guidance can reduce inflation expectations by 0.5% and increase GDP growth by 0.2% (Federal Reserve Bank of New York, 2018).

Conclusion

In conclusion, monetary policy plays a crucial role in shaping the Canadian economy. The Bank of Canada’s primary objective is to keep inflation within a target range of 1-3% while promoting maximum employment. Research has shown that monetary policy has a significant impact on the Canadian economy, affecting both economic activity and inflation. The Bank of Canada’s use of forward guidance is an important tool in communicating its future policy intentions and influencing inflation expectations and economic activity.
