How to Use Bloomberg Terminal for Equity Research


The Bloomberg Terminal is a powerhouse tool widely recognized in the finance industry for its comprehensive data, advanced analytics, and real-time market information. For anyone involved in equity research, mastering the Bloomberg Terminal can significantly enhance your ability to analyze stocks, monitor market trends, and make well-informed investment decisions. This guide breaks down how to leverage the Bloomberg Terminal effectively for in-depth equity research, covering everything from basic navigation to advanced analytical techniques.

Understanding the Bloomberg Terminal Interface

Before diving into equity research tasks, familiarizing yourself with the layout and key functions of the Bloomberg Terminal is essential. The interface is designed for speed and accessibility, providing access to an immense volume of data through commands and shortcuts.

  • Command line: The primary input area where you type ticker symbols, security names, or Bloomberg functions.
  • Function keys: Quick keys like execute commands, and others like , , bring up important resources.
  • Workspace: Displays the output—charts, data tables, analysis reports, etc.

Learning the common keyboard shortcuts enhances efficiency as you move through different modules quickly without fumbling through menus.

Step 1: Identifying and Screening Stocks

The first step in equity research is identifying stocks that meet your criteria. Bloomberg offers powerful screening tools tailored for this.

Using Equity Screening Functions

  • EQS – Equity Screening: This feature allows you to filter stocks based on various metrics such as market capitalization, sector, country, P/E ratios, dividend yield, revenue growth, and more.
  • Setting Criteria: Begin with broad parameters and narrow down as needed. For example, filter for technology companies with market cap above $10 billion and P/E ratio between 15-25.
  • Save Screens: Bloomberg lets you save your custom screens for repeated use, useful for maintaining watchlists or periodic scans.

Advanced Screening Tips

  • Incorporate fundamental data, including financial ratios and earnings estimates.
  • Use ownership filters to analyze stocks heavily held by institutional investors.
  • Combine financial metrics with technical indicators like moving averages.

Step 2: Accessing Company Financials and Filings

Once you have a list of potential stocks, examining their financial health is crucial. Bloomberg provides quick access to detailed financial statements.

Key Bloomberg Functions for Financial Data

  • FA – Financial Analysis: Summarizes income statements, balance sheets, cash flow statements, and key financial ratios.
  • DES – Description: Provides company profiles, descriptions, business segments, and key executives.
  • CF – Cash Flow: Details cash inflows and outflows, essential for understanding liquidity.
  • FIL – Filings: Access to SEC filings such as 10-Ks, 10-Qs, and earnings reports in full document format.

Analyzing Financials

Look for revenue trends, profit margins, debt levels, and free cash flow to gauge operational efficiency. Use ratios like return on equity (ROE) and debt-to-equity for comparative analysis.

Step 3: Utilizing Earnings Estimates and Analyst Consensus

Projecting forward performance is central to equity research. Bloomberg aggregates earnings estimates and analyst opinions from multiple sources.

Functions for Earnings and Estimates

  • EE – Earnings Estimates: View consensus EPS, revenue forecasts, and historical beats or misses.
  • ANR – Analyst Recommendations: Displays buy, hold, and sell ratings plus target prices.

Making Use of Estimates

Track how estimates evolve over time to identify trends. Significant upward revisions can signal positive momentum, while downgrades may warn of bearish sentiment.

Step 4: Conducting Competitor Analysis and Industry Comparisons

Understanding a company’s position in its industry aids in creating a comprehensive equity thesis.

Key Features for Competitor Analysis

  • RV – Relative Value: Compare financial metrics and valuation multiples of peer companies.
  • BI – Bloomberg Intelligence: Offers deep sector research, industry news, and expert insights.

Strategic Use

Identify leaders and laggards within a sector. Look for valuation discrepancies—undervalued companies relative to peers often present investment opportunities.

Step 5: Charting and Technical Analysis

Complement fundamental research with technical analysis to refine entry and exit points.

Bloomberg Charting Tools

  • GP – Graph Price: Customizable price charts with indicators like moving averages, RSI, MACD.
  • Overlay earnings dates, dividends, and news events to correlate price movements with fundamental drivers.

Practical Technical Analysis Tips

Use chart patterns and volume trends to validate or question your thesis. Technical signals can help pinpoint precise timing for buying or selling.

Step 6: Monitoring News and Market Sentiment

Staying informed on breaking news and market sentiment around a stock ensures your research incorporates real-time developments.

News and Sentiment Tools

  • N – News Monitor: Real-time headlines filtered by company or sector.
  • NI EQ – News Impact for Equities: Analyzes market reaction to news events.
  • SPLC – Social Media and News Sentiment: Measures sentiment from social channels and news stories.

Leveraging News

Set up alerts for material developments such as earnings releases, management changes, or macroeconomic updates that may affect stock performance.

Step 7: Building Financial Models and Valuations

Bloomberg integrates modeling tools to build your own forecasts and valuations.

Excel Integration

  • Bloomberg’s Excel add-in enables downloading of data directly into spreadsheets.
  • Use this to construct discounted cash flow (DCF) models or comparative valuation analyses.

Bloomberg Valuation Functions

  • DVD – Dividend Discount Model
  • DCF – Discounted Cash Flow Valuation Tools

These functions provide templates and guidance to streamline valuation processes.

Step 8: Exporting and Sharing Research

Collaboration and documentation are vital in professional equity research.

Export Options

  • Download data tables, charts, and reports in multiple formats including Excel and PDF.
  • Use RV and FA outputs to build comprehensive reports.

Bloomberg Message System

Communicate findings directly through Bloomberg messaging to peers and clients.

Tips for Efficient Bloomberg Terminal Usage in Equity Research

  • Master shortcut keys to quickly switch between functions.
  • Use customized workspaces to save your preferred layouts.
  • Regularly update watchlists and screens based on evolving market conditions.
  • Leverage Bloomberg’s training resources and tutorials for continuous learning.

Mastering the Bloomberg Terminal transforms the equity research process—from raw data gathering to nuanced analysis and timely decision-making. With its vast array of tools and real-time updates, you’ll gain a competitive edge in identifying and evaluating investment opportunities. Whether you’re a novice researcher or a seasoned analyst, employing these steps will deepen your insights and improve the quality of your equity research reports. Dive in and explore Bloomberg’s powerful features to elevate your stock analysis game.
