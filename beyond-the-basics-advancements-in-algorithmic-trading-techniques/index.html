Beyond the Basics: Advancements in Algorithmic Trading Techniques


Algorithmic trading, or “algo-trading”, has been around for several years. Originally, the focus was on automating simple, low-risk trades that could be easily replicated. However, with the advancements in technology and machine learning, the field has evolved rapidly. In this post, we’ll explore some of the more sophisticated techniques that have emerged - beyond the basics - allowing traders to push the boundaries of what’s possible.

Quantitative Trading Strategies

Quantitative trading strategies are programs designed to make profit through the wise use of mathematical models in market analysis. Many of these strategies are rule-based, with rules defined by statistical techniques. For instance, some quantitative strategies opt to trade on the anomalies or inefficiencies they uncover in market mechanisms.

One of the most popular techniques is mean reversion. This is a common trading strategy in which assets are predictably priced based on their average price. When prices deviate from this average, algorithms instinctively know when the prices are too high (overbought) or too low (oversold) and makes trades that allow the trader to profit from these discrepancies.

Another example is algorithmic pairs trading which identifies mispricings in two related assets, buying underpriced and selling overpriced. The difference between the two stock prices is exploited, rather than the individual stock price movement. Eliminating the noise from the market and focusing only on the correlation between two stocks makes it easier to identify any discrepancies in the market pricing.

Beguiling Machine Learning

Machine learning is a rapidly growing field in algorithmic trading. It allows for the automation of tasks, such as the analysis of financial data and optimization of trading algorithms. There are three significant branches of machine learning used in algorithmic trading:

  1. Supervised Learning: Algorithms learn from historical data. For instance, if patterns of past stock prices and trades are fed into a supervised learning model, it can learn to predict future prices or probable trading outcomes.

  2. Unsupervised Learning: Without any prior knowledge of specific data, the algorithm learns relationships and correlations between different stock prices, to understand market mechanisms and make trading predictions.

  3. Reinforcement Learning: This type of machine learning trains trading algorithms by simulating and (slowly) learning from their actions and trade outcomes in the virtual market. Once confident, the algorithms automatically execute trades in the real market, ensuring minimal human intervention.

Exploitation of Natural Language Processing

Another exciting field making waves in algorithmic trading is Natural Language Processing (NLP). With NLP, the raw data of news articles, financial reports, tweets, and similar content can be converted into readable algorithms. This text-based data can tell stories, and when paired with machine learning techniques in the right situations, can make powerful tools for accurately predicting the market trends and movements.

Using these techniques, even the smallest changes in the market can be tracked and quantified leading to predictions and, in turn, profitable trades.

Overall, the field of algorithmic trading is constantly evolving, offering more insight into complex mechanisms and enhancing the power of traders’ tools. Traders are now leveraging this growing field for more advanced tactics, giving them better control, better predictions, and more profit. Algorithmic trading is no longer just automating simple trades. With its steps into machine learning and NLP, it has officially entered the big data age, changing the way we approach and understand the fast-paced world of finance.
