Short Term Trading Strategies for Options Beginners


As a beginner in options trading, you’re undoubtedly eager to generate profits in the short term. Although options are complex financial tools, you can still employ clever strategies to maximize your returns. Here, we delve into five different approaches that have proven successful, particularly for those who prefer swift moves in the market.

1. Building a Strategy with Out-of-the-Money Options: New options traders often gravitate toward in-the-money trades, assuming they reduce expenses. However, out-of-the-money trades can offer significant advantages for short termers.

The rationale? As Asian markets open on Wednesday evening in your time zone, the momentum often dictates that the equities react. Out-of-the-money calls cost less, making this scenario an attractive trading environment as you may reap sizeable profits with minimal investment.

2. Pair Trading for Quick Wins: Pair trading is a unique method, exclusively aimed at profitable short term trading. It involves hedging your bets by combining two similar stocks.

Considered prudent, pair trading is especially useful on days with pronounced market volatility. Why? Since these days see stock prices swinging dramatically, buying both equities simultaneously—hoping one’s price will rise while the other declines—could create healthy profits.

3. Take Advantage of Volatility with Weekly Options: Weekly options are slightly riskier but also tremendous opportunities for short term gains. As the name suggests, these options only last one week. Hence, they react promptly to economic news and announcements.

Listing a weekly straddle (a combination of a call and put) provides you a hedge. When both potential outcomes bring gains, your investment buckles the trend favoring short-term volatility.

4. Rolling Your Options - A Lucrative Approach: Do you know that one strategy can minimize losses and maximize profits? The rolling technique allows you to trade between expiring contracts and new ones.

For instance, if you bought an option that is expiring soon but the stock price hasn’t moved as expected, roll your contract to a more favorable price. Removing the loss-making contract yields three benefits: zeroing out your deficit, securing your initial capital, and reinvesting your money into profitable opportunities.

5. A Winning Habit - Scalping Strategy: Lastly, let’s spotlight scalping in options trading—that practice of making multiple trades within minutes. High-frequency traders have long-used scalping to generate micro-profits.

For beginners, it’s crucial to remember scalping demands a stiffer learning curve. But once perfected, the strategy offers a myriad of opportunities. Before diving in, though, always ensure proper risk management as the market’s rapid changes can quickly turn profitable trades into losses.

To reap the advantages of short term trading, comprehension and knowledge are key. Recognize the tactics mentioned above as a starting point to generate consistent winning trades. With caution and self-education, you’ll further refine and adapt these strategies to suit your preferences.
