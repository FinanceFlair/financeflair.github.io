Low Risk High Reward Trend Following Setups


Trend following is a well-known and widely used trading strategy that seeks to capture profits by riding market trends in either direction. One of the biggest challenges in trading, however, is minimizing risk while maximizing reward. Traders often find themselves stuck between the desire for substantial profits and the need to protect their capital. In this article, we will explore how low risk, high reward trend following setups can be effectively implemented for consistent returns, focusing on strategies, risk management techniques, and how to spot trends that offer excellent trading opportunities.

Understanding Trend Following

Trend following is a trading strategy that aims to capitalize on the momentum of an asset’s price movement. This strategy is based on the premise that once a trend is established—whether upward or downward—it is more likely to continue than to reverse in the short term. Trend followers identify these trends early on and take positions that align with the prevailing market direction.

The fundamental concept behind trend following is simple: buy when the market is going up, sell when the market is going down, and avoid trading during consolidation periods when prices are moving sideways.

The Core Principles of Trend Following

  • Identify a Clear Trend: Recognize whether the market is trending upwards or downwards.
  • Join the Trend Early: Enter trades early enough to capture the bulk of the trend.
  • Ride the Trend: Stay in the trade as long as the trend continues, with strategic exits based on risk management principles.
  • Exit When the Trend Reverses: Use specific indicators or price action signals to exit the trade when the trend shows signs of reversal.

Why Low Risk High Reward Setups Matter

Low risk, high reward setups are the holy grail of trading. The goal is to achieve a greater potential reward for each unit of risk taken. In other words, you want to set up trades that provide substantial profit potential while limiting the downside exposure. Achieving this balance is crucial for long-term success in trading.

Here are some reasons why low risk, high reward setups are essential:

  1. Capital Preservation: The most important factor in trading is to preserve your capital. By setting up trades that have low risk, you reduce the chances of large losses.
  2. Compounding Gains: Low risk setups allow traders to stay in the game longer and grow their account balance through consistent, small wins.
  3. Psychological Edge: When the risk is minimized, traders are less likely to experience emotional stress. A clear risk/reward ratio helps in maintaining discipline and emotional control.
  4. Maximized Profit Potential: By identifying high reward opportunities, traders can amplify their returns on successful trades, even if the success rate is not exceedingly high.

Identifying Low Risk High Reward Trend Following Setups

To identify these setups, traders need to combine several technical tools and strategies. Here are some of the most effective methods for finding low risk, high reward trend following opportunities.

1. Use Trend Indicators

Trend indicators help in identifying the direction of the market and determine whether an asset is trending or in a range. Popular trend indicators include:

  • Moving Averages: One of the simplest and most effective tools, moving averages can help to identify the prevailing market trend. A common setup is using a 50-period simple moving average (SMA) to spot trends and a 200-period SMA to confirm the trend’s strength.

  • Average Directional Index (ADX): The ADX indicator helps determine the strength of a trend. An ADX reading above 25 usually indicates a strong trend. If ADX is below 20, it signals a weak or non-existent trend, meaning traders should be cautious about entering trades.

  • Directional Movement System: This includes the +DI (positive directional indicator) and -DI (negative directional indicator), which work together to show the direction of a trend and its strength. These indicators are helpful in spotting high-probability trades by identifying trends with strong momentum.

2. Look for Breakouts from Consolidation

A consolidation phase occurs when the market trades within a narrow range for an extended period of time. Breakouts from consolidation can provide excellent trend-following opportunities because they signal a shift in market sentiment.

To identify breakouts, traders should monitor:

  • Support and Resistance Levels: These are the price levels where the market has historically reversed direction. A breakout occurs when the price moves beyond these levels, often leading to the start of a new trend.

  • Triangle Patterns: These chart patterns form when the price moves within converging trendlines. A breakout from a triangle pattern indicates that a strong trend is likely to develop.

  • Flags and Pennants: These continuation patterns indicate a brief pause in an ongoing trend, followed by a strong continuation in the direction of the trend. The breakout from these patterns is often a low-risk entry point.

3. Fibonacci Retracements and Extensions

Fibonacci retracements are used to identify potential reversal levels during a trend pullback. Traders use Fibonacci levels (23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%) to spot areas where the price might reverse and resume the trend. These retracement levels can be used to enter trades at lower prices while the trend is still intact.

  • Fibonacci Extensions are helpful in setting profit targets once a trend has resumed. The common extension levels include 161.8%, 261.8%, and 423.6%. These levels are often used to project where the price might go after breaking the previous swing high or low.

By combining Fibonacci retracements with other trend indicators, traders can reduce the risk of entering trades too early or too late, and instead, enter at more optimal price levels.

4. Utilize Risk Management

To ensure that your trend following setups are low-risk, it’s essential to implement solid risk management strategies. Here are some key tactics:

  • Position Sizing: Determine the amount of capital to risk on each trade. A common rule is to risk no more than 1-2% of your capital per trade. This prevents large losses from wiping out your account.

  • Stop Loss Orders: Place stop losses just below key support levels for long trades or above key resistance levels for short trades. This ensures that if the market reverses, your losses are limited.

  • Risk/Reward Ratio: Always aim for a risk/reward ratio of at least 1:2. This means that for every dollar you risk, you should target a reward of two dollars. This allows you to be profitable even if your win rate is below 50%.

5. Combine Multiple Timeframes

Using multiple timeframes can help you confirm the direction and strength of the trend. For instance, a trader might look at the daily chart to identify the primary trend, and then use the 4-hour or 1-hour chart to spot a more precise entry.

A strong trend on a higher timeframe (such as the daily or weekly) combined with a favorable entry signal on a lower timeframe can provide an excellent low-risk setup. This technique ensures that traders are following the larger trend while also pinpointing precise entry points.

Practical Example: Trend Following in Action

Let’s break down a real-world example of a low risk, high reward trend following setup.

Imagine you are analyzing a stock chart with the following conditions:

  1. The stock has been in an uptrend for several months, confirmed by the price being above the 50-period and 200-period moving averages.
  2. The stock is currently consolidating within a narrow range, forming a triangle pattern.
  3. The ADX is above 25, indicating a strong trend.
  4. Fibonacci retracement levels show that the price is currently testing the 38.2% retracement level.

At this point, you identify a breakout above the triangle’s upper trendline. You place a buy order just above the breakout point, with a stop loss below the lower trendline of the consolidation pattern.

Your reward target is set at the next significant resistance level, which aligns with the 161.8% Fibonacci extension level.

This setup offers:

  • Low Risk: The stop loss is placed just below the consolidation range, limiting potential losses if the breakout fails.
  • High Reward: The breakout leads to an immediate surge, and your reward target is far above the entry, providing a good risk/reward ratio.

Key Takeaways for Low Risk High Reward Trend Following

  1. Identify clear trends using reliable indicators such as moving averages, ADX, and price action.
  2. Look for consolidation breakouts or continuation patterns like flags and pennants to enter the market at the optimal time.
  3. Use Fibonacci retracements and extensions to manage entry and exit points effectively.
  4. Manage risk by using appropriate position sizing, stop losses, and a favorable risk/reward ratio.
  5. Utilize multiple timeframes to ensure alignment between short-term and long-term trends.

Incorporating these techniques will allow you to create low risk, high reward trend following setups that can significantly improve your chances of long-term trading success. By focusing on trend-following opportunities with favorable risk/reward ratios, you can build a strategy that not only reduces the potential for large losses but also maximizes the chances of catching significant price moves.
