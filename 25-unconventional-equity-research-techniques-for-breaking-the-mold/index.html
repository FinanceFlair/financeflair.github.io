25 Unconventional Equity Research Techniques for Breaking the Mold


Equity research is a critical part of the investment process, but sometimes traditional methods just don’t cut it. As the market evolves, so must the techniques used to analyze companies and industries. In this post, we’ll explore 25 unconventional equity research techniques to help you stay ahead of the curve and uncover unique investment opportunities.

  1. Online Sentiment Analysis

Social media and online forums offer a wealth of unstructured data about companies and their industries. Sentiment analysis tools can parse through this data to find the underlying tone of conversations about a specific company. This can provide insight into market sentiment and potential future trends.

  1. Twitterstock Analysis

Tracking Twitter handles that can be associated with a company, as well as relevant industry hashtags, can yield real-time insights. This method allows you to sniff out rumors, news, and opinions before they make their way to mainstream media.

  1. Text Mining and Topic Modelling

Text mining involves sifting through large volumes of text to extract useful information, while topic modelling can uncover hidden themes within this data. These methods can be applied to company filings, earnings calls, and news articles to gain a deeper understanding of a company’s operations and strategy.

  1. Alternative Data Sources

Dive into alternative data sources such as satellite imagery, weather data, and even movie trailers. These unconventional sources can reveal unique insights into a company’s operations, consumer behavior, or market trends.

  1. Patent Analysis

Investigating a company’s patent portfolio can provide valuable insights into its innovation capabilities, competitive advantages, and potential future growth prospects.

  1. AI-powered Financial Analysis Tools

Leverage AI-powered financial analysis tools to uncover hidden relationships and patterns in financial data. These tools can help identify undervalued companies, predict future earnings, and assess credit risks.

  1. Non-financial Metrics

Look beyond traditional financial metrics when analyzing a company. Consider non-financial metrics such as customer satisfaction, employee turnover, and environmental footprint. These can give you a more holistic view of a company’s performance and future growth potential.

  1. Company Culture Analysis

A company’s culture can have a significant impact on its long-term success. Research a company’s management style, employee reviews, and industry reputation to get a sense of its corporate culture.

  1. Web Scraping

Web scraping is the process of extracting data from websites. This technique can be used to collect information on companies that may not be readily available through traditional channels.

  1. Integrating ESG Factors

Environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors have become increasingly important in equity research. By incorporating ESG data into your analysis, you can identify companies with attractive growth prospects and lower risk profiles.

  1. Trade Secret Analysis

Evaluate a company’s trade secrets, such as proprietary technology or unique business processes. These can represent valuable assets that may not be reflected in financial statements.

  1. Economic Path Analysis

Economic path analysis involves assessing the impact of various economic scenarios on a company’s financial performance. By considering different macroeconomic conditions, you can better understand a company’s resilience and identify potential tailwinds or headwinds.

  1. Geological and Metallurgical Analysis

For companies involved in mining or resource extraction, geological and metallurgical analysis can provide valuable insights into potential reserves and operational efficiency.

  1. Deep Diligence

Deep diligence involves diving deep into a company’s operations, interviewing employees, and visiting their facilities. This hands-on approach can reveal critical nuances that may not be evident from a top-down perspective.

  1. Sentiment Analysis of Management Discussions

Listen to management discussions about earnings calls and investor conferences. Sentiment analysis can help you gauge management confidence, growth expectations, and potential concerns.

  1. Competitor Analysis beyond the Obvious

Don’t just analyze direct competitors- assess indirect competitors, potential disruptors, and emerging players in related industries. This can help you gain a better understanding of market dynamics and identify potential partnership opportunities.

  1. Industry Innovation Curve Analysis

Track the innovation curve of the industry in which a company operates. By understanding where their industry is positioned along the innovation curve, you can better assess their competitive advantage, growth potential, and potential for disruption.

  1. Consumer Survey Analysis

Directly engage with consumers through surveys to gather information about their needs, preferences, and attitudes towards companies and products. This kind of feedback can provide valuable insights into market trends and potential future growth drivers.

  1. Unstructured Capital Expenditure Analysis

Examine a company’s unstructured capital expenditure, such as acquisitions and other non-routine investments. This can help you assess a company’s strategic focus, growth potential, and risk profile.

  1. Retail Sentiment Analysis

Track retail investor sentiment through forums, social media, and stock picking platforms. This can provide early signals about shifts in market sentiment and potential future trends.

  1. Credit Analyst Techniques for Equity Analysis

Credit analyst techniques, such as dueling credit metrics and scenario analysis, can be adapted for use in equity research. These techniques can help provide an additional layer of rigor and objectivity to your analysis.

  1. Ratio Analysis beyond the Standard

Experiment with unconventional ratio analysis techniques, like economic value added (EVA), free cash flow yield, and dividend payout ratios. These ratios can offer insight into a company’s true economic performance and valuation.

  1. Technographic Analysis

Assess a company’s technical capabilities through technographic analysis. This involves examining a company’s technology infrastructure, software usage, and IT spend to understand their potential for technological innovation and competitive advantage.

  1. Litigation Analysis

Review a company’s litigation history and regulatory filings. This can provide insight into potential operational risks and management integrity.

  1. Cynefin Framework Analysis

Apply the Cynefin Framework, which categorizes complex problems into four domains: simple, complication, complex, and chaotic. This framework can help you understand how to approach different types of research challenges and identify appropriate solutions.

Conclusion

Equity research is an ever-evolving field, and staying on top of the latest techniques and tools is crucial for success. By incorporating these 25 unconventional equity research techniques into your process, you’ll be well-equipped to uncover unique investment opportunities and stay ahead of the competition. Remember, the key to success is striking the right balance between embracing the unconventional and maintaining a disciplined, evidence-based approach.
