10 Savings Challenges That Actually Work


Saving money doesn’t have to feel like a burdensome task. In fact, turning your savings goals into a fun challenge can make the process not only manageable but also enjoyable. Financial strategies are most effective when they create a sense of momentum, and savings challenges are a perfect way to build that momentum while holding yourself accountable. Whether you’re saving for a vacation, a large expense, or just looking to build a more substantial emergency fund, these ten savings challenges are proven to work.

1. The 52-Week Savings Challenge

The 52-week savings challenge is one of the most popular and effective ways to build your savings over the course of a year. Here’s how it works: Each week of the year, you save an amount corresponding to the week number. For example, in Week 1, you save $1; in Week 2, you save $2, and so on. By Week 52, you’ll save $52 for that week. At the end of the year, you’ll have accumulated $1,378.

To make this challenge even more effective, consider switching it up to match your financial situation. If you have a larger income, you can double the weekly contributions or reverse the order so you’re saving the higher amounts earlier in the year when motivation is strongest.

2. The Spare Change Challenge

The spare change challenge doesn’t require a specific amount of money or schedule, making it an incredibly flexible savings option. Each time you receive change from a cash transaction or small unspent funds from your budget, place them into a designated jar or savings account. While this might seem like a slow way to save, it adds up surprisingly fast over time.

To supercharge this challenge, consider going digital. Many banks and apps offer round-up features, where purchases made on your connected debit or credit card are rounded up to the nearest dollar, and the difference is saved automatically.

3. The No-Spend Challenge

This challenge takes a direct approach to saving by asking you to stop spending money on non-essential purchases for a set amount of time. Whether you go for a single weekend, a whole month, or plan periodic no-spend weeks, the goal is to identify discretionary spending and channel that money into savings instead.

To succeed in this challenge, start by defining your essential expenses–like rent, utilities, groceries, and transportation–and create strict boundaries around what you won’t purchase during the challenge period. Use the opportunity to practice mindfulness around spending and reflect on what purchases truly add long-term value to your life.

4. The $5 Bill Challenge

This challenge is as straightforward as it is effective. Each time you receive a $5 bill as change, set it aside in an envelope or money jar. Over time, these small amounts add up considerably.

This challenge works because it’s simple yet consistent. Since $5 bills are common in everyday transactions, the challenge keeps your savings active without feeling restrictive. At the end of the challenge period, you’ll likely be surprised by how much you’ve accumulated.

5. The 30-Day Rule Challenge

The 30-day rule is more of a mindset challenge than a structured savings goal, but it’s one of the most impactful strategies for impulse buyers. The premise is simple: Whenever you want to make a non-essential purchase, wait 30 days.

If, after the waiting period, you still feel the purchase is worth it and can fit it comfortably into your budget, then go ahead. In most cases, however, the initial urge diminishes, and you can reroute that unspent money into savings. This challenge not only helps you save but also builds awareness around your shopping habits.

6. The Pantry Challenge

This challenge is perfect for cutting back on food expenses while reducing waste. The pantry challenge involves committing to eating as much as possible from the food you already have in your pantry, refrigerator, and freezer before buying groceries.

Take stock of everything you have, then get creative with recipes to stretch these ingredients. The money you would have spent on additional groceries can then go directly into your savings. You might even learn new budgeting strategies for meal planning along the way.

7. The Incremental Savings Challenge

This is a customizable approach that lets you increase your savings gradually. For example, you could start with an initial amount of $1 in Week 1 and increase your savings amount by $1 each week (Week 2 = $2, Week 3 = $3, and so on). By Week 52, you’ll be setting aside $52 for that week alone. The slow increase makes the challenge less daunting, and by the end of the year, you’ll have saved $1,378.

A variation on this challenge is to set a specific deadline and shorter timeline, such as a 26-week incremental savings challenge, which makes it more suitable for meeting short-term savings needs.

8. The Expense-Specific Savings Challenge

If you’re saving for a particular expense–such as a vacation, holiday shopping, or home improvement project–tailor your challenge to match your goal. Set a realistic savings target and break it into weekly or monthly contributions.

For example, if you want to save $2,000 for a trip six months from now, you’ll need to save approximately $334 per month, or about $77 per week. Seeing your progress toward a specific goal can be a powerful motivator.

9. The Subscription Audit Challenge

Many people lose money on subscriptions and memberships they no longer use, from streaming services to gym memberships. Using this challenge, take a month to identify and cancel subscriptions you don’t need, and reroute that money into savings.

As a bonus, revisit this challenge a few times a year to catch anything you may have missed or new subscriptions you’ve forgotten about.

10. The Envelope Savings Challenge

The envelope savings challenge involves writing different cash amounts on a series of envelopes–for example, $1, $5, $10, all the way up to $100, depending on your budget. Each week, you draw a random envelope and deposit the stated amount inside.

This challenge gamifies your savings and introduces a fun element of surprise, which keeps it engaging. By the end of the set timeframe, whether it’s a season or an entire year, you’ll have saved a substantial amount of money without feeling overwhelmed.

Making the Most of Your Savings Challenges

While each of these challenges has its unique advantages, the key to success lies in consistency. Savings challenges work best when you approach them with a clear goal in mind and a willingness to stick with them through ups and downs.

  • Track Your Progress: Consider using a spreadsheet, app, or even physical charts to visualize your progress. Regularly checking in with your savings can bolster your motivation.
  • Automate Where Possible: If your challenge allows it, automate your savings contributions through your bank or app. Automation removes the risk of forgetting to save.
  • Celebrate Milestones: Reward yourself when you hit important benchmarks. This doesn’t necessarily mean spending more money, but rather acknowledging your success with meaningful, low-cost celebrations.

Whether you follow one or multiple challenges simultaneously, these strategies can transform your savings journey into an empowering experience. Stay committed, and you’ll be amazed at how much you can achieve.
