The Best Trend Following Resources for Professional Traders


In the world of professional trading, the key to sustained profitability often lies in identifying and following trends. Whether you’re navigating the volatile waters of the stock market, forex, commodities, or cryptocurrencies, trend following is a powerful strategy that has helped countless traders achieve success. To master this method, however, requires more than just knowledge—it requires access to the right resources. In this post, we’ll explore some of the most effective trend-following resources available to professional traders. These tools, educational platforms, and software can help elevate your trading game, streamline your analysis, and provide a robust foundation for identifying profitable trends.

1. Trend Following Books for In-Depth Knowledge

Books have long been the cornerstone of learning for traders seeking to understand market movements, and the trend-following strategy is no exception. Several well-regarded books provide insights into trend analysis, the psychology behind trends, and how to harness market momentum.

“Trend Following: Learn to Make Millions in Up or Down Markets” by Michael Covel

Michael Covel’s “Trend Following” is considered the definitive guide to the trend-following strategy. This book offers practical advice from some of the world’s most successful traders who have made billions by capitalizing on market trends. It explores the philosophy of trend following, covering everything from basic principles to advanced tactics. Covel’s research into the success stories of legendary traders provides valuable insights, and the book is a must-read for anyone looking to develop a disciplined approach to identifying and following trends.

“The Little Book of Trend Following” by Michael W. Covel

For traders who prefer a concise and easy-to-read version of the principles laid out in his earlier work, Covel’s “The Little Book of Trend Following” is a great option. This book breaks down the complex ideas behind trend following in simple, digestible terms. It explains how to recognize trends, when to enter trades, and how to manage risk effectively, all while using examples from successful traders. It’s perfect for both beginners and seasoned professionals.

“The Complete TurtleTrader: How 23 Novice Traders Became Overnight Millionaires” by Michael W. Covel

Another gem by Michael Covel, “The Complete TurtleTrader,” recounts the story of how a group of novices became successful trend followers by following a strict system. It’s a fascinating narrative that not only provides insights into the psychology of trend followers but also showcases the importance of sticking to a system and maintaining discipline in trading.

From platforms offering comprehensive trading education to individual courses focusing on specific strategies, the internet is rife with options for traders at all levels.

The platform covers a wide range of topics, including technical analysis and trend-following strategies. Investopedia Academy’s courses are backed by expert instructors and provide learners with a comprehensive understanding of the concepts behind trend following, making it a valuable resource for professionals looking to refine their trading skills.

Udemy

Udemy hosts a wide variety of trading courses, many of which are focused on trend-following methods. Some of the courses are taught by professional traders and feature live market examples and in-depth tutorials. Udemy’s courses are affordable and cover different approaches to trend following, making it a fantastic option for traders at any skill level.

3. Trend Following Software Tools and Platforms

In professional trading, relying on manual methods for trend analysis can be cumbersome and prone to error. Thankfully, there are several software tools designed to automate and simplify trend-following strategies, making it easier for traders to identify trends and execute trades efficiently.

MetaTrader 4/5

MetaTrader is perhaps the most widely used trading platform among professional traders. It allows users to access a variety of tools for trend analysis, such as moving averages, trend lines, and oscillators. The software is highly customizable, enabling traders to set up automated trend-following strategies using Expert Advisors (EAs). This makes MetaTrader 4 and 5 ideal for traders who want to automate their trend-following methods and reduce the time spent manually analyzing the markets.

NinjaTrader

NinjaTrader is another powerful platform that is particularly popular among futures and forex traders. It offers advanced charting tools and trend-following indicators, such as the Average Directional Index (ADX) and the Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) indicator. The platform’s built-in tools are excellent for identifying both short-term and long-term trends, and it also supports algorithmic trading, allowing traders to implement automated trend-following strategies.

TradeStation

TradeStation is known for its robust charting capabilities and professional-grade trading tools. It provides an array of customizable indicators and a powerful backtesting platform, making it ideal for trend-following traders. The platform also supports automated trading strategies, allowing traders to program and execute their trend-following rules with ease.

5. Technical Indicators and Tools for Trend Analysis

To effectively follow trends, you need the right technical indicators. These tools help you analyze price movements and market conditions, providing clear signals for when a trend is developing or reversing. Here are a few essential indicators used by trend-following traders.

Moving Averages

Moving averages, such as the Simple Moving Average (SMA) and Exponential Moving Average (EMA), are the most basic tools for identifying trends. By smoothing out price data, moving averages help traders identify the general direction of the market. Traders often use a combination of short-term and long-term moving averages to confirm trend direction and potential entry points.

Average Directional Index (ADX)

The ADX is an important indicator that measures the strength of a trend. Values above 25 typically indicate a strong trend, while values below 20 suggest a weak or choppy market. ADX is often used in combination with other trend-following indicators to determine the strength and sustainability of a trend.

Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

The MACD is a versatile indicator used by trend-following traders to spot potential trend reversals and momentum shifts. The MACD consists of two moving averages (the fast and slow lines), and when these lines cross, it can signal a change in market direction. It’s a popular tool for spotting both short-term and long-term trends.

6. Backtesting and Simulation Tools for Trend Followers

Backtesting is a crucial part of trend-following strategies. By testing your trend-following methods on historical data, you can identify what works and what doesn’t, helping you refine your strategy before applying it to live markets.

TradingView

TradingView is one of the best charting platforms for trend-following traders. It allows users to backtest their strategies using historical price data and provides advanced charting tools. Its intuitive interface and social features, such as the ability to share ideas and strategies with other traders, make it a powerful tool for both beginners and professionals.

QuantConnect

QuantConnect is a cutting-edge algorithmic trading platform that allows you to develop, backtest, and deploy trend-following strategies using a wide variety of historical data. The platform supports multiple asset classes, including stocks, futures, and forex, making it ideal for traders who want to test their strategies in different markets. QuantConnect is also a great resource for algorithmic traders looking to implement automated trend-following systems.

MetaTrader 4/5 Backtesting

MetaTrader’s built-in backtesting functionality is a powerful tool for testing trend-following strategies. By using historical data, you can simulate how your strategy would have performed in different market conditions. This allows you to optimize your approach and make necessary adjustments before live trading.

Conclusion

Mastering trend following is a journey that requires continuous learning, the right tools, and consistent practice. The resources mentioned in this post are some of the best available for professional traders looking to improve their trend-following strategies. From insightful books and online courses to advanced trading platforms and tools, there’s no shortage of resources that can help you sharpen your skills and stay ahead of market trends. By leveraging these resources, you can develop a disciplined approach to trading that will increase your chances of success in the dynamic and ever-changing world of professional trading.
