How to Choose the Right Real Asset Investment Manager


Investing in real assets has become a key strategy for many investors seeking stability, long-term growth, and diversification in their portfolios. Real assets, such as real estate, infrastructure, commodities, and natural resources, can serve as a hedge against inflation and provide steady returns in volatile markets. However, selecting the right real asset investment manager is critical to achieving these benefits. This post will guide you through the process of choosing the right professional to manage your real asset investments and highlight the key factors to consider.

Understanding Real Assets

Before delving into how to select the right investment manager, it’s important to have a solid understanding of what constitutes real assets. These are tangible assets that have intrinsic value due to their substance or properties. Real estate, for example, is a physical property that can provide both income and appreciation over time. Similarly, commodities like oil, gold, and agricultural products are essential resources with inherent value.

Types of Real Assets

  1. Real Estate: Residential, commercial, and industrial properties fall under this category. Investing in real estate can offer long-term growth potential through appreciation, rental income, or both.

  2. Infrastructure: This includes assets like toll roads, bridges, airports, and utility systems. Infrastructure investments often provide stable, predictable cash flows.

  3. Commodities: These are raw materials or primary agricultural products that can be bought and sold. Commodities include gold, oil, natural gas, and agricultural products like wheat or corn.

  4. Natural Resources: This includes forestry, mining, and energy assets. Investment in natural resources can yield profits from the extraction and sale of these commodities.

The goal when investing in real assets is to capitalize on their ability to retain or increase in value over time. They provide essential diversification from traditional equities and bonds, which makes them an attractive addition to many portfolios.

The Importance of an Investment Manager

When dealing with real assets, it is crucial to choose an investment manager who not only understands these asset classes but also knows how to navigate the complexities of the market. A skilled manager will help you mitigate risks, identify investment opportunities, and develop a strategy tailored to your specific financial goals.

Choosing the right manager can significantly impact your investment performance. The wrong choice could lead to missed opportunities, underperformance, or even losses. Therefore, understanding the qualities and qualifications to look for in a real asset investment manager is essential.

Key Factors to Consider When Choosing an Investment Manager

1. Experience and Expertise

Real assets are distinct from traditional investments like stocks and bonds, and they require specialized knowledge. Your investment manager should have extensive experience in managing real asset portfolios. Look for a manager who has demonstrated expertise in the specific type of real asset you’re interested in, whether it’s real estate, commodities, or infrastructure.

2. Track Record

A strong track record of success is an indicator of a manager’s ability to generate returns in various market conditions. Ask potential managers for their performance history, and evaluate how they’ve navigated through market downturns or periods of economic uncertainty. A long history of consistent results, particularly during periods of volatility, is a good sign that they can manage risks effectively and deliver solid returns.

3. Investment Philosophy and Strategy

Every investment manager has their own philosophy and approach to managing real assets. Some might take a more conservative approach, focusing on income-producing assets with stable, predictable cash flows. Others might be more aggressive, targeting high-growth opportunities with greater risk. It’s crucial that their investment strategy aligns with your risk tolerance, financial goals, and time horizon.

For instance, if your goal is long-term wealth accumulation, you might prefer a manager with a strategy that emphasizes capital appreciation through investments in high-growth real estate or infrastructure projects. On the other hand, if you’re more interested in preserving wealth and generating income, a manager focused on stable, income-generating assets like rental properties or utility investments might be a better fit.

4. Transparency and Communication

A good investment manager should be transparent about their processes, fees, and investment decisions. They should provide regular updates on portfolio performance and be accessible to answer any questions you may have. Open communication is key to building trust and ensuring that your investments are being managed in line with your expectations.

Transparency also means that they are willing to explain why they make certain investment decisions and how those decisions align with your goals. Be wary of managers who are vague about their strategies or reluctant to provide detailed reports. The best managers will proactively provide performance updates and be available for periodic meetings to discuss the status of your investments.

5. Risk Management Approach

Real assets can be highly volatile, particularly commodities and natural resources, which are influenced by global supply and demand dynamics, geopolitical events, and environmental factors. An effective investment manager should have a clear risk management strategy in place to mitigate potential losses.

Ask how the manager approaches risk, and whether they use hedging strategies, diversification, or other techniques to protect your investments. Look for managers who incorporate risk management as a core part of their investment process and who adjust strategies based on market conditions.

6. Fee Structure

Investment managers charge fees for their services, and these fees can vary widely depending on the type of assets being managed and the manager’s level of expertise. Ensure you fully understand the fee structure before committing. Some managers may charge a flat fee, while others may take a percentage of the assets under management or performance-based fees that are tied to investment returns.

Be cautious of managers who charge high fees without a clear justification for the value they bring. A manager’s fee structure should be competitive but also reflect the quality of service, expertise, and access to exclusive investment opportunities that they provide.

7. Regulatory Compliance and Reputation

Ensure that the investment manager is properly licensed and regulated by the relevant financial authorities. In the U.S., for example, investment managers should be registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) or a state regulator. Regulatory compliance is crucial because it provides a layer of security and ensures that the manager adheres to industry standards and ethical guidelines.

Additionally, investigate the manager’s reputation in the industry. Check for any disciplinary actions or complaints filed against them. A manager with a strong reputation will likely have positive reviews from clients and peers alike.

8. Network and Resources

A successful investment manager often has access to a vast network of industry contacts, including developers, brokers, analysts, and other professionals who can provide valuable insights and deal flow. Look for a manager who has established relationships within the real asset space and can leverage these connections to find lucrative investment opportunities that might not be available to the average investor.

A manager who works with a team of professionals, including legal experts, property managers, and financial analysts, is also likely to be better equipped to handle the complexities of managing real assets. A strong network and the ability to tap into resources can lead to more informed decisions and, ultimately, better investment outcomes.

9. Geographic and Sector Expertise

Real asset markets can vary significantly depending on the location and sector. For example, the performance of real estate investments may differ greatly between urban and rural areas, or between regions within a country. Similarly, the dynamics of the energy sector can vary depending on geopolitical events or changes in global demand.

Choose a manager with expertise in the specific geographic regions or sectors you are interested in. For example, if you want to invest in commercial real estate in Asia, it would be beneficial to work with a manager who understands the local markets and regulatory environment.

10. Alignment of Interests

Finally, ensure that the investment manager’s interests are aligned with yours. Some managers may invest their own capital alongside yours, which can help ensure they are motivated to achieve the best possible outcomes for your portfolio. Others may offer performance-based fees that incentivize them to generate superior returns.

Conclusion

Choosing the right real asset investment manager is crucial to achieving your investment objectives. By considering factors such as experience, investment strategy, risk management, transparency, and fees, you can make an informed decision that aligns with your financial goals. The right manager will not only help you navigate the complexities of real asset markets but will also add value through expertise, a strong track record, and access to unique investment opportunities.

Remember, investing in real assets requires patience and a long-term perspective. By selecting a manager who understands your needs and can manage risks effectively, you can ensure that your investments are positioned for success over time. Take your time to evaluate potential managers thoroughly and don’t hesitate to ask questions that help you understand their approach to managing real asset portfolios.
