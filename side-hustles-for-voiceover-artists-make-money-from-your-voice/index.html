Side Hustles for Voiceover Artists: Make Money from Your Voice


If you’re a voiceover artist looking to expand your income streams and make the most of your unique talent, you’re in luck! There are plenty of side hustles out there that can help you monetize your voice and boost your earnings. In this blog post, we’ll explore some exciting opportunities for voiceover artists to make money from their voice.

Audiobook Narration

Audiobooks have been gaining popularity in recent years, and there’s a growing demand for talented voiceover artists to bring stories to life. As a voiceover artist, you have the skills to captivate listeners and immerse them in the world of the story. By narrating audiobooks, you can tap into this booming market and showcase your talent to a wider audience. Platforms like Audible ACX and Findaway Voices connect narrators with authors and publishers, making it easier than ever to get started.

E-Learning and Corporate Training

The e-learning industry is thriving, and voiceover artists are in high demand to provide engaging and professional narration for online courses and corporate training materials. From instructional videos and language courses to employee training modules, there’s a wide range of opportunities for voiceover artists to lend their voices to educational content. Websites like Upwork and Freelancer can connect you with clients looking for talented narrators in the e-learning space.

Commercials and Radio Ads

Voiceover artists have long been the voice behind commercials and radio ads, and this remains a lucrative side hustle. By lending your voice to advertising campaigns, you can reach a large audience and showcase your versatility as a voiceover artist. Ad agencies and production companies are always on the lookout for fresh voices, so don’t hesitate to reach out and pitch your services. Consider creating a demo reel that showcases your range and style to attract potential clients.

Podcasting

Podcasts have exploded in popularity in recent years, and with that comes a need for captivating voices to host and narrate shows. If you have a passion for a particular subject or enjoy interviewing guests, starting your own podcast can be a great way to showcase your voiceover skills while building a dedicated audience. Additionally, many podcasters are willing to pay voiceover artists to create intros, outros, and advertisements for their shows. Platforms like Anchor and Libsyn can help you get started with hosting and distributing your podcast.

Telephone IVR and Voice Prompts

Have you ever called a customer service hotline or navigated an automated phone system? Those voice prompts and IVR (Interactive Voice Response) systems are often recorded by voiceover artists. Many businesses are in need of professional voice recordings to enhance their customer experience. Websites like Voices.com and Voice123 connect voiceover artists with clients looking to hire voices for telephone IVR and voice prompt recordings.

Video Games and Animation

If you have a flair for character voices and acting, consider venturing into the world of video games and animation. Voiceover artists play a vital role in bringing characters to life in video games and animated films. From voicing heroes to villains, the possibilities are endless. Platforms like Casting Call Club and Voice Acting Club can connect you with indie game developers and animators who are looking for talented voiceover artists.

Final Thoughts

As a voiceover artist, you possess a unique and valuable skill that can open doors to exciting side hustles and income opportunities. Whether it’s narrating audiobooks, lending your voice to commercials, or hosting your own podcast, there are numerous avenues to explore. Remember to showcase your talent, network with industry professionals, and continuously improve your craft. With determination and perseverance, you can turn your voice into a profitable side hustle and enjoy a fulfilling career in the world of voiceover artistry.
