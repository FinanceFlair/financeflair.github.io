The Best Credit Cards for Balance Transfers: Save on Interest


When it comes to managing debt, one of the most effective strategies is a balance transfer. If you’re currently carrying high-interest credit card debt, transferring your balance to a card with a lower or 0% introductory APR can provide you with a way to save money and pay off your debt faster. However, with so many balance transfer offers on the market, choosing the best card for your specific needs can be challenging. In this post, we’ll explore the top options for credit cards designed to help you save on interest and reduce your debt faster.

What is a Balance Transfer?

Before diving into the best credit card options, it’s important to understand what a balance transfer entails. A balance transfer occurs when you move the debt from one credit card to another with better terms, usually a lower interest rate. This can be an ideal way to pay down existing balances on high-interest credit cards. Many cards offer 0% interest for a set period, sometimes as long as 18 to 21 months. This introductory offer allows you to make payments toward your principal balance without accruing additional interest, which can lead to significant savings.

Factors to Consider When Choosing a Balance Transfer Card

When selecting a credit card for a balance transfer, there are several factors to consider. These can make a huge difference in how much you save over the long term. Let’s break down the key elements:

1. Introductory APR Period

One of the most important aspects of a balance transfer card is the length of the introductory 0% APR offer. The longer the 0% interest period, the more time you’ll have to pay off your balance without incurring any interest. Many cards offer 0% APR for anywhere from 12 to 21 months, but some offer even longer periods. Opting for a card with a long introductory period is critical to maximizing your savings.

2. Balance Transfer Fees

While a 0% APR offer may seem like the best deal, it’s essential to pay attention to the fees associated with transferring a balance. Most balance transfer cards charge a fee, typically between 3% and 5% of the transferred amount. Although this fee can seem significant, it’s still worth considering if the overall interest savings are substantial enough to outweigh the fee.

3. Ongoing APR After the Introductory Period

After the 0% APR promotional period expires, the APR will revert to the standard rate, which can be much higher. Make sure to compare the post-promotional APRs across different cards to ensure that it’s still a competitive rate, especially if you expect to carry a balance after the introductory period ends.

4. Credit Limit

Credit limit is another key consideration when choosing a balance transfer card. If you have a large amount of debt to transfer, you’ll need to ensure that the card you choose offers a high enough credit limit to accommodate your balance. Some cards have more flexible limits based on your creditworthiness, so it’s essential to choose a card that aligns with your needs.

5. Other Perks and Rewards

While the primary purpose of a balance transfer card is to help you pay off debt, many cards also come with additional perks and rewards. Some cards offer cashback, travel rewards, or purchase protection, which can be beneficial if you plan on using the card for new purchases after completing the balance transfer.

Top Balance Transfer Cards in 2025

Here are some of the best credit cards for balance transfers available in 2025, offering attractive introductory APRs, low fees, and solid rewards.

1. Chase Freedom Unlimited®

The Chase Freedom Unlimited® card offers a competitive balance transfer option with a 0% intro APR for the first 15 months on balance transfers (and purchases). After the introductory period ends, the variable APR jumps to 19.24%–27.99%. While the balance transfer fee is 5% (or $5, whichever is greater), the 0% APR for 15 months can give you ample time to pay off your balance without paying any interest.

In addition to the introductory offer, the card also provides cashback on everyday purchases, with 1.5% cashback on every purchase, 5% on travel through Chase Ultimate Rewards, and 3% on dining and drugstore purchases.

Pros:

  • 0% APR for 15 months on balance transfers.
  • Cashback rewards on various purchases.
  • No annual fee.

Cons:

  • 5% balance transfer fee.
  • Higher APR after the introductory period.

2. Citi® Diamond Preferred® Card

The Citi® Diamond Preferred® Card is another excellent option for those looking to transfer high-interest balances. This card offers a 0% intro APR for 18 months on balance transfers (and purchases), providing more than a year and a half of interest-free payments. The balance transfer fee is 5% (or $5, whichever is greater), which is standard for many cards in this category. After the 18-month introductory period, the APR jumps to 17.24%–28.99% (variable).

The Citi® Diamond Preferred® Card doesn’t offer any rewards programs, but its lengthy introductory period makes it ideal for someone who needs a long window to pay down their balance.

Pros:

  • 0% APR for 18 months on balance transfers.
  • No annual fee.
  • Long 0% APR period.

Cons:

  • 5% balance transfer fee.
  • No rewards program.

3. Discover it® Balance Transfer

The Discover it® Balance Transfer card provides a solid 0% intro APR on balance transfers for 18 months. After the intro period, the APR ranges from 16.24%–27.24% (variable). Discover’s balance transfer fee is slightly more favorable than other cards, with a fee of 3% for the first 14 months and then a 5% fee after that.

Another unique feature of this card is that it offers cashback rewards, including 5% cashback in rotating categories (up to a quarterly maximum), and 1% cashback on all other purchases. Discover also provides the benefit of a “Cashback Match” at the end of your first year, where they’ll match all the cashback you’ve earned.

Pros:

  • 0% APR for 18 months on balance transfers.
  • 5% cashback in rotating categories.
  • Cashback Match at the end of the first year.
  • 3% balance transfer fee during the introductory period.

Cons:

  • Higher APR after the intro period.
  • 5% balance transfer fee after the introductory period.

4. BankAmericard® Credit Card

The BankAmericard® Credit Card is one of the simplest and most straightforward cards for balance transfers. With a 0% intro APR on balance transfers for the first 18 billing cycles, this card gives you plenty of time to pay off your debt interest-free. The balance transfer fee is 3% of the transfer amount or $10, whichever is greater. After the introductory period, the APR jumps to 16.24%–26.24% (variable).

Unlike some other balance transfer cards, the BankAmericard® Credit Card doesn’t offer rewards, but it’s a good option for someone who just wants to focus on paying down their debt with minimal distractions.

Pros:

  • 0% APR for 18 months on balance transfers.
  • Low balance transfer fee (3%).
  • No annual fee.

Cons:

  • No rewards program.
  • Higher APR after the introductory period.

5. Wells Fargo Reflect® Card

The Wells Fargo Reflect® Card offers one of the longest 0% intro APR periods for balance transfers, with 0% APR for the first 18 months on both balance transfers and purchases. After that, the APR jumps to 18.99%–26.99% (variable). The balance transfer fee is 3% during the first 120 days and 5% after that.

In addition to the lengthy intro period, the Wells Fargo Reflect® Card provides new cardholders with the benefit of 1.5% cashback on qualifying purchases, and it also has no annual fee.

How to Make the Most of Your Balance Transfer Card

Once you’ve selected a balance transfer card, it’s important to make the most of the 0% APR period to reduce your debt as efficiently as possible. Here are a few tips to ensure you stay on track:

1. Pay More Than the Minimum Payment

While it may be tempting to only make the minimum payment, this approach will extend the time it takes to pay off your balance and could result in you paying more in interest once the intro period expires. Try to pay as much as you can afford each month to pay off your balance before the 0% APR period ends.

2. Avoid New Purchases on the Card

Many balance transfer cards offer 0% APR on both transfers and purchases, but new purchases often do not benefit from the same 0% APR deal. It’s best to avoid making new purchases on your balance transfer card until the balance is paid off. If you must make a purchase, use a separate card or pay it off quickly.

3. Be Aware of the Balance Transfer Deadline

Each card will have specific terms related to the balance transfer period. Ensure you make your transfer before the introductory APR period ends, and be mindful of any balance transfer fees or restrictions.

Conclusion

Balance transfer credit cards can be an excellent tool to help you pay off debt more quickly by taking advantage of low or 0% interest rates. With many options available, it’s important to compare introductory periods, balance transfer fees, and ongoing APR rates to find the best card for your financial situation. By choosing the right balance transfer card and following the tips provided, you can save significantly on interest and reduce your debt more efficiently.
