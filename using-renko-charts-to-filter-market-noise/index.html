Using Renko Charts to Filter Market Noise


In the vast world of technical analysis, traders continuously seek tools that bring clarity amidst the chaos of price fluctuations. One such way to achieve this is by using Renko charts, a powerful charting method designed to filter out market noise and highlight true price movement. Unlike conventional time-based charts, Renko charts offer a fresh perspective by isolating price action through bricks or blocks that represent fixed price increments, ignoring the passage of time entirely.

This comprehensive guide explores how Renko charts work, their advantages, and how traders can leverage them to improve decision-making and increase trading accuracy.

What Are Renko Charts?

Renko charts originated from Japan—the term “Renko” comes from the Japanese word “renga,” meaning “brick.” These brick-like bars form when the price moves by a specific amount, determined by the trader, regardless of how long that takes. This uniqueness helps reduce distractions caused by insignificant price fluctuations that commonly appear on typical candlestick or bar charts.

Instead of plotting price at fixed intervals (like every minute or daily), Renko charts plot a new brick only when the price surpasses the set brick size in a given direction — up or down. For example, with a brick size of 10 points, a new brick is added only if the price moves higher or lower by 10 points from the last brick’s close.

How Renko Charts Filter Market Noise

Market noise refers to minor, random price fluctuations that do not indicate direction or significant movement. These oscillations often confuse traders and lead to premature or misguided trades. Conventional charts, while valuable, are susceptible to these distractions because they are time-driven, capturing every uptick and downtick.

Renko charts provide noise reduction in several key ways:

  • Price-Driven Movement: Bricks only form when price exceeds the predetermined threshold. Minor retracements that do not reach the brick size create no new bars.

  • Time-Independent: Time is irrelevant; the chart only progresses when significant price movement occurs, ignoring sideways stagnation or slow volatility periods.

  • Clear Trend Visualization: The chart consists of consecutive bricks in one direction until a reversal surpasses the brick size. This clean alternation helps traders easily spot trending phases.

  • Simplified Entry and Exit Signals: Renko charts reduce false signals such as whipsaws seen in mainstream charts by requiring stronger price confirmation before signaling reversals.

Choosing the Right Brick Size

The magic of Renko charts lies heavily in selecting an appropriate brick size. This decision significantly affects the chart’s sensitivity and the amount of noise filtered.

  • Too Small a Brick Size: Leads to more frequent bricks, making the chart resemble traditional charts by capturing minor price movements and noise.

  • Too Large a Brick Size: Offers extreme noise filtering but may also cause delayed signals, missing some potentially profitable trades or early warnings.

  • Adaptive Brick Size: Some traders use volatility-based brick sizes — for instance, setting the brick size as a multiple of the Average True Range (ATR). This approach adjusts brick sizes dynamically according to market conditions.

The optimal brick size depends on the traded asset’s volatility, the timeframe, and the trader’s strategy.

Using Renko Charts for Trend Identification

One of the greatest strengths of Renko charts is their ability to make trend identification straightforward and intuitive.

  • Uptrend: Characterized by a series of consecutive upward (usually white or green) bricks, showing persistent buying pressure.

  • Downtrend: Series of consecutive downward (usually red or black) bricks indicate steady selling pressure.

  • Sideways/Neutral Market: Whipsawing between up and down bricks with no clear pattern suggests a lack of directional conviction.

By eliminating insignificant price changes, Renko charts help traders confidently recognize when a trend begins, continues, or reverses.

Combining Renko Charts with Technical Indicators

Renko charts work seamlessly with other technical tools to enhance trade signals.

  • Moving Averages: Overlaying a moving average on Renko charts can confirm the trend direction and help identify dynamic support/resistance.

  • Relative Strength Index (RSI): Using RSI helps detect overbought or oversold conditions alongside Renko’s trend clarity.

  • MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): MACD divergence on Renko charts can signal potential reversals with less noise interference.

  • Support and Resistance Levels: Price areas where bricks repeatedly stall or reverse serve as robust support and resistance on Renko charts.

This combination equips traders with multiple layers of verification, minimizing false signals and improving confidence.

Practical Strategies for Trading with Renko Charts

1. Trend Following Strategy

  • Identify a clear trend in the Renko chart (consecutive bricks in one direction).
  • Enter trades in the trend direction on the formation of a new brick.
  • Set stop-loss orders below the last opposite colored brick.
  • Use trailing stops or exit signals upon reversal bricks appearing.

2. Breakout Trading

  • Look for consolidation zones where brick size blocks move sideways.
  • Enter the trade when the price breaks out, forming several bricks in the breakout direction.
  • Confirm the breakout with volume spikes or volatility indicators for higher probability.

3. Reversal Trading

  • Monitor for divergence between Renko charts and technical indicators like RSI or MACD.
  • Spot reversal bricks that appear after extended trends.
  • Use tight stop-loss levels given the counter-trend risk.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Using Renko Charts

Advantages

  • Visual Simplicity: Easily spot trends without distraction.
  • Noise Reduction: Filters small fluctuations that can trigger false signals.
  • Objective Analysis: Brick size provides an objective metric for price movement.
  • Uniqueness: Offers a different perspective that complements traditional charts.

Disadvantages

  • Lagging Nature: Larger brick sizes might delay signal generation.
  • No Time Element: Lack of explicit time data can complicate time-sensitive trading decisions.
  • Limited Insight for Range-bound Markets: Less effective in sideways or low volatility phases.
  • Setup Complexity: Proper brick size selection requires testing and adaptation.

Implementing Renko Charts with Modern Trading Platforms

Many popular trading platforms and charting software support Renko charts, often with flexible brick size options and the ability to overlay popular indicators. For traders looking to automate or deepen their analysis journeys:

  • MetaTrader 4/5: Renko chart plugins or indicators are available for download.
  • TradingView: Offers built-in Renko charts with adjustable brick sizes.
  • NinjaTrader: Supports Renko charts as part of its extensive charting tools.
  • Thinkorswim: Allows customization of Renko charts under its chart settings.

Familiarizing oneself with these environments will smooth the learning curve and open many avenues for backtesting Renko-based strategies.

Conclusion: Filtering Market Noise for Smarter Trading

In the noisy, often overwhelming world of trading, tools like Renko charts provide an invaluable edge. By focusing exclusively on meaningful price movement and dismissing irrelevant fluctuations, Renko charts empower traders to make cleaner, more confident decisions. Properly applied, they enhance trend analysis, improve entry and exit timing, and complement other technical indicators for a robust approach to the markets.

While no chart or strategy is flawless, integrating Renko charts into your trading routine can bring a new level of clarity and precision, helping you tune out the noise and hear the market’s true rhythm.

Trading with Renko charts is not about replacing traditional methods but enriching your toolkit. With practice, experimentation, and a thoughtful approach to brick size settings, Renko charts can be a valuable ally in navigating today’s dynamic markets.
