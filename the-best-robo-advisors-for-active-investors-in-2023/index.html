The Best Robo-Advisors for Active Investors in 2023


As the investing landscape evolves, robo-advisors have increasingly become a popular tool for investors seeking efficient portfolio management, diversification, and the ability to automate investing strategies. While traditional investment methods still have their place, active investors are increasingly turning to robo-advisors for a variety of reasons—chief among them is the convenience and accessibility these platforms offer.

In 2023, robo-advisors have matured, offering sophisticated features that cater to the needs of both passive and active investors. Active investors, in particular, benefit from the ability to manage portfolios with more control, tailor risk levels, and even tweak strategies based on market changes, all while leveraging advanced algorithms and professional-grade insights.

In this post, we will explore some of the best robo-advisors available in 2023 for active investors, providing insights into their unique features, tools, and offerings that make them stand out in the crowded robo-advisory market.

1. Betterment

Betterment is one of the most well-known robo-advisors and offers excellent features for active investors. While it’s known for its passive investment strategies, it also provides customization options that are well-suited for active investors. The platform allows users to adjust asset allocations and offers both standard and flexible portfolios.

Key Features:

  • Flexible Portfolios: Betterment’s customizable portfolios allow active investors to tailor their investments based on their individual preferences and risk tolerance. Investors can modify the asset mix and allocate funds to specific types of investments.
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting: For tax-conscious investors, Betterment provides automatic tax-loss harvesting. This tool is especially beneficial for active investors looking to offset capital gains with losses, thus reducing their overall tax liability.
  • Retirement Planning Tools: Active investors with long-term goals can benefit from Betterment’s retirement planning features, which include a retirement savings calculator and automatic rebalancing.
  • Human Advisors: For those who want a little more guidance, Betterment offers access to human advisors, making it easy for active investors to seek personalized advice when needed.

Best For:

Active investors looking for a balance of automation and control. Betterment is especially suitable for investors who want to maintain a diversified portfolio while having the flexibility to make adjustments as market conditions change.

2. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is another top contender in the robo-advisor market, offering a wide range of features designed for active investors. Known for its innovative approach to investing, Wealthfront provides a combination of automated strategies with the flexibility to customize investments.

Key Features:

  • PassivePlus™: This feature allows active investors to make tactical adjustments to their portfolio. For example, Wealthfront allows for tactical exposure to different asset classes or regions without manually rebalancing the portfolio.
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting: Wealthfront’s tax-loss harvesting tool is automatic, helping investors minimize their tax burden by selling off losing assets to offset taxable gains.
  • Smart Beta ETFs: Wealthfront offers access to Smart Beta ETFs, which use a rules-based approach to invest in a way that potentially outperforms traditional market-cap weighted indexes. This is particularly attractive for active investors who want to increase their exposure to more sophisticated investment strategies.
  • Financial Planning Tools: Wealthfront’s comprehensive planning tools help active investors forecast their financial goals, manage savings, and track progress toward achieving objectives such as buying a home or funding retirement.

Best For:

Active investors looking for a combination of automated wealth management and sophisticated tools to implement strategic asset allocation and tax management. Wealthfront is particularly good for investors who want to minimize their tax liabilities while still having control over portfolio construction.

3. M1 Finance

M1 Finance has emerged as a top choice for active investors who want more control over their portfolios but still want the benefits of automation. What sets M1 Finance apart is its focus on blending automation with customization, giving investors the ability to build highly personalized portfolios.

Key Features:

  • Pie-Based Investment System: M1 Finance uses a pie chart-based system for portfolio management. Investors can create their own “pies” (portfolios) by selecting individual stocks, ETFs, or pre-built portfolios that align with their goals.
  • Fractional Shares: M1 Finance allows investors to buy fractional shares of stocks and ETFs, making it possible to precisely allocate funds to the most granular level. This is particularly helpful for active investors who want to fine-tune their asset allocation without being restricted by whole share pricing.
  • Automatic Rebalancing: While M1 Finance gives investors full control over their portfolio, it also provides automatic rebalancing to ensure that investments stay in line with the desired asset allocation.
  • Leverage: M1 Finance offers margin accounts, allowing active investors to borrow funds for additional investments. This can amplify returns, though it does come with higher risks.

Best For:

Active investors who want to maintain full control over their portfolios while still benefiting from automated features like rebalancing. M1 Finance is ideal for investors who want to customize their asset allocations down to the individual stock or ETF level and use leverage to enhance returns.

4. SoFi Invest

SoFi Invest is a well-rounded investment platform that offers both active trading options and automated investment strategies. Its robo-advisor service allows for a combination of active investment management and the convenience of automation, making it an attractive option for active investors.

Key Features:

  • Automated Portfolio Management: SoFi’s robo-advisor service offers automated portfolio management based on user preferences, including risk tolerance and financial goals. Active investors can customize their asset allocation and opt for a diversified portfolio that suits their strategy.
  • Active Trading Platform: In addition to its robo-advisor offerings, SoFi Invest also provides an active trading platform that allows investors to trade stocks, ETFs, and cryptocurrencies. This flexibility makes it an excellent choice for investors who want to both automate their long-term investments and engage in short-term trades.
  • No Management Fees: SoFi Invest does not charge management fees for its robo-advisor services, making it one of the most cost-effective options on the market for active investors.
  • Socially Responsible Investing (SRI): Active investors interested in sustainable and socially responsible investing can benefit from SoFi’s socially responsible investment options, which focus on companies that prioritize environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors.

Best For:

Active investors who want a hybrid experience—one that combines automated portfolio management with the ability to actively trade and engage in other forms of investment. SoFi Invest is also ideal for investors looking to minimize fees while maximizing investment opportunities in areas such as social responsibility.

5. Ellevest

Ellevest is a robo-advisor that focuses on gender-specific financial planning, with an emphasis on creating personalized portfolios based on the needs of women. However, the platform’s investment tools and services are suitable for active investors of all backgrounds. Ellevest’s approach combines automation with personalized strategies, making it an attractive option for those who want more control over their investments.

Key Features:

  • Goal-Based Investing: Ellevest’s platform is designed around goal-based investing, allowing active investors to create customized portfolios tailored to their specific financial objectives—whether that’s retirement, saving for education, or purchasing a home.
  • Personalized Investment Plans: Ellevest uses algorithms to create customized investment plans that align with individual goals, timelines, and risk tolerance.
  • Sustainable Investing: For active investors interested in socially responsible investing, Ellevest offers portfolios that focus on sustainable and impact-driven investments.
  • Human Advisors: For those who want more personalized advice, Ellevest offers access to human financial advisors who provide tailored guidance for your investment strategy.

Best For:

Active investors who want a highly personalized, goal-oriented approach to investing with access to human financial advisors. Ellevest’s focus on sustainable and gender-focused financial planning also makes it a unique option for those with specific ethical or social investment priorities.

6. Personal Capital

Personal Capital is a robust financial management platform that combines the benefits of a robo-advisor with the option to work with certified financial planners. While it is often used for retirement planning, its portfolio management tools and advanced analytics make it an excellent option for active investors.

Key Features:

  • Personalized Portfolio Management: Personal Capital offers custom-built portfolios based on an individual’s risk profile, time horizon, and investment goals. Active investors can modify their asset allocation as needed and fine-tune their portfolio.
  • Financial Tools and Insights: Beyond portfolio management, Personal Capital offers a comprehensive set of financial tools for budgeting, tracking expenses, and managing overall financial health. Active investors can use these tools to create a holistic financial plan.
  • Retirement Planning: Personal Capital’s retirement planning tools are some of the best in the industry. These tools help investors forecast their retirement needs and adjust their investment strategies accordingly.
  • Human Advisors: For more complex financial questions, Personal Capital offers access to certified financial planners who provide personalized advice to ensure active investors are on track to meet their long-term financial goals.

Best For:

Active investors who want a more comprehensive financial management platform that includes both automated investing and access to certified financial advisors. Personal Capital’s retirement planning and financial tools also make it ideal for those who want to integrate their investment strategies with broader financial goals.

Conclusion

The robo-advisor landscape in 2023 offers a wide range of platforms designed to cater to active investors. Each of the options outlined above provides distinct features, allowing investors to personalize their portfolios while leveraging advanced technology to manage their investments efficiently. Whether you’re seeking control over your asset allocation, tax optimization, or even the ability to actively trade alongside automated services, there’s a robo-advisor suited to your needs.

Choosing the best robo-advisor for active investing depends on your goals, risk tolerance, and the level of customization you require. As with any investment strategy, it’s essential to assess your options and select the platform that aligns with your financial objectives for long-term success.
