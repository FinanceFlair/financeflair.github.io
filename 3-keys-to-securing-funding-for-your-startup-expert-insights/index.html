3 Keys to Securing Funding for Your Startup: Expert Insights


Starting a new business can be an exhilarating ride, but keeping it afloat requires financial support. Securing funding for your startup is a process that needs strategic planning and the right approach. To guide budding entrepreneurs, we reached out to experienced industry professionals for expert insights into obtaining funding for your business. Here are the top three keys to securing funding that can propel your startup to new heights.

  1. Create a solid pitch deck A well-crafted pitch deck is a potent tool to attract investors and secure funding. Derek Andersen, the founder of Startup Festival and successful entrepreneur, emphasizes the importance of having a visually appealing pitch. According to Andersen, a compelling pitch deck presentation should include:
  • A clear problem statement
  • Your solution and how it’s different from competitors
  • Your target market and a size of the market
  • Progress so far and a timeline for milestones
  • The team behind the project
  • Your financial needs and how the funds will be utilized

Remember, your pitch should focus on demonstrating your business’s potential and how the investment will be used to maximize its growth. Raising funds for your startup can take time, but preparing a top-notch pitch deck will significantly streamline the process.

  1. Network within the industry Networking is vital for generating leads and finding potential investors for your startup. According to Justin Grey, investor, and co-founder of Day Trading Academy, building a strong network is crucial to securing funding. Grey suggests focusing on the following:
  • Attend local industry events
  • Connect with professionals on LinkedIn
  • Collaborate with complementary businesses
  • Engage in social media platforms
  • Reach out to Angel investors and VCs

Grey also highlights the importance of acquiring the right skills for building and maintaining strong relationships within the industry. Good networking skills can lead to new business opportunities, mentorship, and potentially, the funding your startup requires.

  1. Be prepared to give up equity or shares Leveraging your startup’s equity may seem daunting, but when used correctly, it can be a viable source of funds. Daniel Ciuruci, Co-founder at e4e, and a successful tech entrepreneur, explains that entrepreneurs need to be prepared to surrender a part of their company to secure funds. Ciuruci says:

“It’s crucial to understand that investors will expect a return on their investment. This often means giving up a portion of your company or shares. However, if managed correctly, it is perfectly possible to retain a significant portion of your company while securing the funding needed to propel your business forward.”

Ciuruci also underlines the importance of detailed due diligence when seeking investment from venture capitalists. Understanding each investor’s expectations and requirements can help you make informed decisions regarding your equity or shares.

Conclusion Securing funding for your new venture may seem like an insurmountable challenge. But by leveraging expert knowledge, you can navigate the process more effectively. Create a compelling pitch deck, build connections within the industry, and be prepared to share your company’s equity or shares. By following these expert-backed strategies, you’ll increase your chances of finding the funding your startup needs for growth and success.
