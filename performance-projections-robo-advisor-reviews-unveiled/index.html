Performance Projections: Robo Advisor Reviews Unveiled


In recent years, robo advisors have revolutionized the world of investing and asset management. These digital platforms offer you the chance to invest in a diversified portfolio at very competitive fees with little or no human intervention. As they continue to gain popularity, it’s become increasingly crucial to understand how these platforms operate and how they can benefit you.

In this blog post, we dive into the world of robo advisors and take a closer look at some of them through performance projections. Our aim is to help you make a well-informed decision about entering the robo advisor investment world.

What Are Robo Advisors?

Robo advisors are automated investment platforms that create and manage a portfolio of financial assets on behalf of their clients. They use advanced algorithms, machine learning, and other computer-powered techniques to analyze market data, predict trends, and make investment decisions. The primary goal of these platforms is to provide cost-efficient and personalized investment services that traditionally required a financial advisor.

Key Features of Robo Advisors

  1. Low Costs: Robo advisors boast very competitive pricing structures, primarily because they operate digitally and have no office space, sales teams, or brochures to maintain.

  2. Automation: One of the most significant features of robo advisors is their ability to invest based on your unique financial goals and risk tolerance.

  3. Accessibility: Anyone can invest with a robo advisor, as they generally have low or no initial investment requirements.

  4. Education: Many robo advisors offer learning resources, making investments more accessible for those new to the world of investing.

How Do Robo Advisors Perform?

Given their automated approach, robo advisors typically aim to outperform benchmark indices such as the S&P 500 over periods of time. Some robo advisors have experienced substantial growth in their managed assets. These performance projections may vary greatly depending on your investment goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions. But it’s essential to remember that past performance doesn’t always predict future results.

Our Robo Advisor Reviews

To help you in your search for the right robo advisor, we’ve summarized some popular robo advisors and what to expect from them in terms of performance.

1. Betterment

Betterment, one of the earliest players in the robo advisor space, offers automated investing and personal finance tools. They claim a weighted average annual return of 6.89% after fees for the period ending December 31, 2017.

2. Wealthfront

Wealthfront offers automated investment and financial planning services. As of December 31, 2017, their cost-based returns average 7.41%, pre-borrowing costs.

3. Schwab Intelligent Portfolios

Schwab Intelligent Portfolios is a robo advisor from Charles Schwab, the well-known investment brokerage. They claim that, since inception in August 2015, 76% of their portfolios have a better return than the net average return of the Russell 3000 Index. However, this only accounts for 54% of their account days.

4. Vanguard Personal Advisor Services

Vanguard Personal Advisor Services combine the benefits of a robo advisor with human financial advisors. As of December 31, 2017, 74% of their portfolios delivered income and growth results within or above predetermined ranges designed to keep them within broad long-term target ranges.

How to Choose the Best Robo Advisor for You

When choosing a robo advisor, it’s essential to consider your investment goals and risk tolerance and look for a platform that aligns with your objectives. Consider the following factors:

  1. Fees: Evaluate the impact of fees on your returns in the long term.

  2. Investment Options: Look for a platform that provides a wide range of investment options tailored to different market segments.

  3. User Experience: Is the platform’s interface and tools user-friendly? Consider the available tools for monitoring, analyzing, and adjusting your investments.

  4. Performance Projections: Don’t solely rely on performance projections when making your decision. Always consider the platform’s overall features and services.

Now that you have a grasp of what robo advisors are, their key features, and performance projections, it’s time to dive in and explore the world of robo advisors yourself. Don’t hesitate to do your research, and remember to follow your unique investment goals.
