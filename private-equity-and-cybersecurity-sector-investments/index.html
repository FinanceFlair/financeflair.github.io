Private Equity and Cybersecurity Sector Investments


In recent years, private equity (PE) firms have increasingly turned their attention to cybersecurity as an attractive investment sector. The reasons for this surge in interest are twofold. Firstly, the soaring demand for cybersecurity solutions due to the growing digital landscape means that these companies have significant growth potential. Secondly, the unique nature of cybersecurity threats creates a high degree of unpredictability, providing private equity investors with potentially lucrative opportunities.

Private equity firms focus on investing in companies with high growth potential at varying stages of development. Cybersecurity as an industry is a perfect fit for this strategy. Cybersecurity is not only a necessity for any business operating digitally, but also a fast-growing market that was around $124 billion in 2020 and projected to reach $277 billion by 2027, exhibiting an incredible CAGR of 23%.

The reason behind this rapid growth is the increased dependency of businesses on digital platforms and the consequent surge in financial losses from cyberattacks. It is difficult to estimate the exact cost of cybercrime, but a recent study by Cybercrime Report and Police estimated it to be more than $1 trillion. The need to mitigate this risk is becoming increasingly urgent, creating a rising demand for cybersecurity solutions and services.

Private equity firms usually invest in companies as part of a long-term strategy. They either buy the controlling interest in the company or help the business achieve its growth objectives, often through mergers and acquisitions (M&A) activities. In the cybersecurity sector, this translates into funding fast-growing cybersecurity companies or those with a unique twist on traditional offerings.

Cybersecurity companies that offer novel solutions or services or demonstrate an inherent ability to disrupt the market are particularly attractive to PE firms. One such example is Cybereason, a cybersecurity startup that received $150 million in funding from Blackstone in 2019. Cybereason’s Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) solutions provide round-the-clock threat detection and response.

Private equity investments can also help cybersecurity companies scale significantly. An example of this can be seen in the successful acquisition of FireEye, one of the top cybersecurity firms, by Blacknell in February last year. FireEye was valued at approximately $12.5 billion and provides premium managed services in cyber security such as threat analysis, incident response and more. This sort of scale-up under PE ownership can lead to remarkable growth and excellent returns.

Furthermore, the volatile nature of cybersecurity threats offers private equity an appealing degree of unpredictability and potential for success. Cybersecurity threats are challenging to predict and require immediate and appropriate solutions. For private equity firms, this adds a sense of thrill and risk to an investment strategy that can sometimes seem stagnant or bureaucratic.

Despite the promising outlook, challenges persist. The ever-evolving nature of cybersecurity threats is not easily navigable for investors. Cybersecurity is complex and requires industry-experienced professionals to navigate the threats effectively. But with the right strategy and approach, PE firms can create a significant impact on the cybersecurity industry, providing much-needed investment and support.

In our ever-growing digital world, where cybersecurity is constantly evolving to counter new threats and attacks, private equity and cybersecurity investments form a perfect union. The confluence of an attractive investment sector with high-growth potential and the volatility of the cybersecurity industry is a magnet for private equity investment. This partnership not only has the potential to create innovative solutions and scale companies but also has the propensity to revolutionize how we view cybersecurity as an industry. Therefore, let us rejoice in the concentration of capital, talent, and technology that ultimately promise a stronger and more secure future for the digital realm.
