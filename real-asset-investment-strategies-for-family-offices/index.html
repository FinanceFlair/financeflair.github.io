Real Asset Investment Strategies for Family Offices


Family offices, with their significant wealth and long-term investment horizons, have increasingly turned to real assets as a crucial component of their investment strategies. Real assets, such as real estate, infrastructure, natural resources, and commodities, offer diversification, inflation protection, and potential for attractive risk-adjusted returns. In this blog post, we will delve into the various real asset investment strategies that family offices can consider to optimize their portfolios.

Real Estate

Real estate has long been a favored investment for family offices due to its potential for stable income and capital appreciation. Family offices can consider direct ownership of commercial properties, residential real estate, or even niche sectors such as healthcare facilities or student housing. Additionally, real estate investment trusts (REITs) and private real estate funds offer opportunities for diversification and professional management of real estate portfolios.

Infrastructure Investments

Investing in infrastructure assets, such as airports, toll roads, and utilities, can provide family offices with long-term, inflation-linked cash flows. Infrastructure investments often exhibit low correlation to traditional asset classes, making them valuable for diversification. Moreover, the essential nature of infrastructure assets can lead to relatively stable returns over the long term, aligning with the objectives of family offices.

Natural Resources

Family offices seeking exposure to natural resources can consider investments in timberland, farmland, or mineral rights. These assets offer the potential for capital appreciation, income generation, and a hedge against inflation. Furthermore, sustainable and responsible management of natural resource investments can align with the values and legacy considerations of family offices.

Commodities

Commodities, including precious metals, energy products, and agricultural goods, can serve as a hedge against inflation and currency devaluation. Family offices can gain exposure to commodities through commodity futures, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), or direct investments in physical commodities. The non-correlated nature of commodity returns can enhance portfolio diversification and risk-adjusted returns.

Considerations for Family Offices

When formulating real asset investment strategies, family offices should consider their unique investment goals, risk tolerance, and liquidity needs. Additionally, due diligence in evaluating investment managers, understanding regulatory considerations, and assessing the impact of environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors is crucial for successful real asset investing.

In conclusion, real asset investment strategies offer family offices the potential for diversification, inflation protection, and attractive risk-adjusted returns. By incorporating real estate, infrastructure investments, natural resources, and commodities into their portfolios, family offices can align their investment strategies with their long-term objectives and legacy considerations. As the investment landscape continues to evolve, the prudent integration of real assets can play a pivotal role in the success of family office portfolios.
