The Top 10 Ethical Investment Platforms to Explore


In recent years, ethical investing has transformed from a niche interest to a mainstream movement. More investors are seeking opportunities that not only yield financial returns but also align with their personal values. Prioritizing environmental sustainability, social responsibility, and good governance, ethical investments focus on companies and projects that contribute positively to the world.

If you are looking to make your investment portfolio more conscientious, understanding the available ethical investment platforms can help steer your choices. Here’s an in-depth look at the top 10 ethical investment platforms that cater to various investor profiles and aspirations.

1. OpenInvest

OpenInvest is a trailblazer in personalized sustainable investing. The platform integrates technology with social impact investing, allowing investors to customize their portfolios based on specific causes they care about—whether it’s climate change, racial justice, or corporate governance.

Key features:

  • Customizable portfolios with thematic filters
  • Automatic portfolio rebalancing
  • Transparency about company impact and controversies
  • Integration with existing brokerage accounts

OpenInvest stands out for its user-focused approach, making ethical portfolios accessible and adaptable to changing values and market conditions.

2. Ethic

Geared toward institutional investors and wealth managers, Ethic offers a technology-driven platform that creates tailored sustainability portfolios. Its proprietary system dives deep into environmental, social, and governance (ESG) data to craft portfolios that faithfully reflect client values.

Highlights include:

  • Detailed custom ESG profiles
  • Integration of advanced data science and impact measurement
  • Ability to exclude certain sectors or practices altogether
  • Focus on long-term societal and environmental outcomes

Ethic represents the cutting edge of ESG investment platform technology, combining rigorous analysis with personalized client engagement.

3. Betterment Impact

Betterment, a renowned robo-advisor, has infused its platform with an ethical investing option called Betterment Impact. This feature offers portfolios constructed with a strong emphasis on ESG factors while maintaining competitive performance.

Why choose Betterment Impact:

  • Automated, low-cost investing with ethical filters
  • Diversification across asset classes including equities and bonds
  • Goal-based investing that aligns social impact with financial objectives
  • Continuous rebalancing to maintain alignment with values

For investors new to ethical investing, Betterment Impact offers an approachable entry point with the sophistication of automated portfolio management.

4. Openfolio (Acquired by Nasdaq)

Before its acquisition by Nasdaq, Openfolio was a community-driven platform that encouraged socially responsible investing through peer comparisons. Although now integrated, its legacy continues to influence tools promoting transparency and shared learning among ethical investors.

Notable aspects include:

  • Community feedback on investment choices
  • Social sharing features to track sustainable portfolios
  • Emphasis on education and collaborative decision-making

This social aspect in investing platforms encourages a more engaged and informed investing community.

5. Swell Investing (Closed but Legacy Influential)

Though Swell Investing ceased operations in 2019, its innovative model of impact investing via themed portfolios is still influential. Swell allowed investors to select portfolios aligned with causes like clean water, renewable energy, and disease eradication.

Why Swell matters today:

  • Pioneered theme-based, cause-driven investing models
  • Raised awareness around ethical impact measurement
  • Catalyzed the development of newer platforms adopting its principles

For understanding the evolution of ethical investment platforms, Swell is a key case study.

6. Wealthsimple Socially Responsible Investing (SRI)

Wealthsimple offers a socially responsible investing option that balances client values with practical considerations. Focused on diversification and ESG integration, this platform appeals to investors seeking straightforward impact investing with minimal fuss.

Platform features:

  • Low fees with a minimalist investing experience
  • Portfolios exclude industries like tobacco, fossil fuels, and weapons
  • Includes global stocks and bonds for diversified exposure
  • Automated rebalancing and dividend reinvestment

Wealthsimple’s SRI option brings ethical investing into the mainstream with simplicity and transparency.

7. Motif Investing (Now part of Folio Investing)

Motif Investing provided thematic investing opportunities, including many focused on sustainable and responsible companies. Offering motif portfolios that concentrate on cleantech, gender equality, or renewable energy, Motif made ethical investing accessible with low minimums.

Key benefits:

  • Thematic portfolio creation with a social responsibility angle
  • Flexibility to modify and trade motifs instantaneously
  • Empowered investors to express values through targeted investment themes

While Motif was absorbed into Folio Investing, its approach to ethical themes persists in various platforms it influenced.

8. Trillium Asset Management

A veteran in the field, Trillium Asset Management combines research-intensive ESG investing with active shareholder engagement. Its platform caters to investors wanting to couple financial growth with advocacy for corporate change.

Unique features:

  • Engagement in shareholder activism to influence corporate behavior
  • Deep research into ESG issues beyond surface-level data
  • Custom portfolios aligned with specific social and environmental goals

Trillium’s approach is ideal for investors who want to be a force for corporate accountability alongside solid returns.

9. ImpactAssets

ImpactAssets is a unique platform for those interested in impact investing—a subset of ethical investing that aims for tangible social or environmental results alongside financial returns. It allows investors to direct capital into funds and projects designed for measurable impacts.

Highlights:

  • Donor-advised fund options that prioritize impact
  • Access to private debt, equity, and impact funds usually unavailable to retail investors
  • Rigorous measurement frameworks to evaluate social outcomes

ImpactAssets is perfect for investors ready to move beyond public equities and into direct impact investments.

10. Swell (Legacy Inspiration for New Platforms)

Though Swell has closed, many newer platforms like EarthFolio and InspireInvest have adopted its mission and framework. These emergent ethical investing sites continue providing curated, values-driven portfolios focused on sustainability and social good.

Emerging traits inspired by Swell:

  • Emphasis on consumer education about impact investment
  • Portfolio options tailored to specific causes and values
  • Robust transparency and reporting on investment outcomes

For those eager to explore cutting-edge, cause-driven investing, keeping an eye on successors to Swell is worthwhile.

Making the Choice: What to Consider When Selecting an Ethical Investment Platform

Each platform varies in its approach, fees, customization options, and the scope of what “ethical” means. Here are some guiding questions to help you decide:

  • Which causes matter most to you? Some platforms allow highly tailored portfolios, while others take a broader ESG approach.
  • What’s your investment horizon? Active platforms like Trillium focus on long-term change, while robo-advisors offer simpler, automated management.
  • How much control do you want? From fully customizable to hands-off management, pick what fits your engagement level.
  • What’s your budget? Fees vary widely, so compare cost structures considering services and impact reporting.
  • Do you want impact or just avoidance? Some platforms focus on screening out “bad actors”—others actively invest in companies creating positive change.

Final Thoughts

Ethical investment platforms represent a vibrant, evolving space bridging finance and values. Whether you’re a seasoned investor or new to sustainable finance, exploring these platforms offers opportunities to grow wealth conscientiously.

As the market for ethical investments expands, staying informed about platform innovations and impact measurement methods will empower you to make wise and rewarding choices. Investing with intention can amplify your influence, nurturing not just your portfolio, but a future aligned with your principles.

Dive into the ethical investing world with the platform that speaks to your passions and financial aspirations—one commitment at a time.
