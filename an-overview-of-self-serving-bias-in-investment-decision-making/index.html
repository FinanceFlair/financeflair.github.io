An Overview of Self-Serving Bias in Investment Decision Making


Self-serving bias is a cognitive bias that affects various aspects of our lives, including investment decision making. In this blog post, we will explore the concept of self-serving bias, its impact on investment decisions, and strategies to mitigate its effects. Self-serving bias refers to the tendency of individuals to attribute positive outcomes to their own abilities and efforts, while attributing negative outcomes to external factors beyond their control. This bias arises from our innate need for self-preservation and the desire to maintain a positive self-image.

The Impact on Investment Decision Making

Self-serving bias can significantly influence investment decision making. When investors experience positive outcomes, they tend to attribute their success to their own skills and intelligence. This inflated self-perception can lead to overconfidence and a willingness to take on more risk.

Conversely, when investors encounter negative outcomes, they are more likely to attribute their failures to external factors such as market volatility or unforeseen events. This defensive attribution shields their self-esteem from being negatively impacted. However, it can also prevent them from learning from their mistakes and making informed decisions in the future.

Strategies to Mitigate Self-Serving Bias

Awareness is the first step in mitigating self-serving bias. Recognizing our own tendency to attribute success to personal attributes and failure to external factors can help us make more objective investment decisions.

  1. Seek diverse opinions: Engaging with a diverse group of individuals with different perspectives can challenge our biases and provide alternative viewpoints. This can help prevent the reinforcement of self-serving bias.

  2. Maintain a record of decisions: Keeping a detailed record of investment decisions, along with the reasons behind them, can help us evaluate the impact of our biases over time. This self-reflection can lead to improved decision making and a reduction in self-serving bias.

  3. Utilize objective data: Relying on objective data and analysis, rather than personal anecdotes or emotions, can help counter the influence of self-serving bias. Implementing systematic investment strategies based on data-driven analysis can increase objectivity and reduce the impact of biases on decision making.

  4. Continuous learning: Embracing a growth mindset and actively seeking opportunities to learn and improve can help reduce self-serving bias. By acknowledging that failure is an essential part of the learning process, investors can avoid the defensive attribution associated with self-serving bias.

Conclusion

Self-serving bias is a common cognitive bias that affects investment decision making. By understanding the impact of this bias and implementing strategies to mitigate its effects, investors can enhance their decision-making process and improve their long-term investment outcomes. Developing self-awareness, seeking diverse opinions, relying on objective data, and embracing a continuous learning mindset are crucial steps in overcoming self-serving bias. Remember, investing is a journey that requires constant self-reflection and adaptation to navigate the ever-changing financial landscape.
