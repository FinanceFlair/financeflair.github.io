How to Approach Angel Investors: A Startup's Perspective


As a startup founder, securing funding is often a daunting task. Venture capitalists, banks, and crowdfunding are all viable options, but none offer the specialized guidance and equity-deal-making of angel investors. When looking to secure financial backing, considering what to expect from this funding channel is crucial. Here, we'll outline what to expect when approaching angel investors, including how to prepare, how to make a successful pitch, and what to look for in an ideal partner. Preparing for the Pitch Before approaching an angel investor, it is essential to have a solid plan and pitch in place. This is a common phrase, but it is critical. Having a persuasive and well-structured pitch will show potential investors that you're serious about your startup's growth and willing to put in the necessary time and effort.

  1. Develop a Clear Business Plan Before sealing the deal, angel investors require a clear business plan detailing goals, milestones, and financial projections. Present your appeal in a brief, three-minute elevator pitch. Stress your unique value proposition and probability of success. Discuss each component of your business as part of your pitch, from how it fills a gap in the market to how it generates revenue.
  2. Develop a Prototype or Minimum Viable Product (MVP) Even before an angel investor feels comfortable committing to your venture, they need a basic prototype to illustrate your concept. This must demonstrate your progress and value for investors. The Pitch Meeting This is where your expert presentation skills come in. With confidence, explain to your potential investors why they should invest in your business, by focusing on your unique selling product or service, marketing strategy and innovation potential without calculations and figures first.
