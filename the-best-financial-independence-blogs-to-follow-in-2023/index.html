The Best Financial Independence Blogs to Follow in 2023


Achieving financial independence is a goal that many strive for, offering the promise of freedom, flexibility, and security. It’s about having enough savings, investments, or income streams to sustain your lifestyle without being solely reliant on a paycheck. But the journey to financial independence isn’t always straightforward. That’s where resources like blogs come into play. The financial independence (FI) blogging community is filled with inspiring stories, valuable strategies, and actionable tips to help individuals on their paths to financial freedom.

In 2023, some blogs continue to stand out, offering guidance and a sense of community for those pursuing financial independence. Whether you’re just starting on your financial independence journey or looking for advanced strategies to optimize your finances, these blogs provide a wealth of information to learn from. Here are some of the best financial independence blogs that deserve your attention this year.

1. Mr. Money Mustache

When discussing the financial independence movement, it’s almost impossible to overlook Mr. Money Mustache (MMM). This blog is one of the pioneers in the FI community and continues to inspire thousands of readers worldwide. Pete Adeney, the man behind Mr. Money Mustache, retired in his early 30s and now shares insights on how others can achieve the same.

What makes MMM unique is its no-nonsense, often humorous approach to frugal living, investment strategies, and crushing consumerism. While some readers may find Pete’s extreme frugality unconventional, his message of intentional spending and smart financial planning resonates deeply with those looking to reclaim control over their finances.

If you’re searching for a mix of practical advice, bold opinions, and a slightly rebellious approach to early retirement, Mr. Money Mustache remains a must-read.

2. The Mad Fientist

Known for taking a deep dive into the intricacies of financial independence and early retirement (FIRE) strategies, The Mad Fientist is a favorite amongst those who love optimization. The blog is run by Brandon, a software developer who retired in his 30s and is passionate about exploring ways to retire more quickly and efficiently.

One of the key features of The Mad Fientist’s blog is his detailed analysis of tax optimization, withdrawal strategies, and maximizing investment returns. Brandon’s content isn’t just about saving; it’s also about minimizing taxes and creating a sustainable withdrawal plan once you do retire. His podcast, which often features some of the biggest names in the FIRE movement, is another valuable resource.

If you enjoy a data-driven and analytical approach to financial independence, this blog deserves a spot on your radar.

3. Afford Anything

Paula Pant’s blog, “Afford Anything,” is built around a simple yet profound philosophy: “You can afford anything, but not everything.” Through her writing, Paula dives deeply into the concepts of decision-making, intentional living, and building financial freedom.

Paula doesn’t limit her content to frugality and investing; she also encourages her readers to consider their values, priorities, and long-term goals. Her blog focuses on real estate investing, side hustles, and developing the mindset necessary for achieving financial independence.

The accompanying “Afford Anything Podcast” features a range of expert interviews, offering a mix of personal finance advice, real estate tips, and entrepreneurship lessons. If you’re seeking a blend of inspiration, actionable strategies, and transformative thinking, Paula Pant’s thoughtful content won’t disappoint.

4. FIREcracker and Wanderer from Millennial Revolution

Millennial Revolution, created by FIREcracker and Wanderer, is anything but conventional. This Canadian duo achieved financial independence in their early 30s and now travel the world full-time. Their blog focuses on smart financial strategies, including index investing and living off portfolio passive income. However, their content is far from dry or technical—instead, it’s packed with humor, quirky anecdotes, and a refreshing sense of realism.

What sets them apart is their laser focus on empowering millennials and others who want to escape the traditional 9-to-5 grind. They debunk myths about homeownership, consumer debts, and highlight how living an adventurous yet financially sustainable life is attainable.

From detailed case studies of readers’ financial situations to travel-hacking insights, Millennial Revolution is a unique blend of financial wisdom and wanderlust.

5. JL Collins’ “The Simple Path to Wealth”

JL Collins’ blog sprang into prominence with his practical advice on investing—particularly when it comes to low-cost index funds. The blog and his well-loved book, The Simple Path to Wealth, focus on simplifying the often complex world of personal finance, making it accessible to everyone.

Collins’ hallmark is his straightforward writing, combined with his emphasis on the long-term benefits of choosing simplicity in investing. His belief in the power of index funds has resonated with many people, regardless of where they are on their financial independence journey.

For anyone overwhelmed by financial jargon or looking for an easy, proven way to grow wealth, JL Collins is a reassuring voice in a crowded financial landscape.

6. Our Next Life

“Our Next Life” is a blog dedicated to the transitions and challenges of life after retirement. Tanja Hester, the author, retired in her late 30s after years of diligent saving and investing. Her content goes beyond financial advice—it delves into lifestyle changes, finding purpose in retirement, and navigating the emotions that come with substantial life shifts.

What makes “Our Next Life” stand out is Tanja’s transparency and honesty about the sacrifices and rewards of early retirement. Her reflections and insights on building a meaningful post-retirement life are a breath of fresh air for those who are focused not just on achieving FI but thriving after it.

7. ChooseFI

ChooseFI is much more than just a blog—it’s a movement. Created by Jonathan Mendonsa and Brad Barrett, this resource has become one of the most popular financial independence platforms, offering blog posts, podcasts, and tools to help individuals fast-track their financial journeys.

The blog covers everything from saving strategies to increasing income, tax optimization, and the concept of value-driven spending. The community aspect of ChooseFI is also notable, with vibrant forums and meetups bringing together like-minded individuals who are passionate about crafting intentional lives.

The accompanying podcast consistently ranks as one of the top personal finance shows, featuring interviews with experts and success stories from everyday people.

8. Frugalwoods

If you’re interested in financial independence with a focus on simple living, the Frugalwoods blog is likely to resonate with you. Liz Thames (also known as Mrs. Frugalwoods) writes candidly about her path to financial freedom through extreme frugality and conscious decision-making.

She shares her family’s journey to leaving urban life behind for rural homesteading in Vermont, all while maintaining financial independence. Her posts are introspective, often diving into larger questions about what truly brings happiness and fulfillment.

Frugalwoods’ content highlights the power of cutting unnecessary expenses, embracing frugality as a lifestyle, and aligning spending with core values. It’s a great resource for anyone looking to simplify their life without compromising on joy.

9. The FI Physician

Doctors and other high-income earners often face unique financial challenges, including student debt and lifestyle inflation. That’s where “The FI Physician” comes in. This blog caters specifically to medical professionals who want to achieve financial independence while balancing demanding careers.

Dr. David Graham, the brains behind the blog, addresses topics like debt repayment, investment strategies, and maintaining balance in life. The content is tailored to the specific needs of physicians but can also provide valuable insights for anyone managing a professional career and complex financial goals.

10. Bitches Get Riches

For those who enjoy financial advice served with sass and sarcasm, “Bitches Get Riches” stands out. Kitty and Piggy, the duo behind the blog, provide no-nonsense financial guidance on everything from saving money to dealing with emergencies.

While the tone is irreverent, the content is solid—offering practical steps to improve your finances while keeping it engaging and fun. Their topics are diverse, spanning beginner budgeting advice to tackling income inequality. If you love advice wrapped in a hilarious and relatable package, Bitches Get Riches is a refreshing read.

Final Thoughts

Financial independence is about more than just numbers—it’s about creating the freedom to live a life aligned with your values and aspirations. Whether you’re just beginning your FI journey or are fine-tuning your plan, these blogs provide inspiration, tools, and community to support your goals.

Diving into the world of financial independence can be overwhelming at times, but the insights from these bloggers remind us that the path to freedom is achievable. They’ve navigated the challenges themselves, shared their lessons, and created blueprints for others to follow. Now, it’s up to you to learn, take action, and mold their wisdom into a strategy that works for your life.

The financial independence blogs mentioned here offer inspiration, strategies, and a supportive community for those looking to rewrite the script on money and freedom. Follow them, engage, and get ready to start crafting your ideal life.
