Short Term Trading: Strategies for Trading Reversal Patterns


Short term trading can be a rollercoaster ride of fray, but with the right strategies, one can potentially experience sky-high returns. Trading reversal patterns is one such strategy. In this blog, we’ll delve into how to identify these patterns, and explore several successful strategies.

First, understanding reversal patterns is crucial. These are identified when a trend transforms directions - from downtrend to up, or from up to down. It is the point where demand surpasses supply, or vice versa.

  1. Support and Resistance Levels

One pivotal pattern to watch is Support Resistance Levels. This occurs when price action bounces off specific levels. Here, analysts use four tactics:

a. Fractal analysis: Analyzing the structure of past eminent patterns. b. Fibonacci retracement: Using Fibonacci ratios that pinpoint areas where price action tends to recoil. c. Pitchfork technique: Drawing a synthetic triple-bottom line which makes trendlines that offer reversal resistance. d. Swing highs/lows: Identifying where momentum traverses.

  1. Chart Patterns

Indicators, tools, and signals are crucial; they help identify patterns. Three significant chart patterns to watch are:

a. Triangles: Symmetrical, descending, or ascending triangles. These tend to resolve in the direction of the prior trend. b. Head and Shoulders: Everything like shoulders with a head on top. It predicts reversal when price action nears and surpasses the head’s high. c. Cups and Handles: Similar to saucers. After climbing unchallenged, they forecast price reversal when the handle’s low-point is exceeded.

  1. Moving averages and momentum oscillators

Leveraging moving averages clobbered with momentum oscillators gives rousing reversal targets.

a. Crossovers: When short-term SMAs cross over long-term ones, a trend change occurs. b. RSI (Relative Strength Index): An oscillator entrenched within 0-100. Overbought and oversold regions define reversal boundaries. c. Stochastic Oscillator: Similar to RSI, but using %K and %D calculation.

A primary focus on risk management manages our trades effectively. Diversifying portfolio, trailing stops, TLBs (Take Profit Levels and Breakeven Pips) all advocate for a successful venture.
