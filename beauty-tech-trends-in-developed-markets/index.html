Beauty Tech Trends in Developed Markets


The beauty industry has been rapidly advancing with technology, transforming the way people look, feel, and take care of themselves. In developed markets such as the United States, Europe, and Japan, the adoption of advanced beauty platforms has become more mainstream. Let’s delve into six notable beauty tech trends that consumers are steadily embracing as they prioritize self-care and maintenance.

1. Home-based Beauty Devices

Home-based beauty devices have surged in popularity, allowing customers to maintain salon-quality treatments in the comfort of their own homes. Devices such as facial roller massagers, derma-rolling machines, and LED light therapy kits have become go-to self-care tools. They help with everything from depuffing and toning the skin, to reducing wrinkles and improving skin elasticity.

2. Personalized Skincare Formulas

Enterprises such as Procter & Gamble’s “Kindly” have begun to offer personalized skincare formulas, using AI-driven algorithms to create a tailored skincare routine based on a customer’s unique skin profile. They gather data through a comprehensive questionnaire, analyze it, and then suggest a fitting skincare regimen that adapts with the user’s skin changes over time. This approach reduces the trial-and-error that usually occurs when choosing products for sensitive skin.

3. Subscription Services

Abundant choices and shifting consumer preferences have led to the rise of subscription-based beauty services. Startups like Henna Sadaf’s Hanif Hair Care and The Grooming Lounge are offering personalized services on a subscription basis. Customers can choose to subscribe for weekly salon visits, a monthly shave set, or another ritual, depending on their preferences. These services take the hassle out of daily grooming, freeing up time and space for self-care.

4. Virtual Reality Try-Ons

Augmented reality (AR) is revolutionizing the way we experience and try beauty products. Applications like Perfect Corp’s “YouCam Makeup” and “FilterFace” have introduced virtual reality try-ons, enabling customers to visualize and experiment with 3D versions of products featuring different colors, brands, and styles. This enhances user experience by making it more interactive, engaging, and reliant on instant feedback.

5. Vertical Compact Farms

Farmed greens are a growing trend in the food industry, and vertical compact farms introduce a way to grow and distribute produce in urban or even household environments. Companies like SQUAREFEETFarms are beginning to apply this concept to the beauty industry, creating vertical farms for premium plants and herbs. These can be a valuable solution for obtaining fresher and locally sourced ingredients for cosmetics or skincare products.

6. Apps for Beauty Education

The market teems with beauty learning apps that take the place of traditional make-up classes or hacks shared by friends. These apps offer tutorials, step-by-step guides, and tips on applying makeup, hairstyling, and skincare. Platforms like “Styleseat” and “Peachy Academy” break down barriers, allowing everyone to learn new beauty skills from their phones at any time.

In conclusion, the growing demand for self-care and the innovation of technology have created an environment ripe for beauty tech development. As the trends analyzed in this article continue to evolve, it will be increasingly important for beauty businesses to embrace technology, providing customers with personalized, efficient, and engaging experiences.
