How Anchoring Bias Impacts Your Spending Choices


Have you ever found yourself splurging on a designer handbag or a fine dining experience simply because you "treated yourself" to it, even if it was way out of your budget? Or maybe you've settled for a lower-end product or service because the higher-end option seemed "too steep" even though you could afford it? If so, you're not alone. You're falling prey to the invisible force of anchoring bias. The concept of anchoring bias Anchoring bias is a cognitive bias where our judgments or spending decisions are influenced by the first piece of information we receive, which acts as an "anchor." This anchor creates a mental reference point for our subsequent decisions, often resulting in a distorted perception of value. In the context of spending, anchoring bias can have far-reaching consequences, leading us to make either unnecessary splurges or underspend on necessary items. How anchoring bias affects your spending The anchoring bias influences our spending in two main ways: The Bandwagon Effect When presented with a premium product or service, our brain instinctively compares it to a standard or base rate. If the premium option seems ridiculously expensive, we might be more likely to opt for something elsewhere. This phenomenon is known as the bandwagon effect. For instance, let's say you're shopping for a vacation package. If you're shown a luxurious option that costs $5,000, but then you see a similar package at $1,200, the high-end package becomes your "anchor" – making the $1,200 option seem reasonable in comparison. The Asymmetric Price Effect Conversely, the anchoring bias also works the other way around. When faced with a cheap option, it can sometimes make a premium product or service seem overvalued. This occurs when we perceive a higher item as extortionate merely because there's a cheaper option nearby. In reality, the cheap option might not be as good quality or provide the same features. For example, if you're buying a laptop, and you see a basic model for $200, you might deem a superior model that costs $800 excessive, despite its advanced features and better build quality. Willpower and self-control are no match The good news is that awareness of the anchoring bias is the first step towards countering its effects. The bad news is that even the strongest willpower and self-control are no match for this cognitive bias. Sensory information has a way of bypassing our rational thinking, making it challenging to make logical, well-researched purchasing decisions. Make informed, clear-headed choices Fortunately, there's a way to overcome the biases and make smart spending decisions. By being aware of anchoring bias, you can:

Use multiple reference points: Don't just rely on the first piece of information you receive. Use multiple sources to get a well-rounded view of the product or service. Understand value: Prioritize what you're getting for your money. Don't fall for flawed evaluations that consider just the price tag. Avoid comparisons: Try to avoid direct comparisons between similar products or services. Instead, focus on what you need and can afford for each transaction. Use mental budgeting: Set a clear budget for each category and separate your essential expenses from discretionary ones. By recognizing that you can't justify overspending on luxuries using the allure of "special deals" or high prices.

Debunking Savings Myth Furthermore, anchoring bias can sometimes make you think you're saving by going for an option that's merely slightly less expensive. Conversely, when it comes to significant purchases, picking the costliest "showroom" option may seem gloomy, but a low price may turn out to be misleading. Never Stop Frugality However, in fact, investing just a tad more may be worth the premium you spend on quality goods. Spending a bit more on newer models for its proper operation lifespan will save you money in the long run. For example, when buying a vehicle, paying more upfront for a newer car may save you significantly on repair costs. Spending your money prudently ensures your more significant returns through less time working on a good technology or investing in exceptional quality today.
