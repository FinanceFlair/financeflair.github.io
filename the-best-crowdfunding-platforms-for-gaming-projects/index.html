The Best Crowdfunding Platforms for Gaming Projects


Crowdfunding has revolutionized the way game developers finance their projects, giving them the opportunity to raise funds directly from their target audience. In this industry, several platforms stand out as leaders, offering unique benefits and advantages to game developers. In this article, we'll delve into the best crowdfunding platforms for gaming projects, exploring their features, success stories, and what sets them apart from the rest.

  1. Kickstarter Kickstarter is one of the most well-known and widely used crowdfunding platforms, having funded over 200,000 projects since its inception in 2009. While it's not exclusively a gaming platform, Kickstarter has a dedicated gaming community and has seen the success of numerous iconic projects. Notable examples include the development of the Pebble smartwatch, the Ouya console, and the card game Exploding Kittens. Kickstarter's all-or-nothing funding model means that projects only receive funds if they reach their funding goal. This model creates a sense of urgency and accountability, as game developers must work to meet their target within a set timeframe. While this can be challenging, it also allows project creators to demonstrate a clear vision and commitment to their project.
  2. Fig Launched in 2014, Fig offers a unique take on crowdfunding by focusing on tabletop gaming and personal projects. This platform has been instrumental in supporting the development of titles such as Pillars of Eternity and Tyranny, both highly-regarded RPGs. Fig's Revenue Share model allows game developers to keep 70% of funds raised, while Fig takes a 15% cut. Fig's emphasis on tabletop gaming and personal projects has helped establish a tight-knit community of developers and supporters. By focusing on these niches, Fig has created an environment where game developers can connect directly with their target audience and share their unique vision.
  3. Gamefunded Gamefunded, launched in 2013, is a dedicated gaming crowdfunding platform. Its flexible funding model allows projects to keep any funds raised, regardless of whether they reach their target. This approach can be beneficial for game developers who need additional funding to meet their project's full potential. Gamefunded's platform features a range of tools and resources for game developers, including project management software and marketing support. By emphasizing the needs of its developers, Gamefunded has attracted a loyal community and has played a significant role in funding numerous successful gaming projects.
  4. Gamefound Gamefound is a relatively new crowfunding platform, launched in 2020. Its flexible funding model mirrors that of Gamefunded, allowing projects to keep all funds raised. Gamefound's platform is designed to cater specifically to tabletop gaming and board game development. One of Gamefound's unique features is its partnership with industry leading organizations, such as the Board Game Design Lab. This collaboration provides project creators with valuable resources and expertise, helping them to refine their ideas and connect with potential backers.
  5. Indiegogo Indiegogo, launched in 2008, is one of the earliest crowdfunding platforms. While it's not primarily focused on gaming, Indiegogo has seen significant success in funding projects like Google Glass and Pebble's first smartwatch. Indiegogo's flexible funding model allows projects to keep any funds raised, and its platform features a wide range of tools and resources for project creators. One of Indiegogo's strengths is its global reach, with a user base spanning over 230 countries. This provides game developers with access to a vast and diverse pool of potential backers, increasing their chances of success.
  6. Bounties Network Bounties Network is a relatively new, decentralized crowfunding platform. Its token-based funding model allows backers to purchase project-specific tokens, which grant access to project rewards and updates. Bounties Network's focus on community-driven, decentralized funding has attracted a dedicated following of game developers and supporters. By giving backers a direct stake in the project's success, Bounties Network has created a more interactive and engaging experience. Choosing the Right Platform Each of the platforms mentioned above offers unique benefits and advantages for game developers. When selecting a platform, consider the following factors:

Reach: How well does the platform connect with your target audience? Funding Model: Does the platform's funding model align with your project's needs and goals? Tools and Resources: What support does the platform offer project creators, such as project management software and marketing support? Community: How engaged is the platform's community, and how well does it support project creators?

Ultimately, the best crowfunding platform for your gaming project will depend on your unique needs, goals, and vision. By understanding the strengths and weaknesses of each platform, you can make an informed decision and connect with your target audience effectively. Conclusion Crowdfunding has revolutionized the gaming industry, providing game developers with the opportunity to raise funds directly from their target audience. Each of the platforms mentioned in this article offers unique benefits and advantages, from flexible funding models to community-driven, token-based funding. By selecting the right platform and understanding the needs of your project, you can maximize your chances of success and turn your vision into a reality. While crowdfunding has its challenges, the potential rewards are significant. With the right platform and support, game developers can connect directly with their audience, iterate on their ideas, and bring innovation to the gaming industry. As we look to the future, one thing is clear: crowdfunding is here to stay, and the best platforms will continue to adapt and evolve to meet the changing needs of game developers and supporters alike.
