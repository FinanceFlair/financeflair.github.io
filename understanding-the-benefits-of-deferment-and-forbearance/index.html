Understanding the Benefits of Deferment and Forbearance


Are you struggling to make payments on your loan or credit card? Are you finding it hard to manage your debt repayments? Deferment and forbearance may be the solution you're looking for. These options can provide temporary relief from paying interest on your debt, allowing you to get back on your feet. What is Deferment? Deferment is an option where you temporarily pause or delay making payments on your loan. This does not mean you are getting out of paying the debt altogether; it simply means that you're postponing payment. During the deferment period, you won't be required to make minimum payments, and the lender won't start calling you non-conforming because of missed payments. Types of Deferment Deferment is available for various types of loans, including federal student loans, private student loans, and personal loans. When it comes to federal student loans, you can opt for the following types of deferment:

In-School Deferment: This allows you to pause payments while you're in school at least half time or during a period of non-enrollment. Graduate School Deferment: Similar to the in-school deferment, this option applies while you're pursuing a graduate or professional degree. Economic Hardship Deferment: If you're experiencing financial difficulties, you may be eligible for an economic hardship deferment. Military Service Deferment: Active-duty military personnel or veterans may be eligible for this type of deferment.

What is Forbearance? Forbearance is a short-term solution that allows you to temporarily reduce or temporarily suspend payments due to financial difficulties. The interest will still accrue during the forbearance period, and this often requires the borrower to agree to this arrangement in writing. Types of Forbearance Forbearance is typically available for loans that are considered high-risk, such as mortgage loans or other secured loans. When it comes to federal loans, some examples of forbearance include:

Temporary Reduction of Payments: You will temporarily reduce the amount of payments made on your loans. Temporary Suspension of Payments: You will temporarily stop making payments. Interest-Only Payments: You will make interest-only payments rather than the full loan amount.

Benefits of Deferment and Forbearance Both deferment and forbearance offer numerous benefits, including:

Reduced Financial Burden: By temporarily pausing or reducing payments, you can free up money in your budget for essential expenses. Avoidance of Late Fees: By temporarily pausing or reducing payments, you can avoid late fees and associated penalties. Preservation of Credit Score: By temporarily pausing or reducing payments, you can avoid damaging your credit score due to missed payments. Extended Repayment Period: Depending on the lender's agreement, you may be able to extend the repayment period.

Eligibility Criteria Before you can apply for deferment or forbearance, you'll need to meet certain eligibility criteria. These may include:

Financial Need: You'll need to demonstrate that you're experiencing financial difficulties. Lender Approval: You'll need to secure approval from your lender before you can start the process.

How to Apply for Deferment or Forbearance To apply for deferment or forbearance, you can:

Contact Your Lender: Reach out to your lender to discuss your eligibility and the application process. Submit an Application: Complete and submit an application to your lender. Provide Documentation: Submit any required documentation, such as proof of income or financial hardship.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid Before applying for deferment or forbearance, be aware of the potential pitfalls:

Additional Fees: Be aware that you may incur additional fees for the deferment or forbearance arrangement. Impact on Credit Score: Deferment or forbearance may still have a negative impact on your credit score, even if it's temporary. Impact on Loan Terms: Deferment or forbearance may change the terms of your loan, including the interest rate or repayment period.

Conclusion Deferment and forbearance can offer temporary relief from paying interest on your debt, allowing you to get back on your feet. By understanding the benefits and eligibility criteria, you can apply for deferment or forbearance and get the financial help you need.
