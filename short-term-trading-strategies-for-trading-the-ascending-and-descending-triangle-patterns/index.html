Short Term Trading: Strategies for Trading the Ascending and Descending Triangle Patterns


Short term trading is a popular strategy among traders looking to capitalize on quick market movements. One common technique utilized by short term traders is the identification and analysis of chart patterns. Two significant patterns in technical analysis are the ascending triangle and descending triangle patterns. Understanding how to trade these patterns effectively can provide traders with valuable insights into potential price movements. In this blog post, we will delve into the strategies for trading the ascending and descending triangle patterns.

Ascending Triangle Pattern

The ascending triangle pattern is a bullish continuation pattern that forms when there is a clear resistance level and higher lows. Traders often look for this pattern as it indicates a potential upward breakout. Here are some key strategies for trading the ascending triangle pattern:

1. Identifying the Pattern

To trade the ascending triangle pattern successfully, you must first be able to identify it on the price chart. Look for a horizontal resistance level where the price repeatedly tests but fails to break above, coupled with higher lows forming an ascending trendline.

2. Entry and Stop Loss Placement

Traders typically enter a long position when the price breaks above the resistance level, confirming the pattern. The stop loss is usually placed below the most recent higher low to manage risk in case of a false breakout.

3. Target Price Projection

To determine a target price for the trade, measure the height of the triangle from the initial low to the resistance level and project that distance upwards from the breakout point. This can give you an idea of where the price may reach post-breakout.

4. Risk Management

It’s essential to practice proper risk management when trading the ascending triangle pattern. Consider your risk-reward ratio and position size to ensure you protect your capital.

Descending Triangle Pattern

Contrary to the ascending triangle, the descending triangle pattern is a bearish continuation pattern characterized by a clear support level and lower highs. Traders often interpret this pattern as a potential signal for a downward breakout. Here are some strategies for trading the descending triangle pattern:

1. Recognizing the Pattern

To trade the descending triangle pattern effectively, look for a horizontal support level where the price repeatedly bounces but fails to break above, along with lower highs forming a descending trendline.

2. Entry and Stop Loss Placement

Traders typically enter a short position when the price breaks below the support level, confirming the pattern. The stop loss is commonly placed above the most recent lower high to manage risk in case of a false breakdown.

3. Target Price Projection

Similar to the ascending triangle, you can project a target price by measuring the height of the triangle from the initial high to the support level and projecting that distance downwards from the breakout point.

4. Risk Management

Maintaining sound risk management practices is crucial when trading the descending triangle pattern. Consider your risk tolerance and adjust your position size accordingly.

By understanding and implementing these strategies for trading the ascending and descending triangle patterns, traders can potentially enhance their short term trading performance. Remember to combine technical analysis with other indicators and tools for a comprehensive trading approach. As with any trading strategy, it’s important to practice discipline and continuous learning to navigate the dynamic financial markets successfully.

Happy trading and may your charts be ever in your favor!
