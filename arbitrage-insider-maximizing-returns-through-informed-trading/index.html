What is Arbitrage Trading?

Arbitrage trading involves taking advantage of price discrepancies in different markets to make a profit. This strategy relies on the principle that assets can be priced differently in various markets due to factors such as supply and demand dynamics, transaction costs, or market inefficiencies. By identifying these pricing discrepancies and acting swiftly, arbitrageurs can buy low in one market and sell high in another, pocketing the difference as profit.
