Top 10 Robo Advisors of 2021: Reviews & Comparison


In the ever-evolving landscape of investment options, robo advisors have emerged as a popular choice for those seeking automated, low-cost, and efficient investment management solutions. These digital platforms are designed to make investing accessible to a wide range of individuals, offering personalized portfolios, automated rebalancing, and often lower fees compared to traditional financial advisors. If you’re considering using a robo advisor to enhance your investment strategy in 2021, here are the top 10 robo advisors to consider, along with reviews and comparisons to help you make an informed decision.

1. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is a well-established robo advisor known for its diversified portfolio options and tax-efficient investing strategies. With a low minimum investment requirement and a competitive management fee, Wealthfront appeals to both new and experienced investors looking for a hands-off approach to growing their wealth.

2. Betterment

As one of the pioneers in the robo advising industry, Betterment offers a user-friendly platform with a range of portfolio options to suit different risk appetites. Their goal-based investing approach and tax-loss harvesting features make it a top choice for those looking for a streamlined investment experience.

3. SoFi Invest

SoFi Invest stands out for its all-in-one financial platform, which includes robo advising services along with banking and loan products. With zero management fees and the option to invest in fractional shares, SoFi appeals to cost-conscious investors who want a holistic financial management solution.

4. M1 Finance

M1 Finance combines the best of robo advising with the flexibility of self-directed investing. Investors can select from pre-built portfolios or create their own, all while enjoying no management fees and the ability to automate their investing strategy through M1’s innovative platform.

5. Wealthsimple

Wealthsimple is a robo advisor known for its socially responsible investing options and human financial advisors available to all clients. With a strong emphasis on simplicity and transparency, Wealthsimple is ideal for those looking to align their investments with their values.

6. Acorns

Acorns takes a unique approach to investing by rounding up everyday purchases and investing the spare change. This micro-investing strategy, combined with low fees and educational resources, makes Acorns a great choice for beginners looking to start investing with small amounts.

7. Charles Schwab Intelligent Portfolios

Charles Schwab’s robo advisor offering provides a mix of automated investing and access to human advisors for personalized guidance. With a focus on goal-based planning and a wide range of investment options, Charles Schwab Intelligent Portfolios caters to investors at all levels.

8. Vanguard Personal Advisor Services

Vanguard is renowned for its low-cost index funds, and their robo advising service, Personal Advisor Services, continues this tradition. With a hybrid approach that combines automated investing with access to human advisors, Vanguard appeals to cost-conscious investors seeking a balanced investment strategy.

9. Ellevest

Ellevest is a robo advisor designed with a focus on women’s financial goals and unique investing needs. With tailored portfolios and a commitment to closing the gender investing gap, Ellevest empowers women to take control of their financial futures.

10. Robinhood

While primarily known as a commission-free trading platform, Robinhood also offers a robo advising service, Robinhood Snacks. With no management fees and the ability to invest in fractional shares, Robinhood appeals to investors looking for a simple and intuitive investing experience.

In conclusion, the world of robo advisors offers a diverse range of options to suit various investment preferences and goals. Whether you’re a seasoned investor or just starting out, there’s a robo advisor out there to help you grow your wealth efficiently and effectively. Before choosing a robo advisor, consider factors such as fees, investment options, and customer support to find the best fit for your financial needs. Happy investing!
