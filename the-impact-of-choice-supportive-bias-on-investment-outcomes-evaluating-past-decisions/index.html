The Impact of Choice-Supportive Bias on Investment Outcomes: Evaluating Past Decisions


Have you ever wondered why it’s so difficult to admit when we’ve made a bad investment decision? Or why we tend to look back on our past investments with rose-tinted glasses? The answer lies in a psychological phenomenon known as choice-supportive bias. In this blog post, we will explore the impact of choice-supportive bias on investment outcomes and how evaluating our past decisions can lead to better financial choices in the future. Choice-supportive bias, also known as post-purchase rationalization, is a cognitive bias that occurs when we tend to remember the positive aspects of our choices and overlook the negative ones. This bias is especially prevalent when it comes to investment decisions. We have a natural inclination to protect our self-esteem and justify our past actions, even if they were not the best choices.

The Emotional Connection to Investments

Investments are not merely financial transactions; they often carry emotional weight as well. When we invest our hard-earned money, we become emotionally attached to the outcome. We want our investments to succeed, and admitting that we made a mistake can be challenging.

Choice-supportive bias allows us to maintain a positive emotional connection to our investments. We conveniently forget about the losses or risks associated with our decisions and focus on the gains and successes instead. This bias can cloud our judgment and prevent us from objectively evaluating our past investment choices.

The Downside of Choice-Supportive Bias

While choice-supportive bias may provide temporary emotional comfort, it can have detrimental effects on our investment outcomes. By selectively remembering the positive aspects of our decisions, we fail to learn from our mistakes and make informed choices in the future.

Ignoring the negative aspects of past investments can lead to a false sense of confidence and increase the likelihood of repeating the same mistakes. It’s crucial to acknowledge and evaluate both the successes and failures to make better investment decisions going forward.

Evaluating Past Decisions for Future Success

To overcome choice-supportive bias and improve our investment outcomes, we must actively evaluate our past decisions. Here are some steps to help you break free from the bias and make more informed choices:

  1. Reflect on past investments objectively: Take a step back and critically evaluate your past investment decisions. Look beyond the gains and losses and consider the underlying factors that contributed to the outcomes.

  2. Seek external opinions: Don’t rely solely on your own judgment. Seek feedback from financial advisors, experts, or trusted individuals who can provide a fresh perspective on your investment choices.

  3. Learn from mistakes: Embrace failure as an opportunity for growth. Analyze your past mistakes, identify the patterns, and make a conscious effort to avoid repeating them in the future.

  4. Diversify your portfolio: By diversifying your investments, you can reduce the impact of any single poor decision. Spread your risk across different asset classes and industries to increase your chances of overall success.

  5. Stay informed: Keep yourself updated on market trends, economic indicators, and investment strategies. The more knowledge you have, the better equipped you’ll be to make informed decisions.

Conclusion

Choice-supportive bias can have a significant impact on our investment outcomes and prevent us from learning from our mistakes. By consciously evaluating our past decisions and breaking free from the bias, we can make more informed choices and increase our chances of financial success.

Remember, investing is a continuous learning process. Embrace the lessons from your past decisions, stay open to new information, and adapt your strategies accordingly. With a clear and unbiased mindset, you can navigate the investment landscape with confidence and achieve your financial goals.
