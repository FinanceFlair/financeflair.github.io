Derivative Delights: Uncovering Lesser-Known Strategies in the World of Derivatives Trading


Derivatives trading has long been a complex and enigmatic world of financial instruments, not fully understood by the average investor. While many have heard of the popular derivatives like futures and options, there’s a lesser-known universe of strategies and instruments waiting to be explored. In this blog post, we’ll dive into these intriguing trades, uncover the fascinating realm of lesser-known derivatives trading strategies, and discover how they can be utilized to diversify your investment portfolio.

1. Contracts for Difference (CFDs)

A Contracts for Difference, or CFD, is an agreement between two parties to exchange the difference in the value of an asset between the opening and closing of a position. Unlike traditional financial products, CFDs do not involve the ownership or exchange of the underlying asset. This unique feature makes CFDs highly versatile instruments, popular for both long-term investment and short-term speculation.

How to Trade CFDs

When you trade CFDs, you’re essentially betting on the price movement of an asset. You can either choose to trade:

a) On an “up-down” basis, meaning whether the asset’s price will rise or fall. This is called a Long or Short position. b) On the exact price of the asset reaching a specific level within an agreed timeframe. This is known as an “Outright” trade.

Advantages of Trading CFDs

  • High liquidity: CFDs provide traders with the ability to trade on various global markets.
  • Flexibility: CFDs can be used for trading assets across various classes - stocks, indices, commodities, and currencies.
  • No ownership: The need to purchase expensive assets is eliminated, allowing investors to enjoy the benefits without the costs associated with owning them.

2. Futures Options

While most people are familiar with standard options contracts, futures options are somewhat less known, but equally important. A futures option is an agreement that gives the buyer the right to enter into a futures contract at a predetermined price, by a specific date. This is effectively a way to speculate on the future price of an asset without directly trading the futures.

How to Trade Futures Options

Trading futures options involves two main types of contracts – Call and Put options. A Call option gives you the right to buy an asset at a specific price, while a Put option offers you the right to sell an asset at a predetermined price. Successful futures option traders capitalize on price movements without having a directional view on whether the market will rise or fall.

Advantages of Futures Options

  • High leverage: Futures options offer substantial leverage, allowing traders to control large amounts of money with small initial investments.
  • Greater profit potential: With limited losses and potentially unlimited gains, futures options traders can benefit from significant returns.

3. Interest Rate Swaps

An interest rate swap is one of the most complex derivatives, where two parties agree to exchange interest rate payments linked to a specific currency and maturity based on a predetermined index or fix rate. This instrument is designed to hedge risks and realize potential gains in interest rate fluctuations.

How to Participate in Interest Rate Swaps

In order to engage in an interest rate swap, you need to enter into a derivative agreement with a counterparty who is willing to swap the interest payments. Typically, banks and large corporations are involved in such trades.

Advantages of Interest Rate Swaps

  • Risk management: Sweeping changes in interest rates can have a major impact on a company’s financial stability. By engaging in interest rate swaps, companies can hedge against such risks.
  • Cost savings: On the first glance, interest rate swaps may seem expensive. However, long-term, they can result in significant savings on funding costs and improve return on investment (ROI).

4. Total Return Swaps

A Total Return Swap (TRS) allows a borrower to obtain the benefit of an investment’s return, without having to own the underlying asset. This agreement usually exists between two parties - one party gains exposure to the returns of an asset that it does not own, while the other party gets the cashflows for the return of an asset it owns.

How to Trade Total Return Swaps

Enter into negotiations with willing counterparties to exchange the total return of an investment – capital gains, interest payments, dividends, and any other sources of return minus fees. You must monitor market conditions and adjust your strategy according to the performance of the asset in question.

Advantages of Total Return Swaps

  • Portfolio diversification: TRS enables investors to access a broader set of asset classes and widen potential returns.
  • Ownership-free: There’s no need to own the asset that you’re obtaining returns from.

5. Credit Derivatives

Credit derivatives allow investors to hedge against and speculate on corporate creditworthiness without directly owning the underlying bonds. These instruments include credit default swaps (CDS), which are agreements that provide protection against losses arising from default on a specific debt obligation.

How to Trade Credit Derivatives

To trade credit derivatives, you need to assess the credit quality of the corporations being tracked and establish a risk-reward analysis based on expected credit events. Find a counterparty to enter into a credit derivative agreement for the exchanges to take place.

Advantages of Credit Derivatives

  • Risk management: Credit derivatives enable financial institutions to hedge their exposure to credit risk and protect against potential losses.
  • Diversification: This investment option can help investors diversify their portfolio against losses arising from credit events in other asset classes.

In conclusion, it’s worth exploring the hidden gems that lie within the vast landscape of derivatives trading. Although these lesser-known strategies may appear daunting, they could offer remarkable opportunities to investors seeking diversification and risk management in their portfolios. As always, thorough research and due diligence are critical for any investment, and the proper knowledge and skills can help you navigate these waters successfully.
