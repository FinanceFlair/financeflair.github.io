Investing in Forgotten Stocks: A Contrarian's Guide


Investing in forgotten stocks can be a lucrative and rewarding experience for contrarian investors who are willing to delve into the uncharted territories of the market. These are the stocks that have been overlooked, under appreciated, and underpriced by the market, waiting to be rediscovered by investors who are not afraid to take a contrarian stance. Why Forgotten Stocks Exist There are several reasons why stocks become forgotten. One reason is that the market is inherently myopic, focusing on the leaders and the laggards, often ignoring the stocks that fall somewhere in between. This is because the media and the analysts tend to focus on the same set of stocks, creating a self-reinforcing echo chamber that excludes lesser-known stocks. Another reason is that forgotten stocks often lack the flashy stories, charismatic CEOs, or innovative products that grab the attention of investors. They might have had a stentorific past, but their present and future prospects are not as itchy, missing the wicking factor that draws investors to more notable stocks. Advantages of Investing in Forgotten Stocks Investing in forgotten stocks offers several advantages to contrarian investors. Firstly, these stocks are often undervalued, providing a lower entry point for investors. The lower price point reduces the risk of investment, allowing contrarian investors to buy more shares with the same amount of capital. Another advantage is that these stocks tend to have lower liquidity, making it easier for contrarian investors to liquidate their shares without moving the price. This lower liquidity also creates an environment where market forces are less influential, allowing contrarian investors to make more informed investment decisions. Finally, investing in forgotten stocks requires less time and effort to research, analyze, and monitor. The negligible public attention directed at these stocks means that contrarian investors must dig deeper to uncover the hidden value, providing a knowledge advantage over other investors. Characteristics of Forgotten Stocks Forgotten stocks often share several characteristics that contrarian investors look for. One key characteristic is a significant discount to their peers, indicating undervaluation. Another characteristic is a mild growth story, which means the company has stabilized and is unlikely to undergo a significant overhaul in the near future. Forgotten stocks also tend to have a low-turnover, demonstrating a clear disconnect between the market's perception and the company's fundamental health.
