The Best Online Resources for Small Business Funding


For small business owners, securing funding is often a pressing concern. Traditional banks and financial institutions may not always provide the necessary support, leaving entrepreneurs to explore alternative options. Fortunately, the digital age has spawned an array of online resources, designed to cater to the needs of small businesses seeking financial aid. In this post, we will delve into the most effective online platforms, lenders, and marketplaces that can help bridge the funding gap. Government-backed Resources The United States Government has a number of programs aimed at supporting small businesses. The Small Business Administration (SBA) is a premier destination for entrepreneurs looking for funding opportunities. The SBA provides two primary loan programs: the 7(a) loan and the Microloan program. These initiatives offer favorable terms, including lower interest rates and longer repayment periods, making them highly accessible to start-ups and small business owners. Another government-backed resource is the SCORE Association, a non-profit organization that offers free consulting and mentoring services to small business owners. SCORE aims to promote entrepreneurship and innovation by providing valuable guidance on business planning, marketing, and financial management. Crowdfunding Platforms Crowdfunding has revolutionized the way small businesses raise capital. Platforms like Kickstarter and Indiegogo have enabled entrepreneurs to turn their visions into reality by gathering funds from a large number of people, often in exchange for rewards or equity.

Kickstarter: A popular platform for creative projects, such as art, design, and technology. With over $500 million pledged since its inception, Kickstarter is an excellent option for innovative entrepreneurs seeking backing for their projects. Indiegogo: A versatile platform that allows creators to raise funds for products, experiences, and community-driven projects. Indiegogo offers flexible funding options, making it an attractive choice for start-ups and entrepreneurs.

Business Lending Marketplaces Online marketplaces have disrupted traditional lending practices by providing access to a wide range of funding options. These platforms review an applicant's business credit and creditworthiness, ensuring the most suitable match between the small business and lender.

Fundbox: A cloud-based lending platform that offers quick, affordable funding options for small business owners. Fundbox focuses on short-term loans, perfect for entrepreneurs who need to cover unexpected expenses or bridge gaps between pay cycles. StreetShares: A pioneer in small business lending, StreetShares provides a unique experience by streamlining the loan process and reducing risks for lenders. StreetShares combines low-cost capital solutions with industry growth, making it an attractive option for entrepreneurs looking for flexible, risk-free funding.

Business Lines of Credit For small business owners who need access to capital for ongoing projects or operational expenses, business lines of credit are an optimal choice. These lines of credit can help alleviate debt, provide working capital, or cover unexpected expenses.

BlueVine: A reputable financial services company that offers fast business lines of credit of up to $250,000. BlueVine uses an algorithm to qualify small business owners, allowing for expedited funding assemblies without the need for collateral or personal guaranties. Fundation: A pioneer in square micro-lending, Fundation disseminates manageable business lines of credit to small businesses lacking appropriate financial arrangements. Fundation opens paths to liquidity that other Lenders might avoid.

Capitalize on Alternative Funding Options In addition to government-backed initiatives and online marketplaces, small business owners can explore alternative funding options, including:

Invoice Financing: Companies that specialize in invoice financing help small businesses access cash for everyday operations or expansions by purchasing and managing invoices from the Entrepreneurs' practices owners. Equipment Financing: Companies specializing in equipment financing provide small businesses with access to machined industry hardware and manufacturing capabilities.
