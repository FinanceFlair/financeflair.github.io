Short Term Trading Secrets Unveiled: Proven Strategies


As a thriving and perpetually evolving market, trading has its ebbs and flows. The art of trading isn’t just about dipping in and out of stocks or investing in properties - it’s about when and how to do so with well-thought-out strategies. Short term trading, in particular, can be an appealing endeavor for many as it requires less commitment and consequently, offers more flexibility. In this blog, we will reveal some proven strategies that every trader should consider, along with some essential tips for success.

  1. Trade with the trend

It might seem obvious, but it’s one of the most overlooked strategies - trade with the flow of the market. This entails identifying trends and utilizing them to your advantage. In an upward market, buying pullbacks to moving averages can create buying opportunities. Conversely, during a downturn, selling breakouts into resistance can be a profitable move. However, be sure to consider the momentum of each market - not all of them move in the same direction.

  1. Risk Management is an absolute must

Risk management is synonymous with successful short term trading. Make sure to only risk a fraction of your overall capital on each trade. It can be devastating to disregard risk management and lose significantly on a single trade. Moreover, set stop-losses to minimize losses. Participating in every trade isn’t always the best strategy - sometimes it’s wise to standby with a “wait and see” attitude.

  1. Identifying and Trading Breakouts

Breakouts in price are easy to anticipate and react to, provided you have a good understanding of market structure. Once you’ve observed these breakouts, a secure trading strategy is essential. An effective way to maximize a breakout opportunity is the use of chart patterns like flag and pennants - they often denote trends and offer clear points of entry and exit.

  1. Utilizing Market Indicators

Confirming your trades with market indicators can be of great benefit. Inevitably, technical indicators are tools to assist with a more profound understanding of the market. They assist traders in anticipating price movements and improving predictive capabilities. Supply and demand analysis, RSI, and Stochastic Oscillator are just a few examples of indicators that can help find entry and exit points.

  1. Strategy Building and Consistency

Lastly, develop and adhere to a set of trading rules for long and short term trades. Methodically deciding upon and sticking to entry and exit points, risk management, and other rules creates a framework that brings discipline, focus, and consistency to short term trading. Over time, your strategies should evolve, but without a foundation or guidelines, your evolution could falter.

Patience and Discipline are the keys to success in short term trading. Profitable trading isn’t always about making the most trades. Instead, it is about smart trading, staying ahead of the trends, and positioning oneself to take advantage of them. Experiment with these proven strategies, develop your own, and always remember - the market is forever evolving, and strategies change with it.
