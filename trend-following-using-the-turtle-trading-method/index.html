Trend Following Using the Turtle Trading Method


Trend following is a timeless strategy embraced by traders aiming to capitalize on sustained market moves. Among the myriad of trend-following systems, the Turtle Trading method stands out as a legendary approach, famous for turning novices into successful traders. Rooted in a fascinating experiment from the early 1980s, this method emphasizes discipline, clear rules, and systematic trade execution. Here, we dive deep into the mechanics of the Turtle Trading strategy, its core principles, practical implementation, and tips to refine your trend-following journey.

The Origin of the Turtle Trading Method

Back in 1983, trading legend Richard Dennis believed that great traders were made, not born. To test this theory, he recruited a group of individuals from diverse backgrounds—dubbed the “Turtles”—and taught them a specific trend-following methodology over just two weeks. The results were astonishing; these rookies generated millions in profits using a systematic approach.

The Turtle Trading method hinges on capturing large market trends across futures markets, relying heavily on rigorous entry and exit rules, position sizing, and risk management. With proper execution, this strategy seeks to ride major price waves while cutting losses quickly.

Core Principles of the Turtle Trading Approach

At its heart, the Turtle Trading strategy involves:

1. Clear Entry Signals Based on Donchian Channels

The method uses Donchian Channels, a technical indicator that marks the highest high and lowest low over a specific lookback period. These channels highlight breakouts, which serve as the primary triggers for entering trades.

  • 20-day breakout: Entry when price breaches the highest high or lowest low of the past 20 days.
  • 55-day breakout: Secondary breakout, used in certain scenarios to increase position size or confirm trends.

The fundamental logic is simple: breakouts often precede sustained trends, so catching these early can maximize profits.

2. Position Sizing Through Volatility

Rather than allocating fixed capital amounts to trades, the Turtles used a volatility-adjusted position sizing method to normalize risk across diverse markets. They measured the Average True Range (ATR) of each contract to define a unit called N, representing the volatility-adjusted dollar risk.

By calculating position size as a function of ATR, the method ensures consistent risk per trade regardless of market volatility. This prevents over-leveraging in choppy markets and maximizes gains during strong trends.

3. Pyramid Scaling for Winners

A key feature of the strategy involves pyramiding, which means adding to winning positions in increments as trends strengthen. This tactic enhances returns during big moves while maintaining risk controls.

Each additional unit of risk is measured carefully, respecting predefined limits to avoid overexposure.

4. Strict Exit Rules

To protect capital, the Turtles adopted predefined stop-loss levels:

  • A 2N stop loss protects against adverse price moves on new entries.
  • A 10-day low exit ensures traders exit trending positions once momentum wanes.
  • A shorter 20-day breakout exit for position reversals or trend changes.

The disciplined use of these exit strategies helps avoid emotional decisions and large drawdowns.

Practical Guide to Implementing Turtle Trading

Implementing the Turtle Trading method today involves several thoughtful steps:

Step 1: Select Suitable Markets

Originally, the system was applied to futures markets including commodities, currencies, and indexes. Today, you can adapt the approach to stocks, ETFs, forex pairs, and cryptocurrencies, but ideally choose markets with good liquidity and clear trends.

Step 2: Establish Your Donchian Channels

Calculate the highest highs and lowest lows over the last 20 and 55 trading days. These will form your entry points:

  • Long Entry: Price closes above the 20-day highest high.
  • Short Entry: Price closes below the 20-day lowest low.

This straightforward breakout strategy works best in trending environments and can be enhanced with volume or momentum filters.

Step 3: Determine Position Size Using ATR

Calculate the 20-day ATR to gauge current volatility and risk range (N). Then, position size is determined by dividing your fixed risk capital by N multiplied by a predefined risk multiple.

For example, if your risk per trade is $1,000 and ATR is $2, your position size would be 500 units to maintain consistent dollar risk.

Step 4: Define Entry and Exit Rules

  • Enter on 20-day breakout signals.
  • Use a 2N stop loss below/above your entry price.
  • Add positions as the trend continues, respecting a maximum number of units (usually 4 units).
  • Exit on 10-day low or opposing 20-day breakout signals to capture trend reversals.

Step 5: Maintain a Trading Journal and Discipline

Meticulous record-keeping of trades, emotions, and outcomes ensures you stay accountable to the system’s rules and can refine your process over time.

Advantages of the Turtle Trading Method

  • Systematic and Objective: Rules-based approach removes guesswork and emotional biases.
  • Adaptable Across Markets: Works well on diverse assets that exhibit trending behavior.
  • Risk Controlled: Volatility-adjusted position sizing and strict stops guard capital.
  • Scalable: Applicable to various account sizes and adjustable for risk tolerance.
  • Proven Track Record: Backtesting and historical results support its efficacy.

Common Challenges and How to Overcome Them

Trend following and Turtle Trading aren’t magic bullets—they require patience and resilience. Some common obstacles include:

  • Whipsaws in Choppy Markets: The method struggles in sideways price action, causing small losses. Mitigate this with additional filters or reduced position sizes.
  • Psychological Pressure: Following rigid rules can be emotionally taxing during losing streaks. Building confidence through paper trading and gradual scaling helps.
  • Slow to React to Sharp Reversals: Markets can reverse quickly, leading to stop-outs. Combining trend following with momentum or volume analysis can enhance timing.
  • Time Commitment: Requires regular monitoring to adjust stops and entries.

Enhancing the Turtle Trading Strategy with Modern Techniques

Traders today can leverage technology to boost the classic Turtle method:

  • Algorithmic Execution: Automate entries, exits, and position sizing to ensure discipline and faster response.
  • Multi-Timeframe Analysis: Use longer timeframes to confirm trends before entering on shorter breakout signals.
  • Diversification Across Asset Classes: Spread risk by applying the method simultaneously to forex, commodities, and indexes.
  • Incorporate Volatility Regimes: Adjust parameters dynamically depending on current market volatility conditions for better adaptability.
  • Use Machine Learning: Apply ML to predict trend strength and filter false breakouts.

Final Thoughts

The Turtle Trading method remains one of the most iconic and educational trend-following systems in market history. Its blend of simplicity, mathematical rigor, and disciplined execution offers invaluable lessons for traders seeking to master trends. While no approach guarantees profits, adopting a systematic, risk-aware style like the Turtles’ can significantly improve your trading edge. Whether you’re a new trader or a seasoned pro, revisiting this method with fresh eyes and modern enhancements might just be the breakthrough in your trading journey.

Harness the waves of market trends confidently with the Turtle Trading method—and remember, patience and discipline are the greatest allies on this path to success.
