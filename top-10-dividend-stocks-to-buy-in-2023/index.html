Top 10 Dividend Stocks to Buy in 2023


Investing in dividend stocks has long been a favored approach for those looking to build passive income streams while benefiting from potential capital appreciation. Companies that pay dividends tend to have stable cash flows, established market positions, and a commitment to returning value to shareholders. As 2023 progresses, dividend investors have a diverse pool of options to consider. Whether you’re crafting a long-term strategy or seeking consistent income, here are the top 10 dividend stocks to explore this year.

1. Johnson & Johnson (JNJ)

Johnson & Johnson has been a pillar of stability within the healthcare industry for decades. Known for its robust lineup of consumer health products, medical devices, and pharmaceuticals, the company has consistently rewarded shareholders with dependable dividend growth. As a Dividend King, JNJ boasts over five decades of dividend increases. With its diverse revenue streams and strong commitment to innovation, the company remains one of the most reliable choices for dividend-focused investors in 2023.

Dividend Yield: Around 2.7%-3%
Why Consider It: Johnson & Johnson’s consistent revenue coupled with its strong history of payouts makes it a cornerstone of any dividend-oriented portfolio.

2. Procter & Gamble (PG)

Procter & Gamble is a global leader in consumer goods, with iconic brands such as Tide, Pampers, and Gillette under its umbrella. PG has a proven track record of delivering steady dividend increases, making it another Dividend King to have on your radar this year. The company benefits from its ability to adapt pricing and maintain strong demand for its inelastic products, even amid economic fluctuations.

Dividend Yield: Approximately 2.5%-2.7%
Why Consider It: A globally diversified product portfolio and resilient performance in various economic conditions ensure this stock’s place in a dividend portfolio.

3. Coca-Cola (KO)

Coca-Cola is one of the most recognizable brands in the world and offers a wide range of beverage products. The company’s ability to expand into healthier drink options and adapt to changing consumer preferences has bolstered its prospects. For dividend investors, Coca-Cola’s impressive history of consistent payouts and decades of annual dividend increases make it a safe choice.

Dividend Yield: Around 3%-3.2%
Why Consider It: KO’s global reach and an enviable history of reliable dividend payments appeal to cautious, income-seeking investors.

4. Realty Income (O)

Widely known as “The Monthly Dividend Company,” Realty Income is a favorite among real estate investment trust (REIT) enthusiasts. It owns and leases a diversified portfolio of retail and commercial properties, benefiting from long-term lease agreements that generate consistent cash flows. Its monthly payouts, coupled with a long history of dividend increases, make it a standout option for those looking for regular income.

Dividend Yield: Roughly 4.5%-5%
Why Consider It: Realty Income combines strong management with consistent income generation, ideal for investors seeking regular cash flow.

5. AbbVie (ABBV)

AbbVie is a prominent player in the pharmaceutical industry, particularly known for its blockbuster drug Humira. While competition has arisen from biosimilars, AbbVie is well-positioned with a promising pipeline and revenue diversification from newer drugs. With a healthy payout ratio and a shareholder-friendly dividend policy, AbbVie continues to offer attractive value to income investors.

Dividend Yield: Around 3.9%-4%
Why Consider It: ABBV offers an appealing combination of growth potential and high-yield dividends backed by strong fundamentals.

6. ExxonMobil (XOM)

ExxonMobil is a leader in the energy sector and has shown resilience over the years, weathering volatile oil prices and market disruptions. As global energy demand recovers, ExxonMobil has positioned itself favorably through investments in sustainable energy while delivering consistent dividend payouts. This makes it solid ground for those benefiting from the energy sector’s rebound.

Dividend Yield: Approximately 3.2%-3.5%
Why Consider It: A mix of strong cash flow, global footprint, and reliable dividends make XOM attractive for investors in 2023.

7. Verizon Communications (VZ)

Telecommunications giant Verizon continues to be a top choice for income-focused investors. With its essential services and growing investments in 5G infrastructure, Verizon offers stability and long-term growth potential. Despite sector-wide challenges, its generous dividend yield and robust cash flows are appealing characteristics.

Dividend Yield: Around 6%-6.5%
Why Consider It: Verizon’s comparatively higher yield and essential industry presence make it a compelling choice for high-income seekers.

8. Chevron (CVX)

Chevron complements ExxonMobil in the energy sector, standing out as another financially strong dividend-paying option. Having navigated the ups and downs of the oil market effectively, Chevron remains a reliable choice for dividend investors. Its diversified energy operations and ongoing projects in renewable energy make it a balanced pick for the current economic environment.

Dividend Yield: Around 3.5%-3.7%
Why Consider It: Chevron’s strong shareholder returns and forward-thinking investment strategy make it a safe bet within the energy dividend stock segment.

9. PepsiCo (PEP)

Rivaling Coca-Cola in the beverage space, PepsiCo goes beyond drinks with its extensive snack portfolio, including brands like Lay’s and Doritos. PepsiCo’s diversified product range has allowed it to outpace competitors and offer dependable returns to shareholders. With a long streak of annual dividend hikes, PEP is an excellent candidate for those seeking both dividend income and portfolio stability.

Dividend Yield: Roughly 2.5%-2.8%
Why Consider It: Stability, diversification, and a reliable history of payout hikes make this stock a well-rounded choice.

10. Microsoft (MSFT)

Although primarily thought of as a growth stock, Microsoft has quietly become a strong contender in the dividend space. As one of the most dominant companies in the world, it offers steady dividend hikes and unparalleled earnings growth from its cloud services, software, and hardware divisions. For those seeking a blend of income and growth, Microsoft is a top choice in 2023.

Dividend Yield: Around 0.9%-1%
Why Consider It: Despite its lower dividend yield, its growth potential and future payout resilience make it a cornerstone for any balanced portfolio.

Final Thoughts on Dividend Investing in 2023

Dividend stocks remain an excellent way to build wealth and generate income. While each of the companies listed above offers unique benefits, selecting the right investment depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and portfolio diversification needs. The key is to conduct thorough research and maintain a long-term focus. A balanced dividend portfolio with a mix of high yielders, growth-focused payers, and steady stalwarts can help you achieve your financial objectives while mitigating risk in unpredictable markets.
