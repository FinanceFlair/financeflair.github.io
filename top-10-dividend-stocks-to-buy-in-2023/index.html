Top 10 Dividend Stocks to Buy in 2023


Dividend investing has long been a favorite strategy for both novice and experienced investors. It combines the stability of income generation with the potential for capital appreciation. In 2023, with economic uncertainties influencing global markets, dividend stocks continue to shine as a beacon of reliable returns. In this post, we delve into 10 dividend stocks that stand out this year for their robust histories of payout growth, solid business fundamentals, and ability to weather market volatility.

1. Johnson & Johnson (JNJ)

Johnson & Johnson, a healthcare giant, remains a cornerstone in many portfolios. The company’s strong position in pharmaceuticals, consumer health products, and medical devices ensures a steady revenue stream. Its history of increasing dividends for over 60 consecutive years epitomizes financial discipline and resilience. The company’s emphasis on innovation in areas such as oncology and biologics ensures growth potential, making it a standout choice for income investors.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: Historically in the range of 2.8–3.2%.
  • Dividend Growth: Over 6 decades of consecutive annual increases.
  • Defensive Nature: Resilience during economic downturns.

2. Procter & Gamble (PG)

Procter & Gamble, a leader in consumer goods, owns a portfolio of trusted brands such as Tide, Gillette, and Pampers. Its products remain household staples, securing steady cash flow even during economic slumps. The company’s ability to adapt to changing consumer preferences, combined with a commitment to returning excess cash to shareholders, makes it a top dividend-paying stock.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: Around 2.5%.
  • Stable Earnings Base: High demand for consumer staples.
  • Dividend Aristocrat: Over 66 years of consecutive dividend increases.

3. Coca-Cola (KO)

Coca-Cola is synonymous with stability. As a global leader in beverages, it boasts a brand value that few companies can rival. With a network that spans over 200 countries and a continuously evolving product portfolio, Coca-Cola’s revenue stream remains robust. The company’s dividend record is exceptional, offering income investors peace of mind.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: Close to 3%.
  • Global Reach: Revenue diversification reduces risk.
  • Dividend King: Over 60 years of uninterrupted increases.

4. PepsiCo (PEP)

Part beverage company, part snack-food powerhouse, PepsiCo is another stalwart for dividend-hungry investors. Beyond its namesake cola, the company owns snack brands like Lay’s and Doritos, creating multiple income streams. Its ability to innovate, focusing on healthy snacks and low-sugar beverages, positions the company for long-term success in evolving markets.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: 2.5–2.8%.
  • Resilient Business Model: Demand for snacks and beverages is stable globally.
  • Strong Dividend History: Over 50 years of consistent increases.

5. ExxonMobil (XOM)

Despite being in a cyclical industry, ExxonMobil is a reliable dividend payer. The company’s focus on energy innovation, including a commitment to renewable energy initiatives, speaks to its adaptability. With soaring energy demand, ExxonMobil provides not only stable dividends but also potential for capital gains.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: Above 3.5%.
  • Recovery in Energy Markets: Higher oil and natural gas prices boost profits.
  • Dividend Growth: Over 40 years of consistency.

6. Verizon Communications (VZ)

In the telecommunications sector, Verizon remains a standout for income investors. The company’s reliable cash flow is tied to its dominant U.S. market position and the high demand for wireless communication services. Its investments in 5G technology amplify its growth potential, while its hefty dividends appeal to long-term investors seeking stable cash flows.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: Between 6–7%.
  • Growth Catalyst: Heavy investment in 5G infrastructure.
  • Consistent History: A strong track record of reliable payouts.

7. Realty Income (O)

Known as “The Monthly Dividend Company,” Realty Income is a real estate investment trust (REIT) with a unique appeal: monthly dividend payments. The firm owns a diversified portfolio of income-generating properties leased to tenants like Walgreens, Walmart, and FedEx. Its business model thrives on long-term leases, ensuring predictable cash flow.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: Approximately 4.5%.
  • Monthly Payouts: Ideal for those seeking regular income.
  • Resilience: Defensive industries make up much of its tenant base.

8. NextEra Energy (NEE)

As a leader in renewable energy, NextEra Energy combines innovation with a strong dividend payout. Its emphasis on renewable power generation distinguishes it from other utility companies. A growing focus on sustainability aligns with global trends, making it attractive for ESG-conscious investors.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: Around 2%.
  • Growth Potential: Significant investment in solar and wind energy projects.
  • Utility Sector Strength: Stability in energy demand ensures consistent revenue.

9. AbbVie Inc. (ABBV)

Once a spin-off from Abbott Laboratories, AbbVie has carved out a niche in the pharmaceutical industry. Known for blockbuster drugs like Humira, the company has managed to diversify its revenue streams through acquisitions and new product launches. AbbVie’s commitment to rewarding shareholders through dividends and buybacks adds to its appeal.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: Approximately 4%.
  • Strong Pipeline: Expanding drug portfolio reduces reliance on any single product.
  • Dividend Growth: Consistent increases every year since the spin-off.

10. 3M Company (MMM)

Despite some challenges in recent years, 3M remains a reliable dividend-paying investment. Its diverse product lineup, from industrial adhesives to personal protective equipment, secures steady cash flows. With over a century of operation, 3M’s ability to adapt and innovate will likely continue providing shareholder rewards.

Why It’s a Good Pick:

  • Dividend Yield: Around 5%.
  • Dividend Aristocrat: More than 60 years of consecutive payout increases.
  • Diversified Revenue Streams: Broad market exposure helps mitigate risks.

Final Thoughts

Dividend investing in 2023 offers opportunities to generate reliable income while participating in long-term growth. The top 10 stocks highlighted above stand out for their stability, resilience, and commitment to rewarding shareholders. Whether you are a seasoned investor or just starting, incorporating some of these dividend stalwarts into your portfolio could enhance your strategy. Always ensure your investment choices align with your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. As with any investment, doing thorough due diligence is crucial.

By focusing on reliable businesses with consistent dividends, you can create a steady stream of income and enjoy peace of mind, even in uncertain markets.
