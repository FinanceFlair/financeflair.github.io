Top 10 Impact Investment Funds to Watch


Impact investing has grown from a niche market into a vital sector in finance, attracting a broader base of investors who seek not just financial returns but measurable social and environmental benefits. As the world grapples with climate change, social inequality, and sustainable development challenges, impact investment funds are becoming pivotal players driving positive change while delivering competitive financial results.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the top 10 impact investment funds that are making waves in 2024. These funds have been selected based on their diversified portfolios, innovative approaches, strong track records, and commitment to transparency and accountability.

What Makes an Impact Investment Fund Stand Out?

Before diving into the list, it’s essential to understand what differentiates top-tier impact funds from the rest:

  • Measurable Impact: They track and report clear social or environmental outcomes.
  • Financial Performance: They balance impact goals with attractive risk-adjusted returns.
  • Diversification: They invest across sectors and geographies to mitigate risks.
  • Transparency: Open disclosure of investment criteria, impact metrics, and stakeholder engagement.
  • Innovation: Pioneering new approaches, tools, and industries in impact investing.

Now, let’s uncover the leaders carving the path forward.

1. Generation Investment Management

Founded by Al Gore and David Blood, Generation Investment Management has been at the forefront of sustainable investing for over two decades. Their funds integrate rigorous sustainability research into traditional financial analysis, prioritizing companies that are managing environmental, social, and governance (ESG) risks thoughtfully.

  • Key Sectors: Clean energy, sustainable agriculture, technology innovation.
  • Impact Focus: Climate change mitigation, corporate sustainability.
  • Highlights: Their flagship Sustainability Fund holds a diverse portfolio of growth-stage companies actively reducing their carbon footprint while delivering steady returns.

Generation’s blend of in-depth research and long-term horizon investment philosophy makes it a consistent choice for those seeking environmental innovation blended with financial stability.

2. BlueOrchard Finance

BlueOrchard specializes in inclusive finance and has become a trailblazer in investing in microfinance institutions, SMEs, and climate-related projects, particularly in emerging markets.

  • Key Sectors: Microfinance, renewable energy, affordable housing.
  • Impact Focus: Financial inclusion, poverty alleviation, climate resilience.
  • Highlights: The BlueOrchard Microfinance Fund has remarkably supported millions of underserved households by funding small businesses that generate income and improve living standards.

Investors attracted to sustainable development and global poverty reduction will find BlueOrchard’s hands-on approach and vast network compelling.

3. Triodos Investment Management

Triodos Bank launched Triodos Investment Management with the vision of financing companies that benefit people and the environment. Their funds are characterized by strict exclusion criteria and a focus on impact sectors.

  • Key Sectors: Renewable energy, organic agriculture, social housing.
  • Impact Focus: Environmental sustainability, healthy living, community development.
  • Highlights: Their Renewable Energy Fund has invested in solar, wind, and hydro projects across Europe, facilitating a clean energy transition while delivering consistent dividend income.

Triodos is celebrated for its high ethical standards and for actively involving investors in understanding the real-world effects of their investments.

4. Parnassus Endeavor Fund

This fund targets companies with excellent ESG practices and a focus on sustainable growth rather than short-term gains.

  • Key Sectors: Healthcare, technology, consumer services.
  • Impact Focus: Community wellness, sustainable corporate behavior.
  • Highlights: Parnassus actively avoids industries detrimental to society such as tobacco and fossil fuels, instead investing in firms with strong governance and social responsibility.

Its strong performance record draws ethically minded investors who don’t want to sacrifice return potential.

5. BlackRock Impact Opportunities Fund

As a giant in asset management, BlackRock’s entry into impact investing with the Impact Opportunities Fund has created a significant market shift.

  • Key Sectors: Green infrastructure, sustainable transport, clean water.
  • Impact Focus: Climate solutions, urban sustainability.
  • Highlights: Leveraging BlackRock’s vast resources and data analytics, the fund invests in scalable projects with strong carbon impact metrics.

The fund’s magnitude and integration with mainstream markets make it a notable option for investors seeking exposure to large-scale sustainable development.

6. Calvert Impact Capital’s Community Investment Notes

Calvert is synonymous with impact bonds and notes that finance social impact projects with clear financial and social returns.

  • Key Sectors: Community development, affordable housing, sustainable agriculture.
  • Impact Focus: Low-income community support, climate resilience.
  • Highlights: Their flagship Community Investment Notes channel investor capital to organizations that improve lives while offering periodic interest payments.

Calvert’s model appeals to investors wanting liquid, fixed-income products aligned with social goals.

7. ImpactAssets Managed Solutions

ImpactAssets offers a curated selection of impact funds through its Managed Solutions vehicle, enabling tailored portfolios aligned with specific impact themes.

  • Key Sectors: Renewable resources, education, healthcare.
  • Impact Focus: Access to healthcare, renewable resource management.
  • Highlights: Their approach provides flexibility for high-net-worth investors and institutions seeking diversified impact exposure across private debt and equity.

Their personalized strategies and emphasis on clear impact metrics offer comprehensive diversification without losing depth.

8. Impax Asset Management

Focused on the transition to a more sustainable economy, Impax targets companies positioned to benefit from environmental change.

  • Key Sectors: Waste management, water technology, renewable energy.
  • Impact Focus: Resource efficiency, pollution reduction.
  • Highlights: Their Impax Environmental Markets Fund invests globally in firms contributing to resource efficiency and sustainability.

Impax is favored by investors aligned with environmental innovation and long-term green growth.

9. Root Capital

Root Capital specializes in financing agricultural enterprises in developing economies, addressing food security and rural development.

  • Key Sectors: Agriculture, sustainable forestry.
  • Impact Focus: Smallholder farmer livelihoods, ecosystem preservation.
  • Highlights: By supporting agricultural businesses, Root Capital drives increased income for rural communities while promoting sustainable farming practices.

Its focus on rural economies and direct community benefits resonates with impact investors focused on social and ecological balance.

10. The Renewable Energy Performance Platform (REPP)

REPP works by mobilizing private capital to fund renewable energy projects across Africa.

  • Key Sectors: Solar, mini-grid, hydroelectric power.
  • Impact Focus: Energy access, climate mitigation.
  • Highlights: REPP combines grants and investments to lower risks and promote affordable green energy infrastructure in underserved areas.

Investors seeking frontier markets with scalable green solutions find REPP’s hybrid funding model particularly innovative.

Final Thoughts on Impact Investment Funds in 2024

The surge of interest in impact investing reflects a broader acknowledgment that capital deployed wisely can drive meaningful change without compromising returns. The funds listed above represent some of the most promising vehicles combining innovation, rigorous impact measurement, and strong financial discipline.

When evaluating impact investment funds, it’s essential to look beyond marketing claims and assess their operational transparency, alignment with your values, and proven performance. Impact investing is not magic—it requires due diligence and a long-term mindset to see both societal benefits and portfolio growth.

For investors eager to contribute to a sustainable future, these funds offer a map to meaningful opportunities that stretch across the economy and the globe.

How to Choose the Right Impact Fund for You

  • Assess Your Impact Priorities: Climate, social equity, clean energy, or community development.
  • Understand Your Risk Tolerance: Impact funds range from venture capital style investments to conservative fixed income.
  • Review Track Records and Reporting: Look for funds that publish audited impact reports and financial statements.
  • Consider Geographic Focus: Some funds prioritize emerging markets, others mature economies.
  • Engage With Fund Managers: Transparency and communication are critical for sustained impact relationships.

The world of impact investing is vibrant and evolving, offering exciting pathways to generate positive change and financial growth simultaneously. Whether you’re a seasoned investor or just exploring, the ten funds outlined here provide a solid foundation for impact investing success in 2024 and beyond.
