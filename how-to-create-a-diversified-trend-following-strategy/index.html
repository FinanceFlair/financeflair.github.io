How to Create a Diversified Trend Following Strategy


As an investor, there's no shortage of conflicting advice on how to make the most of your money. While some experts swear by buy-and-hold strategies, others tout the benefits of active trading. However, one approach that's gained significant attention in recent years is trend following – a systematic method of investing that seeks to identify and ride prevailing market trends. While trend following can be a highly effective way to grow your wealth, it's essential to approach this strategy with caution. The fact is, no single trend following system is foolproof, and relying on a single trade can be catastrophic. That's where diversification comes in – the process of spreading investments across various assets to minimize potential losses and maximize returns. Why Diversify a Trend Following Strategy? Diversifying a trend following strategy is crucial for several reasons:

Risk management: By diversifying across multiple assets, you can reduce the impact of a single poor trade on your overall portfolio. Increased potential for returns: When you're diversified, you can take advantage of multiple trends at once, potentially leading to higher returns. Improved consistency: A diversified strategy can provide more consistent results, as some assets will inevitably perform well during times of market stress.

Building a Diversified Trend Following Portfolio Creating a diversified trend following portfolio requires a thoughtful approach. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Identify Your Trading Markets The first step in building a diversified trend following portfolio is to identify the markets you want to trade. Popular options include:

Forex: With its vast liquidity and 24/5 trading schedule, the foreign exchange market is a popular choice for trend followers. Commodities: Crude oil, gold, and other commodities can offer interesting trends, particularly during market stress events. ** Stocks**: Many investors find success trading stocks in the major indices, such as the S&P 500 and Dow Jones Industrial Average. Cryptocurrencies: Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies have garnered attention in recent years, offering unique opportunities for trend followers.

  1. Choose Your Trend Following Methods With your trading markets in mind, it's time to select the trend following methods you'll employ. Some popular choices include:

Momentum indicators: Tools like the Relative Strength Index (RSI) can help identify overbought or oversold conditions, while momentum indicators like the Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) can signal trend reversals. Price action: By analyzing price movement, you can identify trends and trade opportunities. Fundamental analysis: Some trend followers rely on fundamental analysis to inform their trades, focusing on economic indicators like GDP growth and interest rates.

  1. Select Your Investment Vehicles Once you've settled on the markets and trend following methods, it's time to choose your investment vehicles. Popular options include:

Futures: These contracts obligate the buyer to purchase an asset at a specified price on a specific date, making them ideal for trend following. ETFs: Exchange-traded funds offer a convenient way to invest in a diversified portfolio of assets, such as stocks or commodities. Managed futures accounts: These accounts allow you to trade with a managed futures provider, who will execute trades on your behalf based on pre-defined rules.

  1. Determine Your Risk Management Strategy Risk management is a vital component of any trend following strategy. Here are a few considerations:

Position sizing: This involves allocating a fixed percentage of your capital to each trade, helping to minimize potential losses. Stop-loss orders: These orders can help limit losses by automatically closing a trade when it reaches a specified price. Maximum drawdown: This metric represents the largest decline in your portfolio over a specific period, helping you gauge your overall risk exposure.

  1. Continuously Monitor and Adjust Finally, it's essential to regularly review and adjust your diversified trend following portfolio. This involves:

Market analysis: Continuously monitor market conditions and adjust your strategy as needed. Performance evaluation: Assess the performance of your portfolio and make adjustments to optimize returns. Risk rebalancing: Periodically review your risk levels and rebalance your portfolio to maintain an optimal risk profile.

Conclusion Creating a diversified trend following strategy requires a nuanced approach, emphasizing the importance of risk management, market selection, trend following methods, and investment vehicles. By following the steps outlined above, you can build a robust and adaptable portfolio that positions you for success in an ever-changing marketscape.
