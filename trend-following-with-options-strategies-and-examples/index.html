Trend Following with Options: Strategies and Examples


Trend following is a popular trading strategy that involves identifying and following the direction of a market trend. This approach can be applied to various financial instruments, including options. In this blog post, we will explore the concept of trend following with options, discuss various strategies, and provide examples to illustrate the concept.

What is Trend Following?

Trend following is a trading strategy that involves identifying the direction of a market trend and then following it. This approach is based on the idea that markets tend to move in trends, and by identifying and following these trends, traders can profit from the movement.

What are Options?

Options are a type of derivative instrument that gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a specified price on or before a certain date. Options can be used to speculate on the price movement of an underlying asset, hedge against potential losses, or generate income.

Combining Trend Following with Options

Combining trend following with options can be a powerful approach to trading. By identifying the direction of a market trend and using options to profit from that trend, traders can potentially generate significant returns. Here are some strategies that can be used to combine trend following with options:

1. Trend Following with Call Options

In this strategy, traders identify a trend in the underlying asset and then buy call options on that asset. The idea is to profit from the upward movement of the trend by buying call options at a strike price below the current market price.

For example, let’s say a trader identifies a strong upward trend in the price of gold. The trader buys a call option on gold with a strike price of $1,500 and a expiration date of one month. If the price of gold continues to rise, the value of the call option will increase, and the trader can sell it at a profit.

2. Trend Following with Put Options

In this strategy, traders identify a trend in the underlying asset and then buy put options on that asset. The idea is to profit from the downward movement of the trend by buying put options at a strike price above the current market price.

For example, let’s say a trader identifies a strong downward trend in the price of the S&P 500 index. The trader buys a put option on the S&P 500 index with a strike price of 2,500 and an expiration date of one month. If the price of the S&P 500 index continues to fall, the value of the put option will increase, and the trader can sell it at a profit.

3. Trend Following with Covered Calls

In this strategy, traders identify a trend in the underlying asset and then sell covered calls on that asset. The idea is to profit from the upward movement of the trend by selling covered calls at a strike price above the current market price.

For example, let’s say a trader identifies a strong upward trend in the price of Apple stock. The trader buys 100 shares of Apple stock and then sells a covered call option with a strike price of $200 and an expiration date of one month. If the price of Apple stock continues to rise, the value of the covered call option will increase, and the trader can sell it at a profit.

4. Trend Following with Protective Puts

In this strategy, traders identify a trend in the underlying asset and then buy protective puts on that asset. The idea is to protect against a potential decline in the trend by buying protective puts at a strike price below the current market price.

For example, let’s say a trader identifies a strong upward trend in the price of Amazon stock. The trader buys 100 shares of Amazon stock and then buys a protective put option with a strike price of $2,000 and an expiration date of one month. If the price of Amazon stock suddenly falls, the value of the protective put option will increase, and the trader can sell it at a profit.

Conclusion

Trend following with options can be a powerful approach to trading. By identifying the direction of a market trend and using options to profit from that trend, traders can potentially generate significant returns. The strategies outlined in this blog post, including trend following with call options, put options, covered calls, and protective puts, can be used to combine trend following with options and generate profits in various market conditions.
