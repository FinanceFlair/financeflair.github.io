From Paris to Pittsburgh: How Sustainable Investing Is Riding the Wave of Climate Change Legislation


The Paris Agreement, signed in 2015, has been a turning point in recognizing the impact of climate change on our planet. As communities worldwide confront the challenges brought on by climate change, the global shift towards sustainable investing has emerged as a driving force. In this article, we’ll examine the growing trend of sustainable investing, and its ability to ride the wave of climate change legislation.

Investment based on socially responsible goals has been around for several decades. However, the heightened awareness of climate change has thrust it into the mainstream. This newfound prominence is driven by three main factors: increased public concern, technological advancements, and regulatory changes. As more people become aware of the impact of climate change, sustainable investing has grown into an $8 trillion market (Lavored, 2021) in response.

The Paris Agreement, signed in 2015, has played a significant role in this growth. In the years that followed, cities and states alike began crafting local legislation, ultimately forming a line from Paris to Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Legislation such as California’s cap-and-trade system and New York’s Green Bank demonstrate that jurisdictions around the world are moving towards a more sustainable approach. These and other related policies create incentives for companies to adopt more eco-friendly practices, directing investments accordingly.

Technological innovations have also been a prominent driver in the growth of sustainable investing. Groundbreaking advancements, such as battery technology for electric vehicles and efficient renewable energy sources, have become the standard for what’s possible in the realm of sustainable investments.

Regulatory changes also play an important role in directing investment. As governments globally pursue net-zero emissions, they create an imperative for businesses to transition to clean energy. For instance, in June 2020, the United States Interior Department indicated a ban on new oil and gas leasing in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge (ANWR) (U.S. Department of the Interior, 2020). Actions like these spur on sustainable investing to fill the void left by fossil fuels, thereby fostering a market of sustainable investing opportunities.

Sustainable investing can manifest in various forms, including negative screening, impact investing, and environmental, social, and governance (ESG) integration. Negative screening involves avoiding investments in industries or companies with negative environmental or social impacts. conversely, impact investing entails actively seeking out investments that have a measurable, positive impact. Meanwhile, ESG integration assesses companies based on environmental, social, and governance performance alongside traditional financial criteria.

The shift towards sustainable investing has already begun, with large fund managers such as BlackRock and Vanguard taking significant steps to align their portfolios with net-zero emissions by 2050 or sooner (Fisk, 2021). They’ve been joined by an array of other companies that factor in ESG data when making investment decisions.

In spite of these positive developments, the road to broader sustainable investing adoption remains one fraught with challenges. Companies often require more extensive disclosure of pertinent data, especially when it comes to ESG information. Still, as regulators continue to demand transparency in reporting and the private sector seeks sustainable solutions, data gaps are expected to close.

In conclusion, the sustainable investment market has grown rapidly in response to climate change concerns and the push towards a more sustainable world. As a result, it has formed a strong alliance with climate change legislation in cities and countries, alike. As governments worldwide ramp up their commitment to net-zero emissions, they must encourage sustainable investing and create a more level playing field for companies that commit to these goals. The future of our planet depends on it. And for those willing to participate in this growing and vital trend, the prospects are indeed promising.
