Transportation Trends in Developed Markets


There’s little doubt that throughout history, developed markets have played an essential role in shaping the way people travel. From horse-drawn carriages to the runaway success of the automobile and the dawn of heavy-haul railway systems, they have set global transportation trends that continue to shape what we’re seeing in today’s burgeoning automotive and transit industries. And as we look at the rapidly evolving landscape of urban mobility we must ask, what’s next?

The Emergence of Electric Vehicles ðŸš—

In developed nations, the shift is clearly under way towards more environmentally conscious modes of transport. In Europe, for instance, electric vehicles now account for around one in every four cars on the road. One primary driver behind this trend is the increasing efforts to curb carbon emissions, spawned from environmentally roused policy decisions. This is no more abundantly clear than with the crackdown on traditional fossil fuels, in particular, diesel which finds itself at the center of the European Union’s Climate Change discussions.

Automakers, sensing the prevalent winds, have also reshaped their portfolios to ride the EV wave, with electric, or at least electrified models powering their expansion strategies forwards. Major car manufacturers, like Volkswagen, BMW, and Renault, are powering their businesses forward with electric, zero-emission vehicles. From star performers like the Tesla Model 3 to household names like the Nissan Leaf and Volkswagen e-Golf, consumers in developed countries are no longer being offered just one option when it comes to eco-friendly motoring, but a veritable smorgasbord of choice.

Riding the Bus (Again) ðŸšŒ

With a landmark study from the International Energy Agency suggesting that by 2030, EVs will account for over 50% of new car sales globally, one could be forgiven for overlooking the resurgence of alternative transit options. One such option: public transport.

In cities, at least, the traditional buses that plied our roads not so long ago are making a comeback after some time in the transportation wilderness. As cities grew denser, people began moving away from the traditional status symbol of car ownership. Urban, bicycle-friendly spaces have since mushroomed, fostering a greater appreciation of modal shift towards eco-friendly transport options. Here, bus rapid transit systems, or BRTs, are emerging as the go-to alternative.

From the streets of BogotÃ¡, Colombia, to Johannesburg, South Africa, BRTs are proving their mettle as an efficient, environmentally friendly, and affordable way to ferry hoi-poloi around. Meanwhile, these systems are finding different guises elsewhere. In London, for example, “Boris Bikes” flooded the city, offering cyclists an additional mode of transport, cheap, green, and readily accessible.

Beyond the Roads ðŸš¦

Even as cars themselves are undergoing revolutionary changes, so too are the domains wherein we use them. In places like San Francisco, where the talk is increasingly about “micromobility,” we find smaller, more nimble transport vehicles aimed at handling the tourist-choked streets. Scooters, bikes, and even skateboards offer alternatives to cars, both traditional and electric. They engage pedestrians and cyclists alike. Thanks to advances in technology, these alternative mobility options are also becoming increasingly tracked, managed, and regulated, ensuring smooth and secure rides.

Final Thoughts on Transport Trends ðŸš¦

As we have seen, developed countries are facing a fascinating set of challenges and opportunities when it comes to transportation. From the rise of electric vehicles to the revitalization of public transit options, efficient and sustainable mobility solutions are becoming more popular than ever before. But there’s still work to be done. The way we move around our cities will continue to evolve, thanks to a host of technological and policy-driven developments. Consequently, it is more important than ever for governments, citizens, and automakers alike to continue addressing the changing landscape of sustainable transportation, ensuring a greener, more efficient urban environment for all.
