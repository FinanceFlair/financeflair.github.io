Intertemporal Choice and Time Preferences: Exploring Behavioral Economics


From street vendors offering discounts ’today only’ to our own procrastination on important life decisions, our choices are often influenced by time. This intricate relationship between choice and temporal perspective falls under ‘Intertemporal Choice and Time Preferences’ in behavioral economics. Join me as we discover how this concept shaped our buying habits, understanding of patience, and much more!

As Daniel Kahneman emphasizes in his Nobel Lecture, behavioral economics delves into the psychological aspects of individual decision-making. Our common struggle with impulsive buying or procrastination is rooted in our perception of time and its value, a field known as Intertemporal Choice. Put simply, it studies how people make decisions when they face multiple options across different timeframes.

Temporal preferences, the way we value the present versus the future, significantly impact these choices. People are either impatient with ‘present-biased preferences,’ favoring immediate gratification over delayed benefits, or patient with ‘consistent preferences,’ valuing long-term rewards equally. Interestingly, youngsters tend to be more impatient, a contrast to traditional economic theories.

Moreover, our understanding of time’s variability shapes these preferences. Perceiving time as ‘inconsistent’ (ever-changing) may lead to impulsive choices, whereas perceiving it as ‘consistent’ (stable) might contribute to patience.

Consider the college graduate juggling student loan debts. If they perceive time as inconsistent, they might prioritize immediate debt reduction over longer-term saving plans, risking reduced wealth accumulation. However, if they perceive time as consistent and stable, they may elect to patiently contribute to their retirement savings.

Erica’s story further uncovers this phenomenon. She received a limited time offer for a gym membership at a discounted rate. Having a present-biased preference, Erica hastily signed up despite her crowded schedule, instead of being patient and waiting for a future membership sale. The role of limited-time offers demystifies the notion of impatience and captures the essence of Intertemporal Choice.

Quantifying these time preferences, a scientist would employ techniques like Discounted Utility Theory. This method measures the value customers place on delayed outcomes, similar to how we calculate compound interest. Unfortunately, our impulsive brains often trump rational financial strategies, making us fall prey to ’time inconsistent’ decisions.

To keep your head above the temporal tides of these behavioral traps, the key is to be aware. Acknowledging one’s impatience and its implications can guide us towards more informed and profitable decisions. By embracing these concepts, we can resist the siren call of immediate rewards and navigate confidently towards our long-term goals.

Behavioral economics provides exciting insights into understanding and managing time preferences. Unraveling the secrets of Intertemporal Choice, we may not only tailor our strategies but also enhance our understanding of human behavior’s temporal dimensions. From classroom to marketplace, these lessons contribute towards more logical and beneficial choices for everyone.

As we demystify time’s impact on our decisions, remember to keep your eyes on the prizes and your mind on the present. Embrace the future by staying grounded in today’s choices, aiming for long-term benefits without ignoring the immediate gratification. Let’s explore behavioral economics not as mere theories, but as practical guides in our continuous pursuit of rational decision-making.
