Trend Following with Stocks: Strategies and Examples


Trend following is a popular investment strategy that involves identifying and following the direction of market trends. This approach is based on the idea that markets tend to move in trends, and that by identifying and following these trends, investors can make profitable trades. In this post, we will explore the concept of trend following, discuss the different strategies and techniques used in this approach, and provide examples of how it can be applied to the stock market.

What is Trend Following?

Trend following is a trading strategy that is based on the idea that markets tend to move in trends, and that by identifying and following these trends, investors can make profitable trades. This approach involves using technical analysis to identify the direction of the market and then using that information to make trading decisions.

There are two main types of trends: uptrends and downtrends. An uptrend is a trend in which the market is moving upward, while a downtrend is a trend in which the market is moving downward. Trend followers use a variety of techniques to identify these trends, including moving averages, relative strength index (RSI), and Bollinger Bands.

Strategies for Trend Following

There are several strategies that can be used for trend following, including:

1. Moving Average Crossover

The moving average crossover strategy involves using two moving averages of different lengths to identify trends. The shorter moving average is used to identify the trend, while the longer moving average is used to confirm the trend. When the shorter moving average crosses above the longer moving average, it is a buy signal, and when it crosses below, it is a sell signal.

2. Relative Strength Index (RSI)

The RSI strategy involves using the RSI indicator to identify overbought and oversold conditions in the market. When the RSI reaches a high level, it is considered overbought, and when it reaches a low level, it is considered oversold. Trend followers use this information to identify trends and make trading decisions.

3. Bollinger Bands

The Bollinger Bands strategy involves using the Bollinger Bands indicator to identify trends. The indicator consists of a moving average and two standard deviations. When the price of the stock is above the upper band, it is considered overbought, and when it is below the lower band, it is considered oversold. Trend followers use this information to identify trends and make trading decisions.

4. Channel Trading

The channel trading strategy involves identifying a trend channel and trading within that channel. The channel is identified by drawing a trend line that connects the highest and lowest points of the trend. Trend followers use this information to identify trends and make trading decisions.

Examples of Trend Following with Stocks

1. Apple (AAPL)

In 2019, Apple’s stock price began to trend upward, reaching a high of over $200 per share. Trend followers who identified this trend and bought the stock at the beginning of the year would have made a significant profit.

2. Amazon (AMZN)

3. Microsoft (MSFT)

In 2018, Microsoft’s stock price began to trend upward, reaching a high of over $140 per share. Trend followers who identified this trend and bought the stock at the beginning of the year would have made a significant profit.

4. Alphabet (GOOGL)

In 2019, Alphabet’s stock price began to trend downward, reaching a low of around $1,000 per share. Trend followers who identified this trend and sold the stock at the beginning of the year would have avoided a significant loss.

Conclusion

Trend following is a popular investment strategy that involves identifying and following the direction of market trends. This approach is based on the idea that markets tend to move in trends, and that by identifying and following these trends, investors can make profitable trades. There are several strategies that can be used for trend following, including moving average crossover, RSI, Bollinger Bands, and channel trading. By understanding these strategies and using them in conjunction with technical analysis, investors can make informed trading decisions and potentially achieve higher returns.

In this post, we have discussed the concept of trend following, the different strategies and techniques used in this approach, and provided examples of how it can be applied to the stock market. By understanding trend following and how it can be used to identify and follow market trends, investors can make more informed trading decisions and potentially achieve higher returns.
