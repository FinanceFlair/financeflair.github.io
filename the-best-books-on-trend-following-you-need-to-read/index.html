The Best Books on Trend Following You Need to Read


Trend following is a captivating trading strategy that capitalizes on the momentum of market trends. For those looking to dive deep into this approach, a wealth of literature exists to guide traders of all levels. Here, we explore the best books on trend following, offering insights and actionable strategies that can help you navigate the complexities of financial markets.

1. Trend Following: How to Make a Fortune in the Stock Market by Michael W. Covel

Michael W. Covel’s Trend Following is often considered the definitive guide on the subject. This book is not just for traders but for anyone interested in understanding the principles of momentum investing. Covel distills the wisdom gained from successful trend traders throughout the decades, highlighting the psychological aspects of trading.

Key Takeaways:

  • Systematic Approach: Covel emphasizes the importance of having a systematic approach to trading, focusing on rules rather than emotions.
  • Case Studies: The book features numerous case studies of successful traders, providing real-world examples of how trend following works in practice.
  • Risk Management: Covel discusses risk management strategies that are crucial for long-term success.

2. The Complete TurtleTrader: How 23 Novice Investors Became Overnight Millionaires by Michael W. Covel

In The Complete TurtleTrader, Covel delves into the famous Turtle Trading experiment orchestrated by Richard Dennis. This book not only narrates how a group of novice traders turned into millionaires but also encapsulates the core strategies behind their success—strategies grounded in trend following.

Key Takeaways:

  • Turtle Trading Rules: The Turtle Traders followed a strict set of rules that can be applied today, focusing on entry and exit points based on trend identification.
  • Psychological Insights: Understanding the psychological barriers faced by traders is crucial; Covel sheds light on the mental discipline required for successful trading.
  • Historical Context: The book provides a fascinating historical context, illustrating how trend following evolved over time.

3. Following the Trend: Diversified Managed Futures Trading by Andreas F. Clenow

Andreas Clenow’s Following the Trend is a comprehensive guide to implementing a trend-following strategy using managed futures. Clenow, a hedge fund manager, breaks down complex concepts into digestible insights suitable for both novice and experienced traders.

Key Takeaways:

  • Managed Futures: Clenow explains how managed futures can serve as an effective vehicle for trend following, offering diversification and risk management.
  • Data-Driven Decisions: The emphasis on statistical analysis and data-driven decision-making sets this book apart.
  • Practical Strategies: Clenow provides practical trading strategies that can be immediately applied, backed by historical performance data.

4. The New Trading for a Living by Dr. Alexander Elder

Dr. Alexander Elder’s classic, The New Trading for a Living, has been a staple in trading literature since its release. While it covers various trading strategies, its treatment of trend following is particularly notable.

Key Takeaways:

  • Holistic Approach: Elder integrates psychology, technical analysis, and trading systems into one cohesive framework.
  • Risk Control: The author emphasizes the importance of risk control, particularly in volatile markets where trends can shift abruptly.
  • Tools for Traders: Elders provides a toolkit of indicators and strategies to identify and capitalize on trends.

5. Trading Systems and Methods by Perry J. Kaufman

Perry Kaufman’s Trading Systems and Methods is a comprehensive resource that explores a variety of trading systems, including trend following. Kaufman’s analytical approach combines theory with practical applications.

Key Takeaways:

  • System Development: The book focuses on the development of robust trading systems that can adapt to changing market conditions.
  • Variety of Techniques: Kaufman presents various trend-following techniques, from simple moving averages to more complex algorithms.
  • Performance Evaluation: Understanding how to evaluate the performance of your trading systems is crucial, and Kaufman provides a detailed analysis of metrics and benchmarks.

6. The Trend Following Bible by Andrew Abraham

In The Trend Following Bible, Andrew Abraham combines theory and practical strategies in a thorough guide for traders yearning to master trend following. Abraham’s writing is accessible, making complex ideas easier to grasp.

Key Takeaways:

  • Comprehensive Framework: Abraham provides a structured approach to understanding and implementing trend-following strategies.
  • Psychological Aspects: The book addresses the psychological hurdles traders face, providing tools to overcome them.
  • Real-World Applications: Abraham shares numerous real-world examples, illustrating how the principles discussed can be applied to actual trading scenarios.

7. The Little Book of Common Sense Investing by John C. Bogle

While not exclusively about trend following, John Bogle’s The Little Book of Common Sense Investing offers invaluable insights into the philosophy of investing that can complement trend-following strategies. Bogle advocates for a disciplined approach to investing, which resonates with trend followers.

Key Takeaways:

  • Long-Term Perspective: Bogle emphasizes the importance of a long-term perspective, which aligns well with the trend-following philosophy.
  • Cost Efficiency: Understanding the impact of costs and fees on investment returns is crucial in trend following.
  • Indexing Strategies: The concepts of indexing and passive investing can provide a foundational understanding for trend-following traders.

8. Market Wizards by Jack D. Schwager

Jack D. Schwager’s Market Wizards is a compilation of interviews with some of the world’s most successful traders. While it covers various trading philosophies, trend followers can glean significant insights from these seasoned professionals.

Key Takeaways:

  • Diverse Perspectives: The interviews provide a broad spectrum of trading experiences and methodologies, including those centered around trend following.
  • Lessons Learned: Each trader shares valuable lessons learned, which can be applied to enhance your own trading strategy.
  • Real-World Experience: Schwager’s book emphasizes the importance of real-world experience and intuition in trading.

9. The Art and Science of Technical Analysis by Adam Grimes

Adam Grimes’ The Art and Science of Technical Analysis blends theory and practical trading insights. While focused on technical analysis, it offers essential tools for identifying trends and understanding market dynamics.

Key Takeaways:

  • Trend Identification: Grimes explores methods for identifying and confirming trends using technical indicators.
  • Market Behavior: Understanding market behavior is crucial for trend followers, and Grimes delves into the psychology of market movements.
  • Risk Management Strategies: Effective risk management strategies are highlighted as integral to successful trading.

10. The Complete Guide to Option Selling by James Cordier and Michael Gross

While primarily focused on options, The Complete Guide to Option Selling by James Cordier and Michael Gross provides insights into incorporating trend-following strategies into options trading. This book is especially valuable for those looking to diversify their trading methods.

Key Takeaways:

  • Options as a Tool: Understanding how to use options within a trend-following framework can enhance portfolio strategies.
  • Market Timing: The authors discuss market timing techniques that can be employed alongside trend-following strategies.
  • Comprehensive Insights: The book offers comprehensive insights into the mechanics of options selling, which can be beneficial for trend followers.

Conclusion

Trend following is a powerful trading strategy that can yield significant returns when executed effectively. The books mentioned above provide a diverse range of perspectives and methodologies, offering both foundational knowledge and advanced strategies. Whether you are a beginner or a seasoned trader, exploring these texts will equip you with the tools and insights necessary to thrive in the ever-evolving landscape of financial markets. By immersing yourself in these resources, you’ll not only enhance your understanding of trend following but also develop a more disciplined and informed approach to trading. Happy reading!
