Top 10 Fixed Income Securities to Buy in 2025


If recent years have taught investors anything, it’s the enduring value of stability—especially amid market volatility and global uncertainty. Fixed income securities are making a strong comeback in conversation and portfolios for 2025, spanning everything from classic government bonds to innovative ESG-linked debt products. Navigating the evolving landscape requires balancing risk, yield, and liquidity. Here, you’ll find the top 10 fixed income securities worth a closer look in 2025, each vetted for their resilience, yield potential, and relevance to shifting economic tides.

1. U.S. Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS)

TIPS remain a cornerstone of inflation-hedging strategies for good reason. As the Federal Reserve signals its path on rates and inflation expectations remain sticky, TIPS offer principal protection by adjusting for inflation with every CPI uptick. 2025 is shaping up to be another year where inflation uncertainty could rattle markets, making these securities highly valuable for capital preservation—and for capturing positive real yields if rates remain elevated.

  • Why They Stand Out: Near-zero default risk, steady income, and inflation-linked adjustments.
  • How to Buy: Direct from Treasury auctions or ETFs like iShares TIPS Bond ETF (TIP).

2. High-Quality Municipal Bonds

Municipal bonds are regaining prominence as state and local governments stabilize their balance sheets post-pandemic, with higher revenue collections and improved credit ratings. General Obligation (GO) bonds from fiscally disciplined states like Texas, Florida, and California are starting to offer more competitive yields. For investors in higher tax brackets, these bonds provide a compelling opportunity to earn tax-exempt income—an attractive feature if federal taxes are hiked.

  • Where to Look: GO bonds and essential-service Revenue bonds from top-rated municipalities.
  • Key Consideration: Examine state finances and project specifics, especially in infrastructure and green projects.

3. Investment-Grade Corporate Bonds

Strong balance sheets, cash flows, and credit ratings make investment-grade (IG) corporate bonds resilient even in the face of economic headwinds. By 2025, sectors such as technology and healthcare are expected to outperform, with issuers like Microsoft, Johnson & Johnson, and Google offering stable returns. As recession fears ebb, but growth slows, investment-grade spreads offer an appealing risk/reward profile compared to equities or high-yield peers.

  • Top Picks: 5-10 year maturities in the AA/AAA space; diversified exposure via funds like Vanguard Intermediate-Term Corporate Bond ETF (VCIT).
  • Pro Tip: Ladder maturities to manage rate risk and reinvest at more favorable levels.

4. Agency Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS)

Agency MBS issued by entities like Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, and Ginnie Mae add a unique risk-return profile to bond portfolios. With rates stabilizing, prepayment risk diminishes, and yields are favorable versus comparable Treasurys. Cash flows are protected by government guarantees, minimizing credit risk. For 2025, these securities provide a halfway point between corporate and government debt, with added yield but less credit exposure.

  • How to Access: ETFs such as iShares MBS ETF (MBB) or direct purchase from brokers.

5. Green & ESG-Linked Bonds

Sustainable investing continues to gather momentum. Green bonds, issued to fund environmentally friendly projects, are pacesetters for 2025, with trillions in new issuance expected globally. ESG-linked bonds benefit from both strong institutional inflows and high credit standards, especially from developed market sovereigns and blue-chip corporates.

  • Noteworthy Names: World Bank, Apple, Toyota, and European utilities often lead in green bond issuance.
  • Risk Note: Always assess the legitimacy of “green” claims—look for third-party certifications.

6. Floating Rate Notes (FRNs)

When rate movements are uncertain or trending upward, floating rate notes (FRNs) offer compelling current income. These securities reset their coupons periodically, often to LIBOR or SOFR, insulating investors from the worst of rising rates. Banks and corporates are expected to boost FRN issuance in 2025 as a way to attract yield-seeking investors.

  • Accessibility: Bank-issued FRNs, Treasury FRNs, and ETFs such as iShares Floating Rate Bond ETF (FLOT).
  • Advantage: Lower interest rate risk compared to fixed-coupon bonds.

7. Short-Term Government Bonds

For those prioritizing capital preservation in 2025, short-term government bonds present a reliable anchor. With the yield curve expected to normalize after years of distortion, shorter maturities can lock in real returns without extending into higher-risk, long-duration territory. They also offer easier liquidity for anyone who needs to stay nimble.

  • Options: < 3-year U.S. Treasurys, German Bunds, or UK Gilts, depending on domicile.

8. Emerging Market Sovereign Bonds

Despite concerns about volatility, select emerging market (EM) sovereign bonds stand out for their risk-adjusted yields in 2025. Countries with credible fiscal management and external surpluses—such as Mexico, Indonesia, and India—offer higher yields and the potential for currency appreciation. Look for dollar-denominated (“hard currency”) bonds for lower FX risk, or local currency EM debt for upside on currency if the dollar softens.

  • Approach: Diversify via EM bond funds; scrutinize credit ratings and geopolitical risk.

9. Preferred Securities

Often overlooked, preferred shares blend characteristics of both equity and debt, offering high fixed or adjustable dividends, priority over common stock in payouts, and, typically, call protection. Major U.S. banks and insurers frequently issue preferred stock with appetizing yields, making them attractive as rates plateau and market volatility grows. Their hybrid structure means they’re sensitive to interest rates and issuer credit, but less so to stock market volatility.

  • Best Use: Complement to fixed income portfolio for yield enhancement and diversification.

10. Convertible Bonds

If you’re hedging bets between growth and safety, convertible bonds straddle that line. They pay regular interest but also offer the right to convert into equity under certain conditions, providing upside if stock prices rally. As markets swing and rate hikes slow in 2025, convertibles are positioned for potential appreciation, especially for high-growth tech and healthcare sectors.

  • Vehicles: Individual securities or diversified funds such as SPDR Bloomberg Convertible Securities ETF (CWB).
  • Watch for: Terms of conversion and the underlying issuer’s financial health.

Structuring the Ideal Fixed Income Portfolio in 2025

Building a robust fixed income portfolio this year calls for more than chasing yield. The macro outlook for 2025 includes persistent inflation, gradual central bank tightening, and possible economic deceleration. Here’s how to approach allocation:

1. Diversification is Key
Don’t put all eggs in one basket—balance between government, corporate, municipal, and asset-backed securities.

2. Ladder Maturities
Stagger maturity dates to manage reinvestment risk and capture rising rates.

3. Adjust for Credit Quality
Blend high-grade with a measured allocation to higher-yielding, lower-rated instruments for an extra kick without outsized risk.

4. Consider Tax Efficiency
For taxable accounts, municipal bonds and some Treasurys can reduce your net tax drag.

5. Use Funds and ETFs for Liquidity
While some investors like individual bonds, funds and ETFs offer instant diversification and liquidity, crucial in uncertain times.

Outlook: What to Watch in 2025

Fixed income markets are cyclical—and 2025 promises new opportunities and risks. Several factors could impact securities performance and portfolio returns:

  • Central Bank Moves: Keep an eye on the Fed, ECB, and BOJ for shifts in rate policy.
  • Inflation Trends: Surprises on the upside will benefit inflation-protected securities and hurt long-dated bonds.
  • Global Growth: Slowdowns aid sovereign and high-quality credit, while rebounds fuel convertibles and select high-yield bonds.
  • Geopolitics: Stay aware of emerging market risks and policy changes affecting tax advantages, especially for munis and green bonds.

Final Thoughts

The best fixed income investments in 2025 won’t look the same for everyone. Conservative savers might lean on TIPS or short-term Treasurys, while those hungry for higher yield may find opportunities in emerging markets, convertibles, or green bonds. Portfolio sophistication comes not from chasing the top-yielding security, but from using multiple strategies in tandem to balance risk, income, and liquidity.

If you’re rebalancing for 2025, take time to review your goals, tolerance for risk, and income needs. Monitor the market’s pulse, but don’t let short-term noise drown out strategic thinking. The fixed income market has more depth and opportunity than ever—make the most of it with informed choices and a flexible mindset.
