How Private Equity Works: Fundamentals Explained


Private equity is a billion-dollar industry, yet it remains shrouded in mystery for many people. If you’ve ever wondered how it works and what private equity firms do, you’ve come to the right place. In this post, we’ll explore the fundamentals of private equity, breaking down the process into easily digestible parts.

First, let’s define our terms. Private equity refers to the funds and capital that are not publicly traded on the stock market. Instead, they are typically managed by private investment firms and used to finance growth, buyout, and start-up companies. Private equity comes in many forms, including venture capital, growth capital, and leveraged buyouts (LBOs).

The Life Cycle of a Private Equity Investment

Private equity investments follow a clear progression:

  1. Raising Capital: Private equity firms must first raise capital from limited partners (LPs), such as pensions, endowments, and wealthy individuals, to invest in deals. This capital is invested in a fund, which is then used to make acquisitions or investments.

  2. Identifying Opportunities: Private equity firms have a team of experts who scout for promising companies to invest in. Usually, these opportunities are undervalued or underperforming businesses with growth potential.

  3. Due Diligence: Before making an investment, private equity firms thoroughly examine the financials, management, and operations of the target company. This due diligence period is crucial to ensure the private equity firm understands all aspects of the business.

  4. Deal Execution: Once the due diligence is complete, the private equity firm negotiates a deal with the target company. When the deal is finalized, the private equity firm acquires or invests in the target company, typically using the money from the fund.

  5. Value Creation: The private equity firm’s main goal is to increase the value of the target company. They may streamline operations, make strategic additions or divestitures, or implement improvements to increase profitability. Value creation is the key to private equity’s success.

  6. Exit Strategy: After adding value and growing the company, the private equity firm must exit its investment. This is typically done through an initial public offering (IPO) or a sale to another company or another private equity firm. The goal is to achieve a favorable return on investment for the fund’s investors.

The Roles in Private Equity

Several key roles are involved in the private equity process:

  1. General Partners (GPs): These are the private equity firms themselves. They raise funds, identify opportunities, and manage portfolio companies.

  2. Limited Partners (LPs): These are the investors who provide the capital for private equity funds. They do not have a hands-on role in managing the portfolio companies but enjoy the returns generated by the investments.

  3. Advisors: Private equity firms typically enlist the help of investment bankers, lawyers, and accounting firms during the deal process. These advisors provide critical guidance and expertise.

  4. Portfolio Company Management: After the acquisition or investment, the private equity firm often brings in its own management team or works with the existing management to improve company performance.

Understanding Private Equity Returns

Private equity firms strive to generate high returns for their investors through the buy-and-sell process described above. Returns come from:

  1. Turnaround: Undervalued companies may improve operations, leading to increased profitability.

  2. Growth: By investing in growing businesses, private equity firms can ride the wave of market expansion.

  3. Divestitures: Private equity firms may sell assets or divisions of the target company to generate returns.

  4. Refinancing: By refinancing debt at lower interest rates, private equity firms can save on costs and inject cash back into the portfolio company.

In a nutshell, private equity is about opportunity, risk, and reward. Understanding how it works is essential for anyone interested in the world of finance. By providing capital to companies and guiding their growth, private equity firms play a vital role in the global economy.
