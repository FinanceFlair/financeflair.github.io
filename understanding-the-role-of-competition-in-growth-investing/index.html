Understanding the Role of Competition in Growth Investing


As investors, we often get caught up in the idea of being early movers or pioneers in a particular market or industry. We believe that being the first to market will guarantee success, but this is often not the case. In reality, competition is an essential component of growth investing, and ignoring it can lead to a lackluster performance. In this article, we will delve into the role of competition in growth investing and explore how it can impact your investment decisions. The Myth of First-Mover Advantage Many investors believe that being the first to market is the key to success. This is often referred to as the first-mover advantage. However, this advantage is often short-lived, and it can be quickly eroded by new entrants. In fact, a study by the Harvard Business Review found that 70% of companies that are first to market fail to achieve industry premiums. This is because being first may provide a temporary advantage, but it does not guarantee long-term success. The Rise of Competition As more and more companies enter a market, competition increases, and the landscape becomes more complex. A competitive market is often characterized by a increase in Barry Nalebuff's power of 1/6, which refer to the 1/6 power of the number of competitors, leading to a decrease in the return on investment. However, this doesn't mean that competition is a bad thing. In fact, it can drive innovation and lower prices, making it more accessible to consumers. Types of Competition There are several types of competition that can impact growth investing. Each type has its own unique characteristics and implications for investors. Market Attraction Market attraction competition refers to the competition for market share. In this type of competition, companies compete for customers, and the company with the most customers wins. This type of competition drives the prices down and leads to consolidation of the market. Process Innovation Process innovation competition refers to the competition for efficiency in producing products or services. This type of competition leads to improvements in production processes, leading to lower costs and improved quality. Product Competition Product competition refers to the competition for products or services that meet a particular need. This type of competition drives innovation and leads to the development of new products or services that meet customers' needs. Strategic Competition Strategic competition refers to the competition for intellectual property or technological advancements. This type of competition drives innovations in technology and is becoming increasingly important in growth investing. How Competition Impacts Growth Investing Competition has a significant impact on growth investing, and ignoring it can lead to disappointing results. Here are a few key ways that competition impacts growth investing:

Price Competition: As competition increases, prices tend to decrease. This can lead to a decrease in profit margins and reduced cash flow. Changes in Market Entry: Competition can lead to market saturation, making it increasingly difficult to enter or remain in the market. Value Creation: Competition drives innovation, and companies that innovate and execute continuously outperform those that don't. Growth: Competition can lead to a focus on growth, driving companies to push beyond market boundaries and innovate.

Strategies for Success in a Competitive Market Despite the challenges of competition, there are several strategies that can help investors achieve success in a competitive market. Here are a few key strategies:

Operational Performance: Invest in companies with strong operational performance, as this can lead to sustained profit margins and cost advantages. Business Model Innovation: Evaluate new business models that favor long-term relationships, data acquisition, and continuous innovation. Internal Competencies: Invest in industries or companies with internal competencies or resources that can drive innovation, leading to a sustained competitive advantage. Cultural Competencies: Prioritize companies with strong cultures that can adapt and innovate in real-time.

Conclusion Growth investing is not just about being early or first; it is about adapting to a competitive market and driving innovation. Understanding the role of competition and its various types can help investors make more informed investment decisions and achieve better returns on investment. By focusing on strong operational performance, business model innovation, internal competencies, and cultural competencies, investors can thrive in even the most competitive markets.
