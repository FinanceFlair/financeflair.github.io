Breaking the Mould: Disrupting Conventional Small Business Funding Strategies


Small business funding has always been a bit of a minefield. Conventional approaches such as loans or investors can often come with strings attached. But fear not, brave entrepreneurs – there are alternative funding methods waiting to disrupt and redefine the way you finance your start-up.

Crowdfunding

Let’s start with crowdfunding, a method endorsed by Barack Obama himself, who reportedly raised $90.4m for his non-profit organization, The Foundation. Picture this: instead of relying on banks, or wealthy individuals, your business’s progress is welcomed into the hands, and wallets, of the general public.

Reward-based crowdfunding asks backers for small amounts, often in return for a reward or discount on your product or service. Equity crowdfunding, on the other hand, gives backers a stake in your company.

Affiliate marketing

Affiliate marketing may seem like an unconventional funding route, but it can work wonders for certain kinds of businesses. You essentially partner with other companies to promote their products, take a commission for sales you generate, and then plow the profits back into your own business.

P2P Lending

Peer-to-peer lending, or P2P lending, has been on the rise in recent years. It’s pretty simple, really – just like a conventional loan, you borrow a sum and pay it back with interest. The twist is that instead of borrowing from a traditional bank, you borrow from a pool of individual investors.

Trade Credit

Trade credit is a method where you’re effectively extending credit to your own suppliers. Instead of buying inventory upfront, you defer payment until the sale of your products is completed. This can often result in substantial savings.

Venture Capital

Venture capitalists invest in start-ups with high growth potential. Money is exchanged for shares or equity in your company. But the trade-off for this funding is usually a heavy degree of control over your business.

Donation-based Crowdfunding

Donation-based crowdfunding is different from reward or equity crowdfunding. It’s like standing on a digital street corner with a sign saying, ‘Please give’. It doesn’t require repayment. It’s often used for social causes or personal reasons, but there’s no reason why a small business can’t take advantage of this too.

Closing Thoughts

Breaking the mould doesn’t need to be a daunting prospect. With the variety of unconventional funding methods available, it’s time to forge your own path away from the restrictions of traditional financing. So go ahead, embrace the alternatives, and watch your small business flourish. Remember, it’s not just about the money – it’s about the right kind of help for your business.

In conclusion, understanding alternative funding strategies can open up new opportunities for small businesses. Whether you choose to embrace crowdfunding, affiliate marketing, P2P lending, trade credit, venture capital, or donation-based crowdfunding, remember that each approach requires its uniquely considered strategy to succeed. So what are you waiting for? Time to rethink, redefine, and stand out!
