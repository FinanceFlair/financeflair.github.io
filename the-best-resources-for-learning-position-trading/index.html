The Best Resources for Learning Position Trading


Position trading is a style of trading that appeals to those who prefer a longer-term approach in the financial markets. Unlike day trading or swing trading, which involve quick positions to capitalize on short-term market moves, position trading focuses on holding trades over weeks, months, or even years. This strategy requires patience, thorough market analysis, and a keen understanding of macroeconomic trends and fundamentals.

If you’re eager to dive into position trading and build a solid foundation, finding the right learning resources is critical. This comprehensive guide will lead you through the best materials—from books and courses to websites and tools—that will help you master the art and science of position trading.

Understanding Position Trading: A Quick Overview

Before jumping into resources, let’s clarify what position trading entails. Position traders look to capitalize on medium to long-term trends, often relying on fundamental analysis combined with technical signals to identify entry and exit points. Because timeframes are extended, these traders avoid the stress of daily market noise and frequent trades but must remain diligent in following economic indicators, corporate earnings, and other essential market drivers.

Success in position trading depends heavily on research, strategic planning, and discipline. Once you’ve grasped these concepts, it’s time to explore methodologies, market psychology, risk management tactics, and practical experience.

Top Books for Learning Position Trading

Books are an excellent starting place, providing depth and theoretical underpinnings that build a robust trading mindset.

1. “Trade Your Way to Financial Freedom” by Van K. Tharp

Van Tharp’s classic book isn’t just about day trading but covers broader trading psychology and system development. It emphasizes the importance of crafting a personalized trading system, which is crucial for position traders who must rely on clear strategies rather than impulse decisions.

Key Takeaways:

  • How to develop a trading plan tailored to your goals
  • Risk management techniques applicable to longer holding periods
  • Understanding market cycles and maintaining discipline

2. “Market Wizards” Series by Jack D. Schwager

These interviews with some of the most successful traders provide insight into diverse styles, including position trading. Reading about the experiences of seasoned professionals offers perspective on how patience, emotional control, and thorough analysis can lead to sustainable profits.

Why this is invaluable:

  • Real-world lessons from traders with proven track records
  • Strategies that withstand different market conditions
  • Background on mindset and decision-making processes

3. “A Complete Guide to Volume Price Analysis” by Anna Coulling

Position traders can benefit from interpreting volume alongside price data to validate trends. This book dissects volume price relationships, enabling traders to spot strength or weakness in movement—a vital skill when committing to longer-term trades.

Educational Online Courses and Platforms

While books provide valuable theoretical knowledge, interactive courses often include hands-on exercises, live examples, and community discussions, facilitating faster learning.

1. Coursera and Udemy: Position Trading Fundamentals and Technical Analysis

Both platforms offer courses tailored to intermediate and advanced traders, covering concepts like trend analysis, chart patterns, fundamental analysis, and position trading strategies.

Standout features:

  • Video lectures from market professionals
  • Opportunity for discussions with instructors and peers
  • Flexible learning pace

2. Investopedia Academy

Investopedia offers a structured course called “Become a Day Trader” which, despite its title, includes modules on swing and position trading as well. Their courses blend basics with examples from real markets, allowing learners to test strategies within simulated environments.

Benefits:

  • Clear breakdown of trading styles and when to apply them
  • Strong emphasis on risk management and emotional control
  • Practical quizzes and trading simulators

3. Online Trading Academies

Renowned for their in-depth programs, online trading academies typically charge more but often include mentorship, live trading sessions, and ongoing support. Look for academies emphasizing position trading or a longer timeframe approach to ensure relevant content.

Websites and Forums to Follow

The internet hosts a wealth of information and active communities crucial for continuous learning in position trading.

1. Seeking Alpha

A hub for investment insights, Seeking Alpha covers detailed stock analysis, earning reports, and market trends—all essential for position traders focused primarily on equities.

Use it to:

  • Stay updated with sector-specific developments
  • Read diverse analyst opinions before entering or exiting trades
  • Follow dividend strategies and fundamental company reviews

2. TradingView

A platform beloved by technical analysts, TradingView offers powerful charting tools and a social community sharing trading ideas. You can filter ideas by timeframe to focus on position trading strategies, view detailed technical indicators, and analyze market sentiments.

Big pluses:

  • Access to global markets data
  • Ability to create custom alerts
  • Community-driven insights and backtesting features

3. Elite Trader and Trade2Win Forums

For those who value peer interaction, these forums provide spaces to discuss position trading tactics, share resources, and troubleshoot problems. Engaging with other traders expands your perspective and exposes you to different strategies and tools.

Essential Tools and Software for Position Trading

Success in position trading isn’t just about knowledge but also having the right tools to analyze, monitor, and execute your trades effectively.

1. Charting Software and Technical Analysis Tools

Position traders rely heavily on robust charting platforms to gauge long-term trends and confirm fundamental findings. Notable software includes:

  • MetaTrader 4/5: Popular for forex and commodities trading, equipped with customizable indicators.
  • Thinkorswim by TD Ameritrade: Advanced technical analysis tools with a broad range of assets.
  • NinjaTrader: Well-suited for futures and equities with extensive backtesting capabilities.

2. Economic Calendars

Since position traders track macroeconomic events, an up-to-date economic calendar is essential. Tools like Investing.com’s calendar or Forex Factory’s event feed provide timely alerts about interest rate decisions, GDP reports, employment figures, and other impactful releases.

3. Portfolio Management Software

Handling multiple long-term positions can become unwieldy. Portfolio trackers like Personal Capital or Sharesight help you monitor performance, dividends, and overall risk exposure, critical for informed decision-making.

How to Combine Fundamental and Technical Analysis

One of the biggest challenges in position trading is balancing fundamental insights with technical signals. Neither approach alone gives a full picture.

  • Fundamental analysis guides on what to buy and why, helping identify undervalued assets with growth potential.
  • Technical analysis provides timing—when to enter or exit based on price trends and volume.

The most effective position traders use fundamental analysis to screen and select securities and then apply technical analysis to refine entry and exit points, optimizing returns while managing risk.

Developing a Position Trading Strategy

With resources in hand, the next step is strategizing:

  1. Set time horizons: Define whether you’re a medium-term trader (weeks to months) or longer-term (months to years).
  2. Choose markets and instruments: Stocks, forex, commodities, or indices—select what aligns with your research strengths.
  3. Establish criteria: Use a blend of valuation metrics, trend analysis, and economic indicators.
  4. Risk management: Determine maximum drawdown per position and diversification rules.
  5. Review periodically: Position trading isn’t “set and forget.” Market conditions change, and so should your approach.

Many resources discussed earlier include strategy-building frameworks or templates to help formulate your personalized plan.

Final Thoughts: Continuous Learning is Key

Position trading rewards patience and insight but demands a continuing commitment to education. Markets evolve, economic landscapes shift, and new technologies emerge. The best traders keep refining their methods via books, courses, expert forums, daily analysis, and hands-on practice.

The vast array of educational resources highlighted here will guide you through the journey. Dive into reputable books to lay your foundation, complement them with interactive courses, and engage actively with trading communities. Utilize advanced tools to elevate your analysis, and remain disciplined in strategy execution.

Whether you’re starting fresh or looking to hone your position trading skills, this multi-dimensional approach to learning empowers you to build a profitable and sustainable trading career.

Embrace the long game, invest in knowledge, and let your position trades flourish!
