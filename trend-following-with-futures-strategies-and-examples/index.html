Trend Following with Futures: Strategies and Examples


In the world of investing, there are various strategies that traders employ to maximize their returns. One such strategy is trend following, which involves identifying and capitalizing on market trends. In this blog post, we will explore trend following specifically in the context of futures trading. We will discuss the strategies used by trend followers and provide examples to illustrate their effectiveness.

What is Trend Following?

Trend following is an investment strategy that aims to profit from the long-term trends observed in financial markets. Traders who employ this strategy believe that the price of an asset will continue to move in the same direction as the established trend. Whether the trend is bullish or bearish, trend followers aim to ride the wave and capture profits along the way.

Strategies for Trend Following with Futures

1. Moving Averages

One popular strategy used by trend followers is the moving averages method. This strategy involves using different time periods of moving averages to identify trends. For example, a trader may use a combination of a 50-day moving average and a 200-day moving average. When the shorter-term moving average crosses above the longer-term moving average, it is seen as a bullish signal, indicating a potential uptrend. Conversely, when the shorter-term moving average crosses below the longer-term moving average, it is seen as a bearish signal, indicating a potential downtrend.

2. Breakouts

Another strategy employed by trend followers is the breakout method. This strategy involves identifying key support and resistance levels on a price chart. A breakout occurs when the price of an asset breaks above a resistance level or below a support level. Trend followers believe that these breakouts indicate the continuation of a trend. Once a breakout is confirmed, traders can enter positions in the direction of the breakout to capture potential profits.

3. Trendline Analysis

Trendline analysis is another useful tool in trend following. Traders draw trendlines to connect the highs or lows of price movements on a chart. An upward sloping trendline connects the higher lows, indicating an uptrend, while a downward sloping trendline connects the lower highs, indicating a downtrend. Trend followers use trendlines to identify potential entry and exit points. When the price breaks above or below a trendline, it may signal the continuation of the trend, providing opportunities for trend followers to enter or exit their positions.

Examples of Trend Following with Futures

To better understand how trend following works in practice, let’s look at a couple of examples:

Example 1: Crude Oil Futures

Suppose a trader identifies a bullish trend in crude oil futures. They observe that the price has been consistently rising over the past few months. Using the moving averages strategy, the trader notices that the 50-day moving average has crossed above the 200-day moving average, indicating a potential uptrend. Based on this signal, the trader decides to enter a long position in crude oil futures, expecting the price to continue its upward trajectory.

Example 2: Gold Futures

In this example, a trader identifies a bearish trend in gold futures. They notice that the price has been steadily declining over the past few weeks. Using the breakout strategy, the trader identifies a key support level that the price is approaching. Once the price breaks below this support level, the trader enters a short position in gold futures, expecting the price to further decline.

Conclusion

Trend following with futures can be a profitable strategy for traders who are able to effectively identify and capitalize on market trends. By employing strategies such as moving averages, breakouts, and trendline analysis, traders can enhance their chances of success. The examples provided in this blog post illustrate how trend following can be applied in real-world trading scenarios. As always, it is important for traders to conduct thorough research and analysis before making any investment decisions. Happy trend following!
