The Pros and Cons of Trend Following Trading


As market professionals and enthusiasts continue to explore new strategies to stay ahead of the game, trend following trading has become an increasingly popular choice. This approach involves tracking and profiting from the direction and momentum of the market, with proponents citing its potential for generating impressive returns. However, there are valid concerns about the risks associated with this method, and a closer examination is warranted. A Brief Background on Trend Following Trend following is primarily based on the concept of following and profiting from the overall direction of price movements. Market mavens differentiate between two primary distinctions: a trend is typically seen in higher time frame analysis (hours, days, and months) and more predictable, while smaller time frames reveal erratic movements that rarely create profitable trades. Adherents to trend following theories argue that in varying markets, various types of trends can be found, be it global stock markets, forex, or commodities trading. Some leading proponents of trend following strategies state that by ignoring short-term price movements and solely focusing on long-term trends, investors can sustainably stand through market fluctuations and other financial trends. Many analysts effectively justify trend following disciplines with anecdotes that provide intriguing examples of consistently prosperous results, as trends turn around and thrive where central economies react to politics regime shifts as environment upheavals stimulate firms.
