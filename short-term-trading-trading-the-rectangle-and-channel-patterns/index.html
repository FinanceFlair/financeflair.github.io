Short Term Trading: Trading the Rectangle and Channel Patterns


As an inveterate trader, your journey to success often requires you to explore a diverse palette of trading strategies and tactics. Understanding and adopting various patterns is key to creating dynamic, winning trades. Today, let’s delve into short-term trading and two of its most active participants – trading rectangles and channel patterns.

Rectangle Patterns:

Rectangle patterns form a vital part of the swing and position trader’s toolbox. These appear when an asset’s price gets stuck within a fixed price range. This pattern occurs due to a minor resistance level, followed by support, or vice versa.

There are primarily four sections in a rectangle:

  1. Resistance level: Price struggles to go higher.
  2. Support (Lower Shadow): Price finds a floor and can’t go lower.
  3. Resistance level 2: Another barrier appears for the price to struggle with.
  4. Breakout: Once the price thrusts over the upper or lower line, it signals a probable buying or selling spree.

Consider these courses of action:

  1. Buy: When the price supports from the lower trendline, it indicates a potential buying opportunity.
  2. Sell: A breakdown below the lower trendline signifies selling pressures.

Don’t forget these essential parameters:

  1. Targets: Once a significant breakout happens, place buy/sell limits one times the rectangle size away from the breakout line.
  2. Stop-loss: Place stop-loss just the equivalent of the rectangle size in favor of the breakout direction, near the parallel line.

But remember, not all rectangles are the same. Rectangles can also offer extended opportunities in the form of fake-outs. However, patience and careful analysis are vital, as one must wait for a clear break of the rectangle before taking action.

Channel Patterns:

With time frames varying from days to weeks, channels provide insight into momentum and trend changes. Simply put, a channel consists of two parallel lines— resistance and support, thereby forming a price range.

According to tradition:

  1. Trend Line Resistance: As prices reach the upper line, people generally sell. Conversely, on touch of lower trend line, they buy.
  2. Series of Higher Highs and Lower Lows: Ascending channels denote an uptrend, while Descending channels mark the downtrend.

An intelligent trader takes these factors into consideration:

  1. Entry Prices: Perfect time to buy pullbacks when they touch the lower channel line, or to sell when they touch the upper channel line.
  2. Exit Points: Once the asset price pierces beyond the opposite channel line.

Don’t forget measures like:

  1. Targets: Calculate price levels for channel patterns based on their gradient. Going short or long based on the trend.
  2. Stop-loss Placement: Put stop-loss orders near or just above/below the channel lines.

Remember the challenges:

  1. Shifting Trends: Channels morph when momentum alters, so adapt accordingly.
  2. False Breakouts: Buffers for pullbacks or breaks within channels give temporary incorrect signals. Crossroads demand a specific set of trading strategies.

In conclusion, channel and rectangle patterns form pivotal tools for short-term traders. Wrapped in technical indicators and supportive charts, these techniques provide the blueprint for trading small, quick market movements. Remember, however, that while patterns may guide, it’s up to the trader to execute wisely. Experience and research are your most potent tools. Happy trading!
