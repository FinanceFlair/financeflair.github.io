Estate Planning for Individuals with Parkinson's Disease


Parkinson’s disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that affects millions of people worldwide. It can have a significant impact on a person’s physical, mental, and financial well-being. As the disease progresses, it becomes crucial for individuals with Parkinson’s disease to plan for the future and ensure their wishes are honored. This is where estate planning comes into play.

Understanding Estate Planning

Estate planning is the process of making decisions and arrangements for the management and distribution of your assets after your passing. It involves creating legal documents that outline your wishes and ensure they are carried out, even if you are unable to communicate or make decisions in the future. While estate planning is important for everyone, it holds particular significance for individuals with Parkinson’s disease.

The Importance of Estate Planning for Individuals with Parkinson’s Disease

Parkinson’s disease is a chronic condition that worsens over time. It can cause difficulties in movement, speech, and cognitive function. As symptoms progress, individuals with Parkinson’s may find it challenging to manage their financial affairs or make important decisions about their healthcare. This makes estate planning crucial for ensuring their wishes are respected and their loved ones are taken care of.

1. Appointing a Healthcare Proxy

One of the most critical aspects of estate planning for individuals with Parkinson’s disease is appointing a healthcare proxy. A healthcare proxy is a person you designate to make medical decisions on your behalf if you become unable to do so. It is essential to choose someone you trust to advocate for your wishes and ensure you receive the care you desire.

2. Creating a Living Will

A living will is a legal document that outlines your preferences for medical treatment and end-of-life care. It allows you to specify what medical interventions you would like or refuse in certain situations. For individuals with Parkinson’s disease, a living will can provide clarity and peace of mind, knowing that their healthcare decisions align with their values and desires.

3. Establishing a Power of Attorney

In addition to a healthcare proxy, individuals with Parkinson’s disease should consider establishing a power of attorney. A power of attorney grants someone the authority to handle your financial and legal matters if you are unable to do so. This ensures that bills are paid, investments are managed, and other financial obligations are met, even if you are unable to handle them yourself.

4. Reviewing and Updating Beneficiary Designations

As Parkinson’s disease progresses, it is essential to review and update beneficiary designations on your financial accounts, insurance policies, and retirement plans. This ensures that your assets are distributed according to your wishes and that your loved ones are provided for. Failing to update beneficiary designations can lead to unintended consequences and disputes among family members.

Conclusion

Estate planning is a critical step for individuals with Parkinson’s disease to take control of their future and ensure their wishes are honored. By appointing a healthcare proxy, creating a living will, establishing a power of attorney, and reviewing beneficiary designations, individuals with Parkinson’s can have peace of mind knowing that their affairs are in order.

If you or a loved one is living with Parkinson’s disease, it is never too early to start estate planning. Consulting with an experienced estate planning attorney can provide guidance and support throughout the process. Remember, estate planning is not just about finances; it is about ensuring your wishes are respected and your loved ones are taken care of, even in the face of a progressive illness like Parkinson’s disease.
