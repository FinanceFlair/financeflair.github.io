Navigating Regulatory Compliance in Crowdfunding Platforms


Crowdfunding platforms have revolutionized the way many entrepreneurs and investors connect for funding innovative projects. The exciting prospect of successful crowdfunding can overshadow the need for navigating regulatory compliance. This article will explore the regulatory landscape of crowdfunding platforms, providing tips on ensuring compliance with the relevant laws.

Regulatory Landscape:

The regulatory environment in which crowdfunding platforms operate is complex and multilayered, involving a mix of federal and state laws. The most notable federal laws include Title III of the Jumpstart Our Business Startups (JOBS) Act, the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) rules, and Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA) rules. These laws establish requirements for platforms and issuers alike, creating a solid foundation for securities-based crowdfunding.

JOBS Act:

The JOBS Act, enacted in 2012, specifically legalized crowdfunding for securities offerings. The law allows companies to raise up to a specific amount of money through crowdfunding platforms, depending on their size, from a larger pool of investors. The JOBS Act implemented two primary regulatory changes:

  1. Eliminate the prohibition of general solicitation and advertising of private placements, to allow companies to raise funds from a broader pool of investors through General Solicitation.

  2. Permit companies to broadly advertise their Regulation Crowdfunding offerings without the need for fulsome disclosure documents, provided they do not solicit directly from anyone not accredited.

SEC Rules:

The SEC, which oversees securities regulation, established Regulation Crowdfunding (Regulation CF) to implement the JOBS Act. Regulation CF outlines various requirements for both crowdfunding platforms and issuers. Issuers must provide extensive information on their business, the offer, and related risks. These disclosures include:

  1. Disclosure documents to help investors make informed decisions.

  2. Annual reporting to keep investors informed about the company’s progress.

  3. Limitations on advertising and general solicitation, ensuring that only investors with specific accreditation meet the issuer’s target audience.

FINRA Rules:

Lastly, FINRA regulates the funding portals in coordination with the SEC. FINRA enforces its rules on the platforms by overseeing the onboarding and continued compliance of the platforms themselves. Some of the important rules include honest and fair dealing and provisions against fraud. FINRA also mandates annual audits and background checks to ensure that everyone involved with the platform is genuinely qualified to work with investors’ funds.

Navigating Compliance:

Understanding regulatory compliance can be a challenging process, but utilizing the following tips will help you better navigate it:

  1. Check the SEC website (https://www.sec.gov/) for the latest guidance and updates on regulatory compliance.

  2. Understand the requirements and limitations set by the JOBS Act, the SEC’s Regulation Crowdfunding, and FINRA.

  3. Verify that the crowdfunding platform you choose has met all regulatory requirements and is in good standing, frequently reviewed by a third-party auditor.

  4. Ensure compliance by consulting attorneys familiar with securities laws and crowdfunding practices to assist in disclosure preparation, review, and submission.

  5. Create a plan for annual reporting and ongoing compliance to stay engaged with the disclosures and monitor changes in the regulatory environment.

In conclusion, crowdfunding platforms offer tremendous opportunities for businesses to connect with investors and raise funds. However, regulatory compliance should top the list of concerns for entrepreneurs and investors alike. Platforms, issuers, and investors must adhere to federal and state laws, including the JOBS Act, SEC rules, and FINRA rules to navigate this complex environment successfully. By understanding and implementing these guidelines, you can maximize your chances for crowdfunding success.
