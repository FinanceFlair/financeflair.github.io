Ethical Investing Myths Debunked: What You Should Know


Investors often seek to make a positive impact on the world while generating returns on their investments. However, misconceptions about ethical investing can make it challenging to navigate the complex landscape. In this article, we will delve into common myths surrounding ethical investing and provide a clear understanding of what it entails. Myth #1: Ethical Investing is a recent phenomenon It is often assumed that ethical investing is a trend born from the younger generation's desire to save the planet. However, the concept of socially responsible investing dates back to the 18th century. The birth of the concept can be attributed to Reverend Thomas Chalmers' in 1795 book A survey of the results of the late registration. He elucidated that involvement in charitable causes benefits both the individual and the community. Myth #2: Ethical investing is for the faint of heart Conventional wisdom suggests that ethical investing is a gamble for a secure return, inhibiting diversified portfolios. However, research indicates that ESG (Environmental, social, and governance) factors [2] are becoming increasingly important in evaluating the long-term viability of businesses. Cutting-edge portfolio construction and alignment tools make it easy to integrate ethics into any investment strategy. Numerous studies have demonstrated that effective ESG strategies [3] garnered commensurate returns, if not outperformed, traditional investing. Why are impact investors thriving in today's market? Prioritizing lifestyles that conserve and lessen environmental footprints aligns with the interests of humanity. Furthermore, research suggests that collaborative efforts can yield significant returns for all parties involved. Another assumption holds that a fully integrated ESG approach is inherently more costly. Comprehensive research reveals that the difference lies more so in management opportunity costs due to lost alignments opposed to straying share portfolios.
