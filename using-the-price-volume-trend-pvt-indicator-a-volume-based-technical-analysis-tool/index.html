Using the Price Volume Trend (PVT) Indicator: A Volume-based Technical Analysis Tool


The Price Volume Trend (PVT) indicator is a powerful tool that can help traders make informed decisions in the stock market. By analyzing the relationship between price and volume, the PVT indicator provides valuable insights into market trends and potential price movements. In this blog post, we will explore the concept of PVT, its calculation, and how it can be effectively used in technical analysis.

What is the Price Volume Trend (PVT) Indicator?

The Price Volume Trend (PVT) indicator is a volume-based technical analysis tool that measures the relationship between price and volume in a given security. It was developed by Joseph Granville, a renowned trader and technical analyst. The PVT indicator is designed to show the flow of money in and out of a stock, indicating the strength of buying or selling pressure.

How is the PVT Indicator Calculated?

The PVT indicator is calculated by multiplying the percentage change in price by the volume of the security. The formula for calculating the PVT is as follows:

PVT = [(Close - Previous Close) / Previous Close] * Volume + Previous PVT

To calculate the PVT for a specific period, you need to start with a base value, usually zero, and then apply the formula for each subsequent period. By accumulating the values over time, the PVT indicator creates a line that can be analyzed to identify trends and potential price reversals.

Interpreting the PVT Indicator

The PVT indicator is primarily used to confirm the strength of a price trend or to identify potential trend reversals. When the PVT line is moving upwards, it indicates that buying pressure is increasing, and the price is likely to continue rising. Conversely, when the PVT line is moving downwards, it suggests that selling pressure is increasing, and the price may decline.

Traders often look for divergences between the PVT line and the price chart to identify potential trend reversals. For example, if the price is making higher highs, but the PVT line is making lower highs, it could signal that the buying pressure is weakening, and a price reversal may be imminent.

Using the PVT Indicator in Technical Analysis

The PVT indicator can be used in conjunction with other technical analysis tools to validate trading signals and improve decision-making. Here are a few ways to incorporate the PVT indicator into your trading strategy:

  1. Confirmation of Breakouts: When a stock breaks out of a key resistance level with a significant increase in volume, the PVT indicator can be used to confirm the strength of the breakout. If the PVT line is also moving upwards, it provides additional confirmation that the breakout is valid.

  2. Divergence Trading: As mentioned earlier, divergences between the PVT line and the price chart can be used to identify potential trend reversals. Traders can look for bullish or bearish divergences to anticipate price movements.

  3. Volume Analysis: By analyzing the relationship between volume and the PVT line, traders can gain insights into the overall market sentiment. If the PVT line is trending upwards, accompanied by increasing volume, it suggests strong buying pressure and a bullish market sentiment.

  4. Overbought and Oversold Conditions: The PVT indicator can also be used to identify overbought and oversold conditions in a stock. When the PVT line reaches extreme levels, it indicates that the price has deviated significantly from its average, and a reversal may be imminent.

Conclusion

The Price Volume Trend (PVT) indicator is a valuable tool for traders looking to analyze the relationship between price and volume. By understanding its calculation and interpretation, traders can make informed decisions and improve their trading strategies. Incorporating the PVT indicator into your technical analysis toolkit can provide valuable insights into market trends and potential price movements. Remember, however, that no indicator is foolproof, and it is always essential to use multiple tools and indicators to validate your trading decisions.

Now that you have a better understanding of the PVT indicator, go ahead and explore its application in your own trading strategy. Happy trading!
