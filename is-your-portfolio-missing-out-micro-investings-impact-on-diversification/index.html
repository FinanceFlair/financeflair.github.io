Is Your Portfolio Missing Out? Micro Investing's Impact on Diversification


Internationally, the trend toward micro investing and robo-advice platforms has grown substantially in recent years. With investment amounts starting at just a few dollars, these platforms are making it possible for everyday people to take part in the financial markets. However, many portfolios are still missing out on the full benefits that micro investing can provide when it comes to diversification.

It’s well established that diversification is an essential component of a strong investment portfolio. Including a mix of different types of investments in your portfolio, such as stocks, bonds, commodities, and real estate, helps to mitigate risk and increase potential return. But how can micro investing actually lead to better diversification?

First off, how does micro investing work? Essentially, these platforms use technology to automate aspects of the investing process. They usually use Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) or other low-cost investment vehicles to represent various asset classes and industries.

Here are some reasons why micro investing impacts diversification.

Dollar-cost averaging: Micro investing often employs dollar-cost averaging, where investors buy the same amount of an investment at regular intervals regardless of the price. This technique provides a level of risk management, as it reduces the negative impact of poor timing in the market. Therefore, it helps solidify a diversified investment portfolio.

Accessibility: Micro investing platforms increase portfolio diversification by making investments more accessible. Anyone with just a few dollars can invest, rather than requiring large amounts of capital. This means that the average person can invest in a diverse range of investments without as much risk, resulting in a more diversified portfolio.

Automation: Expanding on the previous point, automation plays a key role here. Investors who use robo-advice services or other automated investing tools can rest easy knowing that their portfolio is being monitored and adjusted as needed for optimal diversification.

Liquidity: Micro investments in ETFs or other low-cost investment vehicles are highly liquid. This is beneficial for diversification because it preserves the opportunity to react quickly and efficiently to market changes.

Efficiency: The technology-driven automation provided by micro investing platforms leads to efficiency. This is particularly valuable when it comes to maintaining and adjusting diversification within portfolios.

Despite these benefits, there are some potential pitfalls to be aware of with micro investing:

Fees: Be aware of fees and make sure to choose a micro investing platform that doesn’t charge any unnecessary or high fees that could eat into your returns.

Lack of customization: Many micro investing platforms might not allow for enough customization to fit unique investor circumstances or preferences.

Over-diversification: Investing a little in many ETFs can lead to over-diversification, which might not actually increase portfolio returns and instead creates too much risk.

In conclusion, micro investing’s impact on diversification provides new opportunities for your portfolio. By making investments more accessible and automating the investing process with low-cost ETFs or other investment vehicles, diversification can be consolidated and improved. Always make sure to be aware of fees and avoid over-diversification, but make the most of these new technological developments in your investment journey.
