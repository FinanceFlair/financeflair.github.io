The Best Retirement Planning Apps for 2023


Retirement may seem a long way off, but planning for it is something that should be considered early on. No matter where you are in your career or how close you are to retirement, using technology to track and improve your retirement savings is essential. Fortunately, numerous retirement planning apps in 2023 make this process easier, more streamlined, and more accessible.

Whether you’re just starting to save or fine-tuning your investments in your later years, there’s a wide variety of apps that can help you prepare for a financially secure retirement. Let’s dive into some of the best retirement planning apps available today and explore the features they offer.

1. Personal Capital

Personal Capital has long been a leading app for retirement planning. It provides an all-in-one platform that tracks not just retirement savings, but all of your financial accounts, helping you get a clear picture of your financial health. The app is known for its comprehensive retirement planner, which allows you to track your progress toward retirement and make necessary adjustments.

Key Features:

  • Retirement Planner: Offers a detailed analysis of your retirement projections, taking into account your current savings, expected future contributions, and your retirement goals.
  • Investment Tracking: Allows you to see your portfolio’s performance, fees, and allocation. It provides detailed recommendations to help you optimize your portfolio for retirement.
  • Budgeting Tools: Helps you track your daily spending and categorize your expenses, ensuring that you stay within budget while saving for retirement.

Best For: Those who want a holistic view of their financial situation and are looking for advanced tools for investment tracking and retirement forecasting.

2. Fidelity Retirement Score

Fidelity’s Retirement Score app is designed to give users a simple yet effective way to assess their readiness for retirement. It helps individuals determine whether they are on track to meet their retirement goals and provides actionable insights for improving savings.

Key Features:

  • Retirement Score: After inputting details such as income, savings, and projected retirement expenses, the app generates a “retirement score” that helps you gauge your financial preparedness.
  • Goal Tracker: Allows you to set specific retirement goals and tracks your progress toward reaching them.
  • Interactive Tools: Includes calculators for estimated retirement expenses, income needs, and how long your savings might last in retirement.

Best For: Those looking for an easy-to-use tool to evaluate their retirement progress without being overwhelmed by complicated features.

3. Betterment

Betterment is a robo-advisor that offers personalized investment portfolios tailored to your retirement goals. With Betterment, users don’t have to worry about day-to-day management of their investments; the app automatically rebalances your portfolio and ensures that you’re on track for retirement.

Key Features:

  • Automated Portfolio Management: The app provides low-cost, diversified portfolios based on your risk tolerance and retirement timeline.
  • Retirement Planning Tools: Includes projections and personalized advice to ensure that you’re saving enough to meet your future retirement needs.
  • Tax-Efficient Investing: Helps you save on taxes through tax-loss harvesting and tax-efficient investing strategies.

Best For: Individuals looking for a hands-off approach to retirement investing and those who want a personalized investment strategy without needing to become a financial expert.

4. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is another robo-advisor that’s perfect for retirement planning. It offers a range of services aimed at simplifying retirement savings, from automated investing to tax optimization. Wealthfront provides users with personalized retirement planning and investment management to help meet long-term goals.

Key Features:

  • Retirement Planning Dashboard: Allows you to see an estimate of how much money you’ll need to retire comfortably and whether you’re on track to meet those goals.
  • Automated Investing: The app automatically allocates your investments across different asset classes, ensuring that your portfolio stays diversified.
  • Financial Planning Tools: Includes tools for budgeting, saving for other financial goals, and understanding how market changes may affect your retirement plans.

Best For: Those who prefer automated investing services that allow them to set it and forget it, while still receiving professional retirement planning advice.

5. Acorns

Acorns is a micro-investing app that allows users to automatically invest spare change from everyday purchases into a diversified portfolio. While it’s often seen as an app for younger generations looking to build wealth, it can also be a useful tool for retirement planning by automatically contributing to an IRA or 401(k).

Key Features:

  • Round-Ups: Acorns rounds up your purchases to the nearest dollar and invests the spare change in a diversified portfolio.
  • Retirement Accounts: Offers both traditional and Roth IRAs, allowing users to build retirement savings through consistent, small contributions.
  • Simple Investment Options: Acorns provides simple, automated investment strategies tailored to your retirement timeline and risk tolerance.

Best For: Young professionals or individuals who want an effortless way to start saving for retirement without making large, manual contributions.

6. Ellevest

Ellevest is a retirement planning app specifically tailored to women, with a focus on closing the gender retirement gap. The app offers personalized investment strategies, including retirement accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s, and helps women build wealth with a focus on long-term financial goals.

Key Features:

  • Gender-Specific Investment Plans: Ellevest provides personalized financial plans that take into account factors unique to women, such as longer life expectancy and potential career breaks for caregiving.
  • Retirement Accounts: Supports both traditional and Roth IRAs, along with taxable investment accounts, making it easy to start saving for retirement.
  • Goal-Oriented Planning: Ellevest offers retirement calculators and personalized advice based on your specific retirement needs and financial situation.

Best For: Women who want a retirement plan tailored to their unique life circumstances, along with a comprehensive investment strategy for the future.

7. Vanguard Retirement Nest Egg Calculator

Vanguard’s Nest Egg Calculator is a simple yet effective tool for anyone looking to understand how long their savings will last during retirement. While not a full-fledged retirement planning app, it’s an excellent tool for calculating how much you need to save to ensure a comfortable retirement.

Key Features:

  • Nest Egg Calculator: This tool helps you estimate how long your current savings will last in retirement based on your expected expenses and withdrawal rate.
  • Real-Time Projections: The app provides a clear, visual representation of how your savings will grow or shrink over time, helping you plan accordingly.
  • Retirement Readiness: Offers advice and suggestions based on your specific financial situation, including strategies for increasing your retirement savings.

Best For: Individuals who want a quick, easy way to evaluate their current retirement savings and assess if they need to make adjustments.

8. T. Rowe Price Retirement Planning Tools

T. Rowe Price offers a suite of retirement planning tools, including a personalized retirement savings estimator, a portfolio allocation tool, and other calculators to help you project your future retirement income.

Key Features:

  • Retirement Savings Estimator: This tool helps you estimate how much you’ll need to save each month to achieve your desired retirement income.
  • Portfolio Allocation Tools: Offers suggestions for how to allocate your assets across different investment options to maximize your retirement savings.
  • Interactive Retirement Calculators: Helps users visualize how changes in their savings rate or investment returns could impact their retirement outcome.

Best For: Those who prefer a more hands-on approach to managing their retirement funds, with the ability to adjust asset allocations and track projections over time.

9. Merrill Edge Retirement Planning Tools

Merrill Edge provides a variety of retirement planning tools aimed at helping users build a secure financial future. Whether you’re saving for retirement through a 401(k), IRA, or taxable account, Merrill Edge provides calculators, projections, and investment guidance to ensure you’re on the right track.

Key Features:

  • Retirement Calculator: A simple, easy-to-use tool to estimate your future retirement income based on your current savings, contributions, and goals.
  • Portfolio Planning: Provides suggestions for diversifying your portfolio based on your retirement timeline and risk tolerance.
  • Guidance from Financial Advisors: Users can access professional financial advice to ensure they’re making the right investment decisions.

Best For: Individuals who want a combination of digital tools and access to professional financial advice.

Conclusion

Planning for retirement is a critical step towards ensuring your financial security in the future, and using the right tools can make the process easier and more effective. Whether you’re just beginning to save or are close to retiring, the retirement planning apps mentioned above offer powerful features that can help you track your progress, optimize your savings, and make informed investment decisions.

No matter your age or stage in life, these apps provide the resources you need to make smarter financial choices and work towards achieving your retirement goals. Start using one of these apps today and take control of your financial future.
