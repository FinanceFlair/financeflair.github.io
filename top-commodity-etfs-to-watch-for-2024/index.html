Top Commodity ETFs to Watch for 2024


Commodity exchange-traded funds (ETFs) have become an increasingly popular investment option for both retail and institutional investors looking to diversify their portfolios and gain exposure to the broader commodity markets. In 2024, investors are closely watching commodity ETFs as they offer a relatively simple way to invest in raw materials such as oil, gold, agricultural products, and precious metals. With commodity markets often showing volatility but also great potential for returns, these ETFs provide a cost-effective means of exposure to a wide array of sectors.

In this post, we will explore some of the top commodity ETFs to watch in 2024, offering a broad spectrum of options to meet different investment goals. These ETFs allow investors to tap into diverse commodity markets without having to worry about the complexities of owning and storing physical commodities themselves.

1. SPDR Gold Shares ETF (GLD)

Gold has been a long-time favorite of investors seeking a safe haven, especially during times of economic uncertainty. The SPDR Gold Shares ETF (GLD) is one of the most well-known and widely traded commodity ETFs, making it an excellent choice for those looking to gain exposure to gold in 2024.

The GLD ETF is designed to track the price of gold by holding physical gold bars in a secure vault. Investors in GLD are essentially gaining exposure to the price fluctuations of gold without the need for physical ownership. As geopolitical tensions, inflation, and financial instability continue to be concerns, gold often sees an increase in demand, which could lead to strong returns for GLD holders in 2024.

Key Benefits:

  • Liquidity: GLD is one of the most liquid commodity ETFs, with high trading volumes that make it easy to buy and sell.
  • Diversification: Gold acts as a hedge against inflation and economic uncertainty, making GLD a good option for risk-averse investors.
  • Transparency: The fund regularly updates the amount of gold it holds, providing transparency to investors.

Risk Considerations:

  • Gold can be volatile in the short term, with price fluctuations driven by market sentiment and other macroeconomic factors.

2. Invesco DB Agriculture Fund (DBA)

Agricultural commodities are a critical part of the global economy, and the Invesco DB Agriculture Fund (DBA) offers exposure to a wide range of agricultural products. The ETF tracks the performance of an index that holds futures contracts on commodities such as corn, wheat, soybeans, and sugar.

As global population growth continues to drive demand for food products and weather patterns become increasingly unpredictable due to climate change, agricultural commodities can experience price swings. For investors looking to capitalize on these trends, DBA provides a convenient and efficient way to gain exposure to the agricultural sector in 2024.

Key Benefits:

  • Diverse Exposure: DBA offers exposure to a broad range of agricultural commodities, helping to diversify risk across various crops and products.
  • Hedge Against Inflation: As the cost of food rises, agricultural commodities can act as a hedge against inflation, which may be an attractive feature for investors in 2024.
  • Global Demand: Increasing demand for agricultural products from emerging markets can drive long-term growth potential for DBA.

Risk Considerations:

  • Agricultural commodity prices can be heavily impacted by weather events, geopolitical tensions, and supply chain disruptions.

3. iShares Silver Trust (SLV)

Silver, like gold, has long been a popular precious metal for investors seeking a store of value. The iShares Silver Trust (SLV) tracks the price of silver by holding physical silver bars. Unlike other commodities, silver has both industrial and precious metal applications, making it an attractive choice for investors in 2024 who are looking for exposure to a metal with both inflation-hedging and growth potential.

As industries such as electronics, solar energy, and electric vehicles demand more silver, the industrial use of silver could drive its price higher in the coming years. SLV is one of the most liquid and widely traded silver ETFs, making it a great choice for investors looking to invest in this metal.

Key Benefits:

  • Precious Metal Exposure: Like gold, silver is often seen as a safe haven investment during times of economic instability.
  • Industrial Demand: Silverâ€™s use in industries like solar power and electronics offers potential for growth.
  • Liquidity: SLV is highly liquid, with tight bid-ask spreads, allowing for easy entry and exit.

Risk Considerations:

  • Silver can be more volatile than gold due to its industrial applications, which can cause its price to fluctuate more dramatically based on economic cycles and industrial demand.

4. United States Oil Fund (USO)

Oil has always been one of the most traded and widely followed commodities in the world. The United States Oil Fund (USO) is designed to track the price of West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil. By investing in futures contracts, USO provides exposure to the daily price movements of oil without the need to deal with physical oil or complex futures contracts directly.

Given the ongoing shifts in global oil supply and demand, particularly with regard to geopolitical instability in oil-producing regions and the transition to renewable energy, USO is one of the commodity ETFs to watch for 2024. As economies recover from the effects of the pandemic, the demand for oil could increase, driving up prices.

Key Benefits:

  • Direct Oil Exposure: USO tracks the price of WTI crude oil, providing direct exposure to the commodity.
  • Energy Demand: As global economies grow, energy demand is expected to rise, which could support oil prices.
  • Liquidity: USO is highly liquid and easy to trade, making it accessible for investors.

Risk Considerations:

  • Oil prices are highly volatile, influenced by supply and demand dynamics, geopolitical events, and broader economic conditions.
  • USO also invests in futures contracts, which can result in price tracking errors over time.

5. iShares S&P GSCI Commodity-Indexed Trust (GSG)

For investors who want diversified exposure to a broad range of commodities, the iShares S&P GSCI Commodity-Indexed Trust (GSG) offers a robust solution. GSG tracks the performance of the S&P GSCI Total Return Index, which includes energy, agriculture, metals, and livestock futures contracts.

This ETF provides investors with diversified exposure to various sectors within the commodities market, reducing the risk associated with investing in any single commodity. With the potential for growth in different areas such as metals, energy, and agriculture, GSG offers a balanced way to invest in the commodity markets in 2024.

Key Benefits:

  • Broad Diversification: GSG offers exposure to multiple commodities, providing broad diversification within the sector.
  • Sector Representation: The ETF includes exposure to a wide array of sectors, from energy and metals to agriculture and livestock.
  • Ease of Access: As a widely traded ETF, GSG is easy for investors to buy and sell.

Risk Considerations:

  • Diversification can reduce risk but does not eliminate it entirely. Commodities are still subject to price volatility due to global supply and demand factors.

6. VanEck Vectors Gold Miners ETF (GDX)

While gold ETFs like GLD offer direct exposure to gold prices, the VanEck Vectors Gold Miners ETF (GDX) provides exposure to companies that mine gold. This gives investors the potential for additional upside if gold prices rise and mining companies benefit from higher margins.

In 2024, with gold prices expected to rise due to ongoing inflationary pressures and geopolitical uncertainties, GDX could be a strong performer as mining companies’ profits grow. GDX offers a great way to gain leveraged exposure to the gold market.

Key Benefits:

  • Mining Exposure: GDX offers indirect exposure to the price of gold by investing in the top gold mining companies.
  • Leverage Potential: As gold prices rise, the profitability of mining companies tends to increase, which can drive the price of GDX higher.
  • Diversification: GDX invests in a range of gold mining companies, reducing individual stock risk.

Risk Considerations:

  • GDX is sensitive not only to gold prices but also to factors like mining efficiency, political risks in mining regions, and operational risks in individual mining companies.

Conclusion

The commodity market remains one of the most dynamic and potentially profitable sectors to invest in. Whether you are looking for safe-haven assets like gold and silver or want to take advantage of price movements in agricultural commodities or oil, there are a range of ETFs that can provide exposure to these assets.

In 2024, some of the most compelling commodity ETFs to watch include SPDR Gold Shares (GLD), Invesco DB Agriculture Fund (DBA), iShares Silver Trust (SLV), United States Oil Fund (USO), iShares S&P GSCI Commodity-Indexed Trust (GSG), and VanEck Vectors Gold Miners ETF (GDX). These ETFs offer investors an efficient and cost-effective way to diversify their portfolios, hedge against inflation, and gain exposure to the opportunities presented by commodity markets.
