A Guide to Robo Advisors for Non-Traditional Assets: Which Ones Excel?


Robo advisors have revolutionized the way people invest, providing automated and algorithm-driven investment services. While they have traditionally focused on traditional assets like stocks and bonds, there is a growing demand for robo advisors that cater to non-traditional assets. In this guide, we will explore the world of robo advisors for non-traditional assets and highlight some of the top players in the industry.

The Rise of Non-Traditional Assets

Non-traditional assets, also known as alternative assets, encompass a wide range of investment opportunities beyond stocks and bonds. These assets include real estate, cryptocurrencies, peer-to-peer lending, and even collectibles like art and wine. As investors seek diversification and higher returns, the demand for robo advisors specializing in non-traditional assets has surged.

Factors to Consider

When evaluating robo advisors for non-traditional assets, several factors come into play. Here are some key considerations to keep in mind:

Expertise and Track Record

It is crucial to assess the expertise and track record of a robo advisor when it comes to non-traditional assets. Look for advisors with experience in the specific asset classes you are interested in. A track record of successfully navigating the complexities of these assets can provide confidence in their ability to deliver results.

Customization and Flexibility

Different investors have varying risk tolerances and investment goals. A good robo advisor should offer customization and flexibility to cater to individual needs. Look for platforms that allow you to tailor your portfolio based on your preferences, whether it be allocating more weightage to a specific asset class or excluding certain investments altogether.

Technology and User Experience

Robo advisors rely heavily on technology to automate investment processes. A user-friendly interface and intuitive platform are essential for a seamless investing experience. Look for robo advisors that offer a clean and intuitive interface, making it easy for you to navigate through different asset classes and track your portfolio’s performance.

Fees and Costs

One of the main advantages of robo advisors is their ability to offer low-cost investment solutions. However, it’s important to carefully review the fee structure of each robo advisor, especially when dealing with non-traditional assets. Some platforms may charge additional fees or higher management fees for certain asset classes. Ensure you understand the fee structure and compare it to the potential returns and value provided by the robo advisor.

Top Robo Advisors for Non-Traditional Assets

Now that we have discussed the key factors to consider, let’s explore some of the top robo advisors for non-traditional assets:

1. Wealthfront

Wealthfront is a leading robo advisor that offers a wide range of non-traditional assets, including real estate investment trusts (REITs) and cryptocurrencies. With their diversified and low-cost approach, Wealthfront aims to provide investors with exposure to alternative assets while minimizing risk.

2. Betterment

Betterment is known for its user-friendly platform and comprehensive investment options. While their focus is primarily on traditional assets, they have expanded their offerings to include socially responsible investing and smart beta strategies, which can be attractive to investors seeking non-traditional assets.

3. Fundrise

Fundrise is a robo advisor specializing in real estate investments. They provide investors with access to commercial real estate projects, allowing individuals to diversify their portfolios beyond stocks and bonds. With low investment minimums and the potential for attractive returns, Fundrise has gained popularity among investors looking for non-traditional assets in the real estate sector.

4. Coinbase

For investors interested in cryptocurrencies, Coinbase is a prominent robo advisor to consider. With a user-friendly platform and a wide range of cryptocurrencies available for investment, Coinbase makes it easy for individuals to enter the exciting world of digital assets.

Conclusion

As the investment landscape continues to evolve, robo advisors for non-traditional assets offer a compelling alternative for investors seeking diversification and higher returns. By considering factors such as expertise, customization, technology, and fees, you can select the right robo advisor to navigate the world of non-traditional assets. Whether it’s real estate, cryptocurrencies, or other alternative investments, these robo advisors can help you build a well-rounded portfolio that aligns with your investment goals. Happy investing!
