How to Choose Between Precedent Transactions and Market Approach


When it comes to valuing a business, there are two primary approaches: the precedent transactions method and the market approach. Both methods have their unique advantages depending on the situation, and understanding how to choose one over the other is essential for accurate valuation. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll delve into the intricacies of each method and explore the factors to consider when selecting the most suitable approach.

The Precedent Transactions Method

The precedent transactions method involves examining the prices paid for comparable businesses in recent past transactions. By evaluating these transactions, you can infer the fair market value of the subject company. This method is particularly useful in sectors where comparable businesses have recently traded, making it relatively straightforward to assess valuation benchmarks.

*Factors to Consider

  • Similarity: The more alike the companies are, the more relevant the comparison will be. Look for businesses in the same industry, of similar size, and with comparable profit margins.

  • Timing: The most important factor to consider is proximity to the transactions in question. The closer they are to the present, the more relevant they are to the current market conditions.

  • Adjustments: When comparing businesses, adjustments must be made for any significant differences between the companies. These could include factors such as intangible assets, growth potential, and regulatory environments.

  • Quality of Data: Ensure that the data used to derive the transaction value is reliable and that the businesses are appropriately valued.

The Market Approach

The market approach involves comparing the subject company to similar publicly traded companies to derive its fair market value. This method is particularly useful in sectors where there isn’t a history of recent transactions or where the company’s value is tied to the overall market performance.

Factors to Consider

  • Comparable Companies: These are businesses that are similar in size, industry, and business model to the subject company. Their financial metrics should closely align with that of the subject company.

  • Comparable Market: The market being used for comparison should be relevant, with companies that are publicly traded and frequently traded for liquidity.

  • Valuation Multiplication Factors: The market approach usually involves using a ‘valuation multiplier’ such as Price-to-earnings, Price-to-book, or Enterprise Value-to-EBITDA. Carefully consider which multiplier best aligns with the business model and risk profile to reliably value the subject company.

  • Adjustments: Just as in the precedent transactions approach, adjustments must be made for differences between the subject company and its comparables. These could include factors such as unique assets, liabilities, or competitive positioning.

Choosing Between Approaches

No one-size-fits-all methodology exists when choosing between the precedent transactions and market approach. The key lies in understanding the factors at play and the strengths and weaknesses of each method. Factors such as market liquidity, transaction data availability, and the uniqueness of the subject business should all be considered.

Ultimately, a blend of the two methods encompassing both current market data and business-specific analysis is often the most reliable valuation approach. By combining the strengths of precedent transactions and market approach, you’ll create a robust and comprehensive valuation, helping you make informed business decisions.

While choosing the right valuation method for your business, it is crucial to select the approach that best suits your subject company’s unique needs and circumstances. A seasoned financial professional can guide you through the process, providing expert insight into which approach would be ideal for your distinct situation.

In conclusion, the precedent transactions and market approach are two valuable methods for valuing businesses. While each has its strengths and weaknesses, understanding these attributes and carefully considering the unique factors involved in your particular valuation will help you select the best approach. With this guide as your starting point, you’ll be empowered to make sound and strategic business decisions for your enterprise.
