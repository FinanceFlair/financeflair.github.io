How to Sell Art and Prints Online as a Side Hustle


Starting a side hustle selling art and prints online is an exciting venture that blends creativity with entrepreneurship. Whether you’re an emerging artist or someone looking to monetize your creative passion, the digital marketplace offers an incredible platform to reach potential buyers worldwide. This guide maps out the journey of selling art and prints online—from setting up your digital storefront to mastering marketing strategies that drive sales. Let’s explore how to transform your artistic talent into a flourishing side income.

Understanding the Digital Art Market

Before diving into selling, it’s essential to comprehend the current landscape. The online art market has exploded over recent years, fueled by the increasing appetite for unique, original, and affordable artwork. Collectors, interior designers, and casual buyers flock to platforms to source prints and originals that match their tastes and budgets.

Key trends shaping the market include:

  • Growing demand for limited-edition prints.
  • Interest in diverse artistic styles, including digital, abstract, and contemporary.
  • Greater openness to purchasing art from emerging artists.
  • The rise of social media as a discovery tool.

Understanding these trends helps you position your artwork effectively to attract your ideal customer base.

Setting Up Your Online Storefront

Choosing the Right Platform

Several online platforms cater specifically to artists looking to sell work and prints. Selecting the best fit depends on your goals, budget, and technical comfort.

  • Etsy: An excellent starting point with a large existing audience. It’s ideal for artists selling prints, originals, or art-related products.
  • Shopify: Offers more customization and control over branding but requires more effort to attract traffic.
  • Fine Art America / Saatchi Art: Tailored to artists who want to sell prints and originals with print-on-demand and shipping handled.
  • Instagram Shop or Facebook Marketplace: Great for direct social media commerce but works best alongside another sales platform.

Consider starting on a marketplace with built-in traffic and later expanding to a personal website for brand control.

Building a Compelling Artist Profile

Your artist profile is your digital handshake. It tells the story behind your work, building trust and emotional connection.

  • Share the inspiration behind your art.
  • Include your artistic background and achievements.
  • Highlight your medium and style.
  • Use high-quality images of your artworks with accurate descriptions.

A well-crafted profile enhances credibility and encourages visitors to explore and purchase.

Crafting Your Product Listings

When selling prints or originals online, product listings are your silent salesperson. Key elements to focus on include:

  • High-resolution images: Showcase your work in the best light from multiple angles or in styled settings.
  • Detailed descriptions: Explain the concept, medium, size, and ideal display environment. Be clear about what the buyer will receive.
  • Pricing transparency: Include information on print editions, framing options, shipping costs, and return policy.
  • Customization options: Indicate if you accept custom commissions or personalized requests.

Clarity and quality in your listings reduce buyer hesitation and increase conversions.

Pricing Strategies for Art and Prints

Setting the right price can be challenging, balancing affordability and perceived value.

Pricing Originals

  • Base your price on size, medium cost, and time invested.
  • Consider the market average for similar artists.
  • Factor in exclusivity — limited editions or completely unique pieces can command higher prices.

Pricing Prints

  • Determine print production costs including printing, materials, packaging, and shipping.
  • Factor in your desired profit margin.
  • Offering various size options can attract a wider range of buyers.

Don’t undervalue your work but remain competitive within your niche.

Optimizing Production and Print Fulfillment

For prints, deciding between in-house or print-on-demand fulfillment is crucial.

  • In-house printing gives you control over quality but requires upfront investment in equipment.
  • Print-on-demand services like Printful or Printify handle production, packaging, and shipping. This reduces your workload but lowers margins.

Whichever route you choose, ensure your prints are archival-quality with fade-resistant inks and durable paper to maintain customer satisfaction.

Shipping and Packaging Tips

Presentation matters just as much as the art itself.

  • Use sturdy tubes or flat mailers with protective inserts.
  • Include a personalized thank-you note to enhance the customer experience.
  • Offer tracking options and clearly communicate shipping times.
  • Consider international shipping restrictions and costs if selling globally.

Well-executed packaging encourages positive reviews and repeat buyers.

Leveraging Social Media and Online Marketing

An online presence extends beyond your store front. Building a dedicated following through social media can drive consistent traffic and sales.

Best Practices:

  • Regularly share behind-the-scenes content revealing your creative process.
  • Engage with followers by responding to comments and messages.
  • Showcase customer photos and testimonials.
  • Collaborate with other artists or influencers to expand reach.
  • Use stories, reels, and live sessions to create interactive experiences.
  • Participate in art challenges or hashtags to improve discoverability.

Email Marketing

Build a mailing list by offering freebies like downloadable wallpapers or exclusive access to new collections. Email campaigns can announce sales, new releases, or share personal updates that deepen the connection with your audience.

Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

Optimize your website and product listings for search engines by naturally incorporating relevant keywords related to your art style, medium, and subject matter. This helps attract organic traffic from people actively searching for art similar to yours online.

Handling Customer Service and Building Reputation

Excellent customer service turns one-time buyers into loyal fans.

  • Respond promptly and professionally to inquiries.
  • Handle disputes or returns with empathy and clear communication.
  • Encourage reviews and feedback.
  • Offer occasional promotions or loyalty discounts.

Authentic interactions contribute to a positive reputation and word-of-mouth referrals.

Expanding Beyond Online Sales

Once your side hustle gains momentum, consider these opportunities to grow:

  • Selling at local art fairs or galleries.
  • Licensing your art for products like apparel or home decor.
  • Offering workshops or tutorials, either in-person or online.
  • Creating limited edition collections timed with holidays or seasons.

Diversification helps stabilize income and broadens your artistic influence.

Launching an online art and prints side hustle is more than just listing your work—it involves mastering the nuances of digital marketing, customer engagement, and quality control. By combining strong creative output with savvy business techniques, you can build a rewarding and profitable venture that shares your art with the world while generating sustainable income on the side. Dive in, experiment, and let your passion transform into a thriving online artistry business.
