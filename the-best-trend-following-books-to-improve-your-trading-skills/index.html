The Best Trend Following Books to Improve Your Trading Skills


Trading is an ever-evolving landscape where approaches and strategies can shift rapidly, but some principles retain their value through decades of market changes. Among these, trend following is a powerhouse—adaptable across markets, clear in its logic, and, for many successful traders, indispensable to a robust trading toolbox. Whether you’re just dipping your toes into trading or you’re an experienced investor aiming to refine your edge, the treasure trove of books devoted to trend following strategies offers practical techniques, insightful theory, and the kind of psychological wisdom that can set you apart from the herd.

So, how do you sort through the sea of literature on this subject? Which books actually deliver actionable, timeless advice worth your attention? Let’s dive into the standout titles that have become foundational for traders hungry to harness the power of market trends.

Why Focus on Trend Following Books?

Trend following isn’t about magic formulas or predictions. It’s about riding the waves of the market—identifying trends early and holding on long enough to profit as others scramble to figure out what’s happening. The beauty of this approach? It slots neatly into various asset classes, including stocks, futures, forex, and commodities. Yet, the simplicity of “cutting losses and letting winners run” hides a web of nuances—entry and exit rules, risk management, emotional discipline—that separate flashes in the pan from consistent long-term performance.

Books on trend following serve as roadmaps, distilling decades of collective trader experience into lessons you can apply right away. Some offer detailed system blueprints, while others zoom in on psychology and the soft skills just as necessary for success.

Let’s look at the books that encompass the full spectrum: from technical nuts and bolts to the mindset that keeps traders from self-sabotage.

1. “Trend Following: How to Make a Fortune in Bull, Bear, and Black Swan Markets” by Michael W. Covel

No discussion of trend following books is complete without this modern classic. Michael Covel’s “Trend Following” is as much a historical account of the discipline as it is a practical manual. It’s thorough, well-researched, and unashamedly contrarian to the cult of perfect market prediction. Instead, Covel champions adaptability, systematic rules, and a big-picture perspective.

What sets this book apart is its case studies—vivid stories about real traders who amassed fortunes not by predicting, but by following trends with disciplined systems. Covel unpacks their journeys and introduces you to the logic behind time-tested strategies. There’s a deep dive into risk management, trading psychology, and why most people fail at trend following: they can’t stomach the inevitable drawdowns or stick to the plan when excitement or fear sets in.

Takeaway: If you want a bigger-picture understanding, with enough concrete tactics to get started on your first (or next) trend following system, Covel’s book is a comprehensive foundation.

2. “Following the Trend: Diversified Managed Futures Trading” by Andreas F. Clenow

Andreas Clenow’s “Following the Trend” demystifies the performance of professional trend followers, often called CTAs (Commodity Trading Advisors). Written in plain, direct language, the book unpacks how large asset managers really make money with systematic trend following. While Clenow focuses on futures, the principles adapt well across markets.

What you’ll find invaluable is the book’s focus on actual strategies—not vague theory. Clenow illustrates a straightforward system, drilling into everything from position sizing and volatility adjustment to portfolio rebalancing. Along the way, he highlights pitfalls that trip up individual traders who chase performance or tweak systems endlessly.

Readers also get a healthy dose of realism. Trend following isn’t a get-rich-quick method. Clenow shows the truth: most years bring modest returns, but persistence through dry spells pays off with occasional monster years that make all the difference.

Takeaway: If you’re hungry for a step-by-step approach to building a robust trend following portfolio, especially in the world of managed futures, this book is your blueprint.

3. “The Complete TurtleTrader: How 23 Novice Investors Became Overnight Millionaires” by Michael W. Covel

Many traders have heard whispers about the legendary “Turtle” experiment—a group of complete novices taught to trade using strict rules, pitted against the best in the business. Covel’s “The Complete TurtleTrader” brings the story to life, blending narrative flair with practical insights.

The book details the rules and parameters the Turtles followed, with a particular focus on risk management and sticking to the system. It’s a striking validation of trend following: that discipline and process matter more than innate genius or market charisma.

Perhaps even more powerfully, Covel emphasizes the psychology of trading—how stubbornness, doubt, and cognitive biases can undermine even the best system unless you remain vigilant.

Takeaway: This book is perfect for those fascinated by trading lore, as well as anyone who wants tangible proof that anyone can learn to trade successfully with the right framework.

4. “Market Wizards” by Jack D. Schwager

“Market Wizards” is more than just a book—it’s an institution in the trading world. Schwager’s interviews with superstar traders span various strategies, but trend followers particularly shine through. The real meat comes from hearing trend traders (like Ed Seykota and Richard Dennis) explain their approach in their own words.

There’s no rigid “how-to” framework, but that’s the point. The book captures the diversity of trend following styles, bridging technical systems with insights on discipline, risk-taking, and the all-important ability to stick to one’s process through periods of doubt.

Schwager’s probing questions highlight not just what worked for these traders, but how they thought about losses, drawdowns, and criticism. There are no shortcuts—only honest reflections on what separates fleeting success from longevity in volatile markets.

Takeaway: If you want to learn from diverse personalities who’ve lived through every kind of market shock, “Market Wizards” delivers priceless wisdom straight from the front lines.

5. “Stocks on the Move: Beating the Market with Hedge Fund Momentum Strategies” by Andreas F. Clenow

Clenow returns to the list with a clear focus on stocks and a modernized take on momentum—the close cousin of trend following. The book is refreshingly hands-on, structured around detailed formulas and rules that are easy to implement if you’re familiar with spreadsheet analysis or programming.

What’s especially compelling here is Clenow’s debunking of common misconceptions: that trend following doesn’t work in equities, that it’s just “buy high, sell higher” with no foundation, or that only “insiders” can exploit momentum. The book lays out why and how diversified, systematic momentum strategies can outperform, especially when executed with discipline over the long haul.

Beyond strategy, readers get tools for backtesting and tracking performance, helping you separate luck from sustainability.

Takeaway: This is a modern, data-driven roadmap for the stock market trader who wants to apply the solid, systematic logic of trend following to equities.

6. “Trade Your Way to Financial Freedom” by Van K. Tharp

Van Tharp’s book is not narrowly about trend following, but it’s an essential companion for any trader looking to master the subtle art of building a robust system. Tharp’s focus on system development, position sizing, and psychological resilience harmonizes perfectly with a trend following approach.

He emphasizes that every winning trading system—especially trend following—must factor in risk, adaptability, and the trader’s own psychological quirks. The book doesn’t deal in “magic numbers,” but instead pushes you to think for yourself, test rigorously, and build rules that reflect your goals and risk tolerance.

For trend followers, the sections on dealing with losses—both statistically and emotionally—are an invaluable safeguard against the all-too-common habits of revenge trading or abandoning a good system at the first sign of trouble.

Takeaway: If you want to understand WHY systems work (or fail), and how to tailor principles to your unique situation, Tharp offers the ultimate toolkit for self-reliant trading.

7. “The Art and Science of Technical Analysis: Market Structure, Price Action, and Trading Strategies” by Adam Grimes

Adam Grimes’ contribution to trading literature stands tall for its blend of rigor and real-world applicability. While not exclusively about trend following, the book places the strategy within the broader context of technical analysis and market structure.

Grimes bridges gaps frequently overlooked: how to distinguish true trends from statistical noise, interpret price action, and design trading systems that adapt to shifting volatility regimes. He’s skeptical of overfit indicators and advocates for simplicity, robust backtesting, and a deep understanding of market dynamics.

Where this book shines is its emphasis on the mindset required for trend following success: tempering expectations, weathering losing streaks, and cultivating the discipline needed to survive the psychological grind of systematic trading.

Takeaway: For traders pursuing a holistic grasp of trends, price action, and system reliability, Grimes’ thorough and practical approach is a guiding light.

8. “Unholy Grails: A New Road to Wealth” by Nick Radge

Nick Radge’s “Unholy Grails” targets the individual trader who’s tired of guesswork and looking for robust, rule-based stock trading strategies. Radge lays out several systems centered on trend following and momentum, emphasizing clear rules, risk management, and simple daily routines.

What stands out is Radge’s balance between transparency and performance—he shows you what works, how to test it for yourself, and ways to avoid classic pitfalls, such as overfitting and emotional decision-making. There’s a refreshing focus on adapting systems to local market conditions and your own constraints.

For readers who want to implement a no-nonsense trend following approach without expensive software or inside access, this book could be a revelation.

Takeaway: If you’re a DIY trader who wants clear strategies with a proven foundation, Radge’s direct approach offers a powerful launchpad.

The Common Threads: What These Books Teach

A quick review of these trend following classics reveals key themes that repeat again and again. Here’s what bubbles up across them all:

  • Systematic Rules Beat Gut Feelings: Every top trend follower relies on a consistent set of entry, exit, and risk management rules. There’s no “tinkering” based on market rumors, hunches, or panic headlines.
  • Accept Losses and Drawdowns: Trend following is not designed for comfort; it thrives on patience and discipline through potentially long stretches of underperformance before delivering windfall profits.
  • Diversification and Adaptability: Effective trend followers diversify across multiple markets, sectors, or assets, avoiding concentration in “favorite” trades.
  • Psychological Resilience Reigns Supreme: All the precision in the world means little if you abandon your system at the first sign of discomfort or lurch into revenge trading after a loss.

These books don’t sugarcoat the challenges, nor do they promise quick riches. What they offer is the groundwork for enduring performance grounded in rules and reason.

How to Use These Books to Improve Your Trading

Reading alone won’t transform you into a proficient trend follower, but using these books as blueprints will accelerate your learning curve:

  • Start with the Big Picture: Dive into “Trend Following” by Covel and “Following the Trend” by Clenow for context and framework.
  • Study Real Traders: Schwager’s “Market Wizards” and Covel’s “The Complete TurtleTrader” will give you insight into trading personalities and common pitfalls.
  • Model and Test Systems: Use Radge’s “Unholy Grails” and Grimes’ text for specific, replicable processes. Experiment, backtest, and adapt.
  • Build Mental Toughness: Tharp’s “Trade Your Way to Financial Freedom” helps you fortify your emotional resilience—a vital edge.
  • Stay Flexible: The best traders revise their systems as markets evolve, always testing changes rigorously before implementation.

Final Thoughts

Trend following is a craft, not a mechanical box-checking exercise. It’s the alignment of sound logic, robust systems, and steady nerves. The books listed above have launched countless traders on their journey to consistency, independence, and sustainable profits—sometimes after years of earlier struggle.

You don’t need newfangled algorithms, market forecasts, or “secret indicators.” You just need the willingness to study hard, test thoroughly, and stick to your plan when the crowd loses its nerve.

Whether you’re starting from scratch or pushing to the next level, these trend following books can act as a compass, guiding you through uncertainty and turbulence to eventual mastery. If you internalize their lessons, the market’s waves become not something to fear—but opportunities to ride, again and again.

So pick one up, dig deep, and see what happens when you begin to follow the trends—methodically, systematically, and with real conviction. The journey is rarely boring, and for those who persist, the rewards can be transformative.
