The Best Robo-Advisors for Automatic Rebalancing


In today’s fast-paced world, managing your investments can be a daunting task. Luckily, technology has made it easier than ever to automate and optimize your portfolio. Robo-advisors have emerged as a popular solution, providing automated investment management services at a fraction of the cost of traditional financial advisors. Among the many features offered by robo-advisors, automatic rebalancing stands out as a key benefit for investors. In this article, we will explore some of the best robo-advisors that offer automatic rebalancing, helping you make informed decisions about your investments.

What is Automatic Rebalancing?

Before we dive into the details, let’s first understand what automatic rebalancing is and why it matters. Automatic rebalancing is a feature offered by robo-advisors that ensures your investment portfolio is aligned with your desired asset allocation. Over time, as certain investments perform better than others, your portfolio’s allocation may drift away from your original plan. Automatic rebalancing helps maintain the desired balance by periodically buying and selling assets to bring your portfolio back in line with your target allocation.

The Best Robo-Advisors for Automatic Rebalancing

  1. Wealthfront: Wealthfront is a well-established robo-advisor that offers automatic rebalancing as part of its comprehensive suite of investment services. With a focus on tax-efficient investing, Wealthfront’s platform monitors your portfolio and rebalances it as necessary to optimize tax-loss harvesting opportunities.

  2. Betterment: Betterment is another leading robo-advisor that incorporates automatic rebalancing into its investment strategy. With a user-friendly interface and a wide range of investment options, Betterment makes it easy for investors to maintain a diversified portfolio while benefiting from automatic rebalancing.

  3. Personal Capital: Personal Capital stands out for its robust financial planning tools and personalized investment advice. Along with automatic rebalancing, Personal Capital offers a comprehensive view of your financial picture, helping you make informed decisions about your investments.

  4. SigFig: SigFig combines the power of technology with the expertise of human advisors. With automatic rebalancing as a core feature, SigFig offers a hybrid approach to investment management, providing the best of both worlds.

Factors to Consider

When choosing a robo-advisor for automatic rebalancing, there are a few factors to consider:

  1. Cost: Different robo-advisors have varying fee structures, so it’s important to understand the costs involved. Some charge a percentage of assets under management, while others have flat fees. Take the time to compare costs and choose an option that aligns with your budget.

  2. Ease of Use: A user-friendly interface and intuitive platform can make a significant difference in your overall investing experience. Look for robo-advisors that offer a seamless user experience, making it easy for you to monitor and manage your investments.

  3. Additional Features: While automatic rebalancing is an essential feature, consider other services offered by robo-advisors. Features like tax optimization, goal-based investing, and access to human advisors can enhance your investment strategy.

Conclusion

Automatic rebalancing is a valuable tool that helps investors maintain their desired asset allocation without the hassle of constant monitoring and manual adjustments. With the rise of robo-advisors, investors now have access to affordable and convenient investment management services that include automatic rebalancing. Wealthfront, Betterment, Personal Capital, and SigFig are just a few examples of robo-advisors that offer this feature. When choosing a robo-advisor, consider factors such as cost, ease of use, and additional features to find the best fit for your investment goals. Take advantage of the power of technology and let automatic rebalancing optimize your portfolio for long-term success.
