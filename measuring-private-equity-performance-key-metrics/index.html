Measuring Private Equity Performance: Key Metrics


Private equity has emerged as an attractive investment option for individuals and institutions alike. With its potential for high returns, it’s no wonder that more and more people are looking to invest in this asset class. However, one of the challenges that investors face is evaluating the performance of their private equity investments. In this blog post, we will discuss the key metrics that can be used to measure private equity performance.

1. Internal Rate of Return (IRR)

The internal rate of return (IRR) is a widely used metric in the private equity industry. It measures the annualized rate of return that an investor can expect to earn from their investment. The IRR takes into account the timing and magnitude of cash flows, which makes it a useful tool for comparing different investment opportunities. A higher IRR indicates a better-performing investment.

2. Multiple of Invested Capital (MOIC)

The multiple of invested capital (MOIC) is another important metric in private equity performance measurement. It measures the total cash returned to investors relative to the amount of capital they initially invested. For example, if an investor receives $10 million in cash from an investment of $5 million, the MOIC would be 2x. A higher MOIC indicates a more successful investment.

3. Public Market Equivalent (PME)

The public market equivalent (PME) is a metric that compares the performance of a private equity investment to an equivalent investment in the public markets. It takes into account the timing and size of cash flows, as well as the risk profile of the investment. A PME greater than 1 indicates that the private equity investment outperformed the public markets.

4. Net Asset Value (NAV)

Net asset value (NAV) is a measure of the value of a private equity fund’s investments. It represents the net value of the fund’s assets after deducting its liabilities. NAV is usually calculated on a per-share basis and provides investors with an indication of the fund’s underlying value. A higher NAV indicates a more valuable fund.

5. Cash-on-Cash Return (CoC)

Cash-on-cash return (CoC) measures the annualized cash flow generated by a private equity investment relative to the amount of capital invested. It is calculated by dividing the cash flow generated by the investment by the initial capital investment. A higher CoC indicates a higher return on investment.

6. Time-Weighted Return (TWR)

Time-weighted return (TWR) is a metric that measures the compound rate of return over a specific time period. It takes into account the timing and magnitude of cash flows, allowing investors to evaluate the performance of their investments over time. TWR is particularly useful for measuring the performance of private equity funds with multiple investment periods.

In conclusion, measuring the performance of private equity investments is crucial for investors to make informed decisions. By utilizing key metrics such as IRR, MOIC, PME, NAV, CoC, and TWR, investors can gain insights into the success of their investments. It is important to note that these metrics should not be considered in isolation but rather as a comprehensive set of tools to evaluate private equity performance. As the private equity industry continues to grow, understanding and effectively using these metrics will be essential for investors seeking to maximize their returns.
