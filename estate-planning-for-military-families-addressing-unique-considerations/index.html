Estate Planning for Military Families: Addressing Unique Considerations


Military families face unique challenges when it comes to estate planning. The nature of military service, frequent relocations, and potential deployment can complicate the process. It is essential for military families to understand these considerations and take the necessary steps to protect their assets and provide for their loved ones. In this blog post, we will explore the key factors that military families should keep in mind when engaging in estate planning.

1. Consider the Uniformed Services Former Spouses’ Protection Act (USFSPA)

The USFSPA is a federal law that provides certain benefits to former spouses of military members. It addresses issues such as the division of military retired pay and survivor benefit plans. When creating an estate plan, military families should be aware of the USFSPA’s implications and ensure that their plan aligns with the provisions of this law.

2. Account for Frequent Relocations

Military families often move from one base to another, both domestically and internationally. These frequent relocations can complicate the estate planning process. It is crucial to keep legal documents, such as wills, powers of attorney, and healthcare directives, updated to reflect the current state’s laws. Working with an attorney experienced in military estate planning can help ensure that your documents comply with the laws of your current location.

3. Prepare for Deployment

Deployment is a reality for many military families. It is essential to have a plan in place that addresses various scenarios during deployment. Consider naming a trusted individual as a power of attorney to handle financial and legal matters while you are away. Additionally, review your beneficiary designations on life insurance policies, retirement accounts, and other assets to ensure they reflect your current wishes.

4. Understand Survivor Benefit Plans (SBP)

Military members who participate in the Survivor Benefit Plan (SBP) provide a monthly income to their eligible survivors upon their death. Understanding the details and options of the SBP is crucial when estate planning. Determine the level of coverage that best suits your family’s needs and ensure that your estate plan aligns with your SBP choices.

5. Utilize the Military Legal Assistance Office

Military families have access to legal assistance through their base’s Legal Assistance Office. These offices can provide guidance on estate planning matters, including the preparation of wills, powers of attorney, and healthcare directives. Take advantage of these resources to ensure your estate plan is comprehensive and up to date.

Conclusion

Estate planning for military families requires careful consideration of unique circumstances and legal protections. By addressing the USFSPA, frequent relocations, deployment, survivor benefit plans, and utilizing military legal assistance, military families can ensure their assets are protected and their loved ones are provided for. Consulting with an attorney experienced in military estate planning is essential to navigate the complexities of this process. Start planning today to secure the future for you and your family.
