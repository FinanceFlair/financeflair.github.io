How to Set Realistic Expectations with Trend Following


Trend following has long been an alluring strategy for traders and investors alike. The promise of riding lucrative market waves and capturing significant gains appeals to both beginners and seasoned professionals. However, success with trend following is far from guaranteed, and unrealistic expectations can quickly lead to frustration and losses.

This comprehensive guide delves into the foundations of trend following, explores the psychology behind it, and offers practical advice on setting achievable objectives. Whether you’re just beginning or refining your approach, understanding how to calibrate your expectations is crucial to navigating the market with confidence and discipline.

Understanding the Essence of Trend Following

At its core, trend following is a trading strategy that attempts to capitalize on market momentum by identifying and following established price trends. Unlike strategies that seek to predict reversals or attempt to time the market precisely, trend followers ride the wave until signals suggest the trend weakens or reverses.

Successful trend following involves:

  • Patience: Trends can take time to develop, and early entries or exits can erode potential profits.
  • Discipline: Sticking to rules even during drawdowns prevents impulsive decisions.
  • Adaptability: Markets evolve, and so must your systems and mindset.
  • Risk Management: Identifying where to place stops and position sizing to protect capital.

Despite its conceptual straightforwardness, trend following isn’t a surefire method to make quick riches. The strategy involves periods of underperformance, retracements, and false signals, all of which test a trader’s patience and emotional resilience.

Why Setting Realistic Expectations Matters

Unrealistic expectations lead to impatience, overtrading, and emotional decision-making, which can decimate returns. Common misconceptions include believing that trend following guarantees consistent daily or weekly profits, or expecting every position to yield sizable gains.

By setting realistic expectations, traders can:

  • Build mental resilience: Accepting losses and fluctuations as part of the process reduces emotional stress.
  • Improve decision-making: A clear understanding of probable outcomes helps maintain discipline.
  • Enhance risk management: Knowing when and where losses might occur allows for better controls.
  • Maintain long-term focus: Recognizing that trend following pays off over the long haul prevents impulsive behavior.

Let’s examine the ways to establish and align your expectations effectively.

The Nature of Returns in Trend Following

The Long Game

One of the most misunderstood aspects of trend following is the time horizon. This approach is not designed for rapid turnover or frequent wins; rather, it thrives over months or years as trends evolve. Drawdowns and consolidations are inevitable, and significant profits often come from a handful of major trends.

Many novice traders get discouraged during extended flat or negative periods because their outlook is skewed toward short-term outcomes. A realistic expectation is that returns are uneven, with intermittent periods of gains separated by drawdowns.

Understanding Drawdowns

Drawdowns refer to the peak-to-trough decline during a trading period. In trend following, sizeable drawdowns can occur when markets move sideways or reverse suddenly. Unlike certain short-term trading styles that aim for a near-constant flow of small profits, trend followers accept that losing periods are part of the game.

Therefore, any approach to trend following must mentally and financially account for these phases. Loss tolerance and capital allocation play key roles in surviving drawdowns without abandoning the strategy prematurely.

Win Rate Versus Profit per Trade

The win rate of a trend following strategy—the percentage of winning trades—is typically lower compared to other strategies. However, the size of the wins tends to far outstrip the losses, resulting in positive expectancy over time.

For example, a trend following system might win only 40% of the time, but those winning trades could average five times larger than the average loss. Preparing for this outcome prevents the misconception that traders must win most of their trades to be profitable.

Steps to Setting Realistic Expectations with Trend Following

1. Educate Yourself About Historical Performance

Before putting real money on the line, studying historical results of trend following systems can ground your expectations. Understand that while past results don’t guarantee future outcomes, they provide a valuable indication of likely volatility, returns, and drawdowns.

Analyze data that spans different market environments, ensuring your view is robust across bull, bear, and sideways markets. This context prevents the common pitfall of overhyping recent trends or anecdotal success stories.

2. Define Your Investment Horizon

Determine whether your strategy suits your time commitment and lifestyle. For instance, daily or weekly monitoring may be sufficient if you employ systematic entry and exit rules but do not expect instant feedback. Longer time horizons mean accepting patience as a core virtue.

Remember, trend following is not day trading. If you want frequent results, consider whether trend following aligns with your personality and goals.

3. Set Measurable Return Expectations

Establish clear, numerical goals based on realistic scenarios rather than guesswork. For instance, a historical annual return of 10%-15% might be reasonable after accounting for volatility, fees, and taxes.

Keep in mind that higher returns correspond with higher risk, so balancing your target with your risk tolerance is vital.

4. Plan for Drawdowns

Determine the maximum drawdown you can psychologically and financially bear without deviating from the plan. This often means allocating only a portion of your total capital to trend following and ensuring proper position sizing.

A trader unable to handle losses exceeding 20% during a drawdown phase may react emotionally, jeopardizing returns.

5. Understand the Psychological Challenges

The emotional roller-coaster from demographic swings of winning and losing trades requires psychological readiness. Develop mental discipline techniques such as journaling, meditative practices, or consulting mentorship to resist panic-driven moves.

Acknowledge that trades don’t always behave rationally, and aligning your mindset before trading can prevent emotional downfall.

6. Create a Robust Trading Plan

Define clear entry and exit criteria, position sizes, stop loss rules, and risk limits. Consistent application of rules avoids knee-jerk reactions driven by greed or fear.

Incorporate monitoring methods to evaluate system performance regularly while refraining from constant strategy tweaking.

Common Pitfalls in Expectation Management

Chasing Quick Wins

Many new trend followers expect to catch the “next big wave” immediately and generate extraordinary profits. This impatience results in jumping into trades prematurely or over-leveraging positions.

Recognizing that most trends take time to manifest and compound prevents chasing after the next hot signal.

Ignoring Risk Controls

Neglecting stop losses or position sizing inflates losses during volatile phases, throwing off the delicate balance between reward and risk.

Realistic expectations include accepting occasional losses but ensuring that each trade’s risk remains manageable relative to overall capital.

Emotional Trading

Reacting emotionally to short-term fluctuations undermines the steady application of the strategy. Impatience, fear, and greed can cause abandoning a sound plan right before it produces gains.

Embracing a calm, systematic mindset bolstered by realistic expectations helps to avoid costly mistakes.

Enhancing Discipline Through Record Keeping

Keeping a detailed trading journal documenting the rationale, emotions, profits, and losses behind each trade fosters self-awareness and continuous improvement. Over time, you will identify patterns of behavior affecting performance, enabling adjustments aligned with realistic expectations.

Incremental growth through ongoing reflection is a powerful tool for long-term success.

When to Reassess Your Expectations

No strategy is infallible. Periodically re-evaluate your trend following system’s performance against realistic benchmarks. If consistent losses or underperformance persist despite adherence, consider whether:

  • Market conditions have materially changed.
  • Your system requires updating.
  • Your personal tolerances have shifted.

Adjustments should be based on data and reason rather than impulses.

Supplemental Tools to Support Realistic Trend Following

  • Backtesting software: Simulate various scenarios and tweak parameters meaningfully.
  • Algorithmic trading platforms: Automate rule-based trades to remove emotional bias.
  • Risk management calculators: Quantify optimal position sizes and stop losses.
  • Community forums and mentorship: Engage with other traders to gain perspective.

These tools, combined with realistic expectations, construct a sturdy foundation for disciplined trend following.

Conclusion

Trend following remains an elegant approach to harnessing market momentum, but success hinges on setting clear, attainable expectations. Patience, understanding drawdowns, realistic performance goals, and strong risk controls form the pillars of long-term profitability.

By embracing the strategy’s natural ups and downs and refusing to chase illusions of immediate wealth, traders cultivate a balanced mindset. This balance ultimately leads to more consistent results, confidence in their methods, and greater peace of mind.

Remember, trend following is a journey, not a sprint. Adjust your expectations accordingly, stay disciplined, and allow the markets to do the heavy lifting.

Ready to hone your trend following approach? Start by defining your personal investment goals today and aligning them with the principles laid out here. The path becomes clearer when expectations meet reality head on.
