Robo Advisors Demystified: A Beginner's Review


A rob o-advisor is an automated online service that invests client funds in a diversified portfolio of securities as per a predetermined algorithm. They have been sprouting up lately, aiming to simplify the complex world of investing for the ordinary person. But as with any investment option, it’s crucial to know how they function before you entrust your hard-earned money to them.

The first thing to understand about robo-advisors is that they do not work for everyone. They are primarily suited for individuals who want an easy, low-cost way of building a diversified portfolio. But if you have specific investment goals, unique financial situations, or an inherent desire to manage your investments personally, a robo-advisor is not fit for you.

There are several platforms available in the market, each with its unique approach and nuances. Here, in this review, we highlight some factors to consider and critique a few popular platforms.

Fees

Fees constitute a significant distinction among robo-advisors. Every platform charges differently, usually as a percentage of the total assets managed. Naturally, a 0.50% fee is less appealing than a 0.25% fee. Additionally, some robo-advisors offer a ‘premium’ service that comes at a higher fee. Opt for these services only if the added features justify the extra expense.

Performance Tracking

One way to choose a robo-advisor is to examine their performance. Many robo-advisors publish backtested portfolios using historic data. While such data is not definitive, it can give you an idea of how a platform might perform in varied market conditions. It’s also worth checking if the robo-advisor is transparent about its portfolio turnover and rebalancing processes. These factors contribute significantly to your investment’s overall performance.

Personalization

As mentioned earlier, robo-advisors work best for those who want a low-cost, easy-to-manage diversified portfolio. However, some platforms do offer options for customization. Personalization in this sense usually means choosing between a ‘conservative’ or ‘aggressive’ portfolio. Others, like Wealthfront, even allow clients to input data to receive a personalized investment plan. While this personalization is an attractive feature, it is crucial not to overestimate your tolerance for risk.

Customer Service

It’s a lesser-talked-about factor, but customer service can be instrumental in ensuring you’re utilizing your robo-advisor’s features optimally. Some platforms like Schwab Intelligent Portfolios offer an integrated customer support option. This can be crucial when you’re first starting and need help navigating the platform.

One of the main drawbacks of robo-advisors is their lack of human intervention. While they can be excellent assistants, there might arise situations when your specific circumstances call for human intervention and understanding.

Conclusion

In conclusion, robo-advisors offer an accessible way for beginners to invest. They might not substitute human advisors entirely, but they can certainly be valuable tools in your investment toolbox. When choosing a robo-advisor, ensure you’re comfortable with the fees, the platform’s performance, and have a good understanding of the available personalization options. Remember, even though it’s the digital age, there’s no substitute for understanding your financial goals and risk tolerance.

The world of investing has diversified and advanced. With robo-advisors and other digital tools at your disposal, you deserve a shot at achieving your financial goals. Whether you’re ready to dive into the world of Robo-Advisor or still pool your understanding, the journey begins here. Happy Investing!
