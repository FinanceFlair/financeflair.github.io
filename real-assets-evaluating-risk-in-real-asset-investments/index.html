Real Assets: Evaluating Risk in Real Asset Investments


Real assets have become a popular choice for investors seeking diversification and tangible returns. While they often offer a stable source of income and an inflation hedge, they can also harbor unseen risks. Delving into various types of real assets, we explore the factors contributing to risk and how to evaluate these assets before investing.

Types of Real Assets

Real assets can be divided into several categories:

  1. Land and Real Estate: A primary and readily recognized form of real asset, land, and real estate offer stable cash flows through rental income, property appreciation, and capital growth. However, factors like location, legal, and zoning issues can impact value and return.

  2. Commodity Assets: Precious metals, livestock, and agricultural commodities are examples of physical goods. While they provide a store of value, they may require storage, management, and appreciation factors to consider.

  3. Art and Collectibles: As a form of investment, art and collectibles can yield lucrative returns when managed correctly. However, they often carry high transaction costs, expert curation, and authenticity requirements.

Evaluating Risk in Real Asset Investments

Before investing in any real asset, several factors should be considered to mitigate risk.

  1. Market Risk: Understanding market trends, supply and demand, and economic indicators affect asset allocation and timing. Market risks are often interrelated with geopolitical, environmental, and social influences.

  2. Monetary Policy and Inflation: Monetary policy shifts and overall economic stability impact asset returns. Inflation, taxation, and devaluations can significantly influence the value of real assets.

  3. Financial Risk: The stability of counterparties, credit risk, default risk, and liquidity all factor in an investor’s decision-making process. Ensuring financial stability of counterparties, currencies, and rates is crucial to mitigating financial risk.

  4. Ongoing Management and Obligations: The management of a real asset requires expertise and attention. Preservation, maintenance, and insurance duties can deplete returns and increase risks. Overlooking these obligations can result in significant liabilities.

  5. Transaction and Holding Costs: Costs related to asset purchase, sale, or holding can impact investment returns. Ensure understanding the full extent of expenses associated with the investment.

Evaluating Your Investment in Real Assets

When investing in real assets, a comprehensive evaluation process will help mitigate risks and improve returns.

  1. Asset Inspection: A comprehensive examination of the real asset must take place. Physical condition, legal documents, and market dynamics should all be considered.

  2. Due Diligence: Conduct thorough research on the real asset. Investigate ownership history, legal challenges, and environmental or regulatory factors.

  3. Real Estate Valuation: For land and real estate investments, engaging a professional appraiser or real estate agent to assess value in comparison to market trends is vital.

  4. Expert Opinions: Consult with domain experts when investing in commodities, art, or collectibles. Their knowledge and experience will assist in understanding asset value, potential risks, and valid exit strategies.

  5. Tax Implications: Investigate legal, taxation, and regulatory implications of owning and selling the asset. Tax compliance is a crucial part of successful real asset investments.

Conclusion

Real assets can be an excellent addition to an investor’s portfolio if evaluated and managed properly. However, understanding and mitigating risks associated with these investments is crucial to achieving maximum returns. Through thorough market and asset evaluation, comprehensive due diligence, and expert consultation, potential real asset investors can capitalize on these tangible investments and bolster their portfolio’s overall stability.
