Breaking the Chains of Impulsiveness: Position Trading as a Tool for Disciplined Investing


We’ve all been there: succumbing to the enticing allure of impulsiveness, especially in the realm of investing. But with position trading as a tool, we can successfully break free from the chains of impulsive behavior and embrace a more disciplined approach to investing.

Every trader has experienced the swift despair of impulsive decision-making. Sometimes, it’s a knee-jerk reaction to sudden market movement, or an emotional response to big news affecting your investments. Regardless of its source, impulsiveness can lead to many undesirable outcomes, including heavy losses and missed opportunities.

Position trading, an approach to investing that involves holding a position for an extended period, can help ward off impulsive behavior. This tried-and-true strategy could be the perfect antidote for chaotic trading habits.

In position trading, we focus on the long-term trend, strength, and momentum of well-defined markets. By closely observing and staying abreast of the most promising long-term trends, we develop a comprehensive plan and gradually assemble a diversified portfolio, made up of several positions as the market evolves. This approach gives both experience and confidence to traders and serves as a near-perfect remedy for impulsive trading habits.

Position trading is a disciplined approach that weaves the patience of a long-term investor with the analytical skills of a short-term trader. It emphasizes discipline, risk management, and execution with a bias towards scalability.

As a position trader, you make research-based decisions; this helps you avoid impulsive activities where emotions dictate your choices. When markets become volatile, your meticulously crafted plan provides perspective and nimbleness, allowing you to shift your market exposure efficiently without falling into the trap of impulsive behavior.

Position traders also learn to accept market volatility and leverage its fluctuations. Instead of fearing it, we capitalize on it. Without succumbing to impulsive trading, we seize the opportunity that inevitable market movements provide. We wait for the right moment when market prices intersect with our well-established long-term position plans.

To master position trading, you need to develop a keen understanding of the market, constantly observe and study any changes that occur, evolve your methods and strategies to these changes, and adapt to it all with an unwavering mindset. With every position acting as a statement of your belief in the market’s direction, you will naturally develop a healthier investment habit.

Position trading doesn’t guarantee a trading windfall, but it does provide the structure and discipline necessary for success. Historically, position traders generally experience fewer emotional setbacks, allowing them to make clearer, better-informed decisions without the influence of emotions.

So, the next time you’re confronted by market movement, remember: impulsive behavior could derail your success, but with position trading, the chains of impulsiveness can be broken to adopt a disciplined approach in investing.
