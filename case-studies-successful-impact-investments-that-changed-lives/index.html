Case Studies: Successful Impact Investments That Changed Lives


Impact investing has been gaining momentum in recent years, with investors increasingly looking for ways to drive both financial returns and positive social and environmental outcomes. One of the most compelling reasons to invest in impact investments is the opportunity to create lasting change in societies and communities worldwide. In this blog post, we will explore five case studies of successful impact investments that have had a profound impact on people's lives. Case Study 1: Finca International Finca International is a non-profit organization that provides microfinance loans to entrepreneurs in Latin America. Established in 1994, Finca has disbursed over $100 million in loans to over 350,000 borrowers, creating jobs and stimulating economic growth in several countries. By providing access to capital for microentrepreneurs, Finca has empowered women and men to start and grow businesses that have improved their livelihoods and strengthened their families. One such borrower is Maria, a Peruvian entrepreneur who started a small textile business with a loan from Finca. With the loan, Maria was able to increase her sales by 50% and employ three other women, providing a vital source of income for her community. Case Study 2: Students Investing in Students The Microfinance for Education initiative, founded in 2001 by Albert Park, aims to provide education to disadvantaged children and young adults in Asia. Microfinance for Education provides grants and loans to low-income families to cover educational expenses such as tuition fees, books, and computers. This initiative has enabled thousands of students to attend school, achieve their academic goals, and break the cycle of poverty. One of the beneficiaries of this initiative is Lailani, a 9-year-old Cambodian girl who was able to attend school and graduate as the top student in her class due to the financial support from Microfinance for Education. Case Study 3: Yoga in the Slums In Nairobi's Kibera slums, millions of people live in poverty, struggling to access basic necessities like healthcare, education, and sanitation. The Kenyan charity Longitude Discovery Society offers holistic education programs, providing residents with skills training, mentorship, and income-generating activities. One of the main programs is a yoga initiative that uses yoga and mindfulness to teach confidence, self-esteem, and resilience to young women. These women are empowered with practical skills and can support their families and gain a better quality of life. A participant in the program was 21-year-old Jacey Johnson, a single mother who lost her husband to HIV/AIDS. Jacey successfully secured a grant to start her own small business making biofuels and now supports three of her children. Case Study 4: Lapiz Saphi Foundation Situated in rural Thailand, the Lapiz Saphi Foundation works with rural communities to promote education, healthcare, and sanitation. After realizing the poor food security in the Bungtam District, Lapiz started a sustainable agriculture project providing training, rice seeds, and equipment to grow quality crops, ensuring their food security. Farmers who continue to borrow tools and grants allow them to keep competing economically. By increasing the community's income and job opportunities, Lapiz's project enables families to achieve full access to total sanitation facilities, primitively protecting their health, the children, and future success.
