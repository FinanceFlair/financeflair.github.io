Best Side Hustles for Food Delivery and Ride-Sharing Drivers


In today’s gig economy, working as a food delivery or ride-sharing driver has become a popular way to generate income. However, many drivers seek additional opportunities that complement their current work, providing extra cash flow without disrupting their schedules. Exploring side hustles tailored to the lifestyle and skills of food delivery and ride-sharing drivers can maximize earnings and open new doors for financial freedom.

The Appeal of Side Hustling for Gig Drivers

Drivers in food delivery and ride-share services enjoy flexibility and mobility, which are valuable assets when selecting a side hustle. The ideal side hustle should align with a driver’s schedule, leverage their vehicle or their time, and minimize startup costs. Many side gigs can be pursued in conjunction with driving jobs, either in the same service hours or during breaks and downtime.

Top Side Hustle Ideas for Drivers

Here are some of the most effective and accessible side hustles specifically suited for food delivery and ride-sharing drivers, along with tips to get started.

1. Vehicle Advertising

Putting your car to work as a moving billboard is an effortless way to make money while doing what you already do: driving. Several companies pay drivers to wrap their vehicles in advertisements or place magnetic signs on doors.

  • How it works: Sign up with agencies specializing in car ad placements. They partner with brands and run campaigns in specific areas.
  • Income potential: Can range from $100 to $400 per month depending on the ad size and campaign duration.
  • Best for: Drivers consistently on the road who want passive income without extra work.

2. Package and Courier Delivery

In addition to meal delivery, expanding into courier services for small businesses or local stores can increase earnings. Many companies and individuals need reliable couriers who can handle small parcels and documents swiftly.

  • How to start: Register with courier platforms or connect directly with local businesses looking for dependable delivery.
  • Why it works: Often pays better per delivery than food, and contracts may be flexible.
  • Tip: Keep an organized schedule to juggle both courier and food delivery demands.

3. Grocery Shopping and Delivery

As grocery delivery demand continues to rise, drivers can join platforms dedicated to this niche or work independently for neighbors and community members. This side hustle involves shopping for groceries before delivering, blending errand-running with delivery work.

  • Platform options: Instacart, Shipt, and local delivery apps.
  • Advantages: Higher tips due to personal shopping and bigger order sizes.
  • Considerations: Requires time to shop, so plan availability accordingly.

4. Mobile Car Detailing

Drivers usually have a good understanding of car care, which can be a springboard for launching a mobile car detailing business. You can visit clients at their homes or workplaces, providing washing, waxing, and cleaning services.

  • Startup costs: Basic car cleaning supplies; the rest is skill and marketing.
  • Flexibility: Schedule appointments during your off-driving hours.
  • Earning potential: Can be substantial, especially with recurring customers.

5. Pet Transportation Services

Many pet owners need help transporting their pets to vet appointments, grooming, or daycare. Drivers can fill this niche by offering pet transport, a service blending driving skills with customer service.

  • Getting started: Advertise locally or join specialized pet transport platforms.
  • Special tip: Insure yourself for pet transport liability and take animal handling courses.
  • Why it’s lucrative: Pet owners often pay premium for trustworthy, caring drivers.

6. Rent Your Car Out When Not in Use

If you don’t drive your car full-time or during certain hours, renting it out via peer-to-peer car rental services can provide continuous passive income.

  • Popular platforms: Turo, Getaround.
  • Safety: Vet renters and understand insurance policies.
  • Benefit: Makes your vehicle an income generator even when you’re off the road.

7. Freelance Delivery Guide or Route Planner

Experienced drivers accumulate deep knowledge of local geography, traffic patterns, and shortcuts. This insight can be valuable to new delivery drivers or small businesses.

  • How to monetize: Offer personalized route planning services or online consultation.
  • Complementary skills: Basic tech knowledge to use mapping software effectively.
  • Market: Delivery startups, local couriers, or individuals new to the area.

8. Selling Customized Driver Accessories and Gear

Drivers often invest in accessories such as insulated bags, phone mounts, or custom apparel. Creating and selling customized or branded gear can turn passion into profit.

  • Approach: Use online marketplaces or social media to sell products.
  • Creation: Partner with manufacturers or create DIY products.
  • Why it works: Targeted marketing to fellow gig workers guarantees customers who understand the value.

9. Offering Driving Lessons or Coaching

For those with solid driving experience and a great record, teaching beginner drivers or offering coaching for ride-share tactics can be rewarding.

  • Getting started: Obtain necessary certifications or partner with driving schools.
  • Additional angle: Offer coaching for ride-share drivers on maximizing earnings.
  • Impact: Helps beginners while supplementing your income.

10. Virtual Assistance and Remote Customer Support

If you prefer less road time during off hours, virtual assistant work or remote customer service positions can generate extra income without leaving home.

  • Why it suits drivers: Work from anywhere; flexible part-time shifts.
  • Popular roles: Scheduling, email management, phone support, and chat assistance.
  • Skills required: Communication, multitasking, basic office software proficiency.

Managing Multiple Side Hustles Effectively

Balancing multiple gigs requires good time management and efficiency strategies. Here are a few tips to maintain productivity while keeping stress low:

  • Plan downtime: Use slow delivery hours to tackle other side hustle tasks.
  • Keep records: Track income and expenses meticulously to simplify taxes.
  • Leverage technology: Use apps for scheduling, route optimization, and communication.
  • Prioritize hustle compatibility: Choose side gigs that don’t clash with your primary driving commitments.

Conclusion: Diversify to Maximize Earnings

Food delivery and ride-sharing offer lucrative gig opportunities, but coupling these with compatible side hustles can significantly boost your income. Whether it’s passive income through vehicle advertising or active engagements like pet transport and car detailing, drivers have diverse options. Experiment with complementary gigs to discover what fits your skills and schedule best. This approach not only grows your earning potential but also opens doors for career flexibility and financial security in an ever-evolving gig economy.
