Top Retirement Planning Apps and Tools in 2024


Planning for retirement can feel overwhelming, but thanks to technology, managing your finances for those golden years has never been easier. In 2024, a wide variety of apps and digital tools offer features tailored to every stage of retirement planning—from budgeting and investing to projecting future income and estimating Social Security benefits. Whether you’re just getting started or are well on your journey, choosing the right retirement planning app can empower you to make informed decisions and keep your financial goals on track.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the top retirement planning apps and tools available today. We’ll cover their key features, strengths, and how they can help you approach retirement with confidence.

Why Use Retirement Planning Apps?

Before diving into specifics, it’s worth understanding why retirement planning apps have become essential tools:

  • Personalized Projections: Apps use your financial data to generate personalized retirement forecasts. They can model different scenarios, helping you understand the impact of saving rates, investment returns, and spending habits.
  • Real-Time Monitoring: Finance changes constantly. These tools sync with your accounts to provide an up-to-date picture of your progress.
  • Goal Setting and Tracking: Setting clear retirement goals is easier with apps that track your progress and send reminders or adjustments needed to hit your targets.
  • Education and Insights: Many tools come with resources to help you learn about retirement accounts, tax implications, optimal withdrawal strategies, and more.
  • Accessibility and Convenience: Mobile-first platforms make it possible to manage your retirement plan anytime, anywhere.

Evaluating Retirement Planning Apps for 2024

When selecting a retirement planning app, consider the following criteria:

  • Ease of Use: Is the app intuitive with a user-friendly interface?
  • Integration: Can it connect with multiple accounts, including investment, bank, and pension accounts?
  • Comprehensive Planning: Does it cover various aspects such as social security optimization, healthcare costs, and inflation adjustments?
  • Customization: Can you adjust variables like retirement age, income goals, or risk tolerance?
  • Cost: Free apps are great, but some paid tools offer expanded features. Analyze value for your specific needs.
  • Customer Support: Availability of help and educational resources.

Top Retirement Planning Apps and Tools in 2024

1. Personal Capital

Overview: Personal Capital blends investment management with comprehensive retirement planning. Its retirement planner tool integrates your entire financial picture, including assets, debts, income, and spending.

  • Key Features:

    • Dynamic retirement planner with scenario analysis
    • Tracks 401(k), IRAs, brokerage accounts
    • Fee analyzer to identify hidden fees in investments
    • Cash flow tracker and budgeting tools

  • Why It’s Great: This app delivers a robust, easy-to-navigate platform that caters to both DIY investors and those seeking professional advice. The personalized retirement planner forecasts your financial future under various conditions—such as market volatility or changing savings rates—giving you powerful insight.

  • Cost: Free for basic tools; wealth management services available for a fee.

2. NewRetirement

Overview: NewRetirement is a retirement-specific planning platform with deep customization options and detailed projections.

  • Key Features:

    • Multi-scenario planning including Social Security, pensions, and Medicare
    • Comprehensive income and expense tracking
    • Tax impact analysis and withdrawal strategy planning
    • Ability to model late retirement or downsizing scenarios

  • Why It Stands Out: NewRetirement’s strength lies in its focused approach, modeling many real-world retirement complexities. Its easy-to-understand dashboards help users make sense of complicated planning variables.

  • Cost: Free basic version; premium plans with advanced features start around $10/month.

3. Fidelity Retirement Score

Overview: Fidelity Investments offers the Retirement Score tool as part of its suite, providing a quick assessment of your retirement readiness.

  • Key Features:

    • Calculates a “retirement score” based on your savings, income, and expenses
    • Provides actionable advice to improve readiness
    • Integrates with Fidelity accounts seamlessly

  • Best For: Individuals who want a fast, no-frills snapshot of where they stand for retirement, especially Fidelity customers.

  • Cost: Free

4. Retirement Planner by SmartAsset

Overview: This planner is a browser-based tool designed to help everyday users plan for retirement by inputting income sources, investments, expenses, and more.

  • Key Features:

    • Simple input forms with easy-to-read output summaries
    • Tax planning projections
    • Social Security timing calculators
    • Monte Carlo simulations to understand risks

  • Why Choose It: It’s an excellent starter tool for those new to retirement planning who want a straightforward but comprehensive snapshot of their situation.

  • Cost: Free

5. Tiller Money

Overview: Tiller Money isn’t a retirement app in the traditional sense but a powerful spreadsheet automation tool that connects bank and investment accounts to Google Sheets or Excel.

  • Key Features:

    • Automated data imports for budgeting and detailed financial tracking
    • Customizable templates for retirement savings tracking and projections
    • Powerful if you want flexibility and control over retirement data

  • Ideal User: Data lovers and spreadsheet enthusiasts who want a highly customizable retirement plan and keen on granular control over inputs.

  • Cost: Subscription-based (~$80/year) after a free trial

6. Wealthfront

Overview: Wealthfront combines automated investment management with a retirement planner capable of detailed financial projections.

  • Key Features:

    • Tax-efficient investment portfolios
    • Retirement planner including 401(k) and IRA accounts
    • Path tool providing a holistic view of your financial future considering spending habits
    • Social Security and Medicare cost estimators

  • Strength: A great all-in-one robo-advisor with powerful retirement planning features baked in, making it ideal for hands-off investors.

  • Cost: Free financial planning tools; investment management fees apply.

7. Betterment

Overview: Another major robo-advisor, Betterment offers integrated goal-based retirement planning combined with automated portfolio management.

  • Key Features:

    • Goal tracking for retirement with personalized spending, saving, and investing advice
    • Social Security optimization recommendations
    • Tax loss harvesting and low-cost ETFs

  • Why It’s Popular: It marries easy-to-use retirement goal setting with investment automation and expert advice.

  • Cost: Fees start at 0.25% of assets managed; planning tools are free to account holders.

8. Social Security Quick Calculator by SSA

Overview: Provided by the Social Security Administration, this tool estimates your future benefits based on your actual earning record.

  • Key Features:

    • Reliable, official projection of Social Security benefits
    • Allows you to explore how benefits change with retirement age
    • Clarifies spousal and survivor benefits

  • Why Use It: Social Security benefits often make up a significant chunk of retirement income. Having an accurate estimate is essential for realistic retirement planning.

  • Cost: Free

9. Monument Advisor

Overview: Monument provides a more personalized retirement planning service combining human financial advisors and technology.

  • Key Features:

    • Individualized retirement analysis and recommendations
    • Comprehensive plan including investments, savings, risk management
    • Access to expert advisors with ongoing support

  • Perfect for: Those wanting expert guidance combined with digital convenience.

  • Cost: Typically fee-based with different tiers depending on service level.

Features to Look for in 2024 Retirement Tools

While all the apps above offer valuable services, consider these cutting-edge features when choosing your tool:

  • AI-Powered Forecasting: Using artificial intelligence to refine investment growth assumptions and spending risk predictions.
  • Health Care Cost Integration: Factoring in expected Medicare premiums, out-of-pocket expenses, and long-term care.
  • Tax Optimization: Automatically analyzing withdrawal sequences to minimize tax penalties and maximize after-tax income.
  • Spousal Planning: Tools that model joint finances, survivor benefits, and estate considerations.
  • Inflation Adjustments: Robust inflation assumptions that reflect current economic conditions.
  • Behavioral Nudges: Gamification and reminders that encourage consistent saving and smart financial decisions.
  • Multi-Device Syncing: Seamless access across mobile, tablet, and desktop.

Tips for Maximizing Your Retirement Planning with Apps

No app can replace thoughtful, proactive engagement. Here’s how to make the most of the technology:

  • Regularly Update Your Data: Keep account balances, income, and spending information current.
  • Test Different Scenarios: Use “what-if” analyses to see how changes affect your retirement age, lifestyle, or savings.
  • Leverage Educational Content: Many apps come with tutorials, blogs, or webinars—take advantage of them.
  • Combine Tools as Needed: Use budgeting apps alongside robo-advisors or Social Security calculators to get a full picture.
  • Review Annually: Life changes, market variations, and policy updates mean your planning needs adjustment over time.

Final Thoughts

In 2024, there’s no shortage of high-quality retirement planning apps and tools designed to help you build a secure and comfortable future. Whether you prefer a hands-on spreadsheet approach, an automated robo-advisor service, or a dedicated retirement calculator, technology now offers precision and personalization once only available through expensive financial advisors.

The best retirement planning tool is one that fits your comfort with technology, your financial goals, and your desire for guidance or control. By selecting the right app or combination of tools, you’ll gain clarity, confidence, and measurable progress on the path to your ideal retirement.

Embrace the power of these digital resources today—your future self will thank you.
