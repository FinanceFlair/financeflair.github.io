The Evolution of Trend Following: From Discretionary to Systematic


Trend following is a popular investment strategy that has evolved significantly over the years. In its early days, trend following was primarily a discretionary approach, relying on the intuition and expertise of individual traders. However, with advancements in technology and the availability of vast amounts of data, trend following has transformed into a systematic approach that utilizes quantitative models and algorithms to identify and exploit market trends.

The Discretionary Era

In the early days of trend following, traders relied on their experience and judgment to identify and capitalize on market trends. These traders would analyze charts and indicators to spot patterns and make decisions based on their interpretation of the market. This discretionary approach was highly subjective and dependent on the individual trader’s skills and insights.

While discretionary trend following had its merits, it also had limitations. The success of the strategy was heavily reliant on the trader’s ability to correctly interpret market signals and make timely decisions. Human biases and emotions could often cloud judgment, leading to suboptimal outcomes. Additionally, the discretionary approach lacked a systematic framework, making it difficult to replicate and scale.

The Rise of Systematic Trend Following

With the advent of computers and the increasing availability of market data, trend following gradually shifted towards a more systematic approach. Systematic trend following involves the use of quantitative models and algorithms to identify and exploit market trends. These models analyze vast amounts of historical data to identify patterns and generate trading signals.

One of the key advantages of systematic trend following is its ability to remove human biases and emotions from the decision-making process. By relying on objective rules and algorithms, systematic trend following seeks to eliminate the impact of subjective interpretations and emotions that can hinder performance. This approach also allows for greater scalability, as the models can be easily replicated and applied to different markets and timeframes.

The Role of Technology

Technological advancements have played a crucial role in the evolution of trend following. The availability of high-speed computers and sophisticated software has enabled traders to process vast amounts of data and execute trades with speed and efficiency. Additionally, the proliferation of online trading platforms and data providers has made market information more accessible than ever before.

With the help of technology, systematic trend following strategies can now analyze large datasets spanning multiple markets and timeframes. These strategies can identify trends, generate signals, and execute trades in real-time, allowing for quick and efficient capital deployment. Furthermore, advancements in machine learning and artificial intelligence have opened up new possibilities for trend following, enabling the development of more sophisticated models that can adapt to changing market conditions.

The Future of Trend Following

As technology continues to advance and market dynamics evolve, trend following is likely to undergo further transformation. The integration of alternative data sources, such as social media sentiment and satellite imagery, may provide additional insights for trend following models. Furthermore, advancements in computing power and data processing capabilities may enable the development of even more complex and accurate models.

However, it’s important to note that while systematic trend following has gained popularity, discretionary approaches still have their place in certain market conditions. The ability to interpret qualitative information and incorporate expert judgment can be valuable in certain situations where quantitative models may fall short.

In conclusion, the evolution of trend following from a discretionary approach to a systematic one has been driven by advancements in technology and the need for more objective and scalable investment strategies. Systematic trend following has proven to be effective in eliminating human biases and emotions, while also providing scalability and efficiency. As technology continues to advance, trend following is likely to undergo further refinements, offering new opportunities for investors seeking to capitalize on market trends.
