Hedging is a risk management technique that involves taking a position in a financial instrument that is designed to offset potential losses or gains from an existing position. In the context of trend following, hedging can be achieved by taking a position in an asset that has a price movement that is negatively correlated with the original investment. For example, if an investor has a long position in a stock that is trending upwards, they can hedge against potential losses by buying a put option or shorting a futures contract on the same stock.
