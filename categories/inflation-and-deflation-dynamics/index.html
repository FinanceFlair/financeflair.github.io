This concept is known as the “paradox of thrift” and it centers on the tension between personal savings and national economic growth. The paradox of thrift emerged in the early 20th century following the publication of “The Theory of the Leisure Class” by the American economist Thorstein Veblen. Veblen postulated that if everyone aims to save more and spend less in response to economic hardship, it may have a counterproductive effect on the economy. On one hand, individual savings foster financial stability and personal security.
