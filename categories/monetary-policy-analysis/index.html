Understanding this relationship is crucial for policymakers, investors, and individuals alike, as it impacts borrowing costs, investment decisions, and overall economic stability. The Fisher Effect Explained The Fisher effect posits that nominal interest rates are influenced by two key factors: inflation expectations and real interest rates. In simple terms, the Fisher effect suggests that if inflation expectations rise, nominal interest rates will also increase to compensate for the expected decrease in purchasing power of money.
