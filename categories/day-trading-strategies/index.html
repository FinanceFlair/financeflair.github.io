The Stochastic Oscillator is a popular technical analysis tool used by day traders to identify potential buying and selling opportunities in the market. It is based on the theory that as prices rise, closing prices tend to be closer to the upper end of the trading range, and as prices fall, closing prices tend to be closer to the lower end of the trading range. The Stochastic Oscillator is a momentum indicator that compares a security’s closing price to its price range over a given time period. It consists of two lines, %K and %D, which oscillate between 0 and 100.
