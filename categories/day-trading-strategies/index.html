The MACD is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a security’s price. It consists of three main components: the MACD line, the signal line, and the histogram. The MACD line is calculated by subtracting the 26-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA) from the 12-period EMA. The signal line, often a 9-period EMA of the MACD line, acts as a trigger for buy and sell signals. The histogram represents the difference between the MACD line and the signal line. ...
