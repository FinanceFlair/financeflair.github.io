While DCA is commonly associated with long-term investment strategies, its advantages extend to retirement planning, particularly in smoothing out market volatility and reducing the impact of market fluctuations on retirement savings. In this article, we’ll delve into the benefits of dollar-cost averaging in retirement planning and how it can help retirees achieve their financial goals with greater confidence and stability. Before diving into its benefits, let’s first understand how dollar-cost averaging works. With DCA, investors contribute a fixed amount of money to their investment portfolio at regular intervals, such as monthly or quarterly. Regardless of whether the market is up or down, the investor buys more shares when prices are low and fewer shares when prices are high.
