Frugal Living Hacks for Homeowners: Lower Your Mortgage Costs


Owning a home is one of the biggest financial commitments many people will make in their lifetime. While it brings the joy of having your own space, it also comes with hefty costs, the most significant of which is your mortgage payment. Whether you’ve just bought your first home or have been living in one for years, finding ways to reduce your monthly mortgage expenses can make a big difference in your financial wellbeing. In this post, we’ll explore practical and effective frugal living hacks that homeowners can use to lower their mortgage costs and create more room in their budgets.

1. Refinance Your Mortgage

One of the most effective ways to lower your monthly mortgage payment is by refinancing. Refinancing allows you to replace your existing mortgage with a new one, ideally with a better interest rate or more favorable terms. By doing so, you could potentially lower your monthly payments significantly.

How to Refinance:

  • Evaluate Current Market Rates: Keep an eye on mortgage rates. If they are lower than what you currently have, it may be time to consider refinancing.
  • Check Your Credit Score: A higher credit score can help you secure a better interest rate. If your score has improved since you first took out the mortgage, refinancing might save you money.
  • Consider the Costs: Refinancing comes with closing costs, which can range from 2% to 5% of the loan amount. Make sure the savings from a lower rate outweigh the refinancing costs before proceeding.

When to Refinance:

  • When Interest Rates Drop: If mortgage rates fall significantly below your current rate, refinancing can provide immediate savings.
  • When You Can Afford to Pay Points Upfront: If you have the cash to pay for points (fees that lower your interest rate) upfront, refinancing could make sense.
  • When Your Loan Term is Shorter: Refinancing to a shorter loan term, such as from a 30-year to a 15-year mortgage, can save you thousands of dollars in interest over the life of the loan.

2. Make Extra Payments

Making additional payments toward your mortgage can significantly reduce the amount of interest you pay over the life of the loan and shorten the term of your mortgage. You don’t have to make massive extra payments either – small contributions can add up over time.

Types of Extra Payments:

  • Biweekly Payments: Instead of making monthly payments, consider paying half of your mortgage payment every two weeks. This adds up to 26 half-payments or 13 full payments a year, rather than the typical 12. This can help you pay off your loan faster and reduce interest costs.
  • Additional Principal Payments: In addition to your regular monthly payment, you can make lump sum payments directly toward the principal. Even small amounts can make a big difference in the total interest you pay over time.
  • Round Up Payments: If you can’t make large additional payments, consider rounding up your regular payment. For example, if your monthly mortgage is $1,180, round it up to $1,200 or $1,250. This little extra can add up to significant savings in the long run.

Benefits of Extra Payments:

  • Save on Interest: The more you pay toward the principal, the less interest you will be charged over the life of the loan.
  • Shorten Your Loan Term: Extra payments can reduce the total length of your mortgage, helping you pay off your home sooner.

3. Shop Around for Homeowners Insurance

Your homeowners insurance can be a hidden cost that you might overlook when managing your mortgage. Shopping around for the best homeowners insurance rates can help you lower your overall monthly expenses. Insurance rates can vary significantly between providers, so it’s worth comparing quotes regularly to ensure you’re not overpaying.

How to Lower Your Insurance Costs:

  • Increase Your Deductible: By opting for a higher deductible, you can lower your premium. Just make sure you have enough savings set aside to cover the deductible if you need to make a claim.
  • Bundle Policies: Many insurers offer discounts if you bundle your homeowners insurance with other policies, such as auto insurance or life insurance.
  • Install Safety Features: Installing safety features like smoke detectors, a security system, or fire extinguishers can lead to lower insurance premiums, as these measures reduce the risk of loss.
  • Shop Annually: Insurance companies often raise their rates after the first year. Make it a habit to shop for new quotes annually and switch providers if you find a better deal.

4. Eliminate Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is typically required if you have a conventional loan with a down payment of less than 20%. PMI protects the lender in case you default on the loan, but it’s an added cost that can increase your monthly payment. Once you have built up enough equity in your home, you can request to cancel PMI, which could significantly lower your monthly expenses.

How to Eliminate PMI:

  • Reach 20% Equity: If the value of your home has increased or you’ve made extra payments to reduce the principal, you may have reached 20% equity, allowing you to cancel PMI.
  • Request a PMI Removal: Contact your lender to request that PMI be removed once you’ve reached 20% equity. Be sure to ask for a written confirmation of the cancellation.
  • Get a Home Appraisal: If you believe your home has appreciated in value since you purchased it, you can pay for a professional appraisal. If the appraisal shows that you have 20% equity, you can use this to cancel PMI.

5. Reduce Energy Costs

While reducing energy costs might not directly impact your mortgage, it can help lower your overall living expenses, freeing up more money to put toward your mortgage. By making your home more energy-efficient, you can reduce utility bills, which can be a significant portion of your monthly expenses.

Ways to Lower Energy Costs:

  • Seal Gaps and Leaks: Check your home for drafts and seal gaps around windows, doors, and vents to prevent heat loss in the winter and cool air from escaping in the summer.
  • Upgrade Insulation: Ensure your home is properly insulated to help maintain a comfortable temperature year-round, reducing the need for heating and cooling.
  • Switch to Energy-Efficient Appliances: If your appliances are outdated, consider upgrading to energy-efficient models. These can save you money in the long run by reducing your energy consumption.
  • Use Programmable Thermostats: Install a programmable thermostat to regulate your heating and cooling system based on your schedule. This ensures that your home isn’t using energy when it’s not needed.

6. Reassess Your Property Taxes

Property taxes can be a significant part of your monthly expenses, and while they may not be directly tied to your mortgage, they still affect your overall housing costs. If you believe your property taxes are too high, it’s worth reviewing your assessment and exploring ways to lower them.

How to Lower Property Taxes:

  • Check Your Property’s Assessment: Ensure that your home is being assessed correctly. If there are errors in the property value or assessment, you may be paying more than necessary. You can request a reassessment from your local tax assessor’s office.
  • Appeal Your Property Taxes: If your home’s assessed value is too high, you can appeal the property tax assessment. This process involves presenting evidence that the value of your home is lower than what the tax assessor has recorded.
  • Look for Tax Breaks: Some areas offer tax breaks or exemptions for certain homeowners, such as seniors, veterans, or those with disabilities. Check with your local tax authority to see if you qualify.

7. Rent Out a Portion of Your Home

If your home has extra space, such as an unused basement, attic, or extra bedroom, you can consider renting it out to generate additional income. The money you earn can be put directly toward your mortgage payment, helping you pay it down faster and reduce your monthly costs.

Ways to Rent Out Your Space:

  • Room Rentals: If you have an extra bedroom, renting it out on a short-term basis through platforms like Airbnb can provide a steady stream of income.
  • Long-Term Rentals: If you prefer a more stable income, consider renting out a basement or an accessory dwelling unit (ADU) as a long-term rental.
  • Shared Living: If you don’t mind sharing your space with others, you could rent out a portion of your home to a roommate and split the costs of utilities and rent.

Conclusion

Lowering your mortgage costs as a homeowner requires a combination of strategic planning, regular assessments, and smart decisions. By refinancing your mortgage, making extra payments, reducing insurance costs, eliminating PMI, and adopting energy-efficient practices, you can take significant steps toward lowering your mortgage and creating a more manageable financial situation. Additionally, reevaluating property taxes and renting out unused space in your home can provide added financial relief. With these frugal living hacks, you’ll not only reduce your mortgage burden but also enjoy greater financial freedom and peace of mind.
