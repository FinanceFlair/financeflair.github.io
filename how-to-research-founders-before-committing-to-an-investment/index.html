How to Research Founders Before Committing to an Investment


As an angel investor or venture capitalist, you're constantly on the lookout for the next big idea. However, you know that the right team can make all the difference between a startup's success or failure. Founders with a proven track record, expertise, and vision can propel a company to great heights, while others may drag it under. But how do you research founders before committing to an investment? In this blog post, we'll delve into the essential aspects to consider when evaluating a founding team. Understanding a Founder's Background and Experience One of the first steps in researching a founder is to understand their background and experience. Check their LinkedIn profile, social media, and professional network to gauge their level of engagement and community involvement. Look for a strong track record of achievements, leadership roles, and relevant work experience. Evaluate their networking skills by identifying the number of connections they have, their active participation in industry-related events, and any notable partnerships they've established. Consider the founder's educational background, including their undergraduate and graduate degrees. While a high-profile university or a prestigious institution can be beneficial, it's equally important to assess their practical skills, entrepreneurial spirit, and ability to learn from experiences. Founders from diverse educational backgrounds and industries can bring unique perspectives and insights to the table. Additionally, research the founding team's age and experience. Age can be an advantage, especially if the team has a wealth of knowledge gained from industry experience. Conversely, younger founders with fresh ideas and innovative perspectives can also be extremely valuable. Team Dynamics and Complementary Skills The success of a startup also depends on the complementarity of skills among the founding team. Look for founders with a mix of business acumen, technical expertise, and a deep understanding of the target market. Evaluate the founder's collaborative skills, communication, and interpersonal abilities. Consider the potential for conflict and tension within the team. Collaborative founders with good communication skills can successfully navigate disagreements, while a single strong-willed founder may ultimately lead to team tension and imbalances in decision-making. Assess the founders' ability to build and sustain a cohesive team through interviews and references from previous colleagues and partners. Understanding the Founders' Decision-Making Process and Leadership Style Different founders have unique decision-making processes and leadership styles, which significantly impact the direction of the startup. Evaluating these characteristics can help you gauge the likelihood of success in various scenarios. Watch for leaders who stay adaptable and innovative in the face of uncertainty, displaying a dynamic ability to pivot plans and push the organization forward. While decisiveness is crucial, don't overlook teams that take the time to debate and fully consider options, as these thoughtful founders can minimize risks and spark meaningful growth. Experience-driven leaders can act as a rock for their teams during turbulent times. However, consider whether they exhibit signs of inflexibility or aversion to innovation, which could potentially hinder your investment's potential long-term. Board Members and Advisor Network Researching the advisors and board members surrounding the founding team can be just as crucial as examining the founders themselves. Evaluate their collective expertise in the industry, funding networks, and international connections. Having a robust advisory board can aid the startup by providing valuable insights on critical operational decisions and help navigate times of crisis. Analyze the advisor's independence and willingness to scrutinize founder decisions from a neutral, outside perspective. When evaluating an advisory board or team, also inquire about how frequently founders meet with board members, seeking regular feedback, input, or direction. Meeting the Founders Directly Although online research provides a wealth of information, talking to the founders and assessing their interpersonal and communication skills directly is crucial. Ask questions, like:

What strengths and weaknesses do each of the co-founders bring to the team? How do the founders anticipate challenges as they scale and grow? Can the founders be open and honest when faced with uncertainty?

Pay attention to their confidence in decision-making and vision for growth. Assess whether you believe their personality type and leadership abilities will lead the startup to become an industry shaker. Developing a Shared Vision with Founders As an investor, you must find a healthy balance between influencing founder decisions while giving room for growth. Establishing trust is essential for success. Check if the founders consistently exhibit accountability and awareness of their influence on stakeholders. Understand their response to constructive criticisms and whether they keep stakeholders' best interests in mind when making business decisions. Seeking Recommendations and Historical Performance Research colleagues, managers, subordinates, friends, and industry experts who've worked with, partnered with, or directly hired the founding team. Carefully consider and review the references and testimonials in light of any current, genuine connections, or potential financial incentives they might have. Take note of when and how often members of the founding team have started their own businesses. Do they appear driven by quick gains or more purposeful passion for executing their vision? Finding Your Niche as an Investor The key to impactful investment strategies is usually to identify underrepresented founders in niche markets and nurture them using the knowledge and professional network we've listed above. There are thousands of angel investors supporting businesses in 10,000 possible categories globally. Knowing your niche lets you research more focused and competitive potential investments accurately, making it likely you become more established in your selected niche over time.
