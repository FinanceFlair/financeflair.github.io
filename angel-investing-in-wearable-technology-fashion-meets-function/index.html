Angel Investing in Wearable Technology: Fashion Meets Function


A rapidly growing industry, wearable technology has disrupted the way we interact with our gadgets. As its popularity soars, angel investors have seized the opportunity to invest in the merging of cutting-edge technology and fashion. This fusion creates products that not only praise our aesthetic sensibilities but also cater to our practical needs.

Wearable technology encompasses a scope of diverse products ranging from smartwatches to fitness trackers, clothing with embedded sensors, and advanced biometric authentication devices. Angel investors are drawn to this space due to the profound impact such innovations have on our daily lives.

Smart jewelry and accessories are increasingly becoming popular as they combine the traditional aesthetics of jewelry with functional capabilities. Smart jewelry often incorporates Bluetooth or wireless connectivity, enabling users to indulge in hands-free experiences. Angel investors also recognize the potential of such products in the colossal market for consumer electronics.

One notable example of an investment success in this domain is the smart ring manufacturer, Ringly. By merging fashion and functionality, Ringly’s rings serve as wireless, remotely controlled, smart devices that subtly notify wearers of incoming calls, texts, and emails. Angel investors in Ringly participated in an industry that generated an impressive $24 billion in revenue in 2020.

Smart clothing investment opportunities also have a broad audience and use-caring technology to improve our lives. This category includes breathable garments with embedded sensors that monitor the wearer’s biometrics, like hydration, blood sugar, and heart rate. Such products can benefit individuals with chronic illnesses or enhance the performance of athletes. Let’s merge fashion with health tracking!

Angel investors have recognized this potential and invested in companies like Hexoskin. This smart apparel company specializes in creating garments with embedded sensors that monitor the wearer’s every move. The technology, in turn, offers an accurate analysis of biometric data, proving invaluable for athletes and health-conscious individuals.

Health and wellness is another domain where investor interest in wearable technology is skyrocketing. The recent surge in the popularity of fitness trackers and smart scales is undeniably connected to this burgeoning trend. By tracking biometrics and providing personalized data, wearable technology inspires individuals to lead healthier lives.

Jeronz, a French start-up, has capitalized on this trend by creating a smart fork that promotes mindful eating. By translating the act of eating into data, Jeronz aims to instill awareness about eating habits. For angel investors keen on investments that make a difference, this fusion of health and wearables presents a lucrative opportunity.

Wearable payment systems are another exemplar of technology converging with fashion. Contactless payment solutions, like Samsung Pay or Google Wallet, have become increasingly popular. An opportunity for angel investors arises in the form of wearable payment devices. With the right combination of aesthetic design and reliable technology, these products could become a trendsetter.

For instance, invest in a company like Oura, which has developed a stylish and customizable smart ring that doubles as a payment solution. Such investment aligns with the rising demand for effortless and secure payment methods, securing a part of an expectedly burgeoning market.

In conclusion, angel investing in wearable technology is not just an opportunity to capitalize on a rapidly growing industry, but also an endeavor that shapes the future of human interaction with technology. A fusion of fashion and function, this sector offers a multitude of opportunities in areas including smart jewelry, clothing, health, and wellness, and payment systems. As a discerning investor, seizing these opportunities is crucial to stay ahead in the ever-evolving market.
