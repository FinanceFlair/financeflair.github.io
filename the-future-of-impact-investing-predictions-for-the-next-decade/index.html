The Future of Impact Investing: Predictions for the Next Decade


As we embark on a new decade, the landscape of impact investing is poised for significant transformation. Over the past ten years, impact investing has evolved from a niche concept to a mainstream phenomenon, with a growing number of investors, institutions, and organizations adopting a results-driven approach to their investment strategies. But what changes can we expect to see in the next decade? Mainstreaming Impact Investing The past decade has seen a surge in popularity of impact investing, with increasing numbers of investors seeking to create positive social and environmental outcomes alongside financial returns. This trend is likely to continue in the next decade, driven by the growing awareness of climate change, social inequality, and economic instability. Mainstream institutions, such as pension funds, insurance companies, and development finance institutions, are increasingly incorporating impact investing into their portfolios. Institutional investors will continue to drive growth in impact investing The road to mainstreaming impact investing has been paved by pioneering investors such as the Global Impact Investing Network (GIIN), the World Economic Forum, and the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation. Their efforts have helped to eliminate the perception that impact investing is a niche, luxury investment. Integration of ESG Criteria One of the key steps towards integrating impact investing is the incorporation of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) criteria. In the next decade, we can expect a significant increase in the demand for ESG-focused asset managers, as investors seek companies that are committed to sustainability. Regulatory pressures and stakeholder expectations will drive ESG adoption European and US regulatory guidelines and laws, such as the European Union's (EU) Sustainable Finance Regulation (EU) 2020/696, will amplify demand for ESG-focused information from asset managers and issuers. Listeners to the needs of their customers and citizens are likely to find complementary investment options protecting their interests. Sophistication of Impact Measurement and Management One of the most significant challenges facing impact investing is the lack of focus on results measurement. As the industry continues to mature, we can expect a greater focus on impact measurement and management.
