The Emotional Ties That Bind: The Influence of Family on Your Financial Decisions


It’s no secret that our families deeply influence our lives, shaping our beliefs and behaviors from an early age. But how much does our family impact our financial decisions as we grow older and start to make choices independently? The influence of family on your financial choices can be either positive or negative, affecting your money habits throughout your life.

One of the most powerful connections we have is with our family. Our family members are the ones we turn to when we need support, encouragement, and love. These emotional bonds can influence our behaviors in various aspects of our lives, including our financial decisions. Many people find themselves imitating the behaviors and habits of their family members, sometimes unconsciously.

From our parents to our siblings, grandparents, and other close relatives, the years spent under their roofs and watching them make financial decisions shape our attitudes toward money. We tend to adopt or mimic these behaviors, often unknowingly. This influence might be due to the connections we have with our family members, especially in our childhood years when we rely on them for guidance and support. It is common for some people to find themselves mimicking their family’s financial habits, both positive and negative, throughout their lives.

The attitudes toward money and investment that you grow up with are deeply ingrained within you. Many of these habits become ingrained, and you carry them with you into adulthood, often without realizing how much they’ve influenced your behaviors. Families differ in their approach to wealth, from those who are frugal and prioritizing savings over expenses, to those who are more open-handed and disregard balancing their finances to some extent.

Your family’s financial habits and their influence on your life can directly affect your spending patterns and financial choices. Growing up in a household with tight budgets and frugal spending habits instills a precocious financial awareness in some individuals. They learn the value of money and regularly plan for their expenses.

On the other hand, others might grow up with more affluent financial habits and attitudes. They may be more spontaneous, impulsive, or careless with their income, hence affecting their spending habits later in life. They might prioritize spending on themselves and their desires rather than savings or investments.

Understanding the financial habits, attitudes, and behaviors of your family can help you become more aware of and in control of your financial decisions. It can be valuable to learn from your family’s positive habits and be mindful of harping on their worst financial mistakes.

The Emotional ties that we share with our families can be powerful, greatly influencing our financial behaviors and life choices. Our upbringing and past experiences initiate our adaptive or imitative pattern of financial habits that persist into our adulthood. By understanding these influences, we allow ourselves to be more in control of our financial decisions and independence.

In thinking about our family, remember to be grateful for the aspects they’ve instilled in you, as well as determining which you wish to keep or alter for your financial well-being. A healthy balance between maintaining the good and avoiding the pitfalls of family’s financial habits will build a strong foundation for your financial decisions moving forward.
