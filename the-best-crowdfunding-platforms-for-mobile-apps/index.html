The Best Crowdfunding Platforms for Mobile Apps


In the competitive world of mobile app development, crowdfunding has become an essential avenue for many entrepreneurs looking to bring their ideas to life. Whether you’re an indie developer with a unique concept or a startup looking to scale, the right crowdfunding platform can provide the financial support, visibility, and validation necessary to launch a successful app. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best crowdfunding platforms for mobile apps, offering insights into what makes each one special, the type of projects they support, and tips on how to maximize your chances of success.

Why Crowdfunding is Crucial for Mobile Apps

Crowdfunding allows mobile app developers to tap into the collective support of backers who believe in their vision. It offers a means of raising funds without giving up equity or taking on loans, which can be especially appealing for independent developers and small startups. The beauty of crowdfunding lies in its potential to create a community around your app. Backers not only provide financial support, but they also become ambassadors, helping spread the word and promoting your app to a wider audience.

Additionally, crowdfunding offers market validation before you launch your app, ensuring there is demand for your product. It also allows for greater creative freedom since you’re not beholden to investors or venture capitalists who might push for changes to align with their interests.

With these benefits in mind, let’s dive into the top crowdfunding platforms that are ideal for mobile apps.

1. Kickstarter

Overview

Kickstarter is perhaps the most well-known crowdfunding platform in the world, and for good reason. It’s been the launchpad for countless successful projects, including mobile apps. Kickstarter operates on an all-or-nothing funding model, meaning you only receive funds if you meet your fundraising goal.

Best For:

  • Innovative mobile app ideas that have a strong market appeal.
  • Projects looking to reach a large, engaged global audience.
  • Developers seeking exposure and building a fanbase early on.

Key Features:

  • All-or-nothing funding: You must reach your goal or you get nothing. This adds a sense of urgency and commitment to your campaign.
  • Large community: With millions of backers, Kickstarter offers a vast pool of potential supporters for your mobile app project.
  • Rewards-based system: Developers can offer exclusive rewards or early access to the app, which motivates backers to support your project.

Tips for Success:

  • Create a compelling video that explains your app’s value and unique features.
  • Set a realistic funding goal that reflects the actual costs of developing your mobile app.
  • Engage with backers regularly through updates to maintain excitement and enthusiasm.

2. Indiegogo

Overview

Indiegogo is another popular crowdfunding platform, often considered a more flexible alternative to Kickstarter. While it doesn’t have the same level of recognition, Indiegogo offers significant advantages, especially when it comes to funding flexibility. Unlike Kickstarter, Indiegogo allows both flexible funding (where you can keep the money raised even if you don’t meet your goal) and fixed funding (all-or-nothing).

Best For:

  • Mobile app developers who need more flexibility in their funding approach.
  • Projects that might need more time to reach their goal.
  • Developers who want to keep the funds even if their campaign falls short of its target.

Key Features:

  • Flexible funding option: You can opt to keep the funds raised even if you don’t reach your goal, which reduces the pressure to meet a specific target.
  • Global reach: Indiegogo has a wide international audience, making it ideal for apps targeting users worldwide.
  • InDemand: Once your campaign is successful, you can continue raising funds with the InDemand feature, offering post-campaign funding.

Tips for Success:

  • Offer early-bird pricing for backers to incentivize early support.
  • Create detailed timelines to show potential backers how their contributions will help move the development process forward.
  • Provide regular updates and engage with your backers to keep them invested in your progress.

3. GoFundMe

Overview

GoFundMe is one of the largest and most well-known crowdfunding platforms, although it is more commonly used for personal causes. However, it can also be an effective platform for mobile app developers looking to raise funds for a specific project, especially if you’re looking for a more personal or community-driven approach.

Best For:

  • App developers who have a strong, personal story behind their project.
  • Projects with a philanthropic or socially conscious aspect.
  • Developers who are looking to build grassroots support for their app.

Key Features:

  • No all-or-nothing model: Unlike Kickstarter, you can keep whatever funds are raised, even if you don’t hit your target.
  • Socially-driven campaigns: GoFundMe is known for helping people raise funds for causes they care about, so if your mobile app has a social impact, this could be the platform for you.
  • Broad audience: With millions of users globally, you have access to a large potential donor pool.

Tips for Success:

  • Clearly explain why your app matters, and how it will help or change the lives of those who use it.
  • Engage with your backers on a personal level, providing updates on how the project is progressing.
  • Offer perks or rewards that tie into the app’s social cause.

4. Crowdcube

Overview

For those looking to not only raise funds but also bring on investors, Crowdcube provides an equity-based crowdfunding model. While this platform is generally used for startups across various industries, it’s also a great choice for mobile app developers looking for seed funding in exchange for equity.

Best For:

  • Developers ready to give away equity in exchange for funding.
  • Mobile apps that are in the early stages of development and need significant capital.
  • Projects that are scalable and likely to appeal to investors.

Key Features:

  • Equity crowdfunding: Investors receive a stake in your business, giving them a direct interest in your app’s success.
  • Access to a network of investors: Crowdcube connects you with a large pool of investors who are looking for promising projects to fund.
  • Business growth focus: Ideal for startups that plan to scale rapidly and have the potential to generate significant returns.

Tips for Success:

  • Make sure your mobile app has a clear, scalable business model that will appeal to investors.
  • Prepare a solid pitch with financial projections and detailed market research.
  • Be transparent about how the funds will be used and how backers will benefit from their investment.

5. Appsplit

Overview

Appsplit is a crowdfunding platform specifically tailored to mobile app developers. Unlike other platforms, Appsplit allows you to not only raise funds but also sell partial ownership of your mobile app. This unique structure allows you to find investors or partners who are willing to fund your app in exchange for a share of future profits.

Best For:

  • Developers who want to raise funds while also sharing ownership of their mobile app.
  • Projects looking for long-term partners, not just financial backing.
  • Entrepreneurs who want to find backers willing to invest in the long-term success of their app.

Key Features:

  • Revenue-sharing model: Investors receive a percentage of the app’s revenue based on their contribution.
  • Targeted at app developers: Appsplit is dedicated specifically to mobile apps, making it a great platform for app creators looking for a tailored crowdfunding experience.
  • Ownership transfer: Developers can also offer partial ownership of the app to backers in exchange for funding.

Tips for Success:

  • Be clear about how you’ll divide ownership and how much revenue backers can expect to earn.
  • Offer milestones that show your app’s growth potential and how you plan to make the app profitable.
  • Provide detailed plans on how you will use the funds raised to achieve key development milestones.

6. Seedrs

Overview

Seedrs is another equity-based crowdfunding platform, similar to Crowdcube. It allows startups to raise funds from investors in exchange for equity in their company. Seedrs is known for its strong legal framework and support for both businesses and investors, making it a great option for mobile app developers looking to gain backing from serious investors.

Best For:

  • Mobile app developers who want to raise substantial funds from serious investors.
  • Projects with a clear business plan and growth strategy.
  • Developers ready to give away equity for long-term investment.

Key Features:

  • Equity crowdfunding: Just like Crowdcube, Seedrs lets you raise funds by offering equity in your business.
  • Investor protection: Seedrs offers a range of protections for both investors and companies, making it a trusted platform for equity crowdfunding.
  • Vast investor network: Seedrs has an extensive network of investors actively looking for startups to support.

Tips for Success:

  • Prepare a solid business case and demonstrate why your app is a viable investment opportunity.
  • Provide a clear path to profitability and growth, showing how investor funds will contribute to the app’s success.
  • Engage with investors and offer regular updates to keep them informed and excited about your app.

Conclusion

Crowdfunding can be a game-changer for mobile app developers looking to turn their ideas into reality. The right platform will depend on your specific needs—whether you’re looking for financial backing, validation, or a community of early adopters. By choosing the platform that best aligns with your app’s goals and target audience, you can increase your chances of success and build a loyal user base from day one.

As you embark on your crowdfunding journey, remember to craft a compelling story, offer clear rewards or incentives, and engage with your backers throughout the process. With the right strategy and a great mobile app idea, crowdfunding could be the key to turning your vision into a reality.
