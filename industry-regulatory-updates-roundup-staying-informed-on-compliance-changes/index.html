Industry Regulatory Updates Roundup: Staying Informed on Compliance Changes


Remaining updated with the latest regulatory changes is crucial for organizations to maintain consistent compliance. Here’s a comprehensive roundup to keep you informed on some of the most recent updates that impact various industries.

New Data Privacy Laws: CCPA, LDAR, and PDPA

The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the Louisiana Data Protection Act (LDAR), and the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) have received significant attention in recent months. These laws impose new requirements on businesses, necessitating specific policies for data breaches, personal data protection, and consumer rights.

  1. CCPA: As of January 1, 2020, the CCPA has enabled California residents to request information on collected data and demand its deletion. Businesses should consider appointing a Privacy Officer and maintaining an inventory of personal information collected to ensure transparency.

  2. LDAR: In August 2020, Louisiana enacted the LDAR, mandating certain businesses operating in Louisiana to disclose certain data collection practices, grant consumers the right to access and delete their information, and allow them to opt-out of the selling of their information.

  3. PDPA: Thailand’s PDPA was enacted in 2019 and has a staggered enforcement starting from May 2021. This law comprises eight chapters, including personal data controller and processor, the right of data subjects to access, rectify, and object to the processing, among others.

Environmental Updates: TSCA & ECHA Restriction

Several countries have amended their laws to regulate the use and disposal of hazardous substances. For instance:

  1. TSCA: The U.S. Toxic Substances Control Act (TSCA) has undergone substantial reform, requiring businesses to report new chemicals and chemicals of concern. Compliance meticulousness, chemical inventories, and safety data sheets must be scruulously reviewed to abide by these new rules.

  2. ECHA Restriction: The European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) imposes restrictions on the use and placing on the market of certain dangerous substances or mixtures. Evaluation of chemical product information and conducting market research to detect the presence of restricted substances is crucial for businesses operating within the EU.

Hospitality Industry Updates: OBS, ADA, and DOT Compliance

Businesses in the hospitality sector should be aware of the following:

  1. OBS: The Ontario Building Code’s 2019 updates ban the installation of fireplaces and wood-burning stoves in new buildings, citing air quality concerns. This update obligates hoteliers, rental property owners, and other accommodation service providers to be aware of the new policies.

  2. ADA: The Department of Justice (DOJ) has updated the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) accessibility guidelines to ensure accommodations for individuals with disabilities. It is essential for hotels, restaurants, and recreational facilities to implement physical and digital accessibility updates.

  3. DOT Compliance: The United States Department of Transportation (DOT) regulates the shipping of hazardous materials. Cargo on passenger flights, ground transportation, and maritime delivery must adhere to these guidelines, necessitating regular audits of shipping procedures.

Pharmaceutical and Medical Device Updates: Drug Distribution, REMS, and MDR

  1. Drug Distribution: Several countries, including the U.S. and the EU, have witnessed changes in how drugs can be distributed, authorized, and tracked.

  2. REMS: Implementing Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategies (REMS) for select drugs is a new responsibility, contributing to drug safety and minimizing potential harms.

  3. MDR: The Medical Device Regulation (MDR) for the European Union goes into effect on May 26, 2021. This complex regulation requires medical device manufacturers to maintain a Technical File, Design Documentation, and a CE Mark Certificate, ensuring compliance with most novel standards.

Conclusion

Staying compliant with ever-evolving regulatory updates is a continuous endeavor for businesses in any industry. Regularly updated resources and training for employees, including Legal and Compliance departments, are essential in maintaining compliance in these rapidly-changing regulatory landscapes.

By keeping informed with these latest updates, not only will organizations navigate these changes smoothly but also demonstrate commitment to corporate responsibility and sustainability. Foregoing preparedness equates to potential non-compliance, undesirable audits, hefty fines, and harm to the organization’s reputation. So, staying informed in the ever-changing regulatory landscape is paramount.
