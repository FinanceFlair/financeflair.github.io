Top 10 Angel Investor Networks You Should Join


Are you a passionate entrepreneur or an angel investor looking to join a community of like-minded individuals? Look no further! Angel investor networks have revolutionized the way entrepreneurs find funding and connect with potential investors. Here are the top 10 angel investor networks that you should consider joining:

  1. Angel Capital Association (ACA) With over 300 angel groups across the globe, the ACA is the largest and most active network of angel investors. Members have invested in excess of $23 billion in startups, making it a go-to platform for entrepreneurs seeking funding. As a part of the ACA, you'll gain access to networking events, educational resources, and market research, ensuring you're well-informed and prepared to take your business to the next level.
  2. AngelList AngelList is the world's busiest platform for startups and investors. With over 1.5 million users, including 10,000 investors, it's the perfect place to find and be found by potential cofounders, partners, and investors. The platform offers a unique feature, "Investor Publicly Viewable Deal Flow," where you can see which deals are being discussed and which investors are participating.
  3. Tech Coast Angels As the largest and most active angel investor network in the United States, the Tech Coast Angels connect investors to top-notch startups. With over $650 million in investments, this network boasts an impressive portfolio, showcasing the value of investing in impactful, scalable ideas. With chapters located throughout Southern California, it's an excellent choice for those looking to tap into a strong entrepreneurial community.
  4. National Angel Capital Organization (NACO) The NACO aims to accelerate the growth of the global angel investor community, promoting cooperation and knowledge sharing between angel groups. This Canadian organization currently counts over 20 network member groups from across North America, providing a unique opportunity to connect with the most prominent angel investors and stay updated on industry trends.
  5. Network Opportunity for Women Investors (NOW) Networking opportunities specifically tailored for women are fewer and far between, making NOW a valuable resource. By building relationships with like-minded female investors, this network provides access to strategic partnerships and growth prospects that would be unavailable otherwise.
  6. Startup vous Located in Toronto, Canada, the Startup vous community fosters networking and educational events. Their unique approach combines startup growth, legal and tax information, and mentorship, creating an atmosphere where entrepreneurs and investors thrive.
  7. SlideShare SlideShare is an innovative platform that unites investors, entrepreneurs, and business professionals in a must-know environment. Offering e-learning courses, multidisciplinary topics, and expert engagement opportunities, SlideShare catalyzes meaningful connections between colleagues and fosters forward-thinking orientations.
