Top 5 Ethical Investment Funds to Consider


As the world becomes increasingly aware of the importance of sustainability and social responsibility, the demand for ethical investments is on the rise. However, with the numerous options available, it can be daunting to determine which funds align with your values and investment goals. In this post, we will explore the top 5 ethical investment funds to consider, examining their unique qualities, investment strategies, and track records.

  1. Vanguard FTSE 100 ESG Screened Index Fund (VFGEN) Launched in 2017, the Vanguard FTSE 100 ESG Screened Index Fund is a UK-based fund that focuses on investing in companies with a strong environmental, social, and governance (ESG) track record. The fund screens out companies thatFail to meet specific ESG criteria, such as those involved in tobacco or defence production. The fund's diversified portfolio ensures a low-risk investment, with an upfront charge of 0.22% per annum. This makes it an attractive option for those seeking to incorporate ESG principles into their investment portfolio.

Fund size: £134.48 million (as of January 2023) Net asset value (NAV): £8.14 per unit (as of January 2023) Annual management charge: 0.22% Investment strategy: FTSE 100 ESG screened index

  1. Baillie Gifford Sustainable Growth Fund (BSGFX) The Baillie Gifford Sustainable Growth Fund is a diversified equity fund that prioritizes companies with a strong commitment to sustainability. The fund's investment strategy focuses on growth-investing principles, with a minimum 70% weightage in the UK and a thematic approach to cover sectors like technology, healthcare, and financials. With a total fund size of £430.77 million, the fund offers a cost-effective entry point for those seeking a responsible investment vehicle.

Fund size: £430.77 million (as of January 2023) NAV: £11.61 per unit (as of January 2023) Annual management charge: 0.75% Investment strategy: Growth-oriented, ESG screened equity investing

  1. Fidelity ESG Enhanced UK Index Fund (FSECX) Fidelity's ESG Enhanced UK Index Fund offers investors the opportunity to invest in the UK stock market while prioritizing companies with a strong ESG profile. With a total fund size of £41.53 million and an annual management charge of 0.20%, the fund ensures low-risk investing and aligns with the ESG investment approach. Fidelity's shareholder engagement and voting policies complement the fund's ESG strategies, creating a robust asset management proposition for responsible investors.

Fund size: £41.53 million (as of January 2023) NAV: £8.24 per unit (as of January 2023) Annual management charge: 0.20% Investment strategy: ESG-screened, passive UK equity investing

  1. iShares FTSE Developed World ESG UCITS ETF (IFDE) The iShares FTSE Developed World ESG UCITS ETF presents a convenient option for passive, cost-effective ESG investing. By tracking the FTSE Developed World ESG Index, the ETF offers investors exposure to developed markets with minimal environmental, social, and governance impact. With over £2 billion in assets under management, this product represents one of the largest ESG-themed ETFs on the market.

Assets under management: £2,152.68 million (as of January 2023) NAV: £59.58 per unit (as of January 2023) Annual management charge: 0.17% Investment strategy: ESG-screened, passive developed world equity investing

  1. Invesco Sustainable Future Equity (UK) Fund (SGSI) The Invesco Sustainable Future Equity (UK) Fund takes a thematic approach to investment management, focusing on companies with a strong commitment to sustainable growth. Utilizing the services of responsible investment manager Hermes, the fund oversees a investment strategy that prioritizes areas like healthcare, environmental, and industrial proteins. This diversified portfolio ensures that investors benefit from sustainable long-term profitability.

Fund size: £256.15 million (as of January 2023) NAV: £10.32 per unit (as of January 2023) Annual management charge: 0.50% Investment strategy: Thematic, ESG-oriented equity investing

The Rise of Ethical Investing In conclusion, ethical investment funds are rapidly growing in demand as consumers increasingly prioritize their environmental, social, and governance footprint. By leveraging the following deals – Vanguard FTSE 100 ESG Screened Index Fund, Baillie Gifford Sustainable Growth Fund, Fidelity ESG Enhanced UK Index Fund, iShares FTSE Developed World ESG UCITS ETF, and Invesco Sustainable Future Equity (UK) Fund investors can make conscious investment decisions and contribute positively to the ESG sector. It is an attractive time to create a sustainable investment portfolio, allowing for a climate-friendly inheritance.

The details stated in this section are intended to be informative. You should consult with a financial professional and consider your individual circumstances before making any investment decisions.
