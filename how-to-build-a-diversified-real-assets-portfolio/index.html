How to Build a Diversified Real Assets Portfolio


Investing in real assets can be an excellent way to diversify your investment portfolio and provide a hedge against inflation. Real assets, such as real estate, infrastructure, natural resources, and commodities, have historically demonstrated the potential to offer attractive risk-adjusted returns over the long term. In this blog post, we will explore the key steps to building a diversified real assets portfolio that can help you achieve your investment goals.

Understanding Real Assets

Before diving into the process of building a diversified real assets portfolio, it’s important to understand what real assets are and their role in an investment strategy. Real assets are tangible assets that have intrinsic value, such as real estate properties, energy infrastructure, agricultural land, and precious metals. These assets have the potential to generate income and appreciate in value over time.

Real assets also have the benefit of being less correlated with traditional financial assets like stocks and bonds, making them a valuable addition to a well-rounded investment portfolio. Additionally, real assets can provide protection against inflation, as their value tends to rise with the cost of living.

Steps to Building a Diversified Real Assets Portfolio

1. Define Your Investment Objectives

The first step in building a diversified real assets portfolio is to define your investment objectives. Consider your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Are you looking for income generation, capital appreciation, or a combination of both? Understanding your investment objectives will guide the selection of real assets that align with your goals.

2. Asset Allocation

Once you’ve defined your investment objectives, the next step is to determine the appropriate allocation of real assets within your overall investment portfolio. Consider the various categories of real assets, such as real estate, infrastructure, natural resources, and commodities. Each category may offer different risk-return profiles and market dynamics, so it’s important to diversify across these subsectors to spread risk and enhance potential returns.

3. Conduct Thorough Research

Thorough research is critical when selecting specific real assets for your portfolio. Consider factors such as location, supply and demand dynamics, regulatory environment, and the potential for income generation. For example, when investing in real estate, factors like location, property type, and rental demand should be carefully evaluated. Similarly, when considering natural resources or commodities, factors like geopolitical risks and global demand-supply dynamics should be taken into account.

4. Risk Management

Risk management is an integral part of building a diversified real assets portfolio. It involves assessing and mitigating risks associated with each real asset category. For instance, real estate investments may be subject to property-specific risks, while commodities may be influenced by geopolitical events and supply disruptions. Implementing risk management strategies, such as diversification, hedging, and asset-specific due diligence, can help protect your portfolio from unexpected market movements.

5. Monitor and Rebalance

Once your diversified real assets portfolio is established, it’s essential to monitor its performance regularly and rebalance as necessary. Market conditions and economic factors can impact the valuation of real assets, so staying informed and making adjustments to your portfolio allocation can help optimize returns and manage risk effectively.

Conclusion

Building a diversified real assets portfolio requires careful planning, thorough research, and a disciplined approach to risk management. By incorporating real assets into your investment strategy, you can enhance portfolio diversification, potentially improve risk-adjusted returns, and benefit from the unique characteristics of tangible assets. Whether you’re a seasoned investor or just starting, incorporating real assets into your portfolio can be a valuable step towards achieving your long-term financial objectives.

Investing in real assets is a complex and ever-evolving process, and seeking professional advice from financial advisors and investment experts is recommended to tailor your real assets portfolio to your specific financial situation and goals.

Remember, a well-constructed real assets portfolio can provide stability, income, and growth potential, offering a compelling investment opportunity for those looking to build a diversified and resilient investment portfolio.
