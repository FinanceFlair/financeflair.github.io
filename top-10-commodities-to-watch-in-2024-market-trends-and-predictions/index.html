Top 10 Commodities to Watch in 2024: Market Trends and Predictions


As we step into 2024, the global commodity market is expected to be shaped by a complex interplay of factors, including economic trends, geopolitical events, and technological advancements. In this comprehensive guide, we will analyze the top 10 commodities to watch in 2024, highlighting the key market trends and predictions that will impact their prices.

  1. Oil Oil remains a crucial commodity in the global energy market, and its price will be influenced by various factors, including OPEC production levels, geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, and the growth of renewable energy sources. In 2024, oil prices are likely to range between $60-80 per barrel, with potential volatility due to supply disruptions and changes in global demand. Demand for oil is expected to increase as the global economy recovers from the pandemic, driven by the growth of emerging markets and the expansion of transportation services. However, the rising adoption of electric vehicles and renewable energy sources may lead to a decline in oil demand, potentially impacting prices.
  2. Gold Gold is a popular store of value and a hedge against inflation, and its price is closely tied to interest rates and economic growth. In 2024, gold prices are expected to range between $1,500-1,800 per ounce, influenced by the direction of interest rates and the overall economic outlook. Investors are increasingly turning to gold as a safe-haven asset, driven by concerns over global economic uncertainty and rising inflation. Central banks have also been increasing their gold reserves, which may support prices.
  3. Copper Copper is a crucial metal in the electronics and construction industries, and its price is expected to rise in 2024 due to increasing demand from these sectors. The global copper market is expected to face a supply deficit in 2024, driven by declining copper ore grades and rising production costs. The growth of renewable energy sources and electric vehicles will also drive up copper demand, particularly for sustainable electricity transmission lines and EV charging infrastructure. This trend is expected to continue in 2024, leading to higher copper prices.
  4. Wheat Wheat is a vital food commodity, and its price will be influenced by factors such as global supply and demand, weather patterns, and trade policies. In 2024, wheat prices are expected to range between $5-7 per bushel, driven by the recovery of global food demand and the growth of emerging markets. The Russian-Ukrainian conflict and droughts in key producing countries have contributed to wheat shortages in recent years. As a result, wheat prices may remain volatile in 2024, with potential spikes due to supply disruptions.
  5. Coal Coal is a critical component of the global energy mix, and its price is likely to be influenced by factors such as government policies, technological advancements, and global demand trends. In 2024, coal prices are expected to fluctuate between $50-70 per ton, driven by the growth of renewable energy sources and strict environmental regulations. Investors should note that the coal market is expected to experience significant shifts in 2024, with potential shifts in global demand and supply trends. Additionally, the implementation of stricter regulations and taxes may negatively impact coal prices.
  6. Lumber Lumber is a highly traded commodity, influenced by factors such as global demand, supply chain disruptions, and forestry industry trends. In 2024, lumber prices are expected to range between $500-700 per thousand board feet, driven by the growth of the construction industry and rising demand for wooden consumer goods. Investors should be aware that lumber prices can be highly volatile due to unexpected weather events, tariff changes, and changes in global trade policies. As a result, investors should closely monitor lumber market trends and adjust their portfolios accordingly.
  7. Iron Ore Iron ore is a critical component of the global steel industry, and its price is closely tied to global demand and supply trends. In 2024, iron ore prices are expected to range between $60-80 per ton, driven by the growth of the steel industry and the recovery of global economic activity. Investors should note that iron ore prices can be influenced by factors such as supply disruptions in major producing countries, steel industry trends, and global trade policies. As a result, investors should closely monitor iron ore market trends and adjust their portfolios accordingly.
  8. Corn Corn is a vital food commodity, and its price will be influenced by factors such as global supply and demand, weather patterns, and trade policies. In 2024, corn prices are expected to range between $3-5 per bushel, driven by the growth of global demand and the development of new technologies. Investors should be aware that corn prices can be highly volatile due to unexpected weather events, changes in government policies, and shifts in global trade flows. As a result, investors should closely monitor corn market trends and adjust their portfolios accordingly.
  9. Lead Lead is a highly traded metal, influenced by factors such as global demand, supply chain disruptions, and technological advancements. In 2024, lead prices are expected to range between $0.90-1.20 per pound, driven by the growth of the electronics industry and the recovery of global economic activity. Investors should be aware that lead prices can be influenced by factors such as supply disruptions in major producing countries, technological changes, and global trade policies. As a result, investors should closely monitor lead market trends and adjust their portfolios accordingly.
  10. Nickel Nickel is a crucial metal in the automotive and electronics industries, and its price is expected to rise in 2024 due to increasing demand from these sectors. The global nickel market is expected to face a supply deficit in 2024, driven by declining nickel ore grades and rising production costs. Investors should note that nickel prices can be influenced by factors such as supply disruptions in major producing countries, changes in global demand, and technological advancements. As a result, investors should closely monitor nickel market trends and adjust their portfolios accordingly.
