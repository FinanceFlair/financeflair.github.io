Crowdfunding for Social Enterprises: Which Platform Is Right for You?


In today’s rapidly changing business landscape, social enterprises are gaining traction as a force for positive change. Social enterprises combine the pursuit of profit with the goal of creating a positive social or environmental impact. These ventures are inherently mission-driven, and while they focus on sustainability and growth, they also strive to address pressing global issues such as poverty, inequality, climate change, and access to education or healthcare.

One of the primary challenges faced by social enterprises is securing adequate funding. Traditional funding sources, such as grants, loans, and investments, may not always align with the mission or values of these businesses. This is where crowdfunding comes into play. Crowdfunding allows social enterprises to raise money from a large number of people, typically through an online platform, without the need for traditional investors or financial institutions. The ability to tap into a global network of backers who share the same passion for social change can be a game-changer for many social entrepreneurs.

However, with so many crowdfunding platforms available, selecting the right one can be a daunting task. Each platform offers different features, target audiences, and funding models, making it crucial to evaluate your specific needs before making a decision. In this post, we will explore various crowdfunding platforms and help you determine which one is best suited for your social enterprise.

Types of Crowdfunding Models for Social Enterprises

Before diving into the specific platforms, it’s essential to understand the different types of crowdfunding models. The crowdfunding model you choose will significantly impact how your social enterprise raises funds and engages with supporters. Generally, crowdfunding platforms fall into one of the following categories:

1. Donation-Based Crowdfunding

Donation-based crowdfunding is the most straightforward model. In this model, individuals donate money to a cause without expecting any financial return. For social enterprises, this is ideal if you are raising funds for a charitable project or a specific social initiative.

2. Reward-Based Crowdfunding

Reward-based crowdfunding allows backers to receive non-financial rewards in exchange for their contributions. These rewards could be anything from branded merchandise to exclusive access to events or services. Social enterprises often use this model when they want to provide a tangible connection to the backers, encouraging further engagement with their mission.

3. Equity-Based Crowdfunding

In equity-based crowdfunding, backers contribute funds in exchange for equity or shares in the business. This model is particularly beneficial for social enterprises that have a more established business model and seek to scale. However, it’s important to note that equity-based crowdfunding comes with legal complexities and obligations to investors.

4. Debt-Based Crowdfunding

Also known as peer-to-peer lending, this model allows social enterprises to borrow money from individuals or institutions and pay it back with interest. It can be a great option for enterprises that need capital but prefer not to give away equity in their business.

Each of these crowdfunding models has its own set of advantages and challenges. The key is to align the crowdfunding model with the goals of your social enterprise, ensuring that it resonates with your target audience.

Evaluating Crowdfunding Platforms for Social Enterprises

With the different crowdfunding models in mind, let’s now dive into the best crowdfunding platforms available for social enterprises. Depending on your business needs, target audience, and project goals, each platform offers a unique set of features and benefits.

1. Kickstarter

Kickstarter is one of the most well-known crowdfunding platforms in the world. It’s primarily a reward-based platform, but it has gained popularity for its wide-reaching audience and its ability to support creative and social impact projects. The platform is open to various industries, including art, design, technology, and social good.

Best For:

  • Social enterprises with a creative or product-based mission
  • Projects that can offer tangible rewards to backers
  • Ventures with clear, time-bound goals

Kickstarter requires campaigns to meet their funding target in order to receive any money (all-or-nothing funding model). This makes it particularly well-suited for enterprises with a clear financial goal and a strong marketing strategy. However, keep in mind that Kickstarter is highly competitive, and campaigns that are not well-promoted may struggle to stand out.

2. Indiegogo

Indiegogo is another major crowdfunding platform that offers both reward-based and equity-based crowdfunding options. The platform has a more flexible funding model compared to Kickstarter, offering both all-or-nothing and keep-it-all options. Indiegogo is an excellent choice for social enterprises that may not be able to guarantee success in meeting their funding goal but still need to raise capital.

Best For:

  • Social enterprises looking for flexible funding options
  • Projects with a broad range of backers, from creative to environmental causes
  • Ventures that can offer rewards or products

Indiegogo also allows for more niche campaigns, meaning social enterprises with a very specific mission or target audience may find it easier to attract backers. Additionally, Indiegogo’s focus on innovation and social causes can be an advantage for ventures in need of early-stage funding.

3. GoFundMe

GoFundMe is one of the largest donation-based crowdfunding platforms, making it an ideal choice for social enterprises focused on charitable causes or social impact projects. Unlike other platforms, GoFundMe does not take a commission on donations, which means that 100% of the funds raised go directly to the campaign.

Best For:

  • Social enterprises that are focused on a charitable cause or nonprofit mission
  • Projects that require direct community support
  • Ventures with a compelling emotional story

GoFundMe is known for its easy-to-use interface and the ability to rapidly mobilize a network of donors. It’s a popular platform for individual fundraisers, but social enterprises can also leverage it to gain the support of a community invested in their mission. However, because it is primarily donation-based, backers on GoFundMe do not receive rewards or equity.

4. Seedrs

For social enterprises looking to raise larger amounts of capital and willing to offer equity in exchange for investment, Seedrs is a popular equity crowdfunding platform. It is one of the most established platforms in Europe and has been used by many social enterprises to attract investors who believe in their long-term impact.

Best For:

  • Social enterprises that need large-scale funding
  • Ventures looking to offer equity in exchange for investment
  • Enterprises that have a proven business model and long-term goals

Equity crowdfunding platforms like Seedrs are ideal for businesses that are ready to scale and can provide investors with a potential financial return. However, it requires a robust business plan and a clear path to profitability. Additionally, equity crowdfunding comes with regulatory requirements, so it’s important to understand the legal obligations before launching a campaign.

5. Patreon

Patreon is a membership-based crowdfunding platform that allows social enterprises to create a community of supporters who contribute on a regular basis. Backers pledge a set amount each month in exchange for exclusive content or early access to products or services.

Best For:

  • Social enterprises that have an ongoing relationship with their supporters
  • Projects that can offer ongoing, exclusive content or updates
  • Businesses looking to build a loyal and engaged community

Patreon works well for social enterprises with a strong following or those that produce regular content, such as podcasts, videos, or educational materials. It’s a great way to ensure sustainable funding and create a consistent revenue stream.

6. Crowdfunder UK

Crowdfunder UK is one of the largest crowdfunding platforms in the United Kingdom and specializes in both donation-based and reward-based crowdfunding. The platform is known for supporting social enterprises, community projects, and charities, with a focus on initiatives that have a positive impact on society.

Best For:

  • Social enterprises in the UK or with a UK-based audience
  • Community-driven projects
  • Charitable or nonprofit initiatives

Crowdfunder UK offers a range of tools to help social enterprises plan, launch, and promote their campaigns. Additionally, it has established partnerships with various local councils and organizations to help backers find campaigns that align with their values.

7. LendingClub

LendingClub is a peer-to-peer lending platform that allows social enterprises to raise funds through loans. This debt-based crowdfunding model can be beneficial for businesses that need capital but prefer not to give away equity.

Best For:

  • Social enterprises that prefer borrowing over giving away equity
  • Ventures that are looking to secure a loan at competitive rates
  • Enterprises with a clear repayment plan

While LendingClub may not be ideal for every social enterprise, it’s a strong choice for businesses that need loans with manageable repayment terms and do not want to dilute their ownership by offering equity.

Key Considerations When Choosing a Crowdfunding Platform

When selecting a crowdfunding platform for your social enterprise, there are several factors to keep in mind. These include:

  1. Target Audience – Understanding who your ideal backers are is crucial. Some platforms, like GoFundMe, attract individuals who are focused on charitable causes, while others, like Kickstarter, may appeal more to those interested in creative or innovative projects.

  2. Funding Model – Consider whether you want to offer rewards, equity, or simply raise donations. Each model has its own set of benefits and limitations, so choosing one that aligns with your business model and goals is essential.

  3. Fees and Costs – Each platform charges different fees, which can vary based on the amount of money raised, the type of crowdfunding, and the country of operation. Be sure to carefully review the platform’s fee structure to understand how much of your raised funds will go toward the platform and other expenses.

  4. Support and Resources – Some crowdfunding platforms provide extensive marketing support, resources, and community engagement tools to help you reach your funding goal. Look for platforms that offer guidance and support, especially if you are new to crowdfunding.

  5. Platform Reputation – Research the platform’s track record and user reviews. A reputable platform will have successful campaigns and a history of helping social enterprises reach their funding targets.

Conclusion

Crowdfunding can be a transformative tool for social enterprises, providing the necessary capital to bring innovative projects to life while also building a community of passionate supporters. The key to success lies in selecting the right platform that aligns with your mission, funding needs, and the type of audience you wish to engage.

By carefully evaluating the various crowdfunding options available and understanding the strengths and weaknesses of each, you can increase your chances of running a successful campaign and making a positive impact on the world. Whether you’re seeking donations, rewards, or investment, there is a platform that suits your needs—so take the time to choose wisely and start building the future you envision.
