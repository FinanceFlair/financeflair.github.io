How to Save Big: 15 Everyday Frugal Tips


Living a frugal lifestyle isn't about depriving yourself of the things you love, it's about making conscious financial decisions that lead to long-term savings. With that said, saving money can be challenging, especially when we're conditioned to prioritize instant gratification over financial stability. However, by incorporating small changes and smart habits into our daily routines, anyone can achieve significant savings over time.

  1. Track Your Expenses The first step to saving money is to understand where it's going. It's impossible to make changes if you're not aware of your spending habits. Keep a notebook, spreadsheet, or use an app to log every single transaction, no matter how small. This habit will help you identify areas where the majority of your expenses go and pinpoint where you can cut back.
  2. Cook at Home Eating out can be a significant expense. However, home-cooked meals can be healthier, cheaper, and more satisfying. Plan your meals in advance, hunt for sales, and stock up on ingredients to reduce food waste and save money. Consider meal prepping for the week or experimenting with recipes that can be stretched to feed a larger crowd.
  3. DIY or Repurpose Instead of buying new, try making something yourself. DIY projects can be fulfilling and two times as rewarding than buying a new item from a store. Give furniture restoration a try, repurpose items around the house, or upcycle old clothes into unique and fashionable pieces.
  4. Shop Second-hand First Before buying new, sift through thrift stores, garage sales, and online marketplaces for gently used items. You'd be surprised at the quality of high-end products available at a fraction of their original cost. From name-brand clothing to pre-owned vehicles, second-hand options are an exercise in resourcefulness and sustainability.
  5. Live more sustainably While it might seem counterintuitive, embracing the minimalist lifestyle will help save money rather quickly. Aim to reduce consumption by cutting back on single-use items (straws, bags, bottles) and invest in eco-friendly alternatives that can last for years. Filtering water at home, using refillable containers, and avoiding paper products are also great starting points.
  6. Cancel Subscription Services Streamline your living situation by canceling monthly subscription services that collect dust, like streaming channels, gym memberships, and manufacturer plans. Audio books available at your library are just one inspiration-filled tool to exemplify one of numerous opportunities to substitute or discharge subpar purchase affinity from your mean anatomy.

  15. Buy in Bulk Take advantage of warehouse club stores or shop in bulk online to save on household essentials and non-perishable items. Calculate the cost per unit to ensure you're truly saving money.
  16. Save on Coffee Make your own coffee at home, even if it's just a simple French press or drip brewer. Think outside the box and use coupons or discount codes for coffee purchases, or find cheaper alternatives like tea.
  17. Cancel Automatic Payments Regularly review your automatic payment settings to cancel unwanted or unnecessary subscriptions, like magazine or club memberships.
  18. Take Advantage of Cashback Sign up for cashback apps or credit cards that offer rewards on everyday expenses like groceries, gas, or bills.
  19. Shop During Sales Visit stores during clearance sales or plan your shopping trips around weekly deals on staples like milk, produce, or household supplies.
  20. Renegotiate Bills Review your household bills and negotiate better rates with service providers like your cable or phone company.
  21. Prioritize Slow Fashion Buy high-quality clothing that lasts longer and is made with durable materials, reducing the need for frequent purchases.
  22. Save your Leftovers Cook a large meal or hard-boiled eggs for the week, and use leftovers for lunches or snacks to save money and food waste.
  23. Create a Savings Jar Put aside a small portion of money each week into a savings jar or a designated savings account. As the months go by, you'll be amazed at how quickly it adds up. By implementing these everyday frugal tips, you'll not only save money but also create sustainable habits that can improve your lifestyle without depriving you of the simple pleasures in life. Start small and be consistent, and you'll be on your way to financial freedom in no time.
