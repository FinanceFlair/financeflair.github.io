Invest In What You Know: Local Opportunities for Ethical Investment


When it comes to investing, most people focus on stocks, bonds, and other traditional assets. However, there is a growing trend towards investing in what you know - local opportunities for ethical investment. This approach involves putting your money into businesses, projects, or ventures that align with your values and contribute positively to your community.

Why Invest Locally?

There are several reasons why investing locally makes sense:

  1. Familiarity: You already know the area and its businesses, so there’s less uncertainty involved. You can stay informed about what’s happening in your community and build relationships with local entrepreneurs.

  2. Supporting local businesses: Local businesses have a bigger impact on the local economy than large corporations. They hire more workers, spend more money in the community, and are more likely to give back to local charities.

  3. Reduction in carbon footprint: Local investments have a smaller carbon footprint than investing in companies that operate on a global scale. By investing locally, you can reduce your environmental impact.

  4. Diversification: Investing in a mix of local and traditional assets can help diversify your portfolio and reduce risk.

Types of Local Investment Opportunities

There are many different types of local investment opportunities to consider:

Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs)

CDFIs are financial institutions that provide financing for affordable housing, economic development, and other community projects. They are a great way for individuals to invest in their community and see the tangible results of their investment.

Local Crowdfunding Platforms

Crowdfunding has become a popular way to finance new and innovative projects. Local crowdfunding platforms can help you invest in projects that support the local economy and meet your values.

Green Energy Projects

Investing in renewable energy projects, such as community solar or wind farms, can help reduce your carbon footprint and support the transition to a more sustainable future.

Local Business Loans

Local business loans can be a great way to invest in entrepreneurs who have a vision for improving their community. These loans can be repaid over time with interest, providing a return on your investment while supporting the growth of local businesses.

###Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)

REITs are companies that own and manage income-producing real estate properties. Investing in a REIT that focuses on sustainable or community-driven projects can provide a stable return on your investment while supporting your values.

How to Get Started

Investing locally may seem daunting, but it doesn’t have to be. Here are some steps to get started:

  1. Do your research: Spend time researching local businesses, organizations, and projects in your community. Look for those that align with your values and have a positive impact on the local economy.

  2. Talk to experts: Reach out to local financial advisers, CDFIs, and other experts to learn about the opportunities available in your community.

  3. Find a local investment platform: Online investment platforms can connect you with local investment opportunities, making it easy to diversify your portfolio and support your community.

  4. Start small: Investing locally means you don’t have to put all your money into one opportunity. Start by investing a smaller amount and see how it performs before putting more money into the investment.

Ultimately, investing in what you know - local opportunities for ethical investment - can be a rewarding and fulfilling experience. By supporting local businesses, projects, and entrepreneurs, you can make a positive difference in your community while earning a return on your investment.
