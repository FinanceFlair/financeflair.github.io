Angel Investing in Insurtech: Innovations to Watch


Insurtech, the intersection of insurance and technology, has been gaining momentum in recent years as it challenges traditional insurance business models. This development opens up exciting investment opportunities for angel investors who are looking to diversify their portfolios and keep abreast with emerging innovations. But, what are the insurtech trends set to make waves in the insurance industry? Let’s take a look.

1. Peer-to-Peer Insurance

Peer-to-Peer (P2P) insurance leverages the power of community to share risks and redistribute savings. Instead of the broad-based approach of traditional insurance companies, P2P insurance is more customer-centric and is tailored to individual needs. This approach has been found to create a more competitive market and even encourage underinsurance.

One can invest in platforms like Leapfrog, Friendsurance or Bunker, companies that leverage P2P insurance technology to offer a range of insurance products. These platforms help drive down costs and increase access to insurance for underserved markets.

2. Predictive Analytics and AI

Machine learning has led to a wealth of valuable insights within the insurance industry, particularly through predictive analytics. With the rise of big data and its effective use, insurers can now offer personalized, real-time insurance solutions.

For angel investors, companies like InTalk, which is pioneering voice-based analytics, and Hive-eyed, with its geospatial big data skills, offer an opportunity to take advantage of the digitally-driven insurance future.

3. Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT)

DLT offers a more secure, faster and cheaper way to manage insurance claim processes. It can aid transparency by providing an immutable record of all transactions. The implementation of DLT in the insurance industry creates a whole new world of opportunities for investors. Companies such as Trovata and Employers Re offer excellent investment prospects in this space.

4. Health and Wellness Platforms

Health and wellness platforms are gaining exuberance and are set to revolutionize insurance offerings. By investing in these platforms, insurance companies are seen moving away from the traditional sickness-focused model to a preventative approach to health. 5. Digital Currencies and Blockchain-driven Insurance

Digital currencies and the blockchain technology have the potential to utterly disrupt the insurance industry. Companies like Token excitingly delve into novel ways of issuing policies on a blockchain. Policyholders have more control owing to the transparency offered by the blockchain technology.

Conclusion

The future of insurance is here. The insurtech space offers a plethora of opportunities for angel investors willing to bet on innovation. The ability to create personalized products, increased efficiency, improved transparency, and better customer experiences are all potential returns on investment. To remain competitive in this era, traditional insurance giants must integrate insurtech solutions, ensuring that insurtech becomes an integral part of their business models. Now is the time to keep an eye out for these insurtech trends.
