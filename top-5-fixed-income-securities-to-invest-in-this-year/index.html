Top 5 Fixed Income Securities to Invest in This Year


If recent market gyrations have you yearning for a little more predictability, you’re not alone. Investors around the globe are revisiting the benefits of fixed income securities, seeking steady interest payments and a buffer against the shocks that come with volatile equities. The landscape has evolved, with shifting central bank policies, inflation concerns, and a world of products offering different yields and risk profiles. Here’s a comprehensive dive into the top five fixed income securities that merit an investor’s attention right now.

1. Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS)

In an era where inflation has proven stubborn and unpredictable, Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS) enjoy renewed relevance. These U.S. government bonds adjust their principal value based on changes in the Consumer Price Index, ensuring that both the principal and the semiannual interest payments keep pace with inflation.

Key Features

  • Inflation Protection: TIPS directly tackle the eroding effects of inflation, making them especially attractive when prices for goods and services are climbing.
  • Government Backed: Like traditional Treasuries, TIPS are backed by the full faith and credit of the U.S. government, which dramatically reduces credit risk.
  • Tax Considerations: Interest and principal adjustments are taxable in the year they are earned, even though you may not receive the payment until maturity, making them best suited for tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs.

Current Dynamics

With recent Consumer Price Index numbers staying elevated, the yield on TIPS has become even more compelling. Investors are looking to lock in real returns, especially as nominal bond yields whipsaw on the back of central bank decisions. Those worried about purchasing power erosion should give TIPS a hard look this year.

2. Investment Grade Corporate Bonds

While government securities are a staple, investment-grade corporate bonds add another layer of attractiveness for fixed income investors by offering higher yields, albeit with slightly higher risk.

Why They Matter This Year

  • Solid Yield Spreads: As central banks have hiked rates, corporate bonds have seen significant spread widening versus risk-free Treasuries. These wider spreads mean higher yields and more compensation for perceived risk.
  • Company Fundamentals: Many large companies boast strong balance sheets after prudent moves made during recent economic uncertainty. This has resulted in upgrades to credit ratings, offering further solace to investors in this space.

Strategic Play

Diversifying a fixed income allocation with investment-grade names in industries like technology, health care, or consumer staples can support portfolio resilience. It’s also possible to access these securities via mutual funds or exchange-traded funds to gain broad exposure and mitigate individual company risk.

3. Municipal Bonds

Tax-advantaged and historically reliable, municipal bonds (“munis”) are finding renewed favor, particularly among those seeking income with tax efficiency. Issued by state and local governments, these securities finance a range of infrastructure and public projects.

Hottest Trends in Muni Bonds

  • Rising Yields: The run-up in interest rates has pushed municipal bond yields to levels not seen in over a decade, enhancing their attractiveness.
  • Credit Strength: Many municipalities entered this year with robust reserves thanks to federal stimulus and strong property tax collections, making defaults less likely in the near term.
  • Tax-Free Income: The federal tax-free interest—and, in many cases, state tax-free status—means munis can offer an after-tax yield that’s tough to beat, especially for investors in high-tax brackets.

Considerations

Certain segments of the muni market may carry risk—think lesser-known issuers or bonds tied to stressed sectors (like certain healthcare projects). Focusing on general obligation bonds from creditworthy states or tapping into professionally managed muni bond funds can help control for risk.

4. Agency Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS)

For investors willing to look beyond the basics, agency mortgage-backed securities are a solid way to boost income and diversify risk.

What Are Agency MBS?

These are bundles of residential mortgages, sold to investors by government-sponsored enterprises like Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac. The mortgages are pooled together, and the resulting securities pass through principal and interest payments to investors.

Why Agency MBS Shine

  • Government Guarantee: While not explicitly backed by the U.S. Treasury, agency MBS carry an “implicit” government guarantee, making them among the safer choices among MBS.
  • Attractive Yields: Yields on agency MBS are currently elevated, thanks to higher mortgage rates and spreads relative to Treasuries.
  • Diversification: Performance tends to be less correlated with corporate bonds and Treasuries, adding a valuable diversification tool.

Things to Keep in Mind

MBS can be sensitive to shifts in interest rates, especially as mortgage prepayments accelerate or slow significantly. However, actively managed MBS funds can help navigate this risk by dynamically adjusting their portfolios.

5. Emerging Markets Sovereign Debt

For those who can stomach a bit more volatility, emerging markets sovereign bonds represent a compelling opportunity, offering yields that outpace most developed-market alternatives.

Growth Story

Many emerging economies have improved fiscal discipline and reduced their reliance on hard currency borrowing. Sovereign debt from countries like Mexico, Indonesia, or Brazil can provide enhanced yield and sizable diversification benefits, especially when global growth prospects become more differentiated.

Risks and Reward Balance

  • Currency Fluctuations: Investing in local currency debt amplifies both the potential for higher gains and unexpected setbacks if the local currency depreciates.
  • Political and Credit Risk: Risk is higher compared to developed markets, but can be managed through diversification and focusing on countries with sound macroeconomic policies.
  • Access via Funds: Numerous ETFs and mutual funds specialize in emerging market debt, offering exposure without the need for deep-dive country analysis by individual investors.

Building a Fixed Income Portfolio: Mix, Match, and Monitor

The real art lies in blending these instruments to create a portfolio that matches your risk tolerance, income needs, and investment horizon. Here’s how to approach the task this year:

1. Understand Your Goals

Are you after steady income, capital preservation, or total returns? Understanding your priorities will guide you when allocating among government, corporate, municipal, and emerging market debt.

2. Mind the Duration

Rising and falling interest rates can impact the value of fixed income securities. Shorter-duration bonds tend to hold up better when yields climb, while long-duration bonds benefit from falling rates. Practically, having a mix of durations offers more resilience in an uncertain rate environment.

3. Consider Active vs. Passive Management

While index funds and ETFs deliver broad, cost-effective exposure, active managers can add value by nimbly rotating among sectors or maturities as market conditions evolve.

4. Keep an Eye on Credit Quality

Don’t be tempted by yield alone—the riskiest bonds pay the most because they’re the most likely to default. Sticking with investment-grade issuers or using mutual funds to access riskier segments can help manage downside risks.

5. Tax Planning Matters

If you’re in a higher tax bracket, municipal bonds or tax-efficient funds can significantly boost your after-tax yield. Meanwhile, holding taxable bond funds in tax-advantaged accounts preserves more of your return.

The Bottom Line: A New Era for Fixed Income

The fixed income world looks dramatically different this year compared to just a few years ago. Higher yields, persistent inflation, and renewed awareness of diversification have made government bonds, corporates, munis, mortgage-backed securities, and even emerging market debt the focal point of investor attention.

Success with fixed income isn’t about chasing the hottest product. It’s about steady, balanced choices made with a grasp of the unique risks and rewards each security offers. With the five options outlined above, there’s no shortage of opportunities for investors willing to think strategically about their long-term plan.

Each of these securities has its strengths and quirks—choosing the right mix can transform a staid fixed income portfolio into a robust, reliable driver of returns, regardless of where the turbulent markets go next.

For those uncertain about how to move forward, a financial advisor or a thoughtful allocation across several of these choices can help navigate the complexity and capitalize on this year’s fixed income opportunities. Whether you emphasize safety, higher yield, or tax advantages, fixed income securities continue to earn their place at the core of an investment strategy—especially in times of uncertainty.
