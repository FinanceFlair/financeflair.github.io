Contrarian Investing: A Contrarian's Perspective


Contrarian investing is a time-tested approach that involves taking an unconventional path in the world of finance. This strategy contradicts the prevailing trends, beliefs, and investor behavior. Persons who practice Contrarian investing are often seen as nonconformists, as they defy the majority’s choices to find investments that are undervalued or overlooked.

In this blog, we will explore the ins and outs of Contrarian investing, glancing into the mindset of a true Contrarian, and dive into the depths of some successful Contrarian investments throughout history.

Understanding Contrarian Investing

Contrarian investing is an umbrella term covering various investment techniques. At its core, it encourages investors to analyze market trends, investor behavior, and financial indicators to determine undervalued assets or sectors and invest accordingly. Unlike herd mentality, investors look for the hidden gems in the market that the majority tends to miss.

The Contrarian Mindset

The Contrarian approach requires investors to step away from the crowd mentality. Be prepared to stand out from the crowd, question the consensus, and act independently:

  1. Independent Thinker: Contrarian investors must be open-minded and independent thinkers, analyzing information without the influence of others. Contrarians know that following the crowd often leads to the wrong side of a trade.
  2. Patient: Contrarian investing often requires a long-term commitment and an exceptional level of patience. As investors wait for the market to recognize the value they’ve identified, patience becomes a crucial trait.
  3. Willing to Act Alone: Following your own convictions can be a lonely road. Contrarian investors must embrace this, and be prepared to act without the benefit of others taking the same path.
  4. Fuelled by Discipline and Sound Analysis: Above all, Contrarian investors rely on sound analysis, discipline, and clear thinking. Understanding their investments is crucial to weather the inevitable financial storms.

Successful Contrarian Investments Throughout History

Contrarian investing has produced many success stories. Unsurprisingly, some of these success stories come from prominent investors like Warren Buffet and Benjamin Graham. However, Contrarian success isn’t only for the rich and famous. Here are a few examples of notable Contrarian investments:

  1. WFCH ( Walter Energy): When the coal industry was at an all-time low during the period of 2011-2015, Contrarian investors saw potential. With a long-term perspective, investors profited significantly when the coal industry bounced back.

  2. Amazon (AMZN): From a small online bookstore in 1995 to the technology behemoth of today, Amazon represents one of the most significant Contrarian investments in modern history. Many investors initially disregarded the company, as it appeared to be a flash-in-the pan. Contrarian investors hung in there, reaping a stunning return on their investment.

  3. Retail Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs): In 2006, just before the subprime mortgage crisis, investors stayed away from retail real estate investment trusts. Contrarian investors knew that the retail industry would bounce back, patience led them to buy the dip, and they’ve reaped the rewards subsequently.

Embracing Contrarian Investing

As the old adage goes, ‘Be Contrary, but not when the crowd is right.’ Contrarian investing requires a practiced hand and a deep understanding of market trends and investor behavior.

The next time you take a look at the financial market, consider standing out from the crowd and taking the road less traveled. You might just find that rare gem that others overlook, all while growing your wealth.

Action tonight may not invite applause; tomorrow brings success if the decision proves right. The Contrarian path isn’t for the faint of heart, but for those up to the challenge, its rewards are immeasurable.
