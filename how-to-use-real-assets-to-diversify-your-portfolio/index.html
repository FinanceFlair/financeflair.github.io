How to Use Real Assets to Diversify Your Portfolio


As an investor, you're likely no stranger to the concept of diversification. It's a fundamental principle of investing that can help mitigate risk, increase potential returns, and provide a more stable long-term portfolio. However, many investors focus solely on traditional asset classes like stocks and bonds. In this post, we'll delve into the world of real assets and explore how incorporating them into your portfolio can lead to a more diversified and robust investment strategy. What are Real Assets? Real assets refer to tangible goods that hold intrinsic value and value fluctuations over time. Examples of real assets include:

Art and collectibles Real estate investment trusts (REITs) Commodity futures and options Gold and other precious metals Private equity and venture capital investments Oil and gas assets Timberland and forestry investments

Why Incorporate Real Assets into Your Portfolio? Incorporating real assets into your portfolio can help balance out the risk associated with traditional investments. While stocks and bonds can be volatile, real assets tend to experience lower correlation with the broader financial markets, reducing overall portfolio risk. Additionally, real assets often provide a steady income stream through rental income, dividend payments, or interest payments. Benefits of Investing in Real Assets

Increased diversification: By including real assets in your portfolio, you can reduce dependency on traditional investments and decrease overall risk. Stable income: Many real assets provide regular income through rental income, dividend payments, or interest payments. Protection against inflation: As inflation increases, the value of real assets often rises, providing a hedge against inflation's erosive effects. Potential for long-term appreciation: Certain real assets, such as real estate and art, have the potential to appreciate in value over time, providing a potential long-term investment opportunity.

Popular Types of Real Assets for Investing Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) REITs allow individuals to invest in real estate without directly owning physical properties. They offer a steady income stream and potentially lower risk compared to directly owning real estate. REITs can be categorized into several sub-types, including:

Equity REITs: Invest in properties and own them directly. Mortgage REITs: Invest primarily in mortgages and other types of real estate debt. Specialty REITs: Focus on specific real estate sectors, such as healthcare or technology.

Commodity Investments Investing in commodities, like gold, oil, or agricultural products, can provide a hedge against inflation and market downturns. Commodity investments can be volatile, but they often exhibit low correlation with traditional investments. Art and Collectibles Investing in art and collectibles, such as fine art, rare coins, or vintage cars, can be a unique way to diversify your portfolio. These investments often hold value due to their rarity and demand. Commodity Futures and Options Futures and options contracts can provide a way to invest in commodities without owning the physical assets. These contracts allow you to speculate on price movements, but keep in mind that trading futures and options can be complex and risky. Investing in Real Assets: Best Practices When investing in real assets, keep the following considerations in mind:

Educate yourself: Understand the underlying asset class and market before investing. Diversify within real assets: Spread your portfolio across multiple types of real assets to minimize risk. Be prepared for illiquidity: Some real assets, such as art or real estate, may be illiquid, making it difficult to quickly sell the asset. Set clear investment goals: Determine your investment objectives and risk tolerance before making a decision. Maintain a long-term perspective: Real assets often require a long-term investment horizon.

By incorporating real assets into your portfolio, you can create a more diversified and robust investment strategy. By understanding the various types of real assets available, their benefits, and the best practices for investing in them, you'll be well-equipped to navigate the world of real assets and make informed investment decisions. This can help you reduce risk, increase potential returns, and achieve your long-term financial goals.
