Breaking Barriers: 31 Groundbreaking Equity Research Techniques for Top Performers


In the competitive world of finance and investment, the battle for achieving higher returns and standing out from the pack is more challenging than ever. Top performers relentlessly push boundaries and innovate, constantly looking for new sources of edge. To help you on your quest, we’ve put together 31 groundbreaking equity research techniques that will transform your approach to identifying, analyzing, and profiting from tomorrow’s opportunities.

1. Cutting-Edge Data Sources

Harnessing new and unconventional data sources can be a game-changer for equity research. Look beyond traditional financial statements and incorporate alternative data such as satellite imagery (for construction activity), social media sentiment, or website traffic. Companies like Sentieo and Orbintelligence provide tools to aggregate diverse datasets.

2. Unstructured Text Analysis

Leverage natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning to analyze vast amounts of unstructured text data, such as news articles, press releases, earnings call transcripts, and social media posts. Tools like RapidAI, Meta’s Text API, or Google Cloud’s Natural Language API can aid in sentiment analysis, topic modeling, and entity recognition.

3. Machine Learning-Based Predictive Analytics

Machine learning algorithms can create powerful predictive models based on historical pricing data and various other company-specific indicators. Popular machine learning libraries like Python’s scikit-learn or R’s caret can help democratize algorithmic trading.

4. Sophisticated Quantitative Analysis

Elevate your research with advanced statistical techniques like causal inference, time-series analysis, and machine learning models. Tools such as Regression Models (Python) or forecast (R) can provide an in-depth understanding of company fundamentals and market dynamics.

5. Network Analysis and Social Graphs

Explore the relationships between companies, people, and industries by constructing and analyzing social graphs. Platforms like NetworkX (Python) and Gephi can help identify hidden patterns and potential risks in your investment thesis.

6. Blockchain and Cryptocurrency Analysis

Diving into blockchain and cryptocurrency applications can open new opportunities for investors. Platforms like Etherscan offer insights into blockchain transactions, tokens, and smart contracts. Additionally, understanding cryptocurrency markets can yield valuable alternative investment ideas.

7. Crowdsourced Data for Market Sentiment

Leverage the wisdom of the crowds by incorporating market sentiment data from platforms like QuantShare, Finviz, or StockTwits. Crowdsourced data can provide valuable information about market perception, which often precedes fundamental changes.

8. Deep Learning with Large Datasets

Deep learning neural networks can unlock complex patterns within large datasets. Deep learning frameworks like TensorFlow or PyTorch are excellent options for tackling high-dimensional or unstructured data challenges.

9. Big Data Platforms for Real-Time Analysis

Leverage the power of big data platforms like Apache Spark, Hive, or Presto for seamless real-time data aggregation and analysis. These platforms can efficiently combine diverse data sources from across the globe, providing real-time insights into global events impacting specific industries and equities.

10. Incorporating ESG Factors

Environmental, social, and corporate governance (ESG) factors are becoming increasingly important for investors and are considered in equity research. Utilize tools like Bloomberg’s ESG data, Refinitiv’s ESG Database, or Morningstar’s Sustainability Ratings & Reports to assess ESG-related opportunities and risks.

11. Factor Investing and Smart Beta

Incorporate factor investing and smart beta strategies to enhance alpha generation, risk management, and portfolio diversification. Blackrock’s iShares offers a comprehensive suite of smart beta ETFs, indexed based on various investment factors like value, momentum, quality, and low volatility.

12. Dividend Discount Modeling

Advanced dividend discount modeling can help capture the value of dividend-paying stocks more accurately. Quantitative Accounting (Python) offers tools to disaggregate cash flows and estimate future dividend policies accurately.

13. Catastrophe Bonds and Insurance-Led Investing

Understanding the growing market for catastrophe bonds and alternative risk transfer (ART) strategies in insurance can uncover new investment opportunities. Platforms like Moody’s, Standard & Poor’s, and Fitch Ratings provide insights on catastrophe bond issuances and exposures.

14. Volatility Trading: VIX and Volatility Arbitrage

Incorporate volatility trading strategies in your research, including VIX options strategies, volatility arbitrage, and risk reversals. Financial tools like OptionMetrics and IVolatility can help fine-tune your understanding of volatility dynamics in the markets.

15. Derivatives for Risk Management and Arbitrage

Derivatives play an essential role in risk management and can also be a source of profitable investment strategies. Platforms like FuturesSource and Benzinga Pro offer comprehensive data and analysis on futures, options, and equity swaps to help inform your research.

16. Spend Analytics and Procurement Data

Economic moats can be found in companies with monopolistic or oligopolistic access to unique resources. Spend analytics and procurement data can uncover these hidden gems. consider using tools like Public Spend Summit, Spend Matters, or Bloomberg Government for high-quality procurement data.

17. Satellite Imagery for Infrastructure Projects

By leveraging satellite imagery, you can monitor infrastructure projects and construction activities across the globe. Companies like Planet Labs, Maxar Technologies, and Orbital Insight provide high-resolution satellite imagery and analytics tools.

18. Geolocation Data for Consumer Behavior

Incorporate geolocation data for better understanding consumer behavior and regional preferences. Companies like Cuebiq and Foursquare provide geolocation data for research purposes.

19. Voice Assistant Usage and Telecommunications

Voice assistant adoption and advancements in artificial intelligence can give you deeper insights into telecommunications trends. Platforms like Google’s Trends, voicebot.ai, or VoiceHub enable access to voice assistant usage statistics and reports.

20. Emerging Technology Trends

Stay ahead of the curve by closely monitoring the emergence and adoption of cutting-edge technologies like AI, blockchain, IoT, and 5G. Industry forums, conferences, McKinsey’s Emerging Technologies, and MarketsandMarkets can keep you updated on the latest technological advancements.

21. Media Habits and Advertising Data

Gain valuable information on consumer behaviors and maritime preferences by analyzing media consumption and advertising trends. Tools like ComScore, Nielsen Holdings, and AdAge provide comprehensive reports and data on media and advertising trends.

22. Supply Chain Resilience Analysis

A well-managed, resilient supply chain can provide a competitive edge in both bullish and bearish markets. Utilize resources from Supply Chain Digital, Gartner, or Resilinc to assess and identify potential risks within supply chains.

23. Cybersecurity and Data Privacy Analysis

With an increasing focus on cybersecurity and data privacy, investing in companies with a significant foothold in securing valuable data can be a profitable strategy. Platforms like Cybersecurity Ventures, Gartner Magic Quadrant, and Forrester provide market research and analyst reports on emerging trends in cybersecurity.

24. Education and Online Learning

The rise of e-commerce, online education, and remote working boasts a vast sector of growth opportunities. Monitor trends from However, tools like EducationReimagined.org, EdTech Magazine, and Class Central to uncover opportunities in this booming industry.

25. Trauma-Informed Investing

The growing attention to mental health and trauma can signal worthwhile investment opportunities in healthcare and mental health services. Utilize resources like Mental Health America, the National Alliance on Mental Illness, and the Global Trauma Research Network for guidance.

26. Beneficial Ownership Transparency

Beneficial ownership transparency can reveal ownership structures, a critical factor for research in governance and corruption. Platforms like OpenOwnership and GlobalWitness offer access to beneficial ownership data and analysis tools.

27. Regulatory Developments

Monitoring and anticipating regulatory changes can be an essential aspect of equity research. Platforms like Sec.gov, FDIC.gov, and FCA.gov provide comprehensive information on regulatory changes impacting various industries.

28. Gender and Racial Diversity Analytics

Diversity and inclusion in the workplace are growing concerns among many investors. Incorporate resources like the World Bank’s Women, Business and the Law, Catalyst, and the Thomson Reuters Diversity & Inclusion Platform to evaluate companies’ diversity performance.

29. Behavioral Economics

A deep understanding of behavioral economics principles can improve investment strategies and decision-making. Stanford University’s CBE, The Hispanic Foundation, and IZA offer resources and insights into behavioral economics research.

30. Developmental Economics

Examining companies that contribute to socioeconomic development enables investors to support and benefit from progressive growth. Explore the World Bank’s Development Research, the UN Global Compact, and the International Growth Centre for research on developmental economics.

31. Public Health Innovations

Emerging public health trends and advancements in medicine can signal lucrative investment opportunities. Utilize resources like the World Health Organization, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), and MedTech Boston for public health data and insights.

The incorporation of new research techniques and ideas has the potential to revolutionize your equity research strategy, thereby enhancing your risk-adjusted returns. By staying informed, being creative, and pushing the boundaries of conventional equity research, you’ll be better positioned for the future of investing. Happy analyzing!
