Mastering Crowdfunding: Best Platforms to Kickstart Your Project


As the crowdfunding industry continues to grow, it becomes essential for project creators to understand the various platforms to choose the right fit. Crowdfunding has democratized access to finance, allowing small businesses, entrepreneurs, and creative individuals to receive funding from a large number of people online. With a thriving crowdfunding market, it’s crucial to evaluate your project idea, target audience, and funding objectives to select the best platform to maximize success. Here are some standout platforms in the crowdfunding landscape:

  1. Kickstarter

Originating in 2009, Kickstarter is one of the most recognized crowdfunding superpowers. With over $4 billion pledged and a completion rate of 44%, Kickstarter focuses on a “all-or-nothing” method, where creators receive all the funds or none at all. Kickstarter demands that projects adhere to strict categorizations, ensuring project visibility and a well-defined audience. The platform offers excellent visibility benefits and functions well for creative, imaginative, and innovative projects, particularly in design, technology, and artistic endeavors.

  1. Indiegogo

Established in 2008, Indiegogo is a more versatile and flexible alternative to Kickstarter, offering various funding models, including fixed, flexible, and perpetual funding options. Indiegogo caters to innovative projects and direct-to-consumer businesses, ranging from arts and culture to technology and eco-conscious sourcing. Indiegogo has a completion rate of 8% for fixed-goal campaigns and 17% for flexible-goal campaigns, making it one of the most adaptable crowdfunding platforms.

  1. GoFundMe

For charitable and personal cause campaigns, GoFundMe stands as the undisputed champion. The platform focuses on supporting individuals and organizations seeking financial assistance to address medical bills, education, personal endeavors, animal welfare, and even funerals. GoFundMe had an impressive $14 billion raised figure in 2020, showcasing its ability to thrive with people-centered campaigns.

  1. Crowdfunder

Focusing on creating a mutually beneficial relationship between project creators and investors, Crowdfunder differentiates itself by offering rewards beyond the completion of a project. Crowdfunder appeals to investment-ready projects, particularly in real estate, film, and socially responsible ventures. The platform’s successes reflect its value-added approach, boasting an astonishing 90% project success rate.

  1. Crowd Supply

Specializing in hardware and technology projects, Crowd Supplyhas meticulously crafted itself for project creators who need support with manufacturing, distribution, and logistics. Crowd Supply ensures transparency by highlighting real-time project updates and progress toward specific funding goals, enhancing investor confidence. Crowd Supply’s secretariat lies in facilitating a smooth supply chain for innovative hardware projects.

In conclusion, mastering crowdfunding begins with a thorough investigation and understanding of your project goals and target audience. Platforms like Kickstarter, Indiegogo, GoFundMe, Crowdfunder, and Crowd Supply offer unique features, strengths, and target areas, granting projects the best opportunity to succeed. Reviewing the platforms, their benefits, and selecting the most suitable for your project takes time and research. However, scoping out the right platform ensures maximum visibility, support, and quantum-leap success for your ambitious project.
