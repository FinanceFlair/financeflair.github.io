From Dollar to Dollar: A Guide to Inflation-Adjusted Investing


Inflation has been an enduring challenge for investors globally, causing them to continuously adjust their portfolios in search of better returns. As they say, “An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure,” so it’s wise to familiarize ourselves with strategies that can help mitigate this phenomenon. In this guide, we’ll delve into the world of inflation-adjusted investing, a technique designed to safeguard against the insidious effects of inflation.

Why We Need to be Inflation-Adjusted Investors

Inflation is the archenemy of savings and investments. It chokes the purchasing power of our money and erodes the value of our assets over time. To counter this, investors need to form a comprehensive strategy to protect themselves from the rampant spread of inflation. Such a strategy involves adjusting investments to account for inflation and considering investment classes that demonstrate an ability to combat its effects.

Understanding the Principal Concepts of Inflation-Adjusted Investing

Inflation-adjusted investing centers on two key principles:

  1. Principal Preservation: This concept highlights the necessity of protecting the real value of investments. As inflation reduces the purchasing power of money over time, it’s pivotal to seek investment opportunities that can preserve the principal sum. Asset classes that regularly generate earnings, either through regular income distributions or capital appreciation, have the potential to safeguard the principal sum over time.

  2. Real Return: Investing with an eye to achieve a real return above inflation is fundamental to the strategy. Consequently, an investment must show an adequate real return to prevent the erosion of the initial investment’s value. Stocks, bonds, certificates of deposit (CDs), and to some extent, precious metals, can be inflation-adjusted investments with potential for a real return above the inflation rate.

Choosing the Best Inflation-Protected Investments

Different investments respond differently to inflation. Comparatively, some inflation-protected investments are superior to others as they hedge against the pernicious effects of inflation. Here’s a look at some top choices:

  1. Inflation-indexed Bonds (IIBs), also known as Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS): These bonds adjust their payouts according to the rate of inflation. They provide steady, fixed returns, but their value grows when inflation rates rise, similar to how you purchase an inflation-adjusted annuity. IIBs’ yields are relatively low, and investing in them requires a significant initial capital commitment.

  2. Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs): Investing in REITs introduces investors to a tangible inflation hedge. As the price of houses appreciates due to higher costs of living and supplies of homes being limited, so should the value of REITs (at least theoretically). However, like all investments, real estate’s performance isn’t guaranteed, and we should consider this with an open mind.

  3. Dividend Stocks: Companies that consistently and sustainably distribute dividends often become attractive to investors looking for a return beyond basic stock market inflation. Such investments might benefit from share price appreciation and effects from dividend income, providing a hedge against inflation – although shared results may differ based on the corporation and industry.

Eagerly Embracing Inflation-Adjusted Investing Strategies

Embracing inflation-adjusted investing starts with understanding the concepts and principles of this powerful investment strategy. Considering the discussed investment classes will provide a foundation for a well-diversified and lucrative investment portfolio, particularly concerning the threat of inflation.

Diligently applying inflation-adjusted strategies is crucial to ensuring our investments endure the ravages of inflation. Institutes an investment style that maintains purchasing power over long-term hold periods and selects assets that show resilience to inflation. The task might not be effortless, but as the great Warren Buffett advises: “The investment of your funds, whether large or small, should be a major discrete part of your life’s purpose.”

By educating ourselves and taking the necessary steps to combat the effects of inflation, we become better-equipped to protect our investments and achieve financial stability and growth. The path to navigating and tackling inflation isn’t necessarily linear, but the results are rewarding for those who dare to tread. Happy investing!
