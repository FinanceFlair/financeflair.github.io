Futures Trading: Roll Yield and Contango/Backwardation


Futures trading is an exciting and dynamic field that offers investors the opportunity to profit from price movements in various markets. Two important concepts in futures trading are roll yield and contango/backwardation. Understanding these concepts can help traders make informed decisions and optimize their trading strategies.

Roll Yield

Roll yield refers to the profit or loss that arises from rolling a futures contract from one expiration month to another. In simple terms, it is the difference between the price of the expiring contract and the price of the new contract. This roll yield can be positive or negative, depending on the market conditions and the specific futures contract being traded.

When the market is in contango, which means that the price of the futures contract is higher than the spot price, the roll yield tends to be negative. This is because traders are effectively selling the expiring contract at a lower price and buying the new contract at a higher price. Conversely, when the market is in backwardation, where the futures price is lower than the spot price, the roll yield is typically positive. Traders can profit from rolling their positions forward in backwardation as they sell the expiring contract at a higher price and buy the new contract at a lower price.

Contango and Backwardation

Contango and backwardation are two market conditions that affect the pricing of futures contracts. Contango occurs when the futures price is higher than the expected spot price at the contract’s expiration. This often happens when there is an oversupply of the underlying asset or when the market expects prices to decrease in the future. In a contango market, the futures curve slopes upward.

On the other hand, backwardation occurs when the futures price is lower than the expected spot price at expiration. This usually happens when there is a shortage of the underlying asset or when the market expects prices to rise in the future. In a backwardation market, the futures curve slopes downward.

Understanding the market conditions of contango and backwardation is crucial for futures traders. It provides insight into the supply and demand dynamics of the underlying asset and can help inform trading decisions. For example, if a trader expects contango, they may choose to short the futures contract to profit from the expected decrease in prices. Conversely, if a trader expects backwardation, they may choose to go long on the futures contract to profit from the expected increase in prices.

Conclusion

In conclusion, roll yield and contango/backwardation are important concepts in futures trading. Roll yield refers to the profit or loss that arises from rolling a futures contract from one expiration month to another. It can be positive or negative, depending on the market conditions. Contango and backwardation, on the other hand, are market conditions that affect the pricing of futures contracts. Contango occurs when the futures price is higher than the expected spot price, while backwardation occurs when the futures price is lower than the expected spot price. Understanding these concepts can help traders make informed decisions and optimize their trading strategies in the dynamic world of futures trading.

Remember to always conduct thorough research and consult with a financial advisor before engaging in futures trading. Happy trading!
