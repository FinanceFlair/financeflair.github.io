How to Apply the Capitalized Earnings Method for Corporate Valuation


Companies have always been scrutinized by investors and stakeholders for their performance, growth, and profitability. One method that helps in understanding a company’s financial health is the capitalized earnings method. In this post, we’ll delve into how this technique can be applied for evaluating a corporation’s worth.

The capitalized earnings method, also known as the earnings-cap method or the capitalized net income approach, is a valuation technique that calculates the economic value of a company based on its historical earnings. This method entails the following steps:

  1. Calculate the sustained earnings: To apply the capitalized earnings method, first, estimate a company’s sustained earnings. These earnings are the average yearly earnings over the past number of years, considering profits from both continuing and discontinued operations. This calculation provides investors with a stable earnings estimate, factoring in the company’s recent financial history.

  2. Evaluate the growth rate: Assess the company’s probable future growth rate. Usually, organizations grow at a slower rate than the overall industry. However, factors like market share, product innovation, and expansion can significantly influence a company’s growth. Investors should consider the company’s growth history and industry trends to estimate prospective growth rates.

  3. Select a discount rate: The most decisive aspect of the capitalized earnings method is the choice of the discount rate. This rate is a key component in determining the present value of future earnings. Typically, adopting the weighted average cost of capital (WACC) or the company’s debt and equity costs is more suitable for discount calculation.

  4. Capitalize the earnings: Once the discount rate is firm, investors can calculate the company’s value using the following formula:

    Value = (sustained earnings) / (discount rate)

The value calculated here represents the company’s worth based on its past performance, estimated growth, and the minimal rate investors would require for the investment. However, it’s vital to bear in mind that this method takes the company’s current circumstances into account and doesn’t consider factors like regulatory changes or economic recessions that might impact the company’s future profitability.

In practice, the capitalized earnings method allows investors and stakeholders to:

  • Evaluate a company’s financial performance
  • Estimate the worth of the business in the context of potential acquisitions or mergers
  • Gain an improved understanding of the company’s potential future earnings
  • Gauge the company’s growth prospects against industry benchmarks

While no single method can capture the full spectrum of a company’s financial worth, understanding the capitalized earnings method can aid in assessing a corporation’s valuation. By considering historical earnings, growth prospects, and a suitable discount rate, investors are armed to make informed decisions about the financial health and value of a company.

In summary, properly applying the capitalized earnings method for corporate valuation requires an analysis of a company’s historical earnings, estimated future growth, and suitable discount rate. Although this technique doesn’t encompass every variable impacting a company’s value, it provides a solid foundation for assessing a corporation’s financial health and potential profitability.
