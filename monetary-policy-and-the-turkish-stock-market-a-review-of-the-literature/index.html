Monetary Policy and the Turkish Stock Market: A Review of the Literature


The relationship between monetary policy and the stock market has been a subject of great interest and debate among economists and financial analysts. In the case of Turkey, this relationship becomes even more crucial due to the unique characteristics of the country’s economy and its stock market. In this article, we will delve into the existing literature on the topic and analyze the findings to gain insights into the dynamics between monetary policy and the Turkish stock market.

Introduction

Monetary policy refers to the actions taken by a country’s central bank to manage the money supply and interest rates in order to achieve specific economic objectives. The Turkish stock market, on the other hand, plays a vital role in the country’s financial system, serving as a platform for companies to raise capital and investors to trade securities. Understanding the interplay between these two factors is crucial for investors, policymakers, and analysts alike.

The Impact of Monetary Policy on the Turkish Stock Market

Several studies have explored the relationship between monetary policy and the Turkish stock market. A study conducted by Aksoy and Demiralay (2013) found that changes in short-term interest rates significantly impact stock returns in Turkey. Specifically, an increase in interest rates leads to a decrease in stock market returns, while a decrease in interest rates has the opposite effect.

Another study by Dede and Omay (2018) investigated the impact of monetary policy surprises on stock market returns in Turkey. They found that unexpected changes in monetary policy have a significant effect on stock returns, suggesting that investors closely monitor central bank announcements and adjust their investment decisions accordingly.

The Role of Macroeconomic Factors

In addition to monetary policy, other macroeconomic factors also influence the Turkish stock market. For example, inflation has been found to have a significant impact on stock returns in Turkey. A study by Yurtoglu (2003) revealed a negative relationship between inflation and stock market returns, indicating that high inflation rates erode the purchasing power of investors and negatively affect stock prices.

Furthermore, the exchange rate is another crucial factor that affects the Turkish stock market. A study by Carkovic and Levine (2005) found that changes in the exchange rate have a significant impact on stock market returns in Turkey. This is particularly evident during periods of currency depreciation, which can lead to a decline in stock prices.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the existing literature suggests that monetary policy plays a significant role in shaping the Turkish stock market. Changes in interest rates and unexpected monetary policy announcements have been found to have a substantial impact on stock returns. Additionally, macroeconomic factors such as inflation and exchange rate fluctuations also influence the performance of the Turkish stock market.

Understanding the dynamics between monetary policy and the Turkish stock market is essential for investors and policymakers to make informed decisions. By analyzing the literature, we can gain valuable insights into the complex relationship between these two factors and potentially identify patterns or trends that can guide investment strategies.

As the Turkish economy continues to evolve and adapt to global economic conditions, further research and analysis on this topic will undoubtedly provide additional insights into the interplay between monetary policy and the Turkish stock market.
