Private Placements: Investing in Infrastructure and Real Assets


Private placements have gained significant popularity among investors seeking alternative investment opportunities. This investment strategy allows individuals to invest directly in infrastructure and real assets, providing them with unique advantages and potential returns. In this blog post, we will explore the world of private placements and delve into the benefits and considerations of investing in infrastructure and real assets.

What are Private Placements?

Private placements refer to investments in securities that are not publicly traded on a stock exchange. Instead, these investments are offered to a select group of individuals, such as high net worth individuals, institutional investors, and accredited investors. Private placements are typically conducted by companies looking to raise capital for various purposes, including funding infrastructure projects and acquiring real assets.

Investing in Infrastructure

Infrastructure investments involve financing or acquiring assets that are essential for the functioning of society, such as roads, bridges, airports, and power plants. These investments offer several advantages to investors:

  1. Steady and predictable cash flow: Infrastructure assets often generate stable and predictable cash flows, making them attractive to income-focused investors. Toll roads, for example, generate revenue through toll collection, providing a reliable income stream.

  2. Long-term investment horizon: Infrastructure projects are typically long-term in nature, providing investors with an opportunity for steady returns over an extended period. This long-term investment horizon aligns well with the needs of pension funds and other institutional investors with long-term liabilities.

  3. Inflation protection: Infrastructure investments often have built-in inflation protection mechanisms. For instance, contracts for infrastructure projects may include provisions for regular price adjustments, ensuring that the returns on investment keep up with inflation.

  4. Diversification: Investing in infrastructure allows investors to diversify their portfolios and reduce risk. Infrastructure assets have historically exhibited low correlation with traditional asset classes, such as stocks and bonds, making them an attractive addition to a well-diversified portfolio.

Investing in Real Assets

Real assets encompass a wide range of tangible assets, including real estate, natural resources, and commodities. Investing in real assets offers unique benefits to investors:

  1. Tangible and appreciating assets: Real assets provide investors with the opportunity to own tangible assets that have the potential to appreciate in value over time. Real estate, for example, has historically shown strong appreciation and can provide both income and capital appreciation to investors.

  2. Inflation hedge: Real assets, particularly commodities such as gold and oil, have traditionally served as a hedge against inflation. When inflation rises, the value of real assets often increases, providing a buffer against the erosion of purchasing power.

  3. Portfolio diversification: Similar to infrastructure investments, real assets offer diversification benefits. These assets have shown low correlation with traditional asset classes, which can help reduce overall portfolio risk.

  4. Income generation: Real assets, such as rental properties or farmland, can generate consistent income in the form of rent or lease payments. This income can provide a steady cash flow to investors, especially those seeking regular income.

Considerations for Private Placements

While private placements in infrastructure and real assets offer compelling advantages, investors should consider several factors before committing capital:

  1. Illiquidity: Private placements are typically illiquid investments, meaning that investors may have limited opportunities to sell their holdings. Investors should be prepared to hold their investments for an extended period and have a long-term investment horizon.

  2. Risk profile: Like any investment, private placements carry inherent risks. Investors should carefully assess the risk profile of each opportunity, considering factors such as project-specific risks, regulatory risks, and market conditions.

  3. Due diligence: Conducting thorough due diligence is crucial before investing in private placements. This includes evaluating the track record and expertise of the investment sponsor, analyzing the financials and projections, and understanding the underlying assets and their potential risks.

  4. Investment size: Private placements often require a significant minimum investment, which may be a barrier for some investors. It is essential to assess the investment size and determine if it aligns with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

In conclusion, private placements offer investors a unique opportunity to invest directly in infrastructure and real assets. These investments provide various benefits, including steady cash flow, diversification, and potential inflation protection. However, investors should carefully consider the illiquid nature of private placements, assess the associated risks, conduct thorough due diligence, and ensure the investment aligns with their financial goals. Investing in private placements can be a rewarding strategy for those seeking alternative investment opportunities and looking to diversify their portfolios.
